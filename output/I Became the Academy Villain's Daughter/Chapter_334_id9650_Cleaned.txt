# Enhanced Translation: 334 - Unfair Snowball

Tito-soga, who had recorded 30 and 33 chapters before and after activating Strength but failed to escape the classroom by the deadline, grumbled with a sulky face.

"Oknodie is banned from taking exams first!"
"Huh? That's unfair. Where's that rule written?"
"Oknodie is the unfair one. With that kind of skill, passing first leaves nothing for those behind. In the end, only one person who beat the record managed to leave the classroom."
"I wanted us to go home together!"
"That... made me a little happy. Hehe."

Zuang, who had been listening nearby, lifted her mask with a surprised expression, revealing her bare face.

"Who was that one person?"
"Senior Bigstone."
"Must be skilled, huh?"
"He combined Strength with penetration magic to pass. The professor said it was fine as long as you master the basics, applications don't matter. Tch. Passing with cheap tricks not taught in lectures is totally cheating. This is why first-years shouldn't take that class."
"That's strange. I take that class with Oknodie too."

Tito-soga's eyes lit up.

"Really? What was it like there? Didn't Oknodie bully the seniors?"
"I wouldn't know about that."
"Didn't the lectures get harder because of Oknodie?"
"Not particularly. It was just fun."

Wednesday, second period.
Professor Bronze's lecture on "Everything About Dismantling."
After the shocking mimic experience in the underground stairway, the two decided to challenge Professor Bronze's lecture, reasoning that they couldn't let Oknodie attend such dangerous special training alone.
Since Oknodie, Zuang, and Tito-soga all attended this lecture together, information exchange was active.

"What did you do in the 'Basics and Understanding of Disguise' lecture?"
"Want me to tell you?"
"Yes, yes! I want to hear!"

Zuang recalled what had happened the day before yesterday.


* * *


"Basics and Understanding of Disguise."
Monday and Wednesday, 6th period, 9 PM to 11 PM.
Professor Pinkberry's lecture.

Zuang had a clear concept when choosing lectures.
It must help develop skills as an assassin!
Disguise techniques were very useful not only for infiltrating while avoiding others' eyes but also for approaching targets even in plain sight.

"What a weakling. What first-year tries to learn disguise techniques already?"
"..."

Among the many older male professors at the Academy, this uniquely young and cheerful professor particularly caught the eye.
The Academy's shortest professor, swinging her pink twin-tails from side to side while prancing in front of the blackboard.
Standing at just 144 cm tall.
She was also the only professor Zuang, the third shortest person at the Academy, could surpass in height.

'She's just like Oknodie.'

Someone stronger than me but simultaneously shorter than me.

"What a strange kid."

Despite her harsh words clearly meant to drive Zuang away, the professor gave up when she saw Zuang sitting calmly.
The disguise lecture was one that first-years typically didn't attend.
Just last week, quite a few first-years had flocked to the class after hearing rumors about a short professor like Oknodie teaching the course.
Professor Pinkberry, with her cute appearance but contrasting temperament, had taught those disrespectful students a lesson by transforming them into mice or fleas.
...It made one wonder if this was a transformation magic class rather than disguise techniques!
Of course, Zuang suffered no such punishment.
Being short herself and quite fond of the short professor, she never made any disrespectful comments.
The professor, sensitive to goodwill as much as malice, recognized Zuang's good nature and even gave her special treatment as a commendable first-year.

"I brought a friend."

When someone I like meets a friend I like, my happiness doubles.
It would be even better if they got along well.
Zuang's simple idea seemed to hit the mark, as Professor Pinkberry's reaction to Oknodie was quite intense.

"You...! The child who had that mosquito nearby!"

Professor Pinkberry, who had recognized Oknodie while watching the "Inter-grade Competition: Dodgeball" at the sports festival.
In response to the professor's reaction, Oknodie feigned ignorance.

"Mosquito? I don't know anything about that!"

To Zuang, who remembered how Oknodie had used that mosquito on the cruise ship to send the Apostle of the Comfortable Chair to his grave with the "Latent Mana-Paralyzing Poison," this seemed like the natural response.
It would be unworthy of an assassin to openly reveal a secret weapon that could threaten every student and professor in the classroom if she wanted.
Pretending not to know even when you do.
Pretending something doesn't exist even when it does.
Shamelessness is a mindset an assassin must possess.
Just like Oknodie's habit of brazenly claiming she'll someday be 230 cm tall despite being the Academy's shortest at 133 cm.

"Reeeeally? So you have absolutely nothing to do with the mosquito master who fearlessly entered a professor's bedroom and sucked blood~?"

Oknodie awkwardly looked away while whistling.
One might wonder if she even had any intention of hiding it.

"Well, this works out. I'll properly educate both of you now. Including you, Zuang, who brought this mosquito-wielding enemy with that innocent face that made your professor let her guard down!"

Professor Pinkberry's disguise lecture last week had emphasized TPO—dressing appropriately for time, place, and occasion to blend in naturally anywhere.
However, today's lecture featured very unique outfits that directly contradicted last week's teachings.

"Fishnet stockings?"
"Bunny ear headbands?"

The second-year seniors jumped up in shock.

"Professor! How could you make us do this? Bunny girl outfits?"
"Forcing us to dress like lowly demi-humans—are you insulting us?"
"If you don't like it, don't attend. I'll only teach students who can wear this."

The second-year seniors left the classroom, grumbling.
Suddenly, only Zuang and Oknodie remained in the disguise classroom.

"Well? Can you two wear this?"

The professor smiled provocatively and annoyingly, just like Princess Mesugakki, deliberately irritating people.
Zuang smiled slightly under her mask and changed into the bunny girl outfit.
Neither the cold night air stinging her skin nor the unfamiliar sensation of fabric brushing against her body felt strange.
Due to her profession, she had experience wearing night infiltration clothes to reach mansion ceilings, then changing into maid outfits to pick locks and enter through windows.
There's no hierarchy among disguises.
Only outfits that are advantageous or disadvantageous for the mission.
Unlike the second-year seniors, her extreme pragmatism found no reason to dislike the bunny girl outfit.

"Are you okay with this, Oknodie?"

Oknodie had changed just as nonchalantly as Zuang.
Seemingly bothered by the bunny ear headband, she fiddled with the long drooping ears as she answered.

"Veteran players collect costumes when they have nothing better to do! Of course I'm fine with it. I actually like it!"

Only Professor Pinkberry was fuming.
Her scheme to teach a lesson to Oknodie, who was connected to the student she had personally thrown into the Great Prison after being bitten by a mosquito, and to Zuang who had brought her, had backfired.
The only ones who left were the second-years, while the remaining students showed no awareness of being insulted.
In a world where demi-humans were widely considered inferior to humans—and rabbit demi-humans were at the bottom of the food pyramid—they should have run out crying. Seeing them actually enjoying it made her blood boil.

"Being able to infiltrate naturally even in unnatural attire. That's the next level of disguise techniques."

Unlike Professor Pinkberry, who was struggling to suppress her discomfort, the two first-years' satisfaction with the lecture remained sky-high.


* * *


"That's what happened. The professor has good sense, right? She must have recognized that we're assassins and focused on teaching disguise techniques that would help us."
"Is that really it? Somehow I doubt that..."

Can I really say I'm taking the same class that all the second-years ran away from?
Tito-soga had some doubts but had no time to raise questions.
Professor Bronze's lecture was beginning.

"Today, I'll teach you how to dismantle magic. Once you complete this course, you'll be able to dismantle the night-time magical traps surrounding the first-year dormitory and wander around campus secretly."
"!"

Professor Bronze wasn't the only one teaching good things to the students.


* * *


Professor Bronze noticed Oknodie's remarkable transformation during the vacation.

'Her total power has increased.'

Not just Oknodie.
Everyone who had gone on the "boat trip" with her had made significant progress.
Exotic foods from around the world, systematic practical training facilities, and even one or two treasure-grade (+5 enhancement) spoils.
Their growth was so impressive it seemed like they had raided a dungeon somewhere.

'Then they should be able to keep up even if I make the lecture a bit more difficult?'

A truly unexpected trial for students who hadn't participated in Oknodie's boat trip!
However, this phenomenon wasn't limited to Professor Bronze's lectures.
After class ended, Professor Bronze happened to overhear other professors' conversation while passing by the faculty lounge.

"Haven't the first-years' overall level improved?"
"Not all of them, but within the lower class, those who were already quite skilled have definitely improved."
"The intermediate class was a system introduced from the second year, but the difference is significant enough that we could request early implementation for first-years next year."
"Is it the same in the advanced class?"
"They clearly spent their vacation diligently training, which is satisfying. We must step up our game too. These youngsters trained so hard to learn more—it would hurt our pride as educators if we delivered lazy lectures."

Professors who believed that pushing students to the brink of begging for mercy was the true mark of a good professor!
After eavesdropping on this wicked meeting, Professor Bronze nodded with satisfaction.
Though they showed excessive favoritism toward Imperial students, there was definitely something that connected professors.

"Wouldn't that be especially burdensome for students taking multiple lectures? I heard there are two first-years taking as many as 13 courses."
"Oknodie and Ishtar. The top and second-ranked students in their year."
"Then we should work even harder on lectures they attend. If one side goes easy on them and their grades drop, wouldn't that be unfair?"

The Imperial professors' eyes sparkled.

"Indeed. We can't let Imperial professors lose to frontier professors. I'll increase the midterm exam coverage by 1.2 times."
"Then I'll double the assignments."
"I should make the practical test standards even more severe."

After overhearing the Imperial professors' ambitious declarations, Professor Bronze secretly made her own resolution.
She would explosively increase the difficulty of her own lectures next time.
