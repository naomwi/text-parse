# <414 - The Decisive Clue>

Mandela Castella's laughter kept the monsters roaming the lava zone at bay.

"Mana control that has reached mastery can imbue even ordinary everyday movements with tremendous effect. For me, it's my 'voice,' as you can see. Ahahaha!"
"If only he weren't so full of himself, he'd be a senior I could really respect."

Dorothy pursed her lips.
Just looking at Irene's sullen face walking beside her, she could tell they were thinking the same thing.
Senior Mandela was strong.
He effortlessly eliminated the danger factors they had been most wary of.
He breezed through the lava zone without even using a protective charm.

"At this rate, are we going to reach the end of the path?"
"Your confidence is admirable, but to put it bluntly, that's impossible!"
"Why?"
"Beyond the lava zone is the deep part of the 'Zone Reserved for Leave of Absence Students.' It's the epicenter of great chaos where countless unauthorized challengers have clashed and fallen throughout history!"
"And Oknodie is in such a place..."
"That's why we need to hurry, right? Before our fearless junior recklessly enters a place even I can't reach!"

Dorothy, who had been following behind the senior, hesitated in her steps.
For a brief moment, she thought she saw Zuang stumble.
But the sight of her walking gracefully with her mask firmly in place seemed perfectly fine.

'Was I imagining things?'

Dorothy tilted her head in confusion before turning her attention back to the front.
Unaware that beneath the mask, Zuang's face was contorted with pain from injuries that had not yet healed.

"Senior, is there any way to send news to our classmates on the other side? If we're much closer to Oknodie, they're just wasting their efforts."
"The other second-years apart from me have joined them. Your concern is admirable, but right now you should worry about yourselves rather than your classmates, don't you think?"

The tension that had eased now pulled taut again.
The deep part of the magical disaster.
Beyond the Level 5 lava zone, the Level 8 "Depths of Chaos" appeared before them.


* * *


While Dorothy, Rockfell, Zuang, and Mandela Castella were on the route Irene had taken, everyone else was gathered on the route Gisele had chosen.

"Our objective is neither to ambush seniors who are scrounging for food throughout the magical disaster areas to extort points, nor to acquire lost items located in the center of powerful magical disasters. Remember that our purpose is solely to rescue Oknodie."

Thanks to purchasing reliable information and strategies from the seniors, they were able to advance steadily even after stepping into the "Consecutive Disaster Zone" where ten magical disasters were occurring simultaneously.

"Here are Oknodie's footprints."
"The scent is faint, but it's there. This way for sure."

The problem was that the direction indicated by Mob and Xing, who were tracking Oknodie's traces, differed from the safe path pointed out by the information they had purchased.

"That's the epicenter of disaster phenomena that even the seniors who sold us the information explicitly warned against entering."
"Our fearless little princess would naturally charge into such places without hesitation."
"What are we going to do now? Take the safe route around, or charge into what's obviously dangerous?"

Gisele made her decision.

"We'll take the safe route. However, I'd like the second-year seniors to accompany Xing and Mob."

The follow-up search party that had joined later with Mandela Castella was able to meet in the safe zone thanks to Gisele's active information gathering near the entrance.
The second-years nodded, judging that with Gisele's amount of information, the first-years would be safe enough on their own.

BOOM!

The "Pushing" trap zone where walls moved like cannons, slamming passersby against the opposite wall.
Oknodie had angled these trap mechanisms diagonally and, amazingly, used the "Pushing Trap" like a cannon to pioneer her own creative shortcut.

<Joint Protective Barrier>
<Shock Absorption>
<Collision Resistance>
<Physical Resistance Level 2 Enhancement>

A large sphere reinforced by the combined power of the second-years collided with the ground, creating a thunderous noise.

Crack, thud.

The second-years emerged from the protective barrier that had cracked and burst in an instant, staggering with pale faces.

"What are you dawdling for? Oknodie went that way."
"Ugh. We need to find footprints..."

Xing, who hadn't contributed to creating the protective barrier, was already walking steadily after landing, with Mob following in heavy armor.
The second-years made pitiful faces at the tough forced march that seemed nothing like first-year behavior.

"Why did we agree to come along?"
"I'm dying..."

After leaping over three disasters at once, the next disaster they arrived at in the consecutive disaster zone was a cannon section where victims would be swept away by "Water Cannons" and sent back to the starting point.
As if bad premonitions always come true, where Xing and Mob had tracked the traces, they could see water pillars shooting high into the air.

"If you're done sightseeing, hurry up and cast a protective barrier."
"Is this a junior or a master?"

All the second-years sighed in agreement with the complaint muttered by one of them.


* * *


"They're like flying squirrels. Do they just take to the sky at every opportunity?"

Son O-cheon clicked his tongue at the sight of the shortcut party consisting of Xing, Mob, and the second-years, who had already taken to the air three times to pioneer creative shortcuts.

"It's hardly befitting a young lady's approach. I find it rather reassuring. Don't you feel certain we're on the right path?"
"I'm not sure if that should even be called a path."

Isabel couldn't even pretend to consider taking such a route.
Unless she was planning to host a raining cookware event, showering utensils from the sky, it was an absurd idea to her.
And Gisele, who carried far more items in her magic bag than Isabel did, would find it even more ridiculous.
Isabel couldn't help but smile as she imagined gold coins pouring down like rain.

"Isabel. What's so funny?"
"I was imagining Gisele falling from the sky while gold coins pour out of her like rain."
"Hahaha! That's a masterpiece. I should definitely make her fly next time."
"Don't say such terrible things. I absolutely won't do it."

Unlike the others whose tension had eased, there was one first-year who couldn't smile.
Jigoku, following at the very back with a gloomy face, showed none of his usual cheerfulness.

"What's wrong with that guy?"
"Jigoku needs time. Leave him be."
"Hey, gentleman. Do you have any idea what's going on?"
"Sigh. Please don't make me say it. It would hurt him more if he heard it."

As Gisele said, Jigoku's insides were burning intensely.

'Oknodie. I thought you had come to follow me enough to attend weekend lectures. Were you just using me?'

Creating the Kraken commotion and infiltrating the Zone Reserved for Leave of Absence Students during the lecture.
Oknodie's unhesitating series of actions convinced him that she had attended the lectures solely for this purpose from the beginning.
That made him even angrier.

'What was I to you?'

When he suggested attending lectures, Oknodie had looked at him with a "hmm~" sound as if measuring something.
The time spent attending lectures with the Jigoku Pirates, teaching them knowledge necessary for naval combat and correcting their sloppy postures even while mocking them as "pathetic pirates."
The journey home afterward, dissolving rock-hard biscuits in their saliva while giggling together.

'After spending all that time together, how could you do this and disappear without saying a word to me?'

Jigoku's fist was clenched tighter than ever before. The firmness of this fist stemmed from anger at his own weakness for not being designated as a reliable adult.
This time would be different.
He would definitely find Oknodie and grab her with his own hands to ask.
What was I, what were we, the Jigoku Pirates, to you?

"Ugh, so these monsters appear in places that aren't shortcuts! Everyone, step back. This giant rock monster can't be destroyed with weapons..."

BOOM!

While the advanced class students were struggling and backing away, Jigoku alone charged forward and shattered the rock monster in one blow with bullets enchanted on the spot with <Penetration>, <Crushing>, and <Explosion>.
The students swallowed nervously as Jigoku, without a trace of his usual playfulness, turned to them with a cold expression.

"Don't stop. I don't care what comes out."

That's how he felt.
The feeling of wanting to blow up anything that stood in his way.

"Was Jigoku always this strong?"
"I don't know."

Gisele answered Isabel's question honestly.

"It means no one knows Jigoku's true worth."
"What? I've had strength contests with that ignorant guy many times, so why wouldn't I know? Wasn't he about the same as me?"
"He's different from you, Son O-cheon. Jigoku never once fought with all his might throughout last semester. He always seemed relaxed, uninterested."

When he formed the Jigoku Pirates, it seemed like he might do something, but even that was just playing pirate with bottom-tier students.
This was the first time anyone had seen Jigoku fighting with such an angry, serious expression.

"Damn it. Him too? Are you also going to climb over me and move on?"

Seeing Son O-cheon grinding his teeth, Gisele stopped Isabel who was about to reach out to comfort him.
Seeing him shake his head quietly, Isabel also realized.
In this period when everyone was either growing or revealing their hidden worth.
The frustration of those falling behind could only be assuaged by their own growth.
Where might Oknodie, the instigator of this growth fever, be now and what might she be doing?

'Whatever the case, I'd like to give her about ten swats on the bottom with a switch.'

It wasn't just Son O-cheon who felt powerless and was stimulated to grow—Gisele felt the same way.

"Now they're not even visible anymore."

Xing's group, who had been flying through the air, had completely disappeared beyond detection range after a few more explosions.
Gisele's main group was the furthest behind in the Oknodie search party.
To make matters worse, the sun was setting, making tracking even more difficult.

"We've reached a safe zone. What do you want to do?"
"Circulating mana cultivation techniques is enough to drive away drowsiness. We'll continue pushing forward."

Sleep was a luxury.
If those who fell behind maintained their own pace while taking care of their health, the opportunity to catch up with those ahead would never come.

"Are you sure that's okay? This isn't your style."
"What is my style?"
"Thorough information gathering, weakness analysis, and strategic approach. Right now, all I see in you is impatience."
"Would you say such leisurely things if an Esonia Adventure Guild comrade was in danger ahead?"
"I would. If the main group collapses from overexertion, then even the possibility of rescuing stragglers who are hoping for help somewhere will disappear forever."
"..."

The first-years were all eager to join forces when it came to Oknodie's situation.
Thanks to Isabel's reminder, Gisele regained her composure.

"Thank you. Thanks to you, I almost made an uncharacteristic mistake."
"Don't waver. You're our center. The information you've been collecting for Oknodie, the connections you've been managing—they're all helping us right now."

A smile curved on Gisele's lips at the warmth of relationships she had momentarily forgotten.

"I wonder if she's eating properly. Our high-maintenance young lady."

That sincere concern slipped out unconsciously.
At those words, Isabel's hair stood on end.

"That's it!"
"Pardon?"
"Food. Track the location of ingredients that exist only in this Zone Reserved for Leave of Absence Students."
"!!"
"You know. Oknodie would think about 'efficiency' and collect unfamiliar ingredients wherever she goes."

Not Oknodie herself.
First find the ingredients that Oknodie would chase after.
The beginning of the thread.
The clue to solve the case.
She had realized the decisive clue that would expedite their search.
