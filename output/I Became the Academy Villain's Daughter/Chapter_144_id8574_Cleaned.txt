# Chapter 144 - Candy Pouch

"Why isn't the instructor stepping in!"
"Probably because he's not confident he can subdue him without causing injury."

Dark mana is a destructive force.
Not only is it powerful, but if handled carelessly, Jaku might self-destruct.
Unlike the instructor who hesitated to step forward in this life-threatening crisis, Oknodie took the initiative, attempting the restraint the instructor should have performed.

"It's not too late. Let's pull Oknodie out."
"No. If we interfere now, it will only divide his attention and make things more difficult."
"The gentleman is right. That lower-class kid has been reacting sensitively to surrounding sounds and movements."

In his unconscious state, he's only reacting instinctively to external stimuli.
If they intervened and caused an unexpected reaction, Oknodie, themselves, and the lower-class student would all be in danger.

"Is Oknodie going to be okay? She's using such a dangerous power against him."
"Look at her output."

Gisele pointed to the dark mana forming on Oknodie's sword.
While her initial output was much higher when she first ignited the dark mana to draw attention, now she was merely coating the tip of her sword with a thin layer.

"It seems our little lady can freely control even the output of dark mana, which is extremely difficult to manage. Judging by how she's reduced that troublesome power to just a thin layer on her sword."
"Is that good news?"
"Absolutely good news. It means we don't have to worry about our little lady losing control and going mad like that lower-class student."

Though Jaku appeared to have the upper hand in terms of raw power output, Oknodie's ability to maintain a lower, controlled output was far more impressive.

'I'm more worried about what comes after this, though.'

Gisele was concerned.
This incident would be the final blow to Oknodie's already poor reputation.
All because of that student.
Jaku, an aspiring knight.
Gisele's eyes grew colder than the winter wind as he looked at him.

* * *

Mob had grown so strong.
It was his friend's growth.
He should naturally feel happy about it.
Yet he wasn't happy.
He couldn't be happy.
Because he couldn't stand alongside Mob in his success.
He knew it himself.
What qualities were needed for this test.
The Mana Verification Test.
A test that questioned one's ability to handle mana.

-If you cannot handle mana, your blade will feed on the blood of people, not monsters.
-Eventually, you too will become a monster. Just a monster wearing human skin.
-Mercenaries who cannot handle mana are such creatures.

His master had told him.
Don't become like me, he said.
Rather than that, risk your life, he said.

His life hung on the vial in his pocket.
A small amount of medicine, not even a full cup of water.

Drink it and die.
Eight times out of ten, you die.
Even if you survive, you can never live the same life again.
Side effects are almost guaranteed.
It depends on miraculous odds.
Yet his master had given it to him.
Saying that taking this medicine was better than not taking it.
Telling him that the remaining years of living as the organization's dog would be that harsh.
That dying would be more merciful than starting the "monster's life" he often spoke of.
Nevertheless, he had made up his mind.

'If Mob leaves the Academy with me, then I can gladly accept my fate.'

He could have dreamed of turning his life around by entering the world's best academy with his friend, only to fail.
They entered together and left together.
Those times were good.
He might have been able to live with those memories for the rest of his life.
But Mob passed.
He was now out of the failing zone.
If he fails here, he'll be alone.
Living a dark life in society's shadows that others can't even glimpse.

'With 10 years of training, I finally stood beside you. I thought so, at least for a moment. But if you move ahead of me again here, if I'm the only one who goes back...'

How many more years would he need then?
20 years? 30 years?
Perhaps even if he spent the rest of his life trying, he might never catch up to Mob again.
But he didn't want to hold Mob back.
He had plenty of opportunities.
He could have tampered with their shared meals.
Or caused accidents during training.
All sorts of dirty methods came to mind, ways to bring Mob down to his level.
Yet he didn't do any of them.

'Mob. We overcame our hellish childhood together. We promised to become knights who would save people like us someday.'

How could he betray someone like that?
So he decided.
To betray himself instead of Mob.

Gulp.

He emptied the vial and got in line.
The instructor saw him drink the potion but didn't stop him.
Potion doping.
Even that was considered part of a student's skill at the Academy.
After all, if you can't maintain the same level of doping consistently, if you can't overcome the aftereffects of doping, you'll eventually be weeded out.
That's actually true.
It's questionable whether he'll even survive this test, and even if he does, how many more times he can endure.
This doping is meaningless.
It's just a momentary extension of life.
Yet the reason he didn't back down.
The reason he stepped forward, even betraying his dream of becoming an honorable knight.
Was because he wanted to show.
That he could stand beside his friend.
That things were different from 10 years ago.
That the past 10 years weren't wasted.

'I'll prove it. My 10 years!'

His heart sank and his senses distorted.
Dark mana rising from the tip of his sword.
The pitch-black sword energy flickered like flames, rising violently.

Kwang!

It created cracks in the rock.
Stone fragments scattered.
There was no time to feel the thrill of this destructive power, greater than usual.

-Remember. If you've taken the medicine, you must stop releasing energy when you feel your heart tightening.
-Dark mana is a power that doesn't yield to its user. The moment you lose control and exceed your control limit, I can't guarantee your safety.

He couldn't stop.
If he failed the test even after taking the medicine, he would eventually collapse from a dark mana-induced seizure anyway.
In a position where he couldn't even guarantee whether he would open his eyes again afterward.

'Master. When I decided to take the medicine, I already gave up on safety. All I can do is run to the end.'

He needed more power.
To narrow the gap of talent.
To disguise the meagerness of effort.
His heart tightening with each moment.
His consciousness fading every second.
He had to hold on a little longer, to swing this sword again and again.

Kwaaang!

It shattered.
He succeeded in a test he should never have passed.
He vaguely heard his classmates looking up to him, everyone's excited shouts, Mob clapping and saying how amazing he was.
The distance between him and the world felt somehow far away, like looking at the world beyond while submerged in water.

'I see.'

He had already exceeded his control limit.
In exchange for this brief achievement, he had lost his entire life.
His master had always regretted not achieving that one moment of success.
What about him?
Was he satisfied with not becoming like his master?
Did he regret giving up his life?
Before Jaku could reach a conclusion, his vision closed and his consciousness evaporated.

* * *

Just by looking at the dark mana output and his intact body, I could tell.
This friend has lived a hard life!

NPCs with dark mana typically harbor much darker vengeance than Isabel's exploration team revenge motivation.
At this level, he must have grown up witnessing the deaths of at least ten thousand people.

A character with an unusual past and encounters.
He probably has too big a backstory to remain just an extra.
If handled well, he might become a named villain rather than a minor role.
But he drank the dark potion that rivals the light potion, so now he's doomed to self-destruct, unable to control his overflowing dark mana.

"Running into me, we're both lucky!"

Until you met me, that is.
Actually, dark mana has an interesting gimmick.
The one with the smaller absolute value (purity multiplied by quantity) of dark mana is strongly influenced by the one with the larger value, consciously or unconsciously.
It's not for nothing that magical beasts are controlled by magical races, or that mages die for magical races.
It's the same principle by which demon lords control demons.
When the absolute value of dark mana becomes overwhelmingly high, lower beings show absolute obedience and feel infinite loyalty, admiration, and even love.
How these feelings are made to manifest determines the individual's identity and personality.

'What identity should I choose for this round?'

Jaku flinched and looked nervous.
I pondered while watching him.
As an experienced player, I can do whatever I want.
Meaning I can act out any personality I choose!
But just because I can do anything doesn't mean a 230cm muscular giant should go "Uwung?" while twisting their body—that would be the wrong concept.
There's a saying in the world: TPO.
T: Time.
P: Place.
O: Occasion.
It means to act according to the time, place, and situation.
TPO is important for concepts too.

A 133cm, 11-year-old girl.
First-year co-valedictorian.
Why did I immediately adopt the speech and behavior fitting a child when possessing this cute little girl character?
Because I've been following this TPO.
The dark mana gimmick concept shouldn't deviate from this either.

'Cute would be best!'

Suddenly bulging with muscles and beating him with superhuman strength or making everyone submit with tremendous charisma wouldn't fit the concept.
So I deliberately lowered my energy to show Jaku an opening, and as soon as he lunged, I corrected my seemingly careless stance and raised my sword.

"?!"

Realizing he had fallen for a strong opponent's trick, Jaku tried to stop his attack following dark mana instinct, but I stepped forward faster than he could retreat.

"Hya!"

Instead of cutting Jaku with my sword, I struck upward at the sword he was holding, momentarily increasing my dark mana output.
With a clang, his sword flew away spinning like a baseball, embedding itself in the far side of the testing ground.

"You shouldn't do bad things. I'll punish you like this!"

As he backed away with his blackened eye whites showing his bewilderment, I pinned his foot to the ground with my sword to prevent him from escaping.
A brief stiffening from the pain in his body.
Not missing that opening, I leaped up and landed gracefully on the sword handle with one foot.

Flutter.

My uniform skirt billowed up to my thighs like a flower before settling down.
When our eyes met, Jaku trembled in fear, unable to move.
Are you scared?
But it's too late!
I raised one hand to his forehead.

"Like this!"

A flick strengthened by tremendous muscle power plus the enhancing effect of dark mana struck his forehead.

Bwak!

With an incredibly painful sound, Jaku's body bent backward.
Thud.
The strong impact that shook his brain made him unable to rise even with dark mana, and Jaku passed out.

"No, if you knock out someone handling dark mana, the energy will go out of control even more...!"
"Ah, that's fine."

I stuck my finger deep into the mouth of the collapsed Jaku.

<Bloody Maria's Mana Training Method>
<Absorption - Maria's Elegant Meal>

Jaku's dark mana was drawn out, attracted to my stronger dark mana energy.
With so much ominous black energy gathering at my fingertips, I wanted to store it in some other form, and fortunately, I spotted something useful.
The candy pouch at my waist.

'If I'm going to eat it anyway, it might as well look tasty!'

The dark mana swirled and condensed into a black sphere.

"If you make it into a snack like this, it works!"

I put the dark mana sphere into the candy pouch and tightened the string to close it.
The instructor's eyes widened to twice their size.

"That pouch is..."
"It's a candy pouch!"
"Ah, if you store dark mana carelessly..."
"It's fine! It's MY candy pouch!"

Instead of being grateful for preventing a disaster, the instructor's eyes only grew more fearful.
I was startled to see him swallowing as he looked at my candy pouch, and I covered it with both hands.

"I won't eat it!"
"You just swallowed while looking at my pouch!"
"I didn't swallow because I wanted to eat it!"
