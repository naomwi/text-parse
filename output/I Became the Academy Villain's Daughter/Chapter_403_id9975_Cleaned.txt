# 403 - Impending Clash

Seven great pirates currently exist on the seas.
And one of them was the man known as "El Draco," the professor in charge of the "Naval Combat I" lecture.

"The Jigoku Pirate Crew, huh? I thought they were just playing children's games... but they're actually quite good."

El Draco had kidnapped a fairly powerful pirate crew, taking into account the excellence of the 981st class.
The great pirate Jormungand, stationed along the trade route connecting the Southern City-State Alliance and the Western Kingdom of Florence.
The pirate crew he had kidnapped was the One-Eyed Pirate Crew of Morso, the left arm of Jormungand and a subordinate pirate crew that had captured over a hundred merchant ships and grown in power.

"If I'd known it would be like this, I should have kidnapped the right arm instead."

The true value of Morso's pirate crew lay in the magic eye implanted after removing one of their eyes.
They were beings who could read wind directions, detect mana flow, or calculate firing distances just by "seeing," without a magician's excellent calculation abilities.
The Morso Pirate Crew was composed entirely of members of this special class, commonly called "Magic Eye Bearers."

"Firing at a speed even faster than reading with the eyes. This level of skill is impossible without experience of firing cannons like water."
"We should give them bonus points."

El Draco's adjutant and dedicated instructor quickly recorded the scores on a mana panel.
The adjutant observed every move of all students throughout the ship, maintaining the special sense that even Magic Eye Bearers couldn't sustain for long periods due to rapid eye fatigue, as naturally as breathing.
His competence also proved the strength of El Draco, who had such a talented person as his adjutant.

"A great subordinate raises the status of their captain. It's rare for subordinates to bow their heads to someone inferior to themselves."
"Are you asking me to flatter you during the exam, Captain? You really don't care what others think."
"You're one to talk! With your insane audacity to bluntly refuse when a notorious great pirate demands flattery."
"Hmph. Even such audacity means nothing in the face of overwhelming force. That Oknodie first-year. What's the spec of the instructors that little monster defeated?"
"They're skilled enough to be considered 'Advanced Combat Personnel' even on the Captain's ship. By the standards of the Morso Pirate Crew we just captured, they're at the level of deck officers and helmsmen."

Talents who could go out into the world right now with such skills had fallen at the hands of a first-year student.
And that wasn't even the limit of Oknodie's abilities.

"The Wiheomhae Foundation is truly remarkable. To secretly create such a secret weapon."
"Her eyes are completely different from those of ordinary students. Who would have thought she'd stage a shipboard rebellion, seize the position of an officer, and aim to acquire massive bonuses by completing 'challenge tasks'?"
"Still, her thinking was shallow. No matter how excellent the Jigoku Pirate Crew is, she rushed too much. Now she'll have to pay the price."
"Is it time for the fearless stowaways to make their move?"
"Yes. As an adjutant who has long since claimed the ship as your 'territory,' you already know."

The adjutant nodded.

"I've identified all the upper-class students who stowed away with malicious intent toward Oknodie."

Captain El Draco, your territory is so vast it could cover an entire fleet, not just one ship.
And the fact that there's one upper-class student you're watching as carefully as Oknodie.

* * *

"Cease all hostile actions against Oknodie."
Mandela Castella had made such a declaration early on.
Unity and solidarity.
The second-year students, tightly united under Mandela's charisma, carried out her instructions thoroughly.
That is, until it led to the complete disappearance of all the medicinal herbs they were growing during the "Let's Try Growing Medicinal Herbs" lecture.

"Ahahahaha!"
"Ugh, what an incredible volume that hurts my ears."
"Is she starting again?"
"How many times has Mandela laughed now?"
"It's a new record. She's exceeded 7 consecutive times in one spot."

Fortunately for the students attending Professor Red Mountain's lecture, they avoided failing as a group thanks to Mandela officially raising an objection to the student council.
However, that didn't erase the resentment of having been one-sidedly beaten by Oknodie.

"Mandela must be furious too."
"She's probably trying to calm her anger with laughter."
"Me? This Mandela Castella? Angry? Not at all! Rather, I'm excited!"
"Gah!"
"W-when did you get here?!"

Mandela Castella, who had been standing on the windowsill laughing like a madwoman just moments ago, poked her head out beside the students whispering in the corridor.
Of course, her high self-esteem peaks preceded her head, but no second-year student dared to give a stern look to the strongest girl in their year.

"I requested a ceasefire for the sake of our cute junior, but after being so openly beaten, I can't just let it end like this!"
"Are you finally getting revenge?"
"Grr, I believed in you, Mandela!"
"But how are you planning to get revenge?"

They wanted revenge but weren't confident.
That was the honest feeling of the second-year students.

"That kid was incredibly strong in the inter-grade competition at the sports festival. Of course, Mandela wasn't going all out either, but even the Four Heavenly Kings of our year were overwhelmed."
"The justification and opportunity are all on our side! Disciplining a junior who stole a senior's assignment. This falls within the range of private sanctions permitted by the student council!"
"Oh, is that true?"
"Moreover, there are seniors other than us who have been robbed by Oknodie!"
"Seniors other than us means... third-years?!"

She did it without fear.
Rarely does that phrase fit a situation so perfectly.

"Yes... we are the seniors who had our summons stolen by that fearless little brat."
"Gasp! That badge on their clothes is exclusive to third-years!"
"Wait a minute. That department is... the Production Department?!"

The Production Department, not the Knight Department or the Magic Department.
The second-year students' disappointment was immense.
Without seniors from combat-related classes, there was no way a fight against the powerful Oknodie could be established.

"I understand your disappointment. You might think we're weak because we're not from the Knight Department or Adventure Department. But that's only when the Production Department isn't prepared to fight."

The third-year students whose contracts with the hard-to-control "Gigatron" and various advanced summons had been forcibly severed.
Moreover, now that even the "Baby Kraken" kept in isolation had been stolen, the third-years' anger had reached its peak.

"Have you ever wondered how the weak Production Department continues to exist between the Knight Department that always tries to take our achievements by force and the Magic Department that tries to steal them with tricks?"

The second-year students were honestly curious.
If there was no way to resist, wasn't the Production Department just... a weak department where students with low grades reluctantly entered to at least get a diploma, suffering bullying from other departments?
However, there was no trend among upper-class students to look down on the Production Department.

"This is the secret."

When the Production Department seniors lifted their cloaks and coats, a dazzling light burst forth.

"Gasp!"
"W-what an incredible magical light."
"What are these absurd magical reactions?!"

A Production Department senior held one of the "numerous magical tools" and said:

"These are the 'Universal Artifact' series possessed by all third-year students in our department."
"All those magical tools are universal?!"
"The weak unite to survive. Each student in our small groups possesses one magical tool developed in their respective fields."

They don't have outstanding swordsmanship or magical talent.
That's why they dedicated their lives to production.
By fairly sharing their achievements within groups, they secured not just one, but dozens of magical tools.

"Even if you did that, your internal mana couldn't withstand it?!"
"Why do you think we couldn't withstand it?"
"There's a clear limit to how much mana a person can increase through mana cultivation methods! Especially for the Production Department with inferior talent..."
"We're certainly behind the Knight Department and Magic Department in mana accumulation speed."

However, that doesn't mean the Production Department is weak.

"Instead, we possess artifacts forged with outstanding talent. And by selling them, we can obtain an enormous amount of funds and points."
"Don't tell me with that wealth...!"
"Yes. We take 'Elixirs' and forcibly increase our mana capacity. Until we can possess multiple magical tools."
"!!"
"Students who fail to secure enough wealth to produce magical tools cannot survive as third-years. Understand? We've accomplished that."

As an enormous amount of mana surged from the Production Department seniors, even more impressive than their vast array of magical tools, the second-year students could barely breathe from the pressure.

"Ohohoho~! With seniors as skilled as you, we second-years feel very reassured!"

Only one person's laughter, that of the top student Mandela Castella, completely pushed back such an atmosphere.
It was evidence that the depth of her mana was not at all inferior to the third-year elixir monsters, and even overwhelmed them.

"Now then, seniors. Are you all available this weekend morning?"
"Don't tell me... you're targeting the Naval Combat lecture time? Even upper-class students would..."
"There's a bypass clause. If by chance a special supplementary lecture happens to overlap with a lower-grade lecture location, the professor takes responsibility for accidents caused by student collisions. The distribution of responsibility costs is determined by agreement between the professors involved."
"!"
"As it happens, Professor Red Mountain's 'Let's Try Growing Medicinal Herbs' lecture has a special supplementary lecture called 'Capturing Aquatic Medicinal Herbs' at 9 AM on Saturday."

Professor Red Mountain had made the decision. To confront Professor El Draco and crush Oknodie.

"Ohohoho~! Oknodie, first-year. This Mandela Castella will personally test how much you've grown in the second semester!"
"By the way, it's confirmed that an advanced class student called Jigoku is also taking the same lecture... what kind of person is this one?"
"Jigoku? Ah. You mean the red-haired pirate with a pirate hat and coat who claims to be male but wraps bandages around their chest like a cross-dressing woman? There's nothing particularly noteworthy about them."

That's extremely distinctive!?
Unlike the bewildered second-year students, Mandela's assessment was cold.

"They upgrade expensive bullets to even more expensive attribute-enchanted magic bullets and fire them recklessly, causing opponents to collapse in one hit if they don't deploy appropriate defensive magic. They're also somewhat skilled in close combat, able to cut down about twenty lower-class students like autumn leaves, but besides that, they're nothing. Don't worry about it!"

...That sounds extremely strong and concerning!?
The second-year students clearly remembered Jigoku's name.

"Then what do you think would happen if that Jigoku fought with Oknodie?"

The second-year student asked, expecting that based on the assessment so far, Mandela would predict Oknodie's overwhelming victory.
However, Mandela's evaluation once again greatly surprised the second-year students.

"I'm not sure. Unless something unusual happens, wouldn't Jigoku win?"
"What?! After that assessment, why would Jigoku win?"
"That's obvious. Jigoku is a first-year who knows Oknodie's weakness. If they're close enough to Oknodie to attend weekend lectures together, they must know it. I noticed it right away after starting my investigation."

Mandela held a strong conviction.

"That child is weak against feminine wiles."
"Feminine wiles?!"
"Moreover, the impact of feminine wiles when a cross-dressing woman reveals her true identity is at least three times greater!"
"Ooh!"
"So Oknodie can never win if Jigoku deliberately employs feminine wiles."

If her identity had been discovered, that would be a different story, but the possibility of penetrating Jigoku's disguise with chest bandages enchanted with body-correction and perception-interference magic was slim.
It was Mandela's miscalculation, as she naturally couldn't have thought that Jigoku's identity had been exposed from the beginning.
