# 389 - Cruel Game

"This is wrong. Why would humanity's heroes sacrifice their lives for each other...!"

The moment Dobi raised his voice, everyone in the gathering turned their eyes toward him.
From that pressure alone, Dobi felt the mana field surrounding him crumple pathetically.
The emotions etched on everyone's faces were indignation and resentment, along with even greater irritation and anger.

"So. What difference does it make if we don't self-destruct?"
"The value of life is—"
"Those precious lives died like insects and became that absurdly huge moon. The stars turned into lands of blood and death. What difference does it make if only we survive?"
"Ugh..."
"I don't even know how someone as weak as you survived in the first place, or why we're wasting precious mana on someone like you. Wouldn't it be better for humanity if you became bait instead?"

The sharp criticism contained no pride as humanity's final suicide squad, no heroism.
They were just ordinary people carrying out a final charge, filled with resentment.
Even if they had more talent and were more exceptional than others, there was no use for such extraordinariness after civilization had collapsed, with no opportunity left to show it off.
It was an era where everyone had lost the meaning of life and was looking for a place to die.
Such an era had arrived.

"..."

Dobi couldn't dare judge that they were wrong.
He couldn't criticize the methods of their leader, a giant standing 2m30cm tall.
Because there were no other alternatives.
That was the apocalypse.
That's why this was also strange.
The squad member's point was correct.
His life was useless.
Why would the squad leader, who disregarded human life and consumed it like resources for the final charge, share precious resources with a weak inferior like himself?

"Why me?"

After climbing to the top of the mountain.
Only two people remained in the suicide squad.
The strongest leader.
The weakest Dobi.
Perhaps that's not quite right.
Someone who couldn't even help defeat a single Bloody Spawn had neither the specs nor qualifications to be recognized as a member of the suicide squad.
Though the giant could have scolded Dobi for his impertinent question, he answered without even turning his gaze.

"New quest."
"?"
"Because a new plot point has appeared."

The man said indifferently.

"First-year Academy uniform. Poor abilities. Presumed capable of time travel if the trigger is discovered. A player wouldn't pass up such a strange fellow."
"Player...?"
"It means someone who fully enjoys the game of this world."

Dobi's hair stood on end.
A game?
That's something only dragons enjoy.
Does that mean this man isn't human but a dragon?

'That actually makes sense.'

Some things were finally becoming clear.
A physique too large for a human.
An unusual aura.
An attitude that treats human lives as consumables.
If he truly were a dragon who treats humans as inferior beings, all the strangeness would fit together like puzzle pieces.

"Did it have to be this way?"

That's why he felt even more wronged.
If he were a dragon, he'd be the strongest living being on earth.
Wouldn't his range of choices be much wider?
Why would such a powerful being...
It's not like he lacks ability.
Why would he devise such a cruel strategy that presupposes the sacrifice of those who joined his game?

"I don't lack ability. But I have no lingering attachments to a world where the happy ending is already over. So I'm maximizing the damage coefficient."
"Damage coefficient...?"
"It's the power of a convenient function correction that increases damage through the power of friendship and anger each time a strong companion dies. Once you stack enough, it's a very useful function that can overwhelm boss patterns."
"So it really wasn't because you lacked ability?"
"I could do anything if I wanted to. But what's the point of a character that can't even maintain its concept if it's not a one-hit character?"

[One-Hit Kill Strength All-In One-Shot Character Lover Marine]

When he revealed his own will, his identity was finally unveiled.
A name so carelessly given that it could never be considered a human name, like a dragon's prank.

'It's a lie. Even if it's not a lie, it should be.'

Such a careless being could never have had any affection for this world from the beginning.
The world's fate is already doomed when it hangs on such a being.

"I can't accept you."
"If you can't accept it. What will you do?"
"I'll repeat. And I'll find something that will change your mind."

Dobi willingly deactivated his mana field.
The man didn't stop him from meeting his miserable end as his boiling blood vaporized and escaped from his body.


* * *


"You want me to tell you about the moment I regret most in my life before I die anyway?"

Northern Archduchess Irene shared freely, saying there were no secrets worth keeping when both the world and her life were ending.

"I regret killing someone with ice magic. The root cause was gathering more mana than my master had set as the limit, determined to defeat powerful demons with strong magic. Mana that overflowed unconsciously manifested as cold magic and froze to death a soldier approaching me."

Imperial Emperor Yayoi recalled painful memories of the past with a somber face.

"Though I can't call them close family, the deaths of competitors vying for succession rights left me with great sorrow. I didn't hate them but rather respected them. The moment I regret most is turning away from deaths I could have prevented."

Supersonic Rockfell looked down at the headband tied to his scabbard with cold, sunken eyes.

"Who are you to pry into others' pasts?"
"No, I mean, we're about to die anyway, so I could be generous and tell you..."

The world split apart.
By the time he realized his neck had been cut by a sword draw that didn't even make a sound, his body had already collapsed.


* * *


He finally found a way to persuade Rockfell.
It was to ask about the owner of the headband.

"Dorothy. It must be that I couldn't protect her. I honed my martial skills to protect her, but she died somewhere beyond my sight."

Thousand-Sound Mandela answered with a noble, bold laugh.

"Ohohohoho! Regret is something felt by failing students who didn't live yesterday to the fullest. It's an emotion that cannot exist for a true noble!"

After hearing the regrets of other suicide squad members, Dobi realized his preparations were complete.

"Why are you digging up old pasts?"
"Just trying to imitate a time traveler."
"What? Time traveler, what nonsense...!?"

Dobi willingly deactivated his mana barrier.
This time too, his body quickly mummified and crumbled.


* * *


"I am a time traveler sent by you from the past. As proof, I know the moments you regret most. First, Irene, you regret the death of a soldier due to forced magic manifestation from excessively accumulated mana."
"!?"
"Imperial Emperor Yayoi, you mourned the deaths of the crown prince and the second princess."
"How do you know my true feelings that I've never told anyone... Did my past self really send you...?"
"Then tell me about my past."
"Rockfell. Uh, that is... what was it again?"

Dobi, having heard too many pasts, momentarily confused the histories and cut off his words.
Rockfell swung his sword without hesitation.

"I cannot let a suspicious person live."
"I really just forgot..."

His eyes closed with one resentful utterance.


* * *


"Rockfell, you regret not being able to protect your lover Dorothy, whom you left in your hometown forest, during her crisis."
"It's true. I'll believe you."

After repeating time over and over, enduring mistakes and forgetfulness, Dobi gained persuasive power over everyone.
The first thing he did was convince everyone to refuse the captain's orders.

"The Marine Captain's plan is wrong. No matter how bleak the world is, throwing away your lives like straw isn't the only solution."
"So what do you suggest? To destroy the Bloody Super Moon, we need to climb Mt. Shinjeong, and to climb the mountain, we need to eliminate the super-sized Bloody Spawn through self-destruction."
"It's possible if everyone works together. We don't have to defeat it in one shot; we can share and distribute the risk among ourselves."

The dragon's plaything, abbreviated as "Strength Marine," strangely emphasized one-hit kills in all battles.
The suicide squad members he brought also had builds specialized for single strikes.
It took several observations to realize that what seemed efficient at first glance was actually inefficient.

'If we don't force it in one hit, but push two or three times, working together to fill in gaps in breathing and timing with coordinated attacks, we can advance much more smoothly.'

It was a basic realization befitting a lower-class student who needed to be clever because he lacked strength.
Those overflowing with talent didn't need such cleverness and hadn't consciously reached this realization.

"That's problematic."

Strength Marine glared at Dobi with fierce eyes.

"The concept must be maintained. No exceptions."
"Is your game that important?!"
"I see. You might be a time traveler from DLC content sent by everyone else's request from the future, but you didn't come at my request. Well, as an NPC, you couldn't help it."

He probably had some trivial reason like setting certain restrictions while enjoying his game.
Dobi's guess was half right.

"Everyone here has set restrictions. They vowed to defeat every enemy in one strike to gain power. The moment they fail to keep that vow, they permanently lose half their abilities."
"!?"

There were restrictions.
But not for trivial game purposes.

"That's why when a single strike isn't allowed, they must self-destruct against strong enemies. Power that would be reduced by half anyway might as well be used to exert maximum firepower for maximum value."

It was because of a cruel game that forced them to place inescapable restrictions from the beginning.
