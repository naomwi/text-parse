# 169 - How Could Such a Terrible Thing Happen

"I solved it!"

I proudly showed off the ring.
Perhaps this alone doesn't convey much.
Xing urged me with a frown on his swollen face.

"Show me."
"Not here. There are too many eyes watching, it's embarrassing..."
"You all. I'm borrowing Oknodie."

Just so you know.
Xing turned his back after making this one-sided announcement.
That certainly couldn't be called a conversation.
It was Xing's typical communication style, having received only announcements from his family his entire life.
Titosogar just crouched down nervously, but Hestia, who had learned the mercenary way of cracking heads when someone was rude, immediately placed her hand on her axe.

"Who do you think you are? What are you planning to do with Oknodie? That child already has a complicated and difficult life without you. Don't even dream of doing anything foolish."
"Who said I'm doing anything foolish?"
"Why are you trying to take a child who's embarrassed by people watching to a place where no one is watching? Hurry up and confess your filthy intentions!"

The atmosphere suddenly turned hostile, which was bewildering.

"Everyone, please don't fight because of me!"
"Who said we're fighting because of you!"
"It must be because of your filthy sexual desires toward a child!"
"You... do you want to die?"
"Stop it, Hestia. Why do you keep provoking Xing with such nasty words? You shouldn't bully someone who's already weak and walking around with a face swollen from being stung by a bee somewhere!"
"I wasn't stung! I was cursed!"

Unable to contain his anger any longer, Xing finally drew his sword.
Not to be outdone, Hestia raised her axe.
On one side, a murderous swordsman.
On the other, a chapter boss.
A crazed swordsman versus a frenzied butcher.
If they fought, it wouldn't end prettily.
At minimum, an arm would be lost; at maximum, a head might roll in this incredible matchup!
The crisis that seemed destined to escalate dramatically ended in an unexpected way.

*Swoosh*

The ghost from the ring, fake Lin.
She became clearly visible to everyone.

"A-a ghost?!"
"Lin. Go back inside."

Because it's dangerous here.
That's what Xing meant, but Lin grabbed onto Xing's pant leg and clung to him.
As if telling them to stop this fighting.
Hestia awkwardly lowered her axe.
With the fake sister clinging to his leg,
Xing, finding it difficult to maintain his hostility, reluctantly sheathed his sword.

"Oknodie. Can you explain? What's with this ghost?"
"Ah, well, actually..."
"Stop."

Xing warned in a low voice.

"Don't involve outsiders. I trust you, but I cannot trust others."

Xing was scarier now than when he was barking angrily just moments ago.
Even with animals, a dog that stares silently is more frightening than one that barks.
He wasn't warning me not to make him draw his sword; he was sharpening his blade, ready to kill at the slightest crossing of the line.
Xing, prepared for murderous destruction.
This couldn't be stopped or prevented.

'Ugh. It's really stinging.'

I can feel it.
Xing's "murderous impulse precursor" is warning me with much greater intensity than before.
Ghosts are originally monsters.
Beings that harm humans.
I may have kept the secret, but others might insist that the ghost must be exorcised for my own sake, claiming one cannot keep a ghost nearby.
If that happens, fake Lin might be exorcised.
If that occurs, the Chapter 1 boss would no longer be Frenzied Hestia but Frenzied Xing.

'He's several times scarier!'

Hestia isn't that frightening unless her berserker switch is flipped, but Xing is scary by default.
What if an already frightening person completely loses it and starts rampaging like a crazed berserker?
Or worse, what if he starts a cold, calculated, mechanical slaughter?

'The Academy would be in chaos!'

From the moment one student dies, the Academy's atmosphere begins to deteriorate into complete desolation.
External interference intensifies, and evil organizations start to flourish, exploiting the vulnerabilities.
Professors become more likely to do strange things to strengthen students, and the principal might grow weary of running the Academy.
Ultimately, if the principal leaves the Academy...
The acting principal would run the Academy, and we'd face even more intense external interference and a changed internal atmosphere to counter it.

'This is definitely a trigger for unfair treatment!!'

I absolutely must not speak.
Fake Lin and Xing's relationship must remain secret.
The fact that fake Lin might be a dangerous ghost who could harm people must not be revealed.
I need to make an excuse.
An excuse everyone can accept.

"Oknodie. Could it be... that?"
"Huh? What?"

But I didn't expect this.

"You're carrying around your friend's soul, right?"
"What??"
"You couldn't bear to see your friend's body put into a plant by the Foundation, so you extracted at least their soul to free them from suffering, right?"
"Plant? Gasp. Did you come into my room?"
"...Recently. I went to give you something at night, saw your door open, and found out."

The fact that Hestia saw the baby mandrake.
And even made a huge misunderstanding because of it.

"Xing found out too, right? So this pedophile is trying to make you do embarrassing things in exchange for keeping your secret?"

How should I put this?
If we go with Hestia's misunderstanding, the relationship between Xing and fake Lin could be perfectly concealed.
But wouldn't that make Xing's treatment too pitiful?
Xing seemed to be caught in the same dilemma, flinching slightly.
A brief but intense agony flashed in his eyes when our gazes met, quickly replaced by resignation.

"That's right."

Not even a real sister, but a fake one.
Xing willingly accepted the stigma of being labeled a pedophile to protect fake Lin who was pretending to be his sister.
I almost burst into tears at this man's touching self-sacrifice.
Xing, you bastard...
I thought you were just an unpleasant murderer character, but you really do care about your sister!

"Don't cry, Oknodie. It's okay now. We're here for you."
"Oknodie, this is my handkerchief. Wipe your tears with this."
"Thank you, Titosogar... sniff!"
"No, no... not your nose, your tears... heeeng."
"Oknodie. We can't take down that man, right? If we did, your ghost friend's identity would be reported to the Academy, and based on reports from other students or professors, they would be hunted down."
"Y-yes...?"

Fake Lin seemed to realize just then that she was in mortal danger.
As she clung even more tightly to Xing's leg, Hestia's face contorted with rage into an expression so terrifying that even evil spirits would flee in terror.

"Xing, you bastard. Even when Oknodie's friend is clinging and begging, you're still trying to satisfy your sexual desires by exploiting friendship...! Unforgivable. You're not even human!"
"...Shut up. If you upset me, this child's life is forfeit. It would be best not to provoke me."
"You're the one who should be careful. Oknodie might have played along with your wishes, but I'm different."

Veins bulged on Hestia's forearm.

"For Oknodie's sake, I might even risk having the ghost friend exorcised to report your criminal activities."

Thanks to Xing's quick wit in playing along with Hestia's misunderstanding, both Hestia and Titosogar now believed their misconception was reality.
My mistake was not realizing that Hestia—who has no friends, treats monsters as post-meal playthings, despises the weak, and feels comfortable around the strong—suffers from serious delusions.

"It would be best to take good care of that little one. If you don't want anything to regret."
