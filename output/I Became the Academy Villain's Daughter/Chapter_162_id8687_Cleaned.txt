# 162 - A Monster Who Doesn't Understand Human Hearts

I trap Lin in a ring.
I put a collar on her so she can't escape and bind her with chains.
Statements that show no respect toward humans.
An attitude like treating a possession.
All of it was no different from the enemies of the Eastern Empire whom Xing hated so much.

"You've crossed the line."

I tried my best to be patient.
I believed this child could become an ally to help with my revenge.
And this is the result.
East and West.
Though their spheres of activity differ, in the end, they're all places where people live.
The bare face of humanity is all the same.

"Why are you drawing your sword?"
"You. You disgust me."

An expression of extreme shock.
Even that clear display of emotion is uncomfortable.
It's even detestable when I think about what lies behind word choices that mock human feelings.

"Did you approach me from the beginning just to toy with me?"
"What did I do wrong? Having a form with arms and legs would only get me exorcised, so isn't a portable ring better when you can still be together with just the soul, even without limbs?"
"Now I understand."
"Phew. Do you finally get it?"
"You're a monster who doesn't understand human hearts."

The discomfort arising from words that at a glance seem to come from a child my age, yet somehow don't.

"This unpleasant conversation stems from the discomfort created when you pretend to know things you don't."
"What?? Me?? That's not true. I know extremely well. At the Academy, it's harder to find things I don't know..."
"The world cannot be defined by knowledge alone."

Far from showing remorse, Oknodie shamelessly makes a tearful face as if to say, "How could you say such harsh things to me?"
Xing pointed his blade at this not-quite-human who hid a terrifying monster's nature behind a cute face.

Flash.

Along with the sword flashing in the sunlight, Xing's killing intent explosively erupted.

"Monster who doesn't understand human hearts. Let me teach you one emotion. The emotion called rage."


* * *


It wasn't difficult to detect <Xing's Murderous Impulse Warning Signs>.
I got goosebumps and had a feeling that if I didn't respond, my hand would be cut off.

Clang!

I blocked it.
Without even time to feel relieved after one defense, a barrage of attacks came at a frightening speed that would slice off my fingers and sever my wrist if I failed to counter.
Xing's sword strikes conveyed strong intent in their trajectory, intensity, and his every movement, including his cold and deadly gaze.
This was a sincere cutting meant to efficiently incapacitate an opponent and behead them.
I felt disappointed.
After all, I had offered to help him.
I was the one who extended goodwill first.

"How can you misunderstand my feelings like this?!"

The tip of Xing's sword wavered.
That moment of hesitation, ridiculously, only added more killing intent to his blade.

"To say such things even knowing the circumstances of Lin and me. Are you determined to mock us siblings to the end!"

I had long surpassed the level of a first-year.
If my spec training had been insufficient, I would have lost my grip on my sword without time to react, and my fingers, wrist, and neck would have gone flying one after another.

"Xiiiing! What do you know! Ghosts are naturally good at running away!"

Ghosts are beings that can break free from artifacts where they're neatly stored, escape beyond effective binding distance on their own, and have no right to complain if they get exterminated at will.
If he had directly captured his sister's soul, it might at least stay close, but this is just a fake that stole Xing's memories and only mimics Lin's appearance.
Moreover, it's not even a single ghost, but closer to a manifestation of collective consciousness formed by multiple vengeful spirits merged together.

'Since they run away at the first sign of trouble, you need to trap them in a ring, set an effective distance with chains, and use shackles to prevent them from breaking the binding, so why does he hate it so much?'

In a previous playthrough when I became Xing's companion, when we trapped a ghost in an item, he even gathered materials for shackles and iron chains himself after I said I would help with his revenge.
Now it seems he can't accept trapping his own sister in an item. Such hypocrisy.

'Fighting Xing with force is too disadvantageous.'

If I summon dark mana and fight seriously, I do have a secret technique that could defeat even the great Xing.
But using that technique would definitely kill Xing, and he wouldn't die peacefully either.
Xing also has his own trump card.
Neither of us can achieve a clean victory.
A pointless fight.
A victory that would only leave wounds and losses even if I win.

"Do we really have to fight?"
"After provoking me like that, did you believe you could avoid a fight?"

It hurts my pride, but I have no choice.
An experienced player should know how to handle various weapons.
This weapon is one that even I, an experienced player who has gone through countless playthroughs, have never used before.
I can't even predict what effect it will have.
It might decide the match in one go, but it could also backfire.
Though its lethality is lower than a finishing move.
This is the only way to end this without either of us getting hurt.

'Here goes!'

Remember the pain of stepping on a thorn.
Remember the sadness of having all your items stolen when you worked hard to fill a pouch with treasures from the Magic Tower's treasure vault, only to discover it was a mimic pouch.
Remember the frustration of becoming a petrified hero because you mismanaged a heroine's affection level right before graduation.

'Don't chicken out. You can do this!'

As I strained to make my face hot, my eyes started to burn.

"You..."

For the first time, Xing's sword, which had been driving fierce attacks, lost its deadly speed and slowed down.

"Are you crying?"

A woman's tears are a weapon.
Successfully handling a weapon that even an experienced player like me is using for the first time in actual combat.
Operation Weapon Master, a great success!


* * *


I thought she was a monster who didn't understand human hearts.
Just clumsily imitating humans.
That thing doesn't understand human emotions.

Drip.

The moment I saw tears flowing from Oknodie's eyes.
Cracks formed in my firm belief.

"Am I crying? Is this crying?"
"...I feel foolish for even asking."
"Yes, that's right! I'm crying right now."

Crocodiles at least open their mouths wide to stimulate their tear ducts to shed tears, but how did this small monster shed tears without even opening her mouth?
Oknodie, who acted out human emotions without knowing what real emotions were.
It's because she felt real emotion.

Is it guilt?
Is it shame?
Or is it for some other reason?

Oknodie shed real tears.
For reasons even she didn't understand.

"...My desire to cut you down is fading."
"Can we stop fighting now?"
"I'll give you a chance to explain. Clarify your intentions behind what you said about my sister."

Would a child who is clumsy with her own emotions speak properly?
I wanted to hear what she was thinking when she spoke those words.

"About ghosts? Since they're a race that tends to run away, you need to make them possess objects..."

After hearing her explanation, I understood.
To use such ridiculous word choices without any malice.
The parents are partly to blame for this.
What did they let this child see to end up like this?
But with a little thought, I can guess.
A human weapon born from the darkness of the Foundation.
The entity she calls "Papa" that I've only heard rumors about probably refers to the Foundation's butler who visited the Academy or the instructor who taught her.
They're probably not her real parents, and if they are, that's even worse.

'A child who didn't grow up under proper parents.'

People who grow up in similar environments feel a sense of kinship and easily empathize with each other.
A child who either had bad parents like him and his sister, or whose parents died due to the Foundation or other factors.
A child with a past not unlike theirs.

"From now on, don't use words that can easily cause misunderstandings."
"Hmph. I spoke clearly! You're the one who misunderstood."

Normally, I would have narrowed my eyes at such a retort and considered whether to cut her down, but now I didn't even feel like drawing my sword against this pitiful child.

"So you're going to trap my sister in a ring?"
"Yes. I happen to have a good ring."
"What's so special about that ring that you need to trap a soul mimicking my sister in it?"
"Ghosts need life force to maintain their existence. To persist without harming people, they need to dwell in objects that can replenish life force. Like this ring."

Although he's a swordsman who uses a sword, in the Eastern Empire, beings originating from ghosts or yokai, known as guimul, are well known.
He knows it's not unheard of to put ghosts back into guimul obtained by exorcising or pacifying spirits.

A gourd that can trap yokai by deceiving them.
A scroll that prevents escape once trapped.

That ring must also be able to become such a guimul.
But unlike the gourd and scroll, Oknodie's ring has one difference.

"That ring wasn't originally created to trap something, was it?"
"Only amateurs use items for their intended purpose. Experienced players create their own traditions!"

An answer overflowing with confidence to the point of absurdity.
An attitude that could be seen as arrogance actually felt reassuring.
Because I know.
Not even my sister, but a fake ghost.
To protect even such a deceptive form, I need to trust Oknodie completely.

"...If you truly protect this child."
"If I protect her?"
"I'll kill someone for you."

So I offer a price.
With the only thing I can do.

Rustle!

I raised my sword at the sound of a rat scurrying away after eavesdropping on our conversation, but Oknodie shook her head.

"That's my friend!"
"...You'd better make sure it keeps quiet."

The deal was accepted.
