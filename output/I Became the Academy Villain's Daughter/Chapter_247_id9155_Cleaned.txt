<247 - Sports Festival Debut>

The Mesugakki princess couldn't hide her envy as she observed Oknodie's dark mana.

So dark mana was such an incredible power after all.

Though dangerous, if handled properly, it could become an extraordinary tonic unlike anything else.

Stability.
Adaptability.
Follow-up management.

Everything was unstable, but what if one supplemented it with the power of capital?

Adding ingredients to increase stability, instructing imperial wizards to install magic circles to improve adaptability, and continuously managing the aftermath through artifact usage.

Even then, would dark mana remain difficult to obtain, difficult to handle, and life-threatening if one let their guard down?

"Amazing♡"

The princess embarked on an experiment to buy mastery with money, and the experiment ended successfully.

She achieved 99% pure dark mana control—the pinnacle of refined mana manipulation without any of the drawbacks of impurity-laden, resentment-tainted dark mana.

This implied something significant.

Dark mana responds according to its total amount—those who possess more can threaten the bodies of those with less and seize control over their life and death.

Without high-level mana control techniques or high-grade artifacts for defense, one could lose their life in an instant.

But purity had become a variable.

Now, even if small fry with less than 10% purity possessed ten times more mana, they wouldn't threaten her life—instead, she threatened theirs.

Dark mana users hiding throughout the continent, human traitors who had become Mains, would become her unwilling servants just by appearing before her eyes.

"Everyone just stares at me like 'Ehhh~? Why does the princess have dark mana~?' Hehe. They don't even care how well I handle this power. Weaklings♡ Cowards♡"

The princess had become so obsessed with dangerous dark mana that she finally accepted its power into her body.

If human traitors who had long handled dark mana came into contact with the princess, her noble body, bloodline, and life would become theirs.

She could clearly see such greedy thoughts appearing in the minds of countless students.

But they only knew half the story.

Logically speaking, what fool would openly reveal their weakness in front of everyone?

At least the Mesugakki princess wasn't such a person.

"Indeed. Handling such ominous dark mana. Has the Empire's Second Princess gone mad with greed and given up her right to succession? Do you plan to spend your life confined in a monastery?"

"Ahaha! Did you think this Mesugakki princess would become a Dark Priestess, offering morning prayers to evil gods and conducting weekend services like some stuffy priest?"

The Mesugakki princess caught the ball flying toward her with "one hand" enhanced with dark mana.

It was an effortless guard that made the imposing presence of the White Paladin Ru, one of the Four Heavenly Kings of the second year, seem insignificant despite his full-power throw.

Ru's gaze shifted downward.

The Mesugakki princess hadn't even stepped back.

Fear of incomprehensible strength began to rise in the eyes of those who had been watching the immature princess.

"So a Paladin is such a stupid class~? Hehe. I understand the desire to possess the body and heart of the Empire's beautiful Second Princess by looking down on her as weak. But I wouldn't simply offer my body and heart to weaklings, you know?"

The princess's mana intensified further, with the darkness of her dark mana rippling in double layers.

This wasn't a change due to technique.

It was a change due to the absolute amount of pure mana.

An incredible amount that was hard to believe she had gathered in just over a month.

Ten years' worth of dark mana in terms of duration.

A concentration ten times denser than the average dark mana user.

It meant she had already obtained power that ordinary dark mana users would need 100 years of effort to achieve.

Avoiding paladins.
Avoiding priests of the good gods.
Avoiding the watchful eyes of the Adventurer's Guild.

She had easily obtained power that should have required desperate effort, using the princess's money and authority.

Ru realized:

'I didn't expose the princess's evil power. This is what she wanted to happen.'

For some, the inter-grade competition at the grand sports festival becomes a debut stage to prove their strength.

The Mesugakki princess had decided to become the protagonist of this sports festival.

"Sister! Why have you turned to such an ominous power...!"

"Shut up♡ I never gave you permission to butt in as you please just because you're also a princess♡"

"Kyaak!"

The Third Princess Yayoi collapsed with a weak scream.

Yayoi, with her righteous character, had been watching from outside the court with eyes similar to Ru's.

Unforgivable.
I will stop you.
I won't forgive users of evil dark mana.

How amusing.

Who was going to stop whom?

Combat power was in her favor.

Authority was in her favor.

Now that all the weaknesses of dark mana had been turned into strengths, she was not in a position to fear being dominated by other followers or fear suppression by public institutions.

Rather, with that mighty power, she was an authority who made other followers tremble in fear and whom public institutions avoided.

"While listening to the Four Heavenly Kings of the second year introduce themselves, there was just one thing I envied. Do you know what it was~?"

"I don't know."

"Cool titles♡ The Fearsome Dead Cat, Blue Fist Io, White Paladin Ru, Orthodox Noble Lady Mandela. Now I'll have such a title instead of just Empire's Second Princess."

The Mesugakki princess, who couldn't contain her excitement as she crossed her legs and grinned, placed both hands over her chest and said:

"Dark Princess Mesugakki. I'm going to snatch all the power, titles, and popularity that Oknodie had♡ When I'm superior in power, authority, and beauty, there's no reason not to be called that~?"

The body of the boastful Mesugakki princess was suddenly teleported outside the court with a swoosh.

"Ahh!? Who's disqualifying me? I was properly inside the line and didn't get hit by the ball!"

An instructor approached and told her:

"Holding the ball for more than 30 seconds is a violation of the rules to prevent interference with the game."

"There was no such explanation before the game..."

"It's the fault of participants who didn't ask. Study the rules yourself. Advantages and disadvantages exist in rules that aren't publicly announced and that others don't tell you about. Take this as a lesson."

It was a pathetic elimination for Mesugakki, one of the Four Heavenly Kings of the first year.

In her eyes, which were welling up with tears of frustration, she saw other first-years deliberately avoiding her gaze.

"Did she get disqualified while showing off?"

"Don't laugh. Her Highness will be furious if she finds out."

"Haah. I think I'm going to laugh. This is driving me crazy..."

The imperial faction students were gritting their teeth and groaning as if they were about to sob.

The Mesugakki princess, with her face red from embarrassment, trembled before sharply turning her head somewhere.

"I-I'm not the only one who lost! Oknodie will definitely struggle too!?"

But reality was completely different.

Rather, the second-years were screaming and desperately trying to avoid her.

The sports festival debut as a powerhouse that the Mesugakki princess had wanted to show was unfolding at Oknodie's hands.

Dead Cat had desperately focused solely on recovering her body for the past two days.

Fantasy Stack.

If she could just stack up the eight spare lives of Nine Lives again, she could pursue her strategy.

The more difficult the conditions of a technique, the stronger the attack it produces.

Dead Cat's powerful counterattack technique is based on "Return to Sender," deceiving the world's principles by treating the death of a clone as the death of the main body.

And not just once, but safely at least eight times.

The capabilities of an overpowered character optimized for single-boss DPS, fully equipped with a damage set that allows one-sided damage trading.

However, in the eyes of a veteran player, it was nothing but a burden.

"Senior~! You won't run away this time, right? I can catch the rainbow ball, right?"

"There won't be a turn for you. Only I will throw, and I'll throw next time too, and the time after that as well."

Dead Cat had recovered three balls amid the confusion.

Oknodie, who would normally have complained about unfairness or begged for just one chance like a child, surprisingly responded with a relaxed "Hmm~?"

As if to say: You won't have your way.
Will things go as you planned?

"Then let me show you."

Her fantasy stack doesn't decrease only from enemy attacks.

It also decreases when she takes her own life by her own hands.

It means supremely efficient attacks can fly even if the opponent does nothing!

"The reason why I'm called the Fearsome Dead Cat...?"

As she was making this proud declaration to restore her senior's dignity and apply pressure, her hand felt empty.

The ball she had been holding was somehow rolling right into Oknodie's hands.

Not only that, but her feet also felt empty.

The balls she had carefully placed at her feet were rolling toward Oknodie on their own.

<Magic Eye>

What trick was this?

As she concentrated mana in her eyes to detect any magical manipulation, she saw something.

Whine whine

Transparent mosquitoes clinging to the dodgeballs, rolling them with their bodies.

One of them even had a faint red body.

Looking down at the hand that had dropped the ball, she saw her fingers were swollen.

Her fingers were so numb that she couldn't feel the ball slipping from her grasp.

In front of the dumbfounded Dead Cat, Oknodie excitedly picked up the ball.

"Level 3 Rainbow Ball~~!"

Moreover, there were two more balls that Dead Cat had carefully collected still lying at her feet.
