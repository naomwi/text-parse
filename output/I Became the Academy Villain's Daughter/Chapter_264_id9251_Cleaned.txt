# 264 - Ingredient Gathering Dungeon

If Oknodie had snuck out to avoid taking her final exams through the power of faith, the same was true for the Andersen heir.

"Are you that sick of divine power, Andersen?"
"When you experience the pain of broken bones twice a week, you lose any desire to learn divine power, even out of spite."
"Still, following me secretly was too much!"
"I'm sorry."
"I could have accidentally killed you! Next time, please make some noise when you follow me!"
'That's the issue!?'

The bewilderment lasted only a moment.
The Andersen heir somehow felt nostalgic.
Most noble boys have a mischievous phase in their childhood.
They hide somewhere in the garden or embark on small adventures to expand their world, evading the strict surveillance of butlers, knights, and servants.
Andersen had such a phase too.
Although he was forbidden from skill training after being caught by the family head, who deemed it unbecoming of a noble, he felt oddly proud that his long-unused "Tracking" and "Taming" skills were still proficient.
Oknodie's backhanded compliment about his threatening stalking skills felt much better than the nobles' praise of "impressive," "amazing," and "sugoi" when his grades improved.
It felt like returning to his childhood!

"Does your forgiveness mean you'll let me join whatever you're planning to do?"
"I'm considering it. Recently, one of my friends got seriously injured while playing with other friends."
"Don't worry. I'm stronger and sturdier than your friends."
"I suppose so? You're a tier 4 character, at least among male characters. Ranking in the top 40 out of several thousand is quite an achievement."
"?"

Though Oknodie was speaking in her usual cryptic way that others couldn't understand, it was clear she had accepted.

"So what are we doing now?"
"Treasure hunting!"
"Treasure hunting...?"
"You know how professors and instructors concentrate their manpower on monitoring religious people during the 'Religious Day' weekly event, right?"
"I noticed something like that. Classes were openly canceled, and we were given free time."
"This is when the Academy's security is most vulnerable in the first semester. So during this gap..."
"You're planning to steal treasure?"
"We're going to steal monsters!"
"...Monsters? Not treasure?"

It sounded bizarre.
But after hearing her out, there was some logic to it.

"No matter how much manpower they pull in, they wouldn't neglect guarding treasures. Those places are always tightly secured. On the other hand, the monster pens operate on the principle of 'be careful if you don't want to die,' so now is actually the perfect time to infiltrate!"

While dedicated personnel would guard the warehouses, the monster pens would only be watched by the regular caretakers and instructors on rotation duty.
If treasures disappeared, it would cause a huge problem, but monsters disappearing was a common occurrence at the Academy.

"So your plan is to enter the monster pens while the Academy isn't paying attention, kill all the monsters, and gain lots of skill experience? Not bad."
"Eek! What cruel things are you saying! I would never do something so inefficient and terrible. We'd be tracked down if we did something that violent!"
"...Then why are we going to the monster pens?"

Oknodie spoke as if stating something completely obvious.

"The campus cafeteria menu hardly ever changes. And my cookbook collection is falling behind. So I'm planning to harvest ingredients directly during this opportunity!"

Looking closer, Oknodie and the maid had large bags on their backs with tools sticking out.

"...Gathering tools?"
"Yep. We're going to collect cooking ingredients from monsters!"

Seeing Oknodie lift a milking machine while wearing white cotton gloves, Andersen couldn't shake the feeling that he regretted coming.

"As you may know, now that you've heard this much of Oknodie's plan, you only have two choices, Young Master."
"What happens if I don't participate?"
"You'll waste a week imprisoned somewhere no one knows about."

With the maid's matter-of-fact warning, Andersen reluctantly joined Oknodie's "Crash Bang Monster Cooking Ingredient Gathering Grand Operation (Beginner)."

* * *

Gift Academy has various daily quest dungeons available each day of the week.

For point mining, there's the Adventure Department's "Professor Bronze's Underground Treasure Vault Stairs" that they visited previously.
For ingredient gathering, there's the Production Department's "Professor Gordon's Monster Farm."
This monster farm is the special facility we're sneaking into while professors and instructors are significantly reduced due to Religious Day.

"Alright, let's steal everything we can!"
"Are you making a declaration of war as we break in...? That enormous hat is just asking to be caught. Don't you want to infiltrate more carefully?"
"Eh, only beginners worry about that stuff. Experts can walk right in without concern!"

Saying that, Oknodie boldly entered the completely unguarded monster farm from the front.
What about the entrance lock?
Before he could ask, she pulled out a hairpin and unlocked it with a click in just three seconds.

'So fast...'

Truly the disciple of a righteous thief.
But this was a monster farm.
Even when breaking into a rich neighborhood house, you'd have to deal with guard dogs first, let alone monsters.
No sane thief would ever break in, and even if they did, they might be killed.
Entering a monster farm without caretakers present was a stupid act that raised only one question: did they want to become food?

"Grrr grrr"
"Kaaak kaaak!"

Sure enough, killer cats guarding the monsters in place of human caretakers immediately bared their teeth and claws.

"Ah, leave this to me. Cats hate having their bellies touched."

April, the cat-person (or not) maid, boldly stepped forward in place of the two.
Andersen watched with interest.
How would a cat-person handle cats?

"Look at this."
"Meow?"
"That's right."

April diverted the cats' attention to the opposite direction with a laser pointer she pulled out.
Then she approached the killer cats, whose punches could intimidate even monsters, and... kicked them hard in the stomach, sending them flying.

"You're kicking them!?"
"It's only natural that the part they hate being touched is their weakness."
"That's true, but... is it okay for a cat-person to kick cats!"
"Huh? Do werewolves keep primitive four-legged wolves as pets? They're inferior species, so treat them accordingly."
"What a mess this one's personality is... it's almost Foundation-like."

More precisely, it was a personality based on reading "99 Characteristics of Cat-People" and following what she learned from books, but April kept quiet because explaining was bothersome.

"Well done, April!"
"Thank you, Oknodie."
"Now, shall we split into three?"
"We're gathering separately!? That's dangerous."
"Ugh. Are you, an upper-class student, saying you're scared because it's your first time? Well, the young master is only known for choosing the wrong lectures rather than any achievements on campus..."

Feeling the disappointment from Oknodie as she turned away with cloudy eyes, Andersen reluctantly extended his hand.

"Fine. I just need to handle one thing by myself, right? What should I gather first?"
"Would you like to shear the bomb sheep?"
"Bomb sheep? What kind of monster is that?"
"Ah, first-years wouldn't know."

Oknodie handed him a gathering dagger with a smile, saying it was no big deal.

"They have special colored wool that you shouldn't touch. If you do, a timer starts ticking, and when it reaches zero, they shoot magic of that color from their mouths. The white wool contains quite a bit of mana, so when you squeeze it and refine it into cooking oil, it's good for your health!"
"You're assigning such a terrifying creature to a beginner!? Give me something else!"
"Tch. You're so picky. Then I'll ask April to handle the bomb sheep!"

April received the gathering dagger and wool sack with trembling hands.
Seeing April glaring at him resentfully as she entered the bomb sheep pen, the Andersen heir felt his noble dignity severely wounded.

"Next is gathering ingredients more important for cooking. Since you found the bomb sheep too difficult, I'll choose something easier for you."
"No, give me something difficult."
"Really?"
"The bomb sheep was particularly troublesome. I'm definitely not afraid of sheep that shoot magic from their mouths. I just... have a wool allergy."
"I see."
"It's true. I never wear woolen clothes, even in winter, because of my wool allergy."
"That could be the case. Then please take this."

Oknodie pulled out a milking machine as tall as herself and something that looked like restraints from her pocket.

"What's this?"
"Hehe. Gisele smuggled it in by bribing the inspection staff. It's a secret from others, okay?"
"No, not the backpack. Why is the milking machine so big? How much are you planning to milk?"

It was so large that one might question if it could hold that much milk even when milking a cow directly.

"Oh, that's fine. This is a monster farm, you know. Of course we need a big one since we're milking minotaurs, not regular cows."
"Mino... what? The muscular bipedal bull monsters that are over 3 meters tall?"
"They should have leashes on, so avoid their headbutts, strikes, and kicks, then use these restraints to secure their bodies before milking them thoroughly!"

This is insane.
The bomb sheep would be much easier.
It's not too late.
I should ask to switch.
As Andersen was about to grab April, he saw the maid who had been walking sluggishly suddenly speed up three times faster, disappearing toward the bomb sheep pen.
