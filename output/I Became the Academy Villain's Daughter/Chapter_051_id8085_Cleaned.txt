# 51 - The Nature-Loving Professor

The Gift Academy is full of eccentric professors.
It's an academy overseen by a principal who seriously dreams of world domination with actual potential to achieve it.
A dreamland where professors can receive generous support or conduct forbidden experiments that would be unthinkable at academies affiliated with specific countries or organizations.
While many professors coveted such support and experiments, Professor Weird of the Magic Department, who taught the Monday third period class "Basics and Understanding of Mana Usage," had slightly different aspirations.
Her goal was to spread the excellence of Dryads, tree spirits, to as many people and as widely as possible!
Gift Academy was the world's premier academy where she could most broadly unfold her ideals.

'Hehe. I made the right choice becoming a professor at Gift Academy.'

From the first lecture for freshmen, her classroom was packed.
It was filled with students eager to learn about the greatness of nature magic.

'Wow, look at the professor's outfit.'
'My goodness, how can she walk around looking so provocative?'
'I've decided. From today, I'm going to become a druid.'

Professor Weird wore vines and leaves draped around her body instead of clothes.
This wasn't because she was an exhibitionist, but because she was a Dryad, a fairy originating from nature.
Fairies are inherently uninhibited about showing their naturally born figures.
While many students, regardless of gender, were entranced by her beauty and sat in the classroom as if spellbound, one particular student showed enthusiasm in a different direction.

'Of course a forest keeper should attend a Dryad's lecture!'

Among students who chose classes based solely on appearance, she was one of the few intellectuals.
The spirited apprentice forest keeper, Dorothy.
Like Oknodie, she sat in the front row, eyes shining as she listened attentively to the lecture.
She believed the professor had allowed her to sit in the front row because she recognized her enthusiasm for learning, but Oknodie sitting next to her didn't think so.

'She said short people should sit in the front row because they can't see otherwise. This means I can't slack off.'

Unlike in the game where she could always choose the back row thanks to being the tallest at 230cm, sitting at the very front at 133cm in reality was humiliating.
Whenever lectures got boring, it had been quite entertaining to emit killing intent toward students sitting in front and watch them jump in surprise!

"Mana is a puzzle. Even if you don't memorize or recognize the entire puzzle, if you familiarize yourself with just one small puzzle piece, you can identify it as mana through that piece."
"It's extremely difficult for ordinary wizards to find that piece, but nature wizards are different. They can find nature-attribute mana puzzle pieces much more easily and frequently than other wizards."
"This means they can find puzzles at a significantly faster rate than other wizards."

A student raised their hand.
It was an imperial student from Group B with an arrogant face that might as well have had "I'm so great" written on it.

"I'm Rogini, an apprentice wizard from the Red Magic Tower. May I ask a question?"
"Go ahead."
"Isn't what you described a characteristic of elemental mana? Couldn't fire mages or ice mages also find fire or ice attribute mana puzzles, not just nature attribute users?"

Filthy fire user.
Professor Weird was slightly irritated by the words of this first-year student with an attribute opposing nature.

"Well, somewhat? To some extent? Something similar to nature attribute? They might be able to imitate it, but other attributes have limitations. They're simply not as common in nature."

Professor Weird eloquently continued with a forceful tone.

"Have you ever thought about why you're able to survive? The ground you stand on originates from nature. The food you eat every day is enjoying nature's grace."
"So we should always be grateful to nature, right?"

Dorothy said with a bright smile.
Professor Weird answered with a wicked smile.

"Yes. Always be grateful. Your daily life continues only because the earth doesn't split open and devour all terrestrial beings."
"......"
"Nature could always consume all living creatures on earth in one bite, but it's being generous."

What's with this professor? She's scary.

"The power of nature magic lies in its versatility—it can be used anytime, anywhere. Today, we'll confirm this fact right here in this classroom."
"That concludes the basic overview. Next, we'll practice actually feeling nature attribute mana puzzle pieces. Everyone, put on the monocles I've distributed."

Unlike her bizarre praise of nature with hints of madness, Professor Weird was not stingy in preparing tools to help students keep up with the lesson.
This meant that while the person might be dangerous, the difficulty of following the lecture was relatively easy.

"Now, you need to find nature attribute mana puzzles in 10 different objects in this classroom. For the student who finds all 10 first..."
"Will you give us points?"
"Or bonus points that would help with our grades?"
"I'll give you the opportunity to become my teaching assistant."

The downside was that she would naturally drag students into the abyss of evil as easily as breathing.
Students who didn't know how difficult it would be to become a teaching assistant with their already busy academic schedules enthusiastically put on their monocles and looked around, assuming they would also get bonus points.
Dorothy was also looking around eagerly when she noticed one student whose reaction was different from the others.

"Oknodie, what's wrong? You need to search hard to become a teaching assistant!"
"Let those who want to do it, do it."

While students from Groups A and B were competing with each other, saying they couldn't lose, Oknodie, the most outstanding talent in Group A, clicked her tongue like an enlightened sage.

"Dorothy, we have to take two common required courses for all departments, three department courses, and choose two liberal arts courses. That's seven courses in one semester."
"So?"
"Each lecture is 2 hours, and with 7 courses that usually meet twice a week, that's 28 hours of lectures per week, right?"
"Yes. It's great that we can learn a little and get much smarter!"
"Do you think the professors don't know this fact?"
"Huh?"
"There are 168 hours in a week, and lectures only take 28 hours, leaving 140 hours. So they can easily assign 20 hours worth of homework, right?"
"Wha-?"
"Get it? Professors are time assassins. If you become a teaching assistant on top of that, you won't even have enough time for assignments."

Dorothy muttered in shock, "Then when do we sleep?" but Oknodie, who had spread the harsh truth about the academy, just turned her head away with an indifferent expression.

'It's not particularly difficult for an experienced player like me.'

Since she had absolutely no desire to become a teaching assistant, she deliberately slowed her pace, pretending not to know while pressing the button on the monocle.

Sneakily.

Seeing the vines growing along the walls blocking the classroom door, it was clear this person was no less of a moral bankrupt than Professor Bronze.
A confinement play that physically prevents students from escaping the classroom until the lecture ends, without them knowing!

'Why are they struggling so much to find these? It's not that hard.'

Unlike her worries about not being able to sense mana in reality compared to the game, she had been using wand magic just fine.
It was the same when practicing with Jonna, and now with the added effect of the monocle's "Magic Detection Assistance" function, she was unstoppable.

'I can see everything.'

The wood of the wooden desk.
The dirt floor of the classroom.
Some idiot's portable mirror ball toy.
A thieving squirrel scurrying under desks, picking students' pockets.
A beetle clinging to a female student's trouser hem, probably mistaking her fragrance for tree sap, and so on.
Objects containing nature mana puzzle pieces were scattered everywhere.

'Can't someone just find all 10 quickly and end this?'

Out of boredom, she had already found over 50 puzzle pieces just by looking around.

"Hehe. I've already found seven!"

While forest keeper Dorothy was excitedly clicking the button on her monocle to collect puzzle pieces like a fish in water, one student from Group B also stood out.

"Is this supposed to be difficult? Everyone can see them, right? You're not pretending not to see them, are you?"
"......"
"I want to hit that guy."
"Be patient. He's in Group B too."
"Wizards are always so annoying."

Sandcooker, an apprentice wizard from the Yellow Magic Tower with talent as an earth mage.
This gifted individual, who was destined to grow as Professor Weird's disciple in the original work, had already started showing off.

"Professor, I've found all ten."
"First-year Sandcooker. Excellent. I grant you the position of teaching assistant to help with my lectures from now on."
"It's an honor to be of assistance to you, Professor."
"Ah, I found them all too!"

Dorothy, who had found all 10 slightly later, stamped her feet in disappointment.
Professor Weird's eyes lit up as she spoke.

"This year we have many students talented in nature magic."
"Professor, wasn't the teaching assistant position limited to the first person only?"

Sandcooker glared at Dorothy with wary eyes.
Professor Weird nodded.

"The position of teaching assistant is an honor. A privilege granted only to the most outstanding talents."
"I knew it!"
"When today's lecture ends, I'd like Sandcooker to place all 100 monocles back in their cases, stack them neatly in the box, and carry them to my research lab."
"...Perhaps the honor of becoming a teaching assistant would be even more joyful if shared with a friend rather than enjoyed alone? Please make her a teaching assistant too!"
"Cooperation and coexistence. That's not a bad way of thinking, naturally. If Sandcooker insists, I'll make Dorothy a teaching assistant as well."
"P-Professor? I don't really need to be a teaching—"

Crack.
The podium collapsed following Professor Weird's hand gesture.

"What did you just say?"
"...I meant I wanted to do it even if it takes up my time! Really, truly!"
"I'm very pleased to see such enthusiastic students. I'll be counting on you both after the lecture ends."

Poor Dorothy.
Despite all the hints, she tried too hard.
Oknodie mercilessly ignored Dorothy's pleading gaze asking for help.


* * *


After the lecture.
Professor Weird was reviewing the visual data uploaded from the monocles linked to the magic clock.
While examining the students' achievements, she noticed something strange in the data.

"What is this student?"

She hadn't told the students, but the monocles had an "auto-capture" function.
During the lecture, she had said that pressing the button was necessary to register finding a nature attribute mana puzzle piece, but in reality, the monocles automatically captured what the wearer visually recognized.
This was a rescue mechanism designed to identify raw talents who unconsciously sensed mana but didn't recognize it as a mana puzzle piece.
But an incredible record had appeared in this rescue mechanism.

<Nature Mana Puzzle Pieces Discovered: 122>
<Time to Discover 10 Pieces: 7 seconds>
<Nature Mana Puzzle Pieces Captured: 10>
<Time to Capture 10 Pieces: 30 minutes>

10 pieces in 30 minutes.
A fairly decent level of talent at Gift Academy, which was full of talented individuals.
However, in terms of potential, it far surpassed the records of apprentice earth mage Sandcooker and apprentice forest keeper Dorothy, whom she had chosen as teaching assistants.

"What kind of background does this student have?"

Was she a secret disciple raised by the Yellow Magic Tower Master?
Or perhaps a wild girl raised directly by the master of the forest?

Professor Weird couldn't contain her curiosity and accessed Oknodie's academy records.

<Special Notes>
-Has a hobby of collecting stones.
-Received reports from guardian that she occasionally eats stones, but this has not been confirmed yet.

"...She eats stones? Is she a stone golem?"

Does feeding children stones increase their nature affinity?
Curiosity sparkled in Professor Weird's eyes.

"I should experiment on the fourth-year students."
