# <364 - Gisele's Interference>

Until now, I had found countless reasons to harbor hatred toward the Foundation.
I had seriously contemplated methods for revenge.
And the moment a man named Jonna Wiheomhae was appointed as a professor at the Academy, Gisele instinctively knew it was time to make a move.

'If I can't stop Jonna Wiheomhae's teaching activities here, even Oknodie's Academy life will fall under the Foundation's control.'

What follows is obvious.
Oknodie, whose psychological dependence on her butler is apparent.
All her interests would return to the Foundation rather than her friends.
And gradually, she would abandon everything.
All relationships that couldn't compare to her relationship with the Foundation.

'Don't be ridiculous. Before you take Oknodie away from us, I'll take her away from you first.'

What methods should be used to drive Jonna Wiheomhae out of the Academy?
Gisele already knew the answer.

"First, we need to minimize the number of students attending the 'Basic Mana Refinement Method' lecture."
"You think that's something we can reduce? Besides, everyone has experienced the effects of this rare refinement method firsthand. No one's foolish enough to miss a growth opportunity, regardless of its magnitude."
"Son O-cheon is right. Even as a cook, I felt my stamina improve when standing in front of the wok. Upper combat classes would be even more eager to attend, wouldn't they?"

Son O-cheon and Isabel's opinions were valid.
Students seek out lectures that benefit them.
They wouldn't attend harmful lectures even if forced to, but they wouldn't skip beneficial ones even if told not to.

"Rumors. We'll spread rumors."
"What kind of rumors?"
"For instance... that the Foundation's mana refinement method contains dark mana that makes you unknowingly dependent on it."

Isabel flinched in surprise.
Son O-cheon was equally startled.

"Gisele... I've thought this occasionally, but I'm truly glad you're on our side. To think of spreading such frightening rumors."
"I agree. If someone like him had been at Rocky Mountain, the beast-people would have been extinct long ago."

If forced to choose between the Foundation and the Dark Association, the choice was clear.
Lower-class students other than Isabel and Son O-cheon reacted exactly as expected.

"Did you hear? If you attend the Foundation professor's lecture, you'll unknowingly develop dark mana."
"That's crazy. Isn't that dangerous?"
"Of course it's dangerous. You'll be forced to obey someone with more dark mana. It's far more dangerous than Carmela's pet contract."
"It's not like we'd suffer much if the Foundation professor disappeared anyway."
"Besides, I heard that lecture doesn't even help weaker students much."

The effectiveness difference proportional to physical ability stats was clearly evident from the first week's "Health Measurement"!
For lower-class students, while it might be a rare experience, the lecture wasn't particularly helpful.
Some students still insisted on attending, but peer pressure wouldn't allow it.

"Are you consuming the Foundation professor's lecture? Will you still attend even if the credits you consume return as bombs that burn down your homeland?"

The gaslighting about being traitors, skillfully spread under Gisele's direction, created an atmosphere where even students who wanted to attend were afraid to step in.

"What does attending a lecture have to do with being a traitor? Does attending an Imperial professor's lecture make you an imperialist, and attending a frontier lecture make you a frontier-ist? Come on."
"Rogini... that's not entirely wrong. Actually, imperialists and frontier-ists do choose lectures based on professors' origins and affiliations."
"Ha. Don't say such stupid things, Sandcooker. Wizards are followers of shining intellect. We don't commit the foolishness of narrowing our knowledge due to groundless prejudices, biases, or slander."

Of course, a small number of principled students still insisted on attending.
Gisele felt the need to spread even more cunning rumors.

"Did you hear, Rogini? Starting next week, the professor will be assigning an insane amount of homework."
"What? Seriously. I finally found a lecture I wanted to attend, but I can't handle that."

Wizards with weak stamina couldn't muster the energy to attend Professor Jonna's lecture while struggling with assignments, and abandoned the second week's attendance.
Not just Rogini and Sandcooker, but practically most lower-class students, and even some from the upper classes, dropped the course at this point.
Those remaining were a tiny handful of solid individuals with tremendous tenacity and skill.

Black Knight Mob.
Apprentice Forest Keeper Dorothy.
High-speed Sword Rockfell.
Berserker Hestia.
Eastern Swordsman Xing.
Imperial Second Princess Mesugakki.
Hero Ishtar.

Gisele decided to personally approach those among the remaining students whom she could persuade directly.

"Mob. It's been a while since we've met face to face."
"Mr. Gisele. I'm indebted to you for the information about the Hook Ghost. Thanks to that, Oknodie was able to rescue us when she came."
"I'm glad it was helpful. It might be a bit early, but may I request repayment of that debt?"
"Well, I don't have enough points yet..."
"Don't worry. I'm asking for repayment in a different form. It won't cost money or time. Rather, it will save both."

Gisele spoke firmly.

"Drop the 'Basic Mana Refinement Method' lecture by Professor Jonna dispatched from the Wiheomhae Foundation."
"Do I really have to?"
"This is the repayment condition I'm proposing. If you don't accept, you'll have to repay the information fee with points, and you should be prepared for forced point borrowing for any shortfall."
"...Wasn't that professor Oknodie's butler? Why are you, who's close to that child, interfering with lectures taught by her person?"

Mob's strong declaration that he wouldn't back down without a convincing reason.
Gisele sensed Mob's courage in his willingness to potentially antagonize the Dark Association.
It's easy to ride the wave of power but difficult to go against it.
Gisele had a weakness for those who accomplished difficult tasks.
Those who live in darkness may verbally avoid and despise light, but a part of their hearts inevitably yearns for it.

"For Oknodie's sake, we need to sever the connection with the Foundation. If you've attended the lecture, don't you know? The reality of the harsh education that breaks bones and makes pain unbearable."

Honestly, anyone would frown seeing that.
Gisele sincerely thought so.
However, the thoughts of someone who had received direct training from Oknodie were slightly different.

"Bones can break during training, isn't that making too much of a fuss?"
"Huh?"
"We jump into burning rings, bang our heads in washbasins for cardiopulmonary endurance training, and swim up from the bottom of rivers wearing heavy armor, so honestly, bones can break, can't they?"
"No, what are you..."

Gisele was about to retort that this was madness, but realized Mob was simply mentioning his own training regimen.
From Mob's perspective, having undergone the same training regimen Oknodie would have received from the Foundation, it was just a normal level of training!
The comparison point was wrong from the start.

"...If that's how you feel, continue attending. Just remember, you must repay the points to the Dark Association."

This way, he'd give up just to save money.
Contrary to his intention, Mob transferred the points right there through his magic watch.

"How does a lower-class student have such a large amount of points!?"
"Professors appreciate students who work hard to attend lectures. Especially the Knight Department professors gave me many bonuses."

Mob's enthusiasm for attending physical lectures while wearing heavy armor was highly valued!
It was a benefit that came from unintentionally following the growth route of "I Love All-In Strength Characters Marine."
In the end, Gisele completely lost control over Mob.

'It's fine. One Mob is manageable.'

While Mob was tenacious and capable for a lower-class student, there were still plenty of students following the Dark Association, including second-year seniors.
She just needed to persuade people like Forest Keeper Dorothy, High-speed Sword Rockfell, and Berserker Hestia.

"No!"
"If Dorothy wants to attend, I will too."
"If the Foundation is dangerous, all the more reason to stay by Oknodie's side."

Contrary to Gisele's intentions, the upper-class students had strong personal convictions.
But unlike losing influence over Mob, he still had means to bind these students.

"I can use the Dark Association's information network to find news about Miss Dorothy's home forest. Would this make negotiation possible?"
"Really!? I'm worried about Poppy I left in the forest, can you check on her?"
"A pet? That's not difficult."

With Dorothy giving in, her shadow-like childhood friend Rockfell followed suit.

"For Lady Hestia, I'll provide several new foods that Oknodie hasn't tried yet. With these, you can spend private time with Oknodie."
"I can be with her during lecture time too, right? We're in the same club."
"But lecture time or club activities aren't one-on-one time, are they?"

Hestia, who was already receiving mana cultivation methods from Oknodie rather than mana refinement methods, had no reason to push herself.
With this, Hestia also withdrew.

"If you wish to die by my blade, I'll grant your wish."
"..."

Negotiations with Xing failed from the start, but the number of students attending the lecture was greatly reduced.
Mob, Xing, Mesugakki, Ishtar, Oknodie.
Only five students attending the lecture.
Rumors said that only three people attended the lecture of Destroyer, a member of the previous hero party, but Destroyer was a member of the hero party.
Jonna's position was completely different.

Originally, this lecture starts at 2 PM on Saturday.

It's not easy for students to attend a lecture held during what should be their rest time while overexerting their bodies.

'To continue such a difficult lecture, you'd need many students. Unlike Professor Destroyer, Professor Jonna has become a target of the Imperial professors.'

If he couldn't maintain sufficient student numbers, Professor Jonna's lecture might naturally be closed due to political conflicts among professors.

"Gisele! I've heard about what you've been doing lately."

The problem was Oknodie hearing the rumors and coming to find him.
But this child, who always exceeded expectations, surpassed them once again today.
He had prepared more than ten excuses to isolate her from the Foundation's embrace, but a situation arose where he couldn't use any of them.

"Well done!"
"...Pardon?"
"I was annoyed at having to share something so good, but thanks to your work, now only we can enjoy it!"
"...I also dropped that lecture though?"
"Wow. You set an example to make other students give up!"

She's happy that people can't attend the lecture.
Beyond the relief of not being hated was an even greater unsettled feeling.
This was an expression of trust.
A firm belief that even with few students, there would be no problem with Jonna's professorship.
It contained deep trust in Jonna as much as trust in his actions.
Have I betrayed such pure faith?
Gisele somehow felt ashamed.
