# 34 - Identity Guarantor

If the first gate was a test of autonomy,
And the second gate was a test of creativity,
Then the third gate was a test of combat ability.

'I never knew getting into the Academy would be this difficult.'

Irene, who barely passed the third gate, asked the examiner:

"Have you been conducting these tests every year?"
"More or less."
"Don't you need to test the combat abilities of those who passed the second gate?"
"Why bother? Combat ability is only necessary for passive underachievers who lack autonomy and creativity."
"...!"
"Northern Duchess Irene. Served as a battle mage in the North. Participated in 13 different frontlines. Having experienced harsh warfare, you should understand better than most—combat isn't everything."

Irene realized it deeply.
No matter how many wars are waged, they never end.
The only things that increase after all the killing are the number of fallen warriors and graves marked with broken weapons.
The Northern Duke, weary from the long war against demon-kind, sought answers in the capital.
That was the background leading to Irene taking the entrance exam for Gift Academy.

"The reason is the same. Was that helpful, Xing?"
"I don't fully agree, but if that's the Academy's policy, I have no choice but to follow it."

Talented individuals like Irene and Xing easily passed the third gate.
Even those who failed the third gate had only failed the advanced class test; they still had abundant opportunities to pass the retest for the basic class.

'It hurts my pride that even after all this effort, I'm still not rated as highly as that little kid.'

Not just Irene and Xing, but all successful candidates thought the same thing.
Oknodie, the most likely top candidate who had proven her superiority not once but twice.
They all decided they needed to monitor her every move and learn whatever they could from her.

'There must be some reason why she collects pretty stones, right?'

Irene carefully placed a round pebble in her inner pocket.

After several days of eating, sleeping, and resting in the lounge to recover depleted energy and heal injuries, the detailed list of successful candidates appeared on the mana board.
Dorothy and Rockfell, the two childhood friends, had passed but seemed uncomfortable seeing each other, so they stayed holed up in their individual rooms.

"That's a lot of people."
"Over 2,000 successful candidates. That's considerably more than I expected."
"Hahaha! Most of them are just small fry who can't even match our little mouse!"

Unlike my excited companions, I felt somewhat uncomfortable.
Not because of the previous conflict with Examiner Minerva.
It was because of the group classifications for successful candidates.

"Groups. This is the first time I've seen something like this."
"It's a regional classification."
"Miss Oknodie, do you know something about this?"
"A is for frontier regions. B is for central regions. C is for special cases. Since the central region is vast, the continental east, west, north, and south are considered frontier regions. We're probably in Group A."
"It's hard to understand why they would separate candidates by origin after already selecting them."
"They're not separating us again. Candidates from frontier regions and central regions took their exams in different venues, so they're naturally grouped separately."

Gisele and Isabel looked at me with curious eyes.

"Did they teach you such things in noble households?"
"Oknodie's knowledge seems disproportionately extensive when it comes to Academy-related matters."
"I-it's just a coincidence! I just happened to remember what I learned!"

As I was trying to get by with Gisele's words, a solemn voice cut in: "That's strange."
The number one ranked noble from the Western Noble Union.
Andersen Pretzel, who had been listening to our conversation from the next table, joined in.

"Even I, Andersen Pretzel, second son of the Pretzel Grand Ducal family—the foremost noble house of the wealthiest Deutsche Kingdom among the Western Three Nations—don't know about this."
"W-well, then my Papa must be an even more extraordinary person!"

Sorry, Papa.
If I don't use you as an excuse now, when would I?

"Information that surpasses the intelligence network of the premier noble family of a major power representing the Western Three Nations. I'm intrigued. Oknodie, I wonder how extraordinary your hidden background truly is."
"I agree. Unless you're royalty from some country, such information access is incomprehensible."

The second-ranked noble of the Western Noble Union.
Arcadia, the lone female, peered over her fan with sparkling eyes as if trying to see through me.
She wore an Eastern cheongsam dress.
With Southern tribal tattoos on her shoulders.
And the fan that was an essential accessory for central noble ladies.
She was a supporting female character ranked in the late 20s in popularity, known among players as the "icon of no roots" for indiscriminately following every latest trend.

'Tch. The nerve of Arcadia talking back to me.'
"Little miss, your expression is somehow irritating. You're not having any disrespectful thoughts, are you?"
"...Not really? I was just thinking that with a Northern fur coat, you'd have collected items from all four directions."
"My, you have unexpected wit? I actually prepared a coat for winter. How cute of you to appreciate my sense of style."

The favor of a 2.5-tier supporting character less popular than Yui, the 2-tier NTR heroine, doesn't make me feel proud at all.

"This is a specialty from our Kingdom of Florence—fried squid. I bought it from the restaurant attached to the rest area. Let's chat about trendy accessories like women do."

That's what I thought until I received the fried squid.

"Arcadia is my new tier-1 favorite from today! No one can disrespect her!"
"...Tier-1? I'm not sure what that means, but I suppose it's some kind of compliment among children? I'll accept it gratefully."

Munch munch munch.
Nom nom nom.

While I happily devoured the fried squid, enjoying the texture crumbling in my mouth and shaking my head in delight, a crow tapped on the lounge window with its beak, demanding attention.

"What's that?"
"There's something tied to its leg."
"It's a messenger bird."

The Eastern swordsman Xing said, leaning against the wall.

"In the East, instead of communication magic, we tie messages to birds' legs and send them flying."

The crow perched on a nearby desk, untied the string with its beak, and dropped the paper.

Flutter

Then the crow charged toward a nearby feed container!

"Just like Oknodie."
"Hehe. I agree."
"Hahaha! The flying little mouse has appeared!"
"Ugh. Don't call me that! I'm not a little mouse. I'm still growing and my height will keep increasing! Up to 2m 30cm!"
"Isn't that too tall?"

When I mentioned my height from my muscular male character days, Son O-cheon burst into laughter.
If I were really a male character, I'd look down at that cheeky monkey and show him what a real height difference is!

"Oknodie candidate. This note is calling for you. Examiner Minerva is summoning you to the consultation room downstairs."

Ugh. Why her of all people?
I headed to the consultation room with reluctant steps.

"What do you want to tell me this time?"
"Today I called you for a different matter."

Minerva showed me a chart.

"What is this?"
"It's the group classification dividing Groups A, B, and C. Successful candidates are divided into Group A for frontier regions, Group B for central regions, and Group C for others. These groups will be maintained after enrollment."
"I'm in Group A anyway, right? I received my ticket from a frontier region and took the entrance exam at a frontier exam site."
"That's the problem. Upon checking, we found that candidate Oknodie has never been registered with the International Identity Registration Magic Repository—you're unregistered."
"Ah."

I had an ominous feeling.
No way, right?

"Unregistered individuals cannot belong to either frontier or central regions. A person who doesn't exist. A person not recognized by society. Group C is for those with unclear origins."
"What? So what happens now?"
"You lose freedom of movement and must always be under the supervision and permission of an instructor wherever you go."

No way!
Then I won't be able to collect stat stones or complete the events I need to clear in advance.
I won't be able to sneak into the archives to tear up forbidden books disguised as regular books, fight gargoyles hidden between statues, or set fire to the garden to burn mandrakes!

"Please, can't you make an exception just this once?"
"No."

Minerva was merciless.

"Therefore, candidate Oknodie must be accompanied by an instructor appointed by the main office 24 hours a day."
"That's so unfair!"
"...is what I'd like to say. But for the past three days—72 hours—we've been waiting for someone to come forward as a guarantor for candidate Oknodie's identity. And a guarantor has appeared."

With a flick of Minerva's hand, the curtain in the corner of the consultation room opened, revealing a familiar man in a suit.

"Jonna!"
"It's been a while, my lady. Have you been well?"

I ran over and hugged his legs tightly.
The butler with 100% loyalty who never betrays you once summoned by the whistle.
The only person I can trust in this other world, my butler who never betrays me under any circumstances, Jonna Wiheomhae.

"That is inappropriate."
"Urk."

Jonna pressed my forehead with one hand and detached me.
Even though I thought I'd gotten stronger, Jonna was still far stronger than me.

"The Academy examiner is watching. Please maintain your dignity, my lady."
"Tch. How is it that you haven't changed at all? The first thing you say after seeing me again is 'That is inappropriate.'"

Is Jonna not happy to see me?
As I pouted and grumbled, Jonna's characteristically stern face lifted at the corners—just a little, but still.
Wow...
Even though he's my butler, that's too much.
Look at that savage smile like he's about to murder someone.
This person should never smile in public.

"Jonna. No smiling for three weeks!"
"...?"

Who else but me could tolerate such a smile?
That's why from now on, I should be the only one to see it.
