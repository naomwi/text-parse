# Enhanced Translation: Chapter 54 - The 5th Period with a Different Art Style

Monday, 5th period.
Adventurer's Night-prowling lecture hall.

"Where's the classroom?"
"There aren't any lights on."
"Did the professor go to sleep?"

The few students who were hesitantly wandering between the darkened barracks turned away with deeply disappointed faces.

"Let's just go to the 'How to Sleep Anywhere' lecture instead."
"Yeah. That classroom is closer anyway."
"I switched to this class because I thought there'd be too much competition for grades with all those people... Tch. But if the lecture itself isn't even happening, there's nothing we can do."

Oknodie silently prayed for the souls of the students who were leaving after failing to find the professor.

'That lecture does teach you how to sleep in any extreme environment. Hang in there, newbies!'

If they run away and give up on the lecture, they'll have nothing to show for it, but still, try to endure until the end!

'But this lecture really is unpopular.'

Well, most students left because they couldn't find the professor.
A tiny minority of students remained, with the strange expectation that they might have come to the right place but, fitting for the "Adventurer's Night-prowling" lecture, it might be held outdoors where no lights were on.
And they were right.

*Rustle rustle*

"Huh? Did something just move in the darkness?"
"Turn on a light spell."

A familiar-looking lighting device turned on, casting pink light with a fluttering flower petal effect.
In the open space illuminated by the light...
A woman in white clothes with long black hair hanging down like waterlogged seaweed was rising with creaking sounds.

"Kyaaaaaaah!!"
"Aaaah! T-together, please...!"
"Ghost!! It's too scary!!"
"Aaaack!! I'm going to lose my mind!!!!"
"Hey, guys...? M-me too!"
"Run awaaaay!!"
"Hey, wait!! The lighting device makes it hard to follow—"

The students scattered in all directions screaming like a Tang dynasty army hit by a surprise attack.
A female student dragging the lighting device behind her tripped over a rock.
The lighting device fell sideways but showed surprising durability by not breaking. However, a button must have been pressed accidentally when it fell, as the color of the light changed to red.

*Creak creak*

The black-haired woman in white clothes turned her head toward the fallen student, her joints making creaking sounds.

"Eek! D-don't come near me! Do you know who my father is? He's the mayor of the Holy City-State Federation! One of the city-states in the south called the Temple of All Gods belongs to my father!!"
"......"
"Y-you understand? So don't come any closer. If you do, I'll... tell my father..."
"......"
"I'll tell him...?"

The silent woman in white.
The lighting girl losing confidence by the second.
The woman looked down at her white clothes now stained blood-red, then looked back up at the lighting girl.
With an ominous feeling, the lighting girl shook her head, unable to even make a sound, but...

*Dash!*

"Ah."

At the sight of the white-clothed woman rushing toward her, the lighting girl finally collapsed and fainted.

"Careless child."

Of course, the woman in white wasn't a ghost who had died in a tragic accident at the Academy and returned as a spirit. She was the professor in charge of the "Adventurer's Night-prowling" lecture.

"...Class is starting. ...Come out quickly."

The professor set the lighting device upright and shone it into the darkness.
I was surprised that she illuminated not only the barracks where I was hiding with my "Hiding" skill but even up into the trees.

"Wow. You were the professor? I thought you were a real ghost and almost attacked you out of fear."

The masked assassin Zuang.
I encountered her in this lecture that seemed truly fitting for an assassin.


* * *


Monday, 5th period.
Professor Sadako, who taught the "Adventurer's Night-prowling" lecture from 7 to 9 in the early evening, was a nocturnal professor with long hair and white clothes.
Her body was cold and often stiff, making creaking sounds when she moved, and her long hair obscured her face, which often led to rumors about her being a ghost.
Her popularity among students was, of course, abysmal.
In one semester, she coincidentally held a night lecture where zero students showed up, giving her the comfortable experience of not having to teach. After that, she had an epiphany.

'If I scare the students, I can spend my time freely without teaching.'

Enjoying the professor benefits provided by the Dragon Principal without having to fulfill the obligation of teaching students—a rational free ride!
Having awakened to the mysteries of free-riding, Sadako would make an *impressive* entrance every year at an eerie time when the sun was setting near a particularly dark forest to frighten students.
She thought that once the student with the lighting device woke up, she would spread rumors that would prevent even curious students from daring to come, but...
There are fearless students in this world.
Unfortunately, the 981st batch of first-year students had two such students.

"...Aren't you. ...Afraid of your professor?"
"If I could kill you, I wouldn't be afraid! ...But since I can't kill professors, I'm actually not okay!"
"I'm just not scared."

Zuang gave Oknodie a reproachful look through her mask, as if asking how she could tell such a blatant lie.

"...Really? You're not afraid of me...?"

Professor Sadako approached with creaking sounds, tilting her head at an unrealistic angle and bringing her face close to Oknodie's.
Oknodie showed some signs of being disgusted but also had eyes filled with expectation.

"Professor, you have a pretty voice. There's no ugly woman with a pretty voice!"

The young one was so bold, saying whatever came to mind.
Sadako felt a strange antipathy.

"Do you know what my nickname is? Sadako of Terror."

Eyes revealed between the long strands of hair.
The emotion in those eyes, stained by darkness, stimulates fear like peering into the depths of human nature.
It's an instinctive fear.
The physiological terror humans feel when facing the inhuman.
A fundamental wrongness that makes you feel something is amiss just by looking at it, making you want to scream and run away.

"The Principal has a talent for gathering professors... but he also has an even bigger problem."

Sadako placed her pale hand on the shoulder of the student who wasn't even as tall as her chest.

"The Principal's problem is that he doesn't discriminate based on species. And the professor before you who will teach you is an undead. A creature that defies life. A creation abandoned by the gods."
"!"
"Can you feel it? The cool hand devoid of life's warmth. If you receive education from the dead, you might become a corpse like me. Are you still not afraid?"

Zuang trembled behind her mask and said:

"I'm terribly scared."

If even a child with an assassin's gait is afraid, that says it all.
This should do it.
Another year with zero students to teach.
She could enjoy her peaceful night time alone again.
The undead have nothing but time.
But at the same time, it's also in the nature of the undead to dislike having their personal peace disturbed.
Sadako felt a pang of conscience for trying to make the child who called her pretty dislike her, but she also looked forward to regaining her tranquility.

"Does it matter?"

But Oknodie wasn't afraid.
Instead, she responded confidently.

"As long as you teach the lecture well, that's what matters. Surely that Dragon Principal wouldn't have brought in a professor who can't teach properly."

Sadako was shocked.
Even imperial professors despised her as a lowly undead, yet this mere student boldly acknowledged her.
And the basis for this trust wasn't unfounded.
She was being recognized.
Someone sincerely expected to learn from her.
Someone expected to receive teachings from a being others feared to even look at.

"...This is the lecture you wanted. Say now if you want to quit. If you come to learn from the dead, disturbing my peace, and then quit on a whim... I will never forgive you."
"I won't quit. This is the lecture I want to learn most during Monday's 5th period!"

At Oknodie's bold declaration, Zuang, who had been about to flee, stopped and turned back with a huff.

"Then I'll take it too."
"You don't have to. I can take it alone."
"I don't like feeling like I'm losing if I back down here."

Sadako smiled with a "jjeojeojeok" sound under her hair that hung down over her face at the lively quarrel between the children.
Though she detested playing professor, she had a feeling this year would be an exception.

Even knowing what she is, she's still scary.

The undead are residents of the night.
It would be hard to find a professor more skilled in night activities than the undead Professor Sadako.
However, the most important element of this lecture isn't the effectiveness of the teaching.

'I want to see Professor Sadako's bare face!'

Seeing the professor's bare face before the end of the first semester.
