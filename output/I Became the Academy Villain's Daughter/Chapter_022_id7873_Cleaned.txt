# 22 - I Feel So Wronged

The girl and her companions couldn't contain their emotions.
The very people who had tracked them down with the intention of eliminating them, even subjecting them to humiliation, had saved them from the landslide and earthquake.

"You... your hands... you held onto the rope until they got like this?"

Isabel, the young female leader who commanded the young blood of the Esonia Adventure Party.
She felt both gratitude and shame toward a child who wasn't even half her size.

"I said such harsh things to you. I wanted to fail you because I couldn't stand the sight of you. Why did you help us?"
"Just because. I could, so I did."

It was pure goodwill without any calculation.
Does one human need a reason to help another?
Selflessness without expecting anything in return!
It was a strength of heart that children who grew up too quickly couldn't possess.
In a world of "little adults" where there were no "children" because everyone was put to work at age seven, this was a pristine clarity that couldn't even be called a child's privilege.

'Could this be what they call a spirit in the old tales?'

A pure, innocent spirit raised in a bountiful forest.
A selfish human raised in the harsh human society.
Every adventurer hears stories about spirits and humans at least once.
And they begin to dream.
That they want to live the life of a spirit, not a human.
Rather than living a selfish life in a closed-off village or region, they want to obtain the purity of spirits who roam the vast unknown world, unbound by anything.

'Our leader was just like that child.'

Isabel saw the image of her deceased leader overlapping with the child.
The leader's face had worn that same pure, innocent smile so uncharacteristic of adults.
He had accepted complete strangers into the adventure party and taught them how to pursue purity together.

"I've lost."
"Completely defeated as a test participant, as an adult, and as an adventurer."
"Is this what it takes to enter the world's best academy?"

As the hostility disappeared from Isabel's eyes, those who followed her let out sighs.

"Sorry, Ms. Isabel. We're giving up."
"I see. You too..."

The participants who declared their intention to forfeit watched as their ticket watches turned red.
The word "Retire" appeared one after another on their watches, indicating their disqualification.

<Golden Ticket Holder>
<Status - Retired>
<Reason - Voluntary Withdrawal>

What face would she have if she remained alone?
When she tried to forfeit with them, those who had already given up stopped her.

"Wait. Not you."
"Why? If you're all giving up, what am I supposed to do alone? Have you forgotten the spirit of our adventure party?"
"Ms. Isabel. We came along because we were worried about you. We never really intended to enter the academy from the start. We knew we lacked the skills."
"So I was the only one who was serious about the adventure party?!"
"That's not what we meant. From the beginning, we were just rear support or rookies without much experience in the adventure party. We barely escaped with our lives. You're different—you've been following the leader from before."
"We almost died when we lost grip of the rope earlier, remember?"

Only then did Isabel notice the condition of her companions.
They were exhausted and could barely stand.
In such a state of fatigue, it would be impossible to pass the next gate.

"We appreciate that you helped us get tickets, but this is our limit."
"We're sorry, Isabel."
"This is cowardly. Everyone's leaving but telling me to stay alone—that's selfish! Wasn't an adventure party supposed to always stay together?"

Isabel protested with tears streaming down her face.
Instead of responding to her pleas, her companions bowed their heads to Oknodie, Gisele, and Son O-cheon.

"We apologize for causing trouble. We sincerely apologize."
"We won't ask for forgiveness since we had our reasons. But please, would you forgive our female leader? Unlike us, she has real skill."
"That's right. Without Isabel, we would never have obtained the golden tickets. If you take her in, I'm confident she'll be a great help."

Gisele looked at Oknodie with a questioning gaze.
It was Oknodie who had noticed the earthquake.
It was Oknodie who had saved them.
Only Oknodie could decide whether to forgive them or not.

The girl asked:

"Do you know how to cook?"

It was a perplexing question for everyone.
Why would she ask about cooking skills during the academy entrance exam?

"In an adventure party, local provisioning is essential. If you don't know how to identify edible ingredients and prepare them on the spot, you'll die from poison or disease."
"Then you pass."

Oknodie extended her hand playfully.
When Isabel hesitated, unsure if she should take the injured hand, Oknodie's small hand grabbed hers firmly.
The adventurers thought:
She was going to forgive us anyway.
The child just needed an excuse.

"This is truly touching."
"Our adventure wasn't in vain after all."
"If that purity isn't a spirit, what is?"
"Right. That child is a spirit."
"Having met a spirit, I have no regrets."

All kinds of praise for pure-hearted people poured toward Oknodie.
Isabel, holding the small but strong hand, found herself smiling genuinely for the first time since the leader's death.

"Spirits truly recognize each other."

The adventurers grinned.
Isabel might not know it, but she was the adventurers' own little spirit.
Many had joined her because they wanted to see the pure smile she used to have when following their leader.
How sad it had been to see Isabel lose her smile.
And now she had found it again.
This would be welcome news for the older adventurers who couldn't challenge the academy due to age restrictions.

"I'm glad we came to take the test."
"Indeed."

The young members of the Esonia Adventure Party departed with their own small enlightenment.
Master Myungho, the examiner of the first gate who had been watching them from a distance, smiled benevolently.

"Those with pure spirits find their own path even in defeat. The future of the Esonia Adventure Party looks bright."

Thirty minutes later, clear bell sounds echoed throughout the collapsed rocky mountain.
The evaluation time for the first gate of the entrance exam had arrived.

* * *

[You have passed the landslide event.]
[You identified the approaching disaster and responded wisely.]
[As a reward, the Intuition function has been unlocked.]
[Observation experience +5]
[Intuition experience +1]

Intuition's warnings are important.
The subconscious instinctively recognizes dangers that the conscious mind cannot perceive.
By simply listening to the warnings of the subconscious, one can overcome unfair elements like landslides.

'I got lucky too. To think I'd find a cook this way.'

She might not be a professional chef, but who cares?
As long as she can cook, that's all that matters.
Good at sourcing ingredients, good at cooking—she's a tier-1 support companion.

"Out of the 500 applicants in this first gate, there were 315 casualties and withdrawals. Therefore, 185 applicants will proceed to the second gate."

Those who relied solely on numbers or were careless due to inadequate skills were largely eliminated.
Of course, the named NPCs all survived and passed, though their dirt-covered faces and torn clothes showed they had been caught in the landslide and suffered hardships.

"I would like to say this to all of you."

Master Myungho struck a bell to wake the applicants from their complacent mindset, his face benevolent.

"Merely praying for salvation is the path of commoners, unsuitable for those who dream of being the best in the world."
"You must not become closed links who dedicate your lives and prayers to small town deities, mountain gods, and minor divine beings."
"That is also why this humble monk left the Eastern Empire and set foot in the center of Western educational institutions."

Master Myungho's test.
It wasn't a test of building stone pagodas to accumulate wishes.
What he desired was not dependence but independence.
His test was a test of autonomy.
The talent that the Gift Academy seeks—the shining Gift—is born from autonomy.

"The first step in polishing the raw gem of shining talent begins with embracing autonomy. Most of you failed to prove this, but you didn't fall in the face of trials."
"Not dying leads to opportunity. The opportunity to transform today's failure into tomorrow's success. So you must prove in the next gate that you are worthy of entering the world's best Gift Academy."

Judging by his words alone, a mass disqualification wouldn't have been surprising, but the generous Master Myungho gave them another chance.
He showed mercy to the applicants who had been desperate just to survive the harsh variables of landslides and earthquakes.

"Of course, there are exceptions."

This was as far as I remembered from the exam evaluation.
Strange lights began to shine around me as I was preparing to move to the next gate.
The subordinates following the examiner started pointing huge light generators with illumination magic at me.

"This child, despite being the youngest applicant in this exam, identified signs of the landslide and wisely positioned herself on flat ground beyond the mountain peak."
"Huh? Me?"
"This child's name is Oknodie! She is the top-ranking applicant to pass the first gate!"

Disbelieving gazes poured in from all directions.
I had only prepared to reduce the drastically increasing probability of elimination (60%) or serious injury (30%) if I failed to notice the landslide.
In a game, this event would have ended with a message like [You avoided serious injury (9.9%)] or [The entire group avoided serious injury (0.1%)], but now there was an unexpected bonus.

"A talent praised by the examiner. How tempting."
"My, what a cute child."

The good-aligned characters.

"That little runt ranked above me?"
"She wasn't just a humble lower noble's pony after all. Tsk. How annoying."

The neutral-aligned characters.

"Looks like she'd break if you kicked her."
"Hehe. I suppose it's about time I changed my pet."

Even the evil-aligned characters.
All the main and supporting characters were focusing on me.
Is this how an introvert feels when they go to see a play, make eye contact with the actors, and get dragged onto the stage?
In a game, I would have said "What are you looking at, scrub?" while flexing my huge muscles, but that's impossible with this small female body barely over 130cm.
Above all, this is reality now, not a game.
Such boldness is impossible for an introvert.

"D-don't look..."

I hid behind Gisele's legs, avoiding their gazes.
Sighs echoed from all around.
Was it because of my shyness?
The gazes I felt on my skin grew even hotter.
