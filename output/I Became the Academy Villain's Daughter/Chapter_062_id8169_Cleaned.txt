# The Pressure of One's Share

Gaining 100 points by abandoning your teammates.
That's a foolish action.
If points were simply the goal, I could have shared them with Isabel, Gisele, and Son O-cheon as a team of four.
This isn't about points.
The goal is to prevent Hestia from being mistreated.

'Hestia is just a child. Her mental state breaks easily.'

She doesn't suddenly turn into a crazed killer when she snaps, but when minor psychological damage accumulates, the stacked trauma eventually bursts and leads to a person's descent into darkness.
Just like Arthur Fleck, the Joker portrayed by Joaquin Phoenix in the movie Joker (2019), who lost all his small joys through countless betrayals and had his will to live destroyed, ultimately walking the path of crime—Hestia goes through a similar process.

'If there were a female Joker, it would be Hestia.'

After surviving a crisis caused by mercenaries' reckless attempts and passing the exam through tremendous hardship, her reputation becomes that of a cowardly berserker who crushed her teammates' skulls to pass alone.
The mercenaries who barely escaped with their lives thanks to her not only fail to protect her honor but make things worse.
The imperial nobles of Group B torment her, seeing her strength as a thorn in their side, while the frontier nobles of Group A refuse to get involved in the affairs of an uncivilized mercenary.
The commoners of Group A are equally unwelcoming due to her reputation for betraying comrades.

'Even her last refuge is a sorry sight.'

The "Answering Door" and the friend beyond the wall.
The sanctuary she trusted was just an illusion.
Room 111 in the original game was vacant.
Her companion wasn't a person but the "Answering Door" mimicking a human.
The door's purpose was merely to use her.
Kindness is repaid with malice, no one protects her from the overwhelming hostility, and even her mental sanctuary turns out to be a false temptation.
With her faith in humanity and happiness fundamentally denied, Hestia's heart darkens, and she truly becomes the murderous berserker everyone feared.
She willfully embodies the derogatory nickname everyone used to mock and insult her.
Instructors who try to stop her at the scene are killed alongside students.
Even professors respond, but they can't stop her astonishing combat prowess alone.

A classmate mass murder incident with danger so powerful it's hard to believe it happens during the first-year midterm exam.
The way to prevent this cruel tragedy is incredibly simple.
Just care for Hestia so she doesn't suffer unjust persecution, and have the player show a little kindness.
Allowing humans to live as humans.
That alone can prevent the tragedy.

"I'm worried about Hestia. Everyone says bad things about her. I want to be in her group."
"I can't help it. If a child wants to do something good, I don't want to discourage them even if I can't actively support them."

Isabel understood my feelings.

"Then we'll form a group with those three people."
"I appreciate it."

Dorothy and Rockfell.
These two people I connected with during the entrance exam formed a group with the three of us.
Thanks to them, I ended up in the principal's lecture as part of the loner group, the dropout group, the leftover group with Hestia, Jigoku, and Lotto.

'Zuang is surprisingly sociable!'

The fox-masked Zuang has an even worse personality than these three, and there are even more sadistic perverts out there, but at least these people could pass as normal.
In contrast, Hestia and Lotto with their critically damaged reputations, and Jigoku who was simply born antisocial, were rejected by everyone.
The result was a bizarre group consisting of Group A's top student Oknodie and the rest.

"Why did she do that?"
"She's just a kid. She probably did it out of kindness."
"Tsk. I can't even comfortably badmouth her. If I say the wrong thing, I'll end up like Lotto."
"How lucky for that big berserker. Thanks to hiding behind a child, she won't get criticized."

Hmph, go ahead and talk behind my back like cowards.

"Our Hestia is so cute. Don't listen to any of those mean things."

I stood on tiptoe trying to reach up to cover her ears, but oh no!
I can't reach Hestia who's over 2 meters tall?
As I was wondering if I should climb on a desk, Hestia put her hands under my armpits and lifted me up smoothly.

"Is this what you wanted to do?"
"No, it's not!"

Who asked for a lift!
When I covered her ears with my hands, Hestia grinned happily.

-Who told you to socialize?
-Groups weren't formed for that purpose!

The principal's stern rebuke startled the students.

"The principal seems to want a serious academic atmosphere. I'm sorry, Oknodie."

Don't treat me like a child disappointed that her piggyback ride is over!

-Group selection exists to cultivate pain, despair, and hatred toward asymmetrical organizational society!

"My goodness!"

The students' eyes were filled with indignation.
That was exactly what Dragon Principal wanted to see.

-This lecture is not for you to walk around facilities open to freshmen to adapt to the academy, nor to communicate with instructors to learn regulations, nor to socialize with students to build friendships.
-It's a trial to make you understand the cruelty of group assignments and grow into excellent, solitary individuals who can accomplish everything alone.

Having said his piece, the principal disappeared with a poof.
The students left behind sat awkwardly in silence throughout the lecture hall, all thinking that whatever just happened seemed like a complete disaster.

"No... I meant dividing areas within the campus..."

How is it possible that none of my three teammates listen to what people say?
If this were any other team, the leader would have every right to abandon such a hopeless group.
But I don't mind since I can manage well enough on my own.
Besides, since I've assigned them tasks, maybe they'll each do their share by the deadline?

'Ah, there's one. And there too. And another one over there.'

I collected flags all over campus, circling through locations I remembered from the game.
