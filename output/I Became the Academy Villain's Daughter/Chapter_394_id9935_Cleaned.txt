# Enhanced Translation: Chapter 394 - Excuses Backfire

Standing before Professor Red Mountain, the practice instructors who had to report their blunders couldn't bring themselves to look up.

"Teaching assistant. Is it true what you claim—that a passing fourth-year student attacked you on your way home at night and stole your key card?"
"It's true. That overwhelming strength could only belong to a fourth-year."
"You're from the Production Department, so your combat ability is weak, right?"
"..."
"Are all the instructors planning to keep their mouths shut like chickens who've eaten honey?"

The instructors tried their best to make excuses.
Of course, their excuses didn't work at all.

"Let me summarize. The teaching assistant was robbed of his key card while walking home alone. The instructors didn't even realize their key card's security had been breached, and the herb garden was completely cleaned out?"
"We're deeply ashamed, Professor..."
"As you should be. As punishment, each of you drink one."

Professor Red Mountain used her tail to open the middle drawer of her cabinet, not the top one.
The instructors' eyes trembled with anxiety, knowing that the lower drawers contained increasingly dangerous potions.
Unlike the labeled top drawer, the middle drawer contained bottles with no indication of their effects.

'Does the professor even know what these are?'
'Why did we make such a stupid key card?'
'That damn assistant, losing to a first-year and getting his key card stolen. We shouldn't have given him a key card in the first place.'

Of course, the instructors who had eagerly handed over the key card to dump tedious work on the assistant wouldn't have withheld it.
Only after confirming that all the instructors had each drunk a potion did Professor Red Mountain close the drawer.

"Huh? Why isn't the assistant drinking one?"
"What further punishment does a guy need who got beaten up by a first-year, passed out, then got caught lying that a fourth-year attacked him because he was embarrassed?"
"Ah..."

The assistant's face was truly a mess of red and blue. His humiliation was complete.
What made it even more embarrassing was that, like the instructors, he had secretly admired Professor Red Mountain, and now he'd completely lost face in front of her.

"Professor, please give me a chance to redeem myself. I can't live with this shame. Wherever I go, I'll be followed by the label of 'the third-year who got beaten by a first-year'!"
"Why state the obvious? You're saying this in front of a professor who now has to be known as someone who keeps a teaching assistant who got beaten by a first-year. At least you know to be embarrassed, which is fortunate."
"Because of me, even your reputation has been...!"

While the instructors only suffered humiliation, Professor Red Mountain had gained valuable information from this incident.

'Quite a shrewd little one. Secretly monitoring what our lab's instructors were doing, immediately identifying the key card as a weakness. Even figured out the weakest assistant's route home.'

Despite having the option to target the second-year students' more valuable greenhouse than the fourth-years', the culprit hadn't even gone near it.
This meant they had the mental discipline to achieve only their targeted goal and cleanly withdraw without getting greedy, despite having the skills to do more.

'They even took the rainbow herb. Based on the traces left in the soil, 23 different status effects were triggered in succession, but they were all blocked within a certain distance.'

They had the discernment and reflexes to immediately detect and defend against various status effects, and could use versatile elemental defense techniques.
No wonder the assistant was helplessly defeated.
But sometimes, exceptional skill leads to unexpected mistakes.

'That very competence triggered the dormant spell.'

Professor Red Mountain activated the control spell on the mana board paired with the rainbow herb.
This would allow the control spell's owner to always detect the location of the dormant spell hidden in the rainbow herb and even force it to permit eavesdropping.

'I'll steal much more from you than what you stole from me, you impudent rookie thief!'

But something was wrong with this mana board's operation.

[SIGNAL LOST]
[Unable to determine target's current location.]
[NO SOUND]
[No sounds currently detected.]

Location unknown.
Audio silent.
All responses completely cut off.
Since the rainbow herb, which required extensive effort to produce, couldn't have been consumed in such a short time, this meant the dormant spell in the rainbow herb was being stored in an environment that blocked external transmission.
Perhaps inside an artifact of relic grade or higher that emitted its own mana.
Considering the value of the rainbow herb, using a special storage container wasn't unusual.
It would have been disappointing if the disciple of the righteous thief hadn't thought of such precautions.

"Tsk. The trail's gone cold."

The tracking had been cut off.
There was nothing to be done until the stolen item was taken out.

'Or so you might think.'

While Professor Red Mountain appeared to give up in front of the instructors and sent them away, she kept the assistant who had been thoroughly beaten by a first-year.

"Poor guy. Is she going to make him drink something from the bottom drawer?"
"The sight must be too horrible for her to show us."
"What if he drinks a potion with a curse that turns him into a water flea? That would be too pitiful."

The horrific scenarios the instructors imagined were playing out identically in the assistant's mind.

'Mom, Dad, I'm sorry. I promised to succeed and return home, but I should have been satisfied with just making it to third year.'

The professor's tail slapped the assistant's trembling arm.

"Why are you shaking so much? If you were that upset about getting beaten, you should have fought back."
"Professor... please don't be so kind to me... you'll make me misunderstand..."

It would be even sadder if she pretended not to make me drink something but then did!
The professor paused at the sight of the suffering assistant.

"Hmm. So the assistant has been looking at me with those eyes?"
"I-I'm sorry!"
"No need. Though I'm not in my prime after what Professor Bronze did to me, I still hold a professorship. It's not unreasonable for you to misunderstand."

The professor was pleased.
Even though she'd lost her charm, there was still a man who looked at her that way!

"Your feelings are admirable, but I'm a professor and you're an assistant. We mustn't cross that line."
"I'm truly sorry!!"
"No need to apologize. Just do me a small favor."

Professor Red Mountain created a tiny totem on the spot.
At first glance it looked like a badge, but it was actually an espionage artifact that neutralized the magical reactions of artifacts the target possessed, enhancing communication performance!

"Attach this to the first-year Oknodie."
"M-m-me!?"
"Heh. No need to be so moved. If you're uncomfortable with physical contact, I'll hand it to you."

She dropped the artifact into the timid assistant's hand, and he nodded with an extremely complicated expression.
The professor thought it was the fresh face of a youth in love, but it was actually the nod of a man who had given up everything after being terrified.
He had already been beaten badly by the student who appeared with Oknodie, and now he was being asked to attach a totem to Oknodie, who might be even stronger.
No matter how he looked at it, this was more difficult than putting a bell on a cat's neck.
But when the professor says jump, an assistant must ask how high!


* * *


'Sob. The professor wants me dead!'

Crying inwardly, the assistant went to find Oknodie's classroom as her lecture was ending.
Thinking it better to get it over with quickly, he went to find her, but was very flustered to see Oknodie surrounded by friends.

'If I approach and speak to someone with so many friends...'

"Oh, hello, senior who got thoroughly beaten up by someone weaker than me!"

Just one greeting like that would spread rumors throughout the school, and he'd become the officially recognized pushover senior of the 981st class.
That would be the end of his academy life.

'I'll follow her. And approach when no one's around!'

There was no guarantee Oknodie would keep his shameful secret, but it was better than being witnessed by third parties and having uncontrollable rumors spread!
But... this child had many lectures.
Beyond just many—even after the sun had set and the moon had risen, she was still attending lectures.

'Is this what a schedule looks like for someone who isn't even a third-year?'

The assistant, who worked until 2 AM for the lazy Professor Red Mountain, thought Oknodie would make a perfect assistant with her diligence.
After attending lectures until late at night, wouldn't she be doing assignments until dawn?
But even after all her lectures ended, Oknodie wasn't alone.

"You're late today, My Lady."
"The lecture ran long!"

A maid, commonly seen around the academy, greeted Oknodie.
However, unlike the beast-kin maids with tails, this maid had no tail visible behind her, which confused the assistant.
Without a tail, she couldn't be a maid working at the academy.
What was this?

"Your mana cultivation method for maid skills has improved greatly. Your posture is proper and your footsteps silent. Now you're ready to learn absolute balance—the ability to not spill a drop of tea from a cup even on the most horrific battlefield strewn with blood and entrails."
"Wow, how exciting!"
"But who is that person following behind you? A friend?"
"!?"
"No? I don't know them!"

The assistant, startled by the chilling conversation, had let his guard down.
Though he quickly focused more on stealth when the maid detected his presence, her eyes were clearly looking at him.
Fearing another unprovoked attack, the assistant hastily dropped his stealth and revealed himself.

"D-don't attack! I didn't come with bad intentions."
"I'm not sure what scheme would make someone follow My Lady, who came alone to meet her maid at this late hour, if not with bad intentions."

As he hesitated and fidgeted with the badge, the maid noticed with sharp eyes.

"Is that badge a gift for My Lady?"
"Ah, that... y-yes, it is..."
"You filthy pedophile."
"N-no! That's absolutely not my intention...!"
"Then what is your intention? Are you trying to harm My Lady?"

A chilling look appeared in her eyes, colder than the night wind.
A sharp dagger had somehow appeared in the maid's hand.

'I came to attach an espionage artifact under the professor's coercion. Would you please let me attach it just once?'

Unable to say that, the assistant could only nod with a resigned face.

"I'm sorry... you're right about the confession... sob. You don't have to accept my feelings, just please take this badge. It would put my mind at ease."

The maid looked at him with contempt, then spoke to Oknodie.

"My Lady. This man's heart is as big as his willingness to offer a gift while crying."
"Yes, it seems so!"
"Though he's despicable, he's pitiful. Perhaps we could spare his life just this once."
"How could you kill someone just for confessing? It might be creepy and unpleasant, like bugs crawling on your skin, but that's not a capital offense!"

Just kill me instead.
The assistant, forced into a fake confession, shed real tears of misery.

"But what do you like about me?"
"I was impressed by your dedication to your studies late into the night."
"You have really strange tastes."

Oknodie looked at him as if she'd spotted a stranger offering candy to follow him.
The assistant's sense of injustice doubled at receiving such a suspicious look from the most suspicious child in the world.
