The second semester had begun.
And Oknodie and Xing had not returned.

"What could be happening at the Foundation?"
"What else? The child's talent is far more exceptional than they expected, so they're keeping her tied down so she can't leave the Foundation."
"How pitiful."
"Did the others who went with her just leave her behind?"
"It's the Foundation. They were probably scared of getting hurt."

It was scary indeed.
But Oknodie had her own wishes.
That's why I could trust her and leave.
But now that things had turned out this way, Arcadia felt regret too.
Should she have brought the child back even if it meant taking risks?
Her hand gripped her gloves tightly.

"Lady Arcadia... don't pay attention to such foolish nonsense."
"Of course. The Oknodie I saw on the cruise ship was not a child who would dance to the Foundation's tune."

That child still showed aspects of being a "good child."
She must have her reasons.
If we wait a little longer, she'll return.
Thus, one day passed after the second semester began, then two.
Early September, the dates flew by as quickly as falling leaves, and a week scattered with the wind.
Arcadia's patience also came to an end with the passing of that week.

"I need to see Year Head Mahabharata."
"The Year Head isn't someone you can meet whenever you want..."
"Tell him to let me in. This isn't just 'whenever.'"

The instructor guarding the entrance to Mahabharata's office reluctantly placed his hand on the office door.
And in that moment, he filled the gap in the [Open] formula with his own mana from the massive mana pattern that appeared, opening the door.
An office where you couldn't even open the door without mastering a considerable level of mana arts!

Creeeeak.
Thud.

As soon as she stepped through the slightly opened door, it closed behind her.
Arcadia, who had barged in with such authority earlier, now timidly rolled her eyes around.
Mahabharata's office was so alien that it was difficult for her to maintain her composure.

"What... is all this?"
"Spirit fish that swim only in high-density mana. By the principal's orders, they're the target of this week's event. They'll appear throughout the academy soon, and if you can withstand the mana pressure and catch the fish, you might receive small blessings from the spirits."

...Another strange gimmick added to the academy.
Still, I should tell my close friends about this after I finish my business.

"Oknodie has been trapped in the Foundation's facility and hasn't returned. Is the academy aware of this fact?"
"Of course. I was the one who received the principal's instructions to infiltrate Professor Ferguson onto the cruise ship."
"What? Ferguson as in that mysterious serial enhancement mage who strengthened the Titoso family's illumination squad... that suspicious person is a professor?!"

The more she thought about it, the more outrageous it seemed.
Despite having a professor present, the hardships they endured were no ordinary trials.

"Then did you also know that Professor Rave from the Empire followed the cruise ship to try to kill Oknodie?"
"I received reports from Professor Ferguson. Initially, we dispatched the professor to secure the students' safety from the Foundation's grasp when we obtained the information, but at that time, I didn't think they would be foolish enough to go that far."
"...I can let that incident pass since it's in the past. But you didn't provide any help during the tragedy of the Apostle of Anrage, which led to casualties."

This was beyond feeling disappointed or resentful.
The students' souls could have been switched.
From the perspective of someone who could have ended up in a fate worse than death, it still makes her blood boil.

"If you weren't going to help at the most critical moment, why did you come at all?"

Professor Mahabharata answered the bold student's question.

"One million points."
"..."
"That's the point penalty I would have to bear for violating the Level 1 Information Non-Disclosure Treaty."
"Sigh. That cursed point supremacy. Even professors can't escape the shackles of points?"
"If you've experienced the Foundation's points, you'll know that the academy's points are much more reasonable."
"If you don't intend to tell me, that's fine. I didn't come here to waste time either."

Instead, Arcadia found a more efficient answer.

"If Oknodie doesn't return and the academy doesn't take appropriate action, I'll go looking for her myself. When that time comes, Professor Mahabharata, you'll see how many students share my thoughts."

The bold declaration against the professor sounded quite rude.
The instructor guarding the entrance glared at Arcadia as she left the office, urging for the professor's instructions.

"If you wish to discipline this impertinent first-year, I'll take care of it personally."
"It's fine. Leave her be. It's natural for children who have gone through such emotional turmoil to be blinded by their feelings."

Professor Mahabharata understood Arcadia's heart.
Students often view professors as disguised demons without blood or tears, but professors aren't such merciless monsters when you get to know them.
Though sometimes they lack the social common sense or normal thinking patterns they should have due to honing their skills to the highest level.
At least Professor Mahabharata wasn't that type of professor.
Rather, he was everyone's guardian who cleaned up after troublemaking professors and restored peace and order to the academy.

"Don't you get tired of always playing the villain? Professor, you're too kind."
"What can I do? She's a first-year. If you're really concerned, go tell that first-year Arcadia that if she wants access to confidential information, she should aim to join the student council."

Even Mandela Castera, the undisputed top student of the 980th class, was clumsy and immature in her first year compared to now.
It was only through time and experience that she transformed into her current self.
Professor Mahabharata believed that Arcadia could undergo a similar transformation.

"Anyway, the principal's counter to the Foundation's 'fishing' has already been implemented. That collector's daughter won't miss the chance to catch spirit fish."

Using force to take Oknodie from the Foundation would be the worst strategy.
The best approach is to make Oknodie herself want to return to the academy by presenting an appetizing bait.
Mahabharata firmly believed that the Dragon Principal's brilliant move would work.
For someone who lives life solely based on interest.
Because they live in the same way, the principal's bait would be even more effective for Oknodie.

'If we're not inferior to the Foundation in material wealth, what will determine Oknodie's whereabouts is quality teaching that cannot be replaced by material things alone.'

The small battle with the Foundation had tilted in the academy's favor.
As Professor Mahabharata was confident in victory, urgent footsteps were heard outside his office.
Though not as extensive as the Dragon Principal's, Professor Mahabharata had a considerable mana field range and was sensitive to approaches that disrupted his concentration.

This guy was a veteran instructor who had been rotting away at the academy for eight years.
He wasn't a fool who would carelessly violate taboos without knowing the value of points.
