# 203 - Finish Line Without a Goal

Zuang was thinking.
There's more than one way to pass the test.
The goal line is the same, but the process of getting there varies greatly.
Otherwise, there wouldn't be so many instructors hiding throughout the forest, watching the students' every move.
Sensitive to presences as an assassin, she began to contemplate the examiner's intentions and recalled the thought process of her colleague friend she'd made at the Academy.

'What would Oknodie do in this situation?'

In her imagination, Oknodie smiled brightly and spouted nonsense.

-I think you should just do whatever you want!

Whatever I want...
Hehe. Telling an assassin to remove her limits and enjoy freely.
I'd get expelled from the Academy.
If I did something like that.
Still, it was helpful.

'So I just need to avoid killing, right?'

Just then, some 2nd-year seniors passed under a tree, checking their wrist watches.

"Is this information reliable?"
"I'm telling you, it is. It clearly showed that there's a 1st-year advanced class student somewhere around here. If we get their advanced class badge, we can pass this damn test immediately!"
"You'll keep your promise, right?"
"Yeah. I'll take the badge and pass, and in exchange, I'll give you all the points I've collected so far. That's our deal."

Zuang spread her arms and dropped from above onto the heads of the seniors who were dreaming of a hopeful future.

"Ambush!?"
"We've been—gack!"

The seniors collapsed instantly without a chance to react.
Zuang grabbed them by the nape and dragged them into the bushes, an undisguisable smile of pleasure appearing on her face.

'Ninety-eight more to go until I reach a hundred.'

Let's just eliminate 100 people lightly.
Zuang's assassination spree, filled with the intention to enjoy herself, began like that.


* *


In Xing's case, he was even more vicious than Zuang.
He was confident in his abilities.
There was no need to hide, avoid, or be cautious.
It didn't matter to him.
He could just cut down anyone who challenged him.
Even Oknodie felt pressured in direct confrontation during the "Principal's Teachings" lecture and avoided him.
Though they never crossed swords directly since they rarely encountered each other, there was no worthy opponent among the 1st-years in close combat except for the hero Ishtar.

This created a thirst within him.

'My sword is getting dull.'

A sword that doesn't taste blood loses its edge.
A sword with too much to protect cannot be bold.

He came to the Academy seeking power for revenge.
Instead of getting stronger, he was getting weaker—putting the cart before the horse.

"Wow. A 1st-year still wandering around here? Do you want to give up and go back to the dorm?"
"For the sake of understanding how juniors feel, I'll give you a chance. Hand over your badge. Then we'll let you go peacefully."
"Heheh. If you don't like it, try fighting back. Of course, we're 3 against 1. We'll attack you unfairly, three against one 1st-year. But we won't feel any guilt about crushing a dirty talent!"

These seniors were refreshingly trashy.
Their appearance was welcome to Xing as well.

"Is that so? Take it then."

Without hesitation, Xing tore off his badge and threw it into the air.
The three 2nd-years looked up in confusion at the badge flying high.
When their eyes came back down with an "oops," one of them had already fallen, cut by Xing's sword.
The student who moved his feet backward along with his eyes could avoid the first strike but couldn't escape the rapid follow-up attacks and collapsed.
The student who moved his eyes, feet, and hands raised his shield to block the sword.

Clang!!

"!!!"

An impact beyond imagination transmitted through the shield.
In the brief moment the shield was kicked up into the air, blood flowed from his ankle, strength left his wrist, and three sword wounds appeared on his abdomen and chest.

Thud thud thud

The 2nd-year, covered in blood, crawled on the ground with tears streaming down his face.
Those weren't tears of pain but tears of despair at the overwhelming talent gap that couldn't be closed even with a year's effort.

"There's no way I could ever catch up to something like this, no matter how many years pass."

Beep.
A red light rose from the wrist watch of the 2nd-year who pressed the retirement button.
It was a warning sign that no further attacks would be permitted.
Xing continued to point his sword without stopping.
The gaze of the instructor stationed nearby also grew fierce.

"How boring."

Xing withdrew his sword that had been aimed at the student's heart.
If he were to fight, that one would be much more interesting.
Although Xing showed killing intent as if to provoke the instructor, the other concealed his presence as if uninterested in students.

"Too boring."

His blade was still hungry for blood.


* *


"Boo. That's not fair."
"......"
"Zuang and Xing are having all the fun by themselves."

Oknodie showed the location information appearing on her magic watch.
The instructor almost burst out laughing, seeing her behavior like a child whose toy had been taken away, but gritted his teeth and held back his laughter.
The evil Oknodie, who threatened even the instructor's professionalism, fell into contemplation.

'Isn't it getting dangerous now?'

It was time for students who had gone to the mount section to find their mounts and start entering the goal line.
Although she desperately wanted to wait for Xing and Zuang, if she remained silent and waited, her ranking would drop.

'If my score falls while I'm concerned about the overall score, that would be troublesome!'

So she decided.
Let's activate the arrangement she had made while breaking the cage that stored the mounts.

"Switch!"

Category - Spatial Magic
Activation - Position Exchange
Specialization - Range Extension

2nd Circle Spatial Magic.
Phase transition magic that exchanges the positions of two objects is typically used by novice magicians when they show a stone hidden in their left hand appearing in their right hand with a "ta-da!"
It's a magic that can be used even at lower circles, but if you make even a slight positioning error, the stone might embed itself in your palm or skin might be torn off—a terrifying magic with unexpected side effects!
In games, it's a shabby magic that causes injuries at certain probabilities or becomes the main reason for being hanged in the town square when magic tricks are exposed.

Flash!

The instructor inwardly scoffed at the sight of a twig in front of Oknodie flashing.

'Is she bored and trying to show me a magic trick turning a twig into candy? That won't impress me. I've seen magic shows by magic department students at school festivals every year!'

Still, let her try if she wants.
A 1st-year's playful act—I'll watch that dedication and potential with both eyes wide open!
A one-sided competition in the instructor's mind, completely unrelated to Oknodie!

As the light faded, the result appeared.
The popcorn in the instructor's hand fell to the ground.

"Yahoo! Success in one try!"
"You, you, what you just switched, that couldn't be...!"
"Oh. Is it because you're an instructor? You recognized it right away?"

The instructor exclaimed in horror.

"What are you doing bringing the finish line here?!"

The 3rd section riding test.
The condition for test-takers riding mounts to "finish."
The finish line, an element that measures records.
A long line with black and white mixed in a checkered pattern appeared at Oknodie's feet, exchanged with the twig.
How could such a thing happen in this world!
Stealing the finish line.
And right in front of the instructor's eyes!
As the instructor was about to scold her severely, Oknodie puffed her cheeks and made a "Boo~ I'm upset!" pose, saying impudently:

"There was no rule saying I couldn't bring it here with magic!"

The instructor, incredulous, picked up his communication magic device and asked Professor Platon, the examiner.

"The student claims this... What should we do, Examiner... What? She's right? That's even more impressive because she did it? No, don't laugh when your own test is in shambles!"

After arguing angrily during the communication, the instructor removed his hand from his ear, drained.

"What did they say?"
"The test continues."
"See? It works, right?"

The instructor felt sorry for the 981st class students.
He could clearly see their future of suffering under that devilish little girl in every test throughout their Academy years.
There is no rule that says you can't cast teleportation magic on the finish line.
That's because it shouldn't be possible to cast teleportation magic on it in the first place.

'The activation condition for teleportation magic is to perfectly analyze the structure and transmit it to another location.'

If you embed magical formulas that are difficult to read in the analysis process, making "perfect" analysis impossible, spatial teleportation magic shouldn't activate in the first place.
Even he, as an instructor, wouldn't dare attempt something outside his specialty, yet a 1st-year student had gone ahead and done it.
This was real magic, different from the shabby magicians no better than street performers.
Professor Platon was right.
What she did was impressive.

'So what about the students who went to where the finish line was originally? What happens to them?'

Sweat beaded on the instructor's face.

Scola reluctantly put aside her regrets.

"You're saying I shouldn't snipe?"
"Oknodie won't reach the goal line for quite some time anyway. Rather than waiting to attack her, it's more beneficial to cross the goal line first."

With the holy maiden Yupi canceling the mount sniping request and advising to prioritize grades, there was no reason to waste her own score by shooting arrows at a golem.
An excellent archer is also skilled at riding, and her pursuit skills need no explanation.
Having easily found her mount, Scola sprinted at full speed toward the goal line.

"Hmph. Stupid Oknodie. She probably never dreamed I'd cross the river using warp arrows without even swimming."

I'll make her suffer the humiliation of giving up her ranking without even seeing my face.

[This is the goal!]
[Juniors, hang in there!]
[Good job on the midterm exam.]
[Professor Platon's protein soup to boost energy can be received at the right tent.]

Having crossed the goal line, Scola was inwardly excited.

-Who's that?
-I heard it's a 1st-year student named Scola.
-Wow, impressive. She came in at a really high rank.
-She'd be perfect for the hero's party.
-I'm jealous. I want to ask her to join our party too.

The admiring gazes of seniors unfolding in her imagination!

"Hey, 1st-year."
"Hmph. I'm afraid I can't give autographs, senior."
"What are you talking about? The race isn't over yet. You can't come in for protein soup when the test isn't finished."

Scola's face twisted in dismay.

"I wasn't going to eat it. I had no intention of eating it. I vowed never to eat that tasteless soup again."

As Scola grumbled, a strange feeling dawned on her face.

"Um, senior? I think I just heard something strange. What do you mean the race isn't over yet?"
"Completion of the riding section is measured by running from the starting line to the finish line. Didn't the instructors tell you?"

Scola looked back at the goal line she had just crossed.

"What is this?"

On the ground, instead of the goal line that should have been there, there was only an unsightly twig trampled by the mount's feet.
