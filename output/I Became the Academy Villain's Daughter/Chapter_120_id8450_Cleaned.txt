# Enhanced Translation: Magic Practice Results

Professor Weird is often called the crazy woman with flowers in her hair, the primal madness of nature, or the man-eating plant that devoured a fourth-year student.
Despite these controversies, she teaches the first-year course on Basic Understanding of Mana Usage because she's excellent at establishing fundamentals for students, regardless of her questionable personality.

"Student Rogini, you've gathered too many flame attribute mana pieces. The vines must be quite hot now, so don't touch them carelessly."
"Student Sandcooker, you have many earth attribute mana pieces. Right now, the vines crumble easily like sand, but if you add appropriate amounts of water and fire attributes, they'll become as hard as pottery that's been moistened, well-formed, and fired."
"You have no talent. No specific properties have manifested, and your amplification level is insignificant. Still, if you work hard, you might learn the skill of making sugar water, so keep trying diligently."

The student who was definitively labeled as having no talent, with tears welling up in their eyes, insisted it couldn't be true and repeatedly cast "Amplify! Amplify!" while receiving sympathetic glances from others.
Though harsh, magic is a field heavily dependent on talent, so such straightforward assessments were better.
The truly frightening professors are those who coddle the incompetent, encouraging them with false hope, taking them on as apprentices only to use them as errand runners, apprentice disciples, or junior assistants.

'Talent is determined by how good your attribute mana affinity is, how well you can visualize magic, and how powerful your magic manifestation is, so effort isn't completely wasted...'

With diligent effort, one can accumulate empirical knowledge to replace scientific understanding and improve output by forming images based on concrete experiences and directly observed phenomena.
However, if the power and intensity of magic manifestation are inherently low, the same spell will yield weaker results.
If one cannot gather attribute mana well, they'll likely remain at the level of non-attribute daily magic or end their career as a magic bullet wrapper.

"Student Oknodie has many dark attribute mana pieces."

Professor Weird had somehow arrived at my desk.
She stared down at the blackened vine.

"A great many, in fact."

When she poked the vine with her short staff, black smoke billowed out.

"Extraordinarily, tremendously many."

Dorothy, who was sitting next to me, looked over with concern.

"Is that bad?"
"Not at all. I'd say it's a blessing. From what I can see, your dark attribute affinity must be at least 50%, which is truly remarkable."
"Wow. Oknodie has talent?"
"In terms of single-attribute affinity, she's beyond students Rogini and Sandcooker."

As the professor lingered at my desk, showering me with praise, gazes from all directions converged on me.

"Isn't it just your impression? Maybe I got lucky."

Character attribute affinity is also a random element that one is born with.
I might genuinely have high affinity, or it could be what they call a fluke.

"Then try casting magic on this water."

<Sweetness Amplification>

The clear water in the cup turned pitch black.
It was obviously water filled with dark attributes.
I felt like something that shouldn't have been amplified was amplified along with the sweetness.

"It might just look that way!"

Professor Weird took out a dropper and attempted to draw up some of the black water.

Bubble bubble

Ignoring the strange sound coming from the dropper, Professor Weird dropped a single drop onto the blackened thorny vine beside it.
The vine suddenly raised its head and clung to the dropper, absorbing all the moisture.

Slurp slurp

Looking at the vine that had grown about half a segment larger, the professor exclaimed in admiration:

"You've created a dark nourishing tonic!"
"What's that, Professor?"
"It's a kind of attribute enhancer that boosts and strengthens dark attribute life forms. Naturally, it's dangerous to other attribute life forms, so Student Dorothy, don't drink it."
"I wouldn't drink it even if you told me to! Such an obviously dangerous liquid."
"Student Oknodie too!"
"What about mixing it into food?"
"If you want to kill someone, you could, but the components would be detected in the corpse, and a mana autopsy would quickly reveal Student Oknodie's crime!"
"I just wanted to fill my food compendium, I wasn't intending to poison anyone!"

Anyway, it was personally disappointing.
When practicing wand magic at Jonna's mansion, I mainly practiced gathering and releasing mana, so I hadn't actually manifested it.
I knew how dangerous it was to use magic carelessly outside of Gift Academy.
That's why my expectations were even higher.
I wondered which attribute I would be strongest in.
Ice magic.
Fire magic.
I thought any attribute would be fine.
But specializing in dark magic was, no matter how I looked at it, too much.

"Student Oknodie is taking Professor Sadako's lecture, right?"
"That lecture is about adventurer's night activities!"
"Originally, black magicians and necromancers prowl around at night, so taking night activity basics is appropriate. It was an excellent course selection considering your future."
"..."

Was my tech tree being mapped out without my knowledge?!
A novice player would be shocked by this.
Of course, being a veteran, I knew this already.
But knowing doesn't make it feel any better.
Everyone has gameplay styles they like and dislike.
As someone who preferred to smash things with straightforward physical attacks, I didn't particularly like shadow minions who used dark mana.
Above all, dark mana is treated as an ominous power used by mages who have made pacts with demon tribes.

"Oknodie will grow up to be an excellent black magician."
"I'm going to be a magic swordsman!"
"Yes. When the professor was young, my dream was to become a world tree."

Sigh. I really am going to be a magic swordsman!

"Anyway, Student Oknodie should train to deliberately mix other attribute mana to suppress the dangers of using magic. Dark mana is inherently an ominous attribute where the same spell can lead to unpredictable results."

"Why does Oknodie have such high dark affinity?"
"That's a question showing the typical qualities of a magic scholar—no talent but high academic enthusiasm."
"..."
"The reason for high dark attribute affinity is a close relationship with darkness or death."
"Death?"
"Death is eternal darkness. A return to eternal darkness where one can never see light again. It's the most intimate act that raises dark attribute affinity."
"So you think Oknodie's dark attribute affinity increased due to environmental factors?"
"That's highly likely."

Professor Weird explained.

Either she saw many dead people.
Or grew up playing in cemeteries as a child.
Or experienced battlefields, villages ravaged by epidemics, or eras of great famine.

"Or perhaps she killed many living beings. Such death-friendly experiences would increase affinity."

Professors specializing in necromancy are blessed.
Unlike the professor who looked at Oknodie with envy, the students were gripped with fear.

"Come to think of it, I've heard rumors that Oknodie might have been raised as an assassin in a noble family."
"Killing many living beings?"
"She killed enough people to reach that level of affinity?"

Oknodie is scary!
Dorothy regretted asking the question publicly when she saw the fearful reactions of the students.
If she had known this would happen, she would have visited the professor's classroom later to ask privately.
But it was too late for regrets.
The students who heard the rumors were busy gossiping about Oknodie.

"Everyone, stop talking carelessly about Oknodie!"

Dorothy was angry.
Oknodie was being unfairly criticized because of her.
She felt the need to actively clarify things here for the sake of her friend's honor.

"The professor said it too, right? Dark attribute affinity rises a lot if you kill people."
"Killing people isn't everything. She said it rises if you live closely with death."
"But what about assassination training?"
"That's just a rumor, not confirmed fact! Besides, Oknodie is young. Even if the rumors were true, would they allow actual field work to such a young child?"

Sandcooker, an apprentice magician from the Yellow Tower who had some social experience outside, expressed agreement.

"They don't allow children or apprentices to practice freely. They make them train with preliminary work for years."

Rogini, an apprentice magician of red magic, asked:

"Then what preliminary work does an apprentice assassin do?"

Students cautiously offered their opinions.

"Maybe transporting corpses?"
"Or cleaning murder weapons?"
"Or burying bodies in the mountains?"

Silence fell over the classroom.
The students' feelings were complicated—both pity and fear.
