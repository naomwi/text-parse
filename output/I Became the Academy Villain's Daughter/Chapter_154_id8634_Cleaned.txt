'He ran away.'

If she had known this would happen, she would have made the first move like Professor Destroyer.
Because she hadn't expected him to bail out, she missed the opportunity to get revenge by confronting and tormenting the child who might have become her apprentice.

"Heheh."

"First-year Oknodie. You seem very pleased to have met your guardian."
"Yes!"
"It's unfortunate that your guardian left early. Today is the practical evaluation day where you show off the skills you've learned to the guardians who are observing the lecture."

At those words, Titosoga, who was standing next to Oknodie, was startled.

"Whaaat! How could you not notify us in advance about something so important!"

Seeing Titosoga flustered, her guardian—a round man shaped like a bowling ball—laughed heartily and said:

"Don't worry, Titosoga. My baby has poor judgment, so this father will pick out clothes for you, choose a house for you, and even select a husband for you!"
"Papa... I'll choose my own husband!"
"Hohoho. Of course you will. I'm sure our daughter has a good eye for men, if nothing else. Just like your mother had when she chose this father."

All the students made sour faces, whether their parents were present or not.
Those with parents thought it was excessive to be such a daughter-obsessed parent, while those without felt it was both burdensome and enviable, wishing he would tone it down.
Facing the critical gazes pouring from all directions, Titosoga boldly snorted as if to say "what are you going to do about it?"

"Hmph."

Titosoga had fallen out with the other students in the Eye for Quality class after the additional assignment incident.
After standing up for everyone only to receive criticism, Titosoga had hardened her heart and become a brave child who no longer cared about others' opinions.

"My baby Titosoga, get a perfect score and crush the other students' grades!"
"Papa. Could you please stop cheering... it's getting embarrassing..."

Even the brave Titosoga had to bow her head in embarrassment at her doting father's behavior.
Professor Bronze, who had been momentarily distracted by the impressive daughter-obsessed father, regained her focus and introduced today's practical exam.

"This lecture's practical test will score you based on how accurately you can appraise the value of items. Since every student will answer every question, this test requires precise judgment, so prepare yourselves thoroughly."

The other guardians, though not as excessive as Titosoga's doting father, also encouraged their students with gleaming eyes.

"Don't record worse grades than commoners. Show the pride of our noble family, Charles Donuts."
"Don't waste the value of the art pieces decorating our home that cost a thousand gold coins to cultivate your artistic eye. I want to believe my investment in you isn't a sunk cost, Marinda."

The guardians showed excessive enthusiasm befitting families and organizations that sent students to the world's best educational institution!

"Press the buzzer and answer correctly, press the buzzer and answer correctly, press the buzzer and answer correctly..."
