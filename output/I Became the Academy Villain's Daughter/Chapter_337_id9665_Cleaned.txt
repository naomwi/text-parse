# 337 - A Test Where Survival Is All That Matters

Jaidas insisted on the goal of allied victory, but that was a foolish thought that missed the essence of the test.
Should I say he made the common rookie mistake of misunderstanding the passing conditions?

Winning on the battlefield.
Defending the fortress until the main force arrives.
These are objectives presented by the commander-in-chief, not the conditions for passing the test.

In short, they're what's called "challenge tasks."
Elements that give bonus points if achieved.
Even if you accomplish the challenge tasks, it means nothing if you die in the process.

The title of this lecture is <Surviving as a Commander on the Battlefield>.
Survival is the top priority.
Also, you must not lose your position as a <commander>.
You must maintain a minimum number of troops to keep your title and command them.
These are the conditions to keep in mind for the turn-based war simulation strategy test.
If you understand these basics, the range of strategies you can employ becomes much wider.

"Hestia is indeed smart, as expected of an active mercenary!"

Unlike Jaidas, the honor-focused holy knight who insisted on an honorable victory, Hestia, despite having the berserker class, avoided death on the battlefield like a ghost avoiding fatal situations.
You should only fight battles worth fighting. On a chaotic battlefield like this, fighting recklessly at any time would be disastrous.
If enemies all swarm you while allies just wait until you fall to claim the credit, how could you live with such injustice?
