The supervisor appeared again with a smiling face.

"Let's talk. I think we have a lot to discuss about Oknodie."
"You're more than welcome."

The supervisor felt completely relieved, like a painful tooth had been pulled.
Oknodie's presence had been burdensome.
Because the Clown Mask Gang had brought in a magical beast too powerful for them to handle, tremendous attention was now focused on the area.
Luring orphans? Human trafficking?
They couldn't even dream of it.
If they boldly committed crimes while all this attention was on them, mercenaries and territorial soldiers would swarm in until not a trace of the organization remained.

"The deadline to return Oknodie to the foundation was three days ago. We've been considerate of the foundation's position, but it's troublesome when you act like this."
"I do feel sorry about it. But what can we do when it's what the child wants?"

The supervisor thought to himself.
It wasn't his will keeping her at the branch, but Oknodie's own desire.
Please, just persuade Oknodie and take her away.
Shouldn't an Academy professor be able to do that?
He wanted to exclude himself from all troublesome problems and pass the responsibility to the student-teacher relationship.
Call it the cowardly survival tactic of an adult avoiding responsibility if you want.
He would welcome anything if it meant freedom from Oknodie.

"...Oknodie. Is what he's saying true?"
"Yes! Can't I stay a little longer? Please? I'm begging you."

Oknodie was pleading like a child who went on a picnic and didn't want to go home.
To someone who didn't know the situation, she might look adorable and heartwarming, but to Destroyer, who had firmly decided to scold her, this came as a shock.

'It was only ten days. What on earth did they do to Oknodie in just ten days?'

He knew better than anyone how strong-willed she was, having taught her personally.
He had witnessed with his own eyes the strength of the foundation's "master" by entering her nightmares directly.
That's why he had his own conviction about how important they considered Oknodie's "graduation."

But the policy had changed.
The foundation was trying to delay Oknodie's graduation.

'Their intentions have diverged. There are different factions.'

A faction that wanted Oknodie to grow.
And conversely, a faction that wanted her identity.
The man before him belonged to the faction that wanted her identity.
He avoided responsibility by claiming it was Oknodie's wish.
But what could a child know to be so stubborn?
The Clown Mask Gang.
In that den of filthy criminals.
What joy and pleasure could a child find helping with their work?
They were notorious human traffickers.
They were also infamous for their harsh child abuse education.

An early assassin training institution.
The Thieves' Guild had thoroughly investigated their true nature.
And confirmed it.
The training methods Oknodie often showed at the Academy.
The techniques she used to teach students.
The acrobatic training all originated from here.
Yes.
The Clown Mask Gang was the educational institution that taught Oknodie the basics of assassination.
She was forced to stop her studies by the foundation's will and was now taking care of the work of the human trafficking organization that had abused and tormented her.
Yet the reason this man in a tailcoat mentioned Oknodie's own will could only be one thing.

Mockery.

-We've already dominated Oknodie's body and mind. Do you think a mere Academy teacher can take this child away from our foundation's influence?

Though he wore a smiling face expressing relief from all worries, behind it was only cruel pleasure sneering that they could do nothing.

You can't do anything.
Give up.
Go back.

Though retired, he was once a member of a hero party that traveled the world.

"If words don't work, there's always the option of taking her by force. Would you still be smiling then?"
