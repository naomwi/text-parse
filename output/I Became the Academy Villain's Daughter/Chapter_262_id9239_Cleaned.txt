# 262 - Reasons to Step into a Trap

This is madness.
This is absolutely insane.
Zuang calculated the difficulty level of this special training.

Master Rung.
Zuang's teacher.

This difficulty corresponded to level 8 in the assassination training she had received directly from him.
A level where one's life is at stake.
If you make a mistake, you'll be grabbed by the incredibly strong hands of a mimic moving at an unmanageable speed and either dragged into the stair blocks or thrown off.
From a staggeringly high staircase with a hundred steps.
And between stairs that trigger something terrible when stepped on.
Even if you're thrown normally, failing to break your fall could result in broken bones or, in the worst case, instant death from a broken neck—and these stairs also contained unknown traps.
You would undoubtedly suffer terribly.
Just looking at the treasure chests, it was clear that several doors in Professor Bronze's secret basement resembled treasure vaults.
I don't even want to know what vicious traps might be hidden on the stairs leading to the world's greatest gentleman thief's treasure chamber.

"Oh, right."

Professor Bronze threw a throwing knife at one of the stairs.
—Whoosh!
Immediately after, a large hammer descended from the ceiling, passed through at torso height, and retracted back into the ceiling.

"If you step on any stair other than the ones you stepped on while coming here, traps like that will activate. Of course, this is just one example, and I'll keep the types of traps secret for the joy of discovery."
"Professor, that's mean. No matter what, Tito got hurt too badly."

When Oknodie expressed her dissatisfaction in a slightly lowered voice, Professor Bronze let out a light "hmph" and smiled.

"There's no treasure without risk. Always be suspicious. That's the seventh rule of the Thief's Ten Commandments, so remember it well."

It's only natural that if you open a box and benefit from it, there would be corresponding dangers.
Though Oknodie didn't hide her dissatisfaction with Professor Bronze's principle, she reluctantly accepted it with a nod.

"May I check on Tito's condition before we start?"
"When you start is up to you. But you can only leave after completing the assignment."

Professor Bronze ostentatiously twirled the basement door key on her finger.

"As always, the rules are the same. Either complete the assignment and leave with dignity, escape stealthily like a proper thief, or become my top disciple and gain formal access."

Oknodie approached Zuang and asked:

"How's Tito's condition?"
"Mild concussion. Impaired judgment. Some bruising on his limbs, his directly grabbed wrist bones are healing with potion, and he's probably developed a fresh case of mimic phobia."
"Tito. Are you okay? Do you recognize me?"
"Hic... sniff sniff... Oknodie..."
"I'm sorry, Tito. I shouldn't have brought you here. Were you scared?"
"I wanna leave... don't wanna watch... wanna go home..."

Professor Bronze took a savory puff of her magic cigarette, unconcerned by the gazes of Oknodie and Zuang.

"Can we stop observing the special training?"
"If first-years' enthusiasm only goes this far, there's nothing I can do. It's a shame. You may go."
"Thank you."

After thanking her and turning to leave, Zuang quickly realized an awkward fact.

"...How do we get out?"
"That way."

The professor pointed to the stairs.
Zuang asked:

"Tito. Do you remember which stairs were safe on the way here?"
"Not at all..."
"Oknodie."
"I know the way, but it won't work."
"Why?"
"The professor showed us the key, remember? The door is locked."

Zuang realized.
They were properly trapped in the gentleman thief's trap.
When the professor gave permission to leave, she didn't mean she would personally carry them out beyond the stairs.

"Is there no safe path?"
"You are not students observing my special training. Rather, you're first-years who have illegally entered an unauthorized area. I'm already showing humanitarian consideration toward first-years by not punishing you."

At the professor's cold words, Tito sobbed, but Zuang stroked his head to comfort him.

"Stop crying. The professor is right. It was disrespectful of us to request to observe the successor training of a rare class passed down to only one person. The professor making you experience pain is a warning not to harbor curiosity with half-hearted determination. We should be grateful to leave alive."
"Sniff... I understand..."
"Just rest a little longer. We'll have to go back via those stairs anyway. When Oknodie finishes her training, we'll return together. Oknodie will know which stairs are safe."

At Zuang's glance, Oknodie nodded.
As if saying to leave it to her.
And so began Oknodie's training assignment: "Find the mimic disguised as a basement stair and trap it in a box."


* * *


The traps were truly relentless.
Just when you think it might be safe, arrows fly from the wall with a clatter.
When her foot touched a stair edge while deflecting them, part of the stair sank in like a switch with a click, and solid steel rods shot up from the floor.
The more you panic, the more easily you trigger chain traps, leading to combo traps that can cause serious injury.
In a game, this would be a special dungeon.
A daily quest dungeon accessible twice a week.

<Bronze Professor's Underground Treasure Vault Stairs ③>

The difficulty level that the professor had been gradually increasing according to my level seems to have risen about 3 stages.

'I used to diligently do this event because you could earn points and one treasure each time.'

I know you suffer terribly if you trigger a trap.
But I can't pass without triggering them either.
The mimic is disguised as a stair.
To find it, I need to check each stair one by one.

'Tch. Why did Tito and Zuang have to come?'

I accepted partly thinking it was a likability event, but I was also curious whether my companions could earn points too.
It was impossible in the game, but now it feels like we've entered a reality modeled after the game.
But Tito got seriously hurt.

'I could take my time and search carefully, but I don't have that luxury now.'

Some traps release paralyzing gas.
There are tools to collect that gas.
The proper strategy is to step only on safe stairs, spray paralyzing gas on each stair, and then extract the mimic while it's disoriented and put it in the box.
But for Tito to recover without aftereffects, we need to get out quickly and send him to a healer at the medical wing.
Zuang's special ointments and potions are effective, but a concussion is no minor injury.
It could lead to permanent damage.
While <Trauma: Mimic Phobia> might be brushed off as a joke, minor yet critical physical abnormalities like <Sensory Impairment>, <Intermittent Paralysis>, or <Frequent Blinking> could easily develop.
I can't waste time when he could be properly treated.
Above all, Tito is my friend.
I can't ignore a friend who got hurt while coming to watch my special training.

"Fool. You'll get hurt too."
"I know that, okay? Be quiet, Second Generation Hat Guy."
"I don't understand why you act like such a good kid. What are you trying to become?"

That's right.
In the original daily quest, you fail if you step on three traps.
I guess I can't call myself a veteran gamer anymore.


* * *


Normal stairs, empty stairs, trap stairs.
If you find the pattern, you can locate the mimic stair.
From the explosions of traps, Zuang discovered a mathematical pattern.

"Okno..."
"Shh."

Professor Bronze warned:

"Oknodie must realize it herself. It's my policy not to forgive interference with teaching."
"...Is it okay if your top disciple gets hurt?"
"Don't you think a professor would have the healing means that even a first-year like you possesses?"

Zuang was even more dumbfounded.

"Then could you heal Tito too?"
"I could."
"But you won't because he's an illegal intruder who refused to observe?"
"You have a good memory."

Fortunately, the exhausted Tito had fallen asleep.
If he had known his lecture professor could be this cold-hearted, he would have gone beyond sniffling and hiccupping to full-on wailing in sorrow.
Having heard Tito's three-stage crying before due to the Skeleton Instructor's intimidation, Zuang didn't want to hear that sorrowful crying again—the kind that planted guilt even in an assassin's cold heart.
Oknodie surely felt the same way.
She also attended Professor Sadako's lectures.
Perhaps that's why the trap activation speed kept increasing.
Oknodie was getting injured bit by bit.

Blood flowed from her foot as she stepped on the bottom of a bar trap that activated at an unavoidable timing.
Her hand swelled red as she smashed a suddenly opening stair with her fist.
She blocked a barrage of throwing knives with her school cloak, but a few grazed her, causing blood to drip from the back of her hand.
Still, Oknodie kept climbing.
In the brief moment Zuang looked away, blood was flowing from her head and she was limping on one leg.

"Shouldn't the test be stopped at this point?"
"Oknodie? Stop the test? Hmph. You may be that child's friend, but you're not her teacher. You don't even understand why she's getting hurt."
"...Are you saying she's not getting hurt because of the test?"
"Oknodie has already identified the location of the mimic stair."
"So she's deliberately stepping on traps?"

Zuang's eyes widened.
Why would she do such a foolish thing?
It's not something one would do for their own safety.
It could only be for someone else.
For Tito.

'She's breaking the traps in advance so that Tito won't fall into them when climbing the stairs together.'

If I hadn't insisted on observing Oknodie's special training, Tito wouldn't have been hurt, and Oknodie wouldn't be getting hurt either.
Zuang's hand, holding the hand of the exhausted and sleeping Tito, tightened with pressure.
