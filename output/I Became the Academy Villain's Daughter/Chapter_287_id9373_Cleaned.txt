# 287 - The Scary Child's Friend

"Titosoga. Other side of the corridor."
"Uwaah! Level 3 beam!!!"
"Argh! I can't see anything!"

In the narrow ship corridor, Titosoga's lighting device demonstrated the power of one against a hundred.
While Irene the wizard projected firepower in one direction, Titosoga excellently blocked the onslaught from the opposite side with just a single lighting staff.

'Actually, do we even need Titosoga? Wouldn't just that +5 reinforced lighting staff be enough...?'

Irene dismissed such thoughts as ice arrows flew from her fingertips.
An ordinary student would find it difficult to cause harm when facing so many people.
The psychological hurdle between students competing for grades and causing harm to crew members in a shipboard rebellion is vastly different.

Tap tap

"Eek! W-what are you doing? Please don't suddenly stick ice arrows in my back..."
"We're moving. Follow me."
"Hmph... I understand you're calling me with magic because the light prevents us from facing each other, but this makes my clothes wet..."

It's not that she lacks psychological resistance or fear.
Titosoga clearly looks like a coward.
A child who screams "Eek!" or "Hyak!" whenever ice touches her.
You can tell just by those cute reactions.
This is the result of steeling her resolve enough to suppress her innate timidity.

'Did Oknodie and Arcadia notice that such a timid child could harbor this much courage?'

In the North, where even boys barely over ten and old men are given spears alongside strong young men to work the frozen, barren northern farmland.
Even in the land where Irene was born and raised, people are drafted into battle regardless of who they are.
But a fragile child like Titosoga would be out just for being female, out again for her thin limbs, and out once more for her cute screams.
They wouldn't even send her to the front lines, maybe just using her as an errand runner to carry stones for siege operations or to make up numbers.
In that sense, Oknodie's decision to entrust her with important matters and Arcadia's insight in keeping her as a confidant could be considered superior to Irene's in some way.

"Waaah! Irene, don't leave me behind~!"

Though she looks like nothing more than a pathetic underling as she scurries after Irene with her small feet, even such a child can wield a dazzling lighting staff to defeat enemies.
From the beginning, that lighting staff wasn't as easy to handle as one might think.

'It's consuming an enormous amount of mana.'

As a wizard, she could tell.
If an ordinary person with insufficient mana were to casually grasp Titosoga's lighting staff, they would quickly collapse from anemia.
A properly trained wizard wouldn't even want to use equipment that drains their mana to convert it into light.
This was equipment for magic users.
It was meant for half-baked individuals with plenty of mana but no knowledge of how to use magic.

"Oh no! The corridor ends here. This floor is extremely wide with many stairs, so I can't block everything with the lighting staff!"
"I'll create ice walls. We can block all paths with reflected light, so don't lower the lighting intensity."
"Okay! I'll do my best!"

Titosoga had fulfilled her role sufficiently.
Now it was Irene's turn.
Irene headed toward the control room while casting ice magic as flashily as possible to draw aggro throughout the ship.
A facility that could freely open and close all systems on the cruise ship.
If they could just infiltrate that place, they could lock up the crew members and easily free the students from the prison.

"In three years, your skills would be quite promising. But you were too hasty. It's beyond your current abilities. Both Themis and Chris within me are saying the same."

The apostle of Anrage, the god of corruption, who freely utilized the experiences of numerous personalities.
He was strong.
Strong enough to remind one of Academy professors.
The Wiheomhae Foundation, comparable to the Academy, would certainly have individuals of similar caliber.
Important facilities like the control room would definitely have at least one such person.

"I advise against resistance. If you wish to enter the underground prison with your body intact, come along quietly. If the pacifist Forcep's personality retreats, the next to emerge will be the flesh-eating Gustave."

The apostle of Anrage.
An executive of the Foundation.
He was certainly strong.
Strong enough to easily subdue both Irene and Titosoga alone.
However, Irene, who had anticipated the appearance of such a powerful opponent, wouldn't have recklessly charged in head-on without a plan.

Boom.
Click. Click. Click.

Darkness suddenly fell as all the lights in the facility went out.
The already dimly lit corridors, which had been darkened to enhance Titosoga's lighting staff, were now completely engulfed in darkness.

"Is this from the control room...?"

While the apostle of Anrage was escorting Irene to prison, Xing had infiltrated the now-empty control room.
He had successfully completed his mission by seizing the opportunity as planned.
Now all the students in the underground prison were freed, and the crew members were thrown into chaos.

"Now!"

In the confusion, enemies suddenly appeared from the darkness and attacked the escort team.
Even the apostle of Anrage frowned, releasing his grip on Irene's arm to block the attack.

Whoosh!
A sensation wrapped around his wrist.
It was a thin string.

"This is... a bikini? Why a bikini...?"

While the apostle of Anrage was distracted examining the object wrapped around his hand, Pyoi opened a nearby ventilation duct and pulled Irene and Titosoga in.

"Phew. Good thing an opportunity came up!"
"Weren't you at the desert island auction?"
"I had to forfeit because we were attacked!"
"Auctions sure are eventful."
"Was the shipboard rebellion successful?"
"Up to phase two."

Phase one: Create a disturbance.
Phase two: Fake infiltration of the control room.
Everything had gone according to plan so far.
However, the truly important facilities were elsewhere.
Namely, the helm that controlled the ship.
And the captain's quarters where the cruise ship's commander stayed. The shipboard rebellion couldn't succeed without taking control of these two facilities.

Foundation executive Jonna Wiheomhae.
Another executive, the apostle of Anrage.
Now that they had drawn out two executive-level powerhouses, the chances of successfully conquering the captain's quarters and helm had greatly increased.
But the apostle of Anrage would surely know this as well.

"We need to head to the underground prison."

It would be good to go back and restore power to the control room, but if they went to defend the control room and captain's quarters, they would almost certainly fail to occupy those facilities.
If they gathered the few available students and combined their firepower, they might stand a chance.
The power increase from adding students would be much greater than adding crew members to the apostle of Anrage's side.

"Thank you, Irene! If it weren't for you, I might have seriously considered breaking my fingers!"
"Titosoga, you're our lower class hero!"

The lower class students welcomed them with intense joy.
However, Titosoga's expression wasn't good.

"What's wrong? Why such a gloomy face?"
"Hnnng."
"You're even crying!?"
"Maybe she's shocked by how ugly you are."
"Seriously...?"

Fortunately for the student shocked by his own ugliness, Titosoga wiped away her tears with her hands and denied it.

"That's not it... I lost my lighting staff..."
"What!? Your lighting staff again!?"

Irene apologized with a shamefaced expression beside her.

"It was taken when we were defeated by the Foundation executive. If I had been just a little stronger, I could have bought time for Titosoga to escape... It happened because of my inadequacy."
"How is that Irene's fault... It's because the Foundation executive was too strong."

Titosoga stopped crying and made a resolution.

"I'll definitely get my lighting staff back. I can't lose the precious gift my sister gave me!"

Apart from Titosoga's resolution, the lower class students were curious.

"Even if silkworm moths have a habit of stealing shiny objects, why would a Foundation executive steal a lighting staff?"
"Yeah. What would they use it for?"

From the perspective of the lower class students who didn't yet know about the lighting staff's enhancement, it was a reasonable question.


* * *


The crew members who had been crawling on the floor or clinging to ice walls in the corridor for quite some time because of a single lighting staff wore miserable expressions as they wrapped bandages around their hands.

"Spreading cold waves that cause frostbite wherever you touch... That was truly a vicious combination technique."
"I never thought a lighting staff could become such a fearsome item."
"How can such a small lighting staff produce light equivalent to a searchlight?"

A magical engineer cautiously approached the apostle of Anrage.

"Would it be alright if we analyzed that lighting staff?"
"One finger."

Regret flashed across the face of the magical engineer who had spoken. The pacifist personality was nowhere to be found, replaced by the flesh-eating Gustave's personality.
Although the magical engineer had to sacrifice a finger on the spot for carelessly speaking up, his eyes gleamed with curiosity and passion for the artifact.
His passion, which didn't shy away from physical mutilation, bordered on madness.

'Unlike Academy students, growth opportunities don't come easily for Foundation members like us.'

In this world, there are people desperate enough to willingly sacrifice a finger if it means they can become stronger, rise to a higher position, or grasp even the edge of mystery.
The magical engineer paid the price and received the lighting staff.
And then he realized a terrible fact.
The mana stone insertion slot on this lighting staff was too small.
The main power source was the mana of the person holding the lighting staff.
It was a weapon powered by human mana.
The man who had become a mere magical engineer despite his knowledge, lacking mana to become a wizard, couldn't handle the sudden massive mana loss and collapsed.
The expressions of the surrounding crew members hardened as they watched the magical engineer aging in real time, losing his bio-mana even while unconscious because his hand still gripped the lighting staff.

"It's a cursed item!"
"It's draining his life force."
"Do Academy first-years really handle such equipment in actual combat!?"

The crew members used long poles to remove the lighting staff from his hand, like rescuing someone from electrocution.
Many couldn't help but sigh at the tragic sight of the magical engineer who had aged at least 20 years.

"So this is indeed a friend of Lady Oknodie."
"Birds of a feather, I suppose."

Titosoga.
That name was beginning to instill fear.
