# 37 - Freshman Dormitory

Jonna received a report from Oknodie.
The round, cute handwriting resembled Oknodie perfectly, making his heart feel warm.

━━━
*Pre-Entrance Ceremony Preparations

①Purchase uniform✔
-Received items supported by the foundation!
-The skirt feels too empty!

②Apply for freshman dormitory✔
-Successfully moved into Room 111!
-Be careful not to enter Room 111.1 even by mistake!

③Apply for items to bring✔
-Request list: compass, luminous stone, candy pouch, stone pouch, wide thick cloth, rescue mirror, knife, shovel, metal rod, rope, notebook, wire.
-Approved list: candy pouch, stone pouch. The rest rejected.

④Support items to request from the foundation✔
-None.
━━━

There was one item that made him sigh as soon as he saw it.
Stone pouch.
Has she still not broken that habit?

'She'll attract attention.'

The foundation's scholarship students receive attention at the Academy.
There had been numerous incidents, and they understood through experience.
The dangers of the Wiheomhae Foundation.
The fate of scholarship students who could no longer develop.
Professors who become attached to Academy students are always forced to make a choice.

Either endure watching the child's growth stagnate as they become subject to harsh orders.
Or forcibly push them to continue growing and elevate them to a higher position.

It's a terrible hostage situation.
They create disciples with outstanding talent, then use the disciple's growth and future to subtly force cooperation.
Which professor would be entangled in the foundation's trap and bleed this time?

'It's already out of my hands.'

Jonna closed his eyes.
The memories of his former mentor.
The incidents that occurred between his teacher, himself, and the foundation.
He closed his eyes to all of it.
There was nothing good in remembering any of it.

'If I need to be concerned, it's about this.'

④Support items to request from the foundation
-None.

Usually children try their hardest to request as much support as possible.
She's afraid to ask for help.
No, she pathologically refuses it.
Oknodie was that kind of child.
A child who rushes as if she must accomplish everything alone, as if she'll be abandoned otherwise.
When she first started training, such behavior seemed admirable, but now it hurt his heart.
However, this too was beyond his control.
That's why he needed to focus even more.
On what he could actually do.

-Be careful not to enter Room 111.1 even by mistake!

Was she sleep-talking?
Or doodling on the report?
He could have dismissed it as a child's prank, but he didn't.
Because he knew.
He too had once been a freshman at the Academy.
That's why he could state with certainty.
That there was no such thing as Room 111.1 in the freshman dormitory.
Whatever it was, if Oknodie was anxious about it, it was worth investigating.


* *


Gift Academy is located on a remote island.
The Academy was built on an island with high mana distribution to facilitate students' rapid growth.
Without a <Dimensional Gate>, it's extremely difficult to reach the island by ordinary means.
This is because it's located in a danger zone that can't be accessed without considerable skill.
For this reason, even the numerous countries on the continent found it nearly impossible to pose a physical threat to the Academy.
So organizations interested in the Academy thought:

-If invasion is difficult, wouldn't it be better to plant spies and at least gather information?

That's why numerous spies roam the Academy.
One of them was a spy for the Wiheomhae Foundation.

'Orders, huh.'

April, a spy who could only contact the outside world through special methods at designated times and places.
She thought this particular order was quite strange.

"Confirm the reality of Room 111.1 in the women's section of the freshman dormitory and report any dangers."

To investigate without arousing suspicion, she needed a plausible reason to approach the dormitory.
Fortunately, spy April had a cover identity prepared for such occasions.

"A cleaner? Great. Please change the sheets in my room too."
"Ma'am, please throw this away on your way out."

The ill-mannered freshmen were annoying, but she could tolerate this much.

"You there, woman. Come serve as my attendant in my room for a while."
"??"
"I shall grant you the opportunity to receive the favor of Yelchin Brownie, eldest daughter of the Brownie family."

This kind of line-crossing was troublesome.
A noble family's eldest daughter who had awakened to women's charms.
Setting aside how unpleasant it was, she wished they'd recognize how shameful it was.
The foundation's spies had guidelines established for situations like this.

-When a student bothers you, say this.

Recalling her superior's advice, April opened her mouth.

"I am an employee of Gift Academy. If an Academy student interferes with an employee's duties, I have the right to report to superiors and exercise disadvantages."
"...I just meant you're attractive. I wasn't trying to interfere with your work. Ahem. The part about serving as my attendant was just a joke."

They say you can feel like a wizard without magic.
The effect is truly outstanding.

<Freshman Dormitory>
<Left Wing - Women's Section>
<Room 111>

Each floor of the dormitory has 20 rooms.
The first floor, with the best convenience, has all single rooms.
They clearly offer differentiated benefits compared to the standard four-person rooms from the second floor up.

'Could there be a problem in this special floor where the Academy pays such close attention to management and supervision?'

Personally, she was half-skeptical, but she was dispatched to find out.

Knock knock.

"Is anyone there?"

Unlike typical freshmen who leave their rooms to run around here and there, the resident of Room 111 was quietly inside and opened the door abruptly upon hearing the knock.
A head peeking out like a sparrow tilted curiously.

"New event?"
"I'm the cleaner."

Was this some kind of code used by kids these days?
Setting aside her confusion, she entered the room under the pretext of cleaning.

"There's a stain on the wall."
"Ah! I didn't do it!"
"I didn't think you did. Don't worry."

The question was what lay beyond this wall.
Between the 11th and 12th rooms, there was a small empty space.
About 0.5 pyeong in area.
It was a space with a wall supporting the floors, designed with load-bearing considerations.

Press press.
Knock knock.

Even when pressing with her hand and lightly tapping, it felt like an ordinary wall.

Knock knock.

As if hearing the knocking from the next room, there was a responding knock.
The Oknodie resident seemed somehow restless, but there wasn't anything unusual.

"Are you having any problems living in this room?"
"Not at all!"

Her guard is high.
There's no information to be gained from this child.
After finishing cleaning, she visited Room 112 next.

"Cleaning? I don't mind."

Again, April changed the sheets and used the wall stain as an excuse to investigate.

Knock knock.
Knock knock.

Apart from knocks being returned in response to knocks, nothing significant happened.

"Heh. That's cute. Is it the kid living next door?"

Did she find the knocking amusing?
Hestia, the resident of Room 112, smiled warmly.
April thought she was cute too.

"I think it's a response to earlier."
"Earlier?"
"When you knocked back to the next room earlier. I knocked then too, just like now."
"I didn't knock though?"
"?"
"Ah~ I get it. You're playing a prank on me because I'm a naive freshman? Haha. I'm not falling for it. We had this kind of culture in the mercenary corps too."

April spoke to the wall with a serious face.

"Miss Oknodie. Can you hear me?"
"......"
"Can you hear me?"

After a moment.
A faint "I can hear you" came back.

"What? So it was a prank after all."

Hestia, the resident of Room 112, tried to act nonchalant.

"...I'm very sorry for startling you. There was a momentary misunderstanding."
"It's fine. It was exciting, made my heart race a bit."
"From now on, please refrain from talking to the next room in the dormitory, as we aim to create an environment conducive to studying."
"But you just did it plenty."
"Please understand that it was an unavoidable procedure for confirmation."
"Alright. I'll stay quiet."

April left the room.
She quietly rubbed her arm.
The goosebumps still hadn't subsided.

'It was brief, but I clearly heard and remember it.'

The answer that just came back from the wall.
It wasn't Miss Oknodie's voice.
Between Rooms 111 and 112.
Standing in the corridor, April glared at the space between the rooms with a serious face.
A space ridiculously narrow for a person to live in.
What on earth was in there?
The ominous imagination wouldn't stop.
She couldn't suppress her fear.

"Ugh."

April turned and hurried away in terror.
She failed to notice.
The fact that the residents of the same floor were watching with interest, wondering why the cleaner was so frightened and running away.

The wall between Rooms 111 and 112.

Then Hestia doesn't go mad.

In the original game.
