# 98 - Culinary Event

In the evening, an unexpected cooking competition was taking place in the Academy main building's kitchen.

"Isabel, you're amazing!"
"She's using six steamers at once."
"Just like a real chef. So cool."

Isabel skillfully prepared Giant King Crab using her high cooking skill experience. Central nobles bit their handkerchiefs in jealousy watching her receive praise, while students from the Capital lingered around, swallowing their saliva but unable to bring themselves to ask for a taste.

"Um, excuse me...!"

As a Capital student was about to overcome their shame and ask for just one bite, Arcadia and the frontier nobles blocked their way in a group.

"The Imperial students always look down on us frontier folk with their noses in the air. Surely they wouldn't be shameless enough to ask us to share our food just because they're a little hungry?"
"Impossible. If they were that shameless, they wouldn't deserve to call themselves Imperial citizens anywhere. Isn't that right, Lady Arcadia?"
"Hoho. How true."

The students retreated without raising their heads, too embarrassed to persist.
They could only helplessly watch others enjoying the delicious crab meat and endure the sweet aroma torturing their sense of smell.

"Hmph. I can eat François' three-tier cake anytime I want at home. I don't care about some Giant King Crab meat!"
"You're drooling..."
"Ah!"
"Why can't we eat that too?"
"Yeah, that's right. We could make it ourselves!"

The Imperial students' eyes lit up at this revelation from one of their peers.
Whoosh.
With newfound energy, they immediately gathered cooking utensils and headed out to catch King Crabs.

"It's impossible!"
"They'll be lucky if they don't get hurt."

The upper-class frontier students shook their heads.
Even they wouldn't have thought of preparing such dishes without Oknodie's help.
Even if cooking was just a matter of steaming,
Even if they could somehow borrow cooking utensils,
Who would catch the Giant King Crab? And how long would it take to break and cut those hard legs to fit in the steamer?

"We'll have plenty of entertainment for dinner tonight."

The frontier students grinned at the thought of soothing their pride, which had been wounded by the Imperial students' contempt, mockery, and ridicule.
But the Empire wasn't called the Empire for nothing, and the Imperial students didn't bear food-related family names for no reason.

"A steamer? Why bother going all the way to the cafeteria? I have what I brought for fried chicken."
"Need to cut it to fit in the steamer? Then let's just grill it. Hand it over. We can make crab meat pizza with just the meat."
"Who only steams food? Proper cooking requires stir-frying. Give me the body."

Honor Fried Chicken of the Fried Chicken Ducal Family.
Cheddar Potato Pizza of the Potato Pizza Ducal Family.
Lef Charcoal Grilled Ribs of the Iron Plate Charcoal Ribs Ducal Family.
The Empire's three great noble families lined up to create a three-course Giant Crab meal set.

"Where are they getting steamers, magic ovens, and iron plates from?"
"They exchanged them for points."
"What?! They spent those expensive points on cooking equipment?!"
"That's incredible. So ducal families can afford such luxuries."

The students thought:
What a waste of money.
But thanks to them, we get to eat delicious food.
The ducal families' sweet flavors are so good!

"Ugh. We're falling behind."
"What should we do? Isabel, can't you make something else?"
"Help out before making demands."

Isabel scolded the frontier students who were pressuring her.
She had shown off her skills because she didn't want to waste the crabs Oknodie caught, but she was annoyed at her classmates who were acting like shameless peddlers demanding handouts.

"I do. If it's another cooking method, I happen to know a good one."
"Lady Arcadia!"
"As expected of the princess from the maritime nation of Florence! Unbeatable when it comes to seafood!"

The baseless Kingdom of Florence, the treasure goblin on the sea that gets raided by pirates whenever they feel like it.
Though Florence was usually the subject of mockery and ridicule, today was different.
It was an excellent country that defended the frontier's honor against the Empire.

"First, extract all the crab meat."
"What dish are you planning to make?"
"Crab meat salad and crab rice bowl."

The students' eyes sparkled.

"A cooking method that preserves the natural flavor of the ingredients!"
"We can make it without spending points on expensive cooking equipment!"
"A dish that even frontier students with limited technological advancement and poverty can make if they just crack the shell. Lady Arcadia thought of a convenient dish for us commoners!"
"Wow!"
"Long live Arcadia! Long live the Kingdom of Florence!"

While a festival atmosphere developed on both the frontier and Imperial sides, new students continued to arrive.

"I heard there's a crab meat festival in the kitchen!"
"Give us a plate too!"
"Is this a free food distribution center...?"

Students naturally gathered—frontier students with the frontier group, Imperial students with the Imperial group.
The crab meat, which they thought would be lucky to be half-eaten by the end of the day, was disappearing at an alarming rate.

"What do we do? We've eaten all the crabs!"
"Let's hunt for more!"
"It's too unfair if we're the only ones who don't get to eat!"

Although Giant King Crabs would normally be impossible for first-years to hunt, when groups of ten or more students with various abilities went hunting together, they occasionally succeeded.

"Amazing. Killing it by freezing its bodily fluids. Now I understand why Northern Grand Duchess Irene is so praised!"
"Ishtar's hero is also impressively understated. Cutting off all the pincers with incredible swordsmanship and stabbing the sword into its mouth."

The newly gathered crabs were once again prepared for cooking, and the students enjoyed their late dinner.
Unusually, almost all of the 981st class students ended the day with a satisfying meal.

"Uhahah! You could open a restaurant, Isabel."
"Shut up. Do you know how hard it was suddenly becoming a chef?"
"By the way, where did our little lady go?"
"I don't know. I haven't seen her since the middle."
"She's over there."

The entrance exam comrades who were looking for Oknodie followed Zuang's direction and looked at the Imperial faction's table.
And they found her.
Oknodie was naturally blending in among the Imperial students, receiving her portion.

"That little mouse is using assassin techniques for this? What a waste of skill."
"Don't be so harsh. That child grew up starving during harsh training and has experience stealing disaster relief supplies while hiding in church warehouses."
"...Is that true?"

When Isabel nodded seriously, Son O-cheon felt like he had become a bad adult.

"Tsk. That little mouse. She never mentioned that past to me. Next time I see her, I'll have to give her 10kg of bananas."
"Where would you get bananas?"
"Deep in the forest, there's a ferocious red monkey with a blue back and shark teeth eating bananas."
"...That sounds like an extremely strong monkey."
"Isabel. Gisele. We need to find something to feed that little mouse. Let's go pick bananas together next weekend if you're free."

The two sighed deeply.

"When you put it that way, we can't refuse."
"Just this once."

It was a productive mealtime.
As I left the kitchen feeling full, I heard a cheerful voice brimming with happiness from inside.

"Wow! Crab is awesome. I never imagined innards could taste this good~!"
"Y-Your Highness! You're compromising your dignity. If you don't mind your vocabulary, your reputation as a member of the Imperial family..."
"Huh~? Your nagging is annoying. You can't even cook."
"Ugh..."
"Learn to cook at least half as well as the Iron Plate Grill family before nagging me♡ You incompetent♡ Lowest cooking proficiency♡ Can't even cook tree berries♡"
"I-I'll start today! From tree berries to tree leaves, even wooden chopsticks, I'll grill everything I can get my hands on! Waaah!"

The princess's follower ran out with tears flying.
I hope he practices hard and makes lots of delicious food!

"Let's go to bed early tonight."
"It was so delicious, I just want to rest well."
"What about the assignment?"
"Ah."

Students fell into despair as they remembered the assignments they had forgotten while enjoying the feast.
Their suffering was the same as mine.

'Ugh. I have to attend the 5th period lecture too.'

Of course, the lecture was scheduled to be held outdoors in the pouring rain.
Having decided to attend the lecture but lacking the courage to go to the venue—or feeling sorry for the Titoso family who might get eaten by Giant King Crabs—I waited in a nearby corridor.

"Oknodie! Did you see the magic watch? Is Professor Sadako crazy?!"
"I think so too..."
"On a day like this, she should hold the lecture indoors. How can she conduct a night lecture outside in the rain where Giant King Crabs are roaming?"
"Did you bring a raincoat?"
"Grand Duke Andersen gave us three, saying you'd have a hard time attending the night lecture."
"Raincoats?"
"Students taking the 'Sleeping Anywhere' lecture gave up because they couldn't bear sleeping in raincoats while listening to Giant Crab footsteps, so three raincoats were left."
"Your lecture also had three students?"
"They said there were seven? Four remained, and the Grand Duke went to attend that lecture. He looked really pitiful."

"?!"

Was the Grand Duke such a mentally strong person?
I didn't think so?
I tilted my head in confusion before realizing the variable.
The culinary event!
When people eat delicious food, their morale improves, and they sometimes do things they wouldn't normally do.

"Sigh. Who's worrying about whom?"
"Exactly."
"What should we do with the remaining raincoat?"
"I'll put it on the lighting stand!"

The Titoso student struggled to put the raincoat on their beloved lighting stand (waterproof).
The covered light cast a long shadow down the corridor.
Following the shadow with my eyes, I spotted someone quickly ducking out of sight.

"?"

A moment later, a head popped out.
I discovered Zuang looking resentfully at the raincoat covering the lighting stand.
It seemed the Grand Duke wasn't the only one whose spirits were lifted by the culinary event, making them attend lectures they would normally skip.
