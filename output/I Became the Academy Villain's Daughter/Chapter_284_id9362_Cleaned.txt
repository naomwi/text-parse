# Enhanced Translation: Chapter 284 - A Reliable Child

Unlike Jaku and Rogini who purchased supplies and defensive measures at the first and second day's auctions with a late-game strategy in mind, Arcadia and Hestia chose an attack strategy.

"Let's raid and take all the auction items from other teams."
"Is that possible?"
"Trust me."

They had abandoned their raid on Jaku the previous day due to the appearance of the Second Imperial Princess Mesugakki.

"Why did you stop me? The princess is fine. We might have managed somehow if we fought."
"Instead, Jaku might have been controlled by the princess and died suddenly while using dark mana recklessly trying to stop us."

Arcadia remembered that Jaku was Oknodie's person and requested to stop the raid.
Hestia also didn't want to incur Oknodie's hatred, so she accepted the first day's retreat.

"The second day is a no-go. If we can't steal auction items here either, we won't be able to get quality auction products with our meager ship points."

One product given each day.
This wasn't an auction house where they could stay for days on end.
All the food they could gather had burned, and their brought supplies were limited.
Above all, they didn't know when their stay on the deserted island would end.
Both Hestia and Arcadia steeled themselves for the second day's raid.

"Hehehe. You were too hasty."

The result was a victory without gain.
Rogini turned into a statue and returned to the cruise ship with Pyoi.
As Zuang mockingly pointed out, all the other teams knew who had caused the commotion the previous night and showed clear wariness, though to varying degrees.

The closely bonded duo Gisele & Son O-cheon.
Hero Ishtar & Saintess Yupi.
Jaku & Imperial Second Princess Mesugakki.
Assassin duo Zuang & Oknodie.

Being the focus of attention from everyone except the Hestia & Arcadia combo among the five remaining teams was quite burdensome for the two.

"Not confident? About holding out in a raid?"
"I don't know. Why don't you try and find out?"

Zuang snickered and hid behind Oknodie.
From Zuang's nonchalant attitude in the face of intimidation, Hestia sensed it.
There was no weak team among those remaining that they could confidently raid and defeat.
Now they too had to focus on winning auction items and protecting them.

"Don't be too disappointed. The previous day's raid wasn't entirely meaningless."

Arcadia whispered quietly beside Hestia so only she could hear.

"We just need to show that we too can act like Rogini if necessary."
"...Indeed."
"Or we could put all our ship points into today's item and return to the cruise ship immediately."

Strong opponents who might make it difficult to last even a day if raided.
Both the suicide strategy of nullifying the item and the all-in strategy of investing everything to secure just one item and withdrawing immediately weren't bad options.
But that was only possible if the item was worth it.

"The worst case for us would be if an ambiguous item with no value worth nullifying comes up, and we either leave with nothing or face a joint attack from other teams at night."
"...Even against a hero, I won't lose easily."
"Don't be reckless. The night is long, and it'll be even more overwhelming to fight while protecting an item."

Arcadia's resolve was firm.

"Even if you want to stay, I'm definitely leaving today. So make up your mind."
"...Alright. Let's just pray for good auction luck."

The third day's auction.
The two prayed earnestly as if greeting a prayer meta.
For an item of even slightly higher value to appear.

"I will now reveal the third item of the deserted island auction. Today's item is 'Port Raid on the Kingdom of Florence.'"

Their prayer was answered. An auction item of such tremendous value appeared that they couldn't even dare to imagine.


* * *


Jonna Wiheomhae declared coldly.

"The starting bid is 10,000 points. Bids will increase by 10,000 each. Is there anyone who wishes to participate?"

Everyone turned to look at Arcadia, the noble lady from a demoted aristocratic family in the Kingdom of Florence.
The Kingdom of Florence and Arcadia had already suffered severe damage from the recent incident.
No one could guarantee how much more damage they would suffer if the Foundation launched a port raid on top of that.

"I said I wouldn't take questions, but I can't help but ask."

The hero stared intensely at Jonna with blazing eyes.

"What is a port raid? Are you selling a product that allows the auction winner to raid a port whenever they want?"
"I will not take questions. Please consider and speculate on the value and usage of the item yourself to establish your auction strategy."

Not even the slightest consideration for Arcadia could be felt in this extremely cold declaration.
The hero's gaze then turned to Oknodie.

"Was this it? The reason you gathered your 'friends'? Were you planning to show off the Foundation's power and intimidate everyone into submission like this?"

Oknodie answered with an innocent face.

"Even I didn't know the auction items would come out randomly. Jonna, didn't you tell me what kind of person Arcadia is to me?"
"Of course I know."
"Then how could you bring out such an item? We came here to have fun, and now the atmosphere is getting weird!"

Oknodie was indirectly suggesting that the auction be canceled or the item withdrawn.
However, Jonna's resolve was even firmer than Oknodie thought.

"Even by my Lady's order, this particular item cannot be canceled."
"Why not?"
"There were special instructions from the Chairman."
"By Chairman, you mean Papa...?"
"The Chairman said this: Make sure to put up an item that your Lady's friends cannot help but purchase."
"To make me feel bad?"
"Because he was curious whether you would help your friends or choose an item that would help individual growth later."
"!"

Not just Oknodie, but Ishtar, Arcadia, and everyone else realized the hidden intention behind this auction.
This was a test.
A test asking if they had accumulated enough ship points to protect themselves.
Simultaneously, a test of how much Oknodie cared for her friends.
A test of the value of friendship, asking if they could avoid fighting among friends to bid high points on an item that would endanger competitors for the sake of growth.

It was blatantly mocking the human heart.
Encouraging desire.
Demanding proof of the value of friendship through points and auction items.

The participants' faces turned serious.
They couldn't help but be solemn.
Because it was an auction item with too much value and risk to be given to mere first-year Academy students.

"That's just like Papa!"

Oknodie's cheerful acceptance was the strange part.
The participants were certain that they weren't the ones being strange.

"Let's all boycott the auction."

If no one purchases the item, all items might be nullified.
At Saintess Yupi's unusually saintly suggestion, everyone began to look around cautiously.

"I'm sure you all know without me saying it. If anyone raises points here... that person and I will become lifelong enemies. Please consider carefully."

Arcadia's stern warning followed.

"If there are no bidders for 60 seconds, this item will be withdrawn."

Just as many were quietly hoping for the 60 seconds to pass safely, one person finally went ahead and did it.

"100,000."

Everyone's gaze turned to the speaker.
Arcadia's face was filled with undisguisable betrayal.

"How... how could you do this?"
"I apologize."
"This is too much."

With trembling lips, Arcadia uttered the name of the auction participant.

"Gisele!"

"Arcadia. 110,000 points."

"Arcadia. 160,000 points."

Gisele, a collaborator with whom she had even run a joint business called the Dark Association.

"Arcadia. 210,000 points."

"Calm down, Arcadia! Gisele has a different intention!"

No choice then.

"..."
