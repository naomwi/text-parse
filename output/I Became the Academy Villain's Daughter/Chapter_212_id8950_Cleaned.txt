# Enhanced Translation: Chapter 212 - A Heart That Wants to Believe

Paper Company was shocked.

'Why is Oknodie here!?'

Hiding around the corner in terror, he peeked at the unfolding scene, which was quite a spectacle.
To think Hestia was friends with that girl!
He had spotted a first-year student who was tall, skilled, but unpopular due to her occupation, and thought she might be useful since she seemed to have time on her hands.
He had simply created a club where second-years could gain indirect experience about dungeons and adventures through Paper Dungeon, and added what he thought would be a good companion—nothing more.
He hadn't targeted her specifically, but now he found himself in the position of a delinquent senior who brought a first-year to a dangerous place full of second-year boys.
There could only be one reason why Oknodie had stormed in here.

"You've got some nerve messing with my friend."

A threat!
Goosebumps rose on the back of his hand from fear.
This was the girl who, after having her feelings hurt once, took revenge by completing his assignments for him, effectively turning him into her teaching assistant.
What would happen if he upset her a second time?
Perhaps he'd go beyond being a teaching assistant and become a graduate student—someone who enters a professor's lab as a top disciple, receiving extensive education.

'But... they're also playing Paper Dungeon?'

Hestia, seemingly flustered, seated Oknodie and brought her a book.
The girl apparently wanted to become a companion, but for some reason, Hestia had put Oknodie into the book and taken the position of librarian to guide the story.
If a first-year carelessly took control, it might turn into quite a rough story, potentially leading to an unpleasant experience.
Would this be okay?
What if Oknodie's feelings were hurt and he ended up taking the blame?

"Hestia, need help?"
"It's fine, Paper Company president. I seem to have an unexpected talent for this."

Was Oknodie satisfied?
Curious about what narrative they were developing, he peeked at the Paper Dungeon.

━━━
Current Difficulty - Extremely Brutal
Characteristics - Realistic, Harsh, Worst
Progress - Attacking a dungeon managed by Crawling Mint Choco, one of the Empire's Three Great Traitor Families, for magic stone mining. Currently infiltrating through a dangerous route after accepting his subjugation request.
━━━

"This will get the kid killed!!"

Paper Company cried out in surprise.
Even though Paper Dungeon is a space for gaining experience, it's not a place where anything goes.
Dangerous actions naturally lead to danger.
Risks beyond what a party can handle can result in consequences ranging from minor injuries to death.
While dying in the book doesn't mean actual death, who would want to experience death?

"It's fine, president. Oknodie chose this herself."

Dangerous route. Difficult challenge.
Oknodie had voluntarily taken on such a challenge.
Relying on chance.
With the librarian's help.
In a comfortable and convenient way.
Despite going solo, she didn't want any luck or unrealistic help.
She intended to overcome everything by herself.

"Look. She's doing well."
"...Really?"

He was dumbfounded at the rapidly increasing dungeon progress.

"How is this working?"

Even the same story varies in difficulty depending on whether it's a low-level or high-level party, but Oknodie was effortlessly navigating a difficult path alone.
In terms of difficulty, it was comparable to a party of four experienced Paper Dungeon explorers.
She's unexpectedly good at playing.
Like someone who's done nothing but play Paper Dungeon after meals.
And she's even showing some hardcore techniques.

"Finally arriving at the scene, the hostage said: 'Help me! Mint Choco hasn't noticed you yet. What will you... Huh? You think that's strange?'"

Hestia looked troubled after seeing Oknodie's explorer opinion that appeared in the book.

"What is it?"
"She says the difficulty is too low. If it's truly one of the Empire's Three Great Traitor Families, the hostages should already be dead with their faces turned blue from choking on mint chocolate, prepared for a sacrificial ritual..."
"Wow. I've seen kids whining to lower the difficulty, but this is the first time someone's voluntarily raising it."

━━━
Current Difficulty - Legendary
Characteristics - Realistic, Harsh, Worst, Ruthless
Progress - Crawling Mint Choco has summoned a demon into his body. Furthermore, he plans to flood the dungeon and contaminate the surrounding flood waters with mint chocolate infused with demonic energy. You are the only hero who can stop him now.
━━━

Oknodie raised what was an ordinary subjugation request to hero-level difficulty.

'These kids have high Paper Dungeon experience? They're playing more intensely than third-years despite being first-years.'

Suddenly he remembered.
This girl was taking the course "Adventure Tales of a Retired Former Hero."
Though she was hostile toward Hero Ishtar, she was having adventures no different from a hero herself.
What did she want to become, what did she want to do?
In the midst of his troubled thoughts, he witnessed Crawling Mint Choco being soaked in dark mana and transformed into Dark Cacao Choco, becoming her minion.

"!!"

Paper Company covered his mouth.
Creating an entity worthy of opposing a hero, and making it her subordinate.
There could only be one reason for such a rehearsal.
This girl intended to do the same in reality.
The act of creating her own subordinates to stand against heroes.
The rumors about the mysterious class "Dark Princess Oknodie," called the new generation demon king candidate, might not be just rumors after all.

"That was fun!"
"I'm glad you enjoyed it."
"Did Hestia have fun too?"
"Yes."
"Should I be the librarian next time?"

Hestia stared down at the book in her hands, marked with absurd play data.

"Not today. It's getting late."

She averted her eyes and expressed her refusal.
Her skills were insufficient to endure the gap and difficulty difference between a mercenary's reality and a demon king's reality.

'Hmm. But friends are friends after all.'

Paper Company thought as he observed Hestia's expressive face.
So that child can show her emotions honestly sometimes, he thought.
For the first time, he realized that the stoic first-year, who had always appeared as a sturdy warrior and veteran mercenary, could also show such vulnerability.

"Mr. Paper Company, thanks for having us!"
"Y-yeah."

Paper Company made a resolution.
Next time, he would create an adventure that could draw out the explorers' sincere feelings with a more realistic story.
...He ambitiously planned to give up only after five members had fled the club.


* *


"Good job, Hestia."
"Thank y... Huh?"

After the club activities ended.
Walking back together.
Hestia stopped abruptly upon hearing the greeting.

"Wasn't that way of speaking only used inside the room?"
"I didn't say anything?"
"It's me. I said it."

The voice came from the large hat worn by Oknodie, who was blinking innocently.
Hestia wondered if she had accidentally fallen asleep in the Paper Dungeon.

"Has the demerit for accidentally knocking down the wall you were building still not arrived?"
"...So I wasn't hearing things."

Hestia was thoroughly confused when the hat spoke about conversations they had shared in the room.
She had thought the voice different from Oknodie's was just because it was coming from across the room, but here was the same voice outside the room.
Even mentioning conversations only they remembered.

"Don't tell me... all this time I've been talking not to Oknodie but to the hat?"

She had to admit it.
She had been conversing with "that thing."
She had made "that thing" her friend.

"Hestia. Don't be surprised. This hat is no ordinary hat."
"Then what is it?"

A toy to play with me?
An object of ridicule to make me feel miserable?
As cold daggers began to stab at her heart, Oknodie said:

"It's a hat that knows my precious secret!"
"Oknodie's... precious secret?"
"Mr. Hat knows secrets about me that I don't want to tell anyone else. So he's what you might call my secret friend!"

Not a toy or object of ridicule, but a secret friend.
A special relationship.
Hestia's heart softened a little.

"All this time I've been sharing my secrets and embarrassing stories because I thought I was talking to you, Oknodie, not the hat."
"I'm sorry. But I often have to leave the room, and when I return, I fall asleep quickly. You needed someone to talk to, right?"
"So you let me talk to the hat?"
"...Of course!"

Her hesitation before affirming might have been due to guilt.
What a pure-hearted child.
Bringing an ego item for company because she worried her friend might be lonely when she couldn't be there herself.

"And Mr. Hat is a real person!"
"...An ego item is something that sprouts in an object, not a real person."
"In that sense, he's even more of a person! What dwells in the hat isn't an object with a sprouting self, but the mind of a real person!"
"What...? What are you saying..."

A child who extracts souls and traps them in beads.
A demon who devours human souls like candy.
A monster that mimics human emotions.
Several malicious rumors about Oknodie flashed through her mind.

"...!"

They might not be just rumors.
Her chest grew cold.
Her hands and feet turned icy.

"Hestia. Do you remember what we talked about?"
"...Which conversation?"
"That I don't care about what people say about me. The Hestia I know is the Hestia I've judged through our conversations and time spent together."
"That's right."

At the hat's words, the sound of her racing heart gradually subsided.
That kind-heartedness undoubtedly belonged to a human.
It was a kindness that couldn't be possessed by a cruel being who played with human souls.
So Oknodie couldn't be a bad child.

"That's right..."

That's what Hestia wanted to believe.
