# 349 - Organizational Conflict 2

Kamela was truly feeling wronged.
If she had at least succeeded in stealing Oknodie's assignment, she wouldn't feel so unjustified.
In reality, she hadn't managed to steal anything.

"Waaaaaah!"
"Kyaaaah! My head feels like it's going to explode!!!"
"Someone shut that crying baby up!!"
"F-fire magic... ugh. My head hurts too much to gather mana puzzles!"
"Ouch! This thing is raising thorns on its vines!"

The crying baby mandrake screamed insanely as soon as someone entered, wailing loud enough to burst eardrums.
When they tried to steal something while wearing earplugs, suddenly growing vines slapped their arms and legs, causing everyone to scream and rush toward the exit.
As the saying goes, "if you can't get a pheasant, a chicken will do," so they did raid the rooms of other students who were part of Oknodie's group.

From Hestia's room, they took a mini golem crawling on the desk; from Isabel's room, a golden ladle hanging on the wall; from Dorothy's room, a luminous white mushroom.

[Automatic Defense Formation Activated]
[Damage Rate 89%]

"Eek!"

...From Zuang's room, they managed to get a throwing knife that nearly pierced their head after breaking through the barrier.

Sizzle!

"Aack. My hand is stuck to the door!"
"It's an ice trap. Use fire magic to raise your body temperature and melt the ice! If you stay like that, you'll get frostbite and have to carry your severed hand to the infirmary!"

From Irene's room, they got a taste of cold magic that made intruders stick to surfaces, but overall, their results weren't bad.

"Make sure you gather everything in the bag and follow—"
"Thieves?"
"H-hero!?"

Of all times, they ran into Ishtar in the hallway just as they were finishing their crime.

"You didn't touch our people, did you?"
"Absolutely not."
"That room... must be Oknodie's side."

Ishtar just clicked her tongue and walked past.

"Don't go overboard with your night games. You'll get crushed badly at that level anyway."

One clueless organization member was about to brag that they accomplished what even the hero couldn't do when Kamela's contract seal began to glow.

[Don't say a word]

As the organization member flinched at the voice echoing in their head, the hero returned to her room.

"Hey, Kamela? I think I just heard your voice in my head..."
"Must be your imagination. Come on, let's go back. When Oknodie's gang finds out their rooms were raided, they'll come for revenge. Let's request protection from the dorm supervisor and instructors, and switch rooms with roommates to gather in group rooms tonight."

Breaking the four-person-per-room rule would result in a fine, but the value of the items they stole today would more than cover the protection service and the fine, leaving them with substantial profit.
Not only did she lead the raid on upper-class students' rooms, but the action team leader also raided lower-class students' rooms, yielding good results.
All they needed to do was use what they could from today's haul and dispose of the problematic items through their seniors.

'Just need to get through one night.'

A third-year senior from the club had already expressed interest in buying after hearing about their plan.
After tonight, she would be rich in points.
Her hopeful expectations were ruthlessly crushed when Oknodie and Hestia barged in through the main entrance, not even the window, in the middle of the night.

"Attack! There are eight of us!"
"Wait, these idiots...!"

Before she could stop them, several female students charged forward.
Kamela witnessed it.
People flying in all directions.
Students who came out to the hallway, startled by the sudden noise, flinched when they saw a student thrown out of the group room and collapsed.
Oknodie walked into the hallway with a bright smile, grabbed the fallen student's leg, and dragged them while saying:

"It's a pillow fight! You'll pretend you didn't see anything, right?"
"I heard a noise like a bomb going off... how is that a pillow fight...?"
"You'll pretend you didn't see anything, right?"

The sound of lower-class students in the group room hurriedly closing and locking their doors could be heard.
It's over.
Oknodie returned after closing the door.
In that short time, all the female students had been beaten down by one angry Hestia.

"Give me back my baby golem."

The baby golem they had taken out of the bag and placed on the floor looked up at Hestia, then rolled across the floor to Hestia's feet.
Hestia carefully picked up the golem with her large hands and put it in her pocket with a relieved expression.

"Return all the stolen items!"
"Alright..."
"Including those stolen from the lower-class students!"
"...Fine."
"You stole from the boys' dormitory too, didn't you?"
"I'll give everything back. Isn't that enough...?"
"I'll send you a receipt for compensation including dormitory repair costs, bribes for the dorm supervisor and instructors, and compensation for psychological damage!"

Kamela nodded with great difficulty while trembling.
The law is distant, but fists are near.
This was a time to nod to whatever was said, no matter what.

"But why isn't my assignment here?"
"What?"
"How could a crying baby mandrake have enough power to protect my assignment? There's no use pretending, so hand it over quickly!"

Tears welled up in Kamela's eyes from the injustice.
Now she understood.
Oknodie was planning to blame her assignment on us out of spite!

"Oknodie. Let me say this again—we didn't touch your assignment. We'll return all the stolen items and pay compensation. But not the assignment. That crosses the line."

This wasn't just an ordinary credit assignment, but one worth 38 credits.
Moreover, it belonged to an upper-class student, specifically Oknodie, who was known for taking courses as difficult as the Grand Duke Andersen's.
Even if they put their heads together, they couldn't guarantee they could complete it with their skills.

If they failed the assignment.
If they couldn't produce satisfactory results.

Oknodie would use this as an excuse to demand even greater compensation, potentially ruining their academy life completely.
She was at a crossroads where she couldn't yield even with a knife to her throat.

"Step back, Oknodie. She won't listen to reason. Negotiations need to be done with strength."

When Hestia pounded her fists together, the entire room shook.
There was no choice.
Now she had to use her trump card.
Even a cornered mouse will bite a cat.
Kamela had teeth that could hurt even more than a mouse's.

[Protect me]

The most brilliant light of compulsion emanated from the contract seal on the back of her hand.
Oknodie's eyes showed surprise as she heard someone approaching.
Her quick awareness impressed Kamela, but it was too late to react.

Bang!

The person who appeared at the opened door was the one who had retreated from the hallway with the dorm supervisor—an "Academy student on leave and instructor" controlled by Kamela's contract.


* * *


Kamela's contract had a gimmick.
The pet contracts she granted increased the pet's debt according to the kindness bestowed by the owner, and decreased it according to the pet's obedience.
However, regardless of the owner-pet relationship, the contract holder incurs a separate debt to Kamela based on the total amount of debt exchanged over the contract.

"Strength-all-in-one-hit-character-lover Marine. Today is the day your cruel antics come to an end."
"You deserved this even without Kamela's orders. Pay for your crime of lifting someone else's experiment table to increase your strength through weight training and vertically cutting it in half!"
"I won't forgive your atrocity of throwing innocent students as part of your 'advanced training' to raise your title! Even with protective magic, I didn't want such a terrible experience!!"

There were students with grudges separate from Kamela's contract, but there were also those who had no grudges yet had no choice but to follow orders against their will.

"Jewel, I never wanted to make you do things like this. Really. This is because of Kamela...!"
"Stop. I'm tired of this. I don't even want to distinguish between what you, May, truly wish for and what Kamela is ordering. I... I can't trust you anymore."

Kamela had manipulated pet contract users through subtle pressure until her second year.
In the original route, those controlled by her ended up forcing each other to perform dangerous tasks against their will.
When suspicion arises that love is merely an emotion created for the effectiveness of the contract, the warmth of couples who engaged in certain activities using the contract as an excuse disappears.
When they can't trust each other and only the contract binds their relationship, the level and difficulty of obedience they demand to maintain the relationship also increases.
Naturally, human patience has its limits.
Male students who initially entered the contracts half-willingly later had their hearts broken by the sadism or lost their affection, giving way to humiliation and hatred.

"If I just kill that guy, I'll move up one rank... so Jewel, kill him. For the sake of my acceptance, please!"

When debt accumulates to the point of requesting murder, the relationship reaches complete catastrophe.
Even if the player prevents and resolves the incident, the couples end up breaking up completely.
If not prevented, it becomes a major incident requiring student council intervention, and the lives of everyone involved begin to twist in a dark direction.

"Wow. This is amazing. So there was even a route where you could target seniors, not just classmates!"

Just as there was an early defeat event with Chapter 1 boss Hestia, Kamela originally had an early defeat event too.
In this case, the original defeat event was "Getting controlled by one of the upper-class students!"
However, since my intervention as the player eliminated any chance of targeting upper-class students, it seems a more daring route of targeting an instructor opened up.

"Don't touch Kamela. If you leave now, I'll overlook today's incident."
"And if I refuse?"
"You'll learn firsthand how strong a student on leave who qualified as an instructor can be."

This would have been a challenging battle in a normal playthrough.
But now, after boosting my growth stats at Papa's mansion...

'I can handle this.'

It didn't seem like I would lose.
