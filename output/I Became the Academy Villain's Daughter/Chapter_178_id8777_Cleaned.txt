# 178 - The Professors' Investigation of the Culprit

Sadako stared down at the device in her hand.
The device she had found after emerging from that nightmare.
She had sensed cursed energy from it, and upon examination, traces of the curse that had tormented Oknodie were indeed stored inside.

'This is worth investigating.'

Oknodie turned her pale face away while looking at the device, seemingly reminded of something.
For a child who showed no fear even against the undead to visibly tremble like this, there must be something involved with this device that terrified her.

"Mirror, mirror. Show me the face of this object's previous owner."
"Give me five lungs and three fresh livers."

Hearing the mirror's response, Professor Sadako immediately opened her magic inventory and took out fresh animal lungs and livers she had prepared for lectures, placing them in the drawer beneath the mirror.
As she closed the drawer, the entire mirror writhed as if tasting them.

Crunch, munch.

The artifact moved with an unpleasant sound.
After writhing lazily like someone satisfied with a delicious meal, images of people appeared on the mirror's surface.

One student.
And one merchant.

Professor Sadako magically printed the images that appeared on the mirror and handed them to Professor Destroyer.

"Track them down. They're the culprits who placed the curse on Oknodie."
"Leave the student to Monk Myungho, not me. I'll find out about the adult male within two weeks and contact you."
"...Can that professor be trusted?"
"He's been monitoring Oknodie at the principal's request longer than we have. Of course, since he's connected to the principal, we shouldn't give him too much information."
"Understood."

Three professors joined forces to rescue Oknodie.
Sadako. Destroyer. Monk Myungho.
Among them, Monk Myungho hadn't seen inside Oknodie's nightmare.
So he doesn't know.
The fact that the principal might destroy the Academy.
They were allies who couldn't truly be together until the end.
Their goals were the same, but theirs was merely a relationship of mutual use.
It was unfortunate for the good-natured Monk Myungho, but considering that any information given to him could also reach the principal, they couldn't share deeper information.

"Find out who this student is."
"That's not difficult. I already know this student."
"...Who is it?"
"Primer. A lower-class student aspiring to join the Knight Department. He mainly takes Enchantment Magic lectures, but he's not skilled enough to cast such powerful curse magic himself."
"What other lectures?"
"Let me check with my magic watch right now. Hmm... he's taking Liberal Arts lectures: Imperial Humanities, Understanding Ancient Scripts, and Social Dancing."
"He's going to have a hard time making a living. He'd be better off becoming undead sooner rather than later."

Even the good-natured Monk Myungho could only laugh at Professor Sadako's frank assessment, unable to deny it.
While students should take electives they're interested in, the reality was that these were escape-type course registrations from students who couldn't handle the competition in demanding departments.
Just taking courses to fill credits makes it increasingly difficult to survive at the Academy as semesters and years progress.

"...Moderately ambitious, moderately incompetent, and moderately stubborn. Such mediocrity would indeed make for an easily exploitable pawn."
"This student? Is this why Oknodie was cursed?"
"Destroyer and I have handled it. You don't need to... get involved."
"No, even the Heavenly Venerable and Buddha themselves wouldn't draw lines in a world where the balance of power with the Western world has collapsed and boundaries have disappeared. How could this humble mortal do so?"
"...Is that so? Then don't strain yourself too much and just look into it moderately. It would be troublesome if the kid gets expelled."

She wants to stop him.
To tell him this is information he shouldn't know.
She wants to prevent it.
Information that would flow to the principal.
Should I kill him?
Sadako momentarily contemplates murder, disguising it within the indifference of the undead, letting it pass as if nothing happened.
But Monk Myungho had already read Sadako's emotions that had failed to maintain their usual clarity.

'It's not uncommon for professors to dislike each other, but I haven't done anything to deserve such murderous intent.'

Deep concern shadowed Monk Myungho's face.

'Oknodie. What information about the culprit targeting that child have they discovered that makes them so wary of me seeking information? Perhaps... maybe...'

Perhaps they had received information that someone among the professors had been recruited by the culprit, and he was under suspicion.
What darkness had they stumbled upon?
How deep into the darkness had Oknodie become involved?
He sensed something darker and deeper than dark magic.
Something massive and terrifying that one feared to even touch.

'However, both the Heavenly Venerable of Taoism and Buddha of Buddhism have said that the light of great compassion and the grace of wisdom must be cast even upon the deepest darkness of the heart.'

Despite Professor Sadako's indirect warning, or perhaps her genuine concern that she failed to hide, Monk Myungho found courage rather than yielding to silence.
First, he would find the student Primer.
And begin by extracting information.
The beginning of a journey to rescue Oknodie from this deep and overwhelming abyss.


* * *


Lucky Harbor in the port city of the Kingdom of Florence.
Despite its name, this city had gone to ruin when sea monster-induced current changes made docking difficult, and with people no longer visiting, it walked the path of decline.
Now, as befitting a failed city frequented only by smugglers, pirate ships, and immigrant fleets, the city lacked vitality even during the day.

"Perfect place for a man to hide."

Destroyer's informant.
Lorenzo Donuts, branch manager of the Thieves' Guild, lit a cigar and added one more plume of smoke to the city already oppressed by gloomy clouds.
It was too dark for sunglasses, but this level of darkness was just right for those living in the criminal underworld.

"Oknodie, was it? The name of the child our hero has taken an interest in."
"Yes, that's correct."
"That child is truly blessed. To receive the attention of a man who once peeled back a layer of the world's darkness."

Throughout his cigar smoking, his direct subordinate maintained his position without showing any displeasure at the harsh smoke.
Lorenzo Donuts was such a dangerous individual that his subordinate carefully monitored his every move, afraid of failing to manage his expression or saying something wrong.
This was especially true given the bloody middle-aged man collapsed at Lorenzo's feet.

"P-please, I'm sorry... I don't know what harm I've caused your eminence, but I'll reform and live properly from now on. Please forgive me just this once..."
"It's fascinating, isn't it? I never ordered anyone to live righteously, yet why do people try to become good persons in front of me?"

With a flick of Lorenzo's hand, the middle-aged man crawled on his knees toward him.

"May I borrow your hand?"

Lorenzo used the dirty, trembling hand as an ashtray for his cut cigar.
The middle-aged man couldn't even scream properly, just clenching his fist and shedding tears.
With another flick of Lorenzo's hand, his direct subordinate forcibly stood the middle-aged man up.

"Do you remember now? This man. Where is that merchant who joined your guild a few years ago but only registered his name? What is he doing and where?"

Less than an hour after the guild master started talking, an explosion sounded somewhere in the city.

"Nothing ever goes smoothly with this job. That's the problem with playing with expensive lords. Human life becomes too cheap."
"Urgent message from the capture team. The fleeing target has gone berserk with dark magic."
"Send in the combat team. People who extracted information even from the Magic Realm wouldn't make such a fuss over this."

The Thieves' Guild, which gathered information and materials by traveling even to remote villages in the Magic Realm where mages who had lost their human form lived, all to obtain information needed by the hero.
For organization members who worked with the world's greatest thief, facing opponents who went berserk with dark mana wasn't new.

"Did he survive?"
"He's dead. He died of shock midway, unable to withstand the dark mana."
"Strip everything—possessions, accounts, identity."

There was still much to do to fill the report for his lord.
Who this person had been meeting, how he had contacted his backers.
But then an unexpected name emerged.

"Seviche? House Seviche was involved in this? Is it possible another Seviche family has appeared in this country without my knowledge?"
"It's the same Seviche family with Princess Arcadia Seviche of the Kingdom of Florence."
"Check again! You don't understand the implications of what you're saying. You're telling me that the 'tail' requested as top priority by our lord, who was training future heroes at the world's premier educational institution, connects to a duchy that's the foremost noble house in a kingdom. Are you certain your information is correct?"
"I'm certain."
"...I see."

Lorenzo Donuts calmed his excitement.
After losing his family to a mage's rampage, there was no longer any warmth in the home he returned to.
The useless junk that had accumulated one by one from falling for peddlers' sales pitches was no longer visible in his house.
When he lay down on the floor where not a single piece of clutter existed anymore.
He felt it.
A longing for the messiness he used to complain about.
The realization that he could never make those complaints again.
Remembering that emptiness, his heart grew cold.
If his information was wrong, someone else would live the rest of their life with such emptiness.

"Review it. Check if it's false evidence or misdirection set up to deceive trackers."

The report would come after.
A thorough investigation that wouldn't miss even the slightest mistake.
His subordinates praised him for being as meticulous as always, but...
He knew.
He might deceive his subordinates, but he couldn't deceive his own heart.
This was a postponement, an escape.
A shocking truth he couldn't accept.
The desire to delay, even slightly, the moment when he would have to report this to his lord.
An expression of fear, sensing that the moment when many people would experience his emptiness was not far off.

"The season when birds cry is approaching."

The season when birds feast on human corpses covering the water.
This summer would be an extremely harsh one.
