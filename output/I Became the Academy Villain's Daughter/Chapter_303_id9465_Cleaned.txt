# Chapter 303 - The Final Item

Leape's attacks were remarkably sharp.
Attacks that stole timing.
Attacks skilled in deception.
All of them were technical maneuvers on par with this Oknodie's prime as a veteran player.

"Please surrender now. Then you can be released safely without experiencing pain or being knocked unconscious."
"Hmm~ I still refuse!"

Where did she get her information about the Apostle of Anrage?
I could make up some answer.
But it's a matter of veteran pride.
Admitting defeat and bowing your head because you're outmatched is a self-destructive act that causes greater mental damage the more pride you have in yourself.
Unfortunately for Leape, the fighting conditions were in my favor.

[Night Vision]

A night activity correction value trained through adventurer's nocturnal activities.
With this, the disadvantage of darkness disappears.

[Sense of Balance]

Maintaining precarious balance on ground that's breaking apart in real-time during intense combat.
Thanks to the experience points I've steadily accumulated for this skill, that disadvantage also disappears.
As functional corrections gave me breathing room, I immediately made an assessment.

'Leape's strength is chapter boss level!'

The crazed Hestia, Camela the Queen of Pets, Silent Death and the Forest of Silence.
Leape possessed strength rivaling the early chapter bosses from Chapter 1 through Chapter 4.
Even if I overwhelmed her with sheer volume, the storm of throwing knives she scattered while maintaining distance had the speed and power to knock down crowds with projectiles that were now accompanied by 'magic'.

BOOM!

The ground where the shuriken hit exploded with a thunderous sound, shattering in all directions.
The surface of the dark curtain I had raised to prepare for the explosion froze with a crackling sound, and the cold began to climb up my arm, attempting to penetrate my body.
No sooner had I vigorously shaken my hand to throw it off than a shuriken accompanied by a <Wind Cutter> disappeared into the darkness after cutting a few strands of my hair.

'Enchant!'

Timing, deception, magic.
The elements to consider keep increasing.

"Are you going to keep taking hits?"
"Hmph. My attacks are already succeeding, you know?"
"None of your attacks have landed though."

Leape easily avoided my counterattack and was about to respond dismissively when her body tilted.

"!"

Now she noticed.
The ground around Leape was completely destroyed and sinking.
My target wasn't Leape.
It was the ground of the deserted island where Leape would step.

"Jonna~! Please redefine what constitutes a deserted island in the deserted island auction!"
"Understood. In the deserted island auction, an area defined as a deserted island refers to land that is not small in size and where drinking water can be obtained, meaning land that is not sea."
"Did you hear that~? 'Land that is not sea.' If Leape puts even one foot in the seawater that's now filling up the area, it will be considered 'out of bounds' for leaving the deserted island!"

Leape honestly acknowledged it.

"You used your head well."
"Hehe. Assassins may be strong in one-on-one combat, but they're weak in other types of battles."

In combat that involves carving up terrain rather than people, she would naturally be weaker than a veteran player who's good at everything.
To make an analogy, I refused the fighting game and changed the genre to a bombardment game.

"But you've underestimated the assassin maid. If you believed you could force me 'out of bounds' with just this, you're gravely mistaken."

Leape's aura suddenly became frighteningly powerful.
Shurikens shot in all directions.
When I focused my mana vision strongly, I could see faint lines connected to the shurikens.
Watching Leape sprint along these lines, I realized.
Those were paths.
Paths that only Leape could use, connecting across terrain features—the assassin's path.

"Hmm~?"

Normally, one would try to cut the lines connected to the shurikens and suffer from the counter-magic traps.
Binding and cutting.
Despite the deadly traps set on those lines, I somehow felt confident.
I think I can do this?
I reached out my hand and devoured the mana protecting the trap formula with dark mana.

"!?"

In an instant, the entire line turned pitch black.
Leape was strong, but she tried to do too many things at once.
Greed is dangerous!
It's impossible to control all the lines spread in every direction, but it's not difficult to steal one of them.
The result of Leape's control being weakened by spreading her power in too many places.

"Reverse binding, activate!"

The line beneath Leape's feet writhed and lunged at her as she leaped into the air.
SPLASH!
Leape fell into the water, bubbled for a moment, then slowly floated to the surface.

"I confirm Leape participant's out of bounds. As of this moment, all types of interference with Leape's deserted island are prohibited and nullified."
"Yahoo! I won!"

As I jumped up and down excitedly, Leape spoke from the water.

"...I don't recall teaching you this binding technique, where did you learn it?"
"Uh, well. The Academy?"
"An academy that teaches turtle binding..."

Step, step.
Leape climbed onto the deserted island of her own accord and flexed her body to untie the ropes that emphasized certain parts of her body.
It was a show of force, demonstrating that she could have escaped even at the moment of being bound if she had wanted to.

"Even if you keep it secret, I've already figured out the source. It must be the professors at the Academy. Among them, those skilled enough to know about the Apostle of Anrage would be the former hero Destroyer or the greatest righteous thief of this era, Bronze de Estrada."
"Leape... are you angry...?"
"I'm not angry at you. I'm angry at the cowardly adults who taught such obscene binding techniques to a child because of their own preferences, and who passed on dangerous knowledge to instill hatred toward the Foundation."


* * *


The Foundation may be trash, but Leape, as a member of the Foundation, had maintained certain principles.
Don't provide sexual education to children.
Don't lead them into dangers they can't handle.
The Academy professors had violated all the taboos that even she, a member of the Foundation, observed.

'And they call themselves teachers and mentors at an educational institution.'

Just as the Academy couldn't understand the Foundation, she, as an assassin maid of the Foundation, couldn't understand the Academy's professors.

"Leape's fighting style was fun! Can I use it when school starts in the second semester?"
"...Do as you please."

Though she had intended to demonstrate the mana cultivation method, Oknodie had completely absorbed the fighting style.
The way she swung her dagger in the air—shwish, shwish, shwish—and threw things here and there, pulling strings to retrieve them, showed considerable skill.
The way she loaded mana into projectiles seemed quite practiced, as if she had been throwing various things all along.

"If I master the maid's mana cultivation method, could Tito become as good as Leape?"
"It requires aptitude and effort."
"But there's a possibility, right?"
"If they're your friend, I believe it's possible."
"Heheh. I'm excited! With this, Tito can stay with me until third year!"

Third year, not fourth year...?
Even after showing the power of the maid's mana cultivation method?
Leape felt her pride wounded.

"Why can't you guarantee until fourth year?"
"To graduate with me, they can't take any leave of absence or repeat a year!"

So she wasn't underestimating the cultivation method.
Leape's expression, which had been grim, softened a little.

"Since I'm remaining on the island as a supervisor's assistant rather than a participant, I can no longer help with meals."
"That's okay. I've been saving food for times like this!"

Oknodie's small hands rummaged through her pockets and pulled out a crumpled envelope.
Inside was a brick-like, hard biscuit chunk typically given to prisoners in underground dungeons.
Oknodie's eyes were saying, "To collect the food encyclopedia, I need to eat the unpleasant things at times like this!"

'When did she visit the underground prison...?'

Had she anticipated this moment since being on the cruise ship?
Leape no longer had the energy to be surprised at such thorough preparation.

"Oh right. Thanks to Leape, I'm glad I'll spend less time on the deserted island!"
"I apologize. If it weren't for me, you could have stayed about two more days."
"Ah. I'm not blaming you at all. It's actually a good thing. The condition for the appearance of the <Final Item>, the most valuable item on the deserted island, is that all individual items must be put up for auction, and you must spend more than 10 days on the island, and it must be the day the island disappears."

Where did she get that information, and what was the identity of the item she was so determined to purchase?
While the former remained unknown, the latter became clear.
The next day at noon.
The item finally appeared at Jonna's final auction.

"The final item of the deserted island auction is the <Indulgence>."

Leape's eyes widened.
An indulgence—equivalent to a spare life that could forgive any crime committed in the Foundation.
The only way to survive without carrying out orders that were tantamount to suicide.

"The starting auction price is 10 million points."
"Me! Me!"
"As Oknodie is the only participant, the <Indulgence> has been sold to her for 10 million points upon bidding. With this, the auction on the final day of the deserted island auction has concluded. The auction is now closed."

On the way back to the cruise ship after the auction ended, Leape thought.
The indulgence was the right to refuse orders and a spare life.
It's not easily obtained, but it's worth it.
But would the young lady ever need to use such an ominous item?
Certainly, the young lady's actions up until today had been quite dangerous.
She might have considerably irritated the 'Chairman' whom she called Papa.
But personally, Leape thought it wasn't enough yet.
Given the young lady's abilities, it was highly likely that only manageable orders awaited her.
Oknodie, having obtained the item she wanted, fell into a deep sleep with a satisfied smile.
While gently combing her hair, Leape asked.

"Would you ever need to use the indulgence?"
"There's plenty of reason. She's already carrying out an order."
"Pardon?"

Unlike the confused Leape, Jonna clearly remembered what order the young lady had received.

"The Chairman clearly gave the order: Bring your friends home for a visit during this vacation. This order is valid throughout the summer vacation period, until arrival at the Chairman's mansion."
"...So boarding the cruise ship wasn't everything."
"And there is no mansion at any of the cruise ship's stops or destinations. There is only this deserted island."

That's right.
The young lady has not yet completed the Chairman's order.
And it's not just any ordinary order.
This order is feedback for actions harmful to the Foundation that the young lady committed at the Academy.
It's an order she must carry out as a consequence.
If she fails to carry it out, the next order will be even more severe.
If she fails to carry out even that, the Foundation will unilaterally 'collect' the price.
The [Indulgence] exists to avoid such a harsh future.
This indulgence might be used not in the distant future, but during this very summer vacation.

"But didn't the young lady already plan a mutiny before boarding the ship?"

That aroused Jonna's curiosity as well.
Did she do it knowingly, or unknowingly?
Either way, the young lady had found the answer.
Take over the ship and head to the mansion.
Unlike the dark merchant who was stopped by the Apostle of Anrage before even reaching the deserted island because he lacked strength, despite arriving at the same answer, could the young lady completely take over the ship?

"We'll soon find out."

In Jonna's hand was the sight doll that Gisele had infiltrated, held up for all to see.
