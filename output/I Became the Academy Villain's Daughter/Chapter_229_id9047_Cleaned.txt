# 229 - Prove Your Skills

"Oknodie. The young lady wants to talk to a male student named Jaku about you. Want to come along?"
"Sure!"

I was intrigued by Titosoga's proposal and found something truly fascinating when I went to check it out.

"Who discusses secrets with the person they're about to talk about? Titosoga, you idiot."
"Waaah! I'm sorry, my lady!"
"I told you not to call me 'my lady' anymore, you stupid Soga."
"But... it's stuck in my mouth and I keep calling you 'my lady' without meaning tooo..."

While Arcadia was thoroughly scolding Titosoga, I approached Jaku and held out my hand.

"Can I see the letter?"
"Sure."

━━━
I already know that you are a scholarship student of the Wiheomhae Foundation.
I also know that you owe Oknodie a debt of gratitude.
If you want to repay that debt, follow the child who delivered this letter to where I am.
I hope you won't refuse.
I'm already fully aware of your lecture schedule and assignment status, and I know you have no further plans since your social relationships are terrible.
━━━

"Wow, that's harsh. Hitting someone with no friends with the cold, hard truth!"
"...Don't you think you're the worst for saying that to my face? And don't arbitrarily treat me like someone with no friends. I do have friends."
"Who's your friend?"
"I can't say."
"See? You don't have any after all."
"......"

Though he's playing dumb, I've already figured out the psychology of this quiet first-year extra NPC.

"You're worried that if you tell me, that friend will also be treated as a foundation scholarship student, right?"
"...!"

Jaku stepped back in surprise, breaking into a cold sweat. He's definitely scared stiff.

"Hmm... is it someone I know?"
"N-no, it's not."
"Why are you lying? I saw you before. You were in a club with Mob."

When I poked his side with my finger while chuckling, Jaku's expression turned absolutely pale.
Seeing his reaction, Arcadia scolded me instead of Titosoga.

"Di. It's improper for a young lady to whisper to a male student and make him squirm."
"Yes~"
"Sigh. But I suppose you've always been like this. Since we're here anyway, I'll just be straightforward."

Arcadia realized her secret game had failed and decided to be honest about her thoughts.

"I suspect the Foundation has issued an assassination order against Di."
"What?!"
"Please be honest with me. Di's testimony might someday help free her from the Foundation."
"That's nonsense."

Jaku replied cynically.

"Don't talk like it's someone else's problem. Do you think the Foundation would quietly watch one of their scholarship students leave? They would definitely retaliate."
"We have the Academy."
"Right. The same Academy that attacked your Sebiche family until they were demoted to an earldom. I'm sure they'd just let Oknodie go so innocently."

Arcadia's face fell as if she'd been accused of making a personal mistake after spending money on a mobile game.

SLAP!

"ARGH!"
"Jaku. No mean comments!"
"Are you trying to kill me, you monstrous brat?!"
"Hey now!"
"I-I'm sorry."

As soon as I raised my hand, Jaku immediately surrendered.
Why act tough when you'll quiet down after one hit?

"Sister Arcadia. I don't care whether you're a young lady or an earl's daughter."
"Di...!"

I knew from the beginning.
If she fails the event, she'll fall to commoner or slave status, and even if she succeeds, she'll have to leave the duchy.
The Sebiche family will only become a ducal family again after Arcadia graduates, achieves great success, and is honored by His Majesty the King to become a duchess.

"But if you're asking whether the Foundation issued an assassination order, I can only say I don't know. Though I think they probably didn't!"
"You're evading the question about your own situation... Are you unable to answer? Afraid I'll be in danger if I get involved with Foundation matters? Don't be ridiculous! You got involved in our family's dangerous affairs, but I can't do the same for you? That's unfair!"

I understand how she feels, but this is really troublesome.

"I really don't know about this!"

Would the Clown Mask Group have ordered an assassination on my body's original owner?
Considering no one recognized me, probably not.
But based on circumstances, scholarship students did seem to pass through these kids.
Yet when I recall my first day of possession when I met Jonna at the inn, it seems unlikely again.
It's like looking at a stock chart and not knowing whether it's going up or down.

"So you're going to keep evading like this? Whenever something seems dangerous, you'll put yourself in danger alone without telling anyone, keeping secrets from us. Is that how you plan to continue living?"

Arcadia's eyes trembled.
They filled with great sadness that wasn't visible even when Jaku mentioned the declining Sebiche family.
Aww.
I didn't mean to make Arcadia sad.
But there's nothing I can do.
There aren't any high-point events with Arcadia in the schedule for a while anyway.

"Yep."

A player can't be led around by NPCs because of emotional attachment.
Sometimes cold, sometimes resolute.
Knowing when to draw the line is how you follow an efficient veteran's route!

"Di..."

I waved goodbye to the shocked-looking Arcadia.

"I have things to do, so I'll go first! It was nice seeing you today!"


* * *


Oknodie ran away with light steps and disappeared like a ghost.
Arcadia's face, which had been following her retreating figure, filled with dismay.
As expected of one of the "Butler's" children, who are called the devil's children for their demonic talents and corresponding misalignments.
Moreover, as befitting the top scholarship student, Oknodie drew a cold line with Arcadia.

"Did you expect to have a genuinely supportive relationship with someone from the Foundation? How foolish."
"You don't need to go that far. Do you really need to hurt Lady Arcadia more when she's already wounded?"

The overly kind Titosoga, torn between chasing after Oknodie or comforting Arcadia, ultimately stayed by Arcadia's side and pointed accusingly at Jaku with wide eyes.
Of course, he didn't look intimidating at all since his face naturally appeared as harmless as a small, timid animal.
Sometimes such weakness can exert power.
Jaku felt a twinge in his chest.
It was the sound of his conscience screaming.

"Bullshit."

Those who followed the voice of conscience when it spoke never became scholarship students.
They either failed the entrance exam or were filtered out earlier in the Foundation's own verification tests for prospective apprentice scholars.
If you weaken, you fall behind.
If you fall behind, you receive harsher orders.
And what awaits after that is only death.

"Don't you think you're the selfish ones?"
"How is Lady Arcadia being selfish?!"
"She demanded acts of treason against the Foundation without knowing how vast its influence truly is. That's something one should never ask of Oknodie. Even more so for me."
"Coward."
"Can you say the same thing to Oknodie's face?"

Titosoga stuck out his tongue and turned away.

"Let's go, Lady Arcadia. There's nothing to discuss with such a super coward."

Arcadia was led away by Titosoga's hand, looking dejected.
Jaku shouldn't be drawn to that pitiful figure.
This wouldn't benefit either of them.
He kicked at the ground and followed the two.

"Wait."
"What is it, Mr. Super-Super Coward?"
"I'll tell you the Foundation's next move."
"What?"
"If you really want to save Oknodie from the Foundation's grasp, then stop their next move. If you can't even do that much, give up gracefully. And don't bother Oknodie or me again."

Life returned to Arcadia's lifeless gaze.
Jaku's heart sent frantic signals at her expression of renewed hope.
Hope is the name for the worst despair sealed at the very bottom of Pandora's box.
Shallow sympathy will only put these fools in greater danger.
Nevertheless, he ultimately couldn't withstand the voice of his conscience.

"Next week, there will be a sports festival at the Academy. The Foundation plans to injure upper-class students one way or another during the event."
"Is their target all upper-class students regardless of who they are?"
"Yes. This is a common directive issued to all lower-class scholarship students. There's no penalty for failure, but success will raise your evaluation by the Foundation."

He'd said it.
He committed to this even knowing that if they became vigilant and started looking for suspicious individuals, they would be the first to catch the Foundation's eye.
Having gone this far, it was better to dive in completely than to pull back halfheartedly.

"I'll give you a hint. The Foundation wants the Academy students to despair. Despairing students are easier to reach out to and manipulate. The quickest way to spread despair is to further accelerate the collapse of balance between the Frontier faction and the Imperial faction."
"So the targets are upper-class students from the Frontier. That's what you're saying."
"What! But that includes Oknodie and Lady Arcadia!"

From Titosoga's outcry, Arcadia realized something.
Perhaps it was a forbidden realization that she shouldn't have come to.

"If it doesn't matter who the target is, then I could become the target too. If I deliberately show vulnerability... scholarship students would flock to threaten me like moths to a flame, right?"
"Perhaps."
"Thank you. For giving us this opportunity."
"We'll see."

Jaku thought to himself.
Whether this would be an opportunity to prove they had the ability to stand against the Foundation, or whether it would be the catalyst to end Arcadia's Academy life that Oknodie had finally permitted.
He began to feel somewhat afraid of the approaching sports festival day when the results would be determined.
