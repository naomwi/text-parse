# Enhanced Translation: Chapter 277 - An Offer That Cannot Be Refused

The final exams were over.

"That's a lie. That guy was a loser just like us. How did he manage to get such high scores?"
"I heard he joined the 'Organization that Plays with Oknodie' too."
"Isn't that just a group of gatherers and herb collectors who catch bugs and dig up medicinal herbs with no real purpose?"

While the mid-term exam sensation was caused by Oknodie, who takes care of her people, and Ishtar, who only knows herself, this final exam's sensation was caused by students belonging to the "Organization that Plays with Oknodie."

"Did they leak the answer sheets or something?"
"That must be it. There's no other explanation."
"There were rumors that Professor Bronze, Oknodie's mentor and the noble thief, stole answer sheets from other professors. Like mentor, like student—they definitely stole them!"
"Let's go confront them."
"The professors will definitely give them penalty points!"

These students couldn't accept that people who were no different from them—or even those who neglected training by playing outside—received higher scores.
Among these students was someone with ambitions to build a reputation and establish a new organization.

"Everyone, would you allow me to help you?"
"Prince Hector!"
"Prince of which country?"
"You know, the prince of Troy, that small southern country."
"The only son of Heather, the founding king and famous general, but rumored to be inferior to his father...?"

He may be a prince, but he's from a weak, small country.
The students who had felt intimidated lowered their guard.

'This is a good opportunity. Oknodie, who surrounds Casia—the chance to build influence to counter that child doesn't come easily.'

Casia's archenemy.
One who had been diligently growing stronger with his followers, awaiting the day of revenge.
Unlike other organizations that exercised their influence, Prince Hector and his followers had quietly observed most major events at the academy.
They received no one's attention and faced no one's opposition.
They simply quietly recruited talented individuals from behind the scenes who could strike at Casia, as well as those who harbored hostility toward Oknodie who helped her.

"Leave the financial support to me. Though not as powerful as the Dark Association, the influence of the Yui Trading Company named after me is certainly not small."

Yui, who would have taken the merchant position instead of Gisele in the original story.
Having failed to advance to the upper class after being outperformed by Dorothy, she had joined under Hector.
Despite her character flaws, Yui—a Tier 2 supporting character and NTR heroine merchant—successfully increased Hector's faction's assets and greatly expanded their influence.
His power was not individual but organizational.
For students going to complain to professors with ugly jealousy, he appeared as the leader of a moderately powerful minor faction—not too overwhelming but a reliable ally.

"What's the problem with that?"

But this crazy professor with flowers in her hair had a mindset as open as her scantily clad appearance.

"In nature, theft is a survival strategy that puts one's life on the line. If caught, you die torn apart by angry predators, but if not caught, you can achieve results beyond your capabilities."
"...Are you condoning cheating?"
"Isn't it rather impressive? That mere first-years succeeded in cheating where even seniors fail. I look forward to the future of these brave mischief-makers who challenge the laws of nature."

What kind of graduates is this person trying to produce?
My face involuntarily contorted.
The faces of the students huddled behind me also twisted unpleasantly.

"If we'd known this, we would have cheated too."
"Why bother studying hard?"
"I think there's some misunderstanding."

As Professor Weird opened the window and extended her hand, a beautiful butterfly that had been fluttering outside perched on the tip of her vine-like fingers.
The students, mesmerized by this beautiful display of communion with nature, stared at the butterfly on her fingertip as if entranced.
To Hector's eyes, there was no difference between the butterfly and these dumbfounded students.

"You fundamentally misunderstand the Gift Academy."
"Misunderstand...? This is the world's finest educational institution where the world's best experts have gathered as professors to nurture the world's greatest talents."
"No, it's not."
"Pardon?"
"The faculty is indeed the world's finest. But we have no intention of nurturing all of you into the world's greatest talents. That's a level permitted only to those blessed with talent, effort, and luck."

Professor Weird, normally known as a perverted plant lady who wraps plant stems around her body and barely covers her private parts with leaves, or as that "bad woman" who says "All done, right?" before quickly erasing the board—right now, she was different.
Standing before them now was a true educator telling students what they needed to learn.

"There are always people who fall behind in the world. In nature too, many plants and animals become extinct. We aim to nurture talented individuals with excellent adaptability who can respond to any situation so that graduates of our academy won't be weeded out in society. At least for the lower classes."
"What does that have to do with the cheating by Oknodie's followers?"
"Let's say the Emperor of the Holy Central Empire dispatched a messenger to the battlefield with an imperial decree about territorial wars. Normally, nobles would just try to gain a little more advantage in territorial wars before the decree arrives."

Hector nodded in agreement.
He believed this was common sense.
If advantageous, conquer as much land as possible; if disadvantageous, resist with determination and fortify defenses.

"But academy graduates would be different. Let's ask a third-year student."

Professor Weird pulled out a senior buried among a pile of stones with one hand.

"What would you do? If your answer satisfies me, I'll overlook your crime of secretly spitting out and selling the practice spirit stones you were supposed to swallow."

The dirt-covered senior answered:

"I would create a fake messenger to deceive the enemy, or kill the messenger with enemy weapons to frame them and incur the Emperor's wrath. If that's impossible, I'd send escorts to the messenger heading to the territory, or destroy waterways or roads to control their arrival time."
"Did you hear that? None of your seniors just sit and wait. Being principled despite incompetence shows a lack of desperation. Such individuals deserve natural selection."

As Professor Weird lightly patted the senior's shoulder, his dirt-covered uniform became as clean as new.
The excited senior shouted "Yahoo!" and raised both arms, but before he could celebrate, the ground collapsed, burying him in the ground once more.

"I overlooked your crime of trying to sell the spirit stones. Now face punishment for calling me 'the chalk devil' and 'the note-taking witch.'"
"..."

The senior's and Professor Weird's responses delivered a great shock to Hector and his followers.
Following rules alone would only lead to the elimination of those lacking ability.
To survive, the weak must employ all manner of wisdom and strategy.
Sticking with Oknodie and stealing answers.
This too was part of strategy.
They did it prepared to face punishment of at least N hours of live burial if caught.
They shared stolen answer sheets through various means.
Neither those who committed the act nor those who benefited could do so without considerable resolve.

'I see. I was still insufficient to face Casia or Oknodie.'

Hector's eyes changed.
They became the eyes of someone close to madness, willing to do anything to avoid elimination.
That gaze was no different from the chilling look in the eyes of third-year students.
