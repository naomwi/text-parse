# 435 - Practical Application

Signs of child abuse toward Oknodie weren't new.

Threats of selling her to a brothel.
Agility training through acrobatics bordering on torture.
Knowledge impartation verging on abuse.
A life perpetually close to death, evidenced by her unbelievably high dark mana affinity at such a young age.

The alarming state of her food collection log, suggesting she'd been force-fed ten meals a day.
The implications of food torture merely added another line to the Foundation's record of abuse.

"Did they make her eat, force her to vomit, then make her eat again?"
"Why would they be so cruel after feeding her such expensive dishes...?"

While the students couldn't understand, Professor Mahabharata noticed.

'She's figured out the Foundation's collection criteria.'

As a Guardian who protected dragons—the species with the most collected items—Professor Mahabharata inevitably recognized the signs.

Those who collect things grow stronger.
The more items collected and the higher their quality, the stronger they become.

Equipment logs only recognize items when ownership is maintained, and equipped items grant additional equipment effects beyond possession effects.
Collection logs grant possession effects just by obtaining preserved specimens, but personally collected specimens provide additional collection effects.

Food logs worked differently.

Collection is only completed when one consumes a standard portion.
Unlike equipment or collected items, the food's characteristics only manifest when eaten.
But this food log had a secret.

'Consumption of a standard portion. Strictly speaking, the criterion is that the food must enter the "stomach."'

Collection effects aren't canceled even if you forcibly empty your stomach after the log registers the food.
Unlike equipment or collected items where effects disappear if lost, food effects remain even after consumption.
The Foundation had exploited this collection feature, subjecting Oknodie to abusive force-feeding and induced vomiting repeatedly.
Her densely packed unique recipe collection status proved this—it couldn't be explained any other way.

"Wow. I really ate a lot!"
"The winner is first-year Oknodie. And... Oknodie, please spare me a moment before leaving the classroom."

Oknodie approached Professor Mahabharata hesitantly, looking puzzled.

"Why? Did I do something wrong?"
"The mischief of Titosoga attaching cellophane to a first-year's spotlight has already been fined, so don't worry about being scolded for that."
"Then why did you call me?"
"Because of your collection log, of course. The food torture the Foundation inflicted on you to make you consume so many dishes is something that occasionally happens among high nobles or royalty who've discovered the secret of collection."
"Wow. Really?"
"But no one has ever been subjected to such cruelty as you have, Miss Oknodie."

Oknodie pouted.

"Papa didn't do anything wrong."
"...Do you want to defend the Foundation's chairman that much? Even though he's the one who made you suffer."
"I ate because I wanted to. Don't speak badly of Papa! He might be a bad person, but he never did anything bad to me."

She's been thoroughly brainwashed.
This is the result of conditioning from a young age.
Professor Mahabharata felt troubled, realizing this was what it took for the Foundation to send their preciously coddled child to the Academy.

"I've taken too much of your time. The second period is about to begin. You may go now."
"Oh no. It's Professor Bronze's class."

Oknodie made a sad face and jumped out the window, taking a shortcut.
Professor Mahabharata muttered sympathetically while watching her leave:

"Using windows for entry or exit, 50 points fine upon detection."

Pity is pity, but rules are rules.
As the grade head trying to draw a strict line, Mahabharata suddenly realized he was judging her too harshly for someone with his usually generous personality.
Why was he evaluating such a pitiful child so meticulously?
Upon reflection, he realized:

'She's so charismatic that none of her actions seem innocent.'

He recalled professors begging the Dragon Principal: "Eek! He's trying to eat us!" or "Please don't be angry, Principal! I'll never pocket experimental funds again!" even when the principal was just yawning without any particular thought.
Strong charisma distorts people's perceptions regardless of the person's intentions.
Beyond Oknodie, the professor's mind turned to someone he'd been acutely aware of until just now.

'The Foundation's chairman, Jonna Wiheomhae.'

If charisma makes people suspicious and conscious of one's every move, how high must the charisma be of that man—the most dangerous person in the world?
If he deliberately used that charisma, how greatly could he influence people's actions and even cause major incidents?
Like father, like daughter—it was a chilling thought.

Professor Mahabharata's homeroom test unexpectedly helped the students.

"Sob sob. The professor brought a vigilante group that robs rich houses in the empire as our test opponents. How can we beat active professionals?"
"Don't be scared, Titosoga. Oknodie and I learned something good during homeroom."
"Something good?"
"When in crisis, just use cuteness to distract. It'll make your opponent falter."

During Professor Bronze's "Everything About Disassembly" test period.
A disassembly competition began where the winner would be whoever unlocked all the locks prepared by Professor Bronze and retrieved the treasure first.
Titosoga, who had been fiddling with locks, made a sad face when she heard the cheerful click of a lock being opened for the third time next to her.
The crybaby Soga who would normally burst into tears immediately!
But today, she had Zuang's advice:

-When in crisis, just use cuteness to distract.

Titosoga naturally appealed with cuteness, as easily as breathing.

"Senior, won't you give me just one hint?"
"Ha, this is cheating. Who told you to act cute during a test?"

While verbally scolding her, the senior's eyes turned toward Titosoga, unable to resist the desire to see her cuteness.

Pop.

"Aaargh!!"

The senior screamed, clutching his eyes as Titosoga turned on her spotlight as if she'd been waiting for this moment.

"This is really cheating!!"
"Objection denied. A proper vigilante should overcome a colleague's interference. The one who lets their guard down is at fault."

Under Professor Bronze's cold declaration, Titosoga continued her disassembly work at a leisurely pace, shining her light in the senior's face whenever he barely managed to open his eyes, making him collapse again.
Watching the aegyo and eye-blinding strategy, Zuang became even more convinced.

"Cuteness really is the ultimate weapon. I should research phrases and actions to bewitch opponents when facing strong enemies."

All the first-years' eyes lit up at Titosoga's aegyo strategy, which even Zuang acknowledged as a great success.

"Senior~"
"Senpai~"
"Stop it! Please just let me take the test!!"

Unprecedented interference began raining down.

"Tito, what did you do with the cellophane?"
"I removed it, of course!! Stupid Oknodie!!"

The light from the swinging spotlight reflected off the metal locks.
Seniors and classmates still taking the test screamed as they were unexpectedly blinded by the delayed light reflections.

"Terrifying kids... they've finished their tests and now they're eliminating competition with area-of-effect interference."
"I heard that Oknodie is the worst, and Titosoga is actually the gentlest."
"Insane. What happens to the test opponents who face her?"

The vigilante leader trembled as he stepped onto the testing ground.

"Then the final round will be between top student Oknodie and the vigilante leader. I've specially increased the difficulty of the locks for you two. Instead, whoever solves it first will receive additional credits or a treasure."

The vigilante leader immediately cast a shield over his eyes to block light.
Similarly, he cast a sound-blocking spell on his ears to avoid being confused by the seniors' tempting whispers.

Unable to see properly or hear properly.
The vigilante leader began his challenge, relying solely on his fingertip sensations!

-Oh, poor thing.
-Poor leader...
-Even demons from hell couldn't be this cruel.

He ignored the faint sighs he could hear.
As long as he didn't get swayed by any tricks, he'd be fine.
Click!
Click!
His confidence grew as he effortlessly unlocked the locks with his fingertips.

'There really are a lot of locks. As expected of a special test. But I can unlock them all!'

The vigilante leader pressed the switch on the mana board hard after unlocking all the locks.
When he removed the spells from his eyes and ears and proudly raised his head, he received sympathetic gazes from everyone present, both vigilantes and first-years.

"What's wrong? I won."
"Good job!"

Oknodie smoothly took away the mana board with the switch.
Professor Bronze clicked his tongue and made his judgment to the leader who still hadn't grasped the situation.

"How pitiful, solving someone else's locks without even knowing what your own locks were."
"What?! No, this was definitely mine from beginning to end!"
"Look at the traces."

Looking behind him, the vigilante leader realized what had happened.
Oknodie held a mana board with locks unlocked from the middle and another mana board with traces of 1.5 portions of unlocked locks.
While his eyes and ears were sealed with magic, that cunning little kid had attacked his mana board, unlocked all the middle locks, and connected them to her own locks.
The locks he had been diligently unlocking were somehow connected to Oknodie's, and he had been earnestly solving Oknodie's test problem instead of his own.

"Victory V!"
"Terrifying kid..."

In the end, Oknodie passed the test and gained 3 experience points in the Scary Child ability.
