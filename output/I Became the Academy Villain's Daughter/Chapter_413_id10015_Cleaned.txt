# <413 - It's Too Early to Give Up>

While Irene, Dorothy, and Rockfell had walked into the safe zone where a senior who craved human organs disguised as a child was waiting, Gisele, Isabel, and Son O-cheon were gathering information before searching for Oknodie.

"Let's exchange information. If you provide us with details about the inside of the leave of absence student zone, we'll tell you about the current situation in the outside world."

Instead of recklessly asking about Oknodie's whereabouts, Gisele approached the situation cleverly.

"This is taking too long. Bookworm, are you sure you're doing this right?"
"Even the seniors of the Esonia Adventure Team first gathered information from locals before acting rashly in new territories. I think Gisele is doing well."

Indeed, Gisele had acquired a wealth of information.

"There are over twenty magical disasters in the safe zone, varying in danger levels. With the skills of first-year advanced class students, we can only handle up to level 2 magical disasters."
"How are these levels determined?"
"They're measured by the depth of will contained in the magical disaster and the danger of monsters or spirits drawn to it. I've also secured a map."

The goal of the students on leave was to obtain the "Treasure" located at the end of the shelter. There were two paths leading to the Treasure.

One: Break through the level 1 magical disaster "Dust Cloud," overcome the level 3 magical disaster "Sky-High Wall," cross the level 5 magical disaster "Lava Zone," and challenge the Treasure via the shortest route.

Two: Avoid the Dust Cloud and Sky-High Wall, and instead challenge the "Complex Disaster Zone" where more than ten level 1 and level 2 magical disasters appear consecutively or simultaneously.

"The former has fewer challengers while the latter has more. Students who failed to obtain the Treasure flock to the latter path to collect 'Lost Items' left behind by other students on leave."
"Oknodie said even in the Library Expedition that shortcuts are dangerous. She would definitely have taken the longer route."
"Wait, is Little Mouse after the Treasure?"

Gisele let out a heavy sigh.

"She caused a huge commotion during class to break into this zone reserved for students on leave. She definitely has an immediate goal she wants to achieve here."
"Why did it have to be now? With Oknodie's skills, she could have grown steadily and challenged the Treasure more easily later."
"In my personal opinion, I don't think she's aiming to retrieve the Treasure."
"Why not?"
"I hear that even an average fourth-year student wouldn't stand a chance at retrieving the Treasure. Apparently, the Treasure is comparable to a double-numbered artifact among the Numbered Artifacts."
"Numbered?"
"You'll learn about this when you become second-years, but the Academy's treasury contains numerous special artifacts."

The Numbered Artifacts rank all artifacts in the world from 1st to 9999th place by value.
Single-numbered: ranked in single digits.
Double-numbered: ranked in double digits.
Triple-numbered: ranked in triple digits.
Quadruple-numbered: ranked in four digits.
Possessing a ranked artifact is something coveted by adventurers, knights, and nobles alike.

"I know about Numbered Artifacts. The Esonia Adventure Team owns several. Any named adventure team would have at least one."

Son O-cheon stood blankly, scratching his head.

"So what does that mean?"
"In the outside world, only a king of a nation or a champion of a race could possess a double-numbered artifact."
"What?! So getting this Treasure is practically the same as becoming a king?"
"One Treasure alone won't get you a country, but you can obtain that level of symbolism and power. The highest achievement a student on leave can reach is tied to that Treasure."

Retrieving the Treasure was something even ordinary fourth-year students wouldn't dare attempt.
This was too much even for Oknodie.

"However, there are three students on leave who are seriously challenging for the Treasure. The murderer Urgas, who has single-handedly retrieved lost items equivalent to a hundred; the sword thief Zeto, who aims for the strongest lost item deep within the Sky-High Wall; and the recluse Ian, who has been in seclusion for over ten years."
"...Is this a zone for students on leave or a lawless land?"
"It's not strange that such powerful individuals would gather for an artifact significant enough to be called a Treasure. In the outside world, this would be something you could only obtain by infiltrating a royal palace and being prepared to make an entire nation your enemy."

The more Isabel heard, the darker her expression became.

"Oknodie does have a collecting habit, but she wouldn't risk danger unnecessarily. This must be... an order from the Foundation, right?"
"It might have been better if she were chasing the Treasure. During my inquiries, I heard from a student on leave who encountered Oknodie. She wasn't after the Treasure but a person."
"A person?"
"Charlotte. 978th batch student. Third-year advanced class student on leave."
"Advanced class student on leave?!"
"Rumor has it that she could have been promoted but intentionally delayed it to enter the zone for students on leave. Most likely to challenge the Treasure."

This was not good news for Isabel.

"If Charlotte is challenging the Treasure, then Oknodie who's following her..."
"Yes. She'll likely follow her deep inside. In the process, she might clash with other Treasure challengers who won't tolerate competitors."

First-year advanced class students couldn't possibly intervene in such a battle between monsters.

"The deadline is within 48 hours. If we fail to rescue Oknodie within two days, she'll step into the territory of the Treasure challengers, and we might have to prepare for her death."

The faces of the three hardened.


* * *


Three Treasure challengers.
The murderer Urgas. The sword thief Zeto. The recluse Ian.
Irene, Dorothy, and Rockfell, who had directly encountered the recluse Ian among them, were so tense they couldn't even wipe away their sweat.

"I don't need help."
"Is that so? That's a shame. Let me know if you change your mind. One can never collect enough mana hearts from talented students."

The tense atmosphere was broken by Zuang, who was calmly conversing with Ian as if she weren't a first-year.

"You can stay in the safe zone as long as you like before leaving. I'll be in Room 1 if you need anything."

After the senior disappeared into Room 1, the three people standing awkwardly in the common area of the dormitory collectively sighed.

"That's a scary senior."
"Phew. I really thought I was going to die. I even held my breath."
"Are you stupid? Only the monsters in our forest get angry at the sound of breathing."
"Hehe. Is that so?"
"..."

Irene decided not to comment further on the strange ecology of the Monster Forest. She was beginning to feel that caring about it would only be to her detriment.

"Zuang. What are you going to do?"
"I have to go back. I followed because I didn't want to lose track of Oknodie, but I lost her anyway."
"Do you think the professor would help if we asked?"
"It won't work. If they could enter themselves, they would have done so already."

While the weak cannot challenge the Treasure, those who are too strong cannot even enter.
As if the Dragon Principal had decreed that those who aren't on leave cannot take the Treasure from this place.

"Let's go back..."

Dorothy's trudging steps toward the entrance suddenly stopped.
It was because of a faint sound coming from beyond the door.

Haha!
Ha-haha!
Aaah-hahaha!

With such a distinctive laugh, there was only one person who came to mind.

"Mandela Castella?!"
"Correct!"

Bang!

"Kyaak!"
"What a girlish scream. The delusion that such a lovely scream makes you look cute—I, Mandela Castella, don't dislike it!"
"W-what? Why is Senior Mandela here?!"

Appearing with an imposing presence, her eyes twinkling like stars, she proudly declared:

"Professor El Draco has promised that if you successfully rescue Miss Oknodie, he will overlook the outrageous act of attacking first-years and impose no future disadvantages!"

For those who had merely been caught in the hail caused by a kraken's tentacles at the coastline, this was bewildering news, but the situation itself wasn't bad.
Mandela Castella, the second-year top student, was helping to rescue Oknodie!
There was a sense of expectation that the stalled search for Oknodie would make significant progress.
Of course, expectations were just expectations.
The cruel reality remained unchanged.
The fact that the situation was not favorable remained constant.

"Senior, you might be able to go ahead, but this is our limit. Please help Oknodie."
"Hahaha! You're underestimating yourselves too much. It's too early to give up. Everyone, please take out your protective charms."
"I have it, but why?"
"What did Professor Red Mountain say when he gave you the protective charm?"
"Um... he definitely said we could go as far as the entrance of a magical disaster with this... but we've already passed the entrance."

Mandela Castella laughed confidently.
The three people suffering from the ear-piercing laughter right in their faces could only hold their ears as they rang painfully.

"Haven't you realized yet? That charm sets the limit at the entrance when you 'enter without doing anything.'"
"Without doing anything...?"
"Do you understand? You've reached halfway into the magical disaster without even using your protective charms!"
"!!"

That's right.
They could still advance.
Toward where Oknodie was, beyond this point.
Their training hadn't been in vain.
They still had a chance to follow Oknodie's trail.
