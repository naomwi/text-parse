# Enhanced Translation: Chapter 8 - The One-Trick Butler

I must have used too much strength yesterday, because today no one was eager to challenge me to arm wrestling.

"Hmph. How long do I have to wait?"

At least when I'm training, I feel time passing.
Standing still and waiting is truly torturous.

"Oh, is this rich young lady's entertainment?"
"Ah, a challenger... has... huh...?"

I kept looking up and up, but there seemed to be no end.
Tilting my head back until my neck nearly broke, I saw a giant bearded man grinning down at me.
This massive, hairy monkey beastkin, who must have been at least 2.3 meters tall, said:

"Hey, how about a match with me? You don't need to bet money. In fact, if you win against me, I'll give you one gold coin."
"Oh, I can't hear you, can't hear a thing."

I quickly hid behind my butler, which prompted jeers from the challengers who had been waiting their turn after being pushed back yesterday: "Booo!" "Coward!" "Accept the challenge, Superhumanly Strong Girl!"
These people, really.
What are they saying to a frail girl who occasionally drops cups and spoons?
My butler gave them a disapproving look and said:

"I'm sorry, but the size difference between you and my lady is far too great."
"I heard your lady has been defeating opponents despite her small stature."
"What's your waiting number, sir?"
"What if I bet money instead?"
"We have plenty of money ourselves. A young lady with a butler and maid would hardly be in financial straits."

The monkey beastkin looked disappointed but approached the people at the front of the line.
I wondered what he was doing—he took out a gold coin and shuffled it rapidly between his hands, moving them left and right.
The first person in line, with wide eyes and an uncertain expression, pointed to the left hand, but the gold coin was in the right.

"Your training is lacking, young friend. Haha."

He gave a hearty laugh while lightly patting the person's back with his palm, sending them tumbling out of line.
It seemed they had bet their place in line for the gold coin and failed miserably.
Watching the monkey beastkin enter the inn, I had an idea.

"Excuse me. Would you like to make a bet with me too?"
"The strong girl? I'm not arm wrestling."
"Not arm wrestling—the coin game. It's a 50% chance, seems fair enough, right?"
"Hmm..."
"Are you afraid you'll lose to me?"

The man with a sword at his waist shook his head.

"If I get distracted by one gold coin and lose time, I might fail the exam. That would be putting the cart before the horse."

Surprisingly principled.
Instead, a man who looked well over 20 gestured to me from behind.

"I'm just here to get tickets and sell them. Let's play, young lady."

A ticket hunter.
These are people who sell tickets to those wanting to take the entrance exam, like scalpers.
In the game, people use them when they don't have time to take the ticket exam or when their basic stats are ridiculously low. But it's always a gamble.
There's a chance you'll be rejected at the entrance exam for having a counterfeit ticket.

"Nope, you guessed wrong~"
"Heh. I missed."

The swordsman who had been watching our bet clicked his tongue, glad he hadn't participated.

"Not only strong but quick-handed too. I would have been thoroughly embarrassed if I'd challenged you blindly."
"Still slower than that big beastkin from earlier."

You can tell just by looking at his muscle mass.
That guy earlier was definitely a monster with at least 40 strength.
What's puzzling is that I've never seen that beastkin at the Academy.
Has he failed every time?
Or was he rejected due to the age limit?
Considering his brutish appearance, it's likely he came to apply without knowing about the age restriction.

"Group guests departing! We'll take five more people now. Come in!"
"Finally!"

What I thought would take a week only took two days.


* *


As soon as we entered, the warm air and peaceful atmosphere naturally relaxed my body.

"Well, well, if it isn't the strong young lady?"
"Just give me a room, please."
"We have two four-person rooms and two single rooms available. Which would you prefer?"
"One four-person room."

No reason to save money when we have it.
I immediately grabbed a table on the first floor and picked up the menu.

"Everyone seems to be waiting for the senior examiner."
"I don't care about that—I want to fill my stomach first."
"Prioritizing food is very like you, my lady."

I didn't even blink at my butler's criticism.
This is all about survival!

From the menu, I ordered three bowls of apple walnut oatmeal, a dish hard to find elsewhere.

"Is there a reason you insist on this menu item?"
"Because I can't eat it anywhere else."

In the game, this was the only place I found that served apple walnut oatmeal.
For some reason, Jonna started making an expression that suggested he'd gladly make oatmeal for a week if only he could cook for me, but that's become common now.
I naturally ignore it.

Collecting food items is something you should diligently pursue for stat increases.
Of course, eating two bowls doesn't double the collection effect, so one bowl is for me and the other two are for Jonna and Leape.

"My lady really loves trying new foods."
"I've had enough experience eating the same food over and over."
"But it's a dangerous habit."

While Leape only says pleasant things, Jonna doesn't spare me the harsh truths.
This is where the difference between a maid and a butler becomes apparent.

"It's difficult to immediately identify what foreign substances might be in unfamiliar food from distant places."
"That hardly ever happens, you know?"

What is he talking about in an Academy game?
Anyone listening would think we're trying to enter a ninja academy or something.

'Anyway, eating lots of the same food isn't very efficient.'

While eating just one of a new food item produces a collection effect, you need to eat the same food a thousand times for a similar effect.
That's how you get titles like Apple Pie Maniac or Strawberry Shaved Ice Maniac.
But unlike rewards from increasing your collection rate by 10 or 20 items, these maniac titles give you effects even if you only earn one.

'Was the Shaved Ice Maniac effect a 1% increase in ice resistance and prevention of brain freeze?'

It might seem useless, but every title has its purpose at least once.
During the Academy's summer festival food-eating contest, having this title lets you quickly finish a bowl of shaved ice without slowing down.
Tooth sensitivity and fullness are separate issues, of course.
Without specific goals for title acquisition, most are a waste of time.
Rather than eating the same dish a thousand times, it's a hundred times more efficient to eat a thousand different dishes.

'No wonder there's a gimmick that players are people who eat different food every time.'

Most people don't easily try unfamiliar foods, but players prefer them.
Sometimes you suffer through bizarre foods like fluorescent chicken or twisted pollack, but apple walnut oatmeal wasn't that bad.

"Nom nom nom."
"Look at the little one eating with such gusto."
"Hmm. Should we get a bowl too?"

The guests filling the inn's first floor restaurant and bar began ordering the breakfast menu, succumbing to the rich aroma of oatmeal.
The innkeeper brought three glasses of apple juice to our table as a service, thanking us for increasing his sales.

"Why so little? Fill my helmet to the brim!"

The monkey beastkin at a nearby table was being difficult, but since he offered to pay extra, the innkeeper happily took his helmet.
Hygiene aside, he's quite a spirited fellow.

"Absolutely not."
"...I didn't even say anything?"
"It is absolutely forbidden."

My butler Jonna's vigilance shows no signs of diminishing today.
I've discovered an unexpected downside to playing nobility.
The freedom is too restricted.

Slurp.
Sip sip.

While I was sucking up the last drops of apple juice to quell my frustration, someone gently knocked on our table with the back of their hand.

"Well, who do we have here? If it isn't my travel companions!"
"Mr. Bard!"

What an unexpected face.
He didn't look as strong as the monkey beastkin, so how did he get into the inn so quickly?

"When did you get in?"
"Haha. I knocked on the inn door yesterday and was let in immediately—not as a guest, but as a musician."

So that's how.
In the game, I wondered who would choose such a garbage profession, but now that it's reality, I'm insanely jealous of bards.

"Absolutely not."
"Tch. I didn't even say anything, but it's always 'not allowed.'"

I'm starting to dislike my butler a little.

"You three came for the ticket exam, right?"

Mr. Bard leaned in and spoke in a low voice so other tables couldn't hear.

"From 6 AM to 6 PM, an examiner comes down to the first floor once every hour. Listen carefully to what they say when that happens."
"Why are you giving us this information?"
"I made your butler quite angry last time, and I'm sure the young lady was startled too. I felt guilty and wanted to apologize by offering this helpful tip."

My butler's expression softened slightly.
Is this my chance? Could I switch to the bard class?
I glanced at him cautiously, and his answer came back sharp as a knife:

"Absolutely not."

I can't say anything around here.
