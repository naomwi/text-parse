# 193 - 70 Consecutive Victories

The morale of the Imperial faction students was at its peak.
After experiencing the mysterious attack, the Imperial students' anger had reached its limit.
Skilled fighters like Dorothy, Sand Cooker, and Rogini had only managed 10 consecutive victories before falling to the targeted attacks of the skilled opponents.
Even worse, the remaining 49 students had been defeated in succession by the Imperial faction's ace who had taken down Rogini!
Thanks to this, an incredible record of 50 consecutive victories had been achieved on the Imperial side.

"That's the all-time record for most consecutive victories in the first-year first-semester inter-class competition."

Despite Professor Rave's provocation, which should have angered Professor Weird whose disciples had been defeated, she was only growing the flower bud on her head.
Wriggle, wriggle... pop!
Professor Weird had finally bloomed a flower on her head, nourished by the warm afternoon sunlight and the screams and despair of the students!

"Huh? What did you say?"
"...I said the match is over now."
"As always, you're talking nonsense. The outcome was decided from the beginning."
"Are you giving up? Well, I suppose. With a student ratio of 1 to 70, you must have realized how pathetic your disciples' skills are."
"They may be pathetic, but you'll be the ones losing."
"Do you really think you can win? Just one Oknodie against 70 students?"
"That child is strong. Stronger than you think."
"On what basis do you believe that?"
"I've looked into the source of that child's power."

This was concerning information, too significant to dismiss as the ravings of a madwoman with a flower on her head.

"I fed stones to the students and tested how their mana increases, and I got results."
"Stones...?"
"When I fed her mana stones, her mana increased."
"What about the impurities?"
"She digested only the mana and expelled everything else through shape transformation. That child's mana capacity is extraordinary."
"Enough to handle 70 opponents?"
"70? Such a number means nothing to that child. She could handle 100 by herself."

This was shocking news—news that Professor Rave, who had been secretly rejoicing at the chance to get revenge on Oknodie, couldn't believe and didn't want to believe.

* * *

As I stepped onto the arena, a surprisingly energetic opponent from the other side taunted me.

"Too bad. If I had defeated you as the 50th, my name would have become much more famous. Well... I suppose taking you down as the 51st will be enough to tarnish your reputation."

This could be considered an unfair situation with the appearance of a strong opponent, but to my experienced eyes, it just looked like a cute tantrum.
Rather, by defeating someone on a winning streak, I could earn the ace-slayer bonus, now upgraded to an ace-of-aces slayer bonus for taking down someone with 50 victories.
Thanks to the increased reward for those with sufficient skill, this unfair situation becomes a positive variable.

"Congratulations. For an extra, you've done quite well!"
"...That arrogant attitude of looking down on others won't continue after this match ends."
"What's your name?"
"Pepe, devotee of the love god Ataraxia. With the power of faith and my infinite stamina, which remains unchanged from before the battle, I'll make you submit!"

More than the strange name, the prefix before it made me blink.
Ataraxia... Atara/xia... Sia?
The sudden appearance of my childhood friend's name struck me like an arrow to the chest.

"Begin."

The match began with Professor Rave's arrogant sneer.
A sinister pink energy coated Pepe's entire body like a film.
This was a divine magic of the body-strengthening type that had instantly crushed Rogini and 49 other students.

"I've been wanting to ask since you first used that—is it allowed to use divine magic in the Imperial Magic Studies Basics and Understanding class?"
"In the Empire, faith is everyday life!"

Pepe charged at me with tremendous speed, like an active knight drawing on mana cultivation techniques to strengthen his body.
Now I understand the secret behind the 50 consecutive victories.
No matter how much endurance and agility training one does, that's training to avoid a mage's long-range magic, not to face off against a knight-level opponent.
In short, it's a matter of compatibility.
Light-armored evasive mages excel at avoiding the stationary spell-casting attacks of Imperial mages.
Close-combat mages overwhelm the inadequate stamina and agility of light-armored evasive mages.
Against someone who has so obviously gone all-in on physical specs, the only answer is to crush them with the powerful firepower typical of Imperial mages.

'Since Rogini and Sand Cooker came from the Magic Tower, they at least had a chance with firepower.'

But this isn't rock-paper-scissors, and there's no rule saying you must defeat rock with paper.
If the rock is strong enough, like Pepe who defeated Rogini, it can tear through paper.

Thwack, thwack, thwack.

Indeed, this was no ordinary rock—my palm stung as I blocked the incoming punches.
This definitely wasn't the power of an extra.
Occasionally, such people end up in the lower class.
"Why are they in the lower class?"
"Why are they extras?"
Students who seem to have upper-class specs by anyone's standards.
The reason is simple.
They encountered existing upper-class students during the entrance exam and suffered unfair treatment.
No matter how experienced or skilled you are, if you accidentally sit on a bush and aggro Xing who was sleeping inside, you'll completely mess up that round of admission.
It's like a natural disaster.
Assuming you didn't start trouble with that insane recklessness in the first place.

'This one must have shown such recklessness every time!'

I don't know who they encountered during the entrance exam, but judging by the fact that I've never seen this NPC before, they probably didn't even remain in the lower class but died outright.
Given the ruthlessness they faced, they were likely Xing's prey.

"The upper class isn't all that great. Even the top student of the year is just desperately running away when confronted!"

Anyway, this student could have been my classmate, so rather than finding comfort in having fallen to the lower class, they must be burning with resentment.
Having achieved 50 consecutive victories, they probably also think about getting revenge by defeating Oknodie, a comrade of the upper class that rejected them.
I understand.
Though I have no intention of letting them win.

"Dark Side."

Category - Dark Magic, Alchemy
Activation - Equivalent Exchange
Specialization - Body Support, Sustained Manifestation, Composure
Penalty - Personality Erosion

I envelop my entire body with dark mana transformed into fighting spirit.
It's my application of the full-body fighting spirit technique that Pepe demonstrated, but in a dark mana version.

"Controlling fighting spirit without faith!?"
"Fighting spirit can originally be handled without any connection to faith. Your god is just conveniently exploiting it."

I wrap dark mana around my entire body to use as fighting spirit.
Naturally, the power of body parts covered with fighting spirit increases dramatically.
Unlike before, Pepe now frantically retreated.
As I chased and landed hits from various angles, Professor Rave, who had been silently observing while Pepe was on the offensive, suddenly raised an objection.

"This isn't a magic duel."
"Professor Rave. Isn't that too shameful? We've been tolerating this throughout their 50 consecutive victories."
"Kuk...!"

He had established the precedent himself.
Though accusations of cowardice came from our side first, Professor Weird, who seemed to be permissive due to her characteristically lazy nature, had actually set a trap.
The professor's laziness became a noose tightening around Professor Rave and Pepe's necks, based on unexpected principles.
Did she know?
That my specialty was body-strengthening magic?
A 2m 30cm warrior would naturally specialize in body-strengthening support magic.
If she guessed that without knowing it for certain, that shows remarkable insight.

"Angel Punch!"

White wings spread around the fist of Pepe, who was driven into a corner, emitting a dazzling light.
Despite the dramatic shout, it was essentially a blinding punch.

"How underhanded!"
"Using cheap tricks until the end!"

Despite the flood of criticism, Pepe confidently threw a straight punch, but my palm caught it perfectly.

"How...!?"
"When you cast dark magic on your own eyes, it becomes sunglasses!"

I fought just fine even when Io of the Blue Fist was flashing his fists, so there's no way I'd fall for a similar trick.
After experiencing enough unfair patterns, you realize they're limited in variety and can easily block most of them.

"You tried hard and did well. Now please give me the points!"
"No!"

I smirked at the magic that Pepe, unable to accept defeat, fired indiscriminately.

Category - Dark Magic, Enhancement Magic
Activation - Giant Punch
Specialization - Body Support, Rapid Overcharge, Protection

What I envision is always a strike delivered with my 2m30cm body that has always been mine.
The students' eyes widened at the blow with unimaginable power—a "magic punch" as basic as a "magic missile."

KWAAANG

Pepe collapsed, squeezing out the last of the love god's divine power that flickered like a dying light from the crumbled wall.
It was an overwhelming victory.

"Wow. Wow......"
"We have to face something like that...?"
"If we forfeit, we'll fail, right...?"

The Imperial students were dumbfounded at the realization that they were next, while Professor Rave, unable to accept the defeat, lost his composure before standing up abruptly to glare at me.
In their place, Professor Weird, who had been lazily growing flowers on her head, spoke:

"Winner, Oknodie."

It was the victory declaration I would hear 69 more times.
