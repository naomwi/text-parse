## 224 - I'm Angry

The little lady is not just an innocent girl.

Such a lady could never be just a good child.

"Perhaps an Arcadia noblewoman would have been shocked by your story. But you chose the wrong opponent."
"Gurk, kuk!"
"From the moment you told me that story, I had no choice but to kill you. I don't want the noblewoman to hate the little lady."

The Pierrot Mask Group follows Oknodie.
They are a human trafficking organization that kidnaps children.
They're also closely connected to the slave trade.
The moment they removed their masks and revealed their faces, Gisele's informants recognized them and had already reported their true identities.
From their somehow familiar threats, Gisele could guess where Oknodie's circus training methods originated.
Oknodie had received their education.
In the process, she underwent improper development from a young age when her personality was still forming.
The occasional glimpses of eeriness amid her innocence must be the result of the wrong common sense instilled through their education.
Perhaps she had already bloodied her hands.
But if she used the Pierrot Mask Group as bait.
If they were the ones who educated her, instilled wrong values, and forced her to walk the path of evil.
Colleagues, peers, friends.
Whatever you call them, if Oknodie grew up watching those who failed to produce sufficient results during their upbringing either die by their hands or be sold off.

"No one will sympathize with things like you being used and discarded."

The truth was heavy, but not heavy enough for him to abandon Oknodie. She had reasons, qualifications, and consequences appropriate for her actions.

"Gurk!"
"Shhhh. The pain will be brief. Go quietly."
"Grrrrk..."

As he placed the breathless corpse in a nearby trash can, Gisele wiped the blood from his hands with a handkerchief.

"Please take care of the cleanup."

His informants, dressed as cleaners, openly carried the trash can to the main street.
Citizens merely frowned and kept their distance from the dirty cleaners, never suspecting the contents.

He'd been had.
Stomping on the back of a Pierrot Mask member rolling in the mud, the executive of the Seviche family realized:

"The noblewoman has joined hands with other Foundation lackeys."

The Wiheomhae Foundation.
They and the Seviche family had maintained a long relationship.
The Seviche family supplied young children the Foundation needed, and the Foundation provided grown slaves as excellent talents in various fields.
Excellent talents who obey orders and occasionally don't hesitate to dirty their hands.
The exchange of human resources led to trade between companies and exchanges between organizations.
Over generations, the Seviche family's wealth would shrink to less than half in an instant without the Foundation's people.
As the family grew, so did the Foundation.
It wouldn't be strange if the Foundation faction they worked with differed from the one the noblewoman had allied with.

"This is difficult. In a factional fight, we must never support the losing side..."

As with any factional conflict, the moment one side loses power, everyone belonging to that faction gets cut down one after another.

"But we don't have the privilege to choose sides."

The weak are always coerced.
Take this hand, they say.
If you don't, we'll drive you out on the spot.
We'll turn all your efforts to nothing.
We'll even take your life.
It was the same for him.
Starting with threats, they interfered with the family's private matters, and now it was too late to switch sides claiming it wasn't their business.
The moment he took their hand, they forced him to become their accomplice.

"If the noblewoman disappears with those slush funds, we're dead men. We must recover them at all costs."

Identifying the noblewoman was simple.
Her appearance couldn't be hidden by just wearing a robe.
The reverse was also true.
Arcadia was a talented noblewoman skilled in both literary and martial arts.
She wasn't someone who could be overlooked even in disguise.

"But she's already headed to the Academy. We're too late."
"Why don't we return to the family and beg forgiveness, or just run away now?"
"Foolish boys."

He sighed deeply.
Did they believe they could somehow get away with it because their hands were less dirty than his, their leader?
Short.
Their thinking was far too short-sighted.

"You still don't understand?"
"I don't get it. Why can't we run away?"
"Even if the family falls, those hunting for its assets will pursue us to the end. And those assets include our lives."
"...!"
"Do I need to explain more clearly?"
"Let's run away. If you come with us, we can make it somehow!"
"I see. You needed an even clearer explanation. Even after all this, it wasn't enough."

The executive heaved a heavy sigh.
The smoke that escaped from his lips enveloped the face of his subordinate who had been urging escape until the end.

"Kuk! Kuk, kuhak!"

His face turned pale blue as he clutched his throat and pounded his chest in agony.
Cloud, the surveillance captain leading the Seviche family's monitors, shook his head at the subordinate's resentful gaze.

"Don't look at me like that. I gave you chances many times. I told you not to have foolish thoughts. To give up quietly. Yet you still didn't understand."
"Khuuk! Hueeeeee...!"

The desperate struggles of the subordinate who had lost his breath were just that—struggles.
There was already a skill gap that would have made victory impossible even with a healthy body, and now he couldn't even breathe properly, let alone muster strength.
Cloud easily avoided the wildly swinging blade, grabbed the sword handle, and kicked the legs out from under him, causing him to collapse.
The sight of the subordinate dropping his weapon, clutching his throat, and crawling on the floor was the very definition of misery.
But this brief pain was better than living a wretched life being hunted by the family forever.

"Look. This is the most intact death a traitor can hope for."

Fear filled the eyes of the other monitors as they watched the one who died in such a miserable state.

"School life dreaming of love and friendship. Work life dreaming of tomorrow and the future. Such things are not permitted for you. The family has bestowed grace upon you. The Foundation has also bestowed grace. Repay that grace."

Now you're speaking correctly.

"From here to the Academy, we can't move with a single teleportation circle due to the distance. First, we must catch up before they use the final teleportation circle to the Academy. After that..."

The noblewoman's boldness had always been admirable, but now she would have to feel regret.

"After that, I'll make it rain with my magic. The teleportation circle cannot function while it rains. Those 10 minutes will be your first and last chance for revenge."

If they fail, only death awaits.
The pursuit by the Seviche family's surveillance team had begun.

"Ugh, I hate it..."
"Di, do you dislike rain?"
"When it rains, troublesome events always follow."
"Fufu. That's true. I was really surprised when the heavy rain warning came as a weekly event, wasn't I? I never imagined we'd have to take a boat to school in the morning."
"That's part of it."

As always, Oknodie was having a conversation that was oddly off-point.
Meanwhile, Gisele quietly approached his secret bodyguard who was protecting him while concealing his identity.

"How is the security situation?"
"A mana field blocking communications has just been deployed."
"...!"

Something is beginning to happen.
No sooner had he thought this than a flaming bomb flew through the teleportation station window, shattering it.

BOOM!

Mining. Subjugation. War.

If someone was using them for terrorism, there was only one group that came to mind.

"Arcadi...!"
