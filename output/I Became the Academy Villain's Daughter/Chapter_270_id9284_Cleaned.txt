# 270 - The Organization That Pays Friend Fees

The foundation scholarship students were extremely flustered by April's summons.

While she occasionally delivered directives, this was the first time she had called for a group assembly.

Foundation scholarship students fundamentally kept their identities hidden.
They were essentially spies infiltrating the Academy.
Like a cell organization, even among themselves, they didn't know who the other scholarship students were.

'What are all these people?'
'What is she planning to make us do?'
'Why are outsiders being called along with foundation business?'

Unlike the ordinary scholarship students who couldn't hide their uneasiness, Jaku, who had somewhat figured out the foundation scholarship students, realized that everyone here was a scholarship recipient.

"Jaku. So you're here too."
"Primer. Don't stick to me, it's annoying."
"Aren't you scared? If scholarship students like us were called together, then probably everyone here is..."
"Your thinking is correct."
"I knew it! Scholarship students gathered in one place. There must be some extraordinary directive coming down!"

Could it be that they're afraid of the 981st class students' talents and will order us to kill as many classmates as possible before committing suicide?
What if they force us to wear bombs and send us on suicide missions?
If I run away, would I be able to avoid getting caught?
Should I request protection from the Academy?
But the foundation would surely retaliate...!

As these thoughts cascaded one after another, Primer circled around someone he knew out of fear, while Jaku calmly sat down and waited.
If no one else knew, he did.
That April had been accompanying Oknodie.
If that was the case, today's meeting wasn't a foundation summons but Oknodie's.

"Hello, everyone!"

At the appointed time.
The scholarship students, bewildered by Oknodie's sudden appearance from the deserted forest, were shocked to see April standing at attention behind her.

"Today, Oknodie, the head scholarship student of the foundation, has special directives for you all. Please listen carefully to her instructions so that she doesn't have to explain twice."

What they had vaguely anticipated—through anxiety, intuition, or clear observation—was coming true.
A foundation directive.
And a large-scale group directive delivered directly by the head scholarship student.
Just how dangerous would this directive be?
Would they survive?
The scholarship students looked up, extremely tense.
Oknodie addressed them.

"Recently, various small groups have begun activities among the first-years. With formidable groups like the 'Hero's Guard,' 'Dark Association,' 'Jigoku Pirates,' 'Camela Division,' and 'Western Nobles Union' taking action, I have no reason to be alone anymore."

Is the foundation's head scholarship student Oknodie finally taking action...!
Tension rose in the scholarship students' eyes.
An organization involving not just them but Oknodie as well.
Activities conducted with her as the leader.
What could the objective possibly be?

"So you all need to form a group with me and follow my instructions! Otherwise, there will be major problems with the foundation's activities!"
"...How can we assist you?"
"Don't be so tense. It's simple—just help me with various errands I'll assign!"

Has the directive-giving authority merely shifted from the foundation spy April to Oknodie?
The astute Primer was relieved that there would be no suicide bombing as he had feared.
That meant he wouldn't have to step forward to make an impression only to end up wearing a bomb vest and charging at the front.

"I have a question."
"Go ahead!"
"If foundation scholarship students gather in one place, the Academy could identify us. After today, will we be operating covertly rather than meeting openly?"
"Don't worry, I have no intention of revealing your scholarship student identities. To hide your identities, I'll bring in plenty of other people into the organization."
"Here is the list of personnel who will be recruited into Oknodie's organization for your reference."

Primer received the roster that April was distributing.
A smile of admiration appeared on his lips.

Black Knight Mob.
Earth Mage Sand Cooker.
Fire Mage Rogini.
These were individuals who had recently gained tremendous recognition in the lower class.
Recruiting skilled individuals is good for enhancing an organization's reputation.
But who is Bikini Warrior Pyoi?

Arcadia the Princess and her three loyal followers: Titoso, Frill, and Canilian Truffle.
Though a group of four women, Arcadia once led the largest faction in the first year, commanding female students from the lower class like clouds.
Perfect for disguising as followers of Arcadia rather than scholarship students.

Hestia.
Someone who frequents the mysterious club called Paper Dungeon.
Though he didn't know much about upper-class students, Oknodie also frequented the same club.
This clearly meant she was actively managing these connections.
He couldn't help but nod at her shrewd choice of networking while running the organization.

There were even more intimidating names.
Xing. And Zuang.
Two upper-class students notorious for their vicious methods and cruelty.
Though different in gender, their dangerousness was equal.
At this point, the power balance was so heavily tilted that it seemed like Oknodie's connections, not the scholarship students, were the main force of the organization.

Even Irene, who had distinguished herself at the sports festival and competed against the Four Heavenly Kings of the second year.
If the Northern Grand Duchess also joined, the strength would be beyond words.
This was the formation of a major organization no less powerful than the "Hero's Guard," which was considered the strongest in combat power.
But who is Lotto?

Yes, Lotto.
Though unknown, if they were a member of the upper class, they must undoubtedly be a mysterious powerhouse.
An individual worth keeping an eye on, having recruited a strong person who hadn't received much attention.

'Is she showing off her connections and political power as the head scholarship student to us...!'

Primer realized it.
The scholarship students now knew each other's identities.
They could join forces and attempt insubordination.
But such attempts were crushed from the very beginning.
What could the scholarship students do against those formidable names?
Even if they ganged up on Oknodie, powerhouses like Hestia, Xing, Zuang, and Irene would step in and wipe them all out in a day.
He had to admit it.
Oknodie wasn't just excellent in academics or powerful; she had surrounded herself with worthy strong allies.
To ensure no one would dare try to usurp her position as head scholarship student or plot rebellion.

"So what's the organization's name? I can't picture how these diverse members will come together."

The name of an organization is important.
The Hero's Guard clearly revealed the identity of an organization centered around the hero.
The same goes for the Jigoku Pirates, Camela Division, and Western Nobles Union, all with distinct identities.
So how would Oknodie unite this strange combination of scholarship students, her allies, and lower-class powerhouses?
If the unification fails, there would be numerous defectors.
The ambitiously prepared organization might disintegrate.
What identity had Oknodie prepared?
In response to Jaku's question, Oknodie cheerfully exclaimed.

"Our organization is 'The Organization That Plays with Oknodie'!"
"..."

Now that's a clear identity.
If the goal is disguise, it might not be bad.
But... is it really okay?
Is it acceptable for an organization to have such a name?
The scholarship students' expressions were uniformly gloomy.

"Even if we accept it, will other students readily accept it?"
"It will definitely be fine!"
"Could you share the basis for that judgment? As a member of the organization, I'm concerned that such an organization name might endanger it from the start."

In response to Jaku's question, Oknodie answered cheerfully, saying there was nothing wrong with it.

"I'll give friend fees to organization members!"
"...Friend fees?"
"In points!"
"...!"

Jaku and all the scholarship students were convinced.
This would work.
No Academy student would dislike points.
If such a fool existed, they would have been expelled long ago.
Compared to senior students' clubs that extort points as club fees or exploit labor, Oknodie's organization was the opposite!
Except for the unfortunate name "The Organization That Plays with Oknodie," it was the ideal organization.
Providing legitimate labor and receiving corresponding friend fees (points) in return.
What could be more ideal than this!

"But 'The Organization That Plays with Oknodie' sounds a bit..."

However, in every organization, there's always at least one tactless person.
Even Primer was wide-eyed at the fearless scholarship student who raised his hand and complained.

"We're scholarship students, aren't we? A secret organization within the organization. Isn't there a separate secret organization name that preserves the foundation's identity?"

Fortunately, the generous Oknodie didn't slap the scholarship student's cheek asking who dared to talk back, nor did she sob saying "You don't like my organization name...?"

"Of course I've prepared a separate name for the secret organization within the organization consisting only of foundation scholarship students!"
"Oh! What's that name?"
"'The Organization That Runs Errands for Oknodie'!"

The scholarship student who had asked the question was confused.

"What's the difference?"
"Errand runners aren't friends, so they don't receive friend fees!"
"...!"

There was a difference.
A very significant one.
