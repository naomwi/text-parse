# Enhanced Translation: Chapter 136 - Oknodie's Future Potential

Ah, I see.

At first, I didn't think much of it, but upon reflection, this wasn't something to just brush off.

"Professor! How could you steal something from Papa's foundation?!"
"...Papa?"
"Papa gave me an allowance to attend the Academy, covered my expenses, found training facilities, and even arranged a guardian to look after me! How could you do such a thing?!"

Professor Bronze's legs wobbled.

"Wait. Oknodie, first-year student. Let me confirm something... By 'Papa,' do you mean your biological father?"
"Yes!"

Papa sent me a note.
Telling me to enter the foundation.
He provided substantial financial support.
As a reward from the Random Papa event.
If someone who fulfills his economic obligation of child support and educational duty for my future isn't Papa, then who else could be called Papa?
After all, there are plenty of "goose dads" in the world who never see their children's faces but faithfully deposit money.

"So Papa is the one who 'educated' Oknodie?"
"If he funded it, then he approved all the training processes, so technically speaking, Papa did teach me!"
"...Indeed. Not an ordinary parent. A parent who would permit 'that kind' of education."
"Did you meet Papa when you went to the foundation?"
"No. I only encountered the watchdog guarding the hat. Don't worry, I didn't harm it in case it was a dog you cared about."
"What kind of professor breaks into a student's home? You went too far this time, Professor."

I tugged at Isabel's waist, making a legitimate complaint and asking for her help.
Isabel, looking like she really didn't want to get involved, reluctantly added a word.

"The professor was wrong."
"Exactly!"
"I agree. This could negatively impact a child's character development. Please apologize immediately."

When Titosoga and Gisele joined in, the professor apologized with a face that seemed to question what she was doing.

"I'm sorry, Oknodie. After this lecture ends, I'll return it to the foundation at an appropriate time."
"That's fine then!"
"...Are you sure it's okay?"
"As long as nobody died, it's fine."

This much is just a minor incident, nothing more.
But I'm more concerned about the hat.

"Now, quickly put your head inside my mouth. I want to glimpse again at those devilish qualities hidden in that delicious brain of yours!"
"..."

It's been saying things like this while clinging to my arm with the hands that have grown from the sides of the hat.

"Okay, I get it, so stop clinging. You're stretching out my already large uniform sleeves."

When I placed the hat on my head, it covered me down to below my nose.
...This hat is too big.
I think I can smell the head odor of previous wearers.
It feels damp and unpleasant.
From outside, I could hear comments like "So cute—!", "She's just a baby," and "The hat covers the kid's whole face. It wouldn't even fit on my head, so the hat punched me instead." The last comment came from Senior Bigstone.
I guess not only is his stone big, but his head must be big too.
What's so great about that senior that makes someone like Senior Rizna hang around him?

"Ooh, ooooh! Ooooooooh!"
"What happened in just a few months?"
"How could someone become this strong?"
"This goes beyond mere growth."
"It's evolution. Yes, evolution!"
"Knight department? Magic department? Administration department? Production department? Adventure department? You could excel at anything! If anything, you have too many talents to know what to choose!"

The commotion grows louder beyond the hat.
At first, having my face completely covered was uncomfortable, but I soon found myself pressing the hat down further to cover my head.
This thing is embarrassing me with too much praise.
This is like public humiliation or a public execution.

"Okay, I get it, so can we wrap this up soon?"
"Oh... are you sure? Couldn't I cover your face a little longer? If given the chance to talk about what you're good at, I could go on for 24 hours!"
"That wouldn't be a proper aptitude assessment. If I'm good at everything, how am I supposed to choose?"

Somehow it noticed the player's talent and says I'm good at everything.
As expected of an artifact owned by the foundation, its performance is just as excellent as the hat at the Academy.
Could it be that it's detecting not only the talents existing in "Oknodie's" body but also my soul—the "possessor's soul" residing in that body—to evaluate talents?

"Well, the professor said that at the Academy, they're curious about what kind of person you'll become in the future, right? Let me tell you about additional potential qualities you possess."

The hat went even further, moving beyond aptitude assessment to recommending suitable professions and suggesting classes.

"First, you have the qualities of a Devil Lord. If you awaken to the essence of evil power, you're qualified to aim for a seat in the Abyss!"

Beyond the hat, I could hear Senior Bigstone's shrill scream and the sound of Senior Rizna slapping his back.

"Your potential as a Dark Princess is also considerable. Your sweet yet deadly charm more than qualifies you to become the most lovable demon in hell!"

I sensed Isabel and Gisele exchanging words in serious tones.

"Perhaps you'd be interested in becoming a Holy Dark Pilgrim? I'd really like that. If so, you could become the master of all dark objects in this world and share the glory of devoting yourself to eternal darkness!"

...But why are all these class titles so ridiculous?
In the game, aptitude tests never calculated the player's "soul," so I never received an evaluation that considered all my death judgments and adventures.
The standard was only the "body" that was randomly given or directly built each playthrough.
Devil Lord?
Dark Princess?
Holy Dark Pilgrim?
Each name was more perplexing than the last.

"Oknodie, first-year student. Take that hat off immediately."
"Don't listen to that cunning professor. She wants to possess you. She's marked you as a tool to satisfy her gloomy desires. You must not be captured."
"Oknodie, first-year! Don't fall for the temptations of that hat with its evil wisdom. Take it off quickly!"

Hmph. It's not like they're wrong, is it?
Professors always try to corrupt talented individuals by turning them into graduate students under the name of disciples.
The professions the aptitude assessment hat is suggesting actually seem much healthier.

"Oh, right. I don't particularly recommend these, but if you really want to, you could become Eddahol's Last Finger, Pignut's Endbringer, or Sonya's Clarity. That is, if you want to serve others and cater to their whims."
"Ugh. I absolutely hate that."
"Good thinking! Those evil gods are all inferior creatures compared to the good gods. They were pathetically crushed in the hegemony war and can't even walk around openly, the losers!"
"Oknodie, first-year student!!"

The professor's shout suddenly pierced through the hat and into my eardrums, causing me to scream "Aaack!" and put my hands inside the hat to cover my ears.
With that movement, the hat slightly shifted upward, and Professor Bronze snatched it away in one swift motion.

"Let go, you thief! I don't allow it. I can't let that child become a mere righteous thief. Someone like you can't nurture this child's brilliant talents!"
"I believe we had an agreement. If you mess with the students, I'll make sure you never leave the laboratory. Do you really want that?"
"Ha, don't make me laugh! If that child borrows my wisdom, there's no class they couldn't achieve. Do you think the key to guiding any of the 12 good gods and 12 evil gods to lordship should be used merely for picking secular locks?"

The hat stretched out both hands, swinging them like windmills and throwing punches rapidly.

"...Sigh. If you had exerted even half of that creepy wisdom as actual power, I would have seriously destroyed you right here. You're lucky to be alive."

The professor, with a disgusted face, took out a dagger and repeatedly stabbed the hat, causing cries of "Ouch, ow, not there!" to leak from the aptitude assessment hat.

"Oknodie, are you okay? Did it do anything weird to you? You don't suddenly feel like destroying humanity or thinking human lives are like garbage, right?"
"I'm fine, Titosoga! Thanks for worrying! But you don't have to ask from so far away!"
"...No, Oknodie! I think this distance is just right for today!"

30 meters is way too far.
We have to shout just to have a conversation.
You're too scared, you coward.

"No! I want to be a Magic Swordsman!"
"You stubborn fool!"
"Hmph. You're just a smelly hat that's losing."
