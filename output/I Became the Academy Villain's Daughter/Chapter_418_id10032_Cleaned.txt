# Graduating from the Academy by Luck

## 418 - Speechless

"Minga gomen!"

After escaping from the sabbatical student zone barrier, I bowed my head in front of everyone.

"Stop using some obscure minority language and speak properly in common tongue so we can understand you."

Isabel scolded me with a stern face.
Even when I made a pitiful expression, Isabel folded her arms and glared, making it clear she wouldn't let me off the hook.
No matter how I turned my head, everyone else just snickered or shook their heads, clearly not taking my side.
Hmph.
I guess I have no choice.

"I'm sorry for making you worry. From now on, I won't cause trouble alone without saying anything."
"And?"

I was flustered by Isabel's prompting.
What else did I do wrong?
Thanks to Gisele who secretly gestured to me from behind, I realized why she was angry.

"I'll also properly apologize to Jigoku and the Jigoku Pirates..."

The shabby pirates of the Jigoku Pirates, who couldn't enter the barrier, put on slightly less angry faces and started showing off.

"Kid, just know we're letting you off this once!"
"If it weren't for the captain, we wouldn't be so forgiving!"
"But what could we do if we didn't forgive you?"
"Someone shut this guy's mouth."

Jigoku sighed at the sight of his members bickering and squabbling.

"Just know I'm letting you off this once."
"Thank you, Jigoku!"
"This little—"

When I stopped my sad face and quickly thanked him, he shook his fist and got even more upset. Why is he like that?

"And?"

More of Isabel's scolding? There's still more?
If I try to think of what else I did wrong, hmm...

"I'll brush my teeth properly after eating late-night snacks before going to bed."
"And?"

Again?
Hmph. It's hard to think of anything else.

"From now on, I won't let Titoso attach blue cellophane to the spotlight so that no matter how much I press the button and make a sad face, only blue light comes out."
"And?"

Oh, come on.
Isabel... please forgive me already.
Isabel maintained her stern expression, determined to correct my behavior with this opportunity.
With no other choice, I had to continue confessing my wrongdoings like I was in a confessional.

"I'll lick the yogurt stuck to the container clean and won't throw it away without licking it, even though the Cooking Book collection works without licking the container."
"And."
"I won't secretly cut off the circuit for an hour and cause a blackout by taking the electric stones that Thunderbird brought to the dormitory's mana power room without permission."
"And."
"I won't secretly kill assassins who infiltrated the Academy by myself and hog all the experience points..."
"And... what??"

Everyone's faces, which had been giggling in a somehow warm atmosphere, suddenly froze.

"I gave the corpse to Professor Sadako as lecture material, so that's not wrong, right? I don't need to apologize for that?"
"Really?"
"Yeah. She's going to use it for next week's lecture. Shouldn't Zuang and Titoso be thanking me instead?"

The corpse will be revived as undead so they can farm sweet experience points, so I deserve praise!

"But Professor Sadako's lecture is 'Adventurer's Terrain Adaptation'...?"
"She said we'll train to adapt to terrain where undead appear!"

As Zuang and I were having our innocent conversation, Isabel rushed in as if jealous.

"Who, when, where did this assassin come from?!"
"Which assassin are you talking about? The one from last week? The one from the week before?"

Isabel's relentless "and" finally came to a halt as she was left speechless.
The awkward atmosphere descended, and the time everyone had gathered to scold Oknodie came to an abrupt end.
Thanks to that, I had the leisure to check my notification window on the way back to the dormitory.

[You successfully encountered Lady Charlotte of the Foundation before she was killed by an unidentified assassin.]
[Swift Mobilization experience +30]
[Swift Reconnaissance experience +30]
[Finding experience +30]

[Successfully searched for and rescued the search and rescue team looking for Oknodie wandering in the sabbatical student zone.]
[Swift Mobilization experience +20]
[Swift Reconnaissance experience +20]
[Finding experience +20]

[You softened the hearts of your angry companions with sincere apology and attitude of reflection.]
[Social Life experience +30]
[Cute Act experience +10]
[Protection Inducement experience +10]
[Good Child experience +10]

Thanks to this, I gained some insight.
So if you do something wrong and apologize, your Good Child stat increases.
It's just like prayer!
You can repent just by apologizing after doing something bad, right?
Now I finally understand the mechanism for increasing the Good Child function.
First, do something bad.
Then apologize and become a good child, right?


* * *


Oknodie had been suffering from assassination attempts.
Until it was revealed from her own mouth, no one knew about it.

"Perhaps while we didn't know, this person was aware of the assassins' existence."

Hestia immediately went to the training ground.

"Miss Leape... I heard a cracking sound from my bones."
"Don't exaggerate, April. People don't die from broken bones."
"Woof woof! Happy is a beastkin! Beastkin aren't human so they die if their bones break! I want to end training!"
"This Leape firmly believes that beastkin have human rights too. Your bones are like human bones, so you won't die just because they break a little."

...There seemed to be no need to look far for an assassin.
There's a trainee assassin right here.

"Why are you breaking the maids' bones?"
"The mana properties embedded in the bones must be changed to enter the advanced stage of the maid mana cultivation method. Hestia, you should also prepare to enter the advanced stage in earnest when the second semester ends and vacation begins."
"I've long been prepared to have my bones scraped. However, I wanted to ask if you knew that Oknodie has been attacked by assassins."
"What difference does knowing that make?"
"Supervisor Leape. All of Oknodie's classmates share the same desire to worry about and care for her."

Leape's eyes contained a murky darkness that was frightening to look at, like muddy water clouded by pouring rain.
However, Hestia, hardened by mercenary activities, had smashed the skulls of enemies and sometimes even betrayers blinded by achievements and spoils among mercenaries with such eyes.
Without showing the slightest intimidation, she stood firm, and seeing her composure, Leape's thin leaf-like lips opened.

"As you suspect. The assassin attacks have been happening weekly."
"Are they enemies of the Foundation?"

That was the extent of enemies Hestia could think of for Oknodie.
Officially acting as a scholarship student, if assassins were only appearing now, the Foundation had so many enemies that it was rather late.
However, Leape shook her head.

"The attackers targeting the young lady are Elven assassins."
"Elves...?"
"It's because it became known that the Foundation attacked the World Tree for the young lady's culinary pleasure."
"!!"

Hestia could recall it.
The Wiheomhae Foundation Director's mansion, the Tower of Training.
The feast of countless foods that came from there.
The food Oknodie ate was even more special among them.
The problem was that she didn't know that specialness was significant enough to incur the resentment of another race.

"The enemy has a justifiable reason to hold a grudge. The attacks will continue for the rest of her life as long as Elves live in this land."
"So Oknodie should never enter the forest alone for the rest of her life?"

Elves demonstrate their true value in the forest.
Assassins who were easily repelled at the Academy could take lives in the forest.
That's because Elves can receive maximum functional corrections for all actions performed in the forest.

"That's correct."
"Why didn't you warn Oknodie about this fact in advance?"
"The Director wishes for the young lady to rely more on the Foundation. I don't have the power to prevent that. I only provide as much teaching as possible so that the young lady can overcome the attacks."

To both the young lady and the maids who will protect her.
Hestia realized.
The reason Leape allowed her to learn the maid mana cultivation method.
It was premised on protecting Oknodie from the Elven attacks that she had noticed early on.
At the same time, she could understand how pitiful the existence of the Foundation's young lady was.
While teaching her to gain strength, they also make her incur resentment so that she cannot survive outside the Foundation's embrace.
No matter how great the power the Foundation's young lady had, she was merely a puppet that moved according to the Foundation's will.
At least, that's what the Director intends.
Even Oknodie.

"Vacation is too far away. Please start the advanced course today, right now."

Leape's withered leaf-like dry gaze examined Hestia's determined eyes.

"Once you start, dropping out is not an option. Depending on the circumstances, you may have to sacrifice your grades to achieve the cultivation method. Are you still willing to take on the challenge?"
"Gladly."


* * *


While Hestia went to find Leape to begin the advanced course of the maid mana cultivation method, Zuang went to find Professor Sadako, who had reportedly received the assassin's corpse.
It was an act of great courage for someone like her.
Hadn't she always been so afraid of attending lectures that she would force her reluctant feet to move, always accompanied by the super-coward Titoso?

"Did you bring a corpse too?"
"I'd like to examine the assassin's corpse that Oknodie handed over."

An assassin can gather much information just by looking at a colleague's corpse.
No matter how hard they try to hide their affiliation, the body cannot lie.
Where they trained, what they're trying to hide, how the corpse responds to mana, and what mana circuits it suggests existed—all can be discovered if one is determined.

"There's an 'Introduction to Dark Necromancy' lecture in the second year."
"Pardon?"
"If you register for the course, I might show you the corpse."

Zuang thought carefully before making her decision.

"I think I'll be fine without seeing the corpse."
