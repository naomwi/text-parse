# Enhanced Translation: Chapter 244 - Equivalent Exchange of Hearts

Thursday.
It was the day when most individual events had finished, and team competitions were being held back-to-back.
The lower-class students worked together, helping each other so the entire class could place second in each event and collectively earn 80 points.
While 80 points might not mean much to someone who dominated all school events or to children of wealthy nobles, royalty, or merchant magnates with generous allowances, for commoners or impoverished nobles, it could be their daily bread (5 points for a regular cafeteria meal)!

"But why aim for second place? Since we're competing anyway, shouldn't we try for first?"

The classroom atmosphere turned ice-cold, as if someone had poured cold water over everyone after a student blurted this out.

"Who is that clueless idiot?"
"I heard he just returned from the medical wing after fainting during midterms."
"Wow... how has he not been expelled yet?"
"They say he did really well on the written exam."
"What a jerk."

Seeing the student on the verge of tears after being thoroughly insulted, Mob approached with his armor joints clanking.

"W-what! There's no need to hit me!"
"...First place belongs to the upper class."
"Phew. Sorry. I got nervous because I thought you were some violent psycho in full armor."

Maybe I shouldn't have helped.
As Mob's gaze behind his helmet was turning regretful, screams from many students could be heard in the distance.

"Follow me. It's an event we'll be participating in too. Seems like some poor fools are getting thrashed."

Mob's group of 50 lower-class students discovered other students rolling in the dirt and coughing at one end of the field. The ground was covered with deeply carved rope marks as if a dragon had danced across it, making it difficult to imagine what the students who had been dragged around had endured.

"Waaah! My knees are all scraped up."
"Even when my mom hits me, I only get bruises!"
"What kind of tug-of-war is this..."

On the opposite side stood students with somewhat remorseful expressions wondering if they'd gone too far, and Oknodie with a displeased look on her face.

"Oh come on. The speed-run reward is worse than the flying reward or the windmill reward. You should either get dragged around nicely until we make a circle, or get lifted into the air completely. When you keep awkwardly putting your feet on the ground, the reward becomes mediocre!"

The upper-class students, led by the superhumanly strong girl Oknodie, had gathered all their monsters together. Naturally, the lower-class students who had to face such a team were arguing about keeping their feet on the ground to avoid worse injuries, pointing out scraped legs, and suggesting they should just let themselves be dragged around in the second set to avoid potential deaths—a suffocating battle just to hear about.

"This is why we don't aim for first place."

The student who had just been discharged from the medical wing blinked and said.

"Then are they idiots? Why compete against the upper class?"
"There's a handicap bonus. It's a special Sports Festival rule that gives you a bonus for fighting against a stronger team. But... if you forfeit, the reward drops to one-tenth. They're gritting their teeth and enduring to get 30 points instead of 3."
"...Looks like it'll all go to medical expenses though?"
"That's why they're pitiful. Poor fellows who made the wrong choice. Tsk tsk."
"But didn't you get your armor from Oknodie too?"

The first poor fellow who made the wrong choice hung his head dejectedly.
If no one else, at least Mob couldn't treat those pitiful students as if they were someone else's problem.


* * *


Before the team competitions began, Isabel and Dorothy seemed quite worried.

"Won't the Imperial faction just sit back and let us Frontier faction handle all the grunt work?"
"It would be better if they just sat back! Those sneaky jerks even set traps in the forest I frequent. Someone could get hurt, but they do whatever they want! Judging by how they act, it wouldn't be strange if they sabotaged us!"
"And no matter what we do, the special C group has no motivation."

Casia gave them a blank stare as if to ask what they were looking at.
Isabel sighed and turned away from the powerless, motivation-draining gaze.

"It'll be fine! We all receive points together!"
"I hope you're right..."
"Anyone who interferes with earning points here becomes an enemy of the entire upper class, you know?"

Not even Oknodie would dare to do such a thing.
Even a fool would realize they'd made a serious mistake if their screen became covered with relationship decrease messages after trying to act tough just once.

"Hmph. I'm only helping until the Sports Festival ends. Don't get the wrong idea."
"What's that hero so proud of?"
"He abandoned us all and finished alone in the midterms. At least Oknodie helped the Frontier kids get points."
"And then he lost the co-valedictorian position because he scored low on Dragon Headmaster's cooperation points."
"What a jerk."

The hero's face showed shock.
He clearly hadn't expected such cold stares and condemnation from the Imperial students who he thought would always support him.
It was only natural that the Imperial students, who were all used to being praised back home, wouldn't look kindly upon a hero who had abandoned them.

"Pfft. Stu~pid♡ If you're going to act out of line, you should have known this would happen♡"

Of course, behind the Imperial students' cold treatment was the Second Imperial Princess Mesugakki, who was instigating antipathy toward the hero.

"They live such exhausting lives over there. It would be nice if everyone just got along like friends in the forest."

At Dorothy's words, Isabel thought to herself.
Aren't animals in a predator-prey relationship?

"Sigh. Anyway, all the good times are over now."

Even the upper-class students who had thoroughly enjoyed bullying their juniors trembled in fear of the approaching Friday.
Tomorrow's schedule featured the year-group competition.
The highest-scoring event with 1,000 points for first place, 800 for second, 500 for third, and 0 for fourth!
For safety reasons, first-years would fight second-years, and third-years would fight fourth-years, but the intention was clear.
The rankings would inevitably follow the year order!

"Oknodie. Can we win?"
"Impossible, right?"
"If even you say that, it must be truly impossible."
"They're second-years! Isabel, could you beat yourself from one year in the future?"
"If my future self doesn't let her guard down, it would be impossible."

That's the kind of battle it was.
The second-years knew everything.
What the first-years had learned and what skills they possessed.
The upper class might be somewhat special.
But the year-group competition wasn't a class-by-class battle limited to 50 people; it was closer to a large-scale war with over two thousand students competing.
Individual strength becomes meaningless when the numerical difference is this large.
Moreover, the upper class wasn't exclusive to first-years.
Second-years had an upper class too.

"The Noble Lady Mandela, the White Holy Knight Lu, Blue Fist Io, and the Terrifying Dead Cat. The second-year upper-class students aren't much weaker than the hero or Oknodie based on their lineup. Those four I just mentioned are especially strong and are called the Four Heavenly Kings of the second-year."

Dorothy quietly shared the rumors she had heard.
Isabel, listening beside her, felt something was off.

"Didn't that Dead Cat senior almost get thoroughly beaten by Oknodie before forfeiting...?"

If someone of that caliber was one of the second-year Four Heavenly Kings, perhaps they stood a chance in terms of top-tier strength?

"Don't get your hopes up too much. The year-group competition involves everyone participating together anyway."

Isabel couldn't understand Oknodie's attitude of giving up before even starting.
She had the same passive attitude as when she used to hide around, as if she didn't deserve to enjoy the Sports Festival event because everyone hated her.
Isabel was worried about her lack of confidence, which was more frustrating to see than anything else.

"But with Oknodie here, would everyone give up so easily...?"

Isabel was skeptical.

"Oknodie, didn't you have a blast during the individual dodgeball event?"
"She certainly did. That little mouse was having the time of her life while the rest of us were fighting for our lives."
"Dorothy says that regardless of how Oknodie felt, others won't help."
"If that's the case, why not ask Gisele? There's no one more knowledgeable when it comes to information than that bookworm."

The well-informed Gisele spoke coldly.

"Give up. More than half of the first-years already consider defeat a foregone conclusion."
"Even with Oknodie? And though I don't like him, the hero is here too. Aren't the 981st class first-years considered the strongest first-year class in history?"
"They think that even if they try their hardest and lose, they won't be able to participate in the remaining individual events today. Even though medical wing use is free during the Sports Festival, no one wants to miss the opportunity to earn points."
"Still..."
"That's not the only problem."
"What else is there?"
"First, there are quite a number of first-years trying to sabotage the Sports Festival instead of Oknodie—scholarship students from the Foundation. We've caught 50 so far."

50 out of over 2,000 first-years.
A simple calculation shows one spy for every 40 students.
It's impossible to expect a normal competition with such numbers.

"You said 'first,' meaning there are other reasons?"
"The C group is also a problem. They generally lack enthusiasm for school activities. They're all people who were either sold to the Academy or enrolled as a means of escape."
"Ah..."

It would be difficult to persuade them with "help us because you want to earn points" when what they wanted was either just enrollment itself or simply to breathe and stay alive!
Even if one wanted to persuade them, they were opponents who couldn't be persuaded from the start.

"Casia."

A girl with short blue hair and indifferent eyes as if she had achieved enlightenment.
Whenever sparks randomly crackled around her body, the instructor standing behind her would tightly grip the restraining rod, creating a perpetually tense atmosphere.
Isabel approached this girl who seemed to rank first in either having the least attachment to life or having the most difficult life.

"Isabel."
"You know my name?"
"I heard it."
"I know it's shameless to approach someone I'm not close with, but I actually came to ask a favor."
"Okay."
"Oknodie is a child disliked by many, but when given the chance, she enjoys herself more than anyone. I hope you can help her... Huh?"
"I said okay."
"Really? You're okay with it? Why?"

To the confused Isabel, Casia answered with an indifferent face.

"She resembles me. The Foundation and the Research Institute are similar too. She's just slower to realize than I am. Someday she'll understand how fleeting the freedom she currently enjoys really is."
"...!"
"If you want to turn a blind eye, feel free to close your eyes. When the time comes, she'll realize it whether she likes it or not. I can help with the games of such a pitiful child."

Isabel, who had set out to lighten Oknodie's heart, found her own heart growing heavier, as if making an equivalent exchange.
