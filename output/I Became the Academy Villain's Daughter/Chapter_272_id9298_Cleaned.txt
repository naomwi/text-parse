# 272 - Same Incident, Different Responses

The various organizations responded quite differently to the Nuea-in attack.

"Please keep your people from going there. We can't afford to have them get caught up in this incident and see their evaluation scores drop."

The Camella Brigade disciplined their members.
The power of the Camella Brigade stems from the "Slave Contracts" provided by Camella and the loyalty of masters who exploit the labor of slaves by managing these contracts.
Those who actively use these sinister contracts for personal gain had no reason to help with the public cause of subjugating the Nuea-in and rescuing the kidnapped first-years.

'With the contribution points those idiots gave me thinking they were getting free power when they eagerly signed slave contracts, I could do anything... but why should I help?'

There was no reason to burn through carefully accumulated contribution points so quickly.
If she was going to collect them, she should definitely take her time to fully charge up on contribution points by the second-year opening ceremony.
Then she would dominate the school year with slaves who were absolutely loyal to her through contracts.
She would pick and choose male students to sleep with as she pleased and treat female students like doormats.
Dreaming of the day she could freely unleash her desires, Camella chose not to get involved in this situation.


* * *


"We will immediately move to subjugate the Nuea-in."

Ishtar judged that this was a good opportunity for the Hero's Guard to gain real combat experience.

"Isn't it too early?"
"We can't focus too much on training the Guard when they're not even a regular hero party. I've taught them enough skills and combos through accelerated training."

Unlike her childhood friend Yupi's concerns, the Hero's Guard didn't show much agitation when encountering the Nuea-in.

"They look quite human-like."
"They look fluffy and soft."
"Focus. We've learned enough that not everything pretty is good."

The Hero's Guard members strongly agreed, thinking of Ishtar and Yupi.

-We don't have much time, but to get stronger quickly, we need to build resistance.
-Yupi's divine healing spell works well, so let's start by building slash resistance.

The method for building resistance was both simple and brutal.
The hero slashes, and the saintess heals.
By resisting out of the desire not to be cut and to survive, their combat abilities naturally improved.
It definitely helped.
They could feel themselves getting stronger as they actively applied the techniques they were taught in real combat.
But after repeatedly getting slashed by a sword, screaming in pain, and rolling on the ground every time they made eye contact, any faint respect for the hero or admiration and obsession for a beautiful woman disappeared completely.

"This damn woman! Pretty face but nothing else! Evil woman who thinks she's so great!"
"Die, you monster! Disgustingly strong woman! Let's see how long you can stay so arrogant!"
"This is for everyone who has suffered at your hands!"

Ishtar felt uncomfortable.
They were clearly shouting at the Nuea-in, but why did it feel like they were directing it at her?

"It's because they don't feel respect."
"I thought I put in quite a bit of effort."
"Maybe the training intensity is too low, making them forget their gratitude."
"You're right. Since we can't increase training time, we should increase the intensity from now on. We've done enough slash resistance training, so we should train impact resistance with a spiked mace."

Not realizing that their venting would return as even greater misfortune, the Hero's Guard diligently subjugated the Nuea-in and, in the process, rescued people as well.
They used their newly taught skills well, maintained high morale, and their formation wasn't bad.

'That was a successful field exercise. I guess I'll need to beat them about twice as hard from now on.'

Ishtar and Yupi returned satisfied with the Hero's Guard's first real combat experience.


* * *


"Captain, let's ambush those who went to hunt the Nuea-in when they return exhausted. Isn't that a genius idea?"
"Genius my ass. Would privateers do something that would get them marked by the authorities?!"
"Argh!"

The third-rate pirate member who had a pirate flag stuck in his backpack collapsed after being hit on the head with Jigoku's gun handle.
Though he fired his gun just for fun, Jigoku was strong enough to slice monsters with pure strength, and his violence was too threatening for the inadequate students who joined piracy due to lack of skill.
Jigoku didn't criticize them for being weak.
Piracy was originally a profession for small fry, beggars, and criminals.
But there was one line he couldn't tolerate.

"Listen up, you lot. Privateers shouldn't upset the powers that allow piracy. Outside, we need to be careful of countries, but here it's the Academy."
"So you're saying we shouldn't rob first-years?"
"Time and place matter. Would the Academy let people get away with attacking those good kids who got caught up in an incident and are returning?"
"Ohhh. As expected of Captain Jigoku who's been a pirate for ten years without getting his head chopped off!"
"Our captain has a secret to his longevity!"
"Amazing that the captain's head hasn't been on the chopping block for ten years!"

These kids know something.
Jigoku wiped under his nose with his finger, feeling oddly proud.

"If you understand, we'll rob the pockets of first-years returning from the student council building. Selfish bastards who only care about club activities while their friends are being attacked by Nuea-in deserve to be robbed by pirates!"

Armed with novel yet practical logic, the pirates ambushed club members on their way home.

"Please don't take my textbooks..."
"I'll be expelled if I lose this hand-carved staff and carving knife... sob."
"Please spare just this black bread. Without it, I'll have nothing to eat but weeds... boo-hoo."

The pirates were taken aback.
The first-years were already being heavily exploited by their club seniors, leaving nothing worth stealing.
Their situation was even worse than the pirates who at least didn't go hungry while robbing others!

"Stop crying and come here. You should just become a pirate. What? You can't leave unless you get two friends to join the club room? Get lost, you crazy bastard!"

The pathetic club member, like a victim of a pyramid scheme, clung to the pirate's arm, but the third-rate pirate member shook him off and threw him to the ground in disgust.
Then several shadows appeared before them, announcing the arrival of strangers.

"Who goes there!"
"People who have come to save the oppressed students."
"C-could it be club seniors?!"
"Wrong. We are the Western Nobility Alliance, composed of first-years."
"Tch, I got scared for nothing. Shoo, shoo. This isn't a playground for young masters."

As the third-rate pirate member mockingly waved his hand, a jelly sphere stuck to his head.

"Mmmrphhh!?"
"This is the versatile magic spell 'Jelly Ball' that can be used as protection when jumping from heights to prevent cartilage damage, as a safety rope while climbing cliffs barehanded, and can stop monster and senior attacks when aimed at the face. Taste magic that's too good for the likes of you."

The Western Nobility Alliance members, who had experience struggling with this jelly ball while learning 'Escaping Water Torture Through Faith,' only released the jelly ball magic after enough time had passed for the third-rate pirate member to faint but not die.

"Ugh, this guy is vomiting jelly!"
"What cruel magic...!"

While the third-rate pirate members were in disarray, Andersen swept his cape and extended his hand to the student lying face-down on the ground.

"Come this way! Give us half of what you have, and we'll save you!"

Andersen's Western Nobility Alliance had perfectly grasped the Jigoku pirate crew's way of thinking and appeared at the right time.
The plan was perfect, but they failed to grasp just how poor the club members were.

"Such cruel people! Taking half of our textbooks, trying to control our grades too! No matter how much these beggars need money, this is a violation of learning rights!"
"How can they threaten us to give up either our hand-carved staff or carving knife? They're worse than pirates!"
"Heartless people who would split black bread in half. When such evil people graduate, they won't understand why commoners starve and will say nonsense like 'let them eat meat if they run out of wheat'!"

The Western Nobility Alliance unexpectedly became the villains because they couldn't imagine students could be so poor, while the Jigoku pirate crew represented the feelings of the poor.
The students who got up from the ground with their shabby appearance didn't even brush off the dirt on their knees as they glared at the Western Nobility Alliance and shouted:

"You evil nobles! Rather than being exploited by you, we'd rather become pirates!"
"We'll escape the club somehow, so please accept us as pirates!"

Both Jigoku, who came to steal supplies but ended up with more mouths to feed, and Andersen, who came to raise his honor but became an evil noble, faced unwanted outcomes.

While the meaningless battle between the Jigoku pirate crew and the Western Nobility Alliance raged on with their roles of good and evil reversed, "The Organization That Plays with Oknodie" issued a major mobilization order.
Due to many members being scattered for insect collection or ingredient gathering, the assembled personnel was less than a tenth of the total organization, but their lineup was impressive.
Hestia. Zuang. Xing. Irene. Pyoi.
Those with enough confidence to overcome the final exams without converting questions and answers into points gathered in the training room and rushed out when called.

"Who's this?"

Hestia was puzzled upon discovering Pyoi in bikini armor.

"She's my friend! Right?"
"Right-! Anyone who knows the benefits of bikini armor is a friend!"

Just one more noisy person added to the group.
The advanced class members ignored it.
Irene praised Oknodie.

"Honestly, I'm surprised. I thought you gathered people just to play when you were bored, but now you're initiating a proper organizational activity to rescue children in trouble. I'm genuinely impressed."
"Hmph. That's mean. Have you been looking at me that way, Irene?"
"Yes."
"...I've always been a good child, you know?"
"Everyone thinks they're good."

While Irene readily came to help Oknodie, unlike Hestia and other students who agreed with everything and coddled her, Irene had a cold edge.
It probably had nothing to do with her being the Northern Grand Duchess from the perpetually winter north, or being a master of ice magic, or having a grudge from having her dodgeball stolen during the grand sports festival.
Probably.

"Zuang~!"

Upon arriving at the scene where the Nuea-in had appeared, a delicate-looking female student who could pass for a tragic protagonist in a romance fantasy cried out and threw herself into Zuang's arms.

"...Who are you?"
"Waaah. It's me, Titosoga!"

Zuang's expressionless face caused a great stir.

"I'm shocked. I didn't realize appearances could change this much without a lighting stand."

To think that Titosoga, who always appeared weighed down and intimidated while carrying a heavy lighting stand, had such feminine charm!
In fact, I was so shocked that I wondered if the lighting stand might be a perception-disrupting magical item given by her older sister to keep unsuitable men away. This level of suppression could rival glasses that halve a person's charm just by wearing them!

'Or was it that she seemed like a crazy person carrying a lighting stand, so I didn't notice her charm before?'

While Zuang was lost in these trivial thoughts, Oknodie asked a practical question.

"We passed the Hero's Guard on our way here. What did they do?"
"They said they didn't come to rescue something like a lighting stand, and they just scolded me, saying that as a first-year, I should act like a grown-up and graduate from lighting stands..."

The hero acted like a hero, indeed.
Everyone had similar thoughts as they steeled their resolve.

"Then let's go deeper into the silkworm habitat that the Hero's Guard didn't touch!"

The Organization That Plays with Oknodie advanced vigorously toward the depths of the silkworm habitat.
