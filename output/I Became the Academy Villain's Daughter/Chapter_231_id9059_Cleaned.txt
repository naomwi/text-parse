In Gisele's private room.
Oknodie lay sprawled on the resting bed in his room, which was piled high with mountains of documents, wiggling her feet while flipping through an information sheet.

"Hmm. There are some strange rounds here."
"Is something bothering you?"
"Arcadia is participating in too many events!"

Oknodie had appeared on her own without being called, having heard rumors about the information sheets somewhere.
Despite Oknodie browsing through the information sheet that cost 50 points as if it were her own, Gisele didn't scold her.
After all, he had asked if he could sell information about which events Oknodie was participating in.
It wouldn't be an exaggeration to say that more than 20% of sales came from people wanting to know which events the top student Oknodie was participating in, so rather than charging her 50 points, he should be paying her points instead.

"If you're that worried, why don't you participate in the same events?"

It was definitely concerning.
The sports festival had a strange gimmick where the risk of injury increased with each additional event entered.
Not just for the player themselves, but if teammates carelessly entered any event that looked good, they'd likely end up bedridden with injuries.
If you got hurt in individual events, you wouldn't be able to perform well in class competitions and grade competitions, leading to greater overall losses.
Worse, if you were unlucky enough to hit that 0.1% chance of critical failure, you'd suffer a serious injury and face medium to long-term penalties for future academy activities.
The prevention method was simple.
Set your abilities and required skills very high, click "Auto-Result View," and it would automatically show you getting first place.
Your physical and mental strength would drop significantly, but that could be replenished with prepared potions.
But this was reality.
In reality, there's no "Auto-Result View for Life" button.
In times like these, a more troublesome method was required.

"More than that, I just had an interesting thought."
"Interesting...?"
"I'll go ahead first!"

Gisele's expression turned somewhat serious, but that man always took life seriously, so I didn't pay much attention to it.

The day of the sports festival arrived.

Now, I declare the sports festival open.
Little morsels, chase after those points!

With the Dragon Principal's shout, colorful magical fireworks exploded high in the sky.
Students began running toward the events they had marked in advance.
In contrast, I ran toward the faculty building in the opposite direction, where no one else was heading.

"Hat, there's no one here, right?"
"...I've confirmed it. There are no instructors left here. At most, just cleaning maids."
"Good. Then let's infiltrate according to the map we prepared!"

From Monday when the sports festival was announced until this Monday, for the past week, I had been counting down the days.
If injuries and bad luck for the player and NPC companions were the problem, then I needed to find ways to prevent them!
Starting from this simple conclusion, my plan first distinguished between injuries from "accidents" and those from "interference."

"We can't help accidents, but we must prevent interference. I've earned way more points than expected anyway. Taking it easy at the beginning of the sports festival won't cause much loss."

The original owner of the "Answering Wall" that I possessed had the abilities of "Etherealization" and "Telepathy."
The reason the first hat wearer couldn't speak when put into the wall was because he lacked telepathy, and the reason he couldn't freely spy through the facilities connected to the dormitory walls was because he lacked etherealization.
It's no wonder that players possessed by walls are essentially treated as game over.
It's not about using the wall's original abilities to find an escape, but using the abilities of the person possessed by the wall, so if your abilities are insufficient when possessed, you're stuck with a game over.
Naturally, if someone had such abilities, they wouldn't waste a year trapped in a wall waiting to steal the body of the next freshman.

'No matter what the wall is like, the second hat wearer can spy using etherealization!'

The soul of the second hat wearer briefly left the hat and explored the faculty building before returning.

"It looks like this."

Thanks to the hand attached to the hat drawing a map on the ground, I could perfectly understand the internal structure of the faculty building, which was randomly generated each round.
Since the location of the destination was fixed every time, it was meaningful work to find an infiltration route without getting lost inside.

"I couldn't peek beyond this point."
"That's okay. I know what's in there!"
"What are you trying to steal?"
"Items confiscated from students by the instructors!"

The confiscated items storage room.
Commonly known as the "seizure room," it's a hidden gem among players.
Normally, it's extremely difficult to infiltrate because of how many instructors pass through, and even when instructors are away, various security devices make it easy to get caught.
If you're detected by intruder detection magic, you'll be immediately restrained or dragged away unconscious by instructors and imprisoned for at least a few days in the first floor of the underground dungeon.

'Hmph. Back when I was 230cm tall, I proudly succeeded in raiding the seizure room.'

Of course, I didn't enter like this before.
Back then, I tore apart the trap damage detector with my fist, broke the laser trap, the heat detection trap, the ankle binding trap, and threw rocks at the gargoyles.
I still vividly remember how the maids fled thinking Northland's magic schools had attacked when they saw the devastated faculty building, while I swept confiscated items into a sack with my huge body.

'Tsk. Now I'm too short.'

Though I can't push through with raw power like before, I'm making remarkable progress by combining my small stature and nimble movements with veteran knowledge and the second hat wearer's information.
I don't have to worry about noise penalties alerting maids who might call instructors.
I can take my time and collect everything I need!

"...Oknodie. Look over there."

But I never expected to see a maid tiptoeing around with her heels raised like a cat.
Just as I had avoided all the gargoyles and was about to enter the seizure room, I made direct eye contact with a maid from the instructor's office across the hall.

"!?"
"!?"

A situation where we were both surprised to see each other.
But looking closely, this maid was different.
She had a flashlight in her mouth.
And her hands were rummaging through the instructors' secret files that a maid would have no business touching.
Aha.
Rather than the 9.9% chance of encountering a slacking maid, I'd hit the even rarer 0.1% chance of encountering a spy maid!

Shh!

I put my finger to my lips, signaling to pretend we hadn't seen each other, and the maid nodded while breaking out in a cold sweat.
And so began the curious faculty building raid, with one side stealing from the seizure room and the other stealing documents from the instructor's office.
