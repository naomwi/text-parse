# 129 - Motivation

She was young, but her skills surpassed his own.
The top student of her year, loved and envied by many.
Opinions about her might be divided, but one thing was certain.
Oknodie was popular.
The kind of popular where people watched her every move.
And here he was, wandering around looking for food with her.

'Wait... isn't this basically a date?'

There was a time when even Mob had such thoughts.

"Save me..."
"Where do you think you're running? You haven't finished your plates yet, Mob!"
"I feel like my stomach's going to burst..."

On Mob's table were the remains of what had once been "Potato Salad," "Tornado Potatoes," "Potato Pancakes," "Braised Potatoes," and "Stir-fried Potatoes."

"You haven't even tried all the dishes that start with 'potato' yet. Don't be such a baby!"
"Who has dates like this?!"
"Date?"
"Ah, no. Not a date—education! Mentoring education!"
"Hmm."

Oknodie, who had been chasing him with plates in hand, put her hand on her chin with an expression like a guard scrutinizing a criminal.
After enduring so much food torture, he'd made a slip of the tongue.
Mob berated himself.
To think he was getting date vibes while with a child who was just trying to help him!
Thoughts might be private, but once spoken aloud, you have to take responsibility for your words.

"Hehe. So Mob was following me around feeling like we were on a date."
"I-I'm sorry, Oknodie."

He had no words, even if he had ten mouths.
Oknodie was just a child, barely ten years old.
Even though they were both teenagers, mentioning dates to someone so young could easily get him labeled a pedophile or pervert, and rightfully so.
Mob's eyes trembled pitifully.
What if Oknodie started crying?
Would he be dragged before the student council as a sex offender?
Could he even be expelled?!
As he trembled, Oknodie casually tossed out:

"It's okay."
"...What is?"
"I said it's okay if you think of it as a date!"

—His brain short-circuited for a moment.
What was she thinking, saying something like that?
A girl to a boy?
And not even a small age gap.
There's a difference between early teens and late teens.
Did she even understand what she was saying?
Mob's heart raced for an entirely different reason now.
A finger poked his side.

"Aaaah!"

The terrifying superhuman strength snapped him back to reality.

"But first, let's work off that belly with some exercise!"

The momentary flutter of emotion was short-lived.
Mob ended up running like crazy.
By the time he was completely exhausted, all thoughts of dates were gone—he just wanted it to end.
If he heard the word "date" one more time, he'd collapse.
He vowed never to mention it again.


* * *


[You have trained Mob from the lower class.]
[Running experience +2]
[Good Child experience +1]

Eating, running, eating, running.
Eating alongside her also helped him collect recipes he hadn't gathered before, making his cookbook completion easier.

[Title "Potato Dish Enthusiast" acquired.]

*Potato Dish Enthusiast*: Having devoured 25 different potato dishes, you've earned the right to call yourself a potato dish enthusiast.
-Title Effect When Equipped: Slight increase in potato detection ability
-Title Effect When Owned: Very slight increase in potato detection ability

A title that allows you to subconsciously detect potatoes nearby.
It's not a title to be underestimated just because it doesn't give stat points.
The ability to find wild potatoes during voluntary starvation experiences when you've spent all your points on impulse purchases is as good as having an extra life.
Moreover, fantasy-world potatoes are plants commonly found everywhere.
During outdoor activities when food supplies are needed, they're like that unwanted relative—you complain when they're around, but desperately search for them when they're not.
Regardless of how poorly regarded they are, it was a decent title that would help find daily sustenance during food shortages.
Collecting many similar "enthusiast" titles could lead to title integration, which was his long-term goal.

'When detection ability titles combine, they'll lead to an overall detection ability increase!'

A high-performance function obtainable through food encyclopedia completion that newbies wouldn't even think of!
It's no wonder the powerful and upper class in this world enjoy fine dining and seek out various cuisines.

"How was it, Mob? Was today's training helpful?"
"I never want to do this again..."
"Don't worry!"

I won't do this twice.

"We'll do it three times a week!"
"You can't be serious..."

I absolutely can't resist bullying newbies three times a week.
Hehe.
Taste the veteran experience!


* * *


After thoroughly tormenting Mob, a week had quickly passed.

'Ah, it's the fourth week.'

Though I moved around the Academy as comfortably as my own home, I needed to be careful during the fourth week.
During the last week of each month, negative incidents occurred almost without fail.
I'm not referring to weekly events.
These weren't Academy-planned events but "incidents" that affected both main and supporting characters.
The fourth week of the first month was just a taste for the newbies.
Generally, it involved spontaneous incidents caused by lower class students.

'Normally, I wouldn't have connections in the lower class to easily get information, but this time is different.'

With Mob as a friend in the lower class, I could get information through him even if the distance between upper and lower classes made reputation building difficult.

"Is that her?"
"Poor thing."
"Don't make eye contact. They say assassins can kill you just by looking at you for three seconds."

While waiting to meet Mob, students passing through the corridor whispered.
It wasn't as openly hostile as when I clashed with the central noble ladies, but it was more insidious—rumors and ostracism spreading in a more sinister way.
Hmph. How pathetic.
These newbies have no fear.
I turned my attention away from these worthless matters not worth my time and twirled the chopsticks in my fingers.
Mob was late today.
Perhaps his previous lecture ran long?
While waiting for him, I stood by a shadowed pillar, eventually leaning against it.
As I tracked a butterfly fluttering outside the window with my eyes, I heard giggling from somewhere.

'Should I throw it?'

I adjusted my grip on the chopsticks while checking if any instructors who might deduct points were nearby, and made direct eye contact with one.

"..."
"..."

The instructor silently looked at the chopsticks in my hand.
More precisely, at the way I was holding them.
I put on an innocent expression that said, "I wasn't about to throw them, honest!"

"Chop-chop-chopsticks~"

I pretended to catch the butterfly outside the window with my chopsticks, and the instructor snorted, giving me a stern look.
I could see that while I might fool passing students, I couldn't fool his eyes, so I pouted.

Tap tap tap.

The next lecture was about to start—when would Mob arrive?
Just as I was grumbling internally, Mob finally appeared.
He rushed down the corridor and grabbed my hand.

"You idiot! What are you doing alone in a place like this?"
"Why am I an idiot? The idiot is Mob who's about to fail!"
"...Being needlessly kind won't help."
"I'm kind?"
"Never mind, just come with me."

As Mob grabbed my arm and pretended to pull me along, I relaxed my body and let myself be dragged.
If he couldn't physically move a small child, he might lose not just mentoring motivation but learning motivation altogether.
Hmph.
Look at me, considering even these small details!
I'm so admirable!
A complete professional tutor!


* * *


Monday's third period lecture on "Learning Linked Skills" ran longer than expected.

"Skills involve activating abilities of a defined specification, similar to magic. However, physical skills gained attention after magical skills emerged. Physical skills are defined as applying the laws of formalization required for magical skill activation to the body, but the convenience of activation comes with drawbacks like fixed casting motions or fixed power."
"There are ways to overcome the drawbacks of physical skills. Who can tell us? You there, Mr. Mob?"

I had told him to meet me during the break before the Ranged Weapon Mastery lecture because Oknodie would be waiting.
But the professor's questions wouldn't end, perhaps because I was sitting in the front row.

"Skills can be simplified in casting motions or incantations as proficiency increases, and connecting them to specific movements for instant activation is called 'Instant Link.'"
"Correct. Mastering Instant Link allows for 'Silent Casting' or 'Zero Motion,' enabling instant activation without preliminary actions. Nevertheless, one person can rarely handle more than 10 instant-cast skills, as human cognition and memorization..."

I already knew all the theoretical parts.
That skill proficiency could somehow be increased through effort.
That talented people mock even that effort, filling their proficiency at an incredible speed, so that even when taking the same lectures, they zoom far ahead.
That I didn't have such talent.
That's why I was working hard on assignments and filling skill proficiency in the secret training ground all night, knowing that if I slacked off even for a day, I would fall behind.
I knew it all.
That's why I was attending the lecture.
To hear "tips" that could reduce the talent gap even a little.
To walk the "right path" of proper practice that fills proficiency with less effort.
But this lecture was too slow.
The pace of the lecture.
The professor's voice.
Even the time it took for the lecture to end.

"That concludes today's lecture."

As soon as the lecture ended, I ran frantically.
I should apologize for being late first.
What if she's angry and already went to the lecture hall?
She's spending her time teaching me, a lower class student.
Feeling guilty for wasting the top student's time, I ran even harder, sweating profusely. Fortunately, I spotted Oknodie leaning against a pillar on the way to the lecture hall.

She looks so pretty today.
Her side profile, silently gazing out the window, is even prettier.
This child, how many men will she make cry when she grows up?
Embarrassment preceded joy.
I shouldn't be conscious of it, but I was a little aware, and feeling awkward, I resolved to treat her more firmly and dignifiedly. But then...

"Doesn't Oknodie seem a bit off? What's with that 'chop-chop-chopsticks' thing anyway?"
"I don't know. Carrying around weird sticks and singing. She seems a bit slow. Like those kids they say are in the Magic Tower?"
"Oh, those? I know. The ones with unfocused eyes, slurred speech, and who look strange even as they age. She's ten, old enough to know better, but she acts just like them."
"She might be flirting with the boys. Such a young thing already acting so flirty and foxy. No wonder those kinds of rumors are going around."
"Maybe that's how she was raised?"
"Enough, stop talking. If we get caught, we'll just be humiliated like Lotto. She's the type to hit people she doesn't like."

Students passing by were badmouthing Oknodie.
They were speaking so loudly.
There's no way she couldn't hear.
Sure enough, looking closely, Oknodie's hands revealed her anxious state.
Her hands trembled, unsure how to hold the chopsticks, fidgeting restlessly.
She must have heard everything.
An adult should intervene.
But no one helped Oknodie.
Not even the instructor.
Despite giving up her weekends to help a failing student, no one was helping her.
Alone.
Lonely.
Just staring at butterflies outside the window.

"You idiot! What are you doing alone in a place like this?"
"Why am I an idiot? The idiot is Mob who's about to fail!"
"...Being needlessly kind won't help."
"I'm kind?"

Oknodie tilted her head.
I firmly grabbed her hand, pulling her along as if nothing was wrong, while she maintained her bright demeanor.

"Never mind, just come with me."

I was angry.
Upset.
Even indignant.

'It must be because of me.'

This poor child who endured such humiliation to keep her promise, steadfastly waiting at the meeting place.

'I want to repay her.'

A desire to help this child, even a little, blazed like a flame inside me.
My attitude, which had dismissed her "eat and run" approach, fundamentally changed.
Don't complain.
Trust Oknodie.
Having made this resolution, Oknodie said to me:

"Don't eat dinner later!"
"What are we eating today?"
"We're going to eat rocks!"
"...Understood."

Oknodie was more surprised by my quick agreement.
