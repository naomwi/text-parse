# 63 - How to Eat Flags as Easily as Cake

Lotto, Hestia, and Jigoku had planned to meet at dinner to share how many flags they'd each collected, but now they sat side by side with shocked, despairing expressions.

"So... how many did everyone collect...?"
"Five. I hiked all over the mountain until I was dead tired..."
"Four. I shook down all the easy targets I could find..."
"I got three. I ran errands for the instructors as hard as I could..."

That level of effort wasn't nearly enough.
No matter how they looked at it, they all reached the same conclusion.

"We'll have to steal flags from others."
"My reputation can't get any worse anyway. For Oknodie's sake, I won't hesitate to get my hands dirty."
"Are we doing this together? Then we can shake down some stronger ones too!"

Just working hard wasn't sufficient.
Now they needed a solution beyond that.

"Let's go. We need 16 each, so we just need to shake down 16 people for one flag each."

The time for honest effort was over.
The three-person flag robbery gang was formed.


* *


After school, Academy students can enjoy additional content by joining clubs.
Newcomers desperate for survival tend to have surprisingly high club participation rates, mainly because they're easily tricked by senior students who lure them with food.

"Lift that rake with more energy!"
"Yaah!"
"Till the field!"
"Yaaah!"

The freshmen diligently turning over and digging up the frozen fields asked their seniors:

"Seniors, how long are we just going to till fields?"
"Yeah, wasn't this supposed to be a hunting club?"
"Ha, look at these kids."

The kind seniors who had grilled meat and offered free tastings during recruitment had completely changed their expressions and were now barking at the poor new members.

"Hey. Did you eat meat or not?"
"W-we did, but..."
"But?"
"We did, sir!"
"Then you need to pay for the meat you ate."
"Then why aren't we hunting instead of farming..."
"What if hunting fails."
"Pardon?"
"What if our hunt fails and we come back empty-handed. What will we eat then?"
"The school meals, maybe...?"
"Listen to yourself. Do points come from digging the ground? We need to be self-sufficient by farming!"

The freshmen realized the truth.
The kind seniors were all acting, pretense, like those kind and conscientious professors who don't exist in reality.
From the beginning, the seniors had tricked them into a fraudulent contract to secure labor!

"I-I didn't join the hunting club to farm like this. Let me leave!"
"Sure? Then pay the penalty fee."
"W-what is this ridiculous amount of points?!"
"What do you mean? You signed it."
"This is fraud!"
"No, it's not. This contract is guaranteed by the Academy administration and you signed it. If you want to leave, you can either owe this many points and start repaying your debt."
"Ugh!"
"Or bring two friends to work in your place."
"F-friends! If I bring friends, I can escape without working?!"

The senior smiled kindly.

"Yes. Just get two friends to sign the contract. If they put your name as their referrer, you can escape without paying a single point."
"I'll do it. I'll definitely find them!"
"But finding them is one thing, you still need to finish today's club activities. If you don't, you know you'll get fined for poor participation, right?"

This is so disgusting, I'll quit no matter what.
The eyes of students trapped in the club filled with resentment.

"The seniors are truly devils. How can they make us do such horrible things without any compassion?"
"Right. If they suffered the same from their seniors, they shouldn't do this to us."

Mob and Jaku.
The two freshmen who had fallen for the temptation of meat smell and juices were genuinely dissatisfied.

"And they just gave us these weird flags. What are we supposed to do with these?"

Mob grumbled, holding the flags the seniors had given them, saying they might be useful somewhere.

"Hey, look over there. Someone carrying a ton of flags on their back."
"I know her. That's Oknodie, the top student in Group A."
"The half-orc who's the youngest and shortest freshman?"

The two rushed over to Oknodie.
Seeing them, Oknodie started running away even faster.

"Hey, hey! Stop for a second!"
"Huff, huff! Damn, how is this kid so fast!"
"No! I won't! Stop it!"

Mob felt wronged.

"What did we do?! We just wanted to ask why you're carrying those flags and what they're used for!"
"Really?"
"Yes! And also to tell you that if you join the hunting club you can eat meat, and to write Jaku as your referrer!"

Oknodie, who had been slowing down at Mob's words, immediately sped up again without looking back when she heard Jaku's comment.

"Hey, you idiot! Keep your mouth shut!"
"Aack!"

An angry Mob pushed Jaku down and chased after Oknodie, desperately trying to convince her that he had nothing to do with it, finally managing to stop her.

"Huff, huff, wheeze..."
"So what is it?"
"J-just a moment, huff, huff..."
"Can I just go?"

What kind of kid has this much stamina? Mob glared resentfully at Oknodie before speaking.

"These are flags we got from hunting club activities. The seniors said they'd be useful somewhere. Since you have so many flags, I thought you might know."
"Ah. So they were at the hunting club this time."
"This time? So you do know how to use these flags! Please tell me!"

Oknodie held out her hand.

"Give me five of them."
"Me? Why?"
"Then I'll tell you."
"Fine. But I'm holding onto you so you can't run away."
"Do as you please."

Mob firmly grabbed Oknodie and handed over the flags with trembling hands from all the running.

"So what are these used for?"
"They're for advanced class assignments. Advanced class students collect flags to submit as coursework."
"That's pretty good?"
"As you can see, there are so many that they're not worth much individually."

Indeed, Oknodie herself was carrying flags like a merchant with inventory.

"Still, it's annoying to collect them, so I'll buy them with points. A meal costs 5 points, so would you trade all of those for 5 points?"
"No way. 5 points only gets you basic rations."
"How much are you trying to sell flags for when a kid like me collected this many in half a day?"
"I just want to recruit advanced class freshmen with these. I want to offer these to get them to join my club."

Oknodie clicked her tongue in annoyance.
Mob smiled at the sight of a child who quickly became sulky when things didn't go her way.
He had imagined the Academy's youngest valedictorian would be either a legendary genius or a physical monster due to her orc blood, but in reality, she was just like any child he'd seen in his hometown.

"Forget the points. If you can get two friends to join the hunting club using my name, I'll give you all my flags."
"Really?"
"Yep. I don't go back on my word."

How many friends could a kid possibly bring? Is it right to trick a child like this?
Mob felt like he was becoming a terrible adult, but when Jaku, who had caught up, said "Whatever she said, I want the same deal!" his guilt completely disappeared.
What matters is escaping from that devilish club, not conscience.
But that was underestimating Oknodie.
Just 30 minutes later.
Four students were following behind Oknodie.


* *


While I was going around checking out which clubs the upperclassmen belonged to, an unexpected stroke of luck came walking right up to me.
The true identity of this luck was the "Flags Collected by Seniors" that rarely drops from the even rarer gift given to new club members.

"Heheh. Free loot!"

I already had plenty of flags, but the more I collect, the more uses I'll find for them.
After luring four gullible Imperial students and selling them to the hunting club, I handed each of them one flag.

"Here's your promised reward."
"Wow! Getting a free flag just for joining a club, I'm so happy!"
"Thanks, Oknodie. You're actually a pretty nice kid, huh?"

I feel no guilt for selling out these poor students.
That's all just pretending to be nice.
Do they think I don't know they were badmouthing me behind my back, saying frontier nobles and commoners have no class, or that Oknodie is an orc-like kid with a cute face but monstrous stamina?
Even now, they're snickering behind my back, thinking I'm just some foolish child.

'Next time, be kinder. That's how you avoid getting tricked by mean kids like me.'

I packed up all the flags I had stored in my bag and happily headed back.
Thanks to this, my flag count doubled overnight.
I must have nearly 40 now.

"Thank you, Oknodie!"
"You saved us!"

Making the loss of 4 flags seem insignificant, Mob and Jaku handed over more than twenty flags.
Trading 4 flags for over twenty is clearly a massive profit by anyone's standards.
The two who had handed over all their flags and escaped from the club waved goodbye cheerfully.
Poor suckers.
I won't tell them the shocking fact that the price of a single flag will eventually rise to 100 points.
You brought this on yourselves.
You made fun of me for being half-orc!
