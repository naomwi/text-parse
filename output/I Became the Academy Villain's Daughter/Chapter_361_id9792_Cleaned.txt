# Enhanced Translation: Chapter 361 - A Deal with the Professor

The skeleton instructor revealed his envy.

"You're really lucky. Without a professor's invitation, you can't enter this deep into the catacombs. Even I never came here when I was alive."

"Ah! I understand your feelings. You're jealous because dark mana increases just by breathing here, right? Just entering fills up your mana reserves, but students usually can't come until third or fourth year. So unfair!"

"The increase in mana is secondary. Only ignorant fools use magic by intuition. Proper magic is strengthened through experience and understanding. Just walking through this place will greatly enhance your necromancy skills."

The instructor wasn't wrong.
Magic grows stronger through imagery.
With prerequisite knowledge and prior experience to visualize more concrete and clear images, magic becomes more powerful.
To experience such terrifying catacombs outside the Academy, wouldn't you need to visit some nationally managed hero's tomb?
Naturally, entry permits aren't easily granted.
You could visit easily if you performed heroic deeds and died, becoming a corpse yourself.
Well, for me, since I've experienced it all before, it's not really a new experience—I just benefit from the increased mana.

'Still, this is the first time I've entered the real catacombs so easily!'

Professor Sadako normally only teaches during the first semester of freshman year, then lives inconspicuously before leaving the Academy in sophomore year.
Seeing her still teaching in the second semester of freshman year, emanating such vigorous malevolent energy deep in the catacombs, makes me feel oddly proud.

"Hello, Professor! You're looking especially energetic today!"

In the center of six glass containers emitting crimson energy, Professor Sadako stood motionless, her long hair covering her face completely.
Even a demon would be horrified by the bizarre mana cultivation technique she was performing. The professor composed herself and released a massive wave of malevolent energy.

*WHOOSH*

The six containers shook violently as if about to burst open.
Knowing what chaos would ensue if even one container opened, it was a startling sight, but the professor calmly spoke.

"I will enter... whichever container opens first..."

The six containers that had been raging like a storm suddenly became still all at once.
Senior-exclusive DLC content successfully sealed!
Having finished her bizarre cultivation, the professor restored her crimson-stained white dress to its original color and turned her head toward me.

"Have you come to learn the secret art of necromancy, the Cruel Mind Cultivation...?"

"Nope! I came to place a permanent petrification curse on a ghost and keep using its replication function!"

The professor nodded as she observed the hook ghost I was dragging with one hand.

"You're not interested in Cruel Mind Cultivation?"

"Not at all!"

Seeing my firm response, the professor pulled a pitch-black dark essence from her sleeve.

"If you learn it now, I can give you this dark essence as a gift..."

"I already have one!"

When I showed her the dark essence I had received as a snack from Papa's mansion, the professor disappointedly put the essence back in her sleeve.
Although hers seemed more colorful and filled with higher quality mana than mine, it wasn't tempting enough to change my mana cultivation technique.

"This is the ghost I need your help with!"

I held up the ghost like a one-handed weapon, and the professor stared directly into its eyes through her long hair with eerie pupils.
The hook ghost trembled and shook its body, suddenly struggling violently.

"Close your eyes."

Tremendous mana gathered in Professor Sadako's eyes.
I quickly closed my eyes as a fierce wave of mana swept through the room.
*Crack*
When I opened my eyes, the hook ghost in my hand had turned into a solid statue.

6th Tier Necromancy Magic <Prison of the Guardian>.
It was a higher-level spell than the 4th Tier Necromancy Magic <Gargoylification>, which petrifies the target and conditionally releases the petrified state.

Now the hook ghost's spirit remained alive while its ethereal body was trapped in a petrified state—its very soul had become a prison.
Professor Sadako explained the poor ghost's condition, pleased to have obtained good teaching material.

"Ghosts are souls wandering between worlds due to lingering attachments. Evil spirits are impure souls that harm the living due to grudges. They gain power from harboring resentment, but temporarily lose strength when that resentment is depleted..."

"Keeeeng..."

"Pretending to be weak is useless... I already know you planned to take hostages in your dimension and torture them to forcibly generate energy from their resentment to replace your depleted power..."

Professor Sadako wasn't easily fooled by the hook ghost's pitiful whining.

"How often do you want the replication...?"

"Once a week would be enough!"

"And any recovery beyond that weekly rate..."

"You can use it however you want, Professor!"

"Then we have a deal... Leave it with me... There's one coffin left anyway."

Unlike Rogini who was freed from petrification after being exposed to sunlight, the hook ghost wasn't even given the chance to be released from its petrified state.
Professor Sadako reached out somewhere in the catacombs, and a stone coffin flew toward us with a rumbling sound.

*Rumble...*
*Thud!*

The hook ghost was shoved into the noisily opened coffin, its eyes brimming with tears as it begged for mercy, but the coffin closed mercilessly.
Sounds of what seemed to be the hook ghost's struggles could be heard from inside, but the professor simply turned a switch on the coffin to create a barrier.
With the eerie energy filling the catacombs serving as a power source for the soundproofing magic, the sounds made by the hook ghost would never reach anyone again.

'Well, it was an evil ghost to begin with, so being confined isn't too harsh!'

"Nyaaaa... How did Oknodie manage to catch such monsters without a scratch?"

Jenya, cursed to be conscious of her blinking, breathing, and tongue placement in her mouth, sprawled across her desk like dried fish, completely exhausted.

"Maybe because I had good home training?"
