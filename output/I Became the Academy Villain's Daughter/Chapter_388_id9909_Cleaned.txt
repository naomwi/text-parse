# 388 - Fragment of the End

A mural of spirits containing the secret of the origin of vampires.
The information received through synchronization magic was shocking.

'I thought synchronization magic was just a beginner-level spell used by healing professionals like therapists or caregivers!

It was completely different.
Oknodie's magic didn't share petty emotions to heal people's hearts.
Nor did she harbor a sick mind like young care magic practitioners or criminal psychotherapists who retire after synchronizing with too much darkness and becoming mentally ill themselves.

'So this is how a high-performance brain works!'

The spirit language came through clearly, as if I had borrowed a genius's mind.
Now I understand.
Oknodie's metaphor about inserting the mana puzzle key into the memory regeneration magic lock and turning it.

'She's impressively intelligent.'

So this is what scholarship means.
I understand the meaning of culture now.
The child educated by the Foundation was truly brilliant.
How many things are there to enjoy in the world?
And how many things are there to learn?
Among all those entertainments and learnings, she accumulated knowledge that was of no practical use in daily life.
It's not something the lower class could even attempt.
Not even all upper-class people would challenge it.
Oknodie devoted herself to archaeological knowledge and spirit language that only stuffy, single-minded scholars would typically delve into.

'It probably wasn't by choice. Surely that child also struggled because of the Foundation's forced teachings.'

But I envy her.
The intelligence and perseverance to learn and digest what is taught.
A narrow-minded jealousy to harbor toward an 11-year-old child.
Was that the root of the problem?
Suddenly, Dobi encountered a strange point permitted by his talent.

[Vampire Possibility]

Beyond the fairy tale that unfolded in his mind through synchronization magic, he saw something else.
It was a door.
A large, bright red door.
A door with elegant embossed carvings following the arch-shaped frame.
A door engraved with the names of countless vampires, from the first vampire Nosferatu to the aristocrats of the night known to the world.

He grabbed the handle.
But the sticky sensation like blood made him feel something was wrong.
The viscosity was thick.
The correct answer from the touch was dried blood.
Not a door that should be opened out of mere curiosity.

Yet the reason he opened it anyway might have been out of stubbornness.

'Even if I'm just an aspiring Production Department student, even if my skills are nothing compared to those brilliant talents, I still got into the world's best educational institution.'

One more step forward.
If I just have a little more courage, maybe an opportunity will come.
Greed pushed him forward.
He opened it.
He went ahead and opened it.

A sky dyed blood-red to the horizon.
A red moon larger than the sun.

The moment of utter destruction when the blood of countless lives was forcibly extracted and fell to the moon in the sky.
Just looking up made him feel his blood being drained from his entire body.
Consciousness fading.
Death approaching slowly.
Just as his consciousness was about to collapse, an enormous mana penetrated and forcibly restored it.

"W-what... what on earth is happening?"
"Bloody Super Moon."

From the deep voice behind him, he realized this was the person who had breathed life into him.

"The progenitor Nosferatu. The ancestor-slayer Dracula. And following them, as the last vampire who carved his name in the heavens alongside theirs, Vladimir's work."

A giant whose face could barely be seen by painfully craning his neck upward.
He had a massive build to match his height.
But the greatest enormity Dobi felt wasn't his height or build.

"Everything is too late. Everyone thought the Blood Clan's open blood competition was someone else's problem."

"When the beastmen requested help, they were ignored as if it was irrelevant, and now that their powers have united, there are no races left anywhere to help humans."

"Who are you? How do you know these things? And what is all this?"

The man didn't answer Dobi's questions.
He simply spoke quietly to those following him.

"But humanity still has us."

Us. Only after hearing the man's declaration did Dobi finally sense numerous presences.

"Those skilled enough to protect the blood in their bodies with their own mana against the 'World Domain' that craves the blood of all beings."

"Those who will bring down the blood star of Vladimir, the master of blood magic and the last vampire."

"Yes. Unlike the student council that turned its back on the mission of world protection, we are the Star Hunters who have not given up fighting even at the end of the declining world!"

Vladimir, who converted all beings made of bone and flesh into pure blood mana.
The hordes of Bloodspawn, creatures rampaging on earth under the influence of the Bloody Moon, the culmination of blood magic he gathered, were gathering at the world's highest mountain that reaches the moon.
A high mountain at 12,500 meters above sea level.
Mt. Shinjeong, called the Garden of the Gods.
The master of this mountain is the Blood God Vladimir, who has ascended to new divinity, and human civilization has already perished with the death of beings who could not protect themselves with mana.
Nevertheless, they climb toward the summit of Mt. Shinjeong for the final battle.

"Let's go. This is our graduation assignment."
"!?"

These people, could they possibly be 4th-year students about to graduate!?
There was much he wanted to ask.
Whether the campus rumor that you had to kill ten 3rd-year classmates to advance to 4th year was true.
What was the origin of what he was seeing now.
Whether this was merely a record of the past like an imprint in a fairy tale.
But questions weren't necessary.
Because among the group of Star Hunters, humanity's last warriors climbing Mt. Shinjeong, he spotted familiar faces.
As if name tags appeared before his uncertain eyes, their identities were revealed.

[Grand Duke of the North Irene]
[Imperial Emperor Yayoi]
[Ultra-speed Rockfell]
[Heavenly Sound Mandela]
[...]

981st batch, 1st year advanced class.
Or skilled individuals from the previous year's advanced class.
A group consisting entirely of Academy notables.

Now he understood.
This was a synchronized memory.
A fragment of memory deep within Oknodie.
Her secret that he had recklessly pried into.

'Oknodie knew about one possibility of the world's destruction!'

She knows about a past that has already happened in another world and a future that hasn't yet arrived.
Whether she experienced it directly or received memory synchronization from someone.
A future too heavy and cruel for that small girl to shoulder.

'Was that why? That smiling face that always tried to enjoy something since we met.'

Because the future of destruction is so horrific.
Because she knows the preciousness of the brief peace that can be enjoyed until then.
Because she remembers the future tragedy that cannot be prevented without sufficient growth before graduation.
She cannot stop smiling.
She cannot stop growing.
Joy, strength.
Behind everything he considered evidence of talent was a spirit that refused to be broken and fought against such a horrific future.

But that fight was too overwhelming for someone as weak as him to follow.

"Bloodspawn are creatures made of blood. To defeat them, you must destroy the mana field that maintains the liquid in monster form while simultaneously evaporating the blood that regenerates the mana field. Put all your power into a single strike to bring down every enemy you face!"

He couldn't even deliver that single strike.
Rather, trying to summon power for futile attacks only broke down his own field that was protecting his internal mana from the power of the Bloody Moon.

"Huk!"

With his barrier collapsed, Dobi experienced the horrific death of his blood boiling alive and evaporating through his skin.


* * *


A sky dyed blood-red to the horizon.
A red moon larger than the sun.
As Dobi, who had once become part of that moon, stared blankly at the sky, a thick, solemn voice awakened his mind once again.

"W-what... what on earth is happening?"
"Bloody Super Moon."

Once again, the man who had previously breathed life into him placed his hand on Dobi's back and filled him with energy.
Dobi realized that he was not only an obstacle unable to defeat even a single Bloodspawn, but also that the man had no reason to help him.
He was just trash wasting precious energy to maintain a worthless life, unworthy of joining humanity's final resistance.

"The progenitor Nosferatu. The ancestor-slayer Dracula. And following them, as the last vampire who carved his name in the heavens alongside theirs, Vladimir's work."

The story repeated once more.
But it didn't reach his ears.
He felt ashamed.
I wish they would leave someone like me behind and go quickly.

"Let's go. This is our graduation assignment."

They left.
Dobi, who survived by not participating in the futile battle.
On the path to the summit of Mt. Shinjeong, colorful mana explosions erupted and giant Bloodspawn as large as parts of the mountain burst one after another.
But at the same time, he realized.
Explosions of that magnitude couldn't be produced while maintaining a mana barrier.
Surely the owners of those explosions had perished in those moments.
The world's most extravagant and cruel fireworks, using human lives as sparks, were launched toward the summit of Mt. Shinjeong.

'It's cruel. Too cruel.'

That this is humanity's future.
That he could do nothing but watch.
This helplessness.
This despair.
It felt painfully cruel every moment.
But when a beam of light shot up from the summit of Mt. Shinjeong, becoming a massive pillar connecting earth and sky, he finally witnessed it.
The fact that salvation exists even at the end of this despair.
The Bloody Moon.
The world-scale domain.
Melting away like snow, restoring the color of the sky.
Although the world may have perished, humanity's victory would come at this end.

'I want to witness it. Even a little closer.'

From the summit of that mountain.
I want to know what happened at the end of this despair.
As he desperately wished, a door appeared before his eyes.
The blood-red door he had opened once before.
The sticky sensation at his fingertips.
Dobi willingly turned the doorknob.
Again and again.
And again and again.
And finally witnessed the truth.

"It's your turn now. Self-destruct, Irene."
"How cruel. Marine, you as a man are always so."

The cruelty of the leader who cleared the path by making each of humanity's final heroes die in explosions.
The man who had saved Dobi.
He was ordering their deaths with his own mouth and pushing them forward with his own hands.
