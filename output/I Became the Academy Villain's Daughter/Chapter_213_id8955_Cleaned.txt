# 213 - Official Document from the Foundation

Oknodie tapped her hat and scolded it.

"You scared me. Suddenly talking to Hestia like that, please consider my feelings too."
"Sorry. I couldn't just watch when a child I've been talking to for so long was troubled..."
"It's because of guilt, isn't it? Actually, you found it painful to see the child who tried to take over your body suffering."

The hat was quite surprised.
A child with an adult-like maturity unlike her age.
Perhaps a sharp child because of her childlike purity.

"Maybe. No, that's right. The heart is like water in a cup. Actions are like water overflowing from that cup. If emotions that are too much to handle spill over into actions, then the final drop was certainly guilt."
"I'm sorry. I didn't mean to blame you."
"I know. You've been watching Hestia as long as I have. I can understand a bit about what kind of child you are."
"Really?"
"I don't fully understand the meaning behind your actions, but you know a lot."
"And?"
"People call you a good child or a bad child, but you're really just doing what you want to do."
"Hehe. You really have been watching me all along."
"You don't think of yourself as either good or bad. You can act however you want to be seen."
"I'm sorry. Let's stop here. Having your heart exposed like this isn't a very pleasant feeling!"

What expression lay behind Oknodie's bright smile?
The Second Hat Master, with her new name, dared to measure those hidden feelings that would never be shown.

"But you actually like it, don't you? Being loved by everyone."
"Tsk. I told you to stop."
"You're embarrassed. Like Hestia. You're not used to being loved, so you try to brush it off."
"Hmph. That was really mean."
"I don't think it's bad. Neither your behavior nor your heart. You don't always need to be a good child, but I understand your desire to be one when possible."
"Are you saying it's okay to do bad things in secret?"
"Good children are weak. They can't detect others' malice and are easily deceived. They get trapped behind walls and lose their own lives. I can say this because I'm like that. Being a bad child... isn't necessarily all bad."

Oknodie reached out and gently stroked the hat, which had shared such sincere thoughts filled with many worries and regrets.
Thinking that doing this would make her a good child.
As if that's what she believed.
Even if the action lacked sincerity, it was fine.
Because she found comfort in doing it.
The Second Hat Master thought:
Hestia and Oknodie.
I really want to protect these children.

"Oknodie."
"What is it?"
"The Foundation's retaliation is about to begin."
"...Huh? Against whom?"
"Against you."

And so she spoke.
About something she could have ignored as someone else's problem.

It's an obvious ploy.
I know more valuable information than you.
I'm more important than you.
Be curious.
About the information I possess.
Cling to it.
To my opinion.
There's no reason to play along with such a shallow 'power struggle.'

"You talk big, but in the end, you're just someone who has to carry out the Foundation's orders when they come, right? Cowards like you should just pray that no frightening orders come your way."

Those who are merely strong will only be manipulated by those stronger than them, but clever ones like himself can establish their position in the Foundation by faithfully carrying out orders.
Perhaps someday he might rise to the position of an educator who gathers and trains apprentice scholarship students like himself.
When that time comes, he'll finally be free.
Free from the fear of not knowing when he might be cut off.
When he might be discarded after his usefulness ends.
He could live without being haunted by the fear of orders.

"I'm going to become someone who gives orders, not someone who receives them. Unlike you."

For that purpose, he can send as many reports as needed to trouble a child.
Because that's what life is about.
When he was a child.
The parents who sold him for the price of a bag of rice.
The people from the Foundation who trained him.
Not a single person listened to a child's whims.
This was the reality he knew, the reality he had learned.

Shing

Whether she bows her head obediently or tries to spread her wings and fly.

The Foundation has ways to deal with both.
