<145 - Follower>

Dark mana is inherently treated as poison.
If even a small amount of dark mana infiltrates the body, one must immediately circulate their mana through meditation techniques to filter out and expel only the dark mana.
Whether expelled as sweat, coughed up with blood, or discharged as bodily fluids, the means and methods don't matter.

"Do you understand, Your Highness? If you consume dark mana, you must expel it rather than trying to use it."
"Pathetic♡"

The young Mesugakki, the Second Princess, scoffed at the tutor employed by the Imperial Palace.

"If you mix just a small amount of dark mana with regular mana, the destructive power increases♡ If handled properly, there's nothing more useful♡ You're just afraid because you lack confidence in controlling it, right~?"
"Well, that's true. However, when you become familiar with dark mana, you eventually succumb to temptation. Thoughts like 'If I handle a bit more dark mana, I could become stronger' or 'If I completely change my attribute to darkness, I could dramatically improve my skills' begin to surface."

The tutor issued a stern warning.

"That's how everyone becomes addicted to dark mana. Once addicted, when you encounter a monster stronger than yourself, a human traitor who's become a mage, or a demon targeting high-ranking imperial officials and royal family members, you won't be able to utilize your full power and will be defeated."

Dark mana is deadly poison to those without talent.
For the talented or wealthy, there exist secret arts to escape the side effects of dark mana.
Using special incantations.
Enhancing safety through medicinal refinement methods.
Artificially building resistance by deceiving the mana into believing the body isn't pure enough to be attacked while being tamed.
Suppressing it with opposing forces.
Such secrets exist even within the imperial family.
This makes it easier to become immersed in dark mana, and to prevent such dangers in advance, the tutor gave strict instructions.

"Look. This guard became obsessed with dark mana and walked the wrong path. He failed to prevent an incubus from entering and defiling the chastity of His Majesty's concubine, for which he is scheduled to be executed."

Thanks to the tutor who was serious enough about early education to show a 10-year-old the scene of someone having their head chopped off on the guillotine, the Second Princess Mesugakki clearly understood the dangers of dark mana.
However, the princess secretly thought to herself.

"Weakling♡ Coward♡ If his dark mana absolute value had been higher than the incubus's, the incubus would have obeyed him instead♡"

If one was going to fall anyway, wouldn't it have been better to gather enough dark mana to make even an incubus say "Oh my, it seems your rank is higher than mine," and then use the incubus as a subordinate to deal with political enemies?
Having grown up with such a fascination for corruption, the Mesugakki Princess became jealous when she heard that Oknodie was deeply versed in dark attributes and knew how to handle black magic.

"What magic did she learn this time~?"
"During the lesson on the theory of sight expansion magic, she learned dark vision magic, and instead of learning automatic tooth-brushing magic, she learned a spell that turns teeth black."
"Huh~? Useless! There must be more impressive magic. Are you sure you got the information right~?"
"I-I'm sorry. That's all for this week."
"There must be more black magic-like spells♡ Like magic that makes people blind, or guided tracking attack magic that automatically breaks others' teeth♡"
"I apologize for not meeting Your Highness's expectations, but it's quite remarkable that she either learned variations of theoretical magic on her own or applied practical magic in new ways."
"Incompetent♡ Your excuses are too long♡ Fine, go study for your assignments♡ By the way, the answer to problem 3 in that workbook is wrong♡"
"Oh! Thank you, Your Highness!"

The follower closed the door and left, and the princess could hear him chatting with a friend waiting outside.

-What did she say?
-She asked what black magic Oknodie learned. And she helped me with my studies too. The princess is truly kind-hearted. She must be deeply concerned about her fellow students' grades.
-Come on, that must be your misunderstanding. Think logically. Why would the princess care about what black magic Oknodie has learned?
-Then why do you think she's interested?
-Obviously to use it as a weakness! She's probably preparing a petition to His Majesty the Emperor asking if it's safe to leave a child who has learned such terrible black magic alone. Ultimately, she plans to have her killed with an imperial execution order!
-Shut up! Don't slander the princess. She doesn't know how to use such sinister methods! She's a good person who even helped me with my assignments both last time and this time!
-Sigh. Your head is completely in the clouds. That's why people say you're completely infatuated with the princess.

The sound of followers arguing—one thinking the princess was a good person and the other thinking she was bad.

'How boring♡'

She simply didn't want her followers' academic performance to suffer because of the tasks she assigned them, as it would reflect poorly on her.
It was true that she wanted to do something about Oknodie, but not because she wanted to kill her—she coveted Oknodie's expertise in black magic.
The fascination with black magic she had harbored since childhood.
The psychological rebellion against her tutor's early education.
All these combined to make her see only opportunity.
The chance to seduce Oknodie into becoming her underling and use her knowledge of black magic and mana-gathering techniques to become a master of dark mana!
The true intentions of the Second Princess Mesugakki were completely different from her subordinates' arbitrary speculations!

"Idiots♡ Just so you know, I won't be cheering for you♡ If you bring me the dishonor of having a princess's follower fail, I absolutely won't let it slide♡"
"Woooah! The princess is encouraging us. Everyone, let's make sure we pass!"
"Isn't this a reward rather than encouragement? Being scolded in the morning makes me so happy!"
"...You fools. She's subtly warning us that if we fail the exam, there will suddenly be problems with our family businesses, twenty-year trading partners will unilaterally cut ties, and merchant groups will stop visiting. She's basically saying to behave properly if we don't want our families ruined."
"Why have you been so negative about the princess lately? Could you be a spy planted by Princess Yayoi?!"
"Ha. Why would I align myself with the Third Princess who doesn't even have a faction?"

Regardless of her true feelings, the Mesugakki Princess diligently managed her followers this morning as well.
Of course, it wasn't encouragement or a reward, but a threat of punishment if they failed.
If they didn't want to be punished by having their palms hit with a rod by the princess, they would really have to work hard!

"Your Highness!! Breaking news!!!"

As soon as the mana verification test ended, a student came running breathlessly.

'Ah, that one...'

He was a follower peculiar enough for the princess to remember.

For Question 3 (short answer): "When James No. 1175, who hasn't learned mana, is pushed from the 50m high Academy dormitory rooftop by someone and falls at 200km/h, what percentage of mana conductivity must the safety net have for James to survive?"

He had confidently answered that since higher conductivity is better, a net with 100% conductivity should be used.

'Idiot♡ If mana flows in that well, the elasticity would be too high and he'd hit the ground anyway♡'

Still, he had correctly solved problems like "Choose the correct number of times James No. 1233, running at 20km/h, must use a body enhancement magic that doubles his speed for 1 minute (but reduces his lifespan each time) to survive without being eaten by a beast chasing him at 30km/h on a 10km mountain path."

He was a typical smart but ordinary student with poor application skills but good at simple calculations!
He wasn't confident in short answer questions but was relatively confident in multiple choice.

"Fufu♡ Did you come running to brag about getting a perfect score on the exam~? That's a little cute♡"
"Ah...! Th-th-thank you! But I didn't come to brag, I came to deliver urgent news!"

He was the one other followers criticized for being infatuated with her.
While she was somewhat annoyed that people expected her to always act like a good person just because she was human, his heart was in the right place, so it wouldn't hurt to reward him occasionally.

"Here's a reward♡ You can take one♡"
"These are... rocks the size of fists? Why would Your Highness have such things..."
"I've been researching Oknodie's training methods♡ If you carry heavy rocks regularly, training marches with supplies will be less difficult later♡"
"Oh, such great wisdom...! Then I'll take the heaviest rock!!"
"Don't overdo it and choose one you can actually carry♡ If your back gives out and you miss lectures, you'll be the only one who suffers♡"
"Sniff. Such kindness, considering even her subordinates' lecture progress. Indeed, Your Highness is a much more proper good person than the infamous Oknodie!"
"Hehe. Your flattery is excessive♡ So what's the urgent news that made you run here out of breath~?"
"Ah."

The follower put down the rock and said.

"Oknodie extracted the soul from the mouth of a student causing a disturbance during the exam and made them faint."
"Whaaaat?! Why are you only telling me this urgent news now?!"
"I-I'm sorry."
"Never mind, just tell me more details!"

Urged by the Mesugakki Princess, who had dropped her characteristic heart-adorned cutesy tone, the follower stuttered before collecting himself and answering.

"She sent the weapon of a student who was breaking rocks with dark mana flying with a single strike, tied up his feet, and then with one hand made him lose consciousness before extracting his soul!"
"Explain that incomprehensible part about extracting the soul in detail!"
"She put her hand deep into his mouth and when she pulled it out, the corrupted soul that had been rising up black was condensed in her hand, transformed into a small bead!"

Good heavens!
The Mesugakki Princess covered her mouth with both hands.
How could such an evil magic exist?

"And then? What happened to that bead?"
"It was stored in Oknodie's candy pouch! The soul bead looked exactly like candy, so perhaps what we thought was a candy pouch was actually a soul pouch all along!"
"...Fine. Take the heaviest rock♡"
"Huff. To consider the dramatic increase in your follower's physical strength by bestowing upon me the most precious weighted rock!"

Leaving behind the follower who was exclaiming that he was overwhelmed by her grace, the Mesugakki Princess immediately flung open her door and hurried to where Oknodie would be.
She could tolerate everything else, but she couldn't stand amazing magic like soul compression and extraction.
She absolutely had to learn such incredible magic somehow!
