# 127 - Lower Class Rescue Project

Oknodie isn't bad.
It's the Wiheomhae Foundation that's bad, trying to manipulate and control Oknodie.
But Oknodie is both a scholarship student and hostage of the foundation.
That child cannot escape from the foundation's grasp on her own.
She needs help.
From those around her.
From those who have been watching her.
Arcadia decided she needed to gather people.

"Gisele. We need to start the project we discussed earlier sooner than planned."
"Didn't we conclude that rushing would only cause us losses in the immediate term?"
"The situation has changed."

After hearing about Oknodie and the foundation, even Gisele's face took on a grave expression.

"If that's the reason, I cannot refuse to help."

To help Oknodie by preventing scholarship students from getting into trouble.
Arcadia and Gisele's business began as the foundation for Operation Rescue Oknodie.


* *


Bellocasio frowned as he looked at the ledger.
The supplies that should have filled a large sack to the brim barely filled half.
A clear anomaly had occurred.
For him, who had been gathering supplies in demand at the Academy through contract fraud, driving up prices, and actively exploiting the advantages of monopoly, a shortage of supplies meant danger.
It could lead to customer defection—people choosing to find items themselves or gather materials directly rather than buying from Bellocasio's business with points—and the emergence of competitors.

Ding-a-ling.

He pulled the string of the call bell, but his secretary was nowhere to be seen today.
Bellocasio, from the Administrative Department rather than the Knight, Magic, or Adventure Departments, could establish himself as a shadowy figure not only because of his own abilities but also thanks to his capable secretary.
But as the saying goes, no one is perfect, and this secretary had the problem of being lazy.

"...Are you pretending not to hear me?"

As he opened a can of preserved food, a drop of water fell from the ceiling.
Sure enough.
The secretary who had been hiding in the ceiling was drooling.

"DeadCat. Do you remember the list of students assigned to supply this drum?"
"Three second-year Knight Department students taking the 'Let's Learn the Suicide Charge Skill' course. Michael, Ro, Sir Randall."
"Gold is the densest material for the weight vests needed for enhancement training, but since gold is so expensive, more students have been making lead vests. That's why we also put in requests for lead mining."

But the sack of lead wasn't even half full.

"Find out immediately why they didn't meet their quota. Whether monsters appeared in the mine, whether a professor assigned new tasks that divided their time, or whether they're on strike."
"Advance payment."
"...Here you go."

When he held out the opened can of preserved food, DeadCat snatched it and emptied the contents in an instant.
Five points each.
A preserved food item that costs as much as a school meal, but DeadCat was a secretary whose skills were more than worth the 5 points.

'It should be about time for her to return.'

Less than an hour later, DeadCat returned.
Blood was smeared on her cat face with three whiskers on each cheek.
While wiping her cheek with a handkerchief, he asked in a kind tone:

"How was it being out and about after so long?"
"Boring."
"But you did what I asked, right?"

DeadCat pulled out a crumpled wad of paper with an expressionless face.
After unfolding and reading the contents, Bellocasio couldn't speak for a while.
Tremble, tremble.
His hand, shaking with anger, tore the paper to shreds.
He'd been had.

"...This year's first-years are quite bold. To dare to repay debts through payment agency and divert both our workers and supplies at the same time."

What DeadCat had brought was a notification document stating that the debt had been fully repaid, including the principal, at the legal interest rate set by the student council, and that if he objected, he should file a lawsuit through the student council.

"Kill them?"
"...What are we, a murder-for-hire organization? Even in the Academy, if we kill lower-grade students recklessly, we'll end up in the 'Great Prison.' The situation isn't that serious yet."

That impudent first-year, Arcadia.
A talent he had marked to subjugate even by using fraudulent contracts.
She had finally raised funds from first-year upper-class students and caused trouble.
The establishment of a new organization, the "Dark Guild."
Securing personnel and expanding business.
It was tantamount to a declaration of war against Bellocasio's "Contract Fraud Business."

But there's a difference in weight class.
Bellocasio, who had been exploiting his peers for two years since his first year, versus Arcadia, who had just started her business.
There's an absolute difference in points between the two.
He would never lose in a power struggle when it comes to money.
If he just damages the workforce they've taken...
If he makes them leave on their own...
If he deals with their customers...
The "Dark Guild" with its grandiose name will collapse on its own as its funding dries up.

"I need to find out what they're planning to do with the lead and manpower they've gathered. There was a Knight Department student named Box Chowder among the ragtag bunch from the Red Tooth Mushroom Cultivation Association, right?"
"Yes."
"Use him to gather information."
"For free?"
"...I'll prepare canned food."
"Tired of it."

Oh? This isn't good.
Bellocasio was flustered by the whim of DeadCat, who rarely demanded anything.

"Then what would you like to eat?"
"I want to scratch."
"...You're not coveting my carpet, are you?"
"I like Knight Department students."

A creepy smile appeared on DeadCat's expressionless face.

"Carelessly grown muscles are crude, rough, and hard. They're satisfying to scratch."
"You, don't tell me that blood drop from earlier..."

Lick.
DeadCat licked the blood drop on her nail.
Her eyes gleamed as she said:

"A first-year would be even better."
"...Don't touch any first-years until I give permission."
"I can't hold back for long."

Bellocasio felt the stress of his hair being pulled out at the sight of his secretary's vicious demeanor, whom he had hired based on her delicate appearance and kept around because she was cheap.

"For the safety of the first-years, I need to take action quickly."

Don't touch first-years if possible.
The moment this implicit rule is significantly violated, the student council moves.
The power players of the Academy who operate in deeper waters than someone like him who just commits petty contract fraud.
The skilled individuals who have reached the pinnacle even among the nation's top talents.
Bellocasio's heart began to race.


* *


As the weekend arrived, the pest control work was also reaching its final stages.
Even the mosquitoes that had been terrorizing students had mostly decreased.
Rogini, who had been earning points by performing escort duties thanks to the increased value of flame mages, felt regret.

"I wonder if a mosquito sorcerer who keeps spare mosquitoes won't show up somewhere?"
"If your clients hear you talking like that, they might misunderstand that you released the mosquitoes and beat you up first. That would be perfect for losing all the money you earned from treatment fees."
"...You should have shown that sharp tongue during our last operation."

The sharp-tongued Echo raised her chin proudly with a "hmph."

"Forget about that, I found a job that smells like money. I owed you last time, so this time I'm introducing it to repay the favor."
"What kind of job?"
"Adding firepower needed to smelt lead."
"Lead?"
"An organization called the Dark Guild has stepped up to rescue lower-grade students."

The more she heard the explanation, the more confused she became.
Why such an ominous guild name, and what's this about rescuing lower-grade students that doesn't match the name?
Once she arrived at the scene, she understood.
Arcadia, who led a group of students, covering her mouth with a fan and laughing demurely like a Central Empire noble lady, though her laughter was anything but demure and rather boisterous.
This kind woman, the spiritual pillar of female students from the frontier who enjoyed taking care of others, had expanded her hobby into a large-scale activity.

"You're running a business where seniors provide intensive training to students at risk of failing, and in return, their debts are repaid?"
"Yes. It's quite a good business, isn't it?"
"Where do you make money?"
"We recover points earned by lower-class students whose grades have stabilized."
"That's clever."

Students who would fail anyway would be willing to give up all their points if they could just stay at the Academy.
It's cheaper than buying grades with points, and if they're grateful for seniors coaching them and actually improving their skills, they should pay points out of gratitude.
Someone like Arcadia could generate more profitable ventures with the same points.
It's something she doesn't have to do.
A business with low profitability.
Yet it's an act of kindness because it's Arcadia.
The Dark Guild's "Lower Class Rescue Project" is essentially a charitable endeavor.

"Is Oknodie also helping with the education?"
"You must have been wondering why Di is here?"
"She's a special child."

Seeing Rogini frankly expressing her doubt, Arcadia laughed proudly and said:

"Our Di turns out to have unexpected experience."
"What kind of experience?"
"She knows quite a lot about weight vests, lead belts, and other training tools."
"...Don't tell me she's tried them all herself?"
"She says there are even lead shoes and training spoons weighing 5kg. Isn't that amazing?"

Wouldn't lower-class students collapse from muscle pain and miss lectures if they used such things?
Instead of voicing the doubt that arose inside her, Rogini extended her hand first.

"50% of the fee is paid in advance."

After all, it's the students at risk of failing who will experience the hardship, not her.
