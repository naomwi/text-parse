The long sports festival finally came to an end.
First-year lower-class students eagerly participated in cleaning duties with dustpans and brooms, earning 10 points per bag of trash collected.
Of course, students who had earned plenty of points during the competition focused on their studies instead of wasting time on manual labor, especially after witnessing the shocking skill level of the upper-class students.

"At this rate, I'm not confident I can advance to second year even if I attend all lectures during the second semester."
"We need to build our skills, not just attend lectures."
"Did you hear? There's a third-year senior who took five years off to earn points before barely advancing."
"Seriously? Then how old will people without talent be when they graduate from the Academy?"
"I don't know. Twenty years? ...Maybe?"
"But we haven't seen anyone that old."
"Maybe because we've never been to the upper-class areas? We're prohibited from entering in the first place."

First-year students who used to chat about how difficult certain lectures were, how delicious lunch was, or how much they disliked certain professors had significantly changed their topics of conversation after the sports festival.
Everyone was seriously contemplating their future, specifically about advancing to the next grade and graduation.
Tito Soga and Zuang were no exception.

"How about you, Zuang? Did you earn a lot of points this time?"
"About 90,000 points?"
"Hieek! How did you earn so many points? Did you participate in some underground chess gambling that I've only heard rumors about?"
"Nothing like that. The Academy gives bonuses for completing hidden challenges in each event. Like eliminating everyone else with a dagger."
"Ugh. You're joking, right?"
"Tito, do I look like someone who jokes around?"
"...Is it too late to start practicing stabbing the torso with this lighting stand?"
"That's not a weapon. It'll break."

After their brief banter, their attention turned to Oknodie.

"Oknodie seems really busy these days. Did she get motivated too?"
"Probably. She ended up conceding to the third-years after all."

For safety reasons, the first-years who competed against the third-years (who had been defeated by the fourth-years) for second and third place predictably secured third place without any surprises.
Perhaps because of this, Oknodie, who disappeared without a trace after lectures ended, attracted attention not only from these two but from many other students as well.
Everyone felt their own inadequacies and was working hard to strengthen their advantages, overcome their weaknesses, or look for opportunities to earn points.
But Oknodie was very different from the other students.

"Oknodie, want to go to the forest with me to find magic items with concentrated natural mana for the 'Basics and Understanding of Mana Usage' lecture assignment?"
"Sorry, I already found them!"
"What about the assignment from 'Ranged Weapon Mastery' lecture to hunt game for a sturdier bow?"
"Did that too, while I was at it!"
"What about Professor Sadako's assignment to retrieve attendance slips hidden in the cemetery at night?"
"Of course I've already finished that!"

No matter what lecture or assignment came up, Oknodie had already completed everything.

"...You already found them? It hasn't even been three hours since the magic item search assignment was given."
"The ranged weapon mastery one ended 15 minutes ago."
"And the attendance slip was just sent as a lecture substitute assignment to our magic watches..."

Oknodie suspiciously seemed to know what assignments would come up and completed them all in advance!

"I can understand the magic items and hunting, but how did you collect the attendance slip that was just hidden?"
"I stole it from Professor Sadako's grave on my way back from hunting! The professor isn't at her grave during the night because she's nocturnal!"
"Wow... so Professor Sadako lives in a grave..."

Tito Soga and Zuang were shocked for different reasons!
Anyway, despite being this diligent, Oknodie was strangely investing a lot of time in just one particular lecture.
The lecture in question was Professor Bronze de Estrada's "Developing a Discerning Eye" held every Monday and Wednesday during second period (11 AM - 1 PM).

"Tito, I think there must be something special about the Developing a Discerning Eye lecture."
"Whyyy?"
"Think about it. The professor of that lecture is called the world's greatest noble thief, right?"
"Wow, really?"
"I heard she once stole answer sheets from other professors' quizzes and gave them to students taking her lecture. If there's a precedent, it could happen again."

Tito Soga's eyes widened.

"Could that be the source of Oknodie's suspiciously thorough information and preparation?"

Zuang nodded seriously.

"Bronze must be leaking other professors' lecture contents to Oknodie in advance."
"Why would the professor do such a thing? Other professors are also experts in their fields. Wouldn't that cause big trouble?"
"She must be confident in her skills. Confident that she'll never get caught. Of course, I don't think she's just giving Oknodie special treatment for nothing."
"Then?"
"Oknodie might be secretly taking successor lessons as the noble thief's disciple. If she's a cherished disciple, the professor might provide that level of information."
"Gasp! Really?"
"She's helping her successor focus more on her own lessons instead of wasting time on unnecessary assignments from other professors. Like they say in the East, 'mutual assistance.'"
"Like scratching each other's backs when they're itchy?"
"...Are you itchy right now?"
"A little?"

Despite giving Tito a look of disdain, Zuang scratched her back with the blunt end of her dagger handle.
After giggling and twisting her body from the ticklish sensation, Tito Soga calmed down and showed interest in Zuang's story.

"So should we go find Professor Bronze and ask her to give us successor lessons too?"
"That wouldn't be easy. Just as the trend is to have one smart house and one smart summon, she'd probably want to raise just one successor, not three."
"That's true!"

Every word made perfect sense.

"So let's persuade Oknodie."
"Oknodie? Us?"

Tito Soga chewed on her finger, contemplating.
Persuading that Oknodie...

"With food?"
"We're not Isabel. Nor Gisele. We can't make it ourselves, and we can't afford to spend points."
"Hmm. Then what should we do?"
"There's a way that only you can do it."
"Only me? Really?"

Tito Soga tilted her head like a baby bird.
Seeing this, Zuang spoke with even more conviction.

"Ask Oknodie to request Professor Bronze to allow us to observe the successor lessons. With your secret weapon: aegyo."
"Secret weapon aegyo!? I don't have anything like that..."
"Just do whatever. You're on the cute side."

Tito Soga, who was so shy that she fell for her sister's trick and dragged around a lighting stand, had no way of recognizing her own cuteness.

"I don't believe it. I'm not cute at all. I'm slow-witted, have low stamina, lack strength, and also..."
"Anyway, trust me. From an assassin's perspective, your cute hostage potential is at least A-rank."
"What kind of brutal standard is that!?"
"Just trust me. As a fellow assassin, I'm sure Oknodie will feel the same cute hostage potential I do. It'll definitely work."

With Zuang's strong insistence, their adorable little plan was set in motion.

* * *

"Oknodie, if you don't allow us to observe, Tito Soga will start crying in front of you right now."
"Show me!!"
"Why do you want to see me cry...?"
"Because it's cute...?"

Tito Soga felt embarrassed.
Am I really cute...?

"Hehe. But I can't cry. I'm not sad."
"Would this make you want to cry?"

Zuang held up a sharp dagger.
Seeing the sharp blade glinting in the sunlight, Tito Soga's face crumpled.

"Zuang, that's too much... You shouldn't hurt your friend!"
"You're right. I can grant your request without going that far. Though I'm not sure if this is good for you two!"
"Sorry for scaring you, Tito. But it worked, right?"

One thing was certain—it was effective.
Oknodie voluntarily took the lead.

"I was just on my way to meet the professor, so let's go together."
"By the way, Oknodie, what kind of successor lessons do you take when you're alone with Professor Bronze?"
"It's not successor lessons. This is just the result of negotiation."
"Negotiation?"
"Listen to how cleverly I avoided the graduate student route!"

Oknodie excitedly explained.

-So you really don't want to become my disciple?
-No! I don't!
-If you really want to refuse, then complete this. Then I'll let you go for today.
-Really? Just this? You promise?

Oknodie, who was released after completing Bronze's daily task instead of becoming her disciple!

"I do get called in like this outside the regular twice-weekly lecture times, but each time I complete the daily task, earn points, and get released."

Tito Soga and Zuang tilted their heads in confusion.
Diligently completing assigned tasks every week.
How is that different from being a disciple?
