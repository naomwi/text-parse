# Graduating from the Academy by Luck - Chapter 325: The Big Shot's Fishing (2)

Xing was left speechless at the sight of the fishing spot that had appeared inside the Training Tower.

'How many people and resources were mobilized overnight?'

The area of the island that had been devastated after being bulldozed by a cruise ship had been restored, and parts of it were filled with freshwater where fish could live. This fishing spot was connected to the first floor of the tower, with water pooling in excavated sections of the otherwise intact floor.

Splash.

The water temperature felt by dipping his finger, and the taste of water when lightly licked with his tongue—both were completely different. Looking closely, he could see that the fishing spots, ranging from pond-sized to lake-sized, had all kinds of stones installed at the bottom, from heat-generating stones to ice-cold stones that lowered water temperature.

In an area where a substantial amount of metal appeared to have been used, Jonna Wiheomhae was emerging from the water with a tired face, drying his legs with a towel received from a maid. As he lowered his dress pants that had been rolled up, Xing struggled to speak.

"What is all this?"
"As you can see, it's a fishing spot. The Chairman and My Lady want to enjoy a new hobby."
"I didn't ask because I don't know. How could such an... impossible scale of fishing spot be created in just one day? And it's not like they just filled it with any random water."

Just then, a fish jumped out of the water with a splash, as if asking "Did you call me?" Xing made eye contact with it. The fish generated steel spines on its scales and shot them out with a swoosh, which Xing blocked with his scabbard. It was a vicious fish that could easily take down an ordinary angler with that surprise attack.

"Thornfish. A fish that grows in Iron Eye Lake, commonly called spine fish. It was caught overnight at the Chairman's request, who is serious about his collection activities."

Not just Jonna, but other butlers were also busy at different lakes—some wearing rubber boots and trembling as they caught and threw in fish that emitted electricity, while others released flame fish into boiling hot springs, with smoke sizzling from their hands.

It would be no exaggeration to say they had gathered rare fish from all over the world—a sight that inspired nothing but dread. The Foundation could travel anywhere in the world overnight if they wanted. They could even collect resources and build environments.

What if this capability were applied to "base construction"? Or to the "assassination" of key figures in important positions around the world?

"This is a rare opportunity. Would you like to join in?"
"I don't have the luxury to—"

As Xing was about to express his refusal, Jonna clapped his hands with a sharp sound that jolted his mind to attention. The noise struck his brain directly, making him stagger from its intensity.

"No luxury? Isn't that statement itself a luxury?"
"What do you know to interfere?"
"You want to help My Lady. Isn't that why you remain despite fearing the Foundation? Yet you refuse opportunities to become stronger by making excuses. Are you that strong already?"
"...!"
"Because you feel uncomfortable. Because harming life is distasteful. Because you want to protect nature. You can make any excuse. But what you lose in return is an opportunity for growth."

Jonna knows.

"My Lady's collection has a clear purpose. The same goes for the Chairman. Think about how rare this opportunity is—to participate in this activity, to use the fishing spot that the Foundation's butlers prepared all night."

Such opportunities couldn't be created even for a thousand gold coins. The manpower, resources, spatial transfer equipment, and facility usage opportunity—enjoying all these services inside the tower in just one day. Even ten thousand gold coins would be insufficient.

The knowledge of ecology required to create appropriate environments for each fish to stay alive until successful "fishing" and collection, not to mention the expertise needed for capture, was extraordinary. Even at this moment, the tower's facilities were constantly working to restore the terrain and return it to its original form.

If the butlers couldn't maintain the fishing spot, it could disappear at any moment—as fleeting as summer snow.

"...My thinking was shallow. I have no right to such arrogance. I'm grateful for your guidance."


* * *


Oknodie had quite a talent for fishing.

"Heya!"

She was strong. This was fundamentally advantageous in a power struggle with fish. If the fishing rod was sturdy enough and had sufficient durability, she could overwhelm the fish's strength in one go.

Whirr!

Her skill at manipulating the reel—releasing and pulling it at just the right speed to counter the fish darting in all directions—was remarkably impressive. She skillfully broke the fierce resistance of rare fish as if she had spent years fishing at sea on a boat. There was a sense of "experience" that seemed far beyond that of freshwater fishing specialists who only caught easy fish while sitting at fishing spots.

"Have you had fishing experience somewhere before?"

When the Chairman voiced the same doubt, Oknodie brushed it off with a laugh.

"That couldn't possibly be the case...?"

The Chairman's murmur suggested that Oknodie had no fishing experience. Was her suspicious and enigmatic attitude, which was evident everywhere, no exception even at the Foundation and even with the Chairman who had fed and raised her?

From Xing's perspective, who had thought the Chairman was the source of all suspicion, this made her seem even more suspicious.

"Xing, the float is moving!"
"...This time I'll catch it for sure."

Instead of forcefully pulling back against the fishing line that was being dragged away at tremendous speed as if pierced by an arrow, he gently pulled it in a circular motion, deflecting the attack. Having learned from losing three fishing lines, he blocked the first dash, and the fish that flew up vigorously into the air following the reeling motion shot its spines.

Ting-ting-ting.

While he was blocking the spines with his scabbard, his fishing rod was left with only one hand holding it. The spine fish took advantage of this gap and accelerated rapidly with an irregular movement. Reading the timing as if facing a swordsman, Xing stepped on the fishing rod with his foot to compensate for the lack of strength.

'Using just the strength of arms and legs would make me lose stability and drop the fishing rod instantly.'

Recalling the five fishing rods he had lost to the fish, he fixed his entire body like a sword stuck in the ground, raising mana to maintain stability. With this unwavering, powerful resistance, he managed to block the surprise attack without losing the rod. Now was his chance, as the spine fish's strength was depleted.

Whish!

The spine fish was pulled above the water surface according to Xing's will this time, not the fish's. Before its spines could spread, Xing dropped the fishing rod and executed a "Battoujutsu" slash, cutting off the fish's spine-shooting organ.

Thud, thud, roll.

Making a sound as if he had caught a steel ornament rather than a fish, the spine fish lost its means of resistance and after floundering about, died with a sword pierced through its gills.

"Wow. You caught it on your 12th try! Congratulations."

Clap, clap, clap.
Oknodie's applause made him feel embarrassed.

"Are you mocking me?"
"Of course not."
"You caught yours on the first try."
"Well, I have the knack for it!"
"Didn't you say this was your first time fishing too?"
"I meant the knack for swordsmanship!"
"..."

She was evading. Though it felt like an excuse, it wasn't entirely untrue. Indeed, without sufficient speed, power, and precision in his sword-drawing technique, catching the spine fish with that pull would have been impossible. If he had missed the timing, the fish would have gone back into the water to regain strength, resist, and shoot more spines. In a prolonged battle, either the angler's stamina would give out first, or they might be taken down by the fish's spine projectiles.

"Fishing is quite challenging. I honestly underestimated it."
"Hehe. But it's fun, right? And the collection effect is good too."
"What changed with that one just now?"
"Hmm~ Projectile blocking probability increased by 5%? You'll be better at deflecting flying daggers or arrows!"

Indeed, having experienced this, he wouldn't fall for the same trick again, so he would be better at deflecting them. However, he felt aversion to her attitude of describing the strength gained through experience as if it were a reward bestowed by someone.

While he had struggled with the spine fish, Oknodie had been effortlessly catching all kinds of fish. Her collection basket was already filled with fish numbering in the hundreds—a sufficient number to feel a sense of achievement. But compared to her bright expression when they started, Oknodie's face now showed a clear decrease in excitement, enjoyment, and fun.

The Chairman glanced at Oknodie's face and saw through her psychology.

"It's because it's too easy a hunt."
"Huh?"
"If someone were to call merely finishing off game that others have driven toward you 'hunting,' lazy nobles would recall only convenient and pleasant memories and agree. But true hunters would deny it. They'd say hospitality and hunting are different. In fact, this isn't ordinary fishing either."
"I'm sorry. I should have enjoyed it more!"
"No, I should apologize to my daughter who tried to enjoy it for the sake of this Papa's face. I should have understood your desire for fresh fishing."
"It can't be helped. We can't leave the tower right now. It's amazing that we got to experience this at all!"

That's true.
Xing also recalled how miraculous the experience created by the Chairman's power had been. But the Chairman's fishing, despite being a first experience like theirs, contained a deeper philosophy.

"Though this is my first time enjoying fishing, I've fully grasped its essence. To perceive the intentions of an opponent hidden underwater and deceive them into being caught according to one's will."
"!!"
"Quite a good hobby we've found. Papa thinks this hobby is quite excellent."

Was fishing... such a dangerous activity?
Xing felt as if he had done something terrifying, like turning a key in a shackle attached to a bear's ankle. Looking at Oknodie's surprised eyes, he suddenly realized.

A frightening child.
A chilling child.

If he had only looked at her with such eyes, he wouldn't have been able to read the true intention of Oknodie hidden beneath the surface of her heart, within her emotion of surprise. It had the color of 'difficulty.'

If Oknodie's view of the world were the same as the Chairman's, this evidence—which clearly showed that she herself was distinctly aware that the direction she and the Chairman were looking in was different—would never have surfaced.

The two were fishing. Not for fish, but casting bait and lines toward each other's hearts.

Only now could Xing properly recognize what kind of fishing spot he had participated in.
