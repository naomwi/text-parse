# Enhanced Translation: Chapter 341 - Villains Not in the Original

Professor Diogenes was given rare praise by Professor Platon, who had taught the advanced class in the first semester.

"How are the 981st batch first-years? Hmph. They're quite tenacious youngsters. They generally follow instructions well. I should have been more ambitious and covered the second-year curriculum too—that's my only regret."

Every year it was the same old complaints: today's students are slacking off, back in my day we weren't like this, today's kids are just lazy—but now Platon was giving a positive assessment!

Since Professor Platon was normally stingy with praise, Professor Diogenes couldn't help but raise his expectations.

"The 980th batch students also showed decent unity and cooperation... but I see why Professor Platon rated these students higher than the 980th batch."

Their level was high. The upper limit of the entire advanced class was visibly superior compared to other years.

For those aiming for the Knight Department, students from other batches wouldn't have been this desperate to practice mana cultivation techniques.

Usually, there would still be room to improve abilities through training, significant growth while attending lectures, and generally less desperation since there was still permitted growth remaining.

This 981st batch was different.
Whether they had trained incredibly hard during the first semester or felt their inadequacies and trained fiercely during the break, they all had reached second-year levels of desperation.

'The frustration of physical training no longer improving your abilities, the advance studying and academic fervor to the point where there are no more easy insights to gain from lectures. At this stagnant level, there are only two ways to pursue growth.'

Either engage in high-intensity practical battles to accumulate titles, achievements, and rewards to surpass the stagnant state.
Or compress and refine the body through mana refinement techniques and increase the total amount and usage efficiency of mana through mana cultivation methods.

"The latter methods are essentially connected. Mana refinement techniques are ultimately training methods for handling mana. Without increasing mana, there are limits to the body's compression and enhancement rates."

Several students noticeably flinched at the word "enhancement," but Professor Diogenes, not imagining that any first-year would already be reaching for the enhancement techniques forbidden in the Empire, simply thought fondly that these first-years must have trained so diligently that even their mana refinement techniques were reaching their limits.

"So, circling back, this 'Advanced Class Mana Enhancement' lecture becomes crucial. Increasing your mana quantity is the only means to accelerate your growth when you've hit a plateau."

The students' eyes filled with enthusiasm.
Their expressions showed they properly understood that this lecture was essential for their growth.

"Now, I'll teach you how to increase your mana."

The professor plopped down on the dirt floor.

"The fastest way to gain mana is through imitation. Species with abundant mana all naturally increase their natural mana reserves through their unique ways of life."

Unlike the hesitant 980th batch students who timidly sat on the ground while checking others' reactions, the 981st batch students sat down without hesitation, thinking that a little dirt was nothing if it meant increasing their mana!

"For instance, dog beastmen cover themselves in soil, frolic on the ground, and acquire natural mana dwelling in the earth. This is a natural mana enhancement phenomenon that occurs by breathing in and absorbing through the skin the mana that has permeated between soil particles settled on the ground."

Sensing something off, the students began to stir.

"Cover ourselves in soil...?"
"Is that right?"

To the bewildered students, Professor Diogenes laughed and presented the harsh reality.

"Spend time on the soil until the lecture ends. I've given you a hint, so find your own way to absorb mana. This is the introductory task of 'Naturalism Mana Studies' that I, Diogenes, established, and the first step of 'Advanced Class Mana Enhancement.'"

The students wandered around the dirt with gloomy faces.
They were told that covering themselves with soil would increase mana, but... how exactly should they do this?
Some students awkwardly dug through dirt piles and sprinkled soil on their knees, but they instinctively felt that this level of soil play wouldn't be enough.

The C-group students, including Casia, who didn't join the commotion and researched in their own way.

Oknodie and Xing.

While making a list of students who couldn't keep up with the progress, he noticed Oknodie and Xing sitting apart from the others.

'Hmm?'

Xing sat in the Eastern meditation posture called lotus position, placing his hands on his knees and closing his eyes.
Instead of forcibly tilling the ground or going underground, he emanated a strong collecting force that greedily absorbed the energy spread around him.

'The Eastern mana cultivation technique called internal energy method is indeed unique.'

Unlike students who guided mana to themselves through actions or condensed earth energy in special places, Xing indiscriminately drew in surrounding mana.
He built a massive soil foundation within himself, making earth mana gather naturally.
Going beyond dog beastmen to become the earth itself—the existence of the earth absorption method and the concept of internal energy methods were honestly impressive.
But when Oknodie placed her hand on him, his admiration turned to shock.

'No, such madness!?'

Diogenes gasped and spread a mana barrier around the two.
Placing a hand on someone's body during cultivation, connecting paths, and intervening in another's cultivation.
This extremely dangerous act could cause heterogeneous energy to seep into the body, damaging blood vessels and even destroying vital internal organs in severe cases.
Even in the Magic Tower, only master-disciple relationships practicing the same school within the same system, or knights who trained under the same master with the same training method would dare attempt such a thing, yet it was happening between Oknodie and Xing.
Forcibly stopping this could easily lead to casualties if something went wrong.

'East and West. Internal energy method and mana cultivation technique. Students with nothing in common trying to interlock heterogeneous energies!'

As he watched tensely, Diogenes witnessed a strange sight.
The mana flow from Xing became rougher and more ferocious, coursing through his entire body.
Yet paradoxically, the energy flow was more stable than when it moved slowly.
Like the difference in stability between driving 50km/h on an unpaved road versus 120km/h on a paved road—the energy's fluctuation had actually decreased!
Of course, if an accident occurred, it would be more severe at 120km/h, but Xing's control wasn't poor enough to allow such accidents.
Above all, Oknodie's guidance was excellent, and Xing's trust in her was remarkable.
