# Enhanced Translation: Chapter 359 - Replication

Dorothy felt wronged.

"After all that hard work, did I really get rejected at the first stage!?"

She had worked like a construction crew—tilling the soil, driving stakes, carefully hanging bells on strings, planting foul-smelling berries inside hollowed-out posts, and even casting security spells!

"Huh? The ghost isn't caught in the strings...?"
"It's not that it didn't get caught. It got caught but moved away immediately so you can't see it."
"Wow. Jaku, you have sharp eyes for someone in the lower class!"

While Dorothy was simply bewildered, Jaku honestly couldn't understand what was happening.

'When I encountered it, that ghost moved so unpredictably, but why did it get caught so easily in Dorothy's trap?'

Oknodie had passed through just fine.

"If we wait a bit, won't it try again?"

Just as Oknodie predicted, while they were diligently working on their assignment, the jingling sound was heard again from far away.
It had fallen into the trap again.
The ghost would appear and irritate them whenever they started to concentrate on studying.
At first, Jaku had been afraid, but now he was just annoyed.

'Just pass through already!'

Unable to watch any longer, Oknodie stood up.

"I'll show you how it's done real quick!"

Oknodie began to navigate through the trap again, as if showing off to the ghost.
Her movements were as light and ephemeral as dandelion seeds floating on a spring breeze.
Behind her seemingly weightless steps was tremendous power—she created a <Mana Shield> in mid-air with the force of a thousand pounds, stepping on it to leap upward.
Completely ignoring the trap's design by simply jumping over it, Jaku felt an intense gaze from the forest beyond.
Looking carefully, he could see a ghost with a long, hook-shaped neck staring intently at Oknodie's movements through the bare branches.

Crack.

A broken branch fell where the ghost had jumped.
That thing is seriously pissed off.
Jaku recalled how futile their resistance had been when the creature went berserk.

"Be careful, Oknodie. It might ambush you. It might be so enraged that it'll just attack you regardless of skill certification or whatever."

Whoosh.
The wind grew harsher.
Sharp killing intent brushed against their skin from all directions, like invisible knives.

This was different.

Jingle jingle jingle!

From the left.
From the right.

"This thing doesn't even care about sneaking up on us anymore!?"
"It probably figures since it's been spotted, it might as well just demonstrate its skills to frighten us!"

The ghost's strategy of proving its abilities by breaking through traps in a terrifying manner rather than perfectly was typically ghost-like and scary.

"Um, so my trap wasn't necessary after all...?"

Dorothy felt discouraged.
If it's just barging through the traps anyway, what was the point of all my work...?

"No, Dorothy's trap is serving its purpose perfectly!"
"Really?"
"Without this trap, the hook ghost wouldn't be this motivated!"

Indeed, while the hook ghost was thrashing about everywhere, it wouldn't go near the stake where Oknodie stood with her arms crossed.
The ghost had developed enough hostility to stay rather than flee, while still being wary of her.
Jaku found the scene rather strange.

'That damn tangsuyuk rice priest was easily attacked, though?'

Could it be that Oknodie was stronger against ghosts than an active priest who was invited to the Academy?

"Aaah, get down!"

Knife-throwing that would be terrifying even without a curse!

"Hmm. There are patterns I haven't seen before. I guess Dorothy's trap map was effective after all?"

Why are you standing there so calmly instead of running away!

'Huh? For a moment, Oknodie's form just...'

Using her exceptional physical abilities enhanced by magic, she had dodged the knife in the split second it passed her, then returned to her position—an extraordinary feat.

"No matter how angry you are, you shouldn't throw knives at people, Mr. Hook!"

After the ghost's knife-winds were repeatedly deflected by Oknodie, it bypassed her.
Watching the bell-strung threads and stakes being cut down, Jaku realized what was happening.

"It's the basket! The creature is stealing the cursed item!"

Instead of confronting the fearless child, the ghost was going for the cursed item.
Oknodie tried to chase after it but suddenly turned pale, covering her nose and mouth with cloth as she coughed violently.

"Cough cough! Ptooey!"
"Oh no!! The stench berries planted in the stakes only trapped Oknodie!"

Dorothy, having never experienced ghost combat before, hadn't anticipated a situation where only humans would be affected.

Hahahahaha!

With ghostly laughter, the hook ghost escaped Oknodie's pursuit and ran to a distant clearing.
It coiled its snake-like body, as long as its head, on a rock in the center of the clearing where the basket sat. Oknodie looked disappointed.

"Ugh. I allowed this because of the smell trap..."

The hook ghost laughed wickedly and grabbed the basket.
As the nail that had secured it to the rock came out with a pop, Dorothy buried her face in the ground, screaming as if watching an unbearable horror movie.
Jaku couldn't tell if she was afraid of the hook ghost or the uselessness of her trap.

"Hahahahaha!"

The ghost laughed maliciously and turned the basket upside down.
Whatever was inside, I'll replicate it before your eyes.
And if you don't take it, I'll kidnap you!
The ghost's evil intentions focused on the object that fell from the basket.
The cursed item that emitted such an enticing smell that it was worth breaking through the terrible trap field.
The ghost's head naturally straightened into a hook shape, curious about its identity.

"?"
