<313 - The Chairman's Banquet 3>

"Let's adjourn this gathering.
You're free to spend your time as you wish now.
Feel free to return to your accommodations on the tenth floor and get some rest."
The students eagerly awaited these words of liberation from the Chairman's mouth.
Despite their burning gazes, like workers yearning for revolution, the Chairman showed not the slightest hint of impatience.
He neither lacked the power to be concerned with mere students' opinions, nor was his personal conviction shallow.
Rather, he felt curious.

'Except for the Mesugakki Princess, she's the only one who truly grasped the value of this opportunity.'

An opportunity to eat whatever dish one desires.
It's an opportunity that those who haven't realized the truth—that collection equals power in this world—cannot appreciate.
Only those who grasp enormous power understand the principles and logic of the world.
Diverse experiences.
Rich collections.
In this world, one's capacity as a collector directly connects to personal strength.
Swinging a sword ten times, a hundred times, a thousand times makes one stronger, but to grow stronger afterward requires ten thousand swings, a hundred thousand swings, a million swings.
If that effort could be replaced by eating a few types of food.
If eating one rare dish could substitute for ten million, a hundred million, or a billion sword swings.
Unless one were a fool, who would stubbornly stick to just swinging a sword?

'When collections of the same type accumulate, their value increases.'

Just as sword practice becomes less effective the more you do it, the rate at which food adds to your strength slows down the more you eat.
So if given a once-in-a-lifetime opportunity, it made sense to eat food you'd never tried before.
But you can't eat food you don't even know the name of.
You can't order what you don't know.
Yet this child boldly placed an order.

"I'd like the Eternal Essence Medicinal Soup made by boiling the sap of a ten-thousand-year-old tree!"

Spirits dwell in trees that have lived a hundred years.
Thousand-year trees are absolutely swarming with them.
As for ten-thousand-year trees, pointy-eared elves from who-knows-where gather around, spouting nonsense about world trees and whatnot, preventing anyone from getting close.
To boil the sap of such an ancient tree and still have enough left to call it a soup would require an unimaginable amount of sap.
The very idea is so sacrilegious that no elf or fairy would have shared it, and it's a dish too audacious for anyone to conceive, let alone create.
That's what made it interesting.

"That dish will require some time. How long will it take to gather the ingredients and prepare it?"

"Three days will be needed."

At the Chief Secretary's answer, the Chairman gave Oknodie a warm smile.

"Exceptional creativity is always admirable in itself. To honor your remarkable idea, I'll serve you another unique dish before the Eternal Essence Medicinal Soup is ready in three days."

"Wow, two uniques! Buy one get one free!"

The dish served instead was a sponsor-exclusive food from Monthly Gourmet.
As soon as a knife touched the seemingly normal dish, screams erupted from the food.
The already tense banquet table now echoed with someone's screams and sobbing sounds.

"What on earth did you give Oknodie?"

"It's the Steak of Lamentation, a sponsor product from Monthly Gourmet. Of course, as it's a limited unique-grade dish, I cannot offer it to everyone."

"Oknodie. Don't eat that."

Even Gisele turned serious, and Isabel grew angry.
A dish so disturbing that even those who had been cautiously observing felt compelled to speak up.

"Why not?"

"I can make you something just as delicious without having to eat that."

Oknodie looked down at her plate with obvious reluctance.

"But unique dishes are so rare..."

Her bold reaction revealed everything.
Oknodie doesn't discriminate against food.
No matter how immoral the food might be.
She has no collector's limitations.
It was evidence that her potential for strength was greater than anyone's.
The range of one's tastes is as important as the potential of one's talent.
The wider the range, the less picky one is.
The collection grows more diverse.
Diverse collections lead to strength.
The more boundaries one crosses, the stronger the collector becomes.

"I'll try one bite and then decide!"

Oknodie finally brought the fork with a piece of steak to her mouth.
The moment her teeth touched the meat, a scream burst forth with the meat juices—"Kyaaaaa!"
The scream came not from Oknodie but from the meat being chewed by her teeth.

'Swallow it. And prove it. That your potential for growth is this extraordinary!'

The Chairman, in a rare display, watched her with expectation.
Those in power do not have faith.
They know how futile such an act is.
Instead of believing, the powerful put on shackles.
Because if you don't obey, your neck tightens and you suffer.
If they believe in anything, it's in the pain felt by those who are leashed, not in someone's goodwill or free will.
Yet he believed in Oknodie.
Her preposterous idea of calling him Papa.
Her unpredictable and extraordinary behavior.
All of these things planted expectations in him.
That this child could do it.
That she could become a unique existence like himself.
That she would be the unique talent who could bring new changes to his collection that had reached its limits.
Oknodie, who had captured the Chairman's expectations.
She responded to those expectations.

"Blehhh."

By spitting out the single bite of steak back onto the plate.

"My goodness. Are you a picky eater?"

"Papa, that's going too far. It's not just making sounds—there's actually a soul in there!"

"That's what makes it so special. A soul is an extraordinary being. That's why soul food can be called a truly unique dish."

"By that logic, human flesh would count for the collection too! No matter how important collecting is, crossing that line makes us no different from demons of the demon realm."

"How regrettable. Perhaps it was too early to serve such food to a young child."

Oknodie cleared her plate.
Not by eating it, but by throwing it in the trash.

"I'll count this as having received the food."

"Very well. Still, I hope you won't show such pickiness at our meal three days from now."

"Of course not! The Eternal Essence Medicinal Soup isn't made with souls or human flesh."

The students felt relieved that Oknodie hadn't crossed the line.
What an amusing sight.
But was that really true?
In this world, you only see as much as you know.
The ten-thousand-year tree sap that must be extracted to make the Eternal Essence Medicinal Soup that Oknodie requested.
They couldn't even imagine how many troops the Foundation would mobilize to collect it.
If they knew the number of elves and fairies who would die trying to stop the Foundation, they would rather recommend eating the Steak of Lamentation—the difference in the number of lives lost would be that significant.
Yet they don't tell her not to eat the Eternal Essence Medicinal Soup.
Because they don't know the price of making the dish.
So this is pickiness.
The pickiness of a child who spits out screams she can see and hear, but gladly swallows the unseen and unheard screams from far away.
And that made her all the more appealing.
Oknodie's way of life and philosophy satisfied the Chairman's expectations.
This child is interesting.
More so than he had thought after reading Inky's report or hearing Jonna's testimony.

"Chief Secretary. I want to see my daughter eating the Eternal Essence Medicinal Soup with my own eyes."

"We've already organized an expedition. We plan to mobilize elite private organizations, including the Esonia Exploration Team, to lure the elves to the border area and target the ten-thousand-year tree in the meantime."

"I won't interfere with the operation. Just meet the deadline. There won't be a second chance. No matter what sacrifices must be made, wasting my time will not be tolerated."

This was a directive.
Given directly from the Chairman to the Chief Secretary.
Those who disobey directives pay a tremendous price.
Either by receiving an even more difficult directive.
Or by paying with their body or the rest of their life a price greater than the benefit gained from fulfilling the directive.
This expedition was prepared with the expectation of tremendous bloodshed.
If one person had to pay that price with their body, what kind of ordeal would they have to endure to repay it?
The Chief Secretary didn't even want to imagine.
Because the repayment would be extremely harsh, spanning a period that would feel like eons to an individual.

"Jonna Wiheomhae. You serve a very frightening young lady."

"I believe the young lady is venting her frustrations. She nearly lost her friends because of the Chairman's test."

"Are you saying she's questioning whether the Foundation has the right to test them in return?"

Away from the banquet, in a private setting.
On the stairway between floors.
Away from the Chairman's eyes, the Chief Secretary asked Jonna, the butler who had directly taught and watched over Oknodie, for his thoughts on today's events.

"No family, regardless of the parent-child relationship, is harmonious without ever fighting. The young lady has reasons to throw tantrums, doesn't she?"

"She claims to be the Chairman's daughter, and indeed, it wouldn't be strange to call her the Little Chairman."

The Chief Secretary's face grew more troubled.
Thinking that if she threw two tantrums, the Foundation might stagger.
Of course, this was just his own exaggeration, born from having to carry out a life-threatening directive because of a child's tantrum.
At most, it was just an invasion into the lair of a barbaric subspecies that hides in forests and worships ancient trees.
The Foundation's power was far too immense to stagger from something like this.
