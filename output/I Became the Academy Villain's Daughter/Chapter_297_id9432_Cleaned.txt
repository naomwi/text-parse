# Enhanced Translation: Chapter 297 - A Change of Heart

As soon as she heard about Oknodie's weakness, Ishtar felt her path forward becoming bleak.

She had no idea.

She couldn't even begin to guess what Oknodie's weakness might be.

At close range, Oknodie displayed the traits of a tricky swordsman who deceived the senses with high-speed movements that left afterimages, exemplified by her [Acceleration Afterimage Sword].

At medium range, she would pull out her wand and employ the wicked arts of dark magic, represented by her [Dark Ball].

And at long range, she would suddenly disappear with [Hiding], becoming impossible to find.

She was a terrifying girl with no predictable pattern—whether she had left the battlefield, was hiding and waiting for an opportunity, or circling around to attack from behind.

'Even saying she's weak against holy swords, holy magic, and divine arts because of her abundant dark mana can hardly be called a weakness.'

What good was knowing a weakness?

She wouldn't let herself be hit in the first place.

The physical weakness of such a person would be nothing but a shiny but useless trinket, even if obtained as an auction item.

'I need something like what the Hero Party's sponsor knows—a fatal weakness that Oknodie herself hasn't even considered.'

Without that, it would be worthless.

But would Oknodie be unaware of this fact?

She was a girl who had plotted to kill a professor more than a month in advance.

Today's events had made it clear that despite the public perception of her as a good girl wrongly labeled as the Dark Princess, Oknodie was indeed the Dark Princess in reality.

She must have meticulously planned for this moment as well.

"Ishtar. 3.4 million points."

She invested all the points she had.

She couldn't bid any higher.

Be satisfied with this much.

Just let us take the weakness and go.

In response to Ishtar's desperate plea, Oknodie smiled brightly and raised her hand.

"Oknodie. 3.7 million points."

Ishtar's head drooped in defeat.

"I'm sorry, Yupi. My strength wasn't enough."

"It's not over yet."

"Yupi...?"

"I'll give you all my points."

Right there, Yupi transferred all her boarding points to Ishtar.

400,000 points.

By adding all of Yupi's points, miraculously, they had one more chance to raise the bid.

Ishtar felt like she might cry.

She thought herself pathetic for causing her childhood friend so much trouble and now borrowing her points, but she simply couldn't refuse them.

"I'm sorry."

"Don't apologize. The Hero Party is a party of both of us—you and me. The risk of losing this auction item affects both of us."

As Yupi eased the burden on her shoulders, telling her not to feel guilty, Ishtar truly felt today that Yupi was indeed a saintess.

We must succeed.

I'll make it big.

And someday, I'll repay this debt to Yupi.

Tenfold, with generous interest.

"Hehe."

But why was that girl, Oknodie, still smiling?

Her resolute determination and burning will wavered severely, like sand thrown onto a campfire.

She felt goosebumps rising.

It's not over yet.

That terrifying smile directed at her seemed to say exactly that.

Why?

Why on earth?

"So 3.8 million points is your limit!"

"You, don't tell me...!"

Even at this point, you won't let us have it?

Regardless of Ishtar's shock, Oknodie mercilessly raised her hand.

"Oknodie. 4.9 million points."

There were no more points to raise the bid.

Even combining with Yupi's strength wouldn't be enough.

The two women lowered their heads helplessly.

Oknodie tilted her head toward the Hero Party.

"Giving up?"

"You cruel girl. Do you really need to confirm the kill to be satisfied?"

"Well, she is an assassin after all."

At Zuang's casual comment from behind, Ishtar raised her head in anger.

Oknodie had already approached and crouched right in front of the hero's indignant eyes, staring directly at her.

...Too close.

This proximity is uncomfortable.

The discomfort she felt inside might have been premature.

Compared to what Oknodie said next, that discomfort was nothing.

"If you don't have enough points, you can earn them here."

"You... don't tell me you were aiming for point collection all along...?"

Oknodie took out a vuvuzela from her chest, put it to her lips, and made a "pwoooo" sound.

"Correct!"

"Since I've made your friends suffer quite a bit, you'll understand this much, right?"

Oknodie's blatant statement clearly conveyed her intent for punishment.

"By the way, I won't update my bid anymore. If you can turn this around, I'll let you have the item!"

The exact total of Ishtar and Yupi's combined points was 3,898,900 points.

They were short 1,101,100 points to reach 5 million.

Ishtar, who had clearly anticipated her childhood friend's thinking, had quickly pressed on Yupi's blood vessel to make her faint.

"The foundation scholarship was 100,000 points, and arranged marriage was 1,000,000 points, right? Breaking finger joints was 100 points per instance."

1,101,100 points.

Before time ran out, Ishtar mercilessly bent her finger joints with a cracking sound.

A violent action of repeatedly breaking her fingers, which quickly returned to their original position thanks to the hero's recovery ability.

If there had been enough time, she might have been able to earn all the points just by breaking her fingers.

"If there are no higher bids, the auction will end in one minute from now."

But the time to update the bid price in the desert island auction was only one minute from when the butler spoke.

One minute was ridiculously insufficient to break finger joints 1,100 times.

'It was because my determination was lacking.'

Despite having skill, she had to pay a greater price.

Because she had been complacent.

Because she had been arrogant about her position as the hero.

Because she had been captivated by the number of points in her hand.

Because she had underestimated the strength of the Dark Princess Oknodie.

It was all her own karma.

Ishtar vowed with tears of blood in her heart.

Never to be careless again.

Never to underestimate again.

Oknodie was not beneath her.

She was a mortal enemy who must be eliminated with all her might.

"Give me the reward for breaking my fingers 11 times."

"11,000 points acknowledged."

"Eleven thousand...? Not eleven hundred?"

Oknodie answered her question.

"If your points aren't negative, you can receive 10 times the reward on the collection board. Isn't that great?"

What?

So I only needed to break my fingers twice...?

The disappointment was brief.

Great joy followed.

"That's wonderful. I don't have to go through with the arranged marriage!"

"...I'll take the foundation scholarship."

"100,000 points have been credited."

To pay the remaining 100,000 points, she chose items worth 10,000 points from the collection board, and all the missing points were filled.

"Ishtar. 5 million points."

Looking at the darkened cruise ship, Ishtar's heart was also dyed in pitch-black darkness.

In her childhood, a powerful figure obsessed with the evil power of dark mana had exchanged the lives of villagers living in a remote hamlet for dark mana.

The girl who had appealed to justice and hoped for a fair trial witnessed the reality of power trampling justice.

With power, you don't get hurt.

Without power, you can only be hurt.

When that fact materialized before her eyes, Ishtar began to earnestly wish for the first time.

That she wanted to gain power.

That she would become stronger than anyone.

Though she hadn't learned how to distinguish right from wrong or what justice was, she had the talent and opportunity to become strong faster than anyone else.

'Oknodie was stronger than me.'

Not in physical power, but in mental calculation.

With an evil wisdom that she herself lacked.

She keenly realized why it was called mental 'power.'

She needed power.

A new kind of power she hadn't possessed until now.

'When I return to the Academy, I'll find a strategist. Yupi can fulfill her role as an advisor, but she's not a formal strategist.'

While physical strength could be increased by learning mana refinement methods, mental power was different.

To walk a path one hasn't pursued before, someone who has walked ahead must first extend their hand.

Who among the first-years could be such a strategist type?

First, there's Gisele.

The guy who took all my points with his gacha product.

But he's Oknodie's strategist.

Though I would never bow my head to an imperial princess.

...Should I recruit hidden talent from the lower class?

Students openly expressing their disappointment.

This works out well.

An opportunity to improve her image with the lower-class students had arrived.

And coincidentally, a chance to get back at the foundation that had put a collar on her.

"I'll help you."

An unexpected figure that no one had anticipated, Hero Ishtar, joined the shipboard rebellion.
