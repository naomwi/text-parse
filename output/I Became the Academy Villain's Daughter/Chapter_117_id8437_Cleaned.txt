# 117 - Senior's Dormitory

"Oknodie. Some seniors have been snooping around about you."
"Oknodie. Did you do something wrong to the seniors?"
"Oknodie. Be careful when you're walking alone!"

Thanks to building goodwill with many students during various events, NPCs kept warning me as I passed by.

"Thank you!"

When I smiled, showing my white teeth, the NPCs would pat my head with faces that said I was adorably cute, then look down at their own hands with satisfaction before walking away.
I might not feel the thrill of terrifying and controlling people with my muscular strength anymore, but this pet-like feeling isn't so bad either.

'I'm not sure if I should be grateful for this though.'

I don't know what my childhood friend Sia was thinking when she sent me to this game world.
I have many questions, but perhaps because I'm in this bright and cheerful body, gloomy feelings and worries quickly fade away.

When problems arise, just solve them one by one!
Since the seniors investigated me first, I should be allowed to investigate them too, right?
Thanks to the students' advice and some light information gathering, I quickly found out who was snooping around.

"Hmm. So they're called 'Paper Company' and 'Box Chowder.'"

Both names I've never heard before.
They're extras.
Second-year students from the Magic Department and Knight Department respectively.
They have enough skill to enter Gift Academy, but they lack the talent to stand out and have low mana reserves.
They barely passed the entrance exam in their first year through effort or minimal talent, but they can't even keep up with the average level of their grade.
They work hard, but their absolute talent value is so low that they've hit a wall and are being weeded out—typical underachievers.

'They're like newbies!'

Beginner players are exactly like that.
At first, they struggle just to survive at the Academy.
Then they start hoping for shortcuts by following others' bad examples.
If they get lucky, they might enjoy the benefits for a while, but nine times out of ten, it's not them but some other lucky NPC who reaps the rewards.
The illegal red-toothed mushroom cultivation was no different.
Wasn't it me and the principal who actually consumed the magical spores?
The fruits of the underachievers' hard labor are often snatched away by passing talented students.
This is why you shouldn't do illegal things.
If something gets taken, you can't even demand compensation or complain about the injustice.

'They're pitiful, so I don't want to punish them too harshly, but maybe I'll tease them a little?'

Before Monday.
Late at night on the weekend.
I quietly made my way to the second-year boys' dormitory.

'Hmm. The security is so sloppy no matter when I see it.'

A magical net.
If any living creature of a certain temperature and size touches the net, a detection spell immediately sends a signal to the supervisor's room.
It's not as strict as the security for the freshman dormitory, but they do have basic scanning to prevent crime!
But if you don't get caught, it doesn't matter.
With my small 133cm height and tiny body, it was simple to climb the window while avoiding the magic detection patterns.

Swish, swish, swish.

Like a lizard, I effortlessly climbed the outer wall and peeked inside the window.
There was no one in the bedroom—perhaps they went out.
That's even better!
When I pushed the window slightly with my hand, it rattled, feeling locked.
Well, I anticipated this and brought a wire.

Rattle, rattle...
Click.

After picking the window lock and entering, I stepped on something like crumbs on the floor.
This senior, really.
He must be the messy type.
Like they say Westerners wear shoes even in their bedrooms, this dormitory really lacks an Asian sensibility.

"Senior, you're so careless. Leaving traces everywhere. Aren't you afraid that living so messily will attract mice?"

No human presence in the room.
I came to teach a lesson about not snooping around others' business, but seeing the dirty room made me so angry that I moved around the dormitory freely.
It's probably a shared room, but luckily, this senior has a four-person room all to himself.
Thanks to that, I could move around freely and find a broom and dustpan.

Swish, swish

While diligently cleaning up the garbage, I found something caught on the broom under the bed.
When I pulled it with my finger, there was a textbook.
But who keeps textbooks under the bed?
Flipping through the pages, sure enough.
There was a magazine with naked women seducing men or doing those kinds of things.

'Hmm.'

This senior has quite the taste.
When I was a man, I came across various adult materials, but I never knew about this kind of preference.

"If this were discovered, you'd be dead. Hehe."

I should understand with the sensibility of my former male self.
Blackmailing someone with this would be a sex crime these days.
Me, upholding gender equality—so diligent!
I put the textbook back under the bed exactly as it was.
While searching the room more and waiting for the senior to return, I noticed an assignment on the desk.
Bored of waiting, I solved the problems, but this person still wasn't showing up.

'Is he staying out overnight?'

I was curious about his strange name and wanted to meet him, but it's unfortunate.
I guess today wasn't my day.
Still, don't be too disappointed.
It's a waste of time, but today isn't the only day.
I'll come play again next time!

But why did my Fear Inducement experience increase?
On my way home, I thought about it carefully and realized.
A room that suddenly cleaned itself.
The Fear Inducement function that activated on its own.
A brilliant rational deduction struck me.

"Aha."

Senior must have a phobia of house fairies!
A senior who fears cleaning—that's interesting.
Later, I should go cook for him too, which would probably scare him more, making me even more excited.
If I have a chance to cook, I'll definitely bring some here!

Late at night.
Just as he was getting used to the uncomfortable sleep in the closet.

Rattle.

His ears caught the sound of the rattling window in his half-sleep.
Paper Company, whose hearing was considered exceptional even among the lower-ranked students.
Living on a high floor, it was common for windows to shake in the strong wind.
He tried to go back to sleep, thinking it was just that.

Rattle, rattle.

Until he woke up from the window shaking unusually loudly.
What kind of wind is this strong?
Are mosquitoes hovering in front of the window?
Just as he was about to open the closet to check, he heard the sound of the window lock turning—a sound that shouldn't be heard.
Do mosquitoes pick locks with their proboscises these days?
It would have been better if he had just had such an absurd thought.
After all, mosquitoes could be swatted if they didn't come in swarms.

Step.

But there was a footstep.
The powder he had spread in front of the window to detect intruders did its job.
It wasn't a sound that would be made by a lightweight mosquito despite its size.
Not a mosquito.
A person.
As if aware that his identity had been discovered, a voice that seemed to look down on him was heard.

"Senior, you're so careless. Leaving traces everywhere. Aren't you afraid that living so messily will attract mice?"

It was a voice that shouldn't be heard from where he was hiding.

'Impossible. This is the 10th floor!!'

An impossibly high place for a person to enter.
There's no way a first-year would know flight magic, which is only taught from the third year.
Then how did this child climb up here?
His terrified brain sought answers.
And an unwanted memory surfaced.

-There's also a rumor that she's an active assassin trained by some noble family.

Yes, an assassin could do anything.
Sure enough, she discovered that he had been snooping around.
Box Chowder was right.
Oknodie is a thief and an assassin.
She must have been displeased with the impertinent senior who got on her nerves.

"If this were discovered, you'd be dead. Hehe."

A voice without a trace of malice, even seeming to enjoy itself.
This child enjoys killing!!!
Paper Company shut his eyes tight.
He was afraid that even the whites of his eyes might be visible through the closet gap.
While trembling in fear and inwardly begging for forgiveness for his foolishness in messing with someone he shouldn't have, Oknodie showed no signs of leaving.

Swish.
Swish.

As if knowing his hearing was sensitive, she made sweeping sounds while wandering around the room, erasing traces of human habitation one by one.
What does this mean?
What is she trying to do?
Surely she's not just cleaning because she wants to.
It's a warning.
Yes, this is definitely a warning.
I already know what you're good at.
Erasing traces of people is nothing to me.

'Please forgive me, please forgive me, please forgive me!'

Paper Company, praying desperately with his eyes closed.
By the time he noticed, the cold breeze coming from outside had long disappeared.
Is she gone?
Is she really gone?
What if I open the door and she's standing in front of the closet saying, "Found~ you. Hehe."?

"Huh...!"

Trembling with fear, Paper Company cautiously emerged from the closet, clutching his pounding chest and belatedly gasping for breath.
All traces of human habitation had disappeared, and the room was neatly organized as if empty.
For some reason, his assignment on the desk had answers written in neat handwriting.
Why did she solve this?
She wouldn't have done it out of boredom.
Is there some hidden meaning?
More importantly, this... although it's an assignment given to prepare for the lecture, it's so difficult that the professor said we could leave early if we solved it all?

'Why are you doing this to me? What are you trying to make me do during the lecture by solving all the problems?!'

Paper Company trembled in fear for a long time.
