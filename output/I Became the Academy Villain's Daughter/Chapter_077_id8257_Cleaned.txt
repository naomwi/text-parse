# 77 - A Child Who Isn't Afraid of Water

Professor Platon was more sincere than anyone about cultivating philosopher-kings.
A healthy mind dwells in a healthy body.
A healthy mind conceives healthy wisdom.
A healthy wisdom builds a healthy nation.
The rootless maritime kingdom of Florence needed well-grounded philosopher-kings, and Professor Platon, a patriot, might abandon his flesh and blood body but could never abandon his patriotism.

'These 981st batch advanced class students have quite good tenacity.'

Compared to the 980th batch just one year ahead, there was a noticeable qualitative difference.
Above all, there was Ishtar, the hero chosen by the monotheistic deity "Sophemia of the Sun."
Her presence had caused talented individuals from imperial noble families to all enroll in the same batch.

'The hero's companions. The hero's friends. They all sent their cherished children, aiming for such positions.'

The Empire's three great meritorious ducal houses:
Duke Fried Chicken's family heir, Honor Fried Chicken.
Duke Potato Pizza's family heir, Cheddar Potato Pizza.
Duke Iron Plate Charcoal Ribs' family heir, Lev Iron Plate Charcoal Ribs.

The three great houses, which typically only sent collateral family members or adopted children, had now dispatched their most promising talents—the most legitimate or skilled individuals who would be responsible for the next generation.
Thanks to this, other families who were mindful of these three houses also sent their outstanding talents in large numbers.
Consequently, renowned talents from various remote regions around the world also concentrated in the 981st batch.

'Though they pretend otherwise, they're all watching that child Ishtar. If they could make the female hero a member of their family, their family's status would rise significantly.'

If the children happened to fall in love and accidentally have a child, it would actually be most welcome.
Who would oppose or create friction by going against someone willing to marry to take responsibility for the hero and her child, thereby incurring the hero's wrath?
Above all, this hero did not yet have an *antagonist*.

An era of peace.
A time without war.

The fact that the enemy had not yet materialized meant the hero had that much more time to prepare for growth.
That's why the hero entered Gift Academy, the world's premier educational institution, aiming for growth.
There was also a calculation to recruit the world's best talents as companions for the hero's party.

Frankly speaking, if the hero were to exclaim, "You, become my companion!" that talent would immediately receive a guarantee of success and advancement chosen by Hero Ishtar.
So everyone was desperately trying to swim impressively around Ishtar, and students who didn't yet know how to swim were learning with burning determination.

'I can praise their concentration, but I don't like their impure intentions.'

Of course, there were rare talents who weren't aiming for positions as the hero's companion or husband.
A prime example was the Empire's Second Princess Mesugakki.

"Ahah. Are you perhaps a hippopotamus? You're drinking so much water. Gulp♥ Gulp♥ Gulp-gulp-gulp♥ How much are you planning to drink~? Pathetic follower. You're so bad at swimming!"
"Ugh, I-I'm sorry, gulp, blub blub!"
"Please wait a little longer, Your Highness. Before this lecture ends, ugh, I'll definitely master freestyle swimming and be of help even underwater!"
"Pfft~ I can't wait until the end of the lecture. Take this, and this!"
"Aaaah, I'm sinking, I'm sink... blub blub!"
"Ahahah. You sink just because the princess climbed on your back? Properly enjoy the lifetime honor of becoming the princess's water chair~"

Even Professor Platon, second to none in poor character, wondered if this was appropriate as the Second Princess thoroughly enjoyed teasing her followers.
The followers being ridiculed were blushing with pleasure, but they were all definitely headed for supplementary education.
Though they had basic athletic ability and were learning quickly, they were still far from reaching the level of swimming skill that would satisfy him.

"Ugh, the princess's water chair has become an underwater chair...! Glub!"

...Well, those ones would quickly adapt if they didn't want to sink and die.

'From the frontier, Seo Gwi-yeon is taking the lead as expected.'

The Northern Grand Duchess Irene, who typically freezes everything first, was learning how to kick while holding a float due to her terrible swimming skills.

"Why am I doing this...?"

Bubble bubble.
Her mouth, half-submerged in water, released bubbles instead of complaints.

"?"

Bubble bubble.

"!"

Blub blub.
As if she had never complained that swimming was child's play, she was now thoroughly enjoying it.
It was amusing to see this unexpectedly childlike side of the cool-headed beautiful girl, but at this rate, she was definitely headed for supplementary education.

"See, this is how you swim. Do you now understand the greatness of me, Arcadia?"
"Nyaaa! Amazing, nya!"
"So what's the method for swimming while wearing armor?"
"Method? I've shown you all the swimming methods. Can't you just copy what you see?"
"...How annoying, nya!"
"...I don't think underwater dolphin kicks are something you can learn just by watching."

Compared to the beginner-level Irene, Arcadia was relaxed, gliding through the water with dolphin kicks and sidestroke.
Her attitude of teaching and trying to raise the level of struggling cadets was commendable, but her teaching method deserved zero points.

'Finally, that group.'

The most eccentric group, who should have been the first to drop out of the advanced class—it was a mystery how they even made it this far.
Gisele and Son O-cheon, Isabel.
Dorothy and Rockfell.
Jigoku, Hestia, and Lotto.
The Oknodie group with its diverse members and composition.

"Splashing water is surprisingly fun. It's not often that I get ahead of Son O-cheon in physical activities."
"Uhahah! I may not be smart, but I'm a genius at using my body. I'll master this swimming thing in no time."

Splash splash.

"...Hey, could you stop making water cannons with your feet? Swimming isn't just about kicking the ground hard."
"Uhahah! I can't hear the words of someone whose splashes are weak!"
"......"

Son O-cheon was making cannon-like splashes while Dorothy, Rockfell, and Lotto screamed as they were hit by the water spray from behind.

"Aaagh, the waves keep coming, and I'm drinking too much water—it's agonizing!"
"It can't be helped. The forest is our specialty. Even for me, Rockfell, known as the Swift Swordsman, this lecture is challenging..."
"Wouldn't it be better to do something about that ignorant monkey first?"

As Son O-cheon was venting his frustration at not being able to swim by tormenting his companions with water cannons, an enormous water cannon drenched him.
Hestia, whose leg muscles were no less powerful, smiled fiercely as she flexed those muscles.

"Then you should be able to hear my voice clearly."
"You bastard. You've done it now?"

The fight between the two had turned from swimming to a water cannon battle.
Amid the chaos of the swimming beginners, only Oknodie was diligently attempting to cross the river.
But Oknodie's speed seemed unusual.

'What's with this child? Why is she better at swimming than the pirate obsessed with shooting or the princess of Florence?'


* * *


Jigoku is a pirate.
Her specialty is shooting.
What she likes is also shooting.
What she knows how to do is also shooting.
This woman obsessed with pulling triggers has a weakness that others wouldn't even consider.

"Jigoku, aren't you swimming?"
"No. I hate water."
"...Didn't you say you were a pirate?"

Jigoku brazenly answered Hestia, who had just finished a water fight with Son O-cheon and wore an incredulous expression.

"Gyahahah! If you shoot and kill all visible enemies, you won't fall into water, right? No need to swim."
"...That's such a trashy answer it's almost amusing."

Of course, it was a lie.
It's true that if you shoot the enemy first, you don't need to swim, but that's because she doesn't want to swim, not because she can't swim.

'I'm disguised as a man, and if I get in the water, my figure will show. And swimming with clothes on feels unpleasant because it gets all wet.'

Even if she hadn't been disguised as a man, she wouldn't have wanted to engage in such meaningless swimming.

Swoosh, swoooosh

When someone crossed just half of the river, waves began to form, and by the time they reached three-quarters of the way, the current became so fierce that one might seriously wonder if they would drown.
It was cruel of the professor to prevent students from crossing the river by attaching automata—self-moving machines—to magical washbasins, but it was also amazing that Oknodie was determined to cross it.

"Why go through all that trouble trying to break through endless waves that will never stop, when I can't even shoot the professor's head? It'll only drain more energy as time goes on."

Wouldn't it be faster to learn magic to calm the river water rather than swimming?
While the female hero was seriously considering this, surprisingly, Oknodie popped her head out from the other side of the river.

"First to arrive!"

Surprised, Jigoku immediately ran to Oknodie.

"How did you do it? You failed several times before. The further you go, the more your strength drains—how did you get through?"
"The surface is like that, but below the surface, you actually gain momentum. The water circulates clockwise."
"That means you had to go all the way to the riverbed!"
"That's right."
"You held your breath from going down to the riverbed until riding the rapid current back up? Is that even possible?"

Oknodie answered cheerfully.

"It worked for me."
"...Aren't you afraid of drowning?"
"Is there any particular reason I should be afraid?"

This child—I'd heard rumors she received assassin training, but did she receive underwater training too?
Even as a pirate, Jigoku was appalled at how insane this feat was, yet the person who had just escaped a life-threatening situation seemed completely unaware of the danger.
Unless she truly did this without knowing anything, this must mean she had learned from experience that this level of activity wouldn't kill her.

'Considering her competence in various areas, this isn't a skill she patiently acquired over time.'

It could only be established through accelerated training where life was at stake, through practical experience.
How many children who entered such training survived, and how many died?
Just imagining it made her head spin.

"Jigoku? Is there something on my face?"

Having once experienced hellish life as a ship slave and even water torture, she understood well.
The terror and agony of being forced into water against one's will was beyond imagination.

"Ji~go~ku~?"
"...No. It's nothing."

But Oknodie, how can you stand there with such a bright face?
