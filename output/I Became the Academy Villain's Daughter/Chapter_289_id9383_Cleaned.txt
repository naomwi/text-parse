# Enhanced Translation: Chapter 289 - The Warmth of Keeping Promises

The formula for dark mana's power is simple.

Total amount of dark mana × Purity of dark mana = Dark mana control power

But like all academic disciplines, as one's grade level and knowledge in mana arts increase, previous common sense gets turned upside down, and new formulas appear out of nowhere.

Total amount of dark mana × Purity of dark mana × Mana control technique = Dark mana control power

High quantity, high purity, good control.
If you nail these three factors, you can gain the upper hand in the final result even if you're slightly lacking in quantity, purity, or control.
Going deeper, mana affinity, individual mental strength, maximum instantaneous output, physical durability, and so on also have an impact, but naturally, all my stats are maxed out.
This means it's impossible for even the Mesugakki princess with her high-purity and massive amounts of dark mana to control me.

Just look at my affinity.
My dark mana affinity is ridiculously high.

Although the princess's dark mana purity that she scraped together with money is impressive, it can't surpass a person's innate affinity or the mana cultivation techniques refined through veteran experience.
No matter how much quality mana a rich person obtains, they can't surpass a born genius's talent or a veteran's accumulated knowledge.
The difference in talent and affinity is that significant.
The princess doesn't realize this fact, so I was planning to use that difference to swallow up the dark organization she's been building in one go later...

'If I get caught here, the princess won't build the organization!'

She might even spit out all the dark mana she's collected like it's trash. That would be troublesome!

"Obey me♡"
"What will you give me if I obey?"

So I started acting.
I pretended to be under "conditional obedience," which happens when dark mana levels are equal.
While the princess was confused, Jude, who knows the characteristics of dark mana well, explained from the side.

"This is happening because your dark mana couldn't overwhelm Oknodie. It's conditional obedience, not complete submission. If you don't break her psychological mechanism or fulfill the conditions, you can't control a dark mana owner in that state."
"Ugh. How annoying. I never expected she would be on the same level as me with my high-purity dark mana."

The princess grumbled with a sulky face, then fell into deep thought.

"What will you give me if I obey?"

When I urged her from the side, a belated realization dawned in her eyes.

"Right. As long as you listen to whatever I say, that's all that matters~! Just tell me. I can do anything for you♡"
"Then get me a hundred unique-grade dishes!"

The princess couldn't readily answer.

"...Unique grade isn't something just anyone can receive."
"Didn't you say you wanted my obedience?"
"Ugh. Do you know? To make a dish unique enough to be called one-of-a-kind in the world requires incredibly original recipes or skills, and a story to match? A soup lovingly made by a mother for her sick child doesn't even come close?"

Yes, I know that.
Even dishes with the subtle flavors of Jonna and Leape, or ones that make your tongue tingle, occasionally numb your senses, or give you stomachaches weren't unique grade.
Certainly, unique dishes are difficult to obtain even for a veteran like me.

"That's exactly why I'm boldly asking you to get them for me because they're so precious?"

You want my obedience, right?
It would be troublesome if you can't do this much!
When I confidently presented my condition, the princess looked hesitant.

"I'll give them to you later, just obey me first♡"
"No!"
"Ugh, I said obey me~!"
"I said no!"
"Should I hit you!?"
"Go ahead! Am I just going to stand here and take it?"
"Use honorifics with royalty!"
"You first!"
"Is this a children's fight...?"

Jude muttered with an exasperated face.
At those words, I cleared my throat, remembering adult dignity, and regained my good child attitude.

"It won't do any good no matter how much you try! Payment is upfront!"
"Hmph! I don't need the obedience of a naughty child like you!"
"Okay, that ends it. I was more mature, right? The princess is less mature than someone younger than her, right?"
"...I'm really going to hit you!"

Thoroughly angered, the Mesugakki princess tore off every flower petal she could grab and threw them like steel darts.
As I ran away, barely avoiding them and sticking out my tongue to tease her, Jude sighed.

"What are you trying to accomplish by fighting among yourselves? Is the hero the only one doing good deeds?"

That would be troublesome too. With our interests aligned, we quietly attended the makeshift auction house where the noon auction was being held.

"You're late."
"Sorry! I was playing with the princess."

Zuang, who had been hiding somewhere to avoid the hero, crawled out from under the shadow of a boulder in the open space a bit late.
Seeing the hero click his tongue, it seemed he was fully intending to disqualify Zuang if he found her alone.
Day 5 of our chaotic deserted island life.
It's pretty fun so far!


* * *


Mesugakki was honestly a bit intimidated.
Oknodie, who wouldn't obey.
If their powers were equal, she would increase her dark mana.
Even though she had raised her energy first with that intention, Oknodie didn't budge at all.
Stop! Sit! Wait!
She tried sneaking in commands during their squabble, but Oknodie didn't listen to any of them.
Though her attitude was light and playful, it actually implied something terribly frightening.
Oknodie was hiding her power.
Perhaps to such an extent that if she sincerely "commanded" her, she would have no choice but to follow unilaterally.

Was she going easy on her?
Or was she hiding it because she thought she would soon be caught up to?

Either way, Oknodie had now become a subject of caution, no less than the hero.

"I will now reveal the fifth item of the deserted island auction. Today's item is the 'One-time Assassin Detection Service.'"

This damn auction always offers tempting items even when you try to boycott it.
The princess could see that not only herself but even the hero and the saintess were hesitating.
The Foundation with eyes and ears everywhere.
With their capabilities, they could certainly detect assassins who might threaten them in advance.
For someone whose life is targeted over the imperial succession rights, this opportunity was too good to miss.

The Imperial Crown Prince.
The Mesugakki princess's older brother.
The heir to the throne who would be desperate to kill her even at this moment.
He wouldn't leave her alone as she threatened his position.
The same goes for the hero.
The continent's great evils, the Foundation, and the Demon King wouldn't leave alone those who threaten their existence.
The dispatch of assassins was an inevitable fate.
Being able to detect this in advance, even if only once, was a strong merit.
Assassins are frightening because they approach from places you don't perceive, but if you know and prepare, there are plenty of ways and possibilities to block them.

"If there are no bidders within one minute, today's deserted island auction will be canceled."
"Hold back, Yupi."
"But still..."
"If it's a crisis you can only overcome with the Foundation's help, then such a hero is doomed from the start. The moment you try to rely on someone else's power, it's already over."
"Hmph. You're finally saying something hero-like for once."
"Are you saying I don't usually seem like a hero?"
"You seemed more like a rowdy thug."
"Ugh. This is what happens when your childhood friend gets too comfortable."

The hero held back.
Because he was confident in his abilities.
He might be a mess of a hero who doesn't care about reputation management, but he couldn't ignore his pride in his skills.
Ishtar was right.
If a crisis comes that he can't withstand without the Foundation's help, the problem was that his preparation was insufficient from the beginning.
By being indebted to the Foundation in such a way, he would only gain new weaknesses.

'I'll hold back.'

Just when everyone was refusing to bid, someone unexpected raised their hand.

"Me!"
"...Oknodie. 30,000 points."

Oknodie, who had never stepped forward in any auction until now, voluntarily raised her hand.
Undeniable agitation appeared on both the hero's and the princess's faces.
Oknodie was fundamentally strong.
Not as much as the hero, but her various talents could threaten even him.
And she had purchased the assassin detection service.
Why?
For what purpose?
With the Foundation's power, they would normally provide such services to her anyway, wouldn't they?

In the midst of shock, a sudden realization dawned.
It was the Foundation's special nature.
Under other organizations, other families, general common sense, this would be incomprehensible.
A child receives help from their family.
A member receives help from their organization.
It's an extremely natural and sensible thing.
But when it comes to the Wiheomhae Foundation, the story is completely different.

'How could there be any common sense in an organization that threatens young children by showing them they could become prostitutes if they don't follow their education?'

She had heard rumors that came from none other than Oknodie's own mouth.
If even the princess, who had only indirectly gathered rumors, knew of such vicious stories, how much worse must the stories be that those close to Oknodie, and Oknodie herself, had seen, heard, and experienced?

"60,000."
"Zuang. 60,000 points."
"Ah, Zuang! You can't do that! I can't give this up."
"I'm by your side, aren't I? Aren't you satisfied with my help?"
"It's far from enough!"

Zuang's action of raising the points and Oknodie's attempt to stop her even stimulated the tear ducts.
A child who receives such threats that she can't feel safe even with the protection of Zuang, an active assassin whose stealth skills could evade even the hero if she wanted.
While Zuang, unable to interfere with Oknodie, kept her mouth shut, Oknodie bid again.

They knew it would be troublesome to take it away from her.
But not now.
Not this time.
Isn't this too pitiful, even for an enemy!
The hero and princess exchanged a wordless understanding.
Let's give up on this auction.

"The fifth auction item, 'One-time Assassin Detection Service,' has been sold to participant Oknodie for 90,000 points."

As the dignified auction where a minimum line was maintained even in a competitive relationship ended, the princess sincerely applauded.

"Congratulations. For getting an item you really needed."
"Don't think of us as too bad. We may consider you an enemy we can't coexist with, but at least the hero's party has the belief of eliminating evil with our own hands."
"Yupi is right. Don't get killed by some random assassin before we can kill you ourselves."

In the warm atmosphere, Oknodie, seemingly touched, turned her head away with an expression like she was about to cry.
