# 149 - The Visitor Interference

"I heard Oknodie is sitting in the visitor's room."
"Why hasn't her guardian come?"
"She's from the Wiheomhae Foundation. They obviously won't come because they'd get beaten up if people found out what they did to Oknodie."

As Zuang passed through the hallway, she thought the girl was quite foolish based on the rumors she overheard.

'How could an assassin meet someone face-to-face? Even if they say they'll come, they won't. And even if you ask them to come, they shouldn't.'

It was an excessively reckless act, even considering Oknodie was still a child.
Today's experience would surely teach her a lesson.
She would learn not to put meaning into empty promises that only cause trouble for both parties, and she wouldn't make such promises herself.
That's how everyone grows stronger.
While Zuang liked Oknodie's bright innocence, she prayed that the period where her innocence caused her pain would end as quickly as possible.
She didn't want to see her newly made colleague and friend suffering emotional distress.

Thud.

"...!"

Maybe she should at least see her face before leaving.
With that thought, she stepped into the hallway where an unusual Boundary expanded.
A unique technique of the powerful who twist internal laws to create their own small world.
A freshman? Not such a low-level power.
A teaching assistant? Second or third-year students wouldn't be capable.
An instructor? Even that would be insufficient.
A professor.
This Boundary was deployed by someone at least at the professor level.

'Whatever it is, I've walked into somewhere I shouldn't be.'

The power emanating from the Boundary caused cognitive distortion and sensory disruption.
The result would be expulsion beyond the hallway.

"Huh? Where was I trying to go?"

A student who happened to be caught in the Boundary wandered away with a blank expression.
Watching him climb to the upper floor, it seemed he would think he just got lost for a moment if he reached his destination, with no awareness he had entered a Boundary.

<Advanced Stealth>
<Boundary Deception - Complete Harmony>

Thanks to her quick perception, she narrowly avoided the Boundary's biological recognition and concealed her presence.
Moving carefully to avoid triggering the Boundary, Zuang detected another layer of Boundary deployed in double.
Unlike the Boundary that expelled others, beyond this double Boundary line was a Boundary of different properties.
She plucked a small thread.
Whew.
When she gently blew the thread from her palm across the Boundary line, it dropped straight down.

'The gravity is different.'

An enormous pressure dominated that space.
Now she understood the meaning of the double Boundary.
The outer Boundary was a safety measure to prevent innocent students from getting involved.
The inner Boundary was in combat mode to achieve its main purpose.
Inside, someone was fighting a professor.

'Who could it be? Who's trying to stop whom?'

Before entering the Academy, she wouldn't have been curious about such matters.
Relationships with others inevitably led to betrayal, pain, disappointment, detachment, and cold revenge.
She had never expected anything from any relationship.
She never wanted to form any relationships.
But things changed after meeting Oknodie.
There was a child in this world like herself.
A child born with the same fate of an assassin, yet living a completely different life and trying to live differently.
As much as that child resembled her, perhaps she could come to resemble that child too.

She had developed such foolish expectations.
She knew it.
That her current self was weaker than her former self.
The knowledge, skills, and relationships she gained at the Academy.
Such things were nothing compared to an assassin's mindset.

Don't step into danger.
Live only for yourself.
Don't get involved in personal grudges.

The numerous pieces of advice left by her master bound her feet.
But one suspicion wouldn't let her turn away.

What if the fight beyond was related to Oknodie?
What if it was related to Oknodie's guardian?
Could she still walk away saying it had nothing to do with her?

Crack.

Zuang heard the sound of something breaking.
It was the sound of her master's shackles, which had bound her feet for so long, beginning to crack.

'Oknodie is not a stranger to me.'

Hadn't she always acted superior to Hestia and Titoso?
Saying assassins don't make friends carelessly?
That those who betray friends should be prepared to receive the pain of betrayal in return?

'I shouldn't be doing this. I really shouldn't.'

Even while thinking how troublesome this was, she took a step forward.
Pressure bearing down as if it would snap her neck.
Thighs straining with force.
Stubbornly trying to keep her back from bending.
More sincere than she had ever been, even during the entrance exam, the hidden scene within the double Boundary finally unfolded before her eyes.

A long-haired man standing proudly with his back turned, arms crossed, his cape fluttering.
Another man in a tailcoat, covered in blood, taking steps forward while enduring the first man's pressure.
If she had to identify them...

'A thief... and a butler?'

Two men of an incomprehensible combination were glaring at each other.


* * *


Jonna Wiheomhae.
He had been counting the days until this one arrived.
The Guardian Visitation Day.

"My Lady has caused quite a lot of trouble. Please give her a stern talking-to on my behalf as well."
"I'm sorry, Leape. I'll convey your message to the Lady too."
"It's enough if you properly deliver the candy basket. She's probably developed some tolerance by now, so it's time to give her different types of poisoned candies I've carefully selected."

Though disappointed at not being able to see her in person, Leape understood it couldn't be helped.
The Academy investigators' attention toward the Wiheomhae Foundation had been unsettlingly intense lately.
With news that an artifact had been stolen from one of the central continent headquarters' key regional bases, they couldn't ignore the Academy's intentions.

"To break through the Academy's interference in trying to take the Lady from the Foundation's embrace, we need a diversion. Please leave this to our assassination maid unit."

Behind Leape, professional assassins disguised as maids revealed themselves.
As soon as they dispersed, explosions erupted throughout the banquet hall, thick smoke spread, and emergency alerts rained down.
More than half would likely not survive, but that too was an investment from the higher-ups for the Foundation's "special scholarship student."

'Live, Leape. I don't want to see the Lady saddened by your death.'

Jonna and Leape.
They hadn't wanted to resort to such measures from the beginning.
But when the "weeding out of minor guardians" disguised as a pre-visitation banquet began, they had no choice but to act.

"Everyone knows that each visitation ticket allows a set amount of time. Wouldn't it be a shame to part so quickly from the children who are eagerly waiting for their guardians' visits?"

Due to the scheme of one malicious individual, a betting game involving visitation tickets began in the banquet hall.
While outwardly presented as betting, it was actually a ploy to extort tickets from powerless guardians.
Even Jonna might have struggled to protect his ticket if targeted by the powerful.

'You brought this terrorism upon yourselves.'

Due to the assassination maids' bombing operation, the scheduled banquet was canceled, and ticket holders were rushed to use the teleportation magic circle.
With influential figures from various countries gathered for student visitations, it was impossible to postpone the schedule or make the procedures more complicated.

"We will expedite the move to Gift Island. Those with visitation tickets, please gather quickly at the teleportation magic circle."

Taking advantage of the chaos, Jonna naturally arrived at Gift Island and felt his body trembling from the immense mana permeating the island.
Though it had been a long time since he returned, the island remained monstrously unchanged.

"As expected of an island with dragon veins, the mana energy is truly magnificent. Don't you agree?"
"Haha. It was worth investing a large sum for admission. If our child grows in a place like this, it's not impossible for our family to produce its first Sword Master."

The country's top merchant.
The head of a martial family expanding its influence.
High nobles and imperial high officials—prestigious guardians visited the Academy.
Prepared identities.
As soon as they were verified through the mana board, movement to the visitation building was permitted one by one.

'I'll have to wait a bit longer.'

Because priority was assigned to those with power and influence, Jonna had to wait a considerable amount of time.
Still, he showed no complaints or loss of dignity.
His every action could affect the reputation of the Wiheomhae Foundation and his Lady.

"......"

Eventually, all other guardians had left, and Jonna remained last in the inspection room.
The reward for his patience was only cold treatment.

"Since there's no one else, could you please proceed with the inspection procedure?"
"I apologize. Due to a malfunction of the mana board, inspection is currently impossible."
"The board looks perfectly fine to me."

As the staff member applied pressure with a smiling face, the mana board's circuit burned with a crackling sound.

"Now it's broken."
"......"
"Don't worry. According to regulations, all inspections must be completed by 6 PM at the latest. We will definitely repair the board by then somehow."

What a childish trick.
Though murderous intent flashed in Jonna's fierce eyes, the staff member smiled with his mouth while his eyes challenged Jonna to try something if he dared.
That was probably exactly what they wanted.
Unlike Leape, Jonna had revealed his identity.
If he caused trouble, he could be immediately expelled.
If he wanted to meet Oknodie, he couldn't dirty his hands.
No matter how dirty and petty the other side was.

"..."

By early evening, as the sun was setting.

"Identity confirmed. Enjoy your visit."

Leaving behind the staff member's shameless mockery, Jonna entered the hallway.
He couldn't help but laugh bitterly at the changing atmosphere as soon as he set foot inside.
This is really too much.
They even deployed a Boundary that increased pain with each step.

"Nice to meet you, Oknodie's guardian."
"And who might you be?"
"I'm a professor who acts as Oknodie's teacher. People call me the Destroyer."
"The Destroyer... the thief from the previous generation's hero party?"
"I'm sorry, but you should turn back now. This hallway has been sealed with a barrier for special reasons. No one can use it until the visitation time ends."
"According to regulations, those with visitation tickets can have an immediate visit once the guardian's identity is confirmed."
"What can we do about an unfortunate accident? Luckily, it happened after all the other guardians had passed through."

After experiencing such shameless behavior bordering on contempt, Jonna felt more like laughing than getting angry.

"This won't make my Lady yours."
"Isn't this what you people do well? Recruiting talented children as disciples, then threatening them using their lives as hostages."

The Destroyer said:

"Today, our roles are simply reversed."

A professor in charge of the "Retired Former Hero's Adventure Tales" lecture.
A companion of the hero chosen by the main god, "Sophemia of the Sun."
A member of the previous generation's hero party, the Destroyer.
From the center of the double Boundary spread across the middle of the hallway, he blocked Jonna's path.

"Turn back if you want to preserve that worthless life of yours."

Without hesitation, Jonna stepped forward.
