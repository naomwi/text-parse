# Chapter 164: Why Is the Professor Here?

Each chapter boss has their own concept.

Appearance - 981st Entrance Exam, Group A Testing Area
Event Period - 1st Year Midterm Exams
Description - Hestia, owner of the despised Berserker class. When she realizes her mental sanctuary, whom she considered a friend, is actually a "Responding Door" mimicking a human, her rage turns toward her classmates.
Feature 1 - Attack power increases as HP decreases.
Feature 2 - Immune to all mental status effects due to loss of sanity.
Feature 3 - Rapidly removes physical status effects due to abnormal blood flow rate.

<Chapter 3: Queen of Pets, Camela>
Appearance - 981st Entrance Exam, Group B Testing Area
Event Period - 2nd Year Opening Ceremony
Description - An emotion manipulator who toys with people's hearts using magical power. Having steadily increased her pets as she advanced through the years, she reveals her ambition to dominate the school year at the opening ceremony.
Feature 1 - Students captivated by Camela become enemies.
Feature 2 - Students who sign pet contracts must respond to their master's requests for help until they fulfill their designated contribution.
Feature 3 - When a master bestows grace upon a pet, the pet's required contribution increases further.
Feature 4 - All masters who use Camela's pet contracts are Camela's pets.

<Chapter 4: Silent Death, Forest of Silence>
Appearance - After the 981st Entrance Ceremony
Event Period - 2nd Year Summer Vacation
Description - A terrible man-made disaster formed from accumulated curse waste. The moment you lose senses you took for granted, you realize the curses abandoned by humans have returned.
Feature 1 - Sound does not exist within the forest's domain.
Feature 2 - The forest seeks out and devours all living creatures that make sound.
Feature 3 - New curses are added each time the forest's growth level increases.

Even excluding Chapter 2's boss that changes every playthrough, each chapter boss has vastly different gimmicks.

Hestia, the Frenzied Slaughterer, a close-combat DPS-type chapter boss who ignores all status effects and slaughters students indiscriminately.

Camela, Master of Masters, a group-control type chapter boss who manipulates numerous students like limbs and reveals her ambition to dominate the 981st class.

The Forest of Silence and Death, a death-field type chapter boss that will cause enormous casualties if not quickly found and eliminated.

The danger level naturally increases as you progress.
Chapter 1: Just need to deal with one Hestia.
Chapter 2: Unpredictable variables that threaten even veteran players.
Chapter 3: Must break through interference from multiple students.
Chapter 4: The entire field becomes the enemy.
What's more, these bosses grow just like players do.
If you think "I'll just enjoy academy life and my harem!" you're setting yourself up for sudden death from chapter bosses that have grown uncontrollably powerful.

'Some newbie on V-Tube went on a picnic with their partner and got devoured by the Forest of Silence, right?'

The unfairness of chapter bosses is that they can strike at completely unexpected times.
After that video spread, newbies never again carelessly went on forest picnics or ventured deep into the woods to collect rare items for points.
While the Forest of Silence won't come running if you don't make loud noises, if it happens to be near your path, being devoured is inevitable.

Children have remarkable energy.
They run around expanding their own worlds without rest, covering distances that adults would find too bothersome to travel.
Most land has owners, and that purity disappears when one becomes an adult, enslaved by labor just to secure a piece of land to lie on.

'It's been a while since I've seen such purity.'

Oknodie, who knows no fear.
A peculiar child possessing both childlike purity and a hero's boldness.

Bags as large as Oknodie's that had been dragged along the ground.

These are what people call "curse waste."
