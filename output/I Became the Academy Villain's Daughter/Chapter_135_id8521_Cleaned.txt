# 135 - Aptitude Assessment Hat

The professor of the common lecture for the lower class.
Professor Van Snake has one philosophy.

"For inferior students, the greatest blessing they can enjoy is to be expelled from this cursed Academy as quickly as possible."

Making sure that inferior students end their miserable Academy life in their first year, before they struggle by forcibly advancing to higher grades!
Of course, he understands that students have their own circumstances.
Some want to become the strongest in one field.
Some want to live up to their family's expectations.
Some want to become heroes of small nations.
Some want to find excellent marriage prospects.
Some want to improve their inferior class.
Some want to change perceptions of their despised race.
Those who enter the Academy for different reasons each carry their own dreams, missions, and goals.
But if their abilities are lacking.
If they suddenly become second-year students anyway.

'Absolutely not. I can't witness such a tragedy again!'

They'll experience something they can't even dream of in their first year—or if they do, it will become a terrible nightmare.
As someone who once served as the second-year student president, Professor Snake sincerely decided that he must take special measures for the students' sake.
As a result, becoming a professor of mandatory common lectures for first-year lower class students, he gained the position and power to achieve his goal.

"Understand, assistants? We must do our utmost to expel as many students as possible from this practical exercise. For the sake of these fragile first-years!!"

At Professor Snake's passionate cry, the assistants' faces darkened, thinking, "This psycho is at it again."

"The entrance exam is over, but the true test for surviving the Academy begins now. Handling mana. And the exam that verifies this!"

Mana is a super-elemental substance that helps humans transcend the limits of their species.
Those who can handle mana can become superhuman.
Elemental mana.
Natural mana.
Divine mana.
Trait mana.
Students who can handle any type of mana earn the qualification to approach world-class status.
And once again, talent must be filtered.

Those who can become stronger quickly.
And those who cannot.

The process of separating superior and inferior students awaits.
That is the Mana Verification Exam.
An exam where first-year lower class students drop out in droves.

"The exam is simple. If you can handle mana, you can easily pass, but if not, you'll face a hurdle that requires training and talent beyond common sense to overcome."

For example, knight department aspirants must cross a bridge blocked by a huge boulder.
Magic department aspirants must defeat a training dummy with extremely high physical resistance.
Administration department aspirants must receive citizens' petitions and answer how to solve their problems.
Production department aspirants must perfectly scan and analyze the structure of specific objects.
Adventure department aspirants must find a hidden certificate of acceptance somewhere on campus.

Five different exams.
Students get the chance to attempt two of them.
Because there might be a gap between their aptitude and what they want to do.
It's giving them the maximum opportunity.
If they pass at least one, the student passes.
If they fail both, they suffer a devastating score drop.
Students disagree and say Professor Snake's exam is the most painful, but.

"It doesn't matter."

Anyway, if they can't even pass this much, staying at the Academy by force would be even more painful.

"Three days remain. The exam will proceed this Friday. Everyone prepare thoroughly to expel as many lower class juniors as possible."

The assistants bowed their heads, feeling like minions of an evil villain.

The ever-stylish Professor Bronze waved at the students, receiving their admiring gazes as usual.
But today, he was carrying an unfamiliar briefcase in his hand.
Perhaps it contained materials for today's lecture?

"While I don't particularly consider you cherished disciples I've carefully nurtured, I will conduct a special lecture today to prevent students taking my lecture from experiencing the disgraceful situation of being expelled due to insufficient scores."

At those words, the lonely eastern swordsman Xing raised his hand.

"This is rare. A question from first-year Xing."

It was the first time seeing such enthusiasm from Xing, who usually sits in the corner of the classroom making sounds like "Tsk," "Tch," "Hmph," while achieving excellent grades before leaving the classroom—a loner outsider style. Despite receiving everyone's interested gazes, Xing spoke without being intimidated.

"There's no benefit for upper class students in this lecture. Can't we proceed with the regular lecture and then provide this special lecture separately to those who want it later?"

The expressions of the lower class students soured.
He was saying something quite offensive, but since Xing was an upper class student, it was natural that he wouldn't find much appeal in such special lectures.
Even if he behaves rudely, he has the skills to cut down any student who might criticize him.

"Don't worry. I've prepared a lecture that won't be disadvantageous for upper class students to hear as well."

The professor placed the briefcase on the podium and clicked open the handle.
A white vapor escaped with a hissing sound.
What appeared beyond it was a bizarrely shaped hat with two palms attached.

'Ah! That's the hat used when advancing to second year!'

Just as a certain Western magic academy has a magic hat that evaluates students' aptitudes, Gift Academy also has a hat that evaluates department aptitudes.
It was the Aptitude Assessment Hat with <Talent Identification>, <Aptitude Evaluation>, and <Status Window Viewing> functions.

"Today's special lecture is about finding out which department you first-years have an aptitude for. And I'll give bonus points to students who correctly guess their aptitude. In the sense that they have an eye for knowing themselves."

There's no guarantee that the field you currently aim for, the field where you currently have skills, and the field where you actually have aptitude will all match.
If you're lucky enough to have all three overlap, you can achieve an amazing synergistic effect like doing what you love.
But what if all three are completely separate?
You'll waste effort, your dreams will be in vain, and your work will be disconnected—a total disaster.
So being able to wear the <Aptitude Assessment Hat> even a year earlier was truly Professor Bronze being generous.

"Inferior humans... come sit before me and reveal those worthless talents of yours..."

But the hat's tone seems somewhat different from usual.
It wasn't such an unpleasant character in the game.
The hands attached to the hat weren't so grotesquely shaped either, and the voice had a much more comical and harmless tone.
Can hats also have different personalities with a 0.1% probability?
It's a bit disconcerting.
