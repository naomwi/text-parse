# 89 - The Thief

After escaping from the chaotic garden, April entered a nearby empty room and finished writing her report.
She doubted whether the higher-ups would want to receive such a report, but then remembered that if she had been caught at the scene, her life would have been ruined.
Suddenly, a surge of resentment welled up against the foundation's upper management who sat safely while enjoying the benefits and assigning difficult tasks.

'If they don't want this kind of report, what are they going to do about it? If they're so upset, they should try being field spies themselves.'

In the morning, cleaning with dog-humanoids who bark "woof woof," at noon, cleaning with bird-humanoids who chirp "tweet tweet," and in the evening, cleaning the stables with horse-humanoids who whimper "neigh neigh."
While enduring the harsh cleaning schedule of a cleaning maid and conducting spy activities, she risked being discovered and having her life ruined.
After barely overcoming all those hardships, all she got in return was a small amount of gold coins and the harsh daily life of a cleaning maid.

'Money was only good when I first received it. When you're forced to continue a job where you might die before retirement, even getting paid doesn't feel good anymore.'

Considering the effort required to continue spy activities, the upper management should accept this level of information accuracy error.

'I should head back now.'

On her way back to the stable.

'Oh right. The tail.'

April took out the cat tail from her handbag.
Having been accepted as an Academy cleaning maid by aiming for humanoid quota points, she was publicly recognized as a "cat-humanoid" and needed to show her tail.
Usually, she would make excuses about hiding her tail inside her skirt or pants while keeping it in her handbag, but when visible to others, she had to properly wear the tail.
Of course, transformation magic was difficult.
And it would be a waste of magical power to maintain it.
So, considering cost-effectiveness, this cat tail flag was a spy disguise item made by the foundation.

"Sigh."
'It feels weird on my butt when I wear it for too long.'

Despite grumbling internally, April diligently attached the cat tail flag.

"This is awesome. I got the best pattern!"

After summer vacation ended.
When the first year's second semester begins, a new chapter boss and new crisis appear at the Academy.
Usually, three crises compete fiercely to determine which will become the real chapter boss, and one of those three candidates was this mandrake.

"Waa."

In a jar filled with culture medium, the baby mandrake splashed around with its root-like hands.
Though cute now, once it grows, it will spread its roots throughout the Academy garden and become a Gigantamax Mandrake that rises up by destroying the student council building.

That thing must be about 230m tall, right?

Personally, I'm a bit envious.
The cause is the special culture medium X-13 developed by the chemistry club.
During midterm exams in the first semester, seniors with approaching project deadlines raid student council clubs to supplement lacking reagents or development products.
If the chemistry club gets raided with a certain probability, an event follows where they carelessly bury unused drugs in the ground.
Here, the first plant to extend its roots to the problematic special culture medium X-13 experiences a chaotic event.

'Gigantamax plant event!'

What if a spore plant reaches it first?
The spore plant that spreads seeds with the wind will scatter giant spores throughout the Academy, starting the "Giant Spore Plant Colony" event.
What if a tree reaches it first?
The "Jack and the Beanstalk" event begins with a tree that grows endlessly toward the sky.
But what if a mandrake reaches it first?

'Worst case scenario, Academy devastation, unfair destruction, straight to doom!'

When the Gigantamax Mandrake, born from coincidence upon coincidence without anyone's malice, screams, thousands of students die.
Professor Weird, who looks naturally harmonious with plants wrapped around his body, can safely resolve the situation by demonstrating the true value of dryads.
But what if bad luck strikes in the 600 to 300 to 99 to 1 rule?

-An accident happened in a club supervised by some nobody from the frontier. We can't trust that person's abilities, so we'll leave this matter to our Imperial professors.

The Imperial professors step forward to handle it themselves.
It's fine if they take care of the aftermath.
The Empire has competent professors too.
The power of frontier-born professors weakens, Imperial professors become more arrogant, discrimination worsens, students suffer, and numerous conflict flags are strengthened.
But at least we won't die immediately, right?

...But what if bad luck strikes again?
What if an incompetent Imperial professor gets involved?

A small mistake carelessly causes dozens or hundreds of deaths or several professors die.
Government officials and secret organizations from around the world, desperate to insert new assassins into the Academy, drool over this tempting situation.
Students attending lectures keep disappearing, some students experience tingling in their lower abdomen and morning sickness, and the Academy atmosphere becomes a bit strange.
But it's still okay.
At least we won't die immediately.

...But what if a professor planted by an organization aiming for world destruction takes advantage of this opportunity?

The Academy explodes that day.
There's a 0.1% chance everything is ruined.

It's been long verified that X-1 doesn't cause rapid growth when consumed by a mandrake!

"Still, I hope it grows quickly!"

When grown in soil, mandrakes scream when pulled out but become valuable elixirs.
On the other hand, when grown in water, they don't kill people and grow docilely, but lose their elixir effect.
Instead, the water in which the mandrake grows becomes an elixir.

I've got a plant friend to keep in the dormitory.

The mandrake was missing.
Orchcatch, the head of the Plant Club, felt the blood vessels in the back of his neck constrict and his blood boil.
Unless it got up and moved away on its own, an intruder must have stolen it.

"Club president. No plants were stolen from inside the greenhouse."
"It seems only the plants in your private security booth were stolen, president."
"What rare thing were you growing that thieves came even before early evening?"
"You don't need to know."

The mandrake was a super rare plant, top-tier even among the rare plants cultivated by the Plant Club.
He was certain that if the fact that he was growing a mandrake became known, club members would be the first to rush to the security booth with planting trowels.

"Tell me honestly. Did you steal it?"
"How could we steal something when we don't even know what's inside?"

Though his club members weren't trustworthy, none were this brazen.

'There are two traces.'

One set of footprints from someone keeping watch at the entrance.
Another set from someone who infiltrated the scene and committed the crime.
The strange thing was that both sets of footprints were peculiarly shaped, making it impossible to determine the shoe size.

"President. The Alchemy Club guys look suspicious."
"That's right. They're bad people who take fertilizer production requests but don't provide the special culture medium on time."
"Oh right. Wasn't today the day they were supposed to give us the culture medium?"
"They said they gave it to us?"
"I looked but couldn't find it."
"...These guys must have claimed they gave us culture medium they never made after hearing about the theft!"

The club members, grumbling among themselves, stormed off to the Alchemy Club.
Orchcatch, alone in his dejection over losing the mandrake, stood in the garden staring up at the sky.

'This isn't the work of a common club thief.'

Orchcatch went to the personal research lab of Professor Bronze, renowned as the world's best when it comes to theft.

"Excuse me. Are you in, Professor?"
"Come in."

Following the calm voice from inside, he opened the door.
Was she thinking of sleeping since it was early evening?
The professor was lying on the sofa.
However, even sleeping on a sofa looked picturesque for a stylish beauty.
With her white suit jacket hanging on the chair back, she reclined in her dress shirt and suit pants.
Not only was her pose chic as she lifted her silk hat covering her face with one finger, but her chest, fully visible through the dress shirt, caught his eye.

"You're not a student attending my lectures."

At those words meant to redirect his attention, Orchcatch belatedly shifted his gaze from her chest to her face.
With the professor's skill level, she must have read his gaze.
It was already gracious of her not to reprimand him.

"I came for consultation."
"I'll hear you out and consider."
"Just now, an ultra-rare plant I was growing in the deepest part of the Plant Club was stolen."
"Security?"
"I used Bowing Grass, Vine Traps, Vampiric Parasitic Vines, and a mana board lock. The thief avoided the first three and smashed the last one with a rock."
"...With a rock?"
"It was quite a large boulder. It must be an upperclassman who knew the location of my favorite plant, infiltrated the scene secretly, and had the boldness to break the lock with a boulder."
"An upperclassman."
"I also think it's a habitual thief. Otherwise, they couldn't have escaped within the 20 seconds it took for club members to arrive at the scene."

Professor Bronze smiled meaningfully.
After initially listening and agreeing, Professor Bronze suddenly fell silent.
There was no response.
Was she not interested?
The longer the silence stretched, the more anxious he became.

"I'll look into it separately."
"Phew."

Professor Bronze gave him a silent cue to leave.

"Thank you. Um, about the stolen item, please..."

The silence lengthened.
It was a more ominous silence than before.
Seeing the smile forming on the professor's lips, he could guess her intentions.
Of course.
A thief wouldn't simply hand over stolen goods when found.

"Professor, you're a noble thief, aren't you?"

Trying to appeal to her conscience, he uttered one sentence.
The professor countered coolly.

"You were looking at my chest, weren't you?"

With a bright red face, Orchcatch shouted "I apologize!" and fled.
It was too overwhelming for a virgin male student to endure.
Instead of watching Orchcatch flee down the dark corridor, Professor Bronze's eyes shone brightly as she recalled the face of a certain child.

"Hmph."

Making Oknodie her personal disciple.
The opportunity to achieve that goal came sooner than expected.
