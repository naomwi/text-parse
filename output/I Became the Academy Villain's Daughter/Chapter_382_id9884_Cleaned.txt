# 382 - The Sound of Shoes

Points become increasingly important as you advance through the years.
Dorothy's ploy was certainly threatening.
That is, if Tetrapos had been an ordinary third-year student struggling with insufficient points for promotion.

*Rustle.*

"Huh?"
"What a shame. Your clever strategy didn't work."

This couldn't be right.
Academy students were supposed to be slaves to points.

*Thud thud thud.*

The signs wrapped around Dorothy's body fell off.
More painful than the cuts on her body from the threads was the opponent's unexpected response that didn't follow her predictions.

"In this Academy, those with real skill are always showered with points regardless of good or evil. My situation is different from the point-beggars you've seen."
"Ugh, this is cheating. Being so strong without any weaknesses means we can't win!"
"Haha. If strength is cowardice, then is weakness justice? What a convenient excuse. A logic that always justifies your own weakness!"

The Tetrapos senior, who had been laughing loudly, sensed something strange.
Despite her whining, Dorothy's eyes showed no sign of fear.

"Oh? Are you stalling for time?"
"Of course not. I'm just a forest keeper!"
"Then what are those signs reattaching themselves to your body?"

Dorothy stopped her cautious expression and grinned.

"I got such a nice shield, it would be a waste to use it just once, right? So I cast a location-specific restoration spell. These signs will restore themselves as many times as needed!"

She had created armor by utilizing the self-restoring property of the signs?
Tetrapos acknowledged his junior's impressive handiness.

"Your talent won't end as an ordinary first-year. What's your name?"
"Apprentice Forest Keeper Dorothy!"
"Yes, Forest Keeper Dorothy. Even if I defeat you today, I'll remember that name. Someday you'll realize it was worth making your name known to me."
"What do you do, senior?"
"976th class, Production Department, 3rd year Tetrapos. If you make it to second year and enter the Production Department, you'll certainly hear my name."

What an incredibly confident senior.
Well, being a third-year would make anyone confident.
Surviving to third year in this Academy was impressive enough.

"Hmph. Still, you can't leave here, senior. I won't fall easily like the others, and I'll keep interfering with you."
"Try if you can."

<Cruel Blood Manipulation>
<Blood Thread>

The Blood Thread that had sent Prince Hector of Troy flying with a single blow now targeted Dorothy.
Unlike before when everyone had joined forces to create a mana shield and physically supported Hector, Dorothy had no companions to help reduce the damage!
Those signs would surely be swept away in an instant, leaving her bloodied and rolling on the ground.
That's how it appeared to Jaku, who was just standing there.

"It's too much. Run away."
"Too late. Pay the price for your recklessness, junior."

The overlapping threads formed a thick blood-colored line that engulfed Dorothy.
Signs fell off with a single blow, and Dorothy rolled on the ground.
Unlike Jaku's dismay, the senior Tetrapos's face hardened.

"This is... a wooden doll? A doppelganger disguised as a human!"
"It's the <Doppelganger Creation> function that unlocks when you raise <Decoration> to 200."

A Dorothy wrapped in signs walked out beside the fallen Dorothy.

"It's a technique I developed making dolls whenever I encountered enemies who would kill me in the forest. Pretty convincing, right?"

Behind that Dorothy, another Dorothy appeared, followed by yet another.
Because of the ridiculous appearance of wearing signs like armor, no one had noticed that the bizarre outfit covered all the joints of the body.
Thanks to this, even with awkward movement, there was no sense of discomfort.
They had been completely fooled by the sign armor, which was designed from the start to deploy doppelgangers.

[Doppelganger Creation][Decoration][Mana Infusion][Remote Control]

Looking at the exposed parts where signs had fallen off the doppelganger, he became even more certain.
Several magical formulas were densely engraved on the wooden boards that replaced flesh.

"Where did a first-year learn such techniques?"
"I learned from watching Oknodie's paper airplanes! And mastered it while copying her notes!"
"There you are!"

Following the sound, threads flew and shattered the wooden doll hiding in the shadows.

"I learned this from Oknodie too. Using vocal magic to make sounds from different locations is basic, isn't it?"
"Ha. I admit you're talented. But I can simply ignore these tricks."
"I can't let you do that!"

One of the doppelgangers approaching Tetrapos with awkward steps suddenly flashed.

"?"

Flash. Flash. Flash flash. Flash flash.
Flash flash flash flash.
FLASH!

The doppelganger, flashing increasingly rapidly, was enveloped in a bright light and exploded right next to Tetrapos.
Tetrapos, who blocked the explosion by spreading threads into a circular shield, looked at the doppelganger with an incredulous expression.

"You even engraved a formula to make it explode when approaching the target if it can't deplete your strength?"

This couldn't be simply ignored.
He needed to deliberately shake them off.

'I should escape along a path with lower risk.'

But they were just doppelgangers.
Their walking was awkward.
Self-destruction was useless if they couldn't catch up.
Such tracking formulas usually had limited effective range, so they would likely explode somewhere or stop functioning after passing through a few quiz signs.

"Oh no, the senior is running away!"
'Foolish ones. I'm not running away—I'm sparing you because I can't kill you.'

Though furious at the nonsense that would have earned them a hole in the neck outside the Academy, Tetrapos quickly calmed his rising emotions.
If his feet slowed due to such light provocation, evading the student council would be impossible.
Let alone surviving in the outside world.

'Farewell, youngsters. See you next year.'

As Tetrapos was about to leave gracefully, a clear footstep sound captured his ears.

—Click.
The sound of shoes.
A distinct sound as if transmitted directly to his brain from above, with no warning, coming from very close.
A chilling fear swept through Tetrapos's body.
He hadn't detected it at all.
Despite spreading his senses widely to listen for sounds, he hadn't noticed the approach even once.
It was evidence that Student Council member Velvet's level far exceeded his perception.

He needed to escape.
In a direction where the sound couldn't be heard.
Right now!

"A third-year? Why is he running away so urgently?"
"That person reeks of blood."

Unfortunately, the path he entered was full of first-years wearing magic robes.
Tetrapos identified their affiliations from the emblems of tower mages who liked to show off their origins.
Earth mages. Fire mages.
Even freelance mages who had learned magic from colored towers without official recognition.
But the one who caught his eye most was a silver-haired woman who possessed the most dazzling magical power yet wore the shabbiest robe without any emblems.

"Dorothy was in that direction. Prepare for battle."

With Northern Archduchess Irene's declaration, the mages quickly began chanting spells.
Fortunate they're first-years.
Tetrapos knew that thanks to some who were casting time-consuming spells, the firing line wasn't very dense, and they couldn't bind his feet with sufficient status effect magic.

<Cruel Blood Manipulation>
<Defense Technique - Blood Scale Armor>

Most mages were greatly flustered by Tetrapos's charge without a moment's hesitation, wrapping his entire body in thread armor.
Most spells missed their aim points, impossible to even see, and magic that didn't require aiming was blocked by the blood scale armor, failing to inflict sufficient power and status effects.

'A powerful fighter with tremendous combat experience!'
'As expected of a third-year?'

However, the earth wall raised by Sand Cooker and the flame breath conjured by Rogini took away the left, right, and upper paths in Tetrapos's direction.
The only one Tetrapos had to face, with no choice but to break through straight ahead, was Northern Archduchess Irene.
In firepower, she was a powerhouse comparable even to Ru, the White Holy Knight of the second-year advanced class.

'The difference between second and third years is like heaven and earth. But the difference between me in the first semester and second semester is also like heaven and earth.'

Irene had been watching all along.
Oknodie's journey.
And she had felt it.
The need for greater strength.
To recruit someone as powerful as him, charm alone wouldn't be enough.
She herself needed to be strong enough not to fall away from Oknodie's side.

<5th Tier - Frost Bomb>

Following the path created by Sand Cooker, frozen ice expanded due to Rogini's flame breath, scattering frigid ice fragments in all directions.
Freezing of the body that couldn't be avoided even with threads wrapped around!

<3rd Tier - Rapid Freezing>

Freezing the scattered fragments once more for double freezing.

<2nd Tier - Ice Binding>

Triple freezing with a targeted spell on the slowed target.

<2nd Tier - Water Bomb>
<3rd Tier - Frost Wind>
<2nd Tier - Strong Wind>

Consecutive follow-up spells to enhance the duration and power of the binding, continuously restricting his movement.
In that gap, all the hesitant mages finished their incantations and launched their spells, causing Tetrapos's body to be surrounded by a large block of ice, unable to move and hit by dozens of consecutive spells.

'This should be enough to take him down, right?'

Defying everyone's expectations, beyond the frozen and tattered thread armor appeared an even redder blood armor formed from blood.

"This isn't worth it. Damn fledglings. I'm starting to use more blood than I've extorted."

The overwhelming pressure emanating from the blood light filling Tetrapos's eyes froze all the mages, including Irene, making them too afraid to even attempt casting spells.

"Is it this difficult to hold back? At this rate, I might as well..."

Kill one of them?
As he formed a round ring of blood in his hand with murderous intent, he heard the sound again.

—Click.

That damned shoe sound.

"Consider yourselves lucky, youngsters."

Tetrapos calmly passed by those who couldn't move, like frogs before a snake.
