# Enhanced Translation: Chapter 122 - Oknodie's Transformation

Until last week, the person who could most be called the protagonist at the Academy was Oknodie.

Small stature.
Vibrant, lively eyes.
An assertiveness that always had her energetically getting into something.
A friendliness that would make her follow strangers just for candy.
Skills that stood out in both written and practical exams.

There were many, many reasons for everyone to like Oknodie.
However, the students' reactions changed dramatically in just one week.

She's kind of scary.
A creepy child who appears without a sound.
An assertiveness where you never know what she might do next.
It's eerie how she pops out from strange places.
It's unsettling how unnaturally competent she is for a child.

Students who were close to Oknodie actively protested that these were false rumors, but prejudice is fundamentally an instinctive mental process for quick judgment.
In an Academy overflowing with countless assignments and adventurous elements, there wasn't much time to spare for a single student.

That the unpleasant child is actually not unpleasant.
That the creepy child is actually a good child.

How much information must be gathered to correct misinformation and eliminate prejudice?
And how troublesome is it to accept that effort?
Most students, rather than putting in the effort to admit their judgment was wrong in an Academy where time was already scarce, simply chose to believe their prejudices.

"Those jerks. When Oknodie was helping them, they praised and encouraged her."
"Let it go, Dorothy. Our efforts won't improve Oknodie's reputation. Those who believe in her already do. Those who don't will only grow more resentful."
"Miss Isabel is right. Gossip and rumors in noble houses are typically like this. Rumors spreading among common students won't be much different."

Arcadia, a noble lady from the maritime nation of Florence.
In a country that was a hotbed of mixed cultures without roots, rumors were generated and spread at a pace rivaling the social gatherings of the Imperial center.

"Though I feel sorry for Oknodie, I actually think this is for the better."
"Why? You're quite fond of Oknodie too. Enough to invite her to your expensive networking tea parties."
"As fewer people seek out Oknodie, the more time she spends with me. I wish she could be my own little Oknodie. Hoho!"

Arcadia's mischievous laugh as she opened her fan had an annoying yet irresistible charm.

"Arcadia, you really act like a noble lady at times like this."
"My! How rude. I am a noble lady, after all."
"When you take care of outsiders like Jenya, Nise, or Zuang, you're secretly called 'Momdia' because you're so motherly."
"What?! Calling an unmarried virgin a mother—are you trying to make me a holy virgin?!"
"It's a joke, just a joke. Arcadia, you could have any man you wanted if you set your mind to it."
"Of course!"

Even Isabel could see that Arcadia was an excellent woman lacking nothing—charm, academic ability, character, charisma, everything.
If anything, her excellence was so overwhelming that men were too intimidated to approach her.

'Would someone like Andersen, the leader of Seogwiyeon, be a good match for her in terms of status?'

One who voluntarily chooses hardship.
Andersen, called the model student for selecting only the most difficult lectures, might make a good match for her.

"I'm actually more worried about Oknodie."
"Are there more students slandering Di?"
"Not that. I'm worried about creepy students approaching her."

Isabel spoke with a concerned expression.

"You know, those guys who see a girl in distress and think it's their chance. They approach her, comfort her, talk to her, build emotional rapport, and voluntarily nominate themselves as boyfriend candidates."
"Such impure intentions!! Oknodie has barely turned ten—age difference aside, I don't like those impure motives!"
"There have been some guys hanging around Oknodie lately. So I was wondering if you could look into it..."

Before Isabel could finish, Arcadia grabbed both her hands.

"Of course! Leave it to me. I'll chase away all those impure men lurking around Oknodie!!"

That wasn't quite what Isabel had hoped for.
She began to worry that she'd unnecessarily ignited Arcadia's passion and might create problems where none existed.


* * *


Arcadia discovered the first man hovering around Oknodie.

"Hell's bells! That deranged pirate is approaching Oknodie while her heart is vulnerable! What is he trying to do?!"
"Arcadia's expression is scary! Jenya wants to go home!"
"Quiet! I asked for your help in case physical intervention becomes necessary. If you run away now, I won't get you any more catnip!"

Jenya, a loyal minion whose soul had already been captured by her favorite treat, had no choice but to follow Arcadia despite trembling at her threat.

"We need to get closer to eavesdrop."
"Nyaa. Jenya has good ears and can hear from far away."
"Then listen for me and tell me what they're saying."

Jenya closed her eyes for a moment and perked up her ears.
Then she relayed the conversation between the two.

"What is this tiny thing thinking? Aiming for a giant mosquito special—that won't die even if you shoot it."
"Hmph. But if I raid the blood stone storage where giant mosquitoes keep their blood, it would be useful for trading with seniors."
"What are you planning to buy with that?"
"A carriage pass."

With those final words, the two moved away.
As they secretly followed them to their new location, Jenya asked Arcadia:

"What's a pass?"
"It's a ticket that allows you to use a facility or transportation a certain number of times within a set period. I didn't know the Academy had such carriages."
"Why would she need that?"
"I'm not sure. Maybe she's trying to reach some distant facility within the Academy grounds? Or perhaps she's riding in carriages to avoid mosquitoes during her commute."
"Nyaa! Jenya wants a pass too! Giant mosquitoes are scary!"
"Alright, stop crying nyaa. They're looking this way!"

Eventually, Arcadia had to abandon her surveillance that day because Jenya nearly blew their cover.


* * *


The next day, instead of the red-haired pirate Jigoku, Arcadia discovered Oknodie talking with a slick-looking, unimpressive second-year senior.

"Jenya said you offered an item that provides supreme mental pleasure as compensation. The nuns of the Thunderbolt Heaven God Church are not permitted to use drugs."
"...Don't misunderstand. I just gave her catnip as payment for cooperation."
"Consumption of animal-based drugs is also not permitted."
"If you're just going to talk nonsense, you can leave."
"If you promise to get me a helmet beetle's shell instead, I won't withhold my assistance."
"I don't know what that is, but if it can be purchased with points, I'll help you."

Having replaced the noisy Jenya who interfered with covert operations, Arcadia had recruited Nise, Jenya's close friend and a member of the advanced class, a nun of the Thunderbolt Heaven God Church.
Arcadia's expression softened as she watched Nise, who looked as neat as a doll, keeping her mouth shut and concentrating.

'If she just stayed quiet, this child would be as cute as Oknodie.'

Nise, who always wore heavy chain mail armor and even iron shoes, looked hot, heavy, and uncomfortable.
Arcadia didn't particularly want to know about the sacred art mechanism of the Thunderbolt Heaven God Church, which claimed that only by enduring such discomfort could one achieve thunderous speed.
The school uniform was already humid and uncomfortable enough without subjecting oneself to such torturous attire.

'In this humid weather, wearing such clothing definitely requires overflowing religious devotion.'

The god of the Thunderbolt Heaven God Church, "Madeus of Sacred Light."
His method of confirming his followers' devotion seemed quite perverse.
It seemed that anyone involved with religion, for better or worse, becomes a madman like Andersen, the young lord who takes the "How to Use Sacred Arts Without Faith" course.

"That senior's name is Bigstone, and he currently belongs to the Alchemy-Chemistry Club. She's trying to purchase a bug-attracting spray from him."
"I see. A mosquito-attracting... Wait, attracting them? Not repelling them?"
"Without a doubt. The senior also asked about the purpose of purchasing such an ominous forbidden item, and she said she wants to gather mosquitoes and fatten them with dark nutritional supplements to make them even more gigantic."

A bullied child.
A forbidden elixir that enlarges monsters.
Mosquitoes that are already huge becoming even larger.
Blood stone storage where giant mosquitoes collect blood.
Sacrificial offerings.
All sorts of ominous imaginations unfolded in Arcadia's mind.

"No, Di!! No matter how angry you are, it's wrong to take revenge by sacrificing classmates who gossip about you as offerings to mosquitoes to steal their blood!!"

Arcadia suddenly jumped up and rushed forward.
Oknodie, caught in the middle of her black market deal, quickly waved her hands.

"That's not it!"
"What do you mean it's not? Then tell me the truth about why you're trying to make giant mosquitoes even bigger!"
"Giant mosquitoes don't grow larger than 1 meter, and if they get any fatter, they become land-walking mosquitoes that can't fly!"
"My goodness. Were you planning to create such horrible mosquitoes to terrorize passing classmates with ground attacks?!"
"That's not it, I tell you. The blood stone storage is up in trees, and fattened mosquitoes can't climb trees."

Phew. So Oknodie wasn't plotting some evil revenge plan with her eyes clouded by vengeance.

"More importantly, please tell me why you've been following me. Why have you been tailing me since yesterday?"
"Eh? You already knew?"
"Of course. With all that nyaa crying."
"Ugh. Jenya really is useless for surveillance... But why didn't you say you'd noticed earlier?"
"I was curious why Arcadia was following me."

Oknodie thought for a moment before answering.

"And if Arcadia was following me with bad intentions, I wouldn't know how to deal with it."

Arcadia's heart melted at Oknodie's uncharacteristic caution and distance.

"It's the opposite! I was only worried that you might be taken advantage of by some bad man while your heart was vulnerable!"
"Really?"
"Really."

The bright smile that Arcadia knew so well returned to Oknodie's previously cautious face.

"Come here, Di. Let me give you a hug."
"Hehe."

Oknodie came running and nestled into her arms.
As Arcadia gently stroked the back of her head, Oknodie suggested in a subtle voice:

"Would Arcadia like to join in?"
"Join what?"
"The carriage pass acquisition plan."

Arcadia held Oknodie by both shoulders, pulled her away, and looked her straight in the eyes.

"I've been wondering since yesterday, what do you need a carriage pass for?"
"After the heavy rain and mosquito outbreak, there's a high probability of a plague event. We need to use the carriage to go to the forest where antidotes are available."
"Oh my. Were you planning to get antidotes in advance for your friends?"

Oknodie tilted her head.

"My friends all have lots of points! They don't need to go get antidotes separately. Mob might be borderline? But I'll lend him some, so it's fine!"

Her friends are fine.
They don't need antidotes.
Then why is she trying to get them?

"Ah, I see. You're trying to get antidotes in advance to help poor classmates who don't have points?"

Oknodie is a good child.
That must be it, of course.
But for some reason, Oknodie couldn't answer Arcadia's assumption.
She looked uneasy.
Avoided eye contact.
Whistled.
Despite her cute behavior, Arcadia felt a chill in her heart.
She had changed.
Just as the students' attitudes toward Oknodie had changed, Oknodie's attitude toward the students had changed as well.
Arcadia could feel the good-hearted Oknodie she knew disappearing.
