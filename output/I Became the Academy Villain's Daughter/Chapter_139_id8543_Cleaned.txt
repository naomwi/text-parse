# 139 - Unwavering Camaraderie

It was practice time for the triathlon competition.
Everyone coveted the inanimate golems that would conserve their stamina and stride forward with long steps, but the golems didn't easily grant access to their saddles.

"You inferior golem! You may bow your head in honor. I, Honor Fried Chicken of the Fried Chicken family, one of the Empire's three great meritorious houses, shall become your master!"

A massive rock fist came crashing down on Honor Fried Chicken's head after his proud declaration.
The other students changed their approach after watching Honor Fried Chicken crawl out of the fist-shaped crater in the ground while blood dripped from his forehead.

"O great Golem! I, Skola, descendant of the divine archer and a marksman, request to become your companion! Please grant me permission to mount your saddle!"

The golem responded to this flattering strategy by kicking the ground with a thud.
Toododoodoo!
Through the rising cloud of dust, Skola could be seen running toward the stream, spitting out dirt and crying.

Neither threats nor submission worked.
These were the violent heavyweights of the mount world.
The absolute dominators in the power relationship.
Next to these golems exercising their absolute authority stood Oknodie and Hestia, already mounted on golems, blankly watching the scene unfold below.
Upon discovering them, the Mesugakki Imperial Princess exploded with anger.

"Pathetic♡ No taste♡ Don't even know how to choose a superior master♡ Why are you so violent with us but easily allowed those two to mount?"

Despite her anger coming out in a cute tone, Hestia readily provided an answer.

"I raise mini-golems. So the golems consider me a friend."

Hestia proudly demonstrated her friendship by taking out a mini-golem from her pocket and tapping it.
The mini-golem that had crawled out of the pocket wobbled as if it had hit a pillar before falling onto her palm.

"Muuuuuu"
"Muuu"

Judging by the full-sized golems' anxious behavior, it wasn't clear whether they recognized her as a friend or were just being submissive out of fear of a child abductor.

"Then what about that little one~?"

"Little Oknodie."
"Hmph! If you're going to mock me like that, don't expect an answer."

The Mesugakki Imperial Princess's mouth protruded several feet as she whined from below, but Oknodie paid no attention.
The golem she was riding swept the ground with its hand and flung the Imperial Princess far away.

"...Is she dead?"
"She'll be fine. The Mesugakki Imperial Princess has quite impressive base stats!"
"Stats?"
"The Imperial family can eat a lot of food!"

Hestia might not know as an NPC, but people from ducal families, royalty, or the Empire don't need to worry about their health.
Having grown up eating various foods, their Cookbook collection rate is naturally high, and they receive significant health stat bonuses as a result.
In the early stages, it's impossible for players to become healthier than those people no matter how hard they try.
Hestia would probably be shocked to learn that the Mesugakki Imperial Princess is actually healthier than her!

While the advanced class students were causing a commotion with the golems, the real chaos was happening in front of the horses.

"Heheng. I got a horse."

"Are you stupid? Let's go, Rudolf. Let's ignore these idiots and take a lap!"

"Muuuuuuh!"

Horse owners fleeing, students chasing after them.
It was complete and utter chaos.

"Hoho. Those kids. I was planning to do escape and pursuit training in the next class. They're getting ahead of the curriculum on their own. There was a reason the principal said this 981st class was special."

Only Professor Platon watched the scene with satisfaction.

Most students couldn't properly handle their reluctantly chosen mounts, failing to complete the taming process as they were either dragged along by their wild horses or engaged in physical fights with cheetahs.

"Whoa, Isabel. What happened to you?"

"I caught a large dog, but the thing kept licking me like crazy."

"It seemed smart, so I thought it would be more useful. I didn't expect this side effect."
"I'm glad I didn't choose that one."

"What did you choose, Son O-cheon?"

"I don't know. Don't ask anything."

After experiencing their mounts, everyone had an inescapable feeling that their hardships were just beginning.
If the principal's flag mission was the first thing that made them nervous after entering the Academy, the approaching danger was Professor Platon's triathlon.
It was essentially the advanced class equivalent of the "Mana Verification Test" that the lower class feared.

"Little lady, does the golem listen well to you?"
"More or less!"
"How strange. As far as I know, golems are extremely aggressive toward humans who don't have ownership rights."

While Hestia might have controlled the golem by taking a hostage, what trick had Oknodie used?
Curious, Gisele asked, and Oknodie answered with a bright and cheerful face.

"I don't know why, but when I showed them my pocket, the golems started listening to me!"

Oknodie showed her pocket full of stones.
Gisele pondered this sight.
Why were the golems terrified?
Golems that grow by eating rocks.
The stones in her pocket.
Stones that are essentially parts of golems' bodies.
A child proudly carrying and showing off what could be parts of their bodies or their kin as trophies.

'...I think I understand the reason, but it's better for our little lady if I don't tell her.'

She's a child destined to become a demon lord.

Those tainted by darkness get along well.

"Argh! What did you throw at me? I can't see. My eyes and nose are burning so much...!"
