# 308 - Pirate-Style Greeting

The cruise ship, with half its crew gone, could hardly be described as having a good atmosphere, even as a polite fiction.

"I'm getting off this ship once this voyage is over."
"What about your life-changing points?"
"I'll earn them by carrying out the Foundation's orders normally. For voyages like this, even several lives wouldn't be enough."

The crew members who had boarded the ship expecting instant fortune, regardless of their abilities or efforts, realized that luck brings not only good fortune but also misfortune. If you don't rely on chance, you can overcome life's challenges through daily efforts, but if you stake your life on luck, a moment of misfortune can end it all. The dead weren't even special in any way compared to the survivors.

Cages 1 through 4.

The crew members had been imprisoned in groups of 500 in differently numbered cages. Just by being trapped in Cages 1 and 2, which had caught the attention of the demon contractor Low, crew members died in droves. If Cages 3 or 4 had been chosen instead, it wouldn't have been strange if the dead and the living had completely switched places.

"Miss Oknodie. As much as I'd like to escort you to your destination, the helmsman was among the crew members killed by the demon contractor, so we can't operate the cruise ship."
"Oh! That's fine. We have a student who's good at steering ships!"

Jigoku, who was singled out by Oknodie, scratched his cheek with a doubtful expression.

"I'm fine with it, but are you sure? Entrusting a ship to a pirate is like leaving a fish shop to a cat, you know?"
"It's fine. Even cats die if they eat pufferfish!"
"..."

Well, if someone tried to swallow this dangerous ship in one gulp, they'd certainly suffer serious consequences.
Jigoku honestly thought a hundred times over that it was fortunate they had acted late.
The student rebels.
Professor Rave.
The apostle of Anrage.
Amid all the chaos, if they had been the first to act, they would have been imprisoned in cages or detained as sacrifices long ago.
It was no exaggeration to say they survived thanks to their caution and hesitation until the very end.

"Captain Jigoku! Does that mean we get to steer the ship too?"
"Add honorifics and call me 'Captain.' It's annoying to steer alone, so yeah, let's do it together."
"Wow! I thought I'd spend my life as a lowly pirate underling, but I never imagined I'd get to experience steering a ship!"
"So how do we steer it? Do we row with oars or something?"
"Idiot. This isn't a galley ship. If we were to row this huge ship, we'd need thousands of mana users in the hold, and even that wouldn't be enough."

So how do we actually steer it?

"Ships like this are mechanized. You don't have to do much; just infuse mana, set the coordinates, and use the auto-navigation function. The helmsman only makes minor adjustments when facing dangerous reefs."
"So we're just the cheerleading squad?"
"As if! The ship's main functions were damaged in battle, and all the mechanical devices are broken. We need to activate the manual systems prepared for emergencies."

Jigoku kicked open the emergency storage box in the warehouse.

"Here. Take one tool each."

The third-rate pirate underlings each received a shovel handed over by Jigoku.

"What's this? What does this shovel imply?"
"Maybe it means we should hit lazy crew members with shovels. We are pirates, after all."
"Ooh!"
"What nonsense are you talking about? Go dig up coal with the crew members and throw it in!"
"..."

The harsh reality of being a bottom-tier pirate with no dreams or hopes!
Even the crew members gave the pirates dirty looks.

"Aren't those the pirates who burst into the grand hall wearing masks?"
"They still haven't returned the jewels they stole..."

The crew members actively assigned the pirates to the most labor-intensive work areas.

"This is too much hard labor."
"My arms feel like they're going to break."
"I miss my mom. I want to go home..."

One crew member, his face covered in soot, secretly glanced outside.
We're working so pitifully here, can't you say something to the captain!
The crew member peeked his head out, secretly sending distress signals by mouthing words to the other students!
A student peeked through the window and made eye contact with the crew member who was trying to appeal to their sympathy.

"Huh!"

The crew member, who fell backward in surprise at the sudden eye contact, was even more shocked by who he had made eye contact with.
The Mesugakki Imperial Princess, with a bandage on her cheek, smiled with intensity in her eyes.

"Oh~? It's one of those third-rate underlings who just watched from the sidelines during the entire battle, not doing any hard work and freeloading♡"
"Ugh. The Imperial Princess..."
"Surely you're not thinking of running away just because your arms hurt a little~? Unless you want to be beaten to death by an angry classmate, there's no fool who would do that~?"

Next to the Mesugakki Imperial Princess, the face of a rough-looking bearded man suddenly popped out.

"Just say the word. If anyone tries to run away, I'll crack their skull with my staff."
"..."

The alliance between the Mesugakki Imperial Princess and Son O-cheon, achieving the difficult integration of the frontier and the empire!
There was no place for the freeloading pirate crew members in this unity of students who had survived a life-threatening crisis, transcending factional logic.
The third-rate pirate underlings realized there was no escape from the karma of their freeloading and picked up their shovels with tears in their eyes.


* *


By the time the crew members were about to collapse from exhaustion, the destination island finally came into view.
The ship's horn announced this fact to all the crew members and students scattered throughout the large cruise ship, and everyone rushed to the deck to see the island.

"Wow. That building is huge."
"What is it? A magic tower?"
"It might be the Foundation's secret base."
"To build such a large structure on an unidentified island. Could it be a treasure vault for the Foundation's valuables?"

Jonna, who was standing at attention next to Oknodie, kindly answered the questions of the many students.

"That place is called the Querannia Archipelago, and the building you see now is the Chairman's mansion."
"That's all a house!?"
"...It has more floors than our magic tower."
"And it's much more luxurious than our yellow magic tower made of earth."

Rojini, who had been freed from petrification, and Sandcooker, whose arms had been repeatedly pinched by her, looked at the tower-like mansion with envious eyes.
A magic tower is considered impressive if it exceeds 30 floors, but the tower they were looking at had over 50 floors.
And it was called a "mansion."
They knew the Foundation operated on a continental scale, but it was absurdly wealthy.

"It's bigger than the palace where the Imperial Princess lived?"

Even the Mesugakki Imperial Princess was somewhat surprised by the mansion's enormous scale!

"The mansion is large because it also serves the purpose of storing collections."
"Collections?"
"Miss, you haven't seen them in person yet, have you? Take this opportunity to look around. It will be a chance to broaden your horizons, especially for someone who constantly talks about collecting guidebooks."
"Does that mean there's lots of food too?"
"You can look forward to it."

Jonna doesn't make empty promises.
Oknodie's expectations grew exponentially.
While her eyes sparkled with excitement, the hero looked quite intimidated and cautious.

"If we go there... will we receive those orders?"

The fear typical of a novice scholarship student who had heard rumors about the Foundation's orders but never carried them out!
The members of the "Secret Scholarship Society," a subordinate organization of the "Organization That Plays with Oknodie," smiled at the naive words of the new scholarship student while keeping their mouths tightly shut.

"They wouldn't give difficult orders from the start."
"Orders are given to inferior scholarship students who fail to prove their worth or don't follow the Foundation's instructions."
"The treatment was actually better when we were new, right?"
"Ah, I miss those days."
"I want to go back to when I was new too."

Even those who regretted becoming scholarship students reminisced about the early days, saying "those were good times," showing how excellent the Foundation's initial investment was.
It was much better than ordinary production workers or adventurers who spend five years washing dishes just to learn how to cook ramen or five years as porters to learn mercenary work!

"Should I hold your hand if you're scared?"
"Ugh. Don't treat me like a child."

Someone firmly grabbed Ishtar's hand after he rejected Oknodie's offer.

"Don't act tough. You're trembling this much."
"Yupi!"
"If you know it's embarrassing enough to make your face red, you shouldn't have done something embarrassing in the first place. This is punishment for knocking me out before when you were looking for patronage."

Rojini and Arcadia's eyes simultaneously sparkled at Yupi's proactive attitude.
Rojini's gaze turned toward Sandcooker, but Arcadia's gaze was directed at Oknodie.

"W-what?"

While Sandcooker, who lacked awareness but had plenty of courage, fled in panic, Oknodie casually entrusted her hand to Arcadia, who had subtly taken it.

"Are you scared too, Arcadia?"
"I'm worried Di might be scared."
"Me?"

Arcadia answered seriously to Oknodie, whose eyes had widened.

"Di's Papa is... a scary person, isn't he?"

Everyone's expression on the deck stiffened.
This was a Papa who had prepared a catastrophe where half the crew died on the ship that came to meet his daughter.
If such a person wasn't scary, then no one in the world was.

"Is he? I don't really know?"
"Sob. That child doesn't even know to fear her own Papa because she's never experienced what a normal family is like."
"Poor thing."
"The Foundation is truly unforgivable."
"Instead of saying 'unforgivable,' shouldn't we be saying 'please spare us'? We might meet the Papa himself who set the trap this time."
"Surely he wouldn't set up horrible traps like on the cruise ship in his own home?"

Home is a place to rest.
Surely not.
He would probably maintain some boundaries, if not extend a welcome.
So let's also hide our hostility tactfully.
If we don't upset him, we might get through this relatively well.
The students making an implicit agreement behind Oknodie's back!

"But why isn't the ship stopping?"
"We're getting closer and closer to the island?"

Everyone rushed to the wheelhouse in panic.
Jigoku, who had become the new master of the wheelhouse, was waving something in his hand.
It was what had once served as a lever.

"What is that?"

Gisele, unable to hide his ominous premonition, asked while sweating profusely.

"Ah, this is what they call a brake. It's broken now, so it doesn't work."

At Jigoku's confident answer, the faces of many students, including Gisele who had asked directly, turned pale.
Despite their determination not to upset Oknodie's Papa, the cruise ship—with a total tonnage of 200,000 tons, still 120,000 tons even after the fierce battle—plowed through the island's sandy beach and charged toward the mansion.
