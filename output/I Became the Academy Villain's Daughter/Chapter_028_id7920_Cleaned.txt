# 28 - Troublesome Consideration

The examinees think:
The first supplementary task is too risky to attempt carelessly.
The second supplementary task still makes them hesitant.
The third supplementary task was different.
In a situation where NPC examinees couldn't know how close the goal was or how many supplementary tasks remained, they couldn't afford to give up on the third one.

"What an incredible crowd."
"Isn't this too many people?"
"It can't be helped. Everyone's at the point where they need to make a decisive move or they'll be in trouble."

We're in the same boat with no room to spare.
We've only earned 10 points.
That's significantly short of the minimum passing score of 50 points.

The third supplementary task: <Tag>.
This task was perfectly timed to release a large number of points.

"Now, everyone who wants to challenge the third task, gather here."

The red-hatted instructor tapped the sign in front of him.

1. Participating in Tag gives everyone 20 points.
2. The tagger loses 1 point every minute.
3. Runners gain 1 point every minute.
4. When the tagger touches a runner, the tagger steals the runner's points and becomes a runner, and the new tagger begins pursuit after a 30-second signal from their ticket watch.
5. The game ends when you find and touch the black-hatted instructor.
6. The exit can only be reached after ending the Tag game.
7. Once ended, you cannot retry.

Across from him, the blue-hatted instructor had relief tents and stalls prominently displayed.

"Come, come! Examinees who need food, lodging, or medical treatment, come this way! You can exchange anything with points!"

A situation where methods to both earn and use points appeared simultaneously.

"How deflating."
"Little mouse. Did we earn those 10 points for nothing?"
"The value of points has hit rock bottom."

Everyone was discouraged, but my thoughts were different.
As they say, experienced players always have a plan.

"Since we earned points in advance, there might be something we can do?"
"Do you really think there will be?"

Even Gisele was skeptical.
I ran straight to the blue-hatted instructor.

"Mr. Instructor."
"Do you have any services or items you wish to purchase?"
"Please bend down for a moment!"

I tugged at his clothes, asking him to bend down so others wouldn't overhear, and a broad smile spread across the instructor's face.

"Yes, my little examinee. What do you need?"
"Do you perhaps sell something like this?"

Whisper, whisper.
After hearing my whispered request, the instructor was surprised and asked me to wait a moment.
Shortly after, he returned with a serious expression and handed me an earpiece.

"It's the chief examiner of the second gate. She wants to speak with you directly. It has communication magic on it, so you just need to put it in your ear."

As I pushed the large earpiece into my ear canal, I felt something connect with a pop.

"Hello?"
"...Examinee Oknodie. Have you used communication magic before? It's not a common experience."
"Ah."

I unconsciously answered as if taking a phone call.
Is this really such a rare experience?

"Well, nobles can make calls."
"...Right. Nobles can do that."

Minerva, the second gate examiner, answered with an unconvinced tone.
She got to the point.

"I thought this during the first supplementary task too, but Examinee Oknodie, your creativity is remarkable."
"Hehe. I'm pretty great, aren't I?"
"If your humility matched even half of that, it would be better. To answer your request, it's impossible in principle. That's not an item for sale."
"Eeh? Really?"
"However, this is the first time I've received such a proposal. In nature, original ideas, thoughts no one else has conceived, often become powerful weapons."

I took a shot thinking it would be nice if it worked, and luckily, fortune was on my side.

"As a tribute to Examinee Oknodie's creativity, I'll specially allow the sale. One single-use <Black Hat> with instructor qualifications, for 10 points."


* * *


Gisele praised me endlessly.

"To purchase a cheat key that lets you end the test whenever and wherever you want—a one-time authority to become a black-hatted instructor—truly amazing!"
"Is that really so impressive?"
"Think about it. In this Tag game, even if you want to quit, you must continue if you can't find the black-hatted instructor. If Son O-cheon were the tagger, where would he be?"
"Near the instructor. The little mice would come looking on their own."
"Exactly. No matter how many points you lose, if you ultimately steal points, and while the new tagger is immobilized, touch the instructor and escape the game, you'll definitely profit."

So any tagger with sense would circle around the black-hatted instructor, and runners would have to approach the instructor at the risk of being caught by the tagger.
It's essentially not a game of tag but a game of breaking through taggers positioned around the instructor.

"In such a structure, Oknodie purchased the authority to become a black-hatted instructor, creating a device to escape the game whenever she wants."
"Ooh, little mouse. That's incredible!"
"Heheh. Now do you see how amazing I am?"

As I struck a proud pose with my hands on my hips, Isabel thoroughly ruffled my hair.
Mmm...
Getting petted by Isabel feels so good.
I brought her along as a cook to work like a slave, but I'm the one being tamed.

"Still, it's not an all-powerful cheat key. If one of us becomes the tagger, someone in the tagger role still has to find other runners."
"I see. So one way or another, we still have to compete with other runners in tag."

Just as I was about to approach the red-head to purchase the instructor authority and participate in tag, a group of four swordsmen blocked our path.

"You there, are you the child called Oknodie?"
"Who are you?"
"I am Maximus Mont Blanc. Max's older brother."
"...Who's Max?"
"Haa. So he wasn't even on your radar. Poor little brother."

The yellow-haired swordsman who resembled my golden hair lamented that his brother had become pitiful and said:

"Didn't you ride an airship on your way here?"
"Ah! I did!"
"There was a boy looking after a baby with bad manners at the playground in the Kids Zone, right?"
"That's right! My hair got pulled out a lot..."

Hmph. That really hurt back then.

"That boy is Max."
"Ah, I see. So what brings Max's brother to me?"
"I received a request. If I met Oknodie at the exam site, please help her."
"I'm not that weak, you know."
"I know. You passed the first gate with top honors, so you can't be weak."

As Maximus said this, he exchanged glances with Gisele and nodded slightly.
I see.
Approaching me using my name was just a pretext.
He must have come to help Gisele because of their personal acquaintance.
Someone as thorough as Gisele might have recruited helpers like this in advance.
Isn't that how he passed the ticket exam too?

Scratch, scratch.

But perhaps he hadn't prepared for mosquitoes.
It's pitiful to see him scratching the back of his hand.

"This task has an absurd trap."
"The mosquitoes?"
"...The mosquitoes are a trap too. But not that."
"Hmm... I don't know!"

Of course I know everything.
I'm just pretending appropriately.

"Think about it. They give ample points and set up a system to use those points, but the possibility of using them is significantly low. It's all just a pie in the sky."

Son O-cheon laughed heartily and patted Maximus's shoulder.

"Yellow-haired fellow. Why so worried? Just accumulate points, meet the instructor, buy lots of necessary items, and track the examiner to the exit."
"Meeting that instructor is the problem. Where's the guarantee that the black-hatted instructor is nearby? Do you even know where he is?"
"Huh?"
"Oknodie, the same goes for you. Rule 6: The exit can only be reached after ending the Tag game. As long as this clause exists, you're essentially trapped in this forest."

That's right.
This is the trap and reality of the supplementary Tag task.
Tag isn't played between the tagger and runners.
It's between the examinees and the black-hatted instructor!
If you can't find the black-hatted instructor, no matter how many points you accumulate, you can't leave the forest due to Rule 6.
And according to my memory, the black hat is a kind of "summon" that moves opposite to the examiner, using the center as an axis.
The standard strategy is one of two options:
Either obtain this hint at the center of the forest, find and touch the black-hatted instructor, then cross the forest again to reach the exit in a tough forced march.
Or use strength and wisdom to put the examiner Minerva in danger to summon the summon, then touch the black-hatted instructor while avoiding the furious Minerva.

'When I was in my muscular body, I mainly used the latter.'

By drawing lots of monster aggro and throwing it at examinees near the exit, and when Minerva inevitably came to rescue them, ambushing her in that moment.
Since Minerva's summon is automatically "summoned" to save her when she's in danger, this is the most efficient route to gather a large number of points without wasting movement.
Ah, those were good times.
Comfortable too.
But with my current body, I somehow lack confidence, so I tried Plan B, which fortunately worked.

"Umm... is that so?"
"Haha. This is troublesome."

Both Mr. Son O-cheon and Mr. Gisele wear extremely awkward smiles.

"Oknodie. If you understand, quickly give up and try to earn points through legitimate methods instead of gambling..."
"Would you extend your wrist to the mana board?"
"Here's my ticket watch."
"Examinees Oknodie, Son O-cheon, Gisele, Isabel. Hide-and-seek challenge accepted! 20 points have been immediately added to your personal ticket watches, so check them."
"Hey!! Did you hear me? I'm sure you heard! Did you still go through with it after hearing me?!"

Sorry, Mr. Maximus.
I appreciate Maximus's consideration, but for us who can become instructors anytime, it's unnecessary meddling.
