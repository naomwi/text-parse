# Enhanced Translation: Chapter 380 - The Answer to the Quiz in My Heart

"Oknodie. Why is the path to the library so treacherous?"

As if this had been bothering him all along, Tito suddenly poked his head out with a bright light.

"Argh! Too bright."
"Oh, sorry! I'll switch to the gray of stealth."

Click.
As the light dimmed, my eyes felt much more comfortable.
I could also see Tito's curious bare face much more clearly.

"Because the library is dangerous!"
"More dangerous than the gates we just passed through?"
"Of course."
"Why?"
"The library is a treasure trove of knowledge, right?"
"Yes?"
"Valuable knowledge would attract many people, wouldn't it?"
"Uh-huh."
"And there would be various security measures to repel such people."

For Tito who still couldn't grasp the concept, I just told him directly.

"If you break security protocols, you get thrown into places like detention rooms, puzzle rooms, or the Forest of Gimmicks that we came out of!"
"Wow. Cutting-edge security! Sounds fun."
"Those places don't even have the newbie rescue policy that automatically releases you after a week if you can't escape!"

No one realizes how frightening the law is until they consider themselves becoming criminals.
Tito's eyes were wide open like a baby who had just woken up at night, thinking what reason could there be for a good person like himself to break library security protocols.

"But newbies don't know the security protocols!"
"Um... then where do you find the security protocols?"
"In the library!"
"Then you can just ask the librarian to find the security protocols. What's the problem?"
"Getting caught by the librarian is a violation of security protocols!"
"Huh?? Then how do you find the security protocols?"
"Without the librarian knowing!"
"...That's absurd!!!"

After pondering for a moment, Tito shouted.

"If they're that strict with visitors, there's no point in opening a library. New users can't use it at all!"
"That's the library's reason for existence."
"That's a lie. What kind of library works like that?"
"Surprisingly many."

Arcadia and Northern Grand Duchess Irene, who had been listening from behind, expressed their agreement.

"During the duchy era, the ducal archives had different levels of access depending on pre-assigned ranks. Those ranks were determined by one's status and position within the duchy. Those who weren't assigned ranks were completely prohibited from entering."
"The Northern Grand Archives are the same. We never know when demons might infiltrate to steal valuable knowledge or use that knowledge to create weapons against us. We don't allow entry or use to anyone who can't be trusted."

The royal archives of various countries or the imperial archives of the Empire are even stricter, not less.
Libraries aren't like 21st century Earth's public libraries that are open to everyone.

"That's strange. We're first-year students! They should be open to students."
"They are open to students. To upper-class students who already know the 'security protocols.'"

Arcadia gave a self-deprecating smile.

"Like any membership facility, new members can't use the library without recommendations, introductions, or knowledge transfer from existing qualified members. They've managed to create a microcosm of the outside world within the Academy. So even students unfamiliar with high society can learn about it."

Knowing there must be a clear reason, Tito hesitated before closing his mouth.
If you lived in a fantasy world, you'd naturally understand this.
In a world where the class system prevails, discrimination, oppression, the abuse of power by the privileged, and the absolute superiority of knowledge weren't strange concepts.
It's just that the true nature of the Academy hidden behind the point-based meritocracy, the structure that makes one face all the malice of the world first, has now entered Tito's eyes and mind.

"Hey, sorry to interrupt your chat, but the fourth gate has appeared ahead."

The fourth gate of the library expedition that can only be reached after sacrificing someone, solving knowledge-based puzzles, and passing through an ominous forest.
What greeted us was a large signpost and numerous branching paths.

[Sudden event <Quiz Corner!> has occurred.]

[Quiz Corner!]
Numerous quizzes are scattered ahead.
If you answer correctly, you'll reach the library faster, but the more wrong answers you choose, the more difficult the path becomes.
The door of knowledge opens to those who seek correctness.
Is your correctness strong enough to open the final trial door leading to the library?
Don't be discouraged if your knowledge is lacking.
The library definitely awaits at the end of the path.
It's just that a very long and arduous journey awaits those who are ignorant before the door opens!

Dorothy quickly ran to the signpost with the question written on it.
And froze on the spot.
The students who followed also covered their mouths in shock.

"Quiz 0001...?"
"Are there questions going up to the thousands?"

This would certainly be bewildering for newbies.
It's like being lured in by "Respond to our survey and get $1," only to find a mountain of questionnaires with over 200 items to click through.

"So which way does everyone want to go?"
"What do you mean which way?"
"Do you want to quickly get the right answers and head to the boss battle, or enjoy a longer endurance battle?"

At my urging question, even Son O-cheon now fully understood the gimmick of the gateway to the library.

"Hey, little mouse. Are you saying the faster we go, the more we suffer, and the longer route means less suffering?"
"That's right!"
"Judging by what you've been doing, taking the long way seems like the right answer, but we have this much strength. Wouldn't it be okay to take a shortcut once?"
"Well, experiencing it wouldn't be bad!"

There's no reason to stop a newbie who's motivated.

"An easy hint."
"My forest keeper's intuition says this is the right answer!"
"This one looks fun to me."

The expedition team's votes were split.
Dorothy for maternal love, Irene for pathetic, and Son O-cheon for just because.

"Oknodie, you choose. Which one is correct?"

I know this pattern. If I choose one person, the other two will lose affection points, right?

"They're all correct, so let's all go separately!"

The three grumbled but gathered children to follow them.
Eventually, the expedition team split into three groups.
Watching them excitedly disappear down different paths makes me proud.

"So the real answer was something else, right?"

Zuang, who remained until the end, asked, and I nodded with a grin.

"Flip over the question sign!"

━━━
The answer is #4.

"But there's no sign #4?"
"If it's not there, we have to find it."

Realizing something from my words, Zuang gathered mana in her eyes and activated Search Eye, a perception skill.
Looking at the path that opened high up in the ceiling, Zuang couldn't hide her astonishment.

"So that's the shortcut?"
"Yes. Want to try it?"
"Don't we need to bring Tito?"
"It's a bit dangerous for Tito!"
"That makes it even more tempting."

Zuang lightly jumped onto the signpost and reached the ceiling passage.
I also used my jump ability that I'd developed as a functional skill to climb up, and we took the shortcut for just the two of us.

"Oknodie. I can see below."
"That's the privilege of taking a shortcut!"

Through the ventilation grill cover below, we could occasionally see students struggling with the signposts, Son O-cheon fighting off monsters that appeared after choosing wrong answers to buy time to solve problems, and Tito happily choosing wrong answers.
Son O-cheon, who mistook Tito for an intelligence character, must be having a hard time!

A kind, friendly smile but cold, ruthless eyes.
