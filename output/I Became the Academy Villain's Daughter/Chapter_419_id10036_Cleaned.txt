# Enhanced Translation: Chapter 419 - The Hat Guarding the House

Though she claimed with her own mouth that she didn't know who had given her the final orders and acted as if she was above revenge, human hearts don't easily abandon thoughts of vengeance.
It wasn't that she was too kind-hearted to seek revenge. She knew there was no way to find her target, and even if she did, she lacked the power to succeed.

"Then we just need to find the foundation person backing Charlotte! Whoever is favorable to Charlotte must be the one who tormented the second hat lady!"

The Academy's extraterritorial zone, the area exclusively for students on leave.

Professor Mahabharata, the principal's loyal guardian, gathered students on leave who would become disasters to the world if left unchecked, isolating them in one place under the pretext of containment. Dragon Principal simply put them there for the short and simple reason that "it's interesting!"

'Will she be okay?'

Despite appearances, Oknodie has a tender heart.
She ate oyakodon katsu rice bowl, which cooks parent and child together, only once, saying it was too pitiful to eat again.
She also ate harako meshi, made with salmon and salmon roe, only once, saying the salmon was too pitiful.

'She's a child with deep compassion.'

She would probably eat bosintang only once if she knew what it was made from, and never again.

No, that can't be right.
I must be mistaken.

Believing that the strong yet tender-hearted Oknodie would return safely, Ellis focused on what she could do. Trapped in a new vessel called the Dark Aptitude Evaluation Hat rather than a wall, what she could do was...

"Waaah!"

Playing with the baby mandrake.

"I wish that child would return soon. Don't you?"

It was both admirable and pitiful.
When you become an inanimate object, effort becomes meaningless.
You could just exist, barely alive.

'What nonsense. That's not living at all.'

Life before being saved by Oknodie was by no means pleasant, even as a polite understatement.
A life where yesterday, today, and tomorrow were all the same.
A life where you merely watched someone else's life, like a voyeur.
How could such an existence be called a life?

But she never once acted on those impulses.

"It was My Lady who acted spoiled."
"But how could you indulge me? I merely chose the place of my death."
"My Lady will be punished. Suffer as you think about what dangers your butler must face because of your childish whims. That is the punishment I impose on you."

A single moment of childish behavior condemned her to a life as a mere observer, worse than death.
Riding on the academy's ghost stories, all she could do was speak to someone who moved in next to an empty room, conversing with them alone.
Perhaps she had hoped to be recognized.
After all, Jonna would know what fate had befallen her.

"Take this. It's a scroll that allows consciousness to inhabit objects, evading the scholarship students' eyes even after abandoning the physical body."

Telling him that his lady was here.
That she would steal the vessel with the greatest talent, evade the foundation's eyes, and return as his lady alone.
She even harbored the wicked intention of stealing an innocent student's body for herself.
But in the end, she couldn't do even that.
Hestia was lonely but strong.
Oknodie was strong without knowing loneliness.
These two people, too strong to the point of solitude, were raw gems too beautiful for her to tarnish.

If it had been Hestia alone, there might have been a chance.

'Hestia had a weak heart. Even at the Academy where the world's greatest talents gathered, she believed she would live forever alone, unable to open her heart to anyone.'

If she could capture that vulnerability, stealing her body would be simple.

They only made Ellis realize how futile and petty her actions were.
How was pushing a person desperate for conversation into solitude any different from what the foundation did?
From the day she realized this, Ellis abandoned her plan to steal Hestia's body.
And then, partly out of curiosity and partly out of resentment, she began to hover around Oknodie.

'The foundation's top scholarship student...?'

As an Eastern proverb says, "A spear in a sack will eventually poke through" - exceptional talent will reveal itself even when hidden.

At the Academy, they even assigned Master Myungho as a bodyguard, secretly defeating enemies.

She envied that.

Unlike herself, who had to risk her life just to receive and confirm Jonna's recognition, Oknodie had everyone's acknowledgment from the start.

'But why do I find her so pitiful?'

'An abnormal obsession with training. This child doesn't understand what real play is.'

Her concepts were broken.

A child thoroughly damaged by the foundation.
How could one not feel sympathy?

'Yes, there's no longer any resentment or jealousy toward Oknodie in my heart.'

Only compassion and a desire to support her.
