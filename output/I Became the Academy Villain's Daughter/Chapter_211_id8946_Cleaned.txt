# 211 - Hestia Has Few Friends

The Academy's first semester midterms had ended.
With the major event of midterms over, students gained their freedom.
And another battlefield began.

"Hey, first-year over there. Want to join the Body Modification Club and modify your body with us?"
"Ah, that's a bit..."
"Join the Alchemy Club and learn alchemy that makes tasteless one-point black bread delicious!"
"Wow, really?"

Like the Pied Piper, winners led flocks of naive first-years into club rooms, while losers sadly stroked their pulsating biceps as they headed to the training room.
This cruel world of winners and losers unfolded in the regular club recruitment zone.

<Weekly Event - Club Promotion Week>

It was the season when seniors devoted to recruitment seized this golden opportunity, their interests aligning with students wanting to compensate for their shortcomings through club affiliations.

"Which club are you joining, Oknodie?"
"Hmm. I don't really have any weaknesses, so I'm not sure."
"That's annoying but true."
"What about you, Isabel?"
"I'm planning to join the cooking club."

Isabel had been starting to feel her limitations.

"I have broad knowledge and know various cooking methods, but I can't keep improvising forever. I want to learn from the seniors' wisdom."
"Wow! That's a great idea."
"You're just excited about eating lots of new food, aren't you?"
"Hehe."
"...Well, fine. It's true that I'm joining partly to feed you."
"So which cooking club are you joining?"
"There's more than one cooking club?"

Of course there is. With food catalogs being so important, how could there be just one cooking club?

"There are mainly three cooking clubs. First, the 'Bone Broth Cooking Faction' that thoroughly masters one type of food each week. They have to fill at least a hundred recipes on one theme before moving to another."
"...Wouldn't you get sick of it?"
"It's not a bad choice if you want to learn different ways to prepare the same dish."
"What else?"
"The 'Gourmet Society' that specializes in rare dishes. They value both taste and quality, but I don't personally recommend it."
"Why? It sounds good."
"The club fee is 200 points every week!"
"...Is that some luxury for the rich? I'll go somewhere else."
"The last one is 'The Dark Feast,' which I recommend!"

Isabel's expression showed she was really reluctant.

"The name matches the Dark Chef class disturbingly well. What do they do there?"
"It's a commendable club willing to make somewhat extreme choices to raise your catalog. Like mixing animal feed into dishes? Or turning non-edible ingredients into edible ones?"
"...Why would anyone go that far?"
"In remote areas far from the outskirts, including forbidden zones like the demon realm where productivity is low, ingredients are naturally scarce. It's about preparing in advance so you can eat anything anywhere."
"That's surprisingly practical."
"Right?"
"You want me to join there, don't you, Oknodie?"
"Yes!"
"...Fine. Since I'm doing this to feed you, I should go where you recommend."

In the end, Isabel joined "The Dark Feast" club.

"So which club are you joining, Oknodie? Cooking? Swordsmanship? Magic? Assassination? Gathering?"
"Well, I don't want to join, but there is a club I'd like to observe. The one Hestia attends."
"Why there?"
"I'm a bit worried about Hestia."

More precisely, she was concerned about Hestia's victim trigger that she had unknowingly activated.

-To think that the friend I stayed up all night talking with, finding comfort in, was... a ghost trapped in the wall? And not really sharing friendship but aiming to trap me instead?

Hestia had gone mad after discovering the true nature of her only friend, as her last sanctuary of peace crumbled.
Since Oknodie had pulled Hestia's wall friend out with her hat, it was understandable why Hestia's expression hadn't been good lately.

"That child must be heartbroken."

The second hat-wearer genuinely worried about Hestia.
Having been freed from the wall, she was now free and no longer used her bond with Hestia for impure purposes.
She worried about Hestia purely as a friend who had shared heart-to-heart talks all night.

"Okay. Let's go see Hestia!"
"Thank you..."
"I should be thanking you for playing with Hestia."
"...You're really a good person."

What an embarrassing thing to say.
Feeling shy, she lightly touched the brim of her hat.


* *


The club Hestia belonged to had the rather unusual name "Paper Dungeon Expedition."

"What's good about joining this club?"
"You can go on adventures with comrades!"
"Comrades...?"
"Seeing is believing. Come and see for yourself."

Hestia didn't resist as she was pulled along by a senior weaker than herself.
Having gained strength but lost all relationships she could call comrades in return, she secretly hoped for a chance to soothe the sorrow of being a Berserker class who was distrusted even by allies.

"Today's paper dungeon theme is stagecoach robbery. Anyone not confident in horseback riding should sit out."
"Damn. Bad luck."
"I'll just watch."
"Me too."
"Newbie, you come watch too."

Several challengers approached the senior holding a book, placed their hands on it, and with a bright flash of light, the seniors' figures were sucked into the book.
As Hestia jumped up in surprise, the senior who had brought her calmed her down.

"Relax. That's a paper dungeon."
"Is it okay for people to be sucked in like that?"
"If the book receives external impact, it forcibly ejects everyone inside. Don't worry about getting hurt from book damage."

The paper dungeon was quite an interesting game for Hestia, who had experienced all sorts of adventures.

"Now, as you cross the border checkpoint, five orcs appear. What will you do?"

The senior holding the book narrated the situation, and the seniors inside the book responded accordingly.
It was truly a story of a paper dungeon and the expedition exploring it!
Moreover, the books on the shelves were of various genres.
Romance.
Action thriller.
Mythical fantasy.
Political fantasy.
Adult fairy tales rated 19+.
Books of all genres were available.

'Orcs don't look like that.'
'The dungeon research is wrong.'
'Clients don't give that much reward.'

To Hestia, a former mercenary, the historical inaccuracies and overly hopeful adventures felt disconnected from reality, but that's precisely why she liked it.

"So, newbie. Want to join us?"
"I'll join."
"Good decision. You'll make an excellent tank!"
"That's unfair. Your party already has another newbie."
"Hehe. If you're jealous, recruit your own newbies."
"Newbies, that guy looks different but he's super stingy, so if you're not satisfied, come join our party later. Got it?"
"I can hear you!?"

Though it was a false world with false adventures, they shared trials and hardships and enjoyed themselves together.
The joy of building bonds and sharing happiness was real, something that couldn't be experienced in reality.
Hestia enjoyed this time so much that she attended the "Paper Dungeon Expedition" without fail every week.

"Hestia, why the long face? Did you fail the exam?"
"I haven't been able to talk much with my friend lately."

From 9 AM to 9 PM.
Drawing a line for 12 hours a day but always responding when spoken to first—her friend beyond the wall.
With that friend remaining silent for days without a single word, she couldn't help feeling anxious and impatient.

"Cheer up, friend. The gallant shield-bearer Skull is here. Let's enjoy another exciting adventure today!"

Second-year senior Skull.
He had decent skills, enough to be in the intermediate class.
He had a personality as good as his skills, but he wasn't a classmate.
Attending lectures, doing assignments.
For most of the week, they couldn't be together—he was just a paper dungeon comrade she could only meet in the club room on leisurely weekends.

"I'm sorry, senior. Today I'm a bit..."
"What? I was really looking forward to it."
"I'm sorry."
"Alright. Let me know if you feel better later."

A real friend would have skipped the adventure for a day to listen to her, but Senior Skull quickly formed a new team with other seniors.

Flash!

With a bright light, Senior Skull disappeared on another adventure with a different party.
Hestia gave a bitter smile.
Somehow, she knew this would happen.
The senior didn't value his relationship with her.
He simply appreciated her mercenary background, rich experience from real battles, and excellent skills—wanting to feel like he was adventuring with a capable comrade.
There was no genuine relationship.

'Should I quit the club?'

Her heart was breaking.
It felt like she was back to when she first entered the Academy.
She was alone.
Before entering.
Right after entering.
Until she met Oknodie, her friend beyond the wall.
Yes, perhaps Oknodie saw her the same way she saw Senior Skull.
Someone to talk to and rely on only when needed.
Someone who only sought to comfort their own loneliness.
A materialistic person.

'No. I'm different.'

She should go find Oknodie.
And ask what's going on.
As she opened the club room door, an unexpected figure appeared before her.

"Hello, Hestia!"
"Oknodie...?"
"I came to observe. Can you play with me?"
"Of course I can."

Hestia quickly grabbed her hand and went inside.
