# Enhanced Translation: Chapter 436 - Practical Application 2

━━━
[Combat Function Tech Tree]
-Monday 3rd and 4th periods, 2PM~6PM
-Professor: Armstrong
-Knight Department, Liberal Arts
━━━

The Combat Function Tech Tree lecture teaches how to link multiple functions to a single judgment to accomplish actions more efficiently and easily. Without careful consideration and acquisition of which functions help which actions, the branching tech tree becomes a bare tree with nothing interlocking. Conversely, with guidance from seniors or mentors to select only the necessary functions, you can achieve interconnected functions with great efficiency for minimal effort.

"For this midterm exam, you'll score higher points by demonstrating linked functions on the same subject to gain advantage over your test opponent. First-years, come forward when called."

The massive Professor Armstrong, rivaling Professor Platon in size, took his seat at the judging table as the assistant instructor opened the attendance book and began calling students one by one.

"The first challenger is 'Grando of Consecutive Strikes.' Even to Oknodie, his skills are impressive, right?"
"He's definitely the grade hunter hiding in each class!"

Ordinary NPCs might use two or three linked functions in one judgment. For example, with a thrust:
A straightforward novice swordsman's combo is very simple.

<Thrust>
<Strong Hit>

Combined for a powerful thrust!
It's basically a system where whoever hits harder wins.
A slightly more advanced street swordsman's combo looks like this:

<Thrust>
<Running>
<Feint>

Rushing in quickly, using a feint to bait the opponent's action while simultaneously thrusting at a different body part.
This is a method where the one with the cleverer psychological warfare wins.
Going further, a named mercenary swordsman's combo looks like this:

<Running>
<Kicking Up Sand>
<Feint>
<Action Prediction>
<Thrust>

While rushing in, they suddenly stop before swinging their sword, kicking up a cloud of sand, then anticipating where the confused opponent will move and delivering a strike.
Even with similar feints, the one with superior detail wins.
To become an Academy lower-class student, you need an extra special move beyond even these named fighters.

<Consecutive Thrust>
<Breathing>
<Boldness>

Using a higher-level thrusting function, extending combat time with special breathing, and preparing to counter-thrust while directly evading the enemy's attack.
This is a method where half-baked tricks are overcome by the power of higher functions trained through skill and hardship.
As you learn through lectures, the three-part combo expands even further.

<Advanced Swordsmanship>
<Dash>
<Consecutive Thrust>
<Distance Control>
<Joint Extension>
<Pain Resistance>
<Mana Cultivation Method>

If the first semester's Ranged Weapon Mastery has "Schola, Heir of the Divine Archer," then the second semester's Combat Function Tech Tree has "Grando of Consecutive Strikes."
He extends his arms to increase the sword's reach while supporting the momentarily weakened strength with mana cultivation.
From aggressive charges to precise sword strike calculations, surprise variable techniques that deceive the opponent's perception, and cultivation methods to counter power reduction...

"Aaaaargh!!"

The Blue Wolf Swordsman Corps examiner's five-function combo left only a scratch on Grando's body before suffering a miserable defeat.

"Amazing! The Blue Wolf Swordsman Corps consists of first-class swordsmen, veterans over twenty years old, yet he directly overcame someone with superior experience and skill!"

Hestia rejoiced as if it were her own victory. Perhaps she preferred the classmates she'd struggled alongside rather than fellow practitioners who looked down on her as a berserker.

"This wounds my pride. I'll step forward to restore the Corps' honor."

The vice-captain of the Swordsman Corps came forward. The students' spirits faltered before the vice-captain's aura as he held a sword with an unusually long blade.

*Slash!*

The students who actually faced him suffered even more miserable defeats. Those who at least understood which functions defeated them were better off. Most couldn't even comprehend what had hit them or what had just been demonstrated.

"Hestia. Did you see that?"
"I did. It's no joke."

The vice-captain's thrust combo technique was quite simple.

<Blue Wolf Mana Cultivation Method>
<High-Speed Charge>
<Swift Thrust>

Enhancing his body stronger than others, charging faster than others, and thrusting his sword faster than others.
His overwhelming offense based solely on speed demonstrated the essence of a swordsman who stakes his life on a single stroke, rendering tricks and petty techniques useless.
Professor Armstrong awarded points just for withstanding or avoiding this single strike.

"Surviving against a strong opponent is already commendable. Feel free to challenge if you think you can defeat them. However, be prepared to lose points if you fail."

Though their actual lives weren't at stake, many students avoided direct confrontation with the Blue Wolf Swordsman Corps vice-captain since their grades—as important as life itself—were on the line.
After several students who rashly challenged him were cut down and lost points, this trend intensified.

"Next, Hestia."

While other students moved like livestock to the slaughter, heads down and dragging their feet, only Hestia held her head high with dignity.

"I'll be back, Oknodie."
"Hestia. Don't forget what you learned during homeroom!"
"I just broke through that with pure strength though?"
"..."
"But I'll keep it in mind."

Standing on the dueling ground, Hestia fell into contemplation.
The pressure felt different facing him directly versus watching from outside.
The pressure was so intense it could have been included in the linked function list as <Intimidation>!

'This isn't a place where charm or cuteness or such petty tricks would work.'

But thinking carefully, Professor Mahabharata's charm evaluation criteria weren't about cuteness or petty tricks.

-Professor... can the calluses on my fists be considered charming too?
-Of course. Nothing is more charming than blood and sweat, so Miss Lotto passes as well.

The Imperial Fighter Lotto, who hadn't stopped her efforts, though not to Hestia's extent.
Her reason for training was somewhat sad—after being humiliated right after enrollment, abandoned by fellow Imperial nobles, and having awkward relationships even with those from the frontier, she had nothing else to do but train...
Anyway, the lonely Lotto who trained diligently every day without fail!

'Yes, these fists and arm strength, these techniques ingrained in my body through daily consistent training—these are my charm.'

Hestia took her stance, gripping a large axe and pulling it inward.
Her horizontal swing, like splitting wood, extended toward the Blue Wolf Swordsman Corps vice-captain's thrust.

<Maid Mana Cultivation Method>
<Superhuman Strength>
<Power>
<Reckless Charge>
<Fierce Slash>

It didn't matter if she wasn't fast enough.
It didn't matter if she couldn't block the thrust.

"A mutual kill?!"
"Is that first-year insane?"

If her trained body could withstand it, she wouldn't fall in one strike.
She could definitely take it with her body.
So she continued her own trajectory to the end.

'This must be the charm you spoke of, Professor Mahabharata!'

The slash unfolded as she took the thrust with her body, trusting in her diligent charm (strength).
The impact tore through Professor Armstrong's protective barrier covering both of them, inflicting damage on each.

"What a reckless one. Were you planning to die together?"
"I believed in my body. That it would endure."
"In a real battle, it would have been more painful and agonizing. You didn't lose strength to pain only thanks to the professor's barrier."
"You were lucky too, Vice-Captain. Without the barrier, my strike would have severed your waist."

Hestia's uniform was torn in a circular pattern at her side, revealing a 20cm purple bruise.
Meanwhile, the confident vice-captain's clothes split at the waist, blood pouring out.
The wound was only 10cm long.
But it was deep.
Hestia's superhuman strength had been powerful enough to penetrate the barrier and wound bare flesh.

"You added the essence of thrusting to your slash, penetrating with energy like an ambush. Impressive technique. Hestia, you're exempted from the second semester's final exam."
"!!"
"Wooooow!"
"Amazing. Was that berserker really that strong?"
"Even Grando of Consecutive Strikes couldn't do that, could he?"
"Well done, Hestia!"
"Thank you for avenging us!"

Returning amid a shower of praise with her final exam exemption, Hestia's joyful steps and gaze turned not to the other students who had doubted and guarded against her, but to Oknodie who had always consistently believed in and supported her.

"Oknodie. Your advice helped me."
"Eh? Really?"
"Definitely. If you believe in your own charm that you've diligently cultivated, you'll never lose either."
"!"

An exclamation mark seemed to appear over Oknodie's face as a powerful realization flashed across it.

"To think a warrior capable of defeating even the vice-captain would emerge. I joined to observe the talents of younger students for future recruitment proposals, but I can't just stand by now."

Among powerful swordsmen and students, the vice-captain had displayed exceptional strength.
Yet with a presence that overshadowed even the vice-captain, a one-armed swordsman stepped forward.

"The one who fought the Sword Saint Reinhardt and the Lily and lost. The one who regained the top position in the Swordsman Corps despite losing his right arm. I am the captain of the Blue Wolf Swordsman Corps."

Just the captain's gaze sweeping once over the students who hadn't yet competed was enough to intimidate everyone.
Professor Armstrong spoke to the captain, not hiding his discomfort.

"Aren't you taking these kids' fights too seriously?"
"It doesn't matter. There's one here who possesses the aura of a sword demon who crosses between life and death."

The captain's left hand pointed with his still-sheathed sword at one person.
At the end of that gesture waited the smallest female student in the classroom.

"Me?"
"Come forward. I'll teach you a lesson."

It was Oknodie, who had been practicing lowering her chin at a flattering angle and opening her eyes cutely.
