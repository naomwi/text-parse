# Chapter 242 - Resolve of Life and Death

Dead Cat recalled.
The power contained in Oknodie's tri-colored ball.

'I've lost one illusion stack.'

When was the last time?
The last time she had lost a stack.
Right...
Second year, first semester, advanced class midterm exam.
This was the first time since then.
Mandela, another advanced class student.
Even when she fell to that girl's hands, she thought:
The Academy is really fun.
Outside, she would have been bored to death, unable to use the abilities she'd worked so hard to develop. Thanks to this place, she had the satisfaction of developing her abilities and growing stronger.
Her impression of receiving Oknodie's tri-colored ball was the same.
It was an interesting power.
The dark mana-enhanced fire would burn her hands like hellfire, and the blue freezing power would freeze any mana attempting to stop it.
A contradictory property that requires mana to block it, yet doesn't allow the attempt itself.
The skill to implant such conflicting attributes without them clashing was extraordinary.
Top of the class.
Truly worthy of being called the pinnacle of the first-year students.

'And she added three more to it.'

The attack coming now was a six-colored ball.
A power with three additional attributes.
Still, the response would be the same.
Take it with her body to reduce its power and secure the ball.
After that, it would be over.
She wouldn't even bother with the sword-wielder paralyzed by killing intent—she would take down Oknodie, the top student.

'If I can just withstand this strike, victory is mine.'

Dead Cat's body was struck by the incoming ball as she moved in a writhing trajectory.

Boom!

Once again, Dead Cat's body was flung away helplessly.
But there was a difference from before.
When Dead Cat reappeared in place after dispelling her illusion, the ball that should have floated above her was gone.
The ball that had struck her body and passed through emerged again in front of her and struck her again!

"!?"

Dead Cat realized.
Oknodie had thrown a truly nasty ball.
"Space Severance" and "Matter Transfer."
A closed loop where a specific section of space was cut and its front and back connected.
That ball never stopped.
It just kept moving forward.
Even if it destroyed all her illusions and blew away her real body, as long as its power remained, it would keep flying at her.

"Nine Lives"

A cat named for having nine lives.
Dead Cat had implemented a similar technique by combining it with magic.
Like a dead cat, she would appear unharmed in place after her illusions died away. The imperial knight who had been counterattacked by her had said:

-Dead Cat...

From that day on, the girl decided on her name.
I am Dead Cat, she said.
A chilling technique acknowledged even by enemies.
Her illusions had nine stacks.
And now, following the second stack, the third stack had been lost.

'I couldn't stop it at all.'

This was the effect of another power contained in the six-colored ball, different from "Space Severance" and "Matter Transfer."
Some kind of means related to "Unstoppable" that maintains the speed and effect of an object at its peak.
Without figuring out its identity and secret, nothing would change even if she wasted several stacks.
A strike that cannot be received without wisdom.
Who wouldn't be surprised that a mere first-year student could throw such a ball?

'She must have figured it out from the first strike.'

Her developed "Nine Lives" had been analyzed from the previous attack, and Oknodie had come up with a perfect counter.
This was a challenge.
Just as I figured out your move, can you figure out mine?
The fourth stack disappeared.
Still, she couldn't understand.
Any more waste would be dangerous.
She received the ball with both hands full of concentrated mana.

Bang!!

She was hit.
The flame carried by the blue metal ball exploded and bounced off.
A trick that defied the assumption that the attributes in the six-colored ball would act the same as those in the tri-colored ball.
Hiding a trap that explodes upon contact with external mana.
The fifth stack disappeared.
Her margin was gone.

Thud thud

Almost simultaneously with the attack she was receiving with her hands, collision sounds came from somewhere behind her.
Her keen hearing claimed it was the sound of a ball colliding with a wall.
Strange.
The ball was right in front of her.
Why was there a sound from behind?
It was almost like her own "Nine Lives"...?

"!!!"

The sixth stack disappeared.
And the moment of realization arrived.
Beyond being analyzed.
She had been completely read.
Despite showing it only once right before her eyes.
The principle of her ability.
The method of manifestation.
All of it had been caught up to.

'This isn't fun.'

This wasn't enjoyable.
This wasn't the pleasure of defeating a strong opponent that she desired.
One-sided hunting wasn't here.
Even if it was, it wasn't for her.
The formidable opponent before her.
It was for that monster.

'This is too much.'

She felt it.
What would happen if she gave that girl a "next chance."
Everything would be taken.
Without leaving a single thing.
She would use everything shown as her own technique.
There was only one chance.
To overcome that girl.
She had to end it with the strongest killing move that would never allow a second chance.

'It doesn't matter if you don't understand the principle.'

She would respond with a strike that doesn't require understanding.
The seventh stack's disappearance.
The eighth repeated offensive.
Simultaneously, Dead Cat unleashed her power.

"Mana Overexpression"
"Domain Declaration - Mana Vacuum Zone"

The explosive release of power that doesn't allow the manifestation of any magical abilities.
In a normal environment, natural mana that would have helped manifest magic instead steals mana from manifested magic, canceling the magic manifestation.
An advanced mana control technique that's impossible without perfect mana usage reminiscent of a vacuum state!

"Mana Blocking"

Hiding mana so that natural mana cannot detect mana to be stolen from within living beings.
This too is a type of advanced control technique solely to nullify the enemy's magic.
Its difficulty was comparable to "Dark Blocking," the superior technique to "Pain Blocking" that Oknodie had demonstrated earlier.

Whoosh.
The energy imbued in the ball dissipates.
This should do it.
If it's just a ball with high physical force, she can stop it with one illusion stack.

'Perfect.'

Stop the ball with the eighth stack and defeat Oknodie with a decisive strike before her real life is threatened.
Before the instructor can intervene.
Faster than anyone else.
The calculation was complete.
Success rate over 70%.
If she doesn't make a mistake, Oknodie won't just lose the match—she'll actually lose her life.
She might even be expelled.
But that doesn't matter.
Because she'll leave after defeating the greatest talent.
The time until graduation.
Trading it all for a moment of victory.
This exchange... not bad at all.
Oknodie.
There's value in the pleasure of taking down this genius.

'All that's left is execution.'

The moment of reproducing the answer drawn in her mind exactly as if replaying it.
It's the time to confirm with her own eyes and feel with her whole body that her answer was correct.

That's how it should have been.

But an impurity she had initially excluded from her perfect calculation intervened.

"Eastern Killing Sword Technique"
"Flash Strike"

A single strike delivered with a flashing light.
Its speed and trajectory were undoubtedly aimed at her neck.
She had to choose.
Would she block the ball with the eighth stack and unleash her killing move to take Oknodie's life, then die by Xing's sword?
Or would she block the ball with the eighth stack, give up the counterattack, and prepare for Xing's strike?

Bang!
The eighth stack disappeared.
With all stacks gone, the physical force carried by the six-colored ball was dissipated.
The hand that should have struck the ball floating in the air ignored it.

Clang!

The blade broke in half.
Dead Cat had chosen to prepare for Xing's strike.


* * *


Death.
Death.
There was no way to avoid death.
Dead Cat.
A second-year advanced class student.
The strength of that cat beastkin was unpredictable.
Xing felt fear.
He would be killed the moment he charged in.
Realizing this by instinct, Xing couldn't move his hand at all.

'How can she block it?'

Looking at Hestia's hammer, he couldn't understand.

'How can she even try to receive it?'

Watching Son O-cheon's block with both hands, he fell into confusion.
They weren't weak.
But the difference between them and himself wasn't great either.
If he fought seriously...
He was confident he would win.
His intuition was sharp.
He had lived in the Eastern Empire where choosing the wrong moment to draw your sword could lead to death.

"Give her a good hit. That arrogant senior who lives to bully juniors."

The answer to his question could be found in Son O-cheon's bold smile.
He hadn't stepped up believing he could win.
That idiot hadn't even detected the killing intent.
He didn't even realize that the opponent could kill someone like him anytime if she wanted to.
Such clumsiness ironically led to an unpredictable result.

'I see.'

There's a difference between what one can do and what one actually puts into action.
Hestia was just being polite.
She hadn't faced real killing intent.
Son O-cheon was a coincidence.
Dead Cat hadn't even included the possibility of him being an interference in her calculations.
What about himself?
The formalities were over.
The coincidental interference had happened.
His case was different from the two.

'I might really die.'

For revenge for his sister, to return home and kill those who needed to be killed.
He should put away his sword now.
He should quietly watch Oknodie take the hit and be content with second place.

'But I refuse.'

He widened his stride, timed to match the ball striking Dead Cat's body.
His sword extended.
The next moment, the blade shattered and three large streaks of blood were carved across his chest.

'So this is it.'

The premonition of death.
The moment he had constantly felt and wanted to avoid.
But he had avoided it.
His sword was broken and his chest severely wounded, but.
He hadn't lost his life.
His intuition.
Dead Cat's killing intent.
Had caused a result different from the calculation.

"Xing. You're out."

Forced return.
Appearing outside the court, Xing gave a hollow laugh.
In the moment of taking the optimal stride with desperate resolve.
He hadn't even realized he had crossed the court line.
That had led to this result.
The resolve to be indifferent to life and death had ironically saved his life.
