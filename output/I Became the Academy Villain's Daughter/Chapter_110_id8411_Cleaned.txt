# 110 - A Deal with the Senior

Everyone was gripped by fear.
Compared to the irritating second-years whose experiences made them act so annoyingly, this was on a completely different level.
The third-year senior before them evoked a fundamental terror that crept up like encountering a serial killer in the city or a mass murderer in the forest.

"First-years."

The senior spoke with a rough, cracked voice.

"Advanced class."

Just as Gisele had recognized their year from the emblem on his collar, the senior also discerned Isabel and Gisele's year from the emblems on their collars.

"Three bodies."

At his words—counting the three Red-Toothed Mushrooms carried on Isabel, Gisele, and Son O-cheon's shoulders—everyone except Oknodie became extremely tense.

"If they've decreased, they must be replenished."

The three students, excluding Oknodie, imagined a cruel scenario where first-years who dared enter the third-years' secret facility would end up trapped in those partitions.
Oknodie quickly grabbed Isabel's hand as she reached for her dagger.

"Does size matter?"
"I can overlook that much."
"Oknodie! What on earth are you saying?!"

Isabel's heart sank more from Oknodie's comment than from the senior's ominous aura.

"Why would you go in there?!"
"What? Why would I go in there?"
"You were offering to go into the partition in place of one of us!!"

The third-year senior spoke with an incredulous expression.

"Who do you take me for, some petty necromancer?"
"You weren't telling us to go in...?"
"Red-Toothed Mushrooms."
"Oh..."

So he meant we should replace what we took.
When the senior nodded, they all sighed in relief.
They really thought they were about to be kidnapped, imprisoned, and murdered.

"When do we need to deliver them by?"
"By the last day my patience allows."

Gisele also finally relaxed and smiled awkwardly.

"You're quite a generous senior. Thank you for being so understanding. I sincerely apologize for intruding into your facility and attempting to steal mushrooms."
"Hahaha! Senior, aren't you a bit too scary? I thought you were some cultist who sacrificed people."

Son O-cheon chimed in to support Gisele's words.
But one should always listen to what people say until the end.

"If you miss the deadline... you'll go in there instead."
"Haha. Your jokes are quite chilling."

The senior spoke in an extremely serious tone.

"I have never made a joke in my entire life."
"......"

We quietly walked up the stairs in an extremely awkward atmosphere.

"Oknodieeee. I told you, didn't I? Why did you bring us to such an ominous place?"
"Uweeeh. My cheeks huuurt."
"I'm pulling them because they should hurt. Do you know how scared I was? My heart is still pounding."

I was a bit startled too.
Who would have thought we'd get caught in the act!

<Equivalent Exchange Event>
You've been caught red-handed after breaking into a third-year senior's secret breeding ground and carrying away three Red-Toothed Mushroom corpses!
The generous senior said he'd forgive you if you bring three live Red-Toothed Mushrooms, but if you miss the deadline set by his patience, you might end up taking their place.
Would it be better to report this terrifying incident to the student council, ask a professor for help, or just keep your promise?

This was quite a brutal event to pop up unexpectedly.
It made sense that a more difficult event would appear here, since the art gallery wasn't a place first-years typically frequented.
Usually, students wouldn't visit this place until their second year unless they were taking related courses.

"Thank you, Oknodie. Regardless of how it happened, thanks to you we escaped from that breeding ground alive."
"Hehe. I feel embarrassed when you praise me like that."
"I'm a bit annoyed that you're enjoying this so much, but my gratitude is genuine. By the way, you handled the negotiation quite skillfully earlier. Are you familiar with such negotiations?"
"Not exactly familiar, but it's just industry standard. If we hadn't been caught, we could have quietly taken what we wanted, but since we were caught, unless we were prepared to fight, we had to replace what we damaged."

After saying this, something suddenly occurred to me.

"Did you want to fight?"
"How could that be? Unlike our little lady here, we don't have such courage."
"Tsk. What are you saying? I was scared too, you know?"

Gisele flinched.
A flash of guilt crossed his eyes.

"I apologize. I was too insensitive to our little lady."
"Fine, apology accepted. I was tense because some people ask for double compensation, but thankfully he only asked for the original amount. It would have been really creepy if he'd asked for the plastic bag cost in points too."
"...I retract my apology. Sometimes it seems appropriate to be insensitive."
"Gisele, you're so mean!"

I hoped Son O-cheon and Isabel would take my side, but their looks suggesting I was the unreasonable one really hurt my feelings.

"That's what happened. Isn't it too much?"
"Indeed. People lack assassin sensitivity."

I was honestly a bit cautious about complaining to Zuang since I couldn't gauge how close we were, but I was pleased when she unexpectedly received my complaints well.
What have we done together again?
We attend "Adventurer's Night Activities" lectures together every Monday and Wednesday during 5th period, visit the "Room with Double-Sided Binding" at dawn, and meet at the "Secret Training Ground."

'That's enough to become friends!'

I thought that even Xing, the Eastern Swordsman who's considered the most difficult character to befriend, would inevitably increase his likability if he spent as much time with someone as Zuang does with me.

"So how are you going to practice, Zuang?"
"I teamed up with Irene."
"The Northern Duchess Irene??"
"I did a group project with her and Arcadia before. Her ability is convenient."

True, party play doesn't only happen when the protagonist recruits NPCs saying "Become my companion!"
They're people too, so if they can't do something alone, they'd naturally team up with others!

"I use an adhesion spell to bind the mushroom, then Irene freezes it, and in that moment, I dissect it. If you avoid the spore sacs carefully while butchering, it's easy to secure the parts for cooking."
"Wow. That's a great combo!"
"What about you? How will you supply the Red-Toothed Mushrooms to the senior?"
"I'm going to do it now. Want to watch?"
"Really?"

Zuang smiled meaningfully.

"If it were a different class, I wouldn't mind, but we're both assassins. I might be able to read your techniques if I watch up close. Are you sure it's okay to show me?"
"I don't mind at all!"

It's not like I have some amazing technique like Zuang and Irene's combo.

"First step: Find other warehouses where Red-Toothed Mushrooms might be!"

Zuang wasn't particularly surprised by the warehouse search, perhaps because she and Irene went through a similar process.

"Ah, it's a smuggled goods storage."
"Potion storage? Ugh, gross. These aren't potions but moonshine stored in potion bottles."
"Sniff, sniff. There are lots of blankets and a strange smell, but no mushrooms!"

Zuang, who had been quietly following, found it amusing.

"Why do you keep searching like this?"
"The spells and places that second-year seniors hide are all pretty similar!"
"This is ridiculous. When do you plan to find the Red-Toothed Mushrooms?"
"Huh? I've practically found them already."
"What?"

I called out to a passing second-year senior carrying smuggled goods and moonshine from the storages we'd just found.

"Senior!"
"Huh? That voice... Ugh. Oknodie."

I ran into Bigstone, the dumbest and most gullible of the seniors—Bigstone, Rizona, and Moss—whom I see regularly in the Eye for Quality lectures.

"It's useless to ask for past exam papers. I won't sell them to someone as smart as you because I'd lose in the grade competition."
"I'm not interested in past exam papers. I want to know about the warehouse where Red-Toothed Mushrooms are kept. Would you like to exchange information?"
"Ha. You cheeky little brat. Already eyeing mana cultivation? If the student council catches you, you'll get bombarded with penalty points and dragged into forced council errands."

Zuang seemed interested in hearing about the forced errands, but I wasn't interested in paying for information I already knew.

"Look, I brought trading goods!"
"These are... the 2000ml beakers, 1000ml distillation flasks, and filters stolen from the 'Let's Learn Alchemy' lecture. And is that moonshine in the beaker?"
"Yep!"
"Oknodie. Tell me honestly."

Senior Bigstone glanced around, then leaned in close and asked in a cautious, low voice.

"You used to be a thief outside, like Professor Bronze, right?"
"No, I wasn't!"

How dare he treat me like a thief!

"If you don't want to buy, just say so."
"No, who said I didn't want to? I'm grateful. I find those cultivation farm guys annoying, but I know of one place where I cultivate them secretly because I'm afraid the student council might discover my identity. Want to trade for that location?"
"...Oknodie. Are you planning to get caught again and then make another deal? Why not just ask him to get you three Red-Toothed Mushrooms?"

Last time we got caught because we were all together. If I were alone, I'm confident I could commit the perfect crime with "Hiding."

"That's what she says."

I can't push it when my friend is worried!

"Fine. I'll get you three right away."
"In exchange, I'll tell you about either the smuggled goods storage or the moonshine storage."
"Then the moonshine."
"Senior Bigstone, didn't you get affected by the truth-telling side effect in the alchemy lecture last time? The professor might be happy if you return the stolen items."
"I'm scared to approach the alchemy professor in case I end up in the lab. The third-years are working without sleep, drinking mental awakening potions, and it's really creepy when you make eye contact with them."
"That could be true!"
"You don't know what you're talking about. They become like addicts with weird, shiny eyes that glow fluorescent in the dark."
"Oh! I'm planning to buy that glowing eye effect later."

Bigstone was speechless with disbelief.
Zuang took the opportunity to add her own comment.

"Senior, you're quite timid. People like us normally function on just one hour of sleep a day."
"What kind of people are 'people like you'...?"

Zuang pulled out a dagger from her sleeve, twirled it around, and threw it with a swish.
A passing moth was pinned to the wall and died, causing Senior Bigstone's complexion to worsen considerably.

"This kind of person?"

Bigstone's talkativeness decreased dramatically.
His eyes silently cursed as if wondering where I found another one like me.

"What's wrong with Zuang!"

She may be an assassin who's good at assassination and has a habit of appearing silently from behind, but she's a kind-hearted friend who's even afraid of undead.
Why is he looking at her like that?
