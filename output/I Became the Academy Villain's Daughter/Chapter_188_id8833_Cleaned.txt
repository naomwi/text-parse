# 188 - You'll Regret It

Students who had been attending the Imperial Magic Theory Basics and Understanding lecture gathered at the closed old campus.

"Is it true? That Professor Leape will give us special training?"
"Since it's secretly helping us outside school regulations, the magic message telling us to come quietly without telling anyone must be legitimate."
"Right. She even arranged a competition against those frontier students to give us points. Who else would look after us this much besides Professor Leape?"

Professor Leape was considered the worst professor by frontier-born students, but the best professor by imperial-born students. The students accepted his cold treatment toward frontier kids but warm benevolence toward imperial students without question.

"Ugh, it's cold. When is the professor coming?"
"This is a waste of time... I'm sleepy. I want to go back."

A look of bewilderment spread across the face of a student who was about to leave the empty lecture hall in the old campus after waiting too long.

"Ah! It's freezing!"
"What's wrong?"
"The door is incredibly cold. I thought my hand would stick to it."
"What... are we trapped?"
"I get it. This must be a test from the professor."
"Hmm. That's plausible in this Academy."
"Alright. Let's blast the door open with magic."
"No, don't! Did you forget the message spell?"

Troubled expressions appeared on the students' faces.

[I will provide special training for additional points in the Imperial Magic Theory Basics and Understanding lecture. Gather at the designated location after curfew.]
[If anyone discovers you, you cannot avoid penalties for violating school rules, so be extremely careful with your actions.]

They knew from their seniors.
How painfully costly Academy demerits could be.
Conversely, how many points additional credits could be exchanged for by students at risk of failing.
Just a little.
Just hold out a little longer.
Whether tempted by points or afraid of demerits.
Having missed the timing to cause a commotion and ask for help, they realized something had gone terribly wrong as the cold intensified.

"Professor, this is beyond our abilities."
"The magical power is too strong for us to melt the door's ice property."
"Please let us out now. Please?"

Crack.

The door vibrated.
The students' expressions brightened.
The professor must be opening it now.
Students gathered in front of the door, eager to get out as quickly as possible.
But something was wrong with the door's condition.

Crackle.

The state of the ice door was closer to "breaking" than "opening."
By the time they realized what this meant, it was already too late.

BANG!

Ice door fragments, shattered into thousands of pieces, violently shot out and pierced the students' bodies.


* * *


"That wasn't my doing."
"What? We thought it was your test, so we kept quiet until the end!"

Professor Leape stared at the student with an incredulous expression, then realized that even as a first-year, such a misunderstanding was possible at this Academy.

"Whatever the reason, keeping quiet was a wise decision. If it had been revealed that you were injured while visiting a crime scene for improper purposes, you would have faced demerits."

At the point where multiple students gathered to gain unfair advantages, their conduct evaluation scores would have plummeted beyond recovery.
They were fortunate to avoid demerits by keeping quiet.

"Come in."
"What are they saying?"
"The students were injured by accident while training for the inter-class competition."
"My goodness. Causing an accident in a restricted area during curfew without calling an instructor—these students will be hit with a mountain of fines."
"I bear some responsibility for this incident as their professor. If I had recognized their enthusiasm and arranged proper training facilities with a supervising instructor in advance, this accident could have been prevented. I'll cover their point payments with my own points."
"Professor Leape, you truly are a rare person of integrity. You're the living conscience of the Empire."

The students' eyes also shone with respect and admiration.

"Then I'll leave the treatment to you."
"Leave it to us."

Though the students kept quiet for the wrong reasons, staying silent was indeed wise.

'Message spell. Rapid freezing magic. Both have completely evaporated without leaving evidence.'

Visiting the scene personally and examining where the door had been, he became even more certain.
The steel door the students mentioned wasn't there.
It wasn't an existing door that had been frozen.
The door itself had never existed in the first place.
This clearly suggested the students had been deliberately trapped, yet the exploded ice door had vanished without leaving even a trace of moisture.

'She must be confident.'

She's signaling that she knows his "rapid freezing" technique while ensuring no evidence remains even if discovered.
She's returning his plan in full—more boldly, causing critical injuries to many people and even threatening his reputation with meticulous methods.

'Oknodie... is this your answer to my revenge?'

Pyoi who got involved without knowing anything, and Isabel who fortunately suffered the same pain.
These two students were in a different category from Oknodie.
This incident made it clear.
The magic used was <Temperature Conversion> magic.
The specialization used was <Rapid>.
Additional formulas were <Timed Explosion> and <Volatility>.
The resulting magic cast was 4-circle <Timed Explosive Ice>.
And it was realized with an enormous amount of mana, creating an ice effect more powerful than his own as a professor.
Definitely not a first-year's mana capacity.
Definitely not a first-year's magical knowledge level.
And most certainly not a first-year's mana control technique.
Everything far exceeded first-year level.

-A real assassin, unlike you, can make open threats without getting caught.

As if saying this, Oknodie involved numerous students yet left no evidence.
His fist clenched.
A rough chill rose from his knuckles.

Hissss.

Professor Leape emitted a bitter cold that would freeze a reptile to death.
His eyes sank deeper and colder.

'Fine. Next time I'll target you directly, not those around you. You won't be able to act so impudent then.'


* * *


Irene looked glumly at the hero who had come to find her with an imposing presence.

"Hello. You're Irene, the Northern Archduchess, right?"
"...And?"
"Surprised by my sudden visit? Sorry. I came to ask for your advice."

Hero Ishtar, with her refreshing and confident smile.
She showed off her sense of justice as impressive as her personality, demonstrating why heroes are called heroes, intimidating female students passing by.
Irene also became conscious of her relatively modest assets, hunching her shoulders and taking a small step back.

I don't want to stand close to her.
Whether aware of this feeling or not, the hero stepped forward and tried to grasp Irene's hand.
A fierce cold wave rose from the hand that lightly brushed her off.

"Don't touch me. That's not proper etiquette toward a frost mage."
"Oh, I'm sorry if I made you uncomfortable! I just really wanted to ask for your help and wanted to make a good impression."

Frost mages can unconsciously freeze anything that approaches them.
Casual contact can cause unwanted accidents for both the person making contact and the frost mage themselves, so taking defensive action is natural, but...

'I felt resistance.'

The hero diverted the freezing magic into the air before it even touched the back of her hand.
Like someone with a license to approach anyone without reservation, the hero who had no worry of getting hurt didn't hesitate to make physical contact with mages.
Her chest and attitude were both equally annoyingly confident.

"Actually, I heard that a group of students were hit by freezing magic and ended up in the medical wing. It's estimated to be at least 3-circle magic, and when it comes to freezing magic experts in our year, there's no one better than you, Irene."

Irene deliberately suppressed her dislike.
Joint top of the year.
A skilled practitioner who stayed one step away from faction fights.
Owner of an almost irritating level of confidence.
She put all that aside for now.
The impoverished North.
If she wanted the hero's help someday, she needed to make a good impression now.
Human relationships begin with the needier person making concessions.

"Alright. I'll help you. But only as much as I can. And I'll stop immediately if it interferes with my assignments or exams."
"That's more than enough."

The hero is one tasked with a mission.
Though charged with eliminating the greatest threat of the era, she needs the support of many to fulfill this mission.
The money needed to equip oneself to slice off the Demon King's head is no small sum.
Securing wealthy sponsors is essential.
And securing such sponsors requires proving one's qualities as a hero.

-Hey, this guy isn't going to take the money and run, is he?
-We gave him expensive equipment to defeat the Demon King, but he got robbed by a highway bandit?

To avoid such ridiculous situations, sponsors request subjugation missions to verify skills, and heroes themselves prove their qualifications.
These qualifications include demonstrating trustworthiness toward sponsors, responsibility as a hero, and positive influence.

'A genuine hero chosen by the gods is indeed different from those fake heroes out there.'

Unlike heroes chosen by gods, self-proclaimed heroes may imitate a hero's tasks but rarely imitate their positive influence.
Because it doesn't pay, doesn't help their career, puts their lives at risk without adequate compensation.
This case is no different.
The hero wasn't receiving compensation from anyone.
Yet she was concerned about the injured students and sought help to find the culprit on her own.
Even Irene's frozen heart softened a little.

"Judging from the damage, the explosive power of the ice door was considerable. However, the explosion path wasn't directed above the neck. It was a meticulously calculated performance."

Irene conducted a thorough scene investigation.

"The additional formula used here is <Path Restriction>. Balancing power and non-lethality is quite contradictory and requires a lot of effort."
"Does that mean the culprit is highly skilled in freezing magic?"
"I'm certain they received high-level professional training for an extended period. With insufficient skill, the power would normally decrease due to path restriction. Or there would have been fatalities despite the path restriction."
"What kind of facility provides such training?"
"Regular mages of the Blue Magic Tower. Academy instructor level or higher. If it's a student, they would have to be an upperclassman close to graduation."
"...Why would such a person target first-years?"
"I wouldn't know. But given there were no fatalities, this can be seen as a 'warning.'"

Hero Ishtar gained a hint from Irene's assessment.

"All the injured students were attending Professor Leape's <Imperial Magic Theory Basics and Understanding> lecture. And... the only lecture where these students might have made enemies is the <Basics and Understanding of Mana Usage> lecture, which has a scheduled inter-class competition."
"That's convenient. We found it quickly. Then I'll step away from here."

As Irene was about to cleanly wash her hands of the matter and no longer be involved, the hero's words continued behind her.

"Oknodie is also among the students taking that lecture."
"...!"
"Aren't you curious?"

Irene's thoughts became complicated.
Could that child really have done this?
And if she did, what would that mean?

"What if it was that child's doing?"
"She couldn't be a candidate for an ally. And..."
"And?"
"She would have to be held accountable."

The hero's blade began to point at Oknodie.
Now Irene had to make a choice.
Should she help the hero?
Should she tip off Oknodie about this?
Or should she close her ears, remain silent, and draw a line saying this has nothing to do with her?

-Your palm is red indeed. Look at your palm. My magic causes frostbite more easily the closer you are.
-Aren't you going to scold me?
-Haven't you already been taught a lesson with your whole body? That you shouldn't approach a frost mage carelessly.

Suddenly, she recalled Oknodie's bold action from Professor Platon's previous lecture, when she had clung to Irene's back while Irene was using her full power.
A distance that normal people couldn't approach due to the unbearable cold, and even if they could, they would freeze to death from the unconsciously emitted magical wind.
Oknodie had clung at close range, breaking through multiple layers of detection measures set up to prevent safety accidents.
Despite such boldness.
And then looking cautious like a child being scolded.

'With that child's resistance to cold...'

Perhaps Oknodie really is the culprit.
What should she do about that child...
Suddenly, she found herself weighing the hero against Oknodie.
And she realized.
A talent that could help the barren North in the future.
It didn't necessarily have to be the hero.
If she could get help from Oknodie, who was as strong as the hero, she might not need the hero at all.

"...I don't think Oknodie did it."

Her decision was almost instinctual.
She had chosen unconsciously.
That Oknodie would be more helpful than the hero.
That she was a more trustworthy being.
No, perhaps a being she wanted to trust.

"Why are you lying? When you thought it might be her."
"...!"

And now the hero had noticed this fact as well.

"That's disappointing. I thought we could be friends."
"..."
"Thanks for your help. In return, let me give you one piece of advice."

A threatening glint flickered in the hero's golden eyes.

"I'd like you to stay away from that child."
"What if. What if."
"Yes?"
"I say I can't do that?"

The hero smiled slightly and said:

"You'll regret it."
