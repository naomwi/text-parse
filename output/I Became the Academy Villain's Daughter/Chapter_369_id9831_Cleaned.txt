<369 - Letters from the Previous Young Ladies>

Zuang left the laboratory and headed to her personal dormitory with a pounding heart.

"Zuang. The professor said he'd give points if we carry these belongings to the personal rooms. Want to join me?"
"No thanks. You take the good stuff, Tito."

She easily brushed off the temptation from the Tito family.

"Hey first-year, sorry but this path is off-limits. The Production Department messed up their slime production material mixture, so the entire pathway is covered in slimes... Huh?"
"Who are you talking to? Are you so overworked that you're seeing things?"
"No, really. There was a short-haired female student who looked like a doll with scary eyes at night..."
"No matter how you describe it, that's a ghost, you know? Get a grip."

She easily leaped over the slime-covered streets with her advanced stealth skills and flash step, just as the second-years had warned.

"Run away!! A senior appeared who curses passing students, forcing them to run at his pace for 'physical training' until they collapse from exhaustion!! Everyone needs to take shelter indoors!!"
"Wait, why do you know that in such detail?"
"Kuk kuk. This is why perceptive underclassmen are troublesome. You managed to escape my mana-draining trap that wouldn't let you go until all your mana was drained. Consider yourself lucky..."

She passed by a group of classmates who were applauding Irene for skillfully avoiding the senior's trap.

"Isabel made too much food while developing a new recipe in the kitchen, and she's giving away the leftovers!"
"Gasp. How many people can get it?"
"Ten people. If you run now, you might not be too late!"

It was quite torturous to resist turning back toward the kitchen, but she forced herself to remember her goal and endured.

Creak. Thud.

With unwavering determination to check it as soon as possible, Zuang returned to her dormitory without taking any shortcuts!

"This better be worth it."

The letters she read, in exchange for missing Isabel's new creation, were diary-like letters that the young lady had sent to Jonna.

━━━
To my dear butler whom I miss.

It's already been two weeks since I entered the Academy.
You might think I'm lying if I say I miss the days when we trained together, but surprisingly, I truly do miss those times.
Compared to the Academy's training intensity, those days were much easier and my mind was more at peace.

Why didn't I realize it before?
That even those difficult training sessions were meant for 'entering' the Academy, so naturally they couldn't compare to the training required to 'advance' within the Academy.

I want to go back to those days.
I want to eat the training diet instead of the black bread that's harder than bricks that need to be dissolved with 1-point needles.
Jonna, if I can return to the mansion during vacation, could you cook for me again?
I miss your stew, even with its bland seasoning and soggy vegetables, but filled with sincerity.
Still, your existence shouldn't be discovered by the Academy, right?
I'll cherish these letters and give them all to you when we meet again.

From your brilliant young lady.
━━━

Was this young lady truly a noble?
From her polite tone to her neat handwriting, she exuded the air of someone well-educated.
A model noble lady who remained charming even when spoiled, maintaining her ladylike demeanor even when relaxed.
This was Zuang's first impression.

The assignments are difficult.
My peers are too talented.
The professors think we only take their classes.

The young lady wrote weekly letters filled with relatable complaints and longing for Jonna.
Starting from the May letter after midterms, her emotions seemed to intensify as her once-neat handwriting began to waver.

━━━
To my sometimes-resented butler.

I thought I was prepared, but receiving direct orders like this brings out my true feelings.
Have I truly become a discarded card?
To deliberately lose in the competition assignment against Charlotte.
It truly hurts to have to deliberately undermine all my efforts so far.

Does that child have a butler too?
Did she receive the Foundation's selection and have time and opportunities to hone her talents like me?
The reason why she benefits from the directives while I must endure the losses must be due to differences in effort, right?

I know I can't say it's unfair.
But thinking this is just the beginning makes me cry.
Please answer me.
Tell me that even if the Foundation has given up on me, you as my butler haven't abandoned your expectations.

From your young lady who needs your comfort.
━━━

Trouble had befallen the young lady who had been working hard despite difficulties.
She couldn't achieve good grades.
She was labeled as the Foundation's "discarded card."
The "young lady" who had received education from a dedicated butler due to her special talents had fallen to the same status as an ordinary "lower scholarship student."
The directive saw the limit of her growth potential and forced her to sacrifice for a more talented scholarship student.
Yet the young lady silently endured the sacrifice.
Even while wavering, grieving, and agonizing.
She silently continued her studies.

━━━
To my butler.

I realized only when vacation came.
The Foundation didn't allow me a place to return to, nor permission to meet with you, my butler.
Since I failed to achieve the targeted grades in the first semester finals, I shouldn't complain even if I had ten mouths.
But resentment keeps growing in my heart.
If only Charlotte wasn't there.
If only I hadn't lost those points to her, I could have reunited with you.
I too would have had a place to return to, a butler to meet.

Please tell me.
Is this as far as I go?

If so... what was all my effort for?
If I can't meet my butler, can't return to the mansion, and just live a life to help Charlotte advance to the upper class...
What meaning does it have?

I've decided.
I'll leave the Academy during this practical training.
Then I'll know the Foundation's true intentions.

If I still have value as a young lady, I'll be forgiven, and if not, perhaps I might be able to meet you again this way.

I'll come see you soon.
From your young lady who longs to see you.
━━━

The first young lady's letters ended here.
A young lady who never wrote her name or Jonna's name, believing it would harm him.
With that thoughtful heart, she couldn't bring herself to harm Charlotte despite her thoughts, yet foolishly gambled with her own life.

Jonna had said that all the young ladies before Oknodie couldn't endure life at the Gift Academy.

It was different.
This was the struggle of a human who had become a sacrificial lamb of the directive, trying to reaffirm her own worth.
She had become the master of her own life, and now only undelivered letters remained here, being read by Zuang's hands.
As if trying to understand the hidden meaning between the empty lines, Zuang caressed the blank spaces before folding the letter.


* * *


After a short rest.
Zuang opened the letter box again.

'The next young lady.'

The quality of the paper was different from the start.
The first young lady's letters were proper enough to be placed in a mailbox.
They would become letters with unknown recipients and senders, unable to reach anyone or find a place to be returned to, with no name or address written.
Unlike the neat letters carefully placed in envelopes, the second young lady's letters were a complete mess.

Roughly torn notebook pages.
Handwriting as chaotic as crawling worms.
The content was quite provocative too.

━━━
You're such a bastard.
I knew I wasn't the first, but did you let the previous young lady die so miserably?
You scumbag.
I shouldn't have fallen for your trick of offering food.
Ugh.
Next time we meet, I'm going to kick you right in the groin.
━━━

"..."

Her personality was quite... free-spirited.
The torn notebook pieces of various sizes that followed contained explicit curses and clear violent intentions toward Jonna.
Assignments are tough? Want to hit Jonna.
Classmates are annoying? Want to beat Jonna up badly.
Even the frequency was irregular.
Some weeks she'd stuff twenty scribbled scraps in one week, while other times she wouldn't write for three weeks because she "couldn't be bothered."
Unlike the first young lady who was raised with affection, there were clear signs that Jonna hadn't formed an attachment to this one.
However, there was a difference in the second young lady's letters compared to the first.

━━━
You come all the way here in disguise for a visit and just say what you want to say before leaving? At least give me some money. Stingy jerk.
━━━

He had come to visit.
Taking a risk.
This passage showed that the loss of the first young lady had clearly affected the Foundation butler's heart.
Zuang couldn't help but smile imagining the second young lady grumbling but smiling with her mouth.
However, what awaited at the end was a tragedy that would be summarized as "failure to adapt."
The tomboy young lady's tragedy came in a way both similar to and different from the first young lady's.

━━━
Are you insane?
Threatening to dispose of perfectly normal kids if they don't cause a rampage with dark mana? Is that something humans do?
Cassy might be less capable than me, but she doesn't deserve such a miserable death.
Was it the same with your first young lady?
Asking if her existence was just fertilizer for Charlotte's advancement?
I can answer that.
Cassy is not fertilizer for my advancement.
If you're going to kill Cassy, I'll hide her.
━━━

Unlike the first who was marked as a sacrificial lamb for advancement, the second was selected as the flower that would grow from consuming the fertilizer.
This tomboy young lady couldn't contain her overflowing sense of justice and took action, and no more notebook scraps could be found in the letter box after that.

'They selected the second to overcome the first's weak heart, but her goodness led her to death just the same.'

Should one say Jonna had good judgment, or none at all?
The thought that Jonna might be the culprit who kidnapped the missing Dobi had now vanished from Zuang's mind.
Even if he were a villain, she had a strong conviction that his method of committing crimes wouldn't involve kidnapping students.

'But I can't stand this.'

There were still letters from the third young lady in the box.
The third young lady who was raised right before Oknodie.
What kind of person would she be?
As she opened the letter with curiosity and read through it, her eyes turned cold.
This young lady was different from the previous ones.
