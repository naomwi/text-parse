# 251 - Those with Swollen Livers

Three of the Four Heavenly Kings from the first and second years have been defeated.
From the first year: Imperial Second Princess Mesugakki.
From the second year: The Terrifying Dead Cat and the White Holy Knight Lu.
And now, while Oknodie wasn't looking, another elimination occurred in the upper part of the court.

*Crackle-zap!*

Sparks and lightning erupted in the air, both inside and outside the boundary lines.
With Casia and Blue Fist Io—both electric attribute specialists—facing off against each other, this electric hell was inevitable.

"Ouch, that's hot!"
"I wasn't even close and it's this bad?"
"Ugh, I've got burn marks on the back of my hand."
"Someone's going to die at this rate. I forfeit!"
"I'm forfeiting too."

The spectators and bystanders had long since withdrawn.
The dodgeball match between these two, who had driven three hundred students to flee, possessed a different kind of deadliness than the confrontations involving Oknodie or other students.

"I heard from the instructor. They said a laboratory experiment subject who can wield tremendous lightning power entered the first year."
"...Don't call me an experiment subject."
"Want to wash away your past? Then you should be careful. Even if you wish to escape everyone's attention, there are certainly organizations watching only you. The moment the world's attention turns away from you, they'll snatch you up in an instant."

Exorcist Io.
One who walks the path of faith and has made it his life's work to destroy all unholy beings.
Due to his occupation, he had grown up witnessing the darkness of the underworld that ordinary people knew nothing about, even before entering the Academy.
What he had seen most often were the fates of sacrificial lambs captured by evil beings.
Or watchdogs forcibly leashed.
Casia's abilities and combat prowess would be highly coveted by notorious evil organizations.

"You should worry about yourself, senior. I'm lucky. I have Oknodie in the first year."
"...Oknodie. That child is unpleasantly strong. One Oknodie is enough for the first year."

An electrostatic phenomenon where the instantaneous current exceeds 1 ampere (A).
Just 0.1A is enough to kill a person.
Normally, static electricity occurs and disappears in an extremely short moment, but what if an electric ability user artificially creates an electrostatic phenomenon?
Indeed.
Static electricity becomes fully capable of killing people.

<Raging Thunderstorm>
<Blue Fist>

As savage lightning that could instantly kill a person with a direct hit came crashing down from the sky, Exorcist Io absorbed it by charging his two fists with blue lightning.
Charging his fists with currents that could kill him if his control slipped, adding to their power!
Casia's lips curled into a smirk.
So confident in your skills?
Well then, try to handle this.

<Thunder Calling>

A massive bolt of lightning became a single streak and struck Io's body.
*Boom!*
The ground exploded and a dodgeball bounced up.

<Yellow Metal Ball>

Despite its name suggesting good electrical conductivity, this ball was an electrical insulator.
Toward this ball made of an alloy with very high electrical resistance that doesn't conduct electricity, Io unleashed all the electric charge he had stored.

<Insulation Breakdown>
<Superconductivity>

An insulating non-conductor is a ball that normally doesn't conduct electricity, allowing even electric ability users to hold it without worry.
However, under certain conditions, this property can be destroyed, forcing electricity to be stored in it.
For instance, under high temperature and high pressure.
A ball launched like a meteor by a fist containing energy rivaling lightning loses its insulating properties.
Yellow electricity rises like an afterimage, creating sparks along its trajectory.
An ordinary first-year would die instantly just from a graze.
Casia stood at the end of the ball's trajectory.
She felt no fear.

'Since you can't win with electricity, you're trying to push me out of the court with the physical force behind the ball... Such a kind yet shallow calculation, I don't dislike it.'

But she was not a first-year who deserved such consideration.
Moreover, the energy occurring above her made any such thoughts disappear.

"Dark mana...? The Imperial Princess, the next generation power player, is openly revealing her energy without even trying to hide it?"

Muttering.
Hero Ishtar stood still with an unpleasant expression.
Seeing the anger emanating from her, the expression of Second Year Top Student Mandela, who had been battling the hero, completely crumbled.
If that anger were directed solely at the second-years, the hero wouldn't be a concern.
The problem was that this killing intent was clearly directed at Oknodie instead of the princess who had triggered the anger.

'Is she planning to take out her frustration? Does she think Oknodie enticed the princess?'

The reason didn't matter.
The hero's killing intent made it clear.
She intended to strike Oknodie.
Indeed, there was no rule against attacking students from the same year.
This hero didn't care about her reputation or standing at all.
With a hero going this far, there was no telling what might happen.
So she abandoned her original plan to naturally drop out by relying on kind consideration.

<Magnetic Hysteresis Loop>
<Force Polarization>
<Multi-Polarization Domain>

Detestable laboratory life.
However, the knowledge they had injected into her was real.
Casia opened the door.
The door to knowledge she had known about but refused to use and turned away from.
She arbitrarily changed a certain portion of the ground to have a single polarity.
What if she converted the opponent's electricity into either an opposing force or a similar force, as if marking it?
She could exploit the property that like poles repel each other and unlike poles attract each other.

<Absolute Separation>

The fist charged with lightning-level power couldn't overcome the repulsive force generated by the polarization created on the ground and rose up. The ball, floating upward, circled widely around her following her outstretched hand.

<Magnetic Field>
<Counter Shot>

A counterattack unfolding as naturally as breathing.
The power that Blue Fist Io had unleashed was now under Casia's command, shooting back at Io along with the dodgeball.

<Magic Field>
<Switch>
<Polarity Reversal>

When the ball approaches, she exerts an attractive force between opposite poles, and the moment the ball crosses the invisible magic field, she reverses the force to create a repulsive force between like poles.
The strike and dodgeball, accelerated by this double force, hit Io like a horizontally striking lightning bolt.

*Kaboom!*

The dazzling flash and explosion drew the attention of all students, instructors, and professors, halting all other fights happening elsewhere on the field.

That kid's got quite the talent!

A strike worthy of praise from Dragon Principal!

"Senior Io... is he dead?"
"Look how black the ground is."
"The ground is torn up like it was whipped."

The students trembled in fear, wondering how these could be fellow students.

"Could we have stopped that if we had intervened?"
"Impossible."
"Unless it was Alexo who's specifically assigned to her, not a chance."

The instructors turned to look at Alexo, Casia's dedicated instructor, and murmured among themselves.

"How boring. There's nothing to teach."
"Good performance, but skills that are a bit burdensome to instruct."

Even the professors were expressing reluctance!

"Is Casia that exceptional? Enough for the principal to covet her as much as Oknodie?"

What nonsense.
Of course he was talking about that mischievous one.

"Excuse me?"

In response to First Year Student Director Mahabharata's question, Dragon Principal pointed to the final destination of the ball that no one had noticed.

"Casia! Since the senior is eliminated, can I use this?"
"Oknodie... that thing will kill you if you handle it wrong."
"It's fine, I won't die!"

With some mysterious skill, she grabbed the ball that no one would dare touch without a swollen liver, and threw it into the infinite loop zone where four balls were floating.
Unlike her ambitious plan to extract the ball by detonating electricity to create an impact, the infinite loop zone became a hell where crazy lightning-rainbow-ice-balls accelerated with electric charges floated in random orbits due to the explosion's aftermath.
The principal burst into laughter while Mahabharata was dumbfounded.
The balls were now moving at such insane speeds that the issue was no longer about retrieving them, but about containing and eliminating them without letting them escape.

"Should I stop it?"

Leave it be.
That's what makes her a match for the hero.

But they're in the same year...?
Mahabharata's empty words didn't even reach the ears of Dragon Principal, who no longer cared about year distinctions.
Of course, neither did Hero Ishtar, who was openly displaying killing intent toward Oknodie.


* * *


The princess had used dark mana.
This was a problem of a different dimension than Oknodie using dark mana.
The Empire had begun to be tainted by darkness.
Starting from the very top.
Admiration was already swirling in the nobles' eyes.
If the absolute amount and safety of dark mana were issues, couldn't they be solved with the power of money?
Princess Mesugakki had shown the answer.
How could a princess who had gathered more mana than what mains who desperately collected dark mana would gather in a lifetime be dominated by anyone?
Even if the nobles following her couldn't surpass the princess's dark mana, what was wrong with nobles following the princess's orders?
It only added strength to the loyalty they should rightfully offer.
All the students.
All the nobles.
Thus, the wariness toward dark mana disappeared.

The hero felt even fear.
Because she knew how reckless this thinking was.
Let's say the mains were one thing.
But what about the demons who tempted people to fall into the path of darkness?
What about non-human races tainted by darkness?
Their lifespans far exceed the human lifespan of at most 60 years.
Dwarves live for 200 years and elves live for 500 years.
Dryads live for 1,000 years and dragons live for 5,000 years.
Can one really claim superiority against beings who have accumulated dark mana over such vast periods of time?

'They'll become enemies.'

The moment a major figure from the Demon King's army appears in the human realm.
Eventually, all of them will become enemies.
From the Imperial Princess to her subordinate nobles, from the Wiheomhae Foundation's scholarship student to everyone else.
There was only one way to stop it.
To show with her own skills how meaningless it was to immerse oneself in dark mana.
The showdown with second-year senior Mandela didn't matter anymore.
Ishtar had set her goal.
Today, within this game, she would defeat Oknodie.
Thoroughly enough that the Imperial nobles wouldn't even dare to think about embracing dark mana.
