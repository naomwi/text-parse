# Enhanced Translation: Chapter 134 - Training Equipment

1.
After diligently making my rounds through the lower class, I returned to find good news waiting for me.

"The training equipment prototype is complete."
"Already?"
"Logini and the production friends helped out. We increased the heat quickly, which greatly reduced the casting time. Money really does make everything possible. Hoho!"

Arcadia showed me the equipment.
When I tried on the gloves and shoes as a test, they were the wrong size—there was space at my heel, and the gloves were loose like I was borrowing an adult's. However, the weight felt very similar to what I had worn at Jonna's mansion.

"This is it!"
"I'm glad you're satisfied. But is it really possible to go about daily life wearing such heavy training equipment?"
"You get used to it somehow and it becomes fine!"

This world has status windows to begin with.
It's a game world with ability stats.
The Gift Academy students, who possess abilities far superior to ordinary people, would greatly benefit from high-weight daily life training as they could easily adapt to their abilities.

'Well, it's nothing compared to what Jonna made.'

It lacks an ergonomic feel.
The oversized gloves and shoes press against my skin and cause friction.
There's no auto-balance function that adjusts the center of gravity in real time.
Of course, expecting such features from training equipment made by first-years, and a prototype at that, would be daylight robbery. If I had any sense, I couldn't complain to Arcadia, Logini, and the other first-year production students who worked so hard!

"Is it okay if I take this to Mob?"
"Go ahead. The size won't fit so it'll be uncomfortable, but you can manage by padding it with cotton, fabric, or cloth like you would with armor."

But I hadn't thought about inner padding, which was becoming another concern.

'I'm giving this to a newbie, but what should I have him wear under it?'

No way. The best way to hook clueless newbies is with expensive gear.
If you start a hobby by splurging on expensive equipment, you'll work hard if only to justify the cost.
Just like how Koreans typically begin any hobby—hiking, fishing, cycling, whatever—by first buying all the top-tier equipment. Even though most people end up carefully storing their expensive toys, passing down full plate armor to their descendants like family heirlooms.
At least I don't need to worry about the inner padding for the training equipment ending up abandoned in a closet or storage box.
After all, fools who are lazy and don't understand their desperate situation despite being on the verge of failing can't survive even a single semester at Gift Academy.

'Mob is quite diligent.'

Despite his unimpressive appearance, Mob is actually quite healthy, and he's been training hard enough to develop dark circles under his eyes from reduced sleep.
Seeing such dedication, I shouldn't let him feel inferior to equipment from neighboring lower classes.

'Alright. I'll get one custom-made!'

Since everyone put effort into making the training equipment, I should at least handle the inner padding myself.


* *


Professor Platon was more serious than anyone about developing iron men.
Due to his excessively harsh educational curriculum, he wasn't very popular with students, and none ever visited him outside of lecture hours.
Most students would panic and avoid him when making eye contact, fearing he might try to feed them protein!

"Hmph. Weak ones."

The professor didn't mind.
The true state of an iron man isn't something those cowards could achieve.
A bit of loneliness.
A bit of desolation.
Only those who can turn even those feelings into materials for growth and training, who constantly test their limits through physical transformation, can take another step closer to becoming an iron man.
Just as he claimed there was no end to learning, Professor Platon devoted himself to personal training daily, even after achieving a statue-like physique.

"Hello, Professor!"

But today, something impossible and unimaginable happened during his usual training.
A student appeared, seeking him out personally and greeting him.
Professor Platon continued his weight training without stopping and said:

"I don't accept grade appeals."
"We haven't even had midterms yet!"
"Is it about assignments then? I don't accept appeals for assignment evaluations either."
"I didn't come about assignments either!"
"Then why are you here? If you're requesting me to be a club advisor, I must decline as I'm already overseeing the 'Superhuman Smashing Club' and the 'Thousand Steps of Physical Response Club'."
"I came to ask if you know where I can find cotton armor!"
"Cotton armor?"

He had thought it was an upperclassman desperate to graduate, nitpicking about scores and asking for higher grades, but now he was hearing something about cotton armor.
The pure voice unlike an upperclassman's had seemed strange, and indeed, it was Oknodie, a first-year child whose baby hair hadn't even fallen out yet.

"My friend's training equipment doesn't fit, so we need armor to pad it."

With her shy smile and cute manner of speaking, she looked just like a little fox.
When she grows up, she'll probably break many men's hearts.
As long as she doesn't undergo a dark evolution like the Mesugakki princess with her "pathetic, pathetic~♡" teasing, whatever she becomes, she's a female student with a very promising future!

"Could you show me the training equipment?"

Professor Platon, who wasn't particularly kind to students, showed a soft and gentle expression to Oknodie, a first-year whose future was promising in many ways.
Oknodie excitedly put down the training equipment she had been carrying on her back with a thud.
The small dust cloud that rose indicated its considerable weight even to the naked eye.

"Hooh."

When he picked it up, it provided a slight stimulus to his muscles.
Indeed, these were quite weighted shoes and gloves for a first-year.

"Whoever it is, I must commend a student who's already practicing heavy armor training."

A sword wielded without armor and a sword wielded while wearing heavy armor differ in weight, required strength, trajectory, and stamina consumption.
Students aiming for greatness should prepare and adapt through training like this in advance.
Of course, most students don't do this.
Using the excuse of busy academy schedules, they play all they want, eat all they want, rest all they want, and waste their precious first year like garbage.

"It's for Mob, a lower class student who's super diligent!"
"Is that so?"

If Oknodie praised him this much, he must certainly be no ordinary student.

"I don't have any lectures right now, so tell him to come here."
"Yes!"

Shortly after, a skinny young man appeared.
He had no unbalanced posture from bad habits, and while his muscle quality wasn't exceptional, he showed signs of having trained hard in his own way.
He seemed to have talent in mental cultivation as well, as his muscles were quite compressed from instinctively manifesting magical power.

"You have talent."
"Th-thank you...?"

Professor Platon's hand lightly tapped Mob's forearm, abdomen, and thigh.
Mob fell into fear, wondering what was happening. Could Oknodie have sold him to the professor for grades? But of course, that wasn't her intention.

'His physical fatigue is worse than it appears.'

The time needed for muscles damaged by exercise to recover couldn't keep up with the accumulating injuries from repeated workouts.
Despite dividing his training by upper and lower body parts to manage fatigue levels, the fact that he was in this state meant he was truly determined.

"I like you!"

Promising talent for a lower class student and an unusual determination unlike a first-year.
The professor was reminded of himself from long ago, when he dreamed of reviving his homeland, the Kingdom of Florence, spending each day with a blood-draining feeling.
Such a student was certainly worth helping.

"I'll give you cotton made from the Barometz plant."

Barometz is known as the first cotton plant, a "mythical plant." The cotton species was born by planting Barometz, which originally grew in the spirit realm, in the middle realm.
Even cotton that had deteriorated in the harsh soil of the middle realm was still useful for clothing, so one can imagine the value of the original Barometz cotton.
It was doubtful whether first-years would know its value, but surprisingly, Oknodie looked like she was dying of concern.

'Unless she's unusually wealthy, she shouldn't easily recognize the value of this cotton...'

Despite Oknodie's pretty appearance and uniform, he understood when he saw the white gloves she always wore up to her elbows.
She must be a child from an extremely wealthy family.
Professor Platon was so dedicated to physical training that he hadn't even heard the rumors about Oknodie that were rampant throughout the Academy.

Professor Platon is often perceived as a dangerous professor who frequently offers protein soup to students, but in reality, he's quite generous.
If you show interest in physical development, he'll gladly serve as a club advisor and provide various forms of help.
The <Superhuman Smashing Club>.
The <Thousand Steps of Physical Response Club>.
Those two clubs are prime examples.
Out of curiosity, I joined and found they were closer to a strength demonstration club and a fitness club.

"Mob is so lucky!"
"Huh? Me?"
"That stuff is super expensive."
"How much?"
"Hmm..."

After recalling its value in the game, I finished calculating.

"The price of a full set of magic armor!"
"What?!"

Magic armor that even nobles couldn't afford to get permission to wear due to its expensive value.
Mob was shocked almost to the point of fainting at the existence of cotton padding comparable to gold in value per weight.

"What if I lose some? After wearing it, cotton will stick to my hands and feet and come off. Dropping even a single strand would be like throwing away something more valuable than gold!"
"Ah, don't worry about that. Just try it on first."

Professor Platon laughed heartily as he stuffed the cotton into the boots and gloves and pushed them toward Mob.
I thought I heard a crunching sound in the middle, but I hope I misheard.

"Ugh... it's heavy."
"But the fit feels better, doesn't it?"
"Yes. Thanks to you, Professor, it feels much better. Thank you."
"This is nothing. The real thing to be thankful for is coming up."
"What?"
"You were worried about losing the Barometz cotton, right?"

Surely he's not trying to take away the training equipment that turned out to be desirable after making it, using the excuse of eliminating the worry by eliminating its source?
I was anxiously watching from the side, but fortunately, my concern was unfounded.
He wasn't taking it away, but modifying it.

*crunch*

Professor Platon, with no malice whatsoever and purely out of goodwill, forcefully crumpled the wrist and ankle parts of the shoes and gloves to make them fit perfectly.

"Ack?!"
"I've eliminated one of your worries."

Professor Platon said, looking extremely satisfied.

"If you wear shoes and gloves that absolutely cannot come off, you won't lose any cotton. Now you can never remove these shoes and gloves with your own strength."

The professor didn't steal the training equipment.
Instead, he made it impossible to unequip, like a cursed item.
