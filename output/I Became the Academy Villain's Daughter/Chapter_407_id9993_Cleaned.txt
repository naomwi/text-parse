# 407 - True Terror

The baby Kraken's cry made the parent Kraken suppress its rage as it slowly lowered its massive tentacle.
Recognizing that it was deliberately calming its hostility, Professor Red Mountain retrieved his talisman.

Gugugugu.

As the tentacle approached the ship, the protective barrier was compressed and looked like it would burst at any moment, causing even El Draco's adjutant to turn pale.
The professor himself was staring at the tentacle with a twisted expression to save face, but he was also sweating profusely inside.

'Damn, it's incredibly strong. If several tentacles like this attack simultaneously, striking, wrapping around, pushing, and capsizing the ship, how the hell would we withstand it?'

The Kraken wrapped its baby in one of its tentacles and began descending back through the summoning circle that had brought it here.
They had survived.
The tension that had been so intense that he only now realized his clothes were damp with cold sweat finally released.

Kugugugugugu

The massive tentacle was disappearing beyond the summoning circle.
But the baby Kraken clinging to its parent's tentacle kept making "moong-moong" sounds, constantly moving its tiny tentacles and chattering away.

'This doesn't feel right...'

His battle-honed "Danger Detection" sounded an alarm.
The descending tentacle gradually slowed down.
Eventually, the tip of the tentacle began shaking violently like an enraged octopus.
Even without expressing its will in human language, its intention was clear.
Any famous pirate would have studied Kraken language at some point, as controlling deep-sea monsters was every pirate's dream.

[Humans who not only deceived my child but also tried to make it food, I won't let you go!]

"Attack! Maximize the defensive barrier immediately...!"

Before he could finish speaking, a massive whirlpool rose from the seabed.
The ship tilted precariously as a "Deep Sea Great Rotation" swept all the way to the ocean floor, caused by just one tentacle.
Of course, its power was less than one-hundredth of what the Kraken's main body could generate.
But this was the coastal waters, set up for a simple test.
The distance to both the seabed and nearby reefs was dangerously close.

<Secret Treasure - Shield of the Great Barrier>
<Protective Talisman>

Just as the great pirate's secret treasure and the great sorceress's talisman simultaneously enveloped the ship, tremendous impacts battered the double-layered protective barrier.
The Kraken's tentacle had completely disappeared beyond the summoning circle, but this was no good news for the ship being dragged to the seabed by the whirlpool.
As the displaced water rushed back in, water currents crashed against the ship from all directions, pressing down on it.

Crack!

A massive fracture appeared across the entire ship.
This couldn't be withstood.
As soon as they made this judgment, the two professors immediately deactivated the large barrier surrounding the ship and simultaneously deployed multiple small barriers around all the students.
When the students, who had been thrown about and drifting in these spheres, regained consciousness, they had already been washed ashore on a sandy beach by the currents.

"Ugh, I apologize, Captain. I fainted partway through and had to be rescued alongside everyone else."
"I'll hear your excuses later. More importantly, the students. We can't have lost any students. Count them immediately!"

Even though Professor Red Mountain would bear the consequences, Professor El Draco didn't want any of his students to die either.
If he gained a reputation for getting first-years killed, how would upper-year students attend his lectures? And without students, how would he fulfill his "quota" to be released from the Academy?

'Please don't tell me anyone died!'

Professor El Draco silently prayed as the instructors counted the students.

"The count is complete."
"...And the result?"
"We have more than twice the original number of people."
"What? They should be fewer, not more! Why the hell are there more?"
"Um... maybe because of the Meurso Pirate Gang?"
"Classify them properly!"

A moment later, the instructor who had been diligently counting returned with a bewildered expression.

"I excluded the Meurso Pirate Gang from the count, but there are still more people than before."

How could the numbers increase rather than decrease?

"Ah, you idiot. Second and third-years attacked us! Exclude them too and count again!"

The instructor scratched his head and counted for a third time.
Then he scratched his cheek and counted for a fourth time.
He checked the result, looked at the attendance list on his magic watch, checked the result again, looked at the attendance list on his magic watch...

"Say something! What's the problem now?"
"Even counting just the first-years, there are still too many people."

It was enough to make ghosts wail.
The instructor grabbed the scruffs of some unfamiliar first-years and lifted them up to show the dumbfounded professor.

"These students aren't on the roster. Same with several others. Interestingly, many appear to be from the first-year advanced class."
"Why the hell were these lunatics hanging around the beach on a weekend when they should be busy with assignments, only to get swept up in this mess? This is driving me crazy. Any injuries?"
"They were all paralyzed with fear when they got swept up by the wave that hit the shore. They're in terrible shape."
"Huff... huff..."
"What a mess. They're even choking on seawater. Tsk. Start by saving those not on the roster."

After reviving the nearly-dead students, one of them managed to regain consciousness, coughing up water and struggling to sit up.

"Who are you people? What were you doing here?"
"We were..."

The extra students explained their identity and purpose.
They were members of the advanced class who had been mobilized by Gisele, who had decided to settle things with Dobi at the same time Oknodie was conducting her business.


* * *


"A trap?"
"Yes. It's a trap."

At the same time Oknodie was attending her exam, but in a different location, Gisele had spread information that Oknodie needed Dobi's help.
Falling into his trap, Dobi had walked right into a place filled with advanced class students.

"I see some people who helped me during the library expedition. I apologize for the trouble I caused then."
"If you want to apologize, do it for the suspicious activities of the Doomsday Cult using Oknodie's name."

Irene glared at Dobi with cold eyes.
Creating opportunities for more bad rumors about Oknodie, whose reputation was already poor, was reason enough to teach him a harsh lesson.

"All my actions are steps away from the moment of doom. I have no reason to apologize to anyone."
"You've become quite insolent. As dangerous as the aura around your body."
"Will you let me go now?"
"That's impossible. If you won't yield willingly, we'll have to correct you by force."

Following Irene's confident declaration, others began to appear one by one, building up pressure.
Among them were the Eastern swordsman Xing and Mob, both of whom had previously been defeated by Dobi.

"I wonder if it'll even be my turn."

Zuang, sitting on a signpost by the shore and spinning a shuriken, displayed boredom beyond mere confidence.
Considering the lineup that would act before her—Gisele, Isabel, Son O-cheon, Hestia, Dorothy, Rockfell, and others—she couldn't help but feel that way.
This force was clearly excessive.
Yet Dobi wouldn't back down.
Had he gone mad from fear?

"If you intend to separate me from Lady Oknodie... then you are my—no, the world's enemies. I cannot forgive harbingers of destruction who hasten the end!"

At this dramatic outcry, Zuang's eyes widened like someone burned by fire, despite her previous nonchalance.

<Fear Induction>

This was different.
As an assassin, she had often used such abilities to prevent targets from fleeing or to stop them from thrashing and splattering blood.
For Zuang, Fear Induction was merely a tool for convenience.
Dobi was different.
The moment he unleashed his ability, Zuang felt an ominous and terrible fear crawling over her skin like insects.
She had never experienced such fear, not even from her master, the Life-Stealer Elder Rung.
If she had to compare it to anyone, it would only be one person:
Professor Sadako, the living nightmare who could instill fear even in an assassin's heart.

'What does one have to do to achieve this level of Fear Induction?'

Even she, an assassin who killed for a living, couldn't dream of inducing such fear.
Had he made a contract with a demon?
No, even that wouldn't be enough.
Low, the demon contractor she had seen on the cruise ship.
Even Low couldn't inspire this much fear.
This was Fear Induction granted only to those who could take something heavier than thousands of lives.

"The power that brings despair to break one's spirit. It won't work twice."

While even Zuang was frozen, one person gritted his teeth and stood up among the students who were kneeling and gasping or unable to rise.
The Eastern swordsman Xing.
The blood flowing from his mouth was evidence that he had forcibly awakened his body from fear through pain.
The warrior's secret technique <Body Awakening>, opposite to the assassin's numbness, forcibly revived sensations to dispel status ailments!

"This malevolence... I cannot let this pass without subduing it first."

Northern Grand Duchess Irene, with a frost-like face emanating coldness, forcibly raised her collapsing body through transcendent willpower using the magician's secret technique <Mental Awakening>.

"The wise understand without experiencing, while ordinary people cannot know without experiencing. It seems you are no different from ordinary people like me. If that's the case, I must show you."

He would use sympathetic magic to show them the moment of doom.
It felt unsettling to show these memories to someone who had died by self-destruction at that very moment of doom, but Dobi had no choice.
Just as Dobi was about to demonstrate his power against multiple strong opponents, as if he had been selected as a new chapter boss...

'The sympathetic wave has been broken!?'

A massive mana wave forcibly destroyed all magical effects.
It was powerful enough to make everyone forcibly recall those memories, though not quite as powerful as the moment of doom itself.
The owner of this power was the Kraken.

Kugugugugugu!

As the massive tentacle burst through the water's surface, fear struck all the students.

Dobi's Fear Induction.
The Kraken's wide-area Fear.

Under this double layer of fear, even Xing and Irene who had used Body Awakening and Mental Awakening, and Zuang who had been moving by erasing even fear through an assassin's numbness, froze again.
Unlike Dobi's fear which they could forcibly shake off, this wide-area fear came with mana's <Pressure>, making it impossible to even attempt shaking it off.
Before they could overcome this power, the tentacle disappeared beneath the surface, and a massive whirlpool formed offshore.
The ship was swept up, rocked, sank, and then burst out in the midst of the chaos.
With a thunderous sound, the ship exploded, sending a massive wave crashing into the shore.

BOOM!

Needless to say, all the students confronting Dobi were swept away by the wave.
As a result, all students on the shoreline were knocked down.

"Cough, cough..."

Zuang rose, trembling with indignation at this bolt from the blue.
Her blurry vision caught sight of a figure.
Beyond the shoreline, a small child running at an unnaturally fast pace toward the restricted area.

"Oknodie...?"

There was no hesitation or wavering in her steps.
As if she had anticipated all this chaos.
As if everything had gone according to plan.

'Could it be that what was in that tentacle box she carried around for her food catalog collection was actually a baby of the deep-sea monster Kraken...?'

Her puzzlement soon turned to agitation, and agitation to shock.
Whatever the plan was for, this was an excessively dangerous act.
It would naturally result in disciplinary action from the Academy.
What place was she trying to infiltrate that required such extreme measures, and what awaited her there?
If she didn't see it, her daily life would probably continue as before.
But she had a strong feeling that if she just stayed in her comfort zone, Oknodie would one day vanish from her side without a trace.

"Ugh..."

Ignoring the injuries she had sustained when her fear-frozen body was hit by the wave that felt as hard as rock, Zuang forced herself to stand.
The small footsteps she was following belonged, of course, to Oknodie.
