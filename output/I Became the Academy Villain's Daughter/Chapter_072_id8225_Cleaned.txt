# Chapter 72 - The Return of the Terrifying Fifth Period

Wednesday, 5th period (17:00 - 19:00).
The return of Professor Sadako's lecture.
In the "Adventurer's Night Activities" classroom, a coffin was leaning against an ominous-looking large stone wall.

Creeak. Creeak.

Professor Sadako, with her long hair hanging down, greeted Titosoga and Zuang who cautiously entered the lecture hall, making that spine-chilling laugh that never failed to disturb.

"Welcome..."
"W-where's Oknodie?"
"I'm right here!"
"Eeeek! Why are you jumping out of a coffin?!"
"I wanted to surprise you?"

[You hid in a coffin for 3 minutes and surprised your friend.]
[Hiding experience +3]
[Fear induction experience +3]

To be precise, I came to the lecture hall early and hid for 3 minutes because I wanted to gain this experience. When I asked if I could hide in the coffin, the professor gave me a look that seemed to say, "Oh my, do you have the makings of a necromancer?" which was a bit concerning, but I figured it would be fine. Professor Sadako doesn't take on apprentices anyway!

"Hey, Professor Sadako. I've been wondering about something. Why is there a huge stone wall set up back there? It wasn't there during the last lecture."
"I brought in terrain that the third-year students made during their practical session for today's lecture."
"Third-year seniors? I have a bad feeling about this."

Zuang had seen what lectures involving "seniors" were like in the "Ranged Weapon Mastery" class we both took. In the stealth arts group test she participated in, the seniors from "Jeneger's Tactics" had used them for point betting. I don't know the outcome, but after experiencing seniors who treated her like a racehorse, it's understandable she'd be worried about what might be inside the practice terrain made for first-years.

"You've figured it out remarkably quickly, just three days after enrollment. That third-years are all garbage."
"Professor, why is there a coffin set up?"
"I brought it to release the corpse that was inside into that area."
"..."
"Living dead. Minions of the unholy night. A field where the undecaying ones roam."

Creeak.
The professor made that sound again, making me fear what kind of smile might be hiding under all that hair.

"I-I-I want to drop this class..."

Titosoga was trembling so badly her legs were shaking.
Clinging to the light stand, she looked pitifully miserable.

"That's too bad. I was going to give 10% of the practical evaluation score just for going in and coming out."
"Ten percent?!"
"And if you reach the exit under your own power within the 30-minute time limit, you'll also receive a maximum escape success reward of 500 points."
"Five hundred points!!"
"I was going to allow carrying a light for timid students, but if you don't want to..."
"I'll go first!"

Perhaps realizing the importance of points, Titosoga raised her hand with tremendous enthusiasm.

"You can go first."
"Let Titosoga do as she wants!"

Zuang and I waved our hands to send Titosoga off.
Titosoga entered the entrance.
As we waited, imagining her timidly wandering around in there with the sound of the light stand being turned off, we heard a piercing scream.

"Kyaaaaaaah!!"
"...Is she dead? With a scream like that?"

Even Zuang, an assassin, got goosebumps from the realistic scream.
She rubbed her arms with her palms to shake off the chills.

"Don't come! Don't come don't come don't comeeeee! Kyaaaaaaah!!!"

A high-pitched scream that seemed to tear through the air.
The sound of someone tightening their vocal cords with strength in their abdomen but none in their neck would make for a great singer.
A three-stage high note rising from B5 to D6 and all the way to C7 echoed deeply and far beyond the stone wall before suddenly cutting off.

"I want to go back."

Zuang's desire to earn credits and points had completely vanished.
Despite being a malicious assassin, she looked as pitiful as a dog being dragged to a slaughterhouse.

"Time's up."

Professor Sadako lightly held an orb in her hand and projected her will into it, causing the sphere to emit a faint black light.
Shortly after, undead creatures emerged with unnatural, jerky steps.
Pale zombies, well-preserved with embalming fluid to maintain their living appearance, carried Titosoga and the light stand together like hospital patients with IV drips.

"Grandpa... save me..."

Coldly ignoring Titosoga's sleep-talking, Sadako turned to us.

"Now, who wants to go in next?"
"I'll go first, no big deal!"

It might look a bit scary, but once you gain experience, it's not such a frightening lecture.

"Alright... what difficulty would you like?"
"The highest!"

Sadako pulled the lever on the coffin attached to the stone wall all the way to the end with a satisfied expression.

Bang crash boom rumble thud

Like drinks pouring out of a vending machine after pressing a button, the sound of corpses tumbling from the coffin into the area beyond the stone wall could be heard.

"Go in."
"Yes!"
"O-Oknodie? Are you crazy?!"

With Zuang's shocked farewell behind me, I entered the stone wall entrance.
Inside was pitch black.
Meaningless moans of "Uuugh-" and "Grrrr-" from the dead could be heard everywhere.
I couldn't see anything ahead, but if I got down on my knees and crawled around, I could get a feel for the place.
Zombie 5m ahead.
At the next intersection, judging by the faint sounds, there's a zombie beyond the blocked wall to the right.
Overall assessment: an easy class to ace!

[You have successfully escaped the highest difficulty stone wall dark room zombie maze within 10 minutes.]
[Hearing experience +15]
[Detection experience +10]
[Night activity experience +5]

[You have perfectly escaped the highest difficulty stone wall dark room zombie maze without encountering or exchanging attacks with a single zombie.]
[You have acquired the title "Night Visitor".]

[You receive 500 points as a reward for completing the challenge "Clearing Highest Difficulty".]

*Night Visitor*: You passed through the trial of night without causing friction with the unholy minions of the night.
-Title equip effect: Increased undead detection ability
-Title possession effect: Increased detection ability in darkness

Compared to struggling against overpowered characters in the Ranged Weapon Mastery class, this one gives much greater results with less effort, like doing something that suits my aptitude.
After all, practical assignments that can be easily completed with minimal effort are the best in the world!

"Was it surprisingly doable?"
"Yeah. It's super easy!"
"...Okay. Then I'll try too."

Zuang stood at the stone wall entrance with trembling legs.

"Go in."
"Professor? Aren't you going to ask about difficulty selection?"
"There's no reason to ask."
"You don't have the narrow-minded thinking that assassins should naturally challenge the highest difficulty, do you?"
"I don't think that way."

Professor Sadako kindly answered the student who was speaking informally to her.

"Humans are all worthless beings who, when dead, all collapse into cold corpses..."
"What class you are, what your aptitude is."
"Whatever you did in life, it doesn't matter once you're dead."

At the professor's characteristic "creeak" laugh, Zuang crossed her legs as if about to wet herself.
It's too scary to say you don't have narrow-minded thinking because we're all going to die anyway!

"Isn't appropriate discrimination a basic human quality needed for humanity?"
"Are you done talking nonsense?"
"...Can't I go to the bathroom first?"
"You had plenty of time to go to the bathroom. Hurry up and go in. Everyone is waiting for you inside."
"W-wait. Difficulty. What about difficulty adjustment? You still haven't adjusted it, right?"

Maybe if the difficulty was lowered, it might be manageable.
Professor Sadako mercilessly crushed Zuang's hopes.

"Closing the lever won't make the corpses go back in. Once the difficulty is raised, it doesn't go down."
"...Oknodieeee!"
"I gave you the chance to go first!"

In the end, Zuang entered the highest difficulty stone wall.


* * *


And five minutes later, she came running out screaming after slicing through eight zombies.

"That was scary!"
"Eeeek!! You running toward me with blood on your face is even scarier!"

Titosoga, who had regained consciousness, screamed at the sight of Zuang covered in the blood of the dead.

"Looks like you had a lot of fun."
"Evil professor! It wasn't fun at all!"
"The children are saying something too. They say they were very pleased to breathe together with young, fresh-skinned beings in a cramped space after so long."
"...That's creepy."
"They say they'll definitely kill you and eat your entrails next time, so please stay healthy until then."

Zuang made up her mind.
She would absolutely not attend this terrible class from next week.

"...Professor... What was the point of this lecture in the end? All I remember is screaming and fainting..."

Fear instills manners.
Titosoga, who had been speaking as informally as Zuang, carefully asked with a completely stiff face.
Professor Sadako answered Titosoga's indignant question as if it were obvious.

"You learned about adventurer's night activities."
"We just went into a stone wall maze crawling with zombies."
"And you naturally learned how to survive in darkness under the fear of being caught and killed."

Titosoga was so dumbfounded she could only open and close her mouth.
Professor Sadako's cold hand patted her shoulder.

"Don't worry... You're still first-years. I won't lock you in a coffin for two days or bury you in a cemetery to increase your darkness affinity just because you failed the practical."
"Do second-years get subjected to such harsh treatment?!"

Professor Sadako didn't answer.
This is scary even for me.
But if you do well, you won't get locked in a coffin, right?

"I spent 3 minutes in a coffin and it was nothing!"
"How can that compare to two days? And what kind of training did you receive to be able to say that with such a bright face?"

Even Zuang the assassin looked disgusted.
Why is she acting like that?
If you're scared, just don't fail the practical.

"The lecture is over. Good thing you didn't eat dinner before coming, right?"

Unlike the shell-shocked Zuang and Titosoga, Professor Sadako nodded with satisfaction as she looked at my face, which had at least not lost its color.

"I know. That the current level was too easy for you."
"I'm fine with the current level!"
"No. Lectures should be prepared according to each student's level."
"I'd prefer if it was adjusted to the level of the struggling students."

Professor Sadako completely ignored Titosoga's mumbled plea.

"You can look forward to it. After the next lecture, you'll definitely gain confidence in night activities."

I had a vague premonition.
Next week, I'll be the only one attending this lecture!
