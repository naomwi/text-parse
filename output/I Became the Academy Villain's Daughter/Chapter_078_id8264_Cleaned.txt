# 78 - The Troll's Dilemma

Jigoku pulled a single bullet from inside her jacket.

"Hey, take this."
"A bullet?"
"It's a special bullet I've been saving. I'm giving it to you."
"?"
"Sell it for points or carry it around—do whatever you want with it."

She didn't dislike people who didn't value their lives.
But she didn't want someone like that to die suddenly.
Without some luck on his side, he'd probably drop dead somewhere.
That's why she gave him the lucky bullet.


* * *


I suddenly received a favor item from Jigoku.
What's this about?
Does this water-attribute pirate have a thing for good swimmers?

'Maybe she'd like it if I invited her to Professor Sadako's lecture where she summons water ghosts?'

After finishing my swimming practice and changing clothes in the locker room, I was fiddling with the bullet when I ran into the hero.
As expected of the most overpowered character in the 981st class, he must have passed through the river despite the magical washbasin's interference, relying on pure skill without any player knowledge or tricks.

"Oknodie. That was your name, right?"
"Yep."
"Would you like to join my party?"
"Huh??"

I received a recruitment offer even more unexpected than the magic bullet.

"So suddenly? Why?"
"There's only one reason a pirate would talk to a child. She saw your talent and asked you to join her crew, right?"
"Not really?"
"Then what did you talk about?"
"She gave me a gift. A bullet."
"...A woman who goes crazy if she doesn't shoot a gun for even one day gave you a bullet as a gift? Isn't that a recruitment offer?"

Eck, is that what it was?

"Well, I'm planning to board a pirate ship once for an event, but I have no intention of becoming an official crew member."
"Then how about joining the hero's party?"
"Me? Really?"
"Really."
"Why?"

Because I'm top of Group A?
Because I showed exceptional skills in the advanced physical training class?
Because my potential is highly valued at such a young age?

"Because of attribute allocation."
"...What?"
"Sea. Volcano. Mine shaft. The hero's enemies hide in various environments, and the hero's party travels through diverse battlefields. Oknodie is my first choice for a water-attribute companion."

This world's Ishtar was quite an intelligent hero.
Is he planning to thoroughly distribute positions and abilities rather than forming a warrior-only party with five warriors or a thief-only party that's five seconds away from betrayal?
This is quite a sensible idea for Ishtar, who tends to get fixated on strange concepts in every iteration.

"Thanks for the offer, but I'll have to decline."
"Why?"
"There are people I want to hang out with."
"Those people?"

Ishtar pointed at Son O-cheon and Hestia, who were splashing water with pure muscle power instead of swimming, and the other students watching them.

"Yes. They look fun, don't they?"
"They do."

A shadow fell across Ishtar's face.
A hint of nostalgia and loss.
His eyes, which had been revisiting the past, returned to normal as he looked away.

"Let me know if you change your mind later. I'll keep your spot open for at least a year."
"Okay."
"And."
"?"
"The professor asked me to tell you he'll make you free protein soup if you get hungry."
"I'll pass on that..."

I've had protein shakes before, but protein soup is crossing the line!
Don't mess with food!


* * *


Ishtar recalled.
The favors bestowed by the lords of territories he passed through on his way to the Academy, all hoping to become his companions.
The rudeness of arrogant noble men proposing engagements with girls barely four years old, or asking how he felt about marrying them when they were over thirty.
Commoners were no different.
Life reversal, social climbing, striking it rich.
People blinded by desire trying to build even the slightest connection to be accepted as companions only brought heartache to Ishtar.
Among such people were even childhood friends who had grown up with him.

"Ishtar. I heard you made a recruitment offer to that child?"
"Sorry, Yupi. I should have consulted with you first."
"Hmph. As long as you know."

Like himself, chosen by the monotheistic Sophemia, Yupi was chosen by one of the twelve gods, "The Beheading Golgotha."
She was the only one among his friends who wished to become his companion as an equal, without any greed.
Though Yupi pretended to be cutely upset and turned her head away, the cross she carried around weighed over 30kg.

'In any profession, those without strength become weak in both body and mind. While extreme strength like Professor Platon's isn't necessary, some degree of strength is essential.'

Oknodie hadn't noticed, but what this iteration's Ishtar was fixated on was strength.
Though he considered job and attributes, the stat he prioritized most wasn't the essential stat for each profession, but rather the 'strength' stat.
The strength to dive to the bottom of a river, withstand water pressure, and swim against rapid currents is no ordinary strength!
Rumors said she had orc-level strength because she was half-orc, which made her even more appealing.

"Ishtar."

Yupi, who knew her friend's companion selection criteria perfectly, said with a blank expression.

"If that's the case, wouldn't the big monkey beastman Son O-cheon be a better candidate? He's strong and big."
"That would be difficult."
"Because he hasn't learned mana refinement techniques?"
"Because I have a fur allergy."
"Ah..."

So that's why he was terrified when that cute cat beastman girl came to say hello.
One of Yupi's questions was answered.

Thanks to finishing today's lecture early, I was able to attend the second period without any issues.

"Hello, Professor Destroyer!"
"Hello."

The professor looked at my face with an inexplicably uncomfortable expression.
He stared at me with an anxious and impatient look, like a programmer watching a program running without knowing why, which made me feel uneasy too.

"What's wrong?"
"Do you often have lucid dreams?"
"No? I don't usually dream at all when I sleep."
"Then how could you break through the nightmare trial so easily?"
"?"
"...Never mind. Looking at that innocently bright face makes even saying this feel pointless. Let's just start the second lecture."

How silly.
This professor must be bored because he has no friends and just shows off by himself every day.

"Hey, aren't you thinking something extremely disrespectful right now?"
"No, I'm not?"

Destroyer looked at me suspiciously, then snorted and leaned back in his chair.

"In the last lecture, we talked about 'The Sheep That Never Decreases.' Today, I'll tell you another story."
"What kind of story?"
"Today's story is 'The Troll's Dilemma.' A story that will make you never want to travel on foot again."

Thursday, second period, "Adventure Tales of a Retired Former Hero."
Professor Destroyer's second story began.


* * *


About 14 years ago.
During the period when the hero's party was earning merit points by completing various requests including "The Sheep That Never Decreases" in the frontier.
The hero's party stopped at a trade center connecting frontier villages and pioneer settlements.

"Hey, you brainless muscle-heads! Your legs are going to break! What are you saving money for? Let's take a carriage, for fuck's sake!"

Destroyer couldn't hold back his curses anymore.
Hero Nyarlathotep and his first companion, the warrior Alpha.
The hero had divine blessing, but Alpha seemed to have received the blessing of muscles. Whenever they voted on whether to walk or take a carriage, it was always 2 to 1.

"Since Destroyer is having a hard time, how about we decide fairly by majority vote?"
"Walk."
"Haha. I also think adventure is more enjoyable when walking on your own feet. So regarding our transportation method, the majority has decided that we will travel on foot!"
"Damn it."

Destroyer, the second companion of Hero Nyarlathotep, was once again abused by the tyranny of the majority.
A helping hand was extended by a coachman driving beside them.

"Ho ho. It's good that young adventurers are economically minded, but wouldn't it be nice to indulge your companion's whims occasionally?"

The hero's party, which had collected several "Savior" titles and upgraded to the "Benefactor of Rural Villages" title, was heading to a mining village that would take a month to reach on foot.
It was a distance that would make even the patient Destroyer lose his temper.

"Are you offering us a ride?"
"If you escort my carriage along the way."

The kind-hearted coachman offered them seats in his carriage for free.
Three men with shabby equipment covered in dust.
Clients willing to hire such a ragtag party for escort duty were rare.
As a thief, Destroyer had higher social skills than his companions.
So he could tell.
The coachman wasn't really expecting an escort but was showing kindness to people in need.

"This carriage journey feels good. It's going to be very fun. Heheheh."
"...I'm getting off the carriage."
"Are you sensing trouble again? Damn, now I want to walk too."

Whenever Nyarlathotep laughed in that ominous way, trouble always followed.
Though Destroyer desperately wanted to walk, he couldn't bring himself to face a month of camping outdoors, so he mustered his courage.
Whatever happens, we can just deal with it, right?

"Don't worry. Six mercenaries traveled this road a few days ago, so they'll encounter any trouble first. If we see battered mercenaries returning, we can turn the carriage around then."

The coachman's words were convincing.

"Me, troll. Tear humans apart."
"I'm full now. I'll let you play a game."
"Choose one path. If you drive through at full speed, I'll let you live."

At least until some crazy troll at a fork in the road tied five mercenaries on one wide path and one mercenary on the opposite cliff-edge path, proposing they run over one group with the carriage.
