# 271 - The Organization's First Call to Arms

The foolish student who had committed the sacrilege of questioning Oknodie's opinion—and consequently caused all scholarship students to lose their friendship funds in an instant—was met with furious glares.

The scholarship student who had asked the question trembled in fear, imagining the revenge his fellow scholarship students would take.

The Primer felt the same way.

'The atmosphere among the scholarship students has changed.'

That previous sentiment of "Sure, you're strong and favored by the foundation, but so what? We're scholarship students too, and if we unite, we can prevent you from pushing us around!" had completely vanished.

One act of defiance had resulted in the confiscation of friendship funds. What disadvantage would the next rebellion bring? Would the consequences affect only the rebel? Would other scholarship students just stand by and watch?

With so many considerations, no one could act rashly anymore. They had realized the power Oknodie wielded. The hierarchy was perfectly established.

'How frightening. To show such calculation behind such an innocent face.'

This terrifying child had, with a single word, ensured that scholarship students would never again question or oppose her instructions, while simultaneously shifting their resentment onto someone else. Her cruel and clever skill, worthy of the foundation's leadership, proved why she was the "top" scholarship student.

It was time to give up. To stand against Oknodie.

As the Primer's resolve crumbled, the minor event known as "The Scholarship Students' Rebellion" disappeared in that moment. However, at the same time, a notification that should never have appeared popped up for Oknodie.

[Bad Kid experience +3]
[Bad Kid experience has reached the threshold.]
[The "Bad Kid" feature has evolved into "Scary Kid".]

She was no longer a bad kid. She had become a scary kid.


* *


*Scary Kid*: Your every move makes people tremble in fear. It becomes much harder to gain anyone's favor.

Oops.
Was I too immersed in playing organization?
My Bad Kid feature finally evolved into Scary Kid.
A feature with massive penalties for increasing likability.
Ordinary players would immediately think "this run is ruined" and quickly press the "Abandon Game" button or return to the lobby to "Restart."
In an academy where relationships with NPCs significantly impact graduation, becoming someone everyone fears is an extremely disadvantageous event.

'This is going to be a bit troublesome.'

Even as a veteran player, I couldn't help but frown for a moment!

"Is something troubling you?"

My expression must have been quite sour if April was concerned.
I shook my head, returning to a smiling face.

"I just had an unpleasant thought. It's nothing to worry about!"
"Whenever you need help, please rely on me or the Secret Scholarship Society. It would be a waste not to use the organization we created specifically to help you."
"I will!"

Well, what's a little inconvenience?
I've already built up considerable likability through my previous efforts.
I've gotten close enough to the necessary supporting and main characters.
Several professors have also developed special affection for me.
Besides, with veteran player techniques, I can instantly reverse high hostility into likability—the famous "This Guy Was Actually Good All Along" gimmick!
Though it can't be used often, when used well, it can bring all characters from the hostile faction up to "Intimate" or "Friendly" levels at once.

'There's no hostile faction I'm particularly interested in right now, so I'll leave it be!'

Organization among first-years has begun in earnest, but it's still in its early stages.
With the first semester finals approaching, competition is fierce among the lower ranks to avoid becoming dropouts who pack their bags and return home.
Everyone's immediate goal is "doing well on the final exams."
Support for their organization's lower-class students is limited to providing supplies, studying together, or sharing the burden of collecting lecture materials.
In other words, for someone like me who has already stolen the professors' exam questions and answers using the two hat gentlemen, this is the perfect time to enjoy an asymmetrical information advantage!

"If you collect insects that look like this, I'll tell you which title will be offered as an alternative final exam assignment in 'Let's Try for a Title'! For other lectures, well, I'll tell you the final exam questions or answers!"

Insects that I would have had to collect myself by trudging all over the mountains can now be easily gathered by others.

[One-Bite Crispy Mantis]
[Strawberry Black-Spotted Bug]
[White Fluffy Silkworm Moth]

The One-Bite Crispy Mantis that dies with a crunch under your shoe if you're not careful.
The mischievous Strawberry Black-Spotted Bug that disguises itself as a black spot on strawberries to eat them from within.
The Silkworm Moth that becomes even cuter and more lovable as the White Fluffy Silkworm Moth.

All sorts of insects filled the collection box.

[You have achieved Insect Collection Introduction Level 5.]
[You have acquired the title "Elite Mite Fabre."]
[Title effect: All activities consume 1% less stamina.]
[Title achievement bonus: 50,000 points acquired.]

"Oknodie, why are you collecting so many insects?"
"It's my hobby to build collections!"
"I don't care about the reason since you're giving me points and exam questions, but you're really something else."
"Well, strictly speaking, I'm only giving you points! The exam questions and answers are organizational products from 'Play with Oknodie Organization' that you purchase with points!"
"Where else would sell these things, let alone this cheaply? I'll take them gratefully."
"Take care, Rogini!"

Creating an organization of errand runners was truly a brilliant move.
Recruiting April as an ally was a divine move.
Thanks to them, my collection quest has accelerated tremendously.
This is an incredible achievement that would have been unimaginable in a normal playthrough.
Back then, there were no foundation scholarship students, so I couldn't get help with collection quests from so many students.
Above all, even without the "Scary Kid" feature, I was already feared.

-Ahhh! Run away. The All-Strength-One-Hit-Character-Lover Marine is coming to talk to us!!
-All-Strength-One-Hit-Character-Lover Marine. We've received multiple complaints from students. Stop approaching other students to talk. You're too big. It's becoming a burden for instructors to constantly move students who faint in shock to nearby benches or the infirmary, so please keep to yourself.
-All-Strength-One-Hit-Character-Lover Marine. Didn't I tell you you'd get penalty points for talking to classmates where instructors can't see? I'll let you off with -500 points this time, but next time it'll be -5,000!

Ugh.
Just thinking about it reminds me what a harsh academy life it was.
The all-strength build life was truly difficult.
Come to think of it, being possessed as a female character is quite fortunate.
Hero Ishtar is also an all-strength character, but being a different gender, she doesn't suffer the oppressive academy life I did as a player.
All that disadvantage just for being male!

"Gasp. It's the Black Knight."
"He's kind of scary."
"Why doesn't he take off that armor? Is he under some kind of curse?"

Just then, a student who was suffering disadvantages for being male approached.

"Hello, Mob! Did you collect insects too?"
"I ran into a problem while collecting."
"Did your friend get caught by a harpy while climbing a tree and get kidnapped to its nest?"
"...Is that what happens when you climb trees?"
"Then did someone try to find bugs under a rock and accidentally touch the face of a wild golem taking a nap, getting hit in return?"
"I should definitely never touch under rocks either."
"Oh come on. If it's neither of those, what problem did you encounter?"

Mob, who was subtly ostracized by his classmates and always trained alone, eating only protein shakes with straws, stopped his amazement and explained his purpose.

"On my way here, I saw some first-years who were kidnapping silkworm moths get caught by human-sized silkworms and get kidnapped themselves."
"Wow. Even the subquests are properly implemented."
"Subquests?"
"Nothing!"
"They looked like they needed help, so I came to tell you. Since we're an organization, it seemed like a good opportunity to build our reputation through positive activities outside."
"The 'Moth People Attack' event that appears as a difficulty spike in the silkworm collection quest does have an extra character likability +20 as a clear reward! But we don't really need to clear it. If we wait, the instructors will rescue them anyway!"
"Is that so? Then it doesn't matter."

With his armor clanking, Mob sat down in a chair, opened his visor to reveal his eyes, and let out a heavy sigh.

"By the way, how do you know so many exam questions and answers? Don't tell me there's a foundation scholarship student hiding among the professors?"
"That's a secret!"
"...When someone says 'secret,' doesn't that usually mean they can't say who it is, but confirming someone exists?"
"It's really a secret!"

For Mob's sake, I needed to draw a firm line here.

"If someone found out that Mob obtained this information, they might silence you with an 'unfortunate accident during education' where no one would ever know what happened!"
"...I'm not curious at all anymore. In fact, I'd rather not know, so please don't tell me."

I was enjoying my days like this, but a few hours later, I had to jump to my feet when Arcadia came running in a hurry, shouting:

"It's terrible, Oknodie! Titosoga had his spotlight stolen by the moth people!"

Goodness gracious. Titosoga without his spotlight? Such a thing shouldn't exist in this world!

"April. Please call the organization members from the training room!"

The first call to arms for "Play with Oknodie Organization" had been issued.
