# Chapter 227 - Arcadia's Suspicion

Arcadia couldn't hide her dazed expression all morning.
Titosoga and her friends were worried about the young noble lady.

"Tito. Shouldn't you go say something to her? I can't stand seeing someone who was once a faction boss looking so miserable and being ridiculed."
"The lady must be heartbroken. Let's go give her a frilly headband as a gift."

Canillian Truffle, daughter of a lord's family, and Frill, a self-made success—both had become friends with Titosoga through Arcadia's introduction.
Titosoga nodded happily, grateful for her friends' unchanging attitude.

"My lady. Let's eat together, okay?"
"Hehe. Why not?"

To cheer her up after the outing, Titosoga had splurged and offered her the strawberry milk that was served as a snack today.

"My lady. Have this!"
"Thank you, Titosoga."

Lady Arcadia always greeted everyone with a kind and polite smile.
Titosoga wanted to erase the worries from the face of this admirable person who had a different kind of coolness than Oknodie, but her efforts weren't very effective.

"M-my lady..."

Even during lunch, Arcadia barely ate, just meaninglessly stirring her soup in an empty tray—a sight that broke the hearts of onlookers.
As Titosoga looked like she might cry, Frill clapped her hands loudly enough for the lady to hear.

"Oh! We were thinking of hosting a special tea party for you today. How about joining us for some light refreshments after lunch to aid digestion?"
"Why not? I think this is my first time being invited by someone else. I hope I won't be a burden..."
"A burden? Not at all! Having you attend and grace us with your presence would be the greatest honor for me, Frill. Please enjoy the frilly-shaped latte art I've been practicing so hard."

At the tea party, Arcadia sat as a participant rather than a host and calmly enjoyed herself.
She complimented Frill's crooked frilly-shaped latte art, calling it cute, and calmed Titosoga, who was nervously holding her cup with shaking hands, by showing her how to support her arm with her other hand to stabilize it.
Arcadia was mature and caring.
However, during the gaps between conversations, her gaze would drift vacantly to her teacup, the patterns on the table, or into nothingness.
As Frill and Titosoga were becoming gloomy in sympathy, Canillian Truffle, the most strong-willed of the trio, spoke directly.

"My lady, you have something troubling you, don't you?"
"I'm sorry. It must look bad."
"Not at all. You're human too—of course you have worries. I understand."
"Thank you. I suppose it's a difficult time to relax, and I've spoiled your kind consideration."
"Please don't think we can't understand your concerns. Titosoga and Frill may be clumsy, but they want to help. So do I."

Perhaps it was the reward for standing by her side during difficult times and protecting her from malicious rumors. Arcadia's heavy thoughts finally surfaced.

"Actually, I have concerns about Oknodie."
"Oknodie? Gasp! Could it be..."

Titosoga's face turned pale, as if she had some idea what this was about.

"Did something happen with you too, Titosoga?"
"If Oknodie is trying to persuade you to attend Professor Sadako's lectures together, please shake your teacup!"
"Pfft! What's that about? That never happened."
"Hehe! That's such a noticeable signal, just like Titosoga who carries around a spotlight."
"Frill's frilly dress decorations are just as flashy!?"

While Titosoga and Frill were having their cute argument, Arcadia's concerns weren't as light as their banter.

"Actually, during our last outing, I needed a large amount of points, so with Oknodie's help, I managed to complete a difficult task."
"And then?"
"After finishing the job, I happened to see Oknodie's point balance, and I was shocked by the enormous amount."

Over two million points.
It was more than enough to pull someone out of a desperate situation.

"Are you perhaps hoping that Oknodie would use those points for you?"

Unlike the straightforwardly innocent Titosoga and the relatively gentle Frill, Canillian Truffle—though from a lord's family—struck with unparalleled sharpness.

"Shamefully, I did feel such hesitation."

With that many points, she wouldn't have needed to go on the outing.
Why hadn't Di used those points for her?

"It would be a lie to say I never had such immature, selfish thoughts."
"You have other concerns about this."
"The fact remains that Di risked her life to help me with a dangerous mission."

In the end, Di had been a great help during the outing, allowing her to seize the slush fund that her family had accumulated through unfair methods.

"If you don't mind, could you tell me what happened?"

Arcadia hesitated.
Could she trust these children?
She had already experienced betrayal from many of her followers.
After a brief hesitation, she decided she wanted to trust these three who had stayed by her side until the end.

"...This is what happened."
"That professor is terrible! 500,000 points—an ordinary student could never have paid that. The professor must hate you!"
"500,000 points equals 5,000 gold coins... Even for me, who's earned considerable money through Frill development and business, that's a burdensome amount."

While Titosoga and Frill were either angry at the professor or shocked by the enormous sum, only the ever-calm and collected Canillian saw through to Arcadia's true concerns.

"You're suspicious of the professor and the Academy, aren't you?"
"Huh? Why would the lady suspect the professor and the Academy?"
"Tito. Just eat your cookie and listen quietly."

If that fool asked questions whenever she was curious, they'd never make progress.
Canillian put a cookie in Titosoga's mouth and gave her two more when she looked dissatisfied.
When Frill pressed her lips together in resistance, Canillian poured her a cup of cocoa with a delicious chocolate aroma.

"Will you still refuse?"

Frill primly accepted both the cocoa and cookie.

"Let me be clear. I never admitted to being a fool. I just couldn't resist the fraudulently good combination of cocoa and cookies."

Whatever.
Having silenced the two fools, Canillian delved into the lady's concerns.

"I think your worries might be unnecessary. Professor Destroyer was surprisingly considerate."
"The professor...?"
"If the Sebiche ducal family was trying to conduct transactions using slush funds, would Professor Destroyer have agreed to the ledger transaction?"
"Probably not."

A ledger that couldn't be traded even with money.
The professor had put a price of 500,000 points on it.

"500,000 points is expensive, but sometimes even agreeing to a transaction is a form of consideration. This ledger falls into that category."
"Looking at it that way, the professor might have been considerate toward me."
"Even the statement about accepting all the illegal items and acknowledging the final 10,000 points—though you might have resented it at the time—looks different to me as a third party."
"For example?"
"If you, as a student, had tried to dispose of items that even the Thieves' Guild would hesitate to handle, you would certainly have gotten into big trouble. Many people who thought you had inherited the Sebiche family's wealth would have targeted you."

It turned out that Professor Destroyer, who had seemed like complete trash, had actually been trying to help Arcadia by purchasing one million points' worth of untradeable stolen goods for just 10,000 points.
Canillian Truffle's hypothesis suggested that behind the professor's cold and calculating behavior was a warm heart concerned for his student.
Though it might be a far-fetched theory, Arcadia felt her heart warming to the idea.

'She may be stiff, charmless, and haughty, but her consideration for others is just as kind as Titosoga's or Frill's.'

A natural smile appeared on Arcadia's face as the tension eased.

"Thank you. There's no guarantee that's the case, but now I can avoid completely hating the professor and the Academy."
"It's nothing compared to how you've cared for us. We know how politically and diplomatically burdensome it is for you to stand against the empire's great nobles."

That's only half the story.
Most students express gratitude for her efforts, but acting on behalf of everyone benefits her as well.
Political power is born from those who acknowledge one's authority; when everyone depends on her, she becomes the leader of the massive Arcadia faction.
She gains political power disproportionate to her status and can later use that enormous influence to gain tangible and intangible benefits both inside and outside the Academy.
...That was when the Sebiche ducal family was still standing strong in the Kingdom of Florence.
There are practical benefits invisible to the eyes of a lord's daughter who doesn't understand the essence of politics.

'Such benefits must also exist for Gift Academy, an educational institution suspiciously powerful.'

While Canillian's hypothesis was touching, the likelihood of it being true was slim.
Just as Arcadia herself acted with ulterior motives even when acting benevolently, the Academy was likely looking beyond simple kindness.

'My real concern is about Oknodie, not the professor.'

More precisely, it was about the two million points Oknodie possessed.
It was about the source of those points.
During the midterm exams, she had realized how difficult it was to accumulate points.
How much brilliant talent one needed to possess and prove to earn them.
The Dark Association had used the labor of nearly hundreds of students for two months to gather just 600,000 points.
The 2 million points shown in Oknodie's balance was three times that amount—comparable to the labor of the entire lower class excluding the advanced class.
Such points couldn't be earned through ordinary labor.
Catching insects.
Collecting stones.
Enjoying new dishes.
Chattering happily while enjoying tea parties.
It was too enormous an amount to be given to a cute, friendly, and spirited little lady.

'The Academy must be having Oknodie do something special, something she had to accept to earn such a large sum.'

And in this Academy, based on what Arcadia had seen so far, there was only one person who would assign such suspicious missions to Oknodie.

Professor Destroyer.

Though Canillian spoke well of the professor, Arcadia couldn't help but be suspicious.
Her worries deepened as she wondered how this professor was any different from the foundation that had exploited young Oknodie at will, and whether the only difference was that one operated in the light while the other in the shadows.
