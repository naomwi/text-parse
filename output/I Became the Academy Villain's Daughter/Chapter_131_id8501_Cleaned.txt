# 131 - Learned Helplessness

Oknodie was pitiful.
That was true objectively, and even more so subjectively.
To abuse such a pitiful child to the point of cruelty.
Do they even have something called a conscience?
For the first time, Mob had a wish.

'Coming to the Academy was just following my friend Jaku, that was all.'

Now it's different.
He has his own goal now.

'I want to set Oknodie free.'

To escape the fate of an assassin and live her own life.
She more than deserves the right to enjoy such ordinary happiness.

"Mob. You've been quite motivated lately."
"You should try it too. The mentoring from the Dark Association is actually really helpful."
"Isn't that just because you got lucky with your mentor?"
"If it's not an upper-class student helping, it's usually second-year seniors. If anything, it's more helpful, not less."
"Really?"

Jaku's face didn't brighten much.
A failing candidate in the lower class.
Someone falling behind purely due to lack of talent, without receiving dramatic deductions like Mob.
Jaku was able to look at himself objectively.
Mob's situation of facing expulsion was different from Jaku's.
With effort and proper guidance, Mob could certainly avoid expulsion.
Jaku was different.
Without any deductions, he was becoming an inferior student purely based on his lack of ability.

"You've got nothing to lose. Just try it once."

Mob felt sorry for his friend and wanted him to enjoy the same opportunity he had gained.

"Thanks, Mob."
"Don't mention it."

But I should definitely mention this.

"If the seniors offer you something to eat, don't eat it."
"Why?"
"Just don't eat it if I tell you not to."

Mob had learned the valuable lesson that from now on, the only food he should eat at the Academy was what he bought with his own money.

* * *

Jaku really liked Mob, who was closer to him than anyone else despite not sharing a drop of blood.

"Man. Was my expression that obvious?"

Jaku had lost his parents to an epidemic and was left all alone.
In the plague zone where even priests hesitated to enter, he met another orphan like himself.
Mob.
He seemed like an unimpressive, ordinary guy not much different from himself.
When they were young, just having a companion to survive with in that harsh plague zone was enough to make him happy.
As time passed, the friendship he felt for Mob began to turn into feelings of inferiority.
Even at the bottom of society, there are differences in talent.
How nimble are your hands?
How quickly do you learn?
How good is your memory?
Mob was better than him in every area.
This couldn't continue.
He could tell.
Mob would become someone far greater than himself.
Even the pretense of friendship couldn't last forever.
Equal relationships eventually end too.
In fact, equality had ended long ago.
Mob just hadn't realized it because he was kind.
For several years, Jaku had been worried that Mob might leave him, wondering if he could survive this harsh world alone.

'Probably not.'

Grown birds soar high into the sky, and mature beasts leave their packs to find their own territory.

-You want to become stronger?

Just when he was feeling anxious, he noticed a wandering mercenary.
Though obviously suspicious, it wasn't the time to be picky.
He thought if he didn't become stronger somehow, he would be alone again and die lonely.
So he approached him.
Said he wanted to become stronger.

-You must leave your friend's side.
-Even if you beg to be sent back, you'll suffer terribly for years, and you might even die.
-If you still want to become stronger, follow me.

Jaku made his decision.
He would follow that mercenary.

-Mob. I want to follow that person and receive his teachings.
-If our friendship remains unchanged, let's meet again where we first met.
-I don't know how many years it will be, but let's meet on New Year's Day. It's a promise.

Ten years had passed since that promise.
Even he thought Mob wouldn't wait.
Ten years is a long time.
How could a childhood promise last through a period long enough for mountains and rivers to change?

"Hey. Grown up a lot?"
"Mob. Is that you?"
"What? Can't recognize me because I've become too handsome?"

He had grown taller, gained weight and muscle.
Mob had grown so much that his appearance alone made it impossible to recognize the snotty kid from before.
But some things remained unchanged.
The same old banter.
Friendship unchanged after ten years.
And... that feeling of inferiority he had felt back then.

'I trained for ten years. I worked so hard. And yet I still can't even catch up to you?'

It was talent that couldn't be overcome with effort.
The reality was that he couldn't even keep up with Mob, who was nothing compared to the real monsters in the upper class.
Yes, he had to admit it.

-With your talent, even if you get into the Academy, you won't last a year.

The instructor had said.
That kids like him were a dime a dozen.
Yet the reason he had taken him in ten years ago was only one.
It was because of his determination to abandon even friendship and set out on an adventure.
The moment to show that determination again was approaching.

Clatter.

A small vial in his hand.
If he swallowed this, he could increase his magic power.
Of course, it was a drug given to low-ranking members.
Naturally, it wasn't a proper product.
An incomplete item.
An experimental product.
The side effects upon consumption were enormous.
If the biological compatibility didn't match, the consumer would die.
If the compatibility was mediocre, they would die after writhing in pain and going berserk.
If there was some decent compatibility, they might save their life despite the terrible pain.
If they were lucky enough to have good compatibility, they might achieve dramatic growth.
What were the odds of that?
30%?
9.9%?
0.1%?

'Give up any expectations. I have no talent.'

After ten years in the organization, he realized.
The moment he took this drug would be his last.
The instructor had also said:

-If you get expelled from the Academy, you'll have no more freedom in your remaining life. Be prepared to live as the organization's dog.

He would never be able to meet Mob again.
The world of darkness.
A life wandering in the darkness where the smell of blood lingers awaits.
He stood at the entrance of a very long downhill path.
Having already taken a few steps forward.
If he resisted with all his might, perhaps he could return to the entrance.
But if it was too late, he would fall into a steep slope from which he could never return to his previous path.
The steep slope was right in front of him.
One wrong step and it would be over.
That one step was contained in the vial in his hand.

"Come on, first-year friends. Don't look so gloomy. We'll help you somehow."

The hand holding the vial returned to his chest.
Let's postpone taking this for now.
Wasn't this an opportunity that Mob had found for him?

"Thank you for your help, senior."

That's definitely a face saying there is something.
Gisele smiled warmly like a parent watching a child trying to hide a secret.
His mature appearance made me feel mischievous.

"Shall I tell you? If you're that curious."

Gisele took a deep breath, seemingly a bit surprised.
His narrowed eyes curved gently.

"I'm a naughty adult who's interested in hearing other people's secrets. Would you kindly share with me?"

Actually, revealing secrets carelessly isn't good.
NPCs knowing what's going to happen doesn't always lead to positive results.
They might cause bigger accidents trying to prevent something.
An accident that would end with minor injuries might worsen to serious injuries or death.
But Gisele is an intelligent character who's meticulous about self-management, so it should be fine, right?

I should focus on the lecture!

Being merely used and knowing about a crime are different matters.

"I don't think so. It might be better if she were."

Gisele couldn't hide his complicated feelings.

"Oknodie is simply indifferent about it. About the casualty that's about to happen."
"Why...? Oknodie is a good child."
"Have you heard of learned helplessness?"
"What's that?"
"It's when someone gives up trying because they believe nothing will change anyway. When effort becomes worthless, they stop trying and give up. That's what it's called."

In his view, Oknodie's passive attitude stemmed from learned helplessness.

"If she's already experienced similar situations multiple times, tried to ask for help or prevent them, but failed every time... then her reaction makes sense."

It's not something a child should experience.
It's harsh enough to break even an adult's spirit.
Just hearing about it fills me with fear.
Does assassin training really need to go this far?
Isabel was shocked.
