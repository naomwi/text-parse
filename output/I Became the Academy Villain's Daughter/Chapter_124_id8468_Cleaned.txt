# Chapter 124 - Third-Year Dangerous Species

Zealot let out a scream-like shout as he watched Jigoku point his gun at the seniors.

"You crazy bastard—! Stop it right now—! If you've even heard rumors about how terrifying third-years are, you shouldn't be doing something like this—!"

They say third-years can turn passersby into hamsters with their magic.
They say if you upset a third-year, you'll be cursed with insomnia—uncomfortable when lying straight, neck and shoulder pain when lying on your side, and chest discomfort when sleeping on your stomach.
They say if you take a third-year's seat in the cafeteria, all food will taste like cucumber.
The Academy has numerous such ghost stories about third-years.
And perhaps most of these aren't just stories but actual events that happened, might happen in the future, and shocking true accounts no one wants to experience.

"Scared?"

The pirate strategist didn't particularly care.

"Hey, juniors. There have been loud noises around here for a while, so you'd better clear out."
"I'm impressed you have the courage for outdoor activities during mosquito season."

The third-years were bewildered in their own way.
Though reluctantly performing pest control and patrol duties at the professors' request, they knew that even if disease spread through the Academy, the professors would remain fine using their own methods.
In the end, only students would suffer.
The third-years were diligently earning points by performing patrol duties for their own safety.
While they occasionally witnessed second-years causing trouble, even they had never seen first-years getting involved in incidents so early.

"This year's first-years are really fearless. How did they deal with the mosquitoes... Ahhh! What is that? Why are the mosquitoes so fat!"

The third-year seniors descending with their staffs spotted giant mosquitoes that appeared much larger than the usual giant mosquitoes.
The mosquitoes, typically large but skinny and creepy, had sucked so much blood that they'd grown as big as large dogs, swollen like balloons.
The mosquitoes, expanded sideways after drinking the dark nutritional tonic, were impossible not to scream at.
That was the problem.
Jigoku, who had been watching with a blank expression waiting for them to come down, flinched at the third-years' loud voices.

Bang!

With a ping sound, the defensive spell floating above the third-year's staff deflected the bullet.

"You, you...!"
"A first-year shooting at seniors?!"

The seniors were dumbfounded.
Jigoku looked around nervously, then opened his eyes wide and said:

"Oops."
"Oops?!"
"Sorry."
"Sorry?!"

An apology with absolutely no sincerity!
This could lead to a fight with the third-years.
Hestia quickly tried to defuse the situation like a firefighter rushing to extinguish a fire.

"Seniors, I'm so sorry. That child has compulsive trigger-pulling syndrome and shoots his gun at random times. Several first-years have already been shot."

An explanation that would astound even the eccentric third-years!

"At that point, shouldn't he be admitted to a mental hospital rather than attending the Academy?"
"I really wouldn't want to live in the same dormitory as him."
"But everyone develops some kind of mental illness while attending the Academy anyway. So it doesn't matter, right?"
"That's true."
"He might be a bad seed, but he'll adapt well."

Seniors generously letting it slide!
That scared Hestia even more.
What experiences do third-years go through that they can brush off a gun incident as casually as a small everyday mistake?!

"You idiots!! Is this the time to be laughing?!!!"

The atmosphere changed abruptly when the apparent leader among the "Flying Green Third-Years" with green scarves around their necks barked at them.

"Wh-what's wrong?"
"Yeah, I was enjoying talking with the first-years for once."
"Those first-years are the problem!"

That's more like it.
Of course it was wrong to casually dismiss a gun incident!

Unlike Hestia who was confident in her abilities, Rogini and Zealot, who feared the third-years, trembled as they stared pitifully at Hestia's back.
Surely a crazy strong woman who could wield a double-headed axe with one hand would handle this somehow!

"Have you not thought about how much blood these mosquitoes must have sucked to grow so large?!"
"?"
"Gasp! Does that mean people were eaten?!"
"??"
"Damn it! Were we too late?"
"Good heavens. How many first-years must have been devoured for so many mosquitoes to become so fat like pigs that they can barely fly!!"
"???"
"Look at the color of the fountain. The water has turned black from the dense magical energy. They've definitely mutated after gaining enough experience points from the slaughter."
"Poor first-years."
"You all stay back. We'll handle this!"

Zealot, who was crouching in the bushes with just his spear sticking out, was bewildered.

"Seniors, I think you're misunderstanding something..."
"No need to say anything!!!"

Icarus, the squadron leader of the five-person third-year flying squadron, comforted Zealot and everyone else with a voice full of firm will and conviction.

"I understand your desire to avenge your comrades, but this is a battlefield that we third-years must handle."
"That's right. First-years should quietly return to the lounge, wrap yourselves in blankets, drink cocoa, and soothe the bitter pain of losing your comrades!"

The squadron leader was joined by his deputy, who would take over in case of emergency during flight.
While they awkwardly stood there, suddenly cast as poor first-years trembling in fear after losing their friends, the third-year seniors' furious attacks rained down on the fountain.

Flash

"What just flashed..."

Boom Crash

A bolt of lightning struck, sending fountain stone fragments flying upward.
As Zealot looked back at the stone fragment that grazed his cheek, he raised his head to look forward again and saw the seniors' magic missiles covering the fountain like carpet bombing.

"Ahhh!"
"Fragments are flying!!"
"H-Hestia, do something!!"

Rogini, who had used all her strength to submerge the mosquitoes in water, screamed pitifully.
Magic spearman Zealot was struggling just to protect himself, and the sharp-tongued Echo wasn't even a combat specialist to begin with.

'Beyond two portions, now it's three portions?'

Hestia swung her axe to deflect the fragments, trying to protect not only herself but also Rogini and Echo.
Even as she gritted her teeth and swung her axe, only Jigoku, who had fired the first shot from under the tree, clicked his tongue in disappointment.

"What's this? How can third-years be so nice? Weren't Academy third-years supposed to be all psychopaths?"
"Don't let your guard down."

While everyone was doubting whether the rumors were wrong as they reassessed the seniors, Oknodie was different.
When pirates engage in piracy, they can distinguish between frightened sailors who want to surrender their cargo and live, and sailors who are tensely prepared for a fight.
The movement of Oknodie's muscles, the force in her voice, her gaze and expression—everything about her current demeanor indicated clear combat readiness as if engagement was imminent.

'Why is she so cautious?'

Though puzzled, Jigoku didn't doubt Oknodie's judgment.
How much guilt had he felt over the incident at the magical beast storage room caused by his mistake?
He felt no guilt or regret about firing the first shot at a third-year since he had shot at a dangerous species.
But what if he hesitated and didn't attack the third-years because he didn't trust Oknodie's judgment, and then something happened?
He would regret that.
He would hate himself for not trusting Oknodie.
He was certain of that.

Bang bang!

So he shot.
At a senior approaching Oknodie during the chaos.

Ping ping—!

The bullets were deflected by the protective barrier again with a weak sound.
The senior raised his staff angrily.

"You. I can let it slide twice, but not three times. If you shoot one more time, I'll fry you!"
"Then don't approach Oknodie. Our girl is extremely wary of you."
"I'm trying to help you!"

The senior looked at the blood stone in Oknodie's hand and said:

"Kid, that's a blood stone, right? It's dangerous, so let us seniors take care of it for you."
"No! This stone is mine!"
"The third-year seniors are using their precious mana to help you, so you should consider giving us something for our trouble. Okay? We need something to show for our efforts. Otherwise, what will we tell the professors when they ask why we wasted mana that should be saved for lectures?"

From Jigoku's perspective under the tree, it sounded reasonable enough, but Oknodie's resistance was fierce.

"Don't lie! The professors encourage mana use because experiencing mana exhaustion expands your mana capacity—they wouldn't scold you!"

Is that so?
Not knowing the professors well, Jigoku was just watching to see if he should fire another shot.
As Oknodie continued to resist, the senior's expression hardened as he tried to establish dominance.

"Sigh. What does a baby know? Hand it over while I'm asking nicely. You should listen to your seniors."
"No! You can't! Don't do it!"
"Words aren't working. I need to teach you a little painful lesson. The more you resist, the more you'll get hurt."

Revealing his true colors, the third-year gathered crackling energy at the tip of his staff.

"Gyahahat! So I can shoot after all, right?"
"Cover me, please!"

Unlike the light warning shots until now, Jigoku loaded a special bullet.
The third-year senior, who had casually deployed an "Electric Discharge" spell to distract Jigoku and prevent him from aiming so he could destroy the annoying girl's gun first, widened his eyes in surprise.
Unlike charging-type archers who stay in place and use "Precise Aim," "Charging," and "Aimed Shot" due to lack of confidence in their accuracy, Jigoku was a rapid-fire trigger-happy type who used "Rapid Fire" and "Mobile Shooting."
Moreover, thanks to his shooting skills honed in real combat, his accuracy wasn't bad at all.

Bang! Crack

"Argh!"

Hit by a bullet that penetrated his barrier, the senior convulsed, crashed into a tree, and fainted while hanging from the trunk.
Attribute-breaking technique! He shot a bullet of the same attribute to penetrate the barrier that repelled different types of matter!
Even Jigoku himself was proud of his remarkable performance—a response too swift and appropriate for a first-year.

'At this rate, Oknodie will say "Jigoku, you're amazing!" and praise me a lot, rely on me, and completely forget about my previous mistake, right?'

Confidently turning to look at Oknodie, Jigoku realized her gaze wasn't directed at him and frowned as he followed her line of sight.
There stood Squadron Leader Icarus and his squadron members, looking dumbfounded at their colleague hanging unconscious from the tree.

"Oops. Do I need to shoot all of them too?"
