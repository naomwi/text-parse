# Enhanced Translation: Chapter 322 - The Scales of Life

Xing felt a chill run down his spine.
He'd been played.
And not just played—he'd been thoroughly outmaneuvered.
Oknodie's illogical knowledge.
Using that knowledge to create a reason not to return to the Academy.
The Chairman's proposal.
His wicked wisdom.
And the way it actually appealed to Oknodie.
Xing could see it.
The future that would unfold.
These compromises would stretch from one week to two, from two weeks to a month, from a month to a semester, from a semester to a year, and then to two years, endlessly expanding.
At the end of it all, returning to the Academy might become impossible, potentially resulting in expulsion.

If the Foundation resolved all of Oknodie's worries and concerns.
If the child no longer had a reason to attend the Academy.
If her bonds with friends were replaced by the Foundation's convenience.

Dark Princess Oknodie would be stained by darkness of an entirely different dimension than before.
A truly wicked child who had awakened to the true meaning of darkness would be born.

"My, you seem concerned about Oknodie's future. Do you believe a student who neglects friendships and studies will struggle to grow into a respectable adult with a bright future?"
"What's your real intention in asking when you already know the answer? I may be indebted to you and the Foundation, but I cannot hand Oknodie over like this. Don't try to manipulate that child."
"Manipulation? Isn't that something you should say to yourself as well? Xing, the heir to the Sword Gate who fled from the powerful families of the Eastern Empire. Don't you need my child's help for your revenge?"
"!!"
"Why are you so surprised? This is a Foundation facility. I am the founder and chairman of the Foundation. There is no place in the world beyond the Foundation's eyes, and there are few things I don't know. If I set my mind to it, uncovering a secret like this is nothing."

His past identity had been exposed.
Information might leak to the Sword Gate in his homeland, to the vile elders of his family, to the enemies he wanted to tear apart.
Before he could accomplish his goals, they might send someone powerful to take his head.
But an even greater fear arose within him.
The nature of this fear was the guilty conscience that whispered, "I am no different from the Chairman."

Using Oknodie and putting her in danger.
The Foundation clearly had such aspects.
They were also trying to separate Oknodie from her friends.
But could he justify making Oknodie participate in his revenge after graduation?
Wasn't it essentially the same thing—making her get caught up in his circumstances?
It was a clear fact that while Oknodie participated in the assassination mission to the Eastern Empire, she would be separated from her other friends.
Strictly speaking, Xing and the Chairman were no different.
The Chairman had mercilessly exposed this contradiction.

"I understand. Everyone becomes earnest when it comes to avenging their enemies. They'll use any means necessary. Some vendettas are promises that stake one's very existence. If not fulfilled, one loses their reason to live, their strength as a human being, and even wastes away their lifespan before dying."

Instead of simply criticizing this, the Chairman made an even sweeter proposal.

"So let's work together."
"Together...?"
"You should stay at the Foundation with my child. If you feel something is unfair, you can intervene, and you'll be reassured by staying close to her."
"Working for the Foundation with Oknodie..."
"In the end, what you want is revenge. The Academy is just a place to gain strength for that revenge. If you have people and an organization to help with both your mission and the power to achieve it, is there really any need to be bound to the Academy?"
"That's... certainly true."

He had a point.
Aside from his aversion to the Foundation, the Chairman's proposal made perfect sense.
He liked the fact that he had a choice.
He could watch from nearby and take Oknodie away if things seemed wrong.
Despite having no real need to do so, the Chairman was making this offer to him.

"Why make me such a favorable offer?"
"Is it strange to care about your child's friendships? And it's not such a bad thing to show kindness to an admirable young man who is seriously contemplating his future and determined to seek revenge."
"Is this how it works? How the Foundation obtains scholarship students and secures influence around the world?"

The Chairman merely smiled.
He was offering a favor.
To his daughter's friend.
Even knowing it was just pleasant words.
Even knowing it was blatant temptation.
This temptation was too sweet.
Like the allure of an unhealthy dessert that you can't resist even knowing it's bad for you, or like falling into a swamp you can never escape once you step in.

"...Anytime. If I ever feel you're crossing the line, I'll convince Oknodie and take her back to the Academy."
"I'm glad. That her friend will be joining her."
"Hmm~"

Oknodie didn't seem particularly pleased.
She was even pouting.

"Tch. I don't like sharing experience points. But since Xing has high likability and good DPS for endgame content, I'll specially allow it!"

Xing discovered he couldn't deny that she was acting as if she'd done him a great favor.
Their relationship had changed.
As soon as he realized this fact, Xing's heart grew heavy.

As soon as it was decided they would stay at the Foundation, the Chairman handed them a catalog.

"Since you've decided to stay at the Foundation instead of the Academy, I've compiled a list of activities that could benefit my daughter and her friend. I've created a list of opportunities to collect rare items or earn special titles that will help with your growth, so please take a look."

Unlike the Foundation's lectures with their lengthy theoretical sessions and systematic teaching, the Chairman's curriculum presented clear objectives and the goals needed to achieve them.
The Foundation would certainly profit from this as well, but that wasn't something to quibble about from the position of receiving help.

'It's frightening how easily I'm convincing myself of this.'

The list wasn't just sweet enough to argue that what's good for one is good for all.

There was a clear pattern in the Foundation's proposals.

'Do they want war?'

The Three Western Kingdoms.
The Deutsch Kingdom, the Florence Kingdom, and the Taland Kingdom.
Deutsch of the plains, Florence of the seas, and Taland of the mountains.
Beyond the westernmost Deutsch Kingdom lay the great forest of the beastmen.
The Human Border.
The land of beastmen living beyond the limits of human-controlled territory.
The war discussed involved conflict with the legion stationed near the border and the benefits to be gained from it.

"Oknodie. What do you think of this catalog?"
"The rewards are juicy?"
"...Let me rephrase. To achieve these goals, we'd have to participate in a plot to annihilate the Deutsch Kingdom's legion. Do you have enough grudge to add your blade to such a large-scale war?"
"Dunno?"
"This concerns you. Answer seriously. I'm asking seriously too."

Perhaps some of his sincerity got through to her.
Her previously flippant attitude began to show some earnestness.

"Hmm. The beastmen territory is connected to the Zenya event, remember that cat-girl classmate who goes 'nya nya'?"
"I remember."
"If conflict breaks out in the beastmen territory, Zenya takes a leave of absence from the Academy! If we go help, she returns to the Academy, but if we ignore it or fail to assist, she gets expelled."

But even this information wasn't entirely helpful.
She spoke of the future as if observing past events.
She easily predicted the impact on a person's life.
Insight.
Deductive reasoning.
Her outlook was so clear it seemed almost like precognition, beyond such capabilities.

"But if we stop this, an event opens up where Andersen, the Grand Duke's son, returns to the Deutsch Kingdom instead!"
"...And?"
"If we consider who would be more helpful between Zenya and Andersen, it's obviously Andersen, right? He seems to get along well with our maid April too."

When that clarity pointed in a single direction, Xing felt afraid.

"So Zenya has to die!"

Oknodie was more broken than he had thought.
