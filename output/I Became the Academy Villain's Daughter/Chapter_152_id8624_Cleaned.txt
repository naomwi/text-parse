# 152 - The Lady's Academy Life

As I chattered all night in Jonna's arms, I remember making a fuss but not falling asleep. Yet when I opened my eyes, I was in bed.
Feeling languid, I stretched with a groan and extended my arms with effort, experiencing the refreshing sensation that comes after a good night's sleep.
Rubbing my eyes as I got up, I realized I had been sleeping in the night duty room prepared in the Academy's visiting quarters.

"I see you've woken up in time for training."
"Hehe. That's basic, isn't it?"
"Well done. After you finish your morning jog, I'll prepare breakfast for you."
"Wow!"

While exercising diligently among the dolphin pants seniors, my excitement grew rapidly.

"Oknodie, did something good happen?"
"Yesterday was visiting day. Looks like her guardian came."
"Wow, I'm jealous. They say less than one in three students get visited by their guardians."

My shoulders perked up involuntarily at the envious voices of the dolphin pants senior squad, mostly composed of lower-class students.

"Heheh. You seniors would be surprised if you saw Jonna! My butler is incredibly capable."
"Butler? Not your parents?"
"Papa was too busy to come!"
"What about your mom?"

Hmm... good question.
I stopped running and fell into blank contemplation, but no amount of thinking would help me know someone I didn't know.

"I don't know!"
"You don't know?"
"I've never heard about Mama!"

The dolphin pants seniors suddenly covered their mouths.
The senior who had asked the question even had tears welling up in her eyes.

"I'm sorry, Oknodie...! My thoughtlessness made you recall unpleasant memories!"
"She must be from a single-parent household."
"Her father raising a daughter alone must have been really tough."
"Seems he never remarried."
"Her father must have truly loved her mother."

Their whispers felt like they were pushing me to cry right now, but I honestly didn't have any particular feelings about someone who wasn't even my real mother.

"Don't you miss your mom?"
"I'm fine! I don't even know what she looks like. Besides..."
"Besides?"
"I have Isabel and Arcadia who take care of me like a mama would!"
"Aww, you're too cute!"
"Those two are so lucky!"
"If Oknodie wants, we can be your mamas too!"

Suddenly, I had five mamas.
Though I didn't understand why, the seniors hugged me, patted me, and gave me snacks, making it a happy morning exercise.

"Oknodie, if it's okay, could we meet this butler person too?"
"Sorry. I was planning to have breakfast with Jonna alone!"
"She might want to spend time with someone as close as family. Let's not interfere and let her go."

The dolphin pants senior with long brown hair tied back in a ponytail saw me off, turning back several times with regret and waving her hand.
I waved back as we parted, and my steps returning to Jonna were twice as light as usual.

"Heheh."

I couldn't help but smile and feel happy.
Was having someone to greet the morning with such a joyful thing?
Until yesterday, the night duty room had no meaning to me, it was nothing.
Yet just knowing Jonna was waiting there.
That alone made me feel like I had a home to return to.

"Jonna, I'm back!"

I flung the door open, expecting Jonna to have prepared breakfast.
By the cooking counter set up in one corner of the night duty room.
Next to an overturned pot and spilled hot pot on the floor, Jonna had collapsed.


* * *


"Mana exhaustion."

Said the healer stationed at the infirmary.
Jonna had barely caught his breath.
Those who handle mana occasionally suffer when they use more power than their permitted mana capacity, drawing on their physical strength or mental energy.
Like him last night when he pushed himself to escape the Destroyer's double boundary.
The finishing blow was probably the cleaning magic.
The spell he had used to reassure Oknodie.
He had somehow endured with superhuman patience until morning, but finally collapsed.

"I'm sorry, Jonna..."
"My Lady has nothing to apologize for."
"But still... you used cleaning magic because of me..."

It was just a brief unconsciousness of about 30 minutes.
His robust body was already absorbing more mana during that short time.
Mana that has once hit bottom tends to refill faster, so one could say Jonna actually grew stronger through this experience.

"I should have realized you were pushing yourself when you couldn't even clean the cloth covering the basket... You were in pain the whole time while I was clinging to you and chattering, weren't you?"

But this kind of growth brings no joy.
Making the young lady sad and filling her with self-reproach.
As a butler, it was truly a disqualifying situation.

"My Lady. May I have your wrist for a moment?"
"*Sniff* My wrist...?"

Oknodie extended her hand with puffy eyes.
Holding her fair, delicate wrist, he gently drew up his magical power.
The bewildered young lady suddenly couldn't bear the increased weight and fell to her knees with a thud.

"Ugh!"
"I can make someone like you collapse with just a touch of my finger. So please don't feel sorry for me or worry yourself sick."
"Okay, okay, just stop it!"

After retrieving the metal coating he had applied to her clothes, the suddenly haggard-looking young lady caught her breath.
The experience of being crushed by such immense weight, enough to break out in a cold sweat, must have left quite an impression.

"How could someone as strong as you collapse from mana exhaustion?"
"I had a conflict with one of the professors. It's nothing for you to worry about, My Lady."
"How can I not worry? My butler collapsed."

Her eyes narrowed into triangles, showing how angry she was.
Even when angry, the young lady looked cute, which made his role as butler quite rewarding.

"Instead of worrying, you should hurry to your lecture. During 'Guardian Observation Week,' guardians can observe the lectures that you normally attend."
"Oh right! I almost forgot about the lecture!"

He pushed away the young lady who tried to support him with just one finger to her forehead, then smoothed out the wrinkles in his clothes.

<Metal Coating>
<Tailcoat Normal Stance>

<Metal Worker>
<Automatic Walking Assistance - Type A>

Using mana again before fully recovering from mana exhaustion causes terrible pain to the mana organs, but he had obligations.
As a foundation representative dispatched by the foundation, as a butler and guardian who serves and cares for the young lady, he had a duty to maintain dignity and attend the observation lecture.
Compared to those who have neither the qualifications nor the opportunity, enduring pain is nothing.

"I'm looking forward to meeting your friends."
"You'll be surprised at how many friends I've made! I'll introduce you to lots of them!"

The young lady kept looking back every three steps, carefully examining his face.
Her apparent concern that he might be pretending not to be in pain when he actually was made him worry about her instead.
How could someone survive at Gift Academy with such a kind heart?

'She must have received a lot of help from her friends.'

On the way to the lecture hall.
Jonna traced old memories.
The opportunity once given to him.
The beings he could call friends who shared difficult times with him.
There were many.
Some seemed like insurmountable walls.
But only a very few survived until the end.
When he closes his eyes, he can still vividly hear their screams and curses.

"You're late, student Oknodie. Even for homeroom, tardiness is not good. Please go to your seat quickly."
"Ehehe. I'm sorry!"
"Hmm. Seeing your smiling face, I can't bring myself to deduct points. I'll let it slide just this once."

Monday, first period.
Professor Mahabharata, in charge of the advanced class homeroom lecture.

"Pleased to meet you, Professor. I am Jonna Wiheomhae, Oknodie's guardian."
"Hmm?"

The master of transformation magic and one of the Dragon Principal's closest aides tilted his head upon seeing Jonna.
He hesitated as if feeling a strange sense of déjà vu, like he had seen him somewhere before, but unable to place it clearly, Mahabharata prioritized his duty as a teacher.

"Yes. During observation, please sit in any empty seat in the section where other guardians are seated."

Befitting guardians of advanced class students, they were all high nobles of nations, high-ranking knights renowned across three countries, and high-level wizards from magic towers.
These guardians with overwhelming presences like massive rocks or tall towers engaged in a psychological battle among themselves, creating an invisible boundary away from the students.
Looking at that intimidating scene, Jonna's presence wasn't particularly noticeable.

"Is that man a representative of the rumored 'Wiheomhae Foundation'?"
"His aura isn't that impressive."
"Perhaps he was selected as a representative not for his skills but through those sinister methods. A man as ominous as the child."

The power advantage clearly belonged to them.
Yet they were the ones being cautious.
This is the value of the Wiheomhae Foundation's name.
The power of the Boss whom Oknodie calls Papa.

*Nod*

A student who made eye contact greeted him with a smiling face.
Gisele, the dark merchant.
The man who had been protecting Oknodie within the Academy.

Isabel. Son O-cheon.
Those who accompanied him were glaring at Jonna with disapproving eyes.
Others quickly turned their heads away, not even daring to make eye contact for fear of facing some trouble.
Fear was actually preferable.
What was irritating were the Imperial Group students who glared with contempt and disgust from behind.

It was the same with the students as with the adults—they were all wary of him.

'The young lady's academy life seems really difficult.'

If students show this kind of attitude even with a foundation representative present, how would they treat the young lady when she's alone as the foundation's official scholarship student?
It would certainly be worse, not better, than now.
The situation was much more serious than what he had heard from the young lady's letters or reports from spies planted in the Academy.
At this point, Jonna set aside everything the young lady had told him the day before.
There was now a possibility that the young lady, who had spoken so casually, had actually been trying not to worry him by pretending serious situations were nothing.
The more she valued him, the less he could trust her words.

'The same goes for her friendships.'

Even those precious friendships were of no help in this situation.
This was evident from the attitudes of Isabel and Son O-cheon, who were more hostile to him than the Imperial students.

They cannot help Oknodie.
The young lady may consider them friends, but do they consider her a friend?
Even if they are truly friends, they show no willingness to help her.
And even if they had the will, they lack the ability.

'A foundation representative has the authority to request support from the foundation headquarters.'

And the young lady needed the foundation's support.
More definite support that would immediately impact her academy life, beyond the indirect support provided so far.

That meant actively protecting the young lady, even if it required deploying additional foundation personnel.
Of course, such matters also require the cooperation of the person involved.
No matter how much help is offered, it's meaningless if the recipient doesn't accept it.

'I need to sort out the young lady's friendships.'

Create opportunities for her to rely on new connections.
For the young lady's sake, cut off unnecessary friendships.
Plant more beneficial relationships in those vacant spots.

It's truly a perfect plan.
This way, the young lady's academy life can proceed more safely.
Of course, it would be difficult for the young lady to cut off familiar relationships herself.
The dirty and difficult work is the butler's role.
Son O-cheon and Isabel.
A smile appeared on Jonna's lips as he looked at the two students.
His eyes did not smile.
