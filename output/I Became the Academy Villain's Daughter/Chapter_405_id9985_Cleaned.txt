# 405 - The Deep Sea Leviathan

Professor El Draco's facial muscles twitched at the <Voice Transmission> magic coming from beyond the arrogant upperclassmen.

[I have the justification in my hands. Turn a blind eye. Otherwise, your position will become very difficult.]

Professor Red Mountain.
Once the master of the Thousand Spirit Mountain Range that dominated the boundary between east and west.
A warning from the ancient great sorceress carried significant weight.

"To think that a mountain beast dares to insult me. Do you wish to have the reputation of the Great Pirate engraved on that meager chest of yours?"

If she was a great sorceress, then he was a great pirate.
El Draco was confident that if he committed himself, he wouldn't easily lose to Professor Red Mountain who had lost her prime powers.

[The Principal placed a <Restriction> on you. My eyes can see the true nature of that restriction. Do not lose any students. For each life lost, you will lose one-tenth of your total power.]
"!!"
[If you're determined to cross the line... who has more to lose?]

A pirate is not a man of injustice.
If there's too much at stake, he can cut his losses without hesitation.
Treasures, subordinates, even ships.
Men who couldn't abandon things all died.
Only the most cowardly men survived as pirate strategists or rebuilt pirate crews from nothing, rebuilding their infamy.

"Kehe. Kehehehehehe!"

Those who have lost much know.
Whether this loss is irreversible, if there's strength to rise again, how much must be lost before there's a future.

"Mountain beast. You know nothing."
[About your ugly struggle?]

El Draco showed signs of anger at being hit at his core, but it was merely his <Deception> and <Acting> skills to fool Professor Red Mountain.
It wasn't genuine defeat or frustration.
As his adjutant said, his domain encompassed the entire fleet's vast range.

'Once on board my ship, nothing can deceive my eyes.'

He already knew.
What tremendous bomb was inside the box Oknodie had brought.
A baby kraken.
The moment it touches water, the mother of the baby will surface.
The deep sea leviathan kraken itself, which no seafarer could fail to recognize.

'The mountain beast must have detected the magical reaction too.'

But she was a being from the mountains.
She couldn't understand the maternal instinct of the great beast kraken.
That was where the difference lay.

"Place a mutual restriction. Then I promise silence."
[Good. Now you're talking sense. You'll need some excuse to give the Principal.]

━━━
Neither shall harm students, and all damages resulting from students who die or suffer fatal injuries shall be compensated by Professor Red Mountain.
However, if Professor El Draco or his subordinates are involved in the process, this restriction becomes void.
The above restriction is certified under the name of the God of Contracts, and those who fail to fulfill the contract swear to be forcibly collected by the god.
━━

The moment the contract was sealed, victory was determined.
Not for Professor Red Mountain, but for Professor El Draco.

[Ultra-massive domain expansion!? What is this reaction!!]
"What else? It's the sound of your debt piling up like a mountain."

Now all casualties in this sea area must be borne by Red Mountain.
Of course, there will be no harm to first-years.
Because the one who summoned it is the first-year Oknodie.


* * *


The third-years were bewildered.
While the second-years were distracting attention, they were performing a summoning ritual to call forth a monster that would wreck the ship.
That was the original plan, but as they cast <Underwater Breathing> magic and performed magic underwater, an enormously ferocious energy overwhelmed them.

"Wh-what!"
"What's coming!?"
"Is it really okay to continue with the summoning ritual? Didn't the fourth-years do something?!"

Their mana-sensing ability, which develops with higher levels of mana arts, warned them fiercely.
That they should flee immediately.
That an unmanageable massive threat was approaching.

"Let's break the summoning!"
"We need to survive first!"

It was an attitude that Great Pirate Professor El Draco would have praised as excellent pirate mindset, but their situation was different from the first-years.
The first-years were still on the collapsing ship.
The third-years had entered the water to perform the summoning ritual.

"M-my body won't listen!"
"What is this? What's happening to us?!"
"Use Search Eye!"

Search Eye.
The ability to discern all magical phenomena with one's eyes revealed the true nature of the status ailment binding the third-years.

"Fear?!"
"The seabed just trembled. Are you saying all these people are afflicted with fear from a single tremor!?"
"This is the kind of phenomenon you'd experience from the area-of-effect Fear emitted by World Monsters."

World Monsters were beings that even third-years couldn't handle, and even exceptional fourth-years would only take on as graduation projects.
And even then, with the goal of repelling rather than subjugating.
The owner of this Fear was now moving in the sea.

"Release the power going into the summoning circle now!!"

Sarpedon, the head teaching assistant leading the third-year assistants who had lost their summons.
Realizing the gravity of the situation, he shouted urgently, but no student withdrew the mana they were pouring into the summoning circle.

"I-I can't stop!!"
"My body won't follow my control!!"
"Aaaah, please make it stop!!!"

Fear washed over them.
A fear different from the instinctive terror felt physically toward a massive creature—a fear that felt all the more real because they clearly recognized it with their brilliant minds.

<Mana Puzzle - Water Attribute>

It's natural to sense water attribute mana puzzles in the sea.
But the water mana puzzle now felt in the summoning circle had a different <weight>.
As if encountering dense mana forged under pressure in the deep sea.

"Oh no, we've been tricked!!"

The culprit behind the fear, the deep sea leviathan, had already noticed early on.
That a summoning ritual was being performed here.
And that it could use this to its advantage.

<Technique Modification>
<Magic Circle Transformation>
<Summoning Ritual Interference>

The summoning ritual meant to call forth a predetermined summon according to the summoner's will was being dominated by mana emitted by a vastly superior being, forcibly transforming its structure.
Into a gate for a great beast summoning ritual that could bring the deep sea leviathan here immediately, even faster than its physical body could surface!

<Forced Summoning>

"Arggghhhh!!"

<Meridian Damage>
<Acupoint Destruction>

"Release all magical tools. If you don't reduce maintenance mana, you'll die from mana depletion!!"

Though they couldn't stop the ritual due to the magic forcing the summoning, they could throw away the magical tools and artifacts in their possession without interfering with the ritual.
Each one was as valuable as a thousand gold pieces, but not more precious than life.
The third-years, in terrible pain from dried-up mana, tearfully and urgently discarded their magical tools.

<Mana Road Damage>
<Mana Heart Overload>

The mana roads throughout their bodies screamed and their mana hearts throbbed as if about to burst from the descent of a being too massive for third-years to handle.

'Bad luck, but fortunate in misfortune.'

Sarpedon realized.
It was only because it was a group ritual with everyone combining their power to summon a common target, and because they were in the sea bottom rich with water attribute mana.
If the ritual had been performed alone or not in the sea, they would have coughed blood and died the moment they were subjected to the forced summoning ritual.
But having abundant mana alone couldn't compare to the talents of the advanced classes who cultivated pure mana through their own training methods, or the monsters of the magic department.
If they were truly strong, they might have endured this ritual to the end, but their mana, built through shortcuts, could neither protect their bodies from external erosion nor resist—it was just being forcibly dragged along.
Relief quickly turned to frustration.

'It was all futile. Both relying on magical tools and the power of a group united to avoid being robbed—all useless before overwhelming violence!'

On the faces of the students who met his eyes, he saw despair greater than the pain transmitted from their acupoints.

'Who on earth summoned such a monster! Was it the Great Pirate El Draco? Was the legend true that infamous great pirates can control the deep sea leviathan kraken?'

A pirate crew that controls monsters, as told in seafarers' legends.
There would be no pirate crew more terrifying in the world.
If it was the great pirate whom Dragon Principal personally kidnapped to be a professor, it was entirely possible.

'Professor Red Mountain was supposed to prevent this from happening, but this is different from what was promised!!'

Though indignant and frustrated, there was nothing he could do.
The deed was already done.
To at least preserve his life, Sarpedon gritted his teeth and projected his will into the summoning circle, twisting his meager talent to its limit.

-You are too massive for us to handle.
-Even if our bodies are squeezed into a handful of blood, we cannot summon your true form!

He raised his voice against the enormous will that was too terrifying to face directly.
The consequence of this reckless act was that the immense pressure, previously distributed among all third-years, now focused entirely on Sarpedon.

"Kuheck!!!"

Just bearing alone the pressure that all third-years had been sharing was enough to crack his mana heart.
Half of his circuits collapsed, and instead of mana, the life force qi was forcibly extracted.
But it wasn't a futile attempt.

[I'll grant you one leg.]

The massive presence trying to break through the summoning circle diminished greatly, and the burden on their bodies lessened.
Not the main body, but just one tentacle—one leg of the deep sea leviathan emerged through the summoning circle.

<Summoning Ritual Successful>
<Tentacle of the Deep Sea Leviathan Kraken>

The tentacle, which had rendered the third-years powerless through mana exhaustion and energy depletion upon summoning, raised its massive leg above the water's surface.
The size of the leg casting a shadow under the sky and causing massive waves was a staggering 100 meters.
Professor Red Mountain's face turned pale as she realized that the enormous disaster that would occur when that leg struck the water's surface would all be her responsibility.
