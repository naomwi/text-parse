# <10 - Cheating the Ticket Exam>

"The food made from ingredients you gathered—not all of them, but several—are rare dishes that people normally can't eat even if they want to."
"Ah, yes... I suppose that's true..."
"As you can see, I grew up malnourished, so I'm short and have many regrets. I don't want to miss this great opportunity to eat various foods!"

Gisele asked with an incredulous expression.

"Then what about the ticket?"
"Can't we eat first and think about it later?"
"Ha. Do you think a solution will magically appear if you just eat without any preparation?"
"Why not?"
"This hurts my pride. To say such lazy things after witnessing my skills firsthand."

Gisele's eyes turned cold.

"If you're that confident, how about a wager?"
"What kind of wager?"
"The 32 types of dishes from your meal voucher. You'll eat three types per day for 11 days. If you acquire a ticket through a method I find impressive before finishing all the dishes, I'll give you the meal voucher for free."
"Hmm..."
"But if I win, you'll pay me 100 gold coins and buy a silver ticket from me. Either way, you get a ticket, and I keep my pride intact."

Gisele lifted his chin arrogantly.

"If you're not confident, you can surrender now before losing face and get the ticket... the ticket..."

Drool dripped down my chin.
Seeing me drooling at the thought of free food, Gisele was at a loss for words.

"My lady. No matter how much you appreciate food, a lady drooling is not an appropriate sight for men outside your household. Please be mindful."
"But you're offering free food."
"Ha. What incredible confidence. I don't even need to ask if you accept the wager."

And so began my ticket exam wager with Gisele.


* * *


On the fifth day of staying at the inn.
Black market merchant Gisele had been monitoring the girl's every move, expecting some brilliant method of obtaining a ticket.
Inwardly, he thought:

'Should I report this as child abuse?'

He knew that noble families had strict education standards.
They lived in a different world from commoners.
But even so, there should be limits.

"This dish has a perfect lingering taste that wraps around the tip of the tongue at the end."

The girl smiled with satisfaction after eating the rare dish.
The fork and spoon she placed on the table made a thud sound, spreading a heavy resonance.
And it wasn't just the tableware.
After finishing her meal, under the pretext of agility training, she practiced a terrifying exercise of jabbing a fork between her widely spread fingers.
One wrong move and a finger would be severed.
Yet that was considered one of the easier exercises.

"With your specs, normal running is no longer beneficial. So you need to practice evasive maneuvers while avoiding attacks."
"Sounds good!"
"I'll announce the target area before attacking. If your evasion is slow, you'll be wounded, though not severely enough to cause amputation. Stay highly alert."

A butler who consistently used honorifics yet threatened his lady with a blade.

*Swish*

A maid who checked a poison that would kill an ant in agony with just one drop, nodded with satisfaction, and applied it to the lady's candy.
The lady who innocently accepted it and hugged the maid's skirt, saying how nice she was.

'This goes beyond child abuse—it's like they've been hired as assassins. This should be reported to the local lord.'

What circumstances could possibly lead to a girl receiving daily death threats and poisoning attempts from her butler and maid?
Gisele sent people to investigate.

"The cool taste of strawberry shaved ice clears my head and feels refreshing! The sweetness that follows is the best!"
"The harmony between the diced ingredients and curry is fantastic! 10 out of 10!"

Completely unaware, the lady innocently offered food reviews like a professional critic.
After watching this scene with complicated feelings for several days...

"Is she just going to pay 100 coins and buy the ticket?"
"If I'd known this would happen, I should have bet on the outcome."
"Damn it, be honest. You're all in this together, acting to take our pocket money, right?"

The long-term guests who had placed side bets on the outcome of the wager became angry.
Startled by the loud voices, the girl shrank back, and a murderous glint flashed in the butler's eyes.
The butler clenched his gloved fist, making a cracking sound as he walked.

"Watch your mouth. Do you think I, black market merchant Gisele, would be blinded by your petty money and fix a match? For an unofficial side bet I'm not even involved in?"

Gisele shouted before the butler could act.
The long-term guests who had witnessed Gisele's skills firsthand didn't dare raise their voices against him.

"Get out of the inn now. If you don't leave, you'll pay the price for irritating me."

Tickets were valuable items.
A man who had used his skills to obtain dozens of such items wouldn't have come here unprepared.
Several mercenaries employed by Gisele placed their hands on their weapons at nearby tables, glaring at the guests.

"Tch!"
"We'll see about this."
"What a jerk."

The long-term guests left, each muttering a complaint.
Annoyed by these cowardly posers, one of the mercenaries drew his sword.

*Shing*

Startled by the chilling sound, the long-term guests fled in a hurry.
Some even ran away without getting refunds for their remaining accommodation fees from the innkeeper.

"Haha. Cowards."
"They're a disgrace to adventurers."

When the mercenaries turned their heads to scan the room, even those who had been mocking the fleeing guests flinched and averted their gaze.
Unlike adventurers who took on all sorts of miscellaneous requests, mercenaries made combat their livelihood.
The mercenaries hired for security were inevitably judged to be of a higher caliber than adventurers.

"Little lady, please come here."

The girl looked at the butler.
Only after the butler nodded did she scurry over.

"I'm not someone who makes wagers with just anyone."
"Are you disappointed in me?"

Gisele shook his head.
What bothered him was that she maintained the same spirited attitude as before.
It wasn't her bold personality that concerned him.
He felt sorry that her perception had been so warped by harsh trials and life experiences that she didn't even recognize such minor troubles as problems.
His contempt for the butler and maid, those suspicious individuals who had driven the girl to this mental state, also played a part.

"I've seen enough these past few days to know how intensely you live your life. I'm not disappointed, and something like a mere wager wouldn't disappoint me anyway."

Disappointment was far from what he felt.
What he felt now was concern.

"If you ever find yourself with nowhere to go, you can mention my name to black market merchants in any city's back alleys. I'll ensure you have a way to make a living."
"Are you scouting me right now?"

Gisele maintained an expressionless face, aware of the butler and maid guarding their table.

"With your strength and bold personality, you might become a rare great merchant in this business. That's all I was thinking."
"Huh... I've never heard that before."

The girl marveled innocently.
As if to counter her reaction, the butler spoke quietly.

"My lady is scheduled to enter the Academy. We appreciate your offer, but she won't be becoming a merchant."
"That's right. That's not my plan either."
"...I didn't make the offer with great expectations. Just remember it for now. Life doesn't always go according to plan."

The girl tilted her head, then grinned like a mischievous boy and nodded.

"I'll remember that."

He considered trying to persuade her further but soon gave up.
Those child abusers wouldn't let her go easily anyway.
This was enough for now.
If difficulties ever arose for her in the future.
Gisele thought that just knowing there was an adult willing to take her side would be of great help to the lady.


* * *


Several more days passed.
Perhaps because a week had already passed since the ticket exam began, ticket exam candidates who had left the inn started returning one by one.

"I've come to deliver ingredients."
"I'm afraid those ingredients have already been delivered."
"W-what?!"

Of course, most of the mediocre ingredients had already been claimed by Gisele, who had beaten them to it.

"Innkeeper, please give me one more chance. If I leave now, I won't have money for accommodation!"
"Our inn doesn't accept credit. Will you leave on your own, or would you prefer to be thrown out?"
"This year is a lost cause. I'll leave on my own..."

The innkeeper was connected to the examiners.
Disobeying his instructions could result in losing eligibility for next year's exam, which would be a much greater loss.

"Hahaha! Little mouse! You're still hanging around here?"

Amid the cascade of failed ingredient collections, a monkey beastman succeeded in a big way.
Thud!
A container full of sap from a thorny vine monster.
The innkeeper checked the scent and taste, then nodded with satisfaction.

"You brought the right thing."

Senior Examiner Michael took out a ticket emitting a soft golden light from his chest.

"A gold ticket."
"Now that I've passed, I don't have to worry about the examiners anymore, right? How about it, mister? Let's have a match."
"It'll make for good after-meal exercise."

After finishing a plate of rare food made from the ingredients they brought, the two went to the inn's backyard.

THUD!!

After a dull sound and several shouts, the nobleman Michael, wearing his usual bored expression, came back inside and went straight up to the second floor.
In contrast, the monkey beastman returned only an hour later, limping on one foot.
As expected—
It's remarkable he even survived after challenging a senior examiner.
The groaning beastman noticed me and sniffed.

"Lazy one. Haven't even started yet?"
"I don't need to."
"How boring."

The monkey beastman left the inn, carrying his staff as if his business was done.
However, Gisele's gaze toward me contained a strange admiration.
It was because he had witnessed my bold act while the monkey beastman was competing with the examiner.

"Was that the opportunity the little lady was waiting for?"
"That's right."

The menu board reserved for examiners, which was always taken back up to the second floor after ordering.
During the brief moment when the examiner left his seat for the duel with the monkey beastman, I opened the menu board.
And I wrote down a <New Menu> that hadn't existed before.

A veteran player's strategy that transcends luck.
A <Special Menu> that forcibly creates the desired mission.
