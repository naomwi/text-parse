# 202 - The Reason for Slipping Ranks

Arcadia asked before crossing the river.

"Why did you make such a strange contract?"
"You mean about not starting the riding competition until 100 people pass through first?"
"Yeah, that."
"If I pass through alone, I'd get good grades, but that's a trap!"
"A trap?"
"Remember how the Principal's Teachings lecture didn't mention any specific exam?"
"I remember."
"Well, it's actually taken together with the Advanced Class Physical Training!"
"...One exam determines grades for two lectures?"
"Yes! And they also calculate the rankings of people in your team, as well as students in the same group! So if someone from the frontier passes through alone, their grades will suffer."

Arcadia couldn't understand.
Instead of resolving her questions, they only multiplied.

"How do you know all this?"
"I have a friend who's good at eavesdropping!"

Eavesdropping on the Principal, of all people?
The more she thought about it, the more absurd the answer seemed.

"So you're not going to cross?"
"I won't turn down the chance for good grades."
"Irene will cross too. She won't let the hero get good grades, right?"
"...That can't happen. Leave the interference to me."

With that, all the frontrunners crossed the river.
Thanks to them, Oknodie felt relieved.

"Heheh. Easy pickings."

All the elements needed to fulfill the area-control meta of single-handedly blocking the student army were now complete.
Send the powerful competitors ahead first.
Get divine certification to prevent hostile NPCs from returning and causing trouble.
Hide magic trees under the river to catch people off guard.
From now on, whether students entering the river can cross or not depends entirely on her will.

'I might lose some points, but I'll make up for it in other ways!'

Using the branches of the magic trees, she caught plenty of underwater monsters swimming in the river.

Ink Octopus, an octopus monster that sprays ink when touched.
Sticky Squid, a squid monster that clings tightly to bodies.
Star Pentagon, a starfish monster that spins through the air and body-slams moving objects.

All seafood that can be grilled and eaten.

She'd teach those Imperial students a lesson.
Help out the frontier students.
Earn exam points at the same time.
And collect entries for her guidebook simultaneously.
If things go well, while the advance party of 100 wastes time looking for their escaped mounts, she might be the first to ride a golem to the finish line!
That would mean effortlessly passing two Advanced Class exams at once.
Gaining so many benefits at once—isn't this the true mark of a veteran player?
Oknodie's shoulders rose proudly as she basked in her self-satisfaction.

Looking at the Imperial faction students and second-years who were just staring blankly, having lost their will to fight, Arcadia had no choice but to gratefully accept Oknodie's consideration.

"Didn't you see Zuang and Xing on your way here?"
"They're probably still running."

In there.
Pointing to the round forest below the circular staircase, Arcadia couldn't help but smile bitterly.
The path doesn't actually form a circle but curves slightly inward like a spiral, so eventually they would reach the stairs and the river.
But following the path properly would waste an unbelievable amount of time.
A test that values wisdom over strength.
The structure ensures that fools who only use physical strength will inevitably get lower grades.
But for the frontier group to score highly overall, even the fools need to score in the upper ranks.

"I guess there's no choice. We'll just have to wait a few more hours!"
"Oknodie. Don't tell me you're planning to guard this place until all the frontier students cross?"
"Of course!"

Her expression seemed to ask why that was even a question.

'Foolish child. Why do you always choose to put yourself at a disadvantage?'

Is group belonging really that important?
Because she couldn't form attachments growing up in the Foundation.
Because she doesn't want to miss out on bonds that form without having to kill or be killed.
Is that why she's forcing herself and desperately taking this exam?
Arcadia made a decision.
She would protect this foolishly pure child.

"You all go ahead. I'll stay here and help Oknodie."
"Arcadia gave me rib-flavored canned food, lobster-flavored canned food, and scallop-flavored canned food! You think I'd leave her behind?"
"The teachings of the Lightning Thunder God Church don't specifically say to repay kindness with kindness, but as a matter of human decency, I wish to help. You won't stop me from doing that, will you?"

Starting with the beastkin fighter Jenya and the Lightning Thunder God Church nun Nise, the male students following the Grand Duke's son also expressed their willingness to help.

"So that's how it is, Di. You'll accept our help, right?"
"Hmm... No!"

Oknodie refused with a bright smile.
The students stood with blank faces.
Behind them.
Magic trees emerged from underwater, extending their branches to place the students on pillars.
The result was that they ended up floating across the river like boat people until they reached the other side!
Watching this scene, the instructor took out a scoring sheet and began busily writing something.
Arcadia could only hope that it wouldn't result in point deductions.
