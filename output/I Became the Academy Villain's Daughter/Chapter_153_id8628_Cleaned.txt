# 153 - The Moment It Becomes Sincere

1.
Isabel didn't like the butler in the tailcoat.
First of all, his face was intimidating.
His body was also disturbing due to the excessively developed muscles that seemed out of place for an ordinary butler.
As for his aura, he was frightening and menacing enough to be mistaken for a henchman of an evil organization, or rather, an executive.

'His mouth is smiling, but his eyes definitely aren't.'

Even compared to Gisele, who also always wore a smile, there was a stark difference.
Isabel realized that the difference lay in consideration.
Gisele always cared about how his face appeared to others.
He knew that a contradictory expression—smiling with the mouth but not with the eyes—created discomfort in others.
So Gisele smiled with his eyes too.
To ease the tension of others and make the atmosphere more gentle.
Jonna Wiheomhae.
The man Oknodie introduced as her butler was different.
A face that smiled with the mouth but not with the eyes.
Did he not know how his face appeared?
That couldn't be.
Look at that intimidating face and developed physique.
No matter how oblivious one might be, growing up seeing people flinch around you would naturally make you aware.
How others would perceive this expression, what effect this expression could have.

'He's doing this deliberately.'

That man was clearly exercising intimidation.
Even toward her, Oknodie's friend.
Toward other students.
Toward the guardians of the upper-class students.

"A student from the frontier? Hmm, well, at least you've lived diligently."
"Honor Fried Chicken. I don't think this would be the case, but you haven't been fraternizing with those country bumpkins from the frontier, have you?"

She didn't like the upper-class guardians either.
All of them were from noble families or high-ranking officials.
They openly looked down on the frontier.
Looking down on guardians from the frontier was just a bonus.
Yet these arrogant people, while showing their discomfort toward the intimidating butler, didn't directly mock or point him out.
It was evident they were being careful not to accidentally get involved with him.

"The Wiheomhae Foundation must be quite something. Even the three great meritorious families of the Empire are cautious around them."

Tension rose at Gisele's words.
This already dangerous man was somehow keeping an eye on her and Son O-cheon.
It didn't bode well.
Jonna approached as soon as the homeroom lecture ended.

"Everyone, say hello! This is Butler Jonna!"

Thanks to Oknodie, who enthusiastically introduced him, they ended up exchanging greetings.
Son O-cheon, sensitive to killing intent, didn't show his usual playfulness and was clutching the staff on his back with one hand, his face tense.

"There's no need to be so tense. I am a representative of the Wiheomhae Foundation. Our foundation is an organization with childcare services that provide opportunities for underprivileged children to grow, and scholarship programs that help them enter higher education institutions."
"We know what you do to your scholarship students."
"Isabel?"
"Stay out of this, Oknodie."
"Little mouse, listen to Isabel here."

Isabel nodded slightly to Son O-cheon in gratitude.
She then confronted Jonna directly.

"Do you think we'd look favorably on a foundation that turns prospective scholarship students into prostitutes or slaves if they don't follow training, and uses scholarship students for experiments or terrorism if they don't perform well?"

Oknodie, whose ears were covered by Son O-cheon's large hands, looked puzzled, as if floating a question mark above her head.
Despite her expressive face showing the tension dissipating, the atmosphere flowing between Isabel and Jonna was extremely dangerous.

"It seems there's been a misunderstanding."
"Explain yourself if you can."
"Our Lady is a top scholarship student. She is the greatest talent with unprecedented abilities in the foundation's history. How could we not cherish and care for such a Lady?"
"...Is that all?"
"However, I do have complaints about her social circle. Won't the Lady feel the sorrow of losing companions if weak humans like you are around her?"

She hadn't expected a proper explanation.
She didn't think they were that kind of organization anyway.
But she hadn't expected it to be this bad.

"You should have something else to say. That's how it should be. Why aren't you denying what you've done!"

She had hoped he would deny it.
Deny that they threatened to turn prospective scholarship students into prostitutes or slaves.
Deny that they used students who didn't perform well for experiments or terrorism.
Deny that they were such an evil organization.
She had hoped it was all a misunderstanding.
Jonna didn't deny any of the allegations.
He only threatened them in return.
Stay away if you don't want to lose your life.
Don't be a nuisance.

"Keep this in mind. As of today, by the judgment of me, 'Jonna Wiheomhae,' a foundation representative, you have become obstacles to 'Oknodie,' an important person of the foundation."
"You think we'll yield to such threats? You're the ones who should be prepared. We will definitely free Oknodie from the clutches of your evil foundation."

As Isabel was about to lash out more with an agitated face, Gisele grabbed her forearm.

"Why are you stopping me? You know too! What they've done to Oknodie...!"
"Now is not the time. Look over there."

Gisele pointed to where Son O-cheon was covering Oknodie's ears.

"No, this little mouse! Don't use your strength!"
"Why are you having secret conversations without me! I want to hear too!"
"Ack! My wrist hurts, you punk. Don't grab my bones!"

Son O-cheon was groaning in pain, his arms trembling as Oknodie tried with all her might to remove his hands.
This was as far as they could raise their voices in front of Oknodie.

"You don't know. What our foundation is capable of doing."
"It's probably some dirty trick anyway."
"If you don't want to experience those dirty tricks firsthand, stay away from our Lady. This verbal warning is the last."

Jonna turned to leave after delivering his warning.

"Ah, wait for me!"
"I apologize, but I think the visit should end here. Until our next meeting, I hope you stay healthy, my Lady."
"Heeeng. You're leaving already? Stay a little longer."

Isabel felt a huge wall as she watched Oknodie push Son O-cheon away and run after Jonna, circling around him and whining.
This affectionate side that she hadn't shown even to Isabel, who had been building rapport by cooking for her every day.
Oknodie was quite dependent on that man.
Mentally.
Probably from a very young age.
There must have been education bordering on brainwashing.
Today's events confirmed it.
The foundation was exactly the kind of organization they thought it was.
I can't forgive the foundation or that man.
Her resolve grew stronger as she welcomed Oknodie, who was returning alone with slumped shoulders.


* *


Jonna had left.
She had wanted to introduce him to other professors too.
Unfortunately, he said he had to leave because one visitation ticket wasn't enough time.

"Can't you steal tickets from someone who has a lot?"
"Unfortunately, now is not a good time. Next time, I will definitely prepare plenty of tickets by any means necessary, my Lady."
"Promise?"

Jonna nodded.
It was reassuring because he was a man who always kept his promises.
She was already looking forward to next month.

"Then see you next time, Jonna! Make sure to send my regards to Leape!"

After seeing Jonna off, she headed to the eye-training lecture room where Isabel and Gisele were waiting.

"Did you say goodbye to your butler well?"
"It's so disappointing. Others could have spent a whole day at the Academy if they came early in the morning, but I only got to spend half a day with him..."
"But we are here with you. We all care for Oknodie equally. We'll take care of you in place of your butler."
"Pfft. Uncle Gisele wants to be a butler? That was funny, but it's absolutely impossible, so give up!"

Gisele showed curiosity.

"Is the role of a butler that difficult?"
"Of course! Jonna grants whatever I ask for."
"Just hearing that makes you sound like a spoiled young lady."
"But isn't that what a butler is supposed to be? Someone who grants whatever you ask for."
"...At times like this, I'm reminded that Oknodie is indeed the daughter of a noble family."
"No, it's not like that. The foundation might be a bit wealthy, but it's not enough to be called nobility."
"Why not?"
"Because there's no food named after us as a mascot!"

Titosoga, who had been waiting in front of the lecture room and eavesdropping on their conversation, suddenly joined in.

"I've been wondering about that for a while. Why do all the human nobles living beyond the mountains have food names as their surnames?"
"When the Demon King of Famine was at the height of his power, there was no food as we know it in the territories occupied by demons. So they created special dishes with locally sourced ingredients during their subjugation campaign, and as a way to honor the achievements of the first-generation nobles, they use food surnames!"
"Huh. There was such an amazing origin."
"But these days, there's no such thing, and since all nobles use food surnames, the trend is to create a new food and name it after the noble!"
"Oknodie, does that mean I could become a noble too if I create a new dish?"

Oknodie grinned.

"Why not! If you develop a new dish to add to the food compendium, you can receive noble status in any country depending on its versatility and grade."
"How do you know so much about this, Oknodie?"
"Well... this is a secret, so don't tell anyone, okay? I'm planning to develop a dish myself if I ever need a noble title!"

The conversation was drifting off-topic, which helped improve the atmosphere, and Isabel, who had been listening quietly, showed shock in her eyes.

"Oknodie, you can cook?"
"Ah."

Oknodie awkwardly looked around and then laughed nervously.

"It's not like I was just too lazy to cook myself and only ate what others made! It's just that Isabel's cooking was delicious."
"How could this be..."
"Isabel...? Are you angry?"

Isabel's hands trembled with shock.
Oknodie could cook.
It wasn't that she felt betrayed.
It was because she realized an important fact.
She had been confident.
Confident that Oknodie wouldn't leave her side because she liked the food she made.
She had even taken pride in the thought that she would protect Oknodie from the foundation's evil temptations through the power of food.
But if Oknodie could cook?
Then it didn't matter if she was there or not.
Oknodie could leave her side anytime.

'I need to get stronger. Much stronger than now.'

She clenched her trembling hands into fists and made a resolution.
She would improve her cooking skills to the point where Oknodie wouldn't even think about cooking for herself.
It was the moment when cooking, which had remained simply in the realm of a hobby, became a sincere pursuit.
