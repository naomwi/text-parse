# 378 - Hector's Divine Group

When I realized that Dorothy, Rockfell, and Yui's random starting points could be more impressive than expected, I suddenly became curious.

"By the way, Dorothy, which forest are you from?"
"One of the Three Forbidden Forests!"

The Three Forbidden Forests.
A term referring to three forests designated as forbidden areas where one should not set foot.
The territory of the Ein race, the Western Great Forest.
The territory of the demon race, the Northern Demon Forest.
The territory of chaos species, the Monster Forest of the Dimensional Rift.

"Since you're not friendly with the Ein race, it can't be the Great Forest, and since I don't detect any dark mana, it must be the Monster Forest. Am I right?"
"Wow. You got it in one try? As expected, Oknodie knows everything. Or is it the Foundation that knows everything?"
"Wow. Then Rockfell and Yui must have become incredibly strong in this round too."
"Rockfell is an idiot. Just swinging a sword quickly doesn't mean you can defeat all monsters."

Rockfell, who had been standing by Dorothy's side, scratched below his nose embarrassedly.

"You're not wrong. The Monster Forest is full of large species monsters, and there are even ultra-large species that can flip the terrain and seasons when they awaken."
"Well, it is difficult to handle such giant creatures with just a sword in the early to mid-stages!"
"Early to mid-stages...? Ah. You mean early to mid-teens."

Rockfell wasn't as quick-witted as Arcadia and fell for it easily.

"I'm already in my late teens, but I think that with your skills, you might be able to handle even ultra-large species somehow when you reach my age. Just like the hero did."
"Did the previous hero visit the Monster Forest too?"
"I only heard the sound from a distance. Both Dorothy and I were still young then."

So the previous hero Nyarlathotep was surprisingly diligent! I should beg Professor Destroyer to tell me the story later.
Through the library expedition, spirits gain old fairy tales, and veterans gain lore from the setting's past.
Huh?
Aren't my operating principles similar to those of fairies...?
Maybe I'm actually a fairy!
I was giggling at this happy thought when I noticed Dorothy still glaring somewhere with a displeased face.

"Are you that concerned about Yui?"
"It's not just the smell of money."

From over there came the smell of blood too.
And it was the different blood scents of many, many people.


* * *

What I thought was darkness wasn't because we had entered the forest at night.
It was because the overwhelming malice beneath the dense branches drove away all the bright energy of the world, creating a high-density dark zone where even moonlight couldn't reach.
The light from the torches couldn't extend beyond 2 meters around the students and was greedily swallowed by the darkness.

R-run away.
That monster is beyond our ability to handle.
The library is not something first-years should challenge!

The students who had been opening and closing their mouths as if screaming looked at each other with surprised eyes.
No sound was coming out.
As if they were being devoured.
When people face a crisis, they instinctively understand the cause of danger.
Now they realized that "Pet Tree Poppy" was the entity consuming their sounds.

Blood splattered.
A sharp branch pierced through one student's forearm.
When a friend drew his sword and cut the branch, blood gushed out from it.
The students became even more terrified.
How could cutting a branch cause blood to pour out like a human's!

<Troy Royal Family 5th Tier Vision Magic>
<Light, Shine Down>

Dazzling light from Hector's drawn sword drove away the darkness and raised the morale that had been plummeting to the bottom of Hector's Divine Group.
Instead of speaking, Hector stepped forward and swung his sword at that grotesque tree.
Facing a tree that blocked vocalization to prevent skill activation and spell casting, Hector's spell mastery had reached such perfection that he could cast magic without incantation.
His determination to surpass the fusion lifeform Cassia and advance to the upper class succeeded in unleashing magic against the chapter boss of <Chapter 4: Silent Death, Forest of Silence> that would normally appear during the second-year summer vacation.
If Oknodie had seen this, she would have sparkled at Hector's brilliant growth, but Pet Tree Poppy did not rejoice at its opponent's strength.

[Dizziness][Vomiting][Fever][Loss of Balance][Chills][Itching][Numbness][Suffocation]

Curses accumulating at a frightening speed by the second.
As Pet Tree Poppy exerted its power with determination, the students who had rushed in behind Hector collapsed or convulsed while vomiting blood.
While the students in the rear frantically tried to rescue their friends, Hector suffered all those curses at the closest range and highest output.

Lead the way!
We cannot leave Lord Hector alone!

The loyal Deiphobus followed with his spear.
His judgment was correct.
Pet Tree Poppy's curses decreased in power when shared among many recipients.
For Hector's sake, they needed to endure the immediate pain.
Only then could they temporarily repel that crazy tree, if not kill it, and open a path.
Although his voice was swallowed by Pet Tree Poppy and reached no one, Deiphobus's determination to not retreat despite suffering from the curses in real-time gave courage back to the students.
Hector's Divine Group was brave.
Though they were lower-class students who had been left behind and abandoned, Hector had taken them all in.
With their leader Hector and the loyal Deiphobus taking the initiative to face the greatest difficulties, it was impossible not to feel respect as their subordinates.

Tsk tsk tsk.

Hector's sword flashed several times with magic, cutting away more than a dozen branches.
When cursed items hanging from the branches like Christmas tree ornaments fell with a thud, the other students gained more courage and rushed in.

"Push a little more!"
"Huh? I can hear sounds again!"
"The monster's power must be weakening!"

It was an illusion.
Hector, fighting at the very front, turned pale.

"Everyone, cover your ears!"

A horrific scream erupted from the vicious mouth that appeared on the tree trunk.
Pet Tree Poppy's new pattern <Flood of Terror> released all the consumed sounds at once.
Students who failed to cover their ears collapsed foaming at the mouth, and even those who covered their ears trembled with fear, unable to raise their swords above waist height.

"Forget about repelling it. Everyone, support those who have fallen and run beyond the gate!"

As if to say it wouldn't be that easy, Pet Tree Poppy raised huge roots from underground.
Facing the spectacle of roots rising high like earthen walls, Deiphobus said with a hardened face:

"We should abandon the library expedition, Your Highness. If we try to advance any further, there will be seriously injured subordinates."

"Ugh. What is this crazy academy thinking, making the library like this? Are the weak ones not even qualified to look at reference materials!"

"Do you need help?"

At this inappropriately calm question, Prince Hector glared angrily at the owner of the voice.

"Senior Tetraphos. Are you just going to stand by and watch?"
"I'd feel bad interrupting when you're fighting so hard."
"Please help us."
"What will you pay me with?"
"...!"

Did he really need compensation even in this situation?
The words that had risen to the tip of his tongue sank back down when he saw the snake-like cold eyes that looked like they were targeting prey, despite the mouth that was smiling as if finding this interesting.
A devil.
This senior was truly a devilish character.
The juniors' reckless adventure and the adversity they faced as the price of their rashness—none of it mattered to him.
Even if a few first-years died here, he would just laugh uproariously and mock them for being too weak, never regretting it.

"What do you want?"
"Points would be nice. Or I could do it for free."

...What does he mean?
Did he suddenly feel compassion?
But it's strange.
Why would such a ruthless senior suddenly offer help for free?
This is dangerous.
A very dangerous smell.

"With points..."
"Would it kill you to help us for free!"

Instead of Hector, who was about to answer that he would pay points even if it meant pushing himself, Deiphobus, who had been fending off the waves of branches with his spear, shouted roughly during the moment Hector's hand paused.
He couldn't be blamed.
Deiphobus's forearms were scratched by the killing intent that he couldn't completely block while stopping the branches, and blood was dripping through his broken arm guards.
But the feared event finally happened.
The students who had been hesitating in front of the roots screamed again.
But this attack wasn't from Poppy.
Even Pet Tree Poppy stared blankly with its knot holes that served as eyes, as if wondering what was happening.

Slash!

Threads flew along the path of Tetraphos's hand gestures, digging into the students' skin and drawing blood.
An act that wouldn't kill or seriously injure them, but they couldn't avoid the pain!
Especially since the surprise attack came from a senior they had trusted, all students were helplessly injured.

"What on earth are you doing!"
"You asked for help, right? For free. So I drew blood. First-year blood doesn't count as points."
"How can you call that help!"
"Haha. Don't get so heated, juniors. This is what's called the <Cruel Blood Manipulation Technique>."

Tetraphos's blood-stained threads glowed bright red and shot out in crisscrossing layers, maintaining formation.
Everyone was astonished when the roots blocking their path were cut down in an instant, as if sieved.

"There, one serving complete. Do you need more help?"

Pet Tree Poppy's assault had stopped as it writhed in pain after losing its roots.
Hector glared at Deiphobus, warning him not to say a word.
Of course, Deiphobus, with a face paler than when he was bleeding, couldn't escape his guilt over the disaster his rash outburst had caused.
The battle was lost.
They needed to either break through the gate or defend, one or the other.
Before everyone collapsed from excessive bleeding, whether at the hands of the monster or the senior.
But even after losing its roots, Pet Tree Poppy attacked again.
By raising eight more roots from all directions, the same size as the one it had just lost.

"Ah, seriously. I didn't want to show my hand in a place like this!"

The one who stepped forward in this desperate crisis was Yui.
Everyone had assumed her combat ability would be negligible since she was from a merchant group and worked as a guide.
