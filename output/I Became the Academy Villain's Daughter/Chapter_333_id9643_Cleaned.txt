# Enhanced Translation: Chapter 333 - Reckless Challenge 2

"Everyone, take out the mana board from your drawer."

Professor Pythagoras thought the "Casting Magic through Knowledge Assessment" lecture was tedious.

"In this world, there's a term called 'magic user.' Those who can't gather mana and instead create magical effects by tearing scrolls and throwing potion vials. Mana board users aren't fundamentally different. However, from an efficiency standpoint, mana board users have the worst cost-effectiveness. First three to answer, give me the reason."

A second-year student with wild, unkempt hair—looking like some bizarre creature who had saved time by skipping haircuts to study—slowly raised his hand.

"Unlike real magic, spells that rely on equipment are difficult to control."

The professor's eyes twisted fiercely, as if berating him for offering such a pathetic answer.
Not wanting to miss their chance, two other second-years quickly raised their hands.

"The casting time can't be shortened!"
"Target designation for targeting spells is impossible!"
"All wrong."

With a flick of Professor Pythagoras's hand, the three second-years who had raised their hands and were now trying to lower their heads and avoid eye contact suddenly floated into the air.

"Wha-what?"
"My body!"
"Stop exaggerating. Inferior students learn knowledge through their bodies, not their minds. Feel your ignorance physically until the lecture ends."

The three wrong-answerers flailed in midair before reaching the classroom ceiling and stopping their movements.
They froze in fear, knowing that if they moved carelessly and fell, they could easily break a bone from that height.
They were fools unworthy of becoming second-years.
Failures who deserved to be held back immediately to satisfy his frustration.

'Most students who need to cast magic through knowledge assessment are this pathetic.'

Professor Pythagoras himself was a magic user who couldn't use magic through normal methods. But his case was different from these students who lacked both passion and talent.

"Using a mana board makes control difficult. Casting time can't be shortened. Target designation for targeting spells is impossible. Who decided such things?"

Brilliant light leaked from the mana board in the professor's hand.
The second-years hanging from the ceiling plummeted toward the classroom floor, screaming.

"If control were truly difficult, your bones would have shattered upon impact with the ground."

As the professor placed his hand on the mana board, the second-years floated back into the air.

"Let's go twice as fast."
"We're sorry, Professor!!"
"We'll never give wrong answers again!!"
"Save us!!"

The second-years' cries grew louder.
It was already terrifying, but falling at an even faster speed would surely mean collision with the ground.
Despite their desperate pleas, the professor lifted them into the air without a hint of mercy or hesitation.
Then he released his power.
The seniors fell, unable even to scream.
The scene was so brutal that the other second-years watching turned pale and trembled.
Just before impact, with their eyes tightly shut, the mana board's magic stopped them in midair.

"If casting time couldn't be shortened, you would have been half-crippled from falling with an acceleration spell."

Of course, injuries sustained during lectures qualify for educational injury compensation and free treatment at the infirmary.
The professor's non-comforting words weren't reassuring at all.
It sounded more like a threat that he really could half-cripple them if he wanted to!

"Sob sob sob. I hate this."
"Please forgive us... waaah."
"Why did I ever... take this lecture..."

The three rose into the air again.
Having realized this process was educational correction through physical means, the students held their breath in tension.
Sharp spikes magically appeared beneath them on the ground.

"I'll cast a protective barrier."

Suddenly, as if the professor had made a mistake, the three students fell in different directions.
The wider the range of a barrier spell, the longer it takes to cast.
Moreover, this wasn't centered on himself but had to be cast simultaneously on three falling targets.
The students' eyes filled with horror.

"No!"
"Oh my god."
"I... I can't watch this."

Students screamed or turned away, anticipating a horrific accident.
The sound of something hitting the spikes echoed through the classroom.

"Open your eyes."

Above the bed of spikes, the three wrong-answerers had landed in different spots, each surrounded by a protective barrier.

"If target designation for targeting spells were impossible, I couldn't have saved all three of you simultaneously. Understand?"

The second-years nodded frantically while crying.
Even among the seated students watching, sniffling could be heard.
Lessons learned through fear are never forgotten.
At the very least, the possibility of mana boards was clearly imprinted in the minds of those who answered incorrectly.
Of course, if they couldn't overcome this moment's terror, the memory might become trauma, causing hesitation whenever they cast magic in the future.
They might become even slower, more timid, and more pathetic than they already were.
Professor Pythagoras didn't care.
If students were going to drop out from this much, it was better they gave up sooner rather than later.
In the world of magic where talent is everything, those lacking willpower could never survive.

"Oknodie. I'm scared..."
"Why? The professor is kind and good. If you don't know something, you should learn it physically."

What polar opposite reactions from these novices.
A sheep and a wolf.
One typical first-year and one not so typical.
Professor Pythagoras became curious about that bold first-year's capabilities.

"Novices. Try to guess the reason."
"Is that allowed?"

Not "please spare me" or "I don't know," but "is that allowed?"
Even her boldness was appealing.

"It is."
"What will you give me if I succeed?"
"Extra credit. But if you fail, you'll learn physically like the second-years did."
"I'll do it!"

The girl with a ribbon headband that suited her well.
Oknodie answered confidently.

"The reason mana boards have poor cost-effectiveness is because they require an overwhelmingly greater amount and depth of knowledge than regular wizards! Unless you completely understand the principles or study extensively, the efficiency is terrible!"

The professor asked:

"Do you think that's correct?"
"Yep!"
"That is correct. I'll give you extra credit."

Titosoga, sitting next to her, sighed with relief more dramatically than Oknodie, placing a hand on his chest.

"Hey, are you crazy? Next time just say you don't know. The professor doesn't half-kill students who admit their ignorance."
"But I knew the answer."

Of course, the professor heard Bigstone and Oknodie's whispered conversation.

"That's right, Bigstone. Why is ignorance forgiven when wrong answers are not?"
"I-I don't know, sir."
"You practice what you preach. The three wrong-answerers from earlier, come forward and hold a mana board."

The three wrong-answerers approached with trembling legs.

"Do you know the 3rd-tier spell 'Strength'?"

All three quickly said they didn't know.
The professor gave them a heart-stabbing, despair-inducing response.

"Then I'll make you learn. Hold the mana board and use Strength. For idiots who don't even know what the spell is, I'll tell you it's an enhancement spell that increases physical strength."

An enhancement buff-type spell that alters physical condition.
Strength.
The 3rd-tier spell unfolded through the three students' mana boards.

Neither high-tier nor exactly low-tier, but a tricky spell.
One student's mana board emitted light but nothing happened.
The spell structure was so chaotic that the magic showed no signs of manifesting.
He was actually the lucky one.
A curly-haired female student who managed to activate the spell turned rapidly pale.
The spell structure barely formed, but mana leaking from incorrect formulas dramatically increased the required mana.
After barely completing the spell, she collapsed gasping for breath, as if exhausted.
The last student formed a strange pattern based on incorrect knowledge, but it did form a structurally complete shape.
Strength was definitely activated.
Instead of evenly increasing strength throughout the body, it concentrated power only in one forearm, causing muscles to suddenly swell. Feeling pain like a cramp, he grabbed his arm, screaming and rolling on the floor.

"Failed spell implementation. Increased mana consumption. Incorrect manifestation. This is the 'price of ignorance' that magic users face when they ignore their lack of knowledge and attempt to use magic."

In the harsh world of magic users, attempts to guess or rely on talent alone were not tolerated.

"Memorize this. These are the theory, formulas, and modification formulas needed to control the spell you'll use today."

With one gesture from the professor, printouts about 30 pages each landed on everyone's desks.
The professor didn't distribute them himself; his assistant, who followed him like a limb, used a mana board to do it.
The second-years desperately studied the printouts, while Titosoga muttered, "Tricked again," flipping through pages with a gloomy face as if receiving a death sentence.
In the classroom filled with the sound of turning pages, one student caught Professor Pythagoras's eye.

"Do you have time to spare?"

Instead of reading the printout, Oknodie was folding it into a paper airplane.
Even faced with the weight of theory and knowledge that would make talented wizards run away, Oknodie showed no fear or irritation.

"I already know it all!"

The 3rd-tier spell Strength.
The first-year's confidence in the spell that had just sunk three second-year seniors seemed extremely peculiar.
A wizard who hasn't trained their body won't get much efficiency from Strength.
There's a fundamental difference between increasing a strength of 10 by 1.2 times versus increasing a strength of 30 by 1.2 times.
That's why most wizards, including magic users, avoid the Strength spell.
A spell with very little chance of accumulating knowledge.
However, in practical situations, using enhancement spells to support allies is far more beneficial than trying to use attack spells with rapid calculations and perfect theory.
A spell everyone hates to learn but would never regret knowing.
How could he not be interested in this barely 130cm tall novice who showed interest in such a "useless" spell?

"Try it. If you succeed, you may leave today's lecture. We'll measure by the score change on the punching machine."
"If I punch lightly at first and then harder later, I wouldn't need to use magic, right?"
"If you think you can deceive me, try. If caught, you'll suffer worse than your seniors did."

At the professor's threat, Oknodie observed Professor Pythagoras intently, as if measuring something.

"Hmm... impossible! I'll be honest."

Oknodie hit the punching machine.
The mana field inside the machine broke through 47 layers at once.
An incredible score that made the second-year wizards' previous year average of 22 layers seem ridiculous.

"...But Strength has clearly been dispelled."
"That was just my normal punch without magic."

After her mana board emitted light, Oknodie punched the machine again.
The mana field broke through 58 layers at once.

"Excellent. You may leave now."
"Can I stay and watch what Titosoga does?"
"By all means."

Encouraged second-years quietly lined up in front of the punching machine.

"For reference, anyone who wants to finish the lecture early and leave must break the highest record set so far."

All the second-years, including Titosoga and Bigstone, looked at Oknodie with expressions that said "that's too much" after hearing this thunderbolt of news.
