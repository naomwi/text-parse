# 409 - Following the Trail

The senior from the Production Department regretted her actions.

'Was I too harsh on that child?'

No, her wariness had been justified.
From the beginning, wasn't it suspicious that such a young child appeared in the sabbatical students' zone without a badge?
But when she realized the visitor had left without touching the supplies she'd placed outside the window, she felt guilty.

'Could I have misunderstood?'

Maybe she really was just a first-year who was genuinely worried about her close senior and risked danger to find her.
As she entertained these softer thoughts, a colleague staying in the same area clicked his tongue and advised her.

"Get a grip. You're losing your rationality because she's a child."
"Aren't you worried about her? Even if we're blinded by points, we don't need to lose our humanity."
"You think I lack compassion? If she were a proper sabbatical student who had completed a full year, I would have taught her how to get a point loan and helped her advance to second year."
"Then why treat her that way?"
"Have you forgotten the circumstances of her appearance? It's the middle of the semester. She appeared from beyond the turbulent air during what amounts to the most serious disaster we've experienced."

Only after hearing her colleague's words did she realize what she had failed to notice.

"Someone skilled enough to pass through a disaster?"
"Beyond that, the person who entered might be the one who caused the disaster in the first place."
"No way. She's far too young."
"Is there a law that says the age you see is real?"
"I... had a conversation with her right in front of me."
"She might be so incredibly skilled that she could fool you even face-to-face."

That was truly... frightening just to imagine.
A skilled individual capable of causing disasters alone, appearing in the sabbatical students' zone.
The thought that a new magical disaster might be added to this already hellish place made her feel her carefully maintained resolve crumbling.
This place was too dangerous.
Too dangerous for those without talent to endure through mere stubbornness and spite.

"Should I... give up on advancing?"
"Do what you want. I'm going to try to hold out for one more year."

Though her colleague spoke that way, from his heavy expression as he turned away, she sensed that one colleague from the safe zone would disappear.
To think that a shoemaker who could safely traverse the thorn-filled mounds mutated by magical disasters would be gone.
Surviving here would become even more difficult from next month.
With a bitter feeling suppressed deep inside, she reached out to retrieve the supplies she had left outside the window, but her hand swept through empty air.

"Huh? They were definitely here."

The supplies had vanished without a trace.
Not a single footprint remained on the ground.
As she felt a chill at this ghostly occurrence, several new entrants appeared from beyond the fog.
An Eastern swordsman who maintained a sharp aura despite bleeding profusely from his head.
A black knight in full armor taking rough steps while unable to hide his indignation.
Seeing these students all without badges, she had a terrifying thought.

'There's no way first-years could appear in large numbers through such a powerful disaster-level mana fluctuation. These must be seniors from much higher years who removed their badges and are pretending to be new students.'

Where worry had been building, only fear remained now.
She should leave with her colleague.
Today, two sabbatical students' dreams of advancement were folded away.


* * *


Zuang "locally procured" the supplies openly placed in the sabbatical students' zone.
Even after examining them with her observational skills, she saw no signs of contract fraud like Velocasio's.

'These seniors are really kind-hearted.'

Thanks to this, she discovered the common thread that Oknodie was heading toward where the compass pointed.

'That place... a magical disaster?'

Despite being the shortest in the Academy, those tiny footsteps were incredibly difficult to follow.
She was even tougher than the necromancer Hestia, which was exasperating.

'Is she stupid? Deliberately seeking out a magical disaster that others try their hardest to avoid.'

When magic imbued with strong will is repeatedly activated in a space, that will becomes imprinted on the land and space.
When multiple such wills exist and conflict with each other, periodic or permanent magical disasters tend to occur in that area.

Zuang knew typical examples of magical disasters.
In the Cheonryeong Mountains where the Great Witch lived, there exists a magical disaster called "Ghost Valley" where the spirits of dead monsters roam and cause all sorts of incidents.
It's a magical disaster hostile to living beings, causing them to get lost in the mountains and fall to their deaths.
Near the resting place of the Great Pirate where pirates hide, the conflicting wills of mermaids and slave mages created a magical disaster called "Nightmare Song."
It's a magical disaster where ships lured by the song inevitably crash into reefs and sink, making deaf helmsmen essential.

'I wonder how dangerous that place is?'

After staring intently, she employed the "Discerning Eye" she had learned from Professor Bronze's lecture.

━━━
[Unceasing Sandstorm]
A localized anomaly limited to a 1.5km radius.
High risk of death due to rapid deterioration of pulmonary function from fine dust penetrating the lungs.
━━━

One must pass through while holding their breath.
Naturally, an ordinary person without mana cultivation techniques could only last about 1-2 minutes before running out of breath and falling victim to the fine dust.
The only option was to use mana cultivation techniques to collect large amounts of oxygen in the surrounding space, then breathe repeatedly within one's own domain isolated from the fine dust.

'Now I understand why enrolled students shouldn't enter the sabbatical students' zone.'

This dangerously treacherous place would be perfect for causing the death of a lower-class novice who wandered in carelessly.

'Neither Oknodie nor I are novices though.'

Her mana cultivation technique, deployed as easily as breathing, drew in the surrounding air.
This was nothing unfamiliar to an assassin.
When jumping down from high ground and landing, when leaping through windows, when disappearing silently before an enemy's eyes.
By stealing the air, one could remove the medium through which sound travels, securing stealth.
A domain where only oneself could breathe was as familiar to an assassin as solitude.

'You're the same, aren't you, Oknodie?'


* * *


Shortly after Zuang chased Oknodie into the mana disaster, an enormous energy forcibly expanded the closing boundary and opened the door leading to the sabbatical students' zone.
The sabbatical students who were startled and peering outside from the safety of their windows were greatly alarmed when they identified the intruders.

"None of them have badges."
"So many first-years entered the sabbatical students' zone? What's going on? Are they on some kind of field trip?"
"Idiot, say something that makes sense. Field trips are only necessary for those inferior lower classes. Do those people look like lower-class students to you?"

First-years without badges, but with skills seemingly no different from third-years.
Looking at their faces, the sabbatical students reached a shocking hypothesis.

"Are they really first-years?"
"What do you mean?"
"The badges that distinguish grade levels. Is there a law saying you can't remove them to hide your grade?"

There was no such law.
Badges were worn as a warning to juniors not to act frivolously.
If one had no intention of warning others, or conversely, if one was full of intent to deceive, they might deliberately choose not to wear a badge.

'Have fourth-years who are hiding their abilities stormed in? Even a professor is opening the boundary at the entrance. Could this be a group rushing in to complete their graduation project!?'

Of course, those causing the sabbatical students to tremble in fear were all first-years.

"The kid named Oknodie is a student from the same year as you. Use your peer bonds to find her somehow. As long as Professor Red Mountain's protective charm lasts, you should be able to catch up at the entrance of the magical disaster."
"Aren't you coming with us, Professor?"
"My mana control technique isn't that great on land. I can force a hole in the boundary and hold it open with strength, but I can't prevent my enormous mana from causing the magical disaster to fluctuate. To leave a trail for tracking, you first-years need to go in and rescue Oknodie yourselves."

Professor El Draco, realizing that Oknodie was missing from among the gathered students, immediately tracked the magical traces and discerned that Oknodie had headed toward the sabbatical students' zone.
Tracking Oknodie's traces in that place swirling with magical disasters would not be easy.
He was a pirate to begin with.
Tracking on land was inevitably his weakness.
That's why he mobilized so many first-years.
Those who were mobilized didn't refuse the call.

"Did Oknodie know we were fighting Dobby at the shore?"
"Whether she knew or not, thanks to the Kraken's rampage, that Dobby's magic was forcibly canceled. Once again, we received help from that little mouse."
"...That's right. If Oknodie has stepped into a dangerous place, we must risk our lives to rescue her."

Those who had intended to teach Dobby a lesson for Oknodie's sake couldn't possibly ignore her disappearance.
Fortunately, traces needed for tracking were found even in this chaotic area teeming with magical disasters.

"I found it. There are small footprints here that seem to be Oknodie's."

Mob shook his head at the marks pointed out by the Northern Grand Duchess Irene.

"That's incorrect. Oknodie's footprints are at least 10mm smaller than that."
"The tin can is right. I don't smell Oknodie's scent here."

Irene was shocked to see Xing agreeing with Mob's words.
Those guys really know Oknodie well.
For the same reason, Gisele, Isabel, and Son O-cheon found the two extremely suspicious.
Why do those guys know Oknodie so well?
