# 245 - What Are You Doing?

1.
On Friday, the day of the inter-grade competition.
The first and second-year students felt a vague sense of unease as soon as they arrived at the vast field and saw countless lines drawn across the ground.

"Don't tell me this is...?"
"Those lines look familiar."
"It's the same court layout, just different in size..."

First-year student council president Professor Mahabharata confirmed everyone's suspicions.

"I'm announcing that this year's inter-grade competition has been decided as dodgeball team match, as per the student council's discussion. Everyone's happy with that, right?"
"Wow! Dodgeball!"

The expressions of Son O-cheon, Hestia, and Xing, who had experienced Gift Academy's dodgeball before, instantly crumpled.
The students who had been cheerfully excited began to realize something was terribly wrong when they saw the serious expressions of these experienced players and others.

"Why is everyone like that...?"
"Is dodgeball not good...?"
"...You'll understand once you experience it."

The bitter smiles of those who knew that others wouldn't understand until they experienced it!
On the contrary, the second-years were extremely excited.

"This is the perfect event to teach these freshmen a lesson."
"I've been stressed because people keep comparing us 980th class to the 981st class, saying they have more talented students."
"Let's show them that no matter how talented they are, they can't overcome the one-year gap."

The second-years stepped onto the court dreaming of a one-sided victory!
The confusion increased because this wasn't ordinary dodgeball where the ground is simply divided in half—this was dodgeball played on thousands of large and small divided sections that players could choose to enter.

"Gisele. How should we position ourselves?"

Gisele, considered one of the top brains even among the advanced class.
Students who had experienced having their points mercilessly stripped by his evil random gacha, or friends who had witnessed the establishment process of the Dark Association, sought his opinion.

"First, the regular class students are just there to fill numbers. Ultimately, this dodgeball is a battle of skill between the first and second-year advanced class students."
"But there are over two thousand regular class students."
"Do you think two thousand regular class students combined could defeat Oknodie?"
"Well, that's..."
"What about Xing?"
"That's also difficult!"
"What about Dorothy?"
"Um... it's embarrassing to say, but I wouldn't be confident even against ten people..."

Gisele gave Isabel a light knock on the head.

"I'm talking about advanced class students with strong combat abilities in general, not terrain-specialized students."
"Then how do you think we should position ourselves against the second-year advanced class seniors?"
"First, there's the 'dedicated marking zone.' Since multiple people can enter the same section in this inter-grade competition, we deliberately crowd in together, prepared to be eliminated together."

Zuang let out an impressed "Heh" at Gisele's brilliant strategy.

"The head of the Dark Association is indeed different. I would have just thought about stabbing someone to death if they were annoying, but I never would have come up with such an eccentric idea."
"...Who are you calling eccentric?"

Despite Dorothy's bewildered response, Zuang pretended it wasn't about her, flipping her short hair and looking away.

"Of course, since the second-years can also use this strategy, we need to form a 'fortress construction zone.' Either have regular class students protect sections where advanced class students are positioned, or have advanced class students support each other from nearby positions."

Building a safe zone on the vast field.

"The more we stick together, the stronger we'll be. We'll need regular class students to block attacks so we don't get eliminated by Senior Deadcat's attacks."

Learning from past defeats, Hestia actively supported Gisele's strategy.

"So which area are we going to take? The center? The edges?"
"The center offers high points, but that's where many of the enemy's advanced class students will be positioned. The high-scoring sections at the edges are the same. We'll take the area in between."
"What about the dedicated marking zone?"
"There will be students among the first-years who want to participate in individual events and are holding back. Let's gather a suicide squad to aim for mutual elimination with the second-year advanced class students—they'll be eliminated quickly anyway."

What seemed like a disorderly match where players could enter anywhere on the board to score points now had meaning and tactics in formation selection once a proper strategy was established.

"Your skill at planning is impressive. If someone like you were under me, that family would prosper greatly."
"You flatter me. Lady Arcadia is the one with the capacity to restore the Sebiche family, which has been downgraded to an earldom, back to a duchy."
"Hehe. I would like that."

The first-year peripheral forces following Gisele's instructions took positions largely in the south of the dodgeball court laid out like a checkerboard.

"It's a decent strategy, but there's no need to follow it♡ The Empire will have its own formation☆"

The Imperial Princess Mesugakki positioned many imperial students at the 1 o'clock and 11 o'clock corners, two high-scoring positions.

"The point of balls is that they need to be passed back and forth to be effective. If everyone's crowded in one place, how do you plan to recover the balls after hitting someone~? Pupu~ Your thinking is so limited♡ Just like your education♡"
"...That nasty brat. She's no different from Oknodie, so why is her attitude so bad?"
"There's nothing to gain by engaging with her. Be patient, Isabel. She's just a child who doesn't even reach your chin."

Indeed, there was no point in seriously arguing with the Imperial Princess, whose mental age was low enough to genuinely bicker with Oknodie.

"Oknodie. Come here!"

Now that they had formed their positions, they just needed Oknodie to come here.
Gisele and the students had finished setting up their formation and were ready to bring Oknodie to the center.

"Huh? I'm going to stay here!"

Oknodie, as if completely oblivious to everyone's thoughts and plans, squeezed her way in among the second-years.

"That idiot."
"Hahaha. Trying to apply common sense to that little mouse will only frustrate the person doing it."
"Sigh. I wonder why I even bothered using my brain."

In the midst of this confusing atmosphere, Professor Mahabharata lowered his flag.
Simultaneously, fireballs shot up from the instructors' staffs.
With the sound of brilliant fireworks exploding, dodgeballs fell from the sky.

"Huh?"
"G-Gisele. What do we do?!"

Dodgeballs fell from the sky.
A lot of them.
At least thirty.

"Be careful. If you only look up and get tackled by the second-years, you'll step on the lines."

Hestia grabbed a second-year senior who was clinging to her by the waist and lifted him up.

"Aaaah! Let, let me go!"
"You asked for this, senior."

When Hestia threw the senior, the second-years in the adjacent section toppled like dominoes and were engulfed in light.
The second-year regular class seniors with dumbfounded faces were teleported out of the court!

"Take this! Take that!"
"Ouch, that hurts!"
"Who scattered thorns on the ground?!"

Proving why they were called advanced class, even Dorothy, who was considered weak among the first-year advanced class, easily evaded numerous second-years.
Rockfell, Dorothy's pair and the high-speed swordsman, swung his sword like a gale, knocking down seniors who were standing in awkward positions, unable to properly put strength in their legs.

"Argh!"
"How frustrating..."
"To be eliminated before even catching a dodgeball."

But there were few advanced class students and many regular class students.
A few second-year regular class students luckily snatched dodgeballs that were falling nearby.

"Hahah! Tremble in fear, first-years. Now I'll show you the skill of a second-year...!"

Bang!

"Aaaaargh!"

The senior screamed, clutching his arm, as the dodgeball rolled on the ground below him.

"Huh? What were you saying about fear?"
"Y-you crazy girl. Who shoots a gun in dodgeball?!"
"The instructor said it was allowed!"

A nearby instructor nodded in confirmation.
As the second-years cautiously approached the ball, Jigoku's gun spat fire again.
Bang!

"You crazy girl! This isn't dodgeball!"
"Gyahahah! Who cares? As long as I can shoot my gun, that's all that matters! Try saying that again from before. Make me tremble in fear? You're second-years, right? Seniors? Come on, make me tremble quickly!"

The seniors in the adjacent section were suppressed, unable to throw the ball due to the truly insane Jigoku's atrocities.

"Take this, die!"

As the seniors, unable to hold back any longer, bombarded Jigoku with magic, the regular class students in the same section shielded her with their bodies.

"Aaaagh!"
"Quickly, shoot all those seniors dead!"
"Gyahahaha! I like it. I might start liking dodgeball from today!"

Jigoku began enjoying something that could absolutely not be called dodgeball.
The advanced class students who benefited greatly from Gisele's "fortress construction zone" strategy, though not as much as her, safely made it through the initial phase.

"Where's Oknodie?"
"She must have been eliminated. She's beyond that crowd."
"At least she probably enjoyed herself before going out."

As Isabel and Gisele exchanged bitter smiles, Son O-cheon, staring at the wall of second-years in the distance, said:

"I don't think so."
"What do you hear?"
"Screams."
"Surely not Oknodie's?!"
"No, the second-years are screaming."

While the first-years who went over for suicide attacks were getting thoroughly beaten just like the second-year regular class students, only one person remained perfectly fine amidst it all.
The protagonist who was reversing the situation and making the surrounding second-years tremble in fear was, of course, Oknodie.
When the wall of second-years was breached and the scene beyond became visible, the first-year advanced class students realized what Oknodie had done.

"Isn't that worse than Jigoku...?"

Everyone agreed with Dorothy's murmur.
