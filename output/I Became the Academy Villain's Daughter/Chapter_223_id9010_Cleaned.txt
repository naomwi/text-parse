# 223 - A Heart Not Taken Lightly

The secret funds of the Sebiche ducal family weren't hidden in an abandoned warehouse by the docks or in the middle of some deserted island.
What if a typhoon came and washed away their assets, or if someone stole their secret funds without anyone knowing?
It was better to entrust valuables to a place guarded by trustworthy people or organizations.

"That's the place. We just need to enter the guild, give the password, go into the vault, and retrieve what was deposited."

Arcadia pointed to the guild from the corner of a nearby building.
Gisele nodded at the clever storage method, but also harbored some doubts.

"Then why are we watching from here? Why not just go in and retrieve it?"
"Because there are watchers, of course. They don't know the password, but they've positioned themselves to monitor in case any family member comes to withdraw the secret funds."
"Hmm. That's troublesome."
"There's one more troublesome issue. I only know part of the password."
"What? Then why did we come here without a plan?"

Arcadia wasn't a fool either.
She did have a plan.

"Do you see them? Those two pot-bellied men sitting in the restaurant in front of the guild."
"I see them."
"They're watchers too. And where the watchers return to, there's a Sebiche family retainer waiting with part of the password, ready to move the assets stored in the guild elsewhere in case of emergency."
"So we need to grill that retainer, I see."
"That's right. If he tries to attack me with some strange idea in mind, please help me."

She planned to use Oknodie and Gisele as bodyguards during the negotiation.
Gisele the merchant and Oknodie the top student.
Not a bad combination for achieving her goal.
Gisele could help with persuasion.
If things fell apart, Oknodie could help with force.
But Oknodie didn't seem to like this plan.

"There's no need for that. There's a much simpler way!"

Arcadia and Gisele's eyes filled with bewilderment when they heard Oknodie's plan.


* * *


Oknodie asked, looking at the tin cans lined up making clanking armor sounds.

"How do I look? Do I look like a holy knight now?"
"Not at all. The armor is too rusty, and your posture is sloppy."

Gisele nitpicked harshly.
He had no choice.
Those tin cans were the core of the operation.

"Did you hear that? Bank robbery is all about angles and polish. Quickly clean up your armor and fix your posture!"
"...Wasn't the little lady's plan to lead a fake holy knight unit, attach heresy charges, and use evidence confiscation as an excuse to extort the secret funds and run away?"
"That's right."
"Since when are holy knights bank robbers?"

For some reason, Oknodie became extremely angry, waving her arms around and shouting wildly.

"Holy knights are thugs! Even if you do linked quests for them, they slash your compensation in the name of faith, they have so many taboos that they're always saying you can't do this or that, and if you want to learn some skills, they charge you like highway robbers!"

This child, has she actually met real holy knights somewhere?
The question lingered only briefly.
If it's the Wiheomhae Foundation, it's possible.
He found himself accepting this explanation.

"Do it properly. Regardless of Oknodie's anger toward holy knights, you're the ones going in, so it's on you. If you actors fail in your performance, you'll be attacked in the middle of the guild."
"Oh, don't worry about that."
"That's right. We have our pride too."
"Even if those bastards catch us... ack!"

Oknodie struck the helmet of one of the tin cans who was showing inexplicable confidence, making it ring.

"You mean you're confident you won't get caught at all, right?"

Just try saying no.
I'll lay you down next to the guy who just passed out.
The tin cans trembled and nodded at the glaring Bulnopnodie's threat.

"Who are these people anyway?"
"Does it matter? The operation is already about to begin."
"Sigh. You're right. Let's just pray we get through this safely."
"I'll go to the bathroom one last time."

Gisele calmed Arcadia in front and encouraged her to focus on the operation, but behind the scenes, he asked his informant who had already entered the bathroom:

"Are they from the Foundation?"
"Yes."
"What's their level?"
"They've eaten their share of sword meals. They're moderately skilled and moderately dirty-handed. Most have 3rd-tier level skills, and their leader is at 4th-tier level."
"I understand. Please continue monitoring and stand by for reinforcement in case of unexpected incidents."

4th-tier. A measure of strength.
While it might sound vague when described numerically, it's actually quite simple.
The Garden of the Gods, a high mountain at 12,500m elevation.
Commonly known as Mt. Shinjeong.
It means they have the skill to regularly traverse up to the 4th level, where survival conditions become more challenging every 1,000m.
Gisele knew 4th-tier level practitioners.
Son O-cheon had reached 4th-tier level around the time of the Academy entrance qualification exam.
Oknodie had achieved 5th-tier level performance at that time, one level higher than Son O-cheon.
While it might sound insignificant compared to Oknodie at enrollment, that's only because Oknodie was exceptionally strong from the beginning. Usually, being at Son O-cheon's level would be impressive.
Adventurer's Guild.
Cooperative.
Mercenary Group.
These are people with the skills and career to aim for positions like new president, high-ranking executive, or senior employee in various organizations.

"Which religious order will you be impersonating?"
"The Whole Body Mark Order!"
"The order that teaches 'How to Use Divine Arts Without Faith'?"
"That's right! They're all bad guys!"
"Hehe. Indeed. Duke Andersen always denounces the viciousness of that lecture."
"How should we imitate holy knights who believe in the Whole Body Mark?"

The one presumed to be the leader among the tin cans asked.
Oknodie's answer was simple and clear.

"Act like you have anger management issues!"
"......"


* * *


Until they stormed into the guild, Arcadia, Gisele, and all the tin can-clad helpers were half-doubtful.

"The Knights of the Whole Body Mark have arrived! Evil heretics, are you prepared to end your lives?"
"Gasp!"
"No, we're not heretics!"
"Silence! We know full well that heretical sacred objects are hidden in this building, how dare you play dumb!"

Is this right?
Is this really right?
While they were half-doubtful, shouting like section chiefs and breaking things to intimidate people, the storage room was eventually opened.
The tin cans' anger management disorder acting played a significant role in the theft.

"Did this bastard say he'd open the safe or not? Do you want to come with us? Want to try jumping onto a burning ring?"
"Yeah, yeah, keep moving slowly. If you go through 720 hours of training until the 720-degree aerial trapeze rotation ends, you'll get real quick."
"Oh? Are you putting your hand on the external communication device? If you play with your hands, you'll experience the full course of putting your hand in an alligator's mouth with pepper powder, chili powder, and magic stone powder!"

Strangely specific and realistic threats focused on circus themes!
The guild employees, who were stalling for time until support arrived from the domain, had their momentum thoroughly broken by these unexpected circus threats.

"Why are these holy knight guys so desperate to make us do circus acts?"
"I don't know. They're crazy."
"That's just how the Mark Warriors are."

Due to their notorious reputation, even when they only chose to do outrageous things, it was attributed to the order's behavior. Meanwhile, Arcadia signaled from the storage room.

"I've retrieved everything. Let's go now."
"Please wait a moment. This guild seems to store secret funds from other families besides the Sebiche family. How about taking those as well?"
"We don't have much time. Even as we speak, soldiers from the lord's castle will be rushing here."

Gisele quickly sorted out high-value items using his merchant's eye.

"Let's go."

They successfully escaped from the guild, but tails began to follow them from a distance.
One group was the Sebiche family's watchers, and the other was trackers sent by the guild that had lost its items.

"They don't seem willing to let us go easily. If we can't shake them off now, we won't be able to change clothes and use the teleportation magic circle."
"It's fine. These people will take care of it somehow. Right?"

When Oknodie tapped her foot and urged an answer, the tin cans sighed deeply and nodded.
As they scattered in all directions, the trackers split up, allowing the three to leisurely increase their distance.


* * *


The identity of the tin cans was, of course, the Pierrot Mask Group.
Oknodie.
The Foundation's top scholarship student.
The one who slaughtered the branch manager in one breath.
The person receiving teachings from the former hero Destroyer.
The circus members didn't dare to disappear or rebel against her call.
Who knows if they might face the Destroyer's subordinates and be swiftly disposed of if they dared to disobey?

"Did you all discard the armor?"
"Running in that stuff drains all your stamina."
"Those Sebiche family trackers, unnecessarily agile. Even harder to shake off than the guild's security personnel."

The pierrots decided to flee in different directions to disperse the enemy's pursuit as much as possible.
Oknodie made a somewhat reasonable distribution.
She gave each fleeing person one piece of loot from the storage room.
If they escaped safely, they could make a big profit.
With hope for the future, they became that much more desperate in their escape.

"Argh!"
"Aaah!"

Hearing the screams of their comrades, those who hadn't been caught felt relieved.
They're not chasing after me.
However, as time passed, the screams kept increasing.
One clever masked man, just in case, threw his loot under a nearby tree and climbed to the top of a nearby tree to watch below.

"It's here."
"Under the tree."
"Did he hide the loot and run away?"
"We'll search later. Let's find the others first."
"Lucky guy."

The guild security officers threw comments as they moved away.
The Pierrot mask realized.
Oknodie didn't give them loot for nothing.
She intended to use them as sacrificial lambs.
Her vicious skill made his teeth chatter.
The previous branch manager was better in comparison.
Whenever he indulged in greed, he gave them a small compensation as hush money.
Oknodie didn't even do that, scattering tracking-spelled loot for all of them to die.

'If I want to live, I need to follow those people.'

Thanks to running with all his might, the Pierrot mask was able to reach the transmission station where spatial movement magic circles were placed.
Trudging along.
As he was trying to enter the building, struggling to gather his tattered clothes, Gisele, who had come out of the building first, called him into an alley.
After hesitating briefly, the Pierrot mask followed him.

"Oknodie used us as disposable cards. Did you know?"

He thought it was nonsense.
But he couldn't hold back.
If they were in cahoots, he was already a dead man.
But what if they didn't know about the true nature of Oknodie, that despicable new master?
Then he could get back at Oknodie.
If he could plant distrust in these people who were close enough to go on such secret adventures together, it would be a way to make them pay for their hard work.
Burning with revenge, he blurted it out, and Gisele showed a surprised expression.

"I had no idea."
"Don't be fooled. That child's true nature is far more vicious than what you've seen at the Academy."
"Lower your voice. This way."

Gisele beckoned to the Pierrot mask.
Thinking he wanted to whisper, he approached, and a wire wrapped around his neck.
Surprised, he grabbed his neck and struggled, but it was already impossible to remove the wire wrapped around his neck.

"When I said I had no idea, I meant I didn't know that you thought I was unaware of that fact."

A dark merchant's insight is not to be taken lightly.
Similarly, Gisele's affection for Oknodie was not so light as to be shaken by such a fact.
