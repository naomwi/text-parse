<141 - Ominous Premonition>

Powerful NPCs exude intimidation by their mere presence.
This is especially true for Hero Ishtar, considered one of the strongest NPCs of the 981st generation.
My body hair stands on end and the back of my hand tingles.
My skin feels hot where her gaze lands, as if singed by fire, and a fighting spirit burns deep in my chest, making me want to challenge her.
But I know better.
I know it's impossible to fight a hero with my initial specs.
Even though my strength alone might let me hold my own in the advanced class, my other abilities can't compare to the playable characters with their impressive stats.
So I turned around with a smile.

"Hero unnie. What brings you here?"

I meant it as a friendly greeting to lower her guard, but Ishtar's expression hardened.

"As I thought... You've been trained to resist killing intent to the point where this level of intimidation doesn't faze you at all. An assassin can't afford to freeze up in real combat."
"Hmm... I guess I've adapted for similar reasons!"

This isn't easy.
Instead of lowering her guard, she's become even more suspicious.
Why is she acting like this?
I haven't built up any bad history with Ishtar.
Last time, she even invited me to join her team.
Could she be holding a grudge because I refused her offer?!

It's possible.
NPC personalities aren't always the same in every playthrough.
In some runs, a character might be kind because their family is successful, while in others, they might be bitter and aggressive because their family went bankrupt.

"Oknodie. I'd appreciate if you answered my next questions honestly. It won't be bad news for you."
"What is it?"
"Don't you want to escape from the Foundation's clutches?"
"Huh?"
"The Wiheomhae Foundation. The education they've given you clearly crosses the line of common sense."

Ishtar listed the rumors she'd heard.

"I've heard the Foundation poisons children who don't follow their harsh training, and sells those who refuse to suffer to brothels or labor camps."
"Gasp. Really?"
"Don't act like it's someone else's problem. The same applies to you, Oknodie. From a young age, you've undergone various environmental adaptation training, resistance training, and all sorts of assassination training including acrobatics."

That was too rude to say to someone's face out of nowhere.
What does she take me for?

"That's not true! Don't badmouth Papa's Foundation like that. I've never received such harsh training!"
"...You're lying. With so many terrible rumors circulating, they can't all be false."
"I became strong because of training I chose to do because I liked it. Why would I do something I don't enjoy?"

The hero's face turned pale. Her hand on her sword hilt trembled. This pathetic person, is she angry because I won't speak ill of the Foundation?!
Seems like this run's hero has a broken personality.
Is this hero really threatening to cut me down if I don't badmouth Papa's organization to his own daughter?
Hmph.
If she thinks I'll back down just because she's being aggressive, she's mistaken.
I'm strong against the strong and strong against the weak.
That's my personality.

"If you speak ill of the Foundation and Papa, I won't let it slide, even if you are a hero."
"Sigh. Are you serious? You're standing before the current generation's strongest talent, a working hero."
"A wicked hero who demands filial impiety doesn't deserve to call herself a hero!"

I see.
Ishtar let out a dejected sigh.
Looks like she's not trying to fight right now.
Phew.
As I was catching my breath, the hero turned away and said:

"If that's how you feel, I can't help it. I meant well, but if I've offended you, I apologize."

All she did was try to make me betray my family.
And she calls that good intentions?
This run's Ishtar is definitely a psychopath!

"Hmph. Just go already. You're interrupting my training."
"Is that child training voluntarily?"

Mob spoke in a dying voice.

"Please... no more... save me..."
"...Oknodie. I'm sorry, but I'd like you to let that child go. As a hero, I can't ignore someone asking for help right in front of me."
"Seriously? He asked for my help first."

I grumbled as I released Mob, and only then did the hero leave.
What a jerk.
If Mob fails because of this, it's all your fault!


* * *


Ishtar couldn't help feeling devastated.
The One True God "Sophemia of the Sun" who had chosen her as a hero.
The holy sword bestowed upon the hero by Sophemia possessed "The Power to Detect All Lies."

"As I thought... You've been trained to resist killing intent to the point where this level of intimidation doesn't faze you at all. An assassin can't afford to freeze up in real combat."
"Hmm... I guess I've adapted for similar reasons!"

The lie detection didn't activate.

"That's not true! Don't badmouth Papa's Foundation like that. I've never received such harsh training!"

It was the truth.

"I became strong because of training I chose to do because I liked it. Why would I do something I don't enjoy?"

She genuinely enjoyed receiving harsh training.

'She's been trained from too young an age.'

Before she could even develop likes and dislikes.
To accept cruel training as joy.
The Foundation had modified the child's mind from its very core to have preferences that were not childlike.
It was too late.
Though she appeared innocent and lovable on the surface, inside was a broken mind that viewed harsh training as play and found it enjoyable.
Murder.
Assassination.
It was clear these would be no different.

"If you speak ill of the Foundation and Papa, I won't let it slide, even if you are a hero."

Truth.

"A wicked hero who demands filial impiety doesn't deserve to call herself a hero!"

Truth.

She neither admired nor feared the hero.
She considered it natural to make the hero her enemy.
As if someday she would inevitably become the hero's adversary.
A future that even grown adults feared.
While the heads of major organizations feared being labeled as evil by the hero, this small child showed not the slightest fear.
Because she had never been taught fear.
Because she had been educated not to fear.
This child had been raised and educated to be not just an assassin but a candidate for the next Demon King.

"Seriously? He asked for my help first."

Making someone carry over 50kg of weight.
Forcing a classmate to jump through a burning ring.
Compelling them to juggle five sharp daggers while balancing on a rope.
She doesn't even think these things are wrong.
Because she went through it herself.
Because to her, it's simply training and play.
So the boundary between good and evil is blurred.

'I realize now. I must kill her while she's still relatively weak.'

But such a young and innocent child?
The blurred boundary between good and evil means that while she could easily fall into evil, she could also return to good at any time.
There might be another way.
Without killing her.
Without harming her.
Without making her an enemy.
Some way to guide this child to the path of good.

'I need Yupi's help.'

Her longtime friend and first companion, Yupi.
She needed the saintess's understanding and compassion.
Ishtar turned away.
And made a promise.
Though she was backing down quietly now, next time would be different.
Someday she would make that poor child understand that what she experienced was neither play nor training but abuse, that her Papa was not a good person, and that the Foundation was an evil organization.


* * *


"Feel better? Now that we've stopped the special training?"
"Sorry, Oknodie... My stamina just isn't enough. I really can't handle any more."
"What a weakling, really. What have you been eating all these years to be so unhealthy at this age?"
"Black bread and hodgepodge soup?"
"Sheesh. As expected of an extra, your collection rate status is truly legendary."

I did what I could.
Though I couldn't complete all the special training Oknodie assigned, I could feel my sword trajectory becoming more even and the tip no longer wavering.
My body moves as I think, and the good feeling I used to get only two or three times out of a hundred sword swings now comes once every ten times.
Considering we only did weight adaptation training and acrobatic stunts instead of sword practice, I don't understand why I've gotten stronger, but that makes it feel even more impressive.

'No wonder she's the joint top student in first year.'

After finishing the special training, I returned to the dormitory, slept like the dead, and woke up.
With my strangely faster recovery speed and improved condition, I even felt healthier.

The mana verification test.
The crucial exam with failure at stake was approaching fast.

"Mob. How's your preparation? I heard you went through some rough training."
"Just right. My body and mind are in top condition."

Frankly, I was confident that whatever test came would be easier than jumping through a burning ring with 50kg of weights attached or juggling five razor-sharp daggers while balancing on a rope.

"Jaku. How about you?"

My old friend wore a somewhat detached smile.

"I'm ready too."
"Did the mentoring help a lot?"
"Yes, it was helpful. If only I'd received it a week earlier..."
"Huh?"
"Nothing. It's nothing."

His expression doesn't look good.
Something feels off.
Did Jaku have trouble sleeping?
I wanted to have a conversation to help him relax and see if something was bothering him, but the timing wasn't right.
The sound of many footsteps coming from outside.
The noise announcing the arrival of Professor Van Snake, who always brings along numerous assistants, made me miss the chance to address the unease I felt.

"Jaku!"

Raising my fist to cheer him on was the best I could do.
Jaku also raised his fist in return to encourage me.
The classroom door slid open, and finally the professor appeared.
Now the test was unavoidable.

"It's time to prove whether the past four weeks have been meaningful or a waste of time. Everyone, do your best."

The mana verification test, the exam for the fourth week of the lower class common required course, had begun.
