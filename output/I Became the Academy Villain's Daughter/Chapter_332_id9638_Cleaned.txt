<332 - Reckless Challenge>

The strategy to win over the elf archer Scola faced an unexpected obstacle.

"What's so pitiful about that?"

Ishtar, who had overheard the approach to Scola, suddenly interrupted.

"Nobody forced her to take those classes; she chose them herself. Did someone hold a knife to her throat and threaten her to take 38 credits?"

'What a cruel woman.'

Ishtar was an incredibly mean woman beyond imagination.
Gisele secretly criticized the hero.

"Anyone with common sense would see that she received orders from the Foundation!"

"She could have refused. It was Oknodie's decision to follow the Foundation's orders."

There was a reason for her hostility.
She herself had fallen to become a Foundation scholarship student.
At the chairman's banquet, she discovered that the cause was Oknodie's sticker copying scam!
It wasn't even a fair competition—she was tricked by such a cowardly method and ended up with her nose tied to Oknodie's organization. How could she not be furious?
While that was half the reason, the other half stemmed from anxiety.
The Foundation's orders.
Scholarship students carrying dark mana that could explode at any moment, like terrorists wearing bomb vests.
Who knew if those unreasonable orders would be given to her as well?
The hero was no longer free.
Even without any orders coming down, just that anxiety and fear alone caused considerable stress.
In short, this was her taking out the stress she received from the Foundation on Oknodie.

"So if the Foundation gives you orders, will you also take 38 credits?"

"...I'll figure that out when the time comes."

"Ha. How amusing. You freely mock our little lady, but when it comes to doing it yourself, you're afraid. Is 'Hero Ishtar' now synonymous with someone whose words and actions don't match?"

Ishtar took the bait at Gisele's bold provocation.

"Don't be ridiculous. I'm a hero. If I set my mind to it, I can do anything. Besides, Oknodie isn't a hero or anything special."

A provocative smile that said "Gotcha" appeared on Gisele's face.
For a moment, a look of regret flashed across Ishtar's face.
It was quickly replaced by a mask of indifference, but Gisele knew better.
A person's true expression reveals itself in an instant.
If it's not that momentary sincerity, everything else is just an act—emotions they want others to see.
At least for a black market merchant living in the underworld, this was common sense.

"If you look down on Oknodie that much, you could do it too, right?"

"Of course I can! What's so hard about it?"

Ishtar swallowed the provocation whole.
Though she knew it was foolish, she had her pride to maintain.
The pride of being called a hero.
The humiliation on the cruise ship still stung Ishtar like a thorn in her foot.
Especially since Oknodie had used trickery to defeat Low, the apostle of Anrage and demon contractor, whom she herself couldn't overcome.
But completing all those credits was a long-term battle requiring physical endurance and comprehensive skills—a marathon in everyday life.
Even Oknodie, who excelled at momentary tactics, wouldn't be able to endure the long period until the end of the semester.

'She'll definitely drop one class after another along the way.'

Thirteen courses?
As a performance, it was impressive.
Anyone would be amazed.
But it couldn't be sustained.
Whether it was mental strength, physical endurance, surrounding environment, or even living conditions—there would definitely be a limit.

'This time it's a real match, Oknodie.'

With that, Ishtar immediately added seven more courses to her existing six, bringing her total to thirteen.
Professor Mahabharata was troubled by the late registration, but considering the applicant was Ishtar, he pondered.

'Well, she certainly has the talent, so it should be fine. If it becomes life-threateningly difficult, the saintess or the holy sword will somehow save the hero.'

And so the Scola strategy unexpectedly turned into the hero's challenge of 38 credits across thirteen courses.


* * *


"Oknodie, did you hear? The hero is challenging herself with 13 courses for 38 credits."

"Wow. That's amazing."

Honestly, I was surprised.
This was the first time in history that the hero had burned with such academic fervor.
She would be the strongest in her year if not for the player.
Even at this point, her strength was recognized by third-year students.
It's only natural that she developed an arrogant attitude.
Neglecting academics and finding her own path through training was a cliché shared by heroes in every iteration.

"It's not just the hero. The Loner Princess also increased her courses from nine to ten!"

If the hero could be compared to the lazy hare in the tortoise and hare story, then the Third Princess <Yayoi> was like a textbook example of life reversal through slow but steady effort—a playable character.
Unlike the Second Princess Mesugakki, Yayoi lacked both followers and personal strength, so in her early years, she quietly attended the Academy, struggling just to keep up with the curriculum.
Even without knowing these details, ordinary students found Yayoi's challenge more impressive than the hero's.
The hero was inherently exceptional, so people thought, "She's the hero, so she'll manage somehow despite this eccentric behavior." But when an ordinary princess with no particular strengths added one more course, it seemed like a tremendous challenge.
I don't want to exercise.
I don't want to study.
The courage to overcome the laziness that anyone might feel in daily life is easier to empathize with than the challenges of a great hero.

"So guess what? I decided to add one more too!"

While the Tito family's gossip was interesting, what was most intriguing was Tito herself.

"Really? What are you going to take?"

"Hmm~ One of the courses Oknodie is taking?"

"Then let's go to this class together!"

"But you haven't even registered for the additional course yet?"

"I'll just say I'm sitting in!"

The class I was taking this time was a 2-credit course held on Tuesdays from 8 PM to 11 PM for three hours.
It was "Casting Magic Through Knowledge Judgment" taught by Professor Pythagoras from the Magic Department.
It was a cost-effective class that met once a week for three hours, easier than a 3-credit course that met twice a week for a total of four hours.

"Is it okay if you don't know how to use puzzle magic?"

"Yes. It's a different mechanism of magic. Let's hurry!"

When we entered the classroom, we saw the backs of students' heads as they lay slumped over their desks with exhausted faces.
It was 8 PM, an hour after dinner.
For students who were starting to feel the murderous amount of assignments, a class at this hour was the ultimate trial that they endured only through sheer willpower.

"Everyone looks tired."

"Second-year students have it harder, so it can't be helped!"

"Second-year?"

Tito tilted her head cutely like a sparrow.

"Second-year? Why? Are there seniors who failed again?"

"...Not failed, they're retaking the course."

One of the slumped seniors raised his head.

"Ah, Senior Bigstone! Are you retaking the course again?"

"No, I'm not! Don't treat me like an idiot, you disrespectful junior!"

Senior Bigstone, with whom I had become somewhat acquainted during the Eye for Value class in the first semester.
Among the second-year trio, he was the most approachable and comfortable person, which is why Tito was genuinely happy to see him.

"Then why are you taking this class, senior?"

"I'm not the strange one here. You two are."

Bigstone pointed at the students collapsed on their desks.

"These poor souls lying here like they're about to pass out are all second-years. You two are the only first-years taking this class."

"Whaaaat?!"

Tito turned to look at me with tears welling up in her eyes, unable to hide her sense of betrayal.

"You're the one who wanted to take this class together!"

"Hmph. There are so many other classes, but you had to bring me to one that seniors take?"

"It's too late. Class is starting."

As Senior Bigstone spoke, the bell signaling the start of class rang.
Having missed her chance to escape, Tito sat down next to Senior Bigstone.
Tito, who had been making angry expressions like an upset small animal, was surprised by the changed atmosphere in the classroom.

'Second-year students really do have a different study atmosphere!'

Unlike the freshman chickens, the point burden felt by second-year students was on a different level.
The teacher who appeared from the summoning magic circle with a burst of light when the class time struck was also quite different from the first-year teachers.

"Seeing you all again after a week, you look even more wretched. With that level of mental strength, challenging knowledge judgment won't be easy."

The professor, who had been looking at the frustrated faces of the second-year students, noticed Tito and me with our eyes wide open.

"What are these greenhorns doing here?"

"We're first-years!"

"Place your magic watches against the panel and register your identities."

Whoosh, thud!
The panel the professor casually threw stuck into the desk like a throwing star.
After I pulled out the panel and registered my identity, Tito tremblingly placed her magic watch against it.
Beep.
Professor Pythagoras reached out and retrieved the panel that had made a sound like a transit card.

"Are we in the wrong class...?"

"No, we're in the right one!"

It was understandable that Tito felt uncomfortable.
Professor Pythagoras was a muscular statue, with a burly physique like Professor Platon.
But his demeanor was completely different.
If Professor Platon was a protein fanatic with clear eyes, Professor Pythagoras possessed a wild ferocity like a leopard ready to pounce and kill his opponent at any moment.
Even I had wondered if he was a professor from the Knight Department during my newbie days, so I understood Tito's confused feelings.

"First-year Oknodie, first-year Tito. The top student and a lower-class student. Like a wolf keeping an emergency snack by its side."

"Would it be possible for Tito to just audit the class for now?"

"The path of learning is open. But only to those who can endure its harshness."

Tito was trembling, apparently feeling a different kind of fear than with Professor Sadako.
Regardless of her pitiful appearance, the professor began a light review for us who were attending his class for the first time today.

"Let's review. First come, first served. One person explain the principle of using magic through knowledge judgment."

Senior Bigstone stood up abruptly.

"Unlike conventional magic that requires assembling mana puzzles, this method instantly implements mana through a mana board enchanted with <Brainwave Detection>, <Knowledge Discrimination>, and <Implementation Guidance>!"

"Continue with the advantages and disadvantages of using a mana board."

"The disadvantage is that, unlike when using a staff or wand, most magical auxiliary abilities are concentrated on magic implementation, resulting in reduced power. Also, you can't concentrate specific attribute mana puzzles. However, even those unskilled in conventional magic can easily use magic!"

"Did you hear that? This is what you'll learn in this class. If you're disappointed, leave the classroom right now. If you try to escape during class, I'll hang you upside down outside the window."

Though timid, Tito seemed to realize what kind of fortune she had encountered and shook her head vigorously, expressing her desire to definitely attend the class.
A new means of using magic would be attractive to Tito, who only had a large amount of mana.
Using a <Mana Board> to cast magic based solely on knowledge of the spell.
This fact was appealing to me as well, as a veteran player.
Implementing 'known magic.'
While this class might be an insurmountable wall for first-years with limited learning or students not majoring in magic, it's not a reckless challenge for me.
For a multi-cycle veteran who knows more spells than can be counted, this class is practically being served on a silver platter.
