# <59 - Adventure Tales of a Retired Former Hero>

In this world, there exist beings known as heroes.

"Child, you who possess talent surpassing all others. I, Sophemia of the Sun, the sole deity of the continent, choose you. Become a hero and embark on your journey to vanquish demons."

Since the deity possesses eyes that can discern talent, whenever priests discover a child with the qualities of a hero, they immediately bestow the hero's blessing upon them.
Heroes who receive this blessing grow steadily, viewing status windows and receiving quests just like players.
And there's one more thing.
A powerful privilege granted only to heroes.
It's the ability to make others their companions through the declaration, "You, become my companion!"
Those chosen by the hero are also called heroes, but unlike true heroes, they don't receive "quests," known as the goddess's guidance.
This means that if the true hero dies or their journey ends, these companion heroes lose their way, not knowing what to strive for.
The retired former hero known as "Destroyer" was one such lost pseudo-hero.

"Aren't you tired? You just suffered abuse from that crazy statue guy."
"Not at all! I paced myself while running here."

The educational status displayed on the professor's magical teaching clock showed not a mark of skipping class and running away, but even bonus points for following the lecture with excellent grades.
While knowing how to push their physical limits, their recovery ability is also extraordinary.

'What they call the qualities of a hero. A talent with the minimum requirements.'

Moreover, they never frown and genuinely seem to enjoy themselves.
They possess the masochistic mental strength unique to heroes who perform difficult trials as if they were enjoyable daily routines.
This means they're an excellent talent in both body and mind.

'Above all, they pursue what they want to do more clearly than anyone else. Just like a true hero.'

Their confident movements and actions reminded him of the eccentric behaviors that the former hero Nyarlathotep used to display.

'That hero was quite self-centered too. Perhaps all true heroes loved by the gods have such personalities?'

He closed his book with a thud, also putting aside his unnecessarily complicated thoughts.

"Fine. If you want a lecture that badly, I'll give you one."
"Yay~"

As Oknodie clapped her hands in delight, Professor Destroyer began his story with a look of disdain.

"'Adventure Tales of a Retired Former Hero' is a lecture where I tell strange stories that a hero party experienced, heard, or witnessed during their adventures, and you complete tasks related to them."
"The first story for our first lecture is 'The Sheep That Don't Decrease.' It's a tale that gave even the hero of reversal quite an eerie feeling."

Destroyer began his story, recalling his distant past.


* *


About 15 years ago.
There was a village in a meadow of an impoverished remote area where shepherds gathered to graze their sheep.
Dangerous monsters appeared in the rough terrain where humans rarely ventured, but the fertile plains had the exploitation of lords, so these shepherds chose to be neighbors with monsters and naturally formed a village.

<Subjugation Request>
Please find and resolve the reason why our village's sheep are disappearing.
Reward: 5 gold coins, Guild's Certificate of Appreciation, Title [Savior of the Mountain Village]

The newly formed hero party, having just registered with the guild and started their activities, needed not only money but also as many achievements as possible.
To embark on proper heroic adventures, the fastest way to advance in the Adventurer's Guild was to collect multiple "village savior" titles to earn the "Savior of Remote Villages" title.

"Could it be wolves?"
"Boring. We already found wolf fur."
"Hmm~ This request is quite interesting."

Nyarlathotep, the chosen hero and sorcerer.
Alpha, his first companion and an excellent warrior.
Destroyer, the second companion and an apathetic thief.

The three quickly found the wolves' den and killed five of the twelve wolves targeting the sheep, driving away the remaining seven.
The strange thing was that all the wolves were so thin their ribs were visible, and there was no trace of them having hunted sheep anywhere.

"Another monster perhaps?"
"This is troublesome. There's no trace of other monsters."
"Interesting. Just as I expected. Hehe."

Nyarlathotep said:

"Haven't you all noticed?"
"What?"
"The monster."
"Are you saying it wasn't wolves but people?"
"Destroyer's idea isn't bad, but there's something more interesting. Why were there no traces of other monsters? With hundreds of appetizing sheep in such a remote village."
"Now that you mention it, it is strange. If it were a village where the lord regularly cleared out monsters, I could understand, but there's no way such benefits would exist in a village of fugitives who turned their backs on tax obligations."

The shepherds seemed unaware of the reason, and the inexperienced rookie hero party at that time couldn't reach the root of the problem and ended the request prematurely.
They consoled themselves that they had completed the request since they had caught the wolves targeting the sheep.

"It's been ten years since I returned to this village."

Destroyer, now alone, revisited the shepherds' village that the three had once visited.
After finishing his adventures and reflecting on the past, curiosity led him back when he recalled the past incident.
The village still had a request posted about missing sheep, and the village chief wore a face full of worry.

"Huh?"

Destroyer, now a veteran hero, felt a strong sense of dissonance from the village's appearance.
So he decided to ask the village chief a few questions.
The chief said he would answer only three questions as it was getting late and he had work to attend to.


* *


"Now it's time for new student Oknodie to step up. Imagine yourself as the veteran hero Destroyer and ask the village chief three questions."
"I'll answer as if I were the village chief myself."

This is indeed interesting.
The "Adventure Tales of a Retired Former Hero" lecture takes the peculiar form of listening to a hero's adventure and responding as if you were the hero yourself.
It's a brain-torturing session after mercilessly abusing the body, but that's what makes it even better for someone like me who's been around the block.

'Professor Destroyer's stories are different every time.'

One might expect the story to follow the same pattern at some point, but strangely, it changes every time.
Moreover, the content is always fascinating.

"The hint has already been given. This is a story about 'The Sheep That Don't Decrease.' The hero's sense of dissonance must be related to the sheep. The fact that the same request from 10 years ago is still posted also implies that the root of the incident hasn't been resolved."

Professor Destroyer scratched his beard and looked at me with eyes that seemed to say, "Look at this one?"

"Are the shepherds the same people as before?"
"Is that a question for the village chief?"
"Yes."
"The village chief would answer like this: Most have left the village, and new shepherds have filled their places."

That's quite a valuable answer.

"So the new shepherds didn't know what was happening in the village. Neither did the lord. The incidents here are occurring very discreetly."

I extended my index and middle fingers simultaneously.

"My second question: How many adventurers have accepted this request over the past 10 years, how did they complete it, and what was their survival rate?"
"Due to the modest reward for coming all the way to this remote village, we were lucky to get one or two visitors a year. Some caught nearby wildlife or monsters, others taught us how to set up fences and thorny vines. Occasionally, some adventurers didn't return, perhaps having encountered monsters in the deeper areas."
"May I see the request notice?"
"Is that your third question?"
"Um... no! I thought I might be able to see the latest request posted in the village without using a question."
"I see. Take this."

The professor reached into his chest and pulled out a laminated piece of paper.
What was blank paper when he took it out transformed into an old adventure request notice as I received it.

<Subjugation Request>
Please find and resolve the reason why our village's sheep are disappearing.
Reward: 6 gold coins, 15 silver coins, Guild's Certificate of Appreciation, Title [Savior of the Mountain Village]

A player might easily dismiss this as a "repeatable quest," but this is an experience from an NPC hero party, not a player.
There's always a reason why an incomprehensible quest is given repeatedly for 10 years.
Above all, the reward has increased.
Considering that the number of sheep in the village remains the same and most of the original shepherds have left, yet the village has paid rewards each time, this amount is excessive.

"My final question: Who is the merchant who buys sheep from this village, and when can I meet him?"
"A friend named Benjamin. You can meet him soon when winter comes. Will you stay until winter?"

I've got it figured out.

"That's all for the questions. And Destroyer went around the village once, then solved the case. Can new student Oknodie solve it?"
"Let me take a look around too."
"What do you want to check while looking around the village?"
"I'd like to examine the sheep directly."
"Then you'll see sheep lying down sleeping in the meadow, standing still, or occasionally waddling around, along with the leisurely daily life of the shepherds."

Well, it wasn't a difficult problem, befitting the first lecture.

"There were no sheep grazing on grass, were there?"
"You've caught on."
"Can I give the answer?"
"By all means."
"Initially, it might have been sheep that decreased, but over time, it was the shepherds who decreased. They must have employed a terribly eerie wisdom to avoid being caught by adventurers."
"What wisdom is that?"
"They stuffed corpses or missing people inside sheep skins."

Destroyer grinned.

"Correct."
