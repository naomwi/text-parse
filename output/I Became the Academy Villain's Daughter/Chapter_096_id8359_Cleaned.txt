# 96 - A Child Who Cherishes Friends

Monday, second period.
Professor Bronze's Developing an Eye class.

"Seniors!"
"Can I ask you something?"

After the last lecture, the freshmen rushed to their seniors, whom they now knew had unexpected skills.

"What's going on? The juniors are being so respectful."
"We heard from our advisor that you seniors will catch the Giant King Crab that invaded the school. Is that true?"
"Please catch the King Crab in the first-floor lounge quickly... We have nowhere to sit and do our assignments..."

Bigstone broke into a cold sweat at the freshmen's pleas.

"Huh? Well, I might catch it if I get hungry. But I'm not particularly hungry right now."
"Stop lying. You've been starving since morning to save points..."
"Waaah! Be quiet, Liznah!"

Moss also took a jab at Bigstone, who wanted to show off in front of the juniors who had gathered around but was too scared to face off with the Giant Crab in a battle of souls.

"Just tell them you can't do it before you embarrass yourself further."
"Moss. Don't you want to look good in front of the juniors? This is our chance to stand above someone with our meager title of 'senior' after enduring humiliation and disrespect all year!"
"Bigstone. You look like absolute garbage right now."

Just as Moss said, the freshmen's faces showed regret—thinking they shouldn't rely on this senior no matter how urgent the situation, wondering why they approached him in the first place.

"If you try to show off beyond your abilities, you'll get hurt. They're just venting because they're at a loss, not expecting anything big. Why don't you just be honest?"

Unlike the annoying Moss who quietly minded his own business, Liznah—who didn't dress up much but was pretty and smelled nice—was the female classmate Bigstone had feelings for.
Receiving sincere advice from her, Bigstone put aside his unnecessary pride and answered honestly.

"The Giant King Crab is an incredibly strong monster. Its massive front claws have tremendous physical resistance, and if ordinary metal weapons get caught in its pincers, they'll snap instantly."
"Eh? So you can't catch it either, senior?"
"Of course I can't! Only upper-class students or a skilled few among second-years can catch that thing. Maybe lower-class students from third year could handle it too."

Though disappointed, the freshmen grasped the harsh reality thanks to his explanation.
If they foolishly tried to eat King Crab, they might end up becoming the King Crab's meal instead!

"Now that you understand, don't even dream of trying to catch that thing. If you see it, run away immediately. Especially if there's water nearby—it gets incredibly fast, so get away from the water right away."
"Yes, sir."

Though they initially seemed doubtful, the freshmen responded positively to Bigstone's subsequent sincere advice and encouragement, feeling they had made the right choice in approaching him.
Bigstone felt proud too.
They say sincerity comes through—following Liznah and Moss's advice to be honest earned him the freshmen's respect.
That's right, exceptional skills aren't always necessary.
Sometimes sincerity alone can convey your feelings!

Thud.

"Ugh, it's too heavy."

Titosoga, who had been listening to Bigstone's advice while blending in with the students, suddenly stood up and shouted.

"Oknodie? That's a Giant King Crab behind you! How did you catch it?!"
"This? It was fearlessly blocking the hallway, so I caught it. The cafeteria has already captured so many that it's hard to get a good price, so I'm planning to cook it for dinner."
"I heard those have extremely high physical resistance."
"Then you just flip them over."
"Flip them...?"
"Crabs can't get back up on their own once they're flipped on land. Unlike their pincers or shell, their underbelly is easily pierced by knives and breaks easily."

How do you even do that?
Oknodie casually demonstrated to the bewildered freshmen.

"Heave!"

① Grab the Giant King Crab's hind legs.
② Lift it up and slam it down.
③ Thrust a knife into its underbelly and you're done!

"See? Easy, right?"

How does that even get flipped over?
How do you even approach it?
Among the dumbfounded students, Bigstone quietly sat at his desk with his head bowed low.

"I can borrow a steamer and heavy-duty cooking scissors. I've borrowed cooking tools from the cafeteria many times."
"Wow! Oknodie is amazing!"
"Isabel will become the world's greatest chef! We'll definitely support you!"
"No, I'm going to be an expedition leader..."
"Come visit my family during vacation. I'll show you the chef's sword that's only permitted to three-star chefs!"

The juniors instantly stole the popularity Bigstone had been enjoying.
Liznah patted her pitiful classmate's shoulder.

"Cheer up. They're naturally talented upper-class students."
"Sob. I'm going to get stronger. I'll work super hard and become much stronger than those nasty upper-class kids!"
"Oh, would you seniors like to join us for dinner tonight? It's so big that we won't be able to finish it all ourselves."
"Sorry, Bigstone is currently nursing an emotional wound..."
"Of course we'll come!"
"...Bigstone?"
"...I need to eat to get stronger!"

There was no pride in the face of hunger.


* * *


The third session of the "Developing an Eye" class began with everyone distracted by the Giant King Crab, eagerly waiting for dinner time.
But the students' attention wasn't on dinner or on the justice-obsessed professor who taught them.

Swoooooosh!!

The rain poured down so intensely that it was hard to tell if this was an academy or a rainy-concept café with artificial downpour.
Everyone was distracted by the sound of rain that made the journey home seem daunting.
Honestly, I'm a bit scared too.

'Experienced or not, getting swept away in that would mean instant drowning!'

Considering the unsettled atmosphere, this lecture became a regular theory class rather than a practice session requiring concentration.

"As a bonus, in the next assignment period, I'll conduct a lecture on developing an eye for property valuation, which the noble students will particularly enjoy. So research related books or official documents."
"Professor, where can we find these books or documents?"
"Books are in the library, and documents can be obtained from the academy's administrative office. While external borrowing is difficult in principle, it's possible if you prove you're attending my lecture."

Learning how to use the library and visit the administrative office as academy students was quite a valuable opportunity.
Despite testing students with strange lectures every time, the professor surprisingly included subtle considerations to help students adapt to academy life.

"This alone might not motivate you. So I'll add bonus points."

Of course, if the professor were only kind, he wouldn't be a Gift Academy professor.
Professor Bronze revealed his wicked true colors.

"I plan to select three students from each section who bring the most appropriate related books and documents to the next lecture and give them bonus points, so work diligently."

Titosoga pressed the spotlight button, emitting a bright white light.

"Professor! I have a question!"
"How distracting. Turn that off before speaking."
"Ah, yes."

After turning off the light, Titosoga asked:

"There are three second-year seniors taking this lecture, so wouldn't selecting only three people per section put first-years at a disadvantage in the bonus point competition?"
"Indeed it would."

The professor readily agreed.

"So?"
"Pardon?"
"Are you asking me to increase the bonus point quota exclusively for first-years because second-years have an advantage in this competition?"

T.O. (Table of Organization).
This term, referring to an organizational chart, is commonly used to mean "a position has opened up" or "a job opportunity has become available."
Here, it means asking to increase the opportunities or positions eligible for bonus points.

"Oh, this doesn't look good."

A cruel smile appeared on the professor's lips.

"Very well. I'll add three exclusive spots for first-years."
"Wow!"
"Thanks, Titosoga!"
"Thanks to Titosoga, we have more chances to get bonus points!"
"Professor! If multiple students prepare the same book, will they all receive bonus points?"
"Yes, they will. If they've made exactly the same preparations, they all deserve praise."

Titosoga, who was just a weakling coward in the fifth period Adventurer's Night Action class, was now an honor student receiving recognition and gratitude from peers in the second period Developing an Eye class?
Such an otherworldly experience was completely overturned by the professor's next words.

"However, according to Gift Academy's academic grading regulations, penalty points must increase by the same amount as bonus points exceeding the standard."
"So let's do this. The bottom three with inadequate preparation will receive penalty points equal to what the top three receive."

The classroom turned ice cold.
The professor's verbal assault didn't stop.

"Of course, just as there are shared bonus points, there should be shared penalty points, right? Students who make no preparation at all will all receive penalty points together. I hope you prepare plenty of points in advance."

"Titosoga, you piece of trash!"
"How can you call yourself human?!"
"You villain!"
"Professor's lackey!"
"Apocalyptic demon who summons penalty points!"
"Someone like you doesn't deserve to eat Oknodie's Giant King Crab meat!"

Titosoga, who had been shining the spotlight and hearing comments like "Isn't he cute?" and "He reminds me of my little sister I left at home," suddenly became a great criminal.

"N-no, you misunderstand. Don't look at me like that. I just wanted to help everyone..."

Despite Titosoga's excuses, the condemnation didn't stop.

"You're wasting your years!"
"Idiot who carries around toys!"

When even the spotlight, which had been rising as a charming point, became a target of personal attacks, tears rolled down Titosoga's eyes.

"Waaah!"

Titosoga burst into tears and ran out of the classroom.

"Oh dear. That's unfortunate."
"Sigh. I thought he was being too forward. I'll go comfort Titosoga for a bit."
"Ah, Oknodie. Have you made a decision about our previous conversation?"
"I'll answer you later because of Titosoga!"

Oknodie is a child who cherishes friends.
Comforting a friend is more important than answering the professor's offer to become his apprentice and graduate student!
This is definitely not about running away from the answer because she doesn't want to become a graduate student!

"Wait, wait a minute! Oknodie, if you leave alone, what am I supposed to do with this Giant King Crab!"

Oknodie is a child who cherishes friends.
Comforting a friend is more important than struggling to carry a heavy Giant King Crab for dinner!
This is definitely not about running away because the Giant King Crab is heavier than expected!
