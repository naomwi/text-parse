# 240 - Wrong Idea

"To participate in the individual competition, you need a minimum of five people. Find more members."

Dead Cat and Oknodie, who were about to face off, confronted this inconvenient reality.
They had brought Xing and Hestia, but they couldn't even start the match without five people!

"I'll participate."

Isabel volunteered to fill the numbers, but Oknodie firmly refused.

"Um, no!"
"Why? You're short on people. Isn't it better if I join rather than some stranger?"
"Because Isabel could get seriously hurt."
"What about Hestia and Xing?"
"Those two have the physical ability to minimize injuries even in dangerous situations. You don't!"

Though Isabel knew there was a skill difference, hearing it stated so bluntly to her face left a bitter taste.
So to this child, I'm only useful for cooking.

"Any of you want to take on the challenge?"

When she casually asked the onlookers, they all paled and shook their heads vigorously before running away.
It made sense—if even an advanced class student like her was rejected for being at risk, lower class students wouldn't dare accept.

"Ah, Paper Company President! Want to play dodgeball?"
"With Dead Cat? Me? In the individual competition?"
"Yep!"
"With one of the top three most vicious second-years? Absolutely not."

When even a second-year senior who was a club president drew such a clear line, other second-years didn't even dare to approach.

"Find someone."
"Don't you have anyone you can bring, senior?"

Dead Cat's tail swished as if she had someone in mind.

"Too weak."
"Hmm. Then we have no choice. Isabel, could you please bring one more person?"
"I'll try. I happen to know an idiot with free time."


* * *


"Am I that idiot?"
"You are. You didn't plan which events to participate in, so you just stood in lines all day yesterday and today, wasting your time like an idiot."
"That's rich coming from someone who's been running around beating up delinquents all day after volunteering to help Oknodie this morning."

When Son O-cheon complained, Isabel retorted:

"So you won't help?"
"Who said that? I was just talking back because you kept nagging me."

Son O-cheon gladly accepted the request.
He felt a little nervous.
Isabel had been rejected.
What about him?

"O-cheon mister is too weak, which is problematic!"

If he heard the same rejection, his monkey-shifter pride would be crushed, and he'd spend days in a daze.
But anxiety tends to arise from premonitions of what might happen.
Son O-cheon himself was already aware of his shortcomings.

-O-cheon has remarkable innate dragon power. Until now, you've actively used your racial traits to become stronger more easily and quickly than others. But that's as far as it goes.

His mana sensitivity wasn't particularly outstanding.
His endurance and strength were enough to bully those with rudimentary mana awakening, but insufficient to compete with the true elites of the academy's advanced class.
And since he didn't have any exceptional techniques to compensate, he could only rely on his instinctual wildness in battle.
The professors had observed his efforts.
And evaluated him coldly.

-You're a downgraded version of Hestia.
-Battle-hardened mercenary intuition. Berserker-class rage. All superior to the strength gained from unleashing wild instincts and primal fury.
-Find a reason why you should remain in the advanced class when Hestia is here.

Otherwise, he'd be demoted from the advanced class as early as the second semester, or at the latest, the first semester of his second year.
Son O-cheon felt considerable pressure.
On the surface, he might look like a carefree vagrant who just plucks banana bunches in the forest, but behind the scenes, he was secretly training in an environment similar to his homeland.
Yet he still lacked confidence.
Confidence that he could escape the evaluation of being Hestia's inferior version.
Lost for direction, he had helped Isabel after hearing about Oknodie, and assisted Gisele in subduing and detaining delinquents.
But it was clear that this approach was merely avoiding his real problem.
Then Isabel provided another opportunity.

'Hestia. Xing.'

They were genuine powerhouses in the advanced class, never criticized for their physical abilities.

"Hmm. Hmmmm."

Looking at Son O-cheon, Oknodie placed her hand on her chin, deep in thought like a sports coach deciding whether to field a bench player.
This made both Isabel and Son O-cheon tense up.

"O-cheon mister should be fine!"
"That's the conclusion after all that deliberation?"

As he chuckled with relief while teasing her, an unexpected challenge came:

"It won't work."
"Hestia?"
"You feel it too, don't you, Oknodie? That this senior is barely adequate to face that opponent?"

Son O-cheon's skills were insufficient for this competition.
At Hestia's blunt assessment, Xing glanced at him and turned away.
An attitude suggesting it wasn't even worth discussing.
Son O-cheon realized it.
Indeed, he was inadequate.
Oknodie was probably just trying to be nice.

"Am I just going to be in the way?"
"I hate to say this to someone who came to help, but I'm more worried than concerned about interference."

Worried that he might get hurt.
Even Son O-cheon, who prided himself on being stronger than most knight department aspirants, couldn't help feeling hurt.

"Hey, little mouse. Be honest. If you're saying this out of pity, that's even more pitiful."
"Honestly, it was a bit ambiguous."

An immediate answer.
His heart grew cold.
Being rejected by a child with the greatest talent.
Feeling like he'd been told this was his limit.

"But I think it'll be fine!"
"What? Are you messing with an adult?"
"Owww, that hurts!"

She whined as he tormented her by twisting his knuckles against her temple.
Hestia, who had been watching him with complicated eyes, seemed to accept Oknodie's judgment and extended her hand first.

"I'm sorry. I meant no harm, so I hope you won't take it badly."
"I know. I'll be careful not to hold you back, so don't worry."

Second-year Dead Cat.
First-years Oknodie, Hestia, Xing, and Son O-cheon.
Individual dodgeball competition, five participants registered.

"Each of you, get inside a line."
"What's this...?"
"Sixty seconds. During that time, you'll receive points every second based on the value of the white-lined space you occupy. If a ball hits you and falls to the ground or is caught by someone else, you're immediately eliminated, transported outside, and your score calculation ends."

The court was chaotically marked with white lines creating areas of various sizes.
Naturally, smaller areas were worth more points.

"Would you like to choose first, senior?"
"I don't mind if I do."

Dead Cat walked straight to the center of the court.
The smallest space located right in the middle of all the areas.
Son O-cheon felt strong anxiety at her bold move to occupy the center despite facing a 1 vs 4 situation.

"Is she confident she can win even when surrounded by everyone from the narrowest spot?"

Hestia also frowned, seeming uncomfortable.
Xing had already placed his hand on his sword hilt for some time.
Does that guy even understand what dodgeball is...?

"Don't underestimate her. If she's taken the high-scoring position, our goal should be to eliminate her as quickly as possible. Agreed?"
"Oh, sword guy. Surprisingly thinking about strategy properly?"
"I will stand where my sword can reach her."
"...Are you really thinking about strategy?"
"I told you not to underestimate her."

Xing glared at Son O-cheon with murderous eyes, then asked the instructor:

"Is there a rule against cutting opponents with a sword?"
"No."
"What if we leave our designated square?"
"That's also immediate transportation and elimination."
"What about weapons extending outside the line?"
"It's not elimination as long as there's no 'contact' with the floor of another square. Contact includes abilities and weapons connected to your body."

In other words, as long as the sword doesn't touch the ground, you can swing it at an opponent in a nearby square.

"...I have a bad feeling about this."

Unlike Xing who chose a position close to Dead Cat, Hestia took a middle-distance position.
Son O-cheon also read the room and stood at a diagonal, slightly further away, as if to surround her.
Oknodie, smiling brightly, stepped into a square that completed the triangle's last vertex.

━━━
<Individual Dodgeball Competition>
◇Points per second:
Dead Cat: 10 points
Xing: 9 points
Hestia: 5 points
Son O-cheon: 5 points
Oknodie: 4 points
━━━

"The ball goes to the challenger with the lowest points."

Until this moment, Son O-cheon had thought:
That Dead Cat was the only one they needed to be wary of.
That there was no reason for him to be cautious of Oknodie, whom he was helping.
It was a perfectly reasonable assumption.
But Oknodie was a child who defied common sense.

"Can I choose the type of ball too?"
"You may."
"Then please give me the hardest ball!"

Everyone assumed she wanted a sturdy ball with good grip that fit well in the palm, rather than a squishy one that deformed when pressed.
But when a ball appeared that mercilessly destroyed that common sense, even the confident second-year senior Dead Cat looked confused, as if wondering if this was correct.

"Little mouse."
"Yes?"
"Is that really a dodgeball? It looks more like a steel ball that could match the hardness of a siege weapon for breaking castle gates."
"Oh come on, mister. That's impossible."
"Right? It just looks shiny, but it's actually a normal dodgeball, isn't it?"
"It's a blue metal ball that's much harder than steel and has higher mana conductivity!"

It seemed they needed to protect the senior from Oknodie rather than protecting Oknodie from Dead Cat.
