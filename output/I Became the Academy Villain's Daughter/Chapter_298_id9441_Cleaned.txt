# 298 - The Auction Isn't Over

Unlike the other students who were frantically retrieving their weakness items, Zuang felt no tension at all.

"Zuang, are you going to participate in tomorrow's auction?"
"I'll hold out as long as I can."
"Ugh. This puts me at a disadvantage."
"What's disadvantageous?"
"In the deserted island auction, besides the existing auction items, each participant adds one personal item."

In short, this meant the number of days needed to endure until the last auction item appeared would increase.
Hearing this, I began to understand why Oknodie had encouraged forming teams of two and allowed eliminations.

"So there's an item you want to buy on the last day?"
"Yeah. Originally, we would have had some leeway at this point, but since the professor suddenly accelerated things, the island has become like this..."

Ice pillars were melting in real-time as mana dispersed, with the cold weakening from the edges, while metal structures loosened from within, pieces breaking off and falling into the waves.
Even an assassin with strong survival skills would find it difficult to endure long on such a deserted island.

"Click-click-click"
"Click-click"

Sea crab monsters were approaching, clicking their pincers. With the island's predators—the Blue Metal Rats—gone, they were cautiously testing the waters, poking their feet onto the deserted island.
By tomorrow morning at the latest, or as early as tonight, we would have to compete with monsters while trying to endure.

'I might as well hold out.'

Zuang challenged the seventh day.
Throughout the night, crabs that had climbed onto the island tried to attack her, but she already knew how to drive away Giant King Crabs much larger than these pathetic small ones.

"Mwoong!"

The small crabs couldn't possibly withstand the cry that made even Giant King Crabs tremble and retreat.

'How strange. To think I'd use skills learned from Oknodie like this. I thought I'd never use them in my life.'

Everything she had learned in the "Adventurer's Night-prowling" lecture worked—how to sleep well without being attacked at night, and how to avoid being swept away by waves in the middle of the night.
It's truly amazing how experiencing something firsthand makes those vague sayings about not knowing until you experience it make sense.

As time passed, noon on the seventh day arrived.
Familiar Giant King Crabs crawled up from somewhere beneath the sea.

"Mwoooong!"
"Mwooooong!"

When she waved her arms like an octopus and shouted powerfully with high vocalization, just as Oknodie had taught her, the Giant King Crabs positioned themselves around the auction site.
They seemed to be watching Jonna, ready to pounce if he stepped even one foot outside.

"I will now reveal the seventh item of the deserted island auction. Today's item is 'Thief Finder.'"

The item presented was a Mana Board.
It was a kind of high-grade parchment or control panel used for special operations or functions, closer to an advanced version of the ancient mana scrolls.
As such, its effectiveness and range were far more powerful and broader than ordinary scrolls.

"The starting bid is 250,000 points. Bids will increase by 25,000 points. Is there anyone who wishes to participate in the bidding?"

Zuang asked honestly.

"Oknodie, are you going to buy that?"
"Hmm~ I don't really have a reason to? I already know the locations of the Ten Great Thieves."
"...Really?"
"Yes. Remember when Professor Bronze said Zuang's master was the Life Thief? I know that the Life Thief is hiding in the underground waterways of the Empire!"

It must be knowledge from the Foundation.
The reason she knows so much about influential figures and powerful individuals across the continent must be because of the common knowledge implanted by the Foundation.
Zuang thought she was lucky.
She could roughly guess how to use the Thief Finder Mana Board.
It probably worked by writing the name of the thief you wanted to find on the Mana Board, and then their detailed whereabouts would appear or be inscribed on the board.
It could be used to find one of the Ten Great Thieves, but it could also be used in the future to find Zuang, the disciple of the Life Thief.
By any measure, it was an item that could be considered a fatal weakness for Zuang.

"The seventh day auction item 'Thief Finder' has been sold to participant Zuang for 250,000 boarding points."

Zuang unwrapped the protective scarf from her neck and wrapped it tightly around the Mana Board.
The scarf left by her master to protect the girl's vulnerable vital point was now protecting an item as precious as her neck—continuing her master's legacy admirably.

"Are you going back?"
"Since I bought the item."
"Go ahead and have fun!"

What would Oknodie's weakness item be?
It would surely be something insignificant.
With a light heart, Zuang boarded the boat heading to the cruise ship.
The Giant King Crabs circled around the boat with regret, but scattered in panic when an electric shock spell spread across the water's surface.

'How many days will I have to wait?'

Spending time on the cruise ship without Oknodie would be extremely boring.
Since points had lost their meaning, maybe she should just hide somewhere deserted and pass the time.
Hugging the Mana Board to her chest, Zuang returned to the cruise ship and quickly felt perplexed.

"No! If the coolant freezes, the engine room will be ruined. Please, just don't use ice magic!"
"If you don't want to pay for repairs due to machine damage, cooperate with the rebellion."
"Is this really okay? Isn't raising a flag against the Foundation much more dangerous?"
"Idiot. Our role is to protect the engine room, not stop the rebellion. Even if we don't participate in the rebellion, we'll be in debt for life if the equipment breaks down."

Students threatening crew members with magic, and crew members surrendering in tears.

"Unlike you cowards, I'll show you Foundation crew members how terrifying a..."

The brave crew member who stepped forward was kicked away faster than he could run, rolling across the corridor floor.
The female student lifted her robe and revealed her face, causing even greater commotion among the crew members.

"That distinctive orange hair must be the same color as the hero Ishtar's!"

The arrogant hero Ishtar, who only cared about herself.
For some reason, she was helping the shipboard rebellion by subduing resistant crew members.
Her neat appearance was greatly distorted by an expression full of malice.
When that expression was directed at crew members rather than fellow students, it couldn't have been more reassuring.
Was this really the same hero who had made her classmates tremble in fear until recently?
Why was someone receiving a Foundation scholarship beating up Foundation crew members?
Faced with this fascinating scene, Zuang realized that the time waiting for Oknodie's return wouldn't be boring at all.

The crew members who should have been stopping the students swarming like a pack of dogs were instead lending their strength to the other side.
By the time he realized that the crew members, who had been gradually decreasing one by one, had been recruited by the enemy, it was already too late.

"I can't. If you leave now, both the helm and the captain's quarters will fall!"
"If you don't let go, I'll tear your hand off and eat it."

The chief navigator turned pale and released his grip on the Apostle of Anrage.
The Apostle of Anrage was a military spirit with multiple personalities residing in one body.
The personality of the human flesh-eater Gustav had risen to the surface after driving out the pacifist Posep's personality and had continued for several days, but even he began to detect the emergence of the next personality.

"The flesh-eater's personality is weak against those who know no fear. Frustrating as it is, I have no choice but to surrender this body to the next personality."

Gustav allowed the personality transition.
His intensity, which had been rolling his eyes and convulsing, subsided with surprising coldness.
His bristling hair settled down on its own, and his awkward gait and unrefined senses were sharpened like a blade.

"Excuse me, but which personality has awakened...?"
"Hero candidate Kei."

The chief navigator thought that Oknodie's friends were truly unlucky.
One of the top-tier personalities possessed by the Apostle of Anrage had emerged.

"Mapping."

The chief navigator, startled by the command, hurriedly operated the Mana Board.
The mana coordinates connected to the boarding cards appeared on the helm's control panel.

"Classify."

At Kei's instruction, the coordinates of the students most needed in the current situation appeared among the numerous coordinates.

"Link."
"Synchronization complete."

The information was transmitted to the mana chip implanted in the Apostle of Anrage's brain.
With this, the location information of the students appeared like a mini-map in one corner of Kei's vision.
The guardian of the cruise ship.
An apostle bound by debt to the Foundation.
The mini-map ability, a shackle forced upon the corrupted military spirit to fulfill its purpose and optimized for defense, was indeed useful.

"...You, are you really human?"

Kei, who had suddenly burst into the control room while evading the eyes of the monitors.
As soon as Sing saw Kei's sword split the ice wall reinforced by Irene with a single strike, he realized he had encountered an opponent he could never defeat.
His plan to dump all outside matters on the hero or Irene until Oknodie returned, while spending time training, was ruined.

"Choose. Be cut down, or surrender."
"...I surrender."

He thought that somehow things would work out once Oknodie arrived.
It was a complete misjudgment.
Oknodie was such a stubborn person that she wouldn't return even after three days on the chaotic deserted island.
And Kei, who had emerged as the new main personality of the Apostle of Anrage, was a far more dangerous being than expected.

"They might exchange us for crew members."

Kei responded in a cold voice to the casual conversation of the students who had been attacked after Sing.
