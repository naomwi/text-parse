# 392 - Greenhouse Security Measures

Unlike the rash and reckless second-year students, the Plant Club president Orkcatch was a third-year who had been through it all.
After Oknodie stole his baby mandrake, he had once requested help from Professor Bronze while carefully searching for the culprit.

'She may have cunningly deflected with her chest, but thanks to that, I learned that the professor was suspicious.'

Shortly after, rumors spread that Professor Bronze had marked a first-year named Oknodie as her protégé.
The information came from a Chemistry/Alchemy Club member named Bigstone who supplied culture medium to the Plant Club and attended the same lecture, so its credibility was high.

'Was the professor trying to protect that child?'

After keeping an eye on her, he realized she was a complete serial offender.
Every night, she would scurry down the dormitory walls and dash across the lawn on her nightly excursions.
She frequently stole strange objects or stones from various places, promptly putting them in her mouth or pockets.
When bored, she would go to the beach and pretend to build sandcastles while actually digging up the ground—clearly investigating suitable soil for hiding stolen goods.
She might not be an apprentice of a righteous thief, but her talent as a young thief was certainly in full bloom!

'She really committed to her thief build during the break.'

After the break, she somehow acquired a magic backpack and stuffed it with items pilfered from everywhere.
She skillfully stole items that students had hidden around campus using sight obstruction, perception interference, and spatial distortion magic for assignments or personal growth.
She cleaned out everything so thoroughly that the red-toothed mushroom cultivators should consider themselves lucky if they even realized they'd been robbed.

'Magic armor, magic swords, explosive powder that requires careful handling, and even past exam papers!'

As if planning to establish a thieves' guild at the Academy, she even distributed stolen items to children who joined the ridiculous organization called "The Group That Plays with Oknodie."
Judging by the dramatic improvement in the grades of those organization members, she must have stolen plenty of past exam papers while he wasn't looking.
While others were honorably struggling without knowing last year's exam questions, she was unfairly analyzing the examiner's intentions and tendencies to study efficiently!

"It's outrageous. It's so outrageous I can't forgive it."

Whether it was because of the grudge from having his baby mandrake taken or because of the enviably outrageous juniors who were cheating their way through exams, his feelings of indignation were genuine!

"Let's go raid the professor's herb garden!"

Orkcatch quickly moved as he watched the tiny but strong girl persuade a large female student who could wrestle a bear without losing as her escort.
Finally, it was time to teach that outrageous freshman thief a bitter life lesson.
In the name of Orkcatch, third-year Plant Club president...

"Professor Red Mountain. A first-year has appeared who dares to steal from your herb garden."

...with the power of Professor Red Mountain who would receive this theft report!

"Huh? A first-year is stealing from my herb garden?"
"Yes. Without a doubt. I heard it clearly with my own two ears!"
"A first-year stealing a second-year's course assignment? And from a course where I, Red Mount, am the professor?"
"Yes! I definitely heard it."
"And you expect me to believe that?"

The female professor, whose skin was fair and glossy perhaps from lounging around and enjoying the good life, had a suspicious look in her eyes. Though she wore a long, carelessly thrown-on t-shirt, she had no pants, with only a monkey tail swishing back and forth underneath.
The professor doesn't believe me!
Before the flustered Orkcatch could try to be more persuasive, the professor's magic sealed his mouth.

"Mmph!"
"I've seen despicable third-years like you before. Trash who claim they can't bear to see juniors suffering with assignments and try to secretly harvest growing herbs. Did you think I wouldn't realize you were planning to steal herbs yourself under the pretext of a first-year?"
"Mmph mmph mmph!"

Truly wronged, Orkcatch expressed his innocence with his whole body, blood vessels popping in his eyes.
Seeing his desperate gestures, the professor used her tail to feel along the wall until she grabbed a drawer handle.

*Slide*

Orkcatch trembled in fear as he watched her tail rummage through the drawer, tossing out potions labeled "Sleep for One Year Potion," "Add 'Wunya-nya' to Every Sentence Potion," and "Walk on All Fours for 30 Days Potion."
Professor Red Mountain was famous for developing all sorts of bizarre potions, as she exploited students while consuming plenty of good herbs herself.
Her tail finally selected one bottle, closed the drawer, and placed it firmly in front of Orkcatch.
His gaze momentarily drifted to her thighs, smooth and silky as her t-shirt rode up along her tail, but the sound of her tail tapping on the table brought him back to his senses.

"Drink it. If you have something to say, I'll listen afterward."
"Mmph mmph mmph?"
"What kind of potion is it? See for yourself."

Professor Red Mountain turned the bottle halfway with her tail to make the label easier to read.

"Potion that Transfers Points to Professor Red Mountain When You Lie"

"MMPH MMPH MMPH!!"

How could such a terrible potion exist?
This is a nightmare!
After force-feeding the potion to the protesting Orkcatch, the professor was very surprised when he repeated his earlier statement without transferring any points.

"So a first-year really did say they would raid my herb garden?"
"That's what I've been saying! How could you trample on someone's goodwill like this!"
"I'm sorry. As an apology, I'll let you touch my tail once."
"I don't need that!"
"Really? My tail is extremely soft."

Orkcatch's face reddened as her tail curved seductively like a snake's tongue, wrapping around his arm.

"Puhah. Just kidding. Why are you taking it so seriously? Thanks for the report, you can go now."
"Before I leave, I'd like to ask one thing."
"Curious about how I'll deal with that first-year?"
"How long will the potion I just drank make me send points to you when I lie...?"
"Tch. It's only for a week, why is a man being so petty about such a small thing?"
"Release me!!"

After taking the antidote and safely being freed from the curse-like effect, Orkcatch left.
Alone, swishing her tail and putting the potion bottle back in the drawer, the professor recalled something.
Do I really have to prevent this myself? How annoying.

"Assistant. Post a notice on the magic watch lecture board."

Don't forget to include a warning that since they've been notified of an impending raid in advance, students will be responsible for any lost items!

"Hehe. I really am a genius."

Now all the hassle belongs to the students.
The responsibility for lost herbs belongs to the students.
And the herbs that students work hard to cultivate belong to me!

"Excuse me, Professor. I hate to interrupt your pleasant daydreaming, but you should be especially careful of this particular first-year."
"What's wrong with you too, assistant? Do you also want to steal some potion from the lab?"
"Although the Plant Club president isn't much except for his harvesting skills, the first-year Oknodie he warned about is no ordinary person."
"She's still just a first-year."
"That child is known as the Continental Top Thief and is the protégé of Professor Bronze, the righteous thief."

The assistant, who had witnessed the professor being so lazy that she was nicknamed the incarnation of sloth—floating around on a levitating lounge chair with flight magic—saw for the first time Professor Red Mountain jump to her feet, something she rarely did.

"The protégé of that bewitching woman who stole my lover dares to come steal from my herb garden? Well, that changes everything!"

The professor stared at the assistant with eyes burning with anger instead of her usual sleepy, languid gaze.

"Let's set a trap."
"Who will?"
"Summon all instructors."
"Sigh."
"And you too."
"..."

I shouldn't have tried to sound smart in front of the professor.
The assistant wept internally.


* * *


"Have you all heard? That little Oknodie apparently pulverized the second-year leave-of-absence student Russo."
"Wasn't Russo assigned as a dormitory supervisor, the lowest position among security assistant instructors, after taking a leave of absence because he couldn't pass the third-year advancement exam? And for first-years at that."

Not all instructors are the same.
There are security assistant instructors, the lowest of the low.
Administrative assistant instructors, evaluated for their diligence.
Practical assistant instructors, evaluated for their versatile skills.
And professor-affiliated instructors, who possess something special that catches professors' attention and are envied by all other instructors.

The instructors employed by Professor Red Mountain were practical assistant instructors who had been deceived for five years by sweet talk and the professor's provocative attire, believing they would eventually become professor-affiliated instructors.
Whatever the case, these instructors, who prided themselves on being far superior to security instructors like Russo, weren't particularly impressed by Oknodie's exploits.

"She might be somewhat capable among first-years. After all, Russo did somehow complete the curriculum up to the second year. But she's no match for us."
"Right. We're trusted assistants whom Professor Red Mountain entrusts with installing periodic flight magic formations on her lounge chair, checking and replacing mana stones, maintaining greenhouse temperature, preventing external monster invasions, and sometimes playing the role of armed robbers to instill tension in relaxed classrooms!"

For this reason, these instructors, already accustomed to terrorizing current second-years as armed robbers, could easily predict how Oknodie might infiltrate the greenhouse.

"She'll probably tamper with the control magic formation for the greenhouse's heating and cooling system to lure us out."
"She might blend in with students and brazenly enter through the front."
"Or she might create confusion with fake notifications about canceled lectures or classroom changes to target the herb garden."

Whatever the approach, exploiting gaps would be the best strategy.
Trying to force her way through would make her vulnerable not only to the security measures installed by the assistants but also to those set by the professor herself, which they had never experienced.
Still, since the professor had instructed them to prevent her own security measures from activating if possible, the assistants diligently reconfigured existing traps and installed new ones.

"Aren't there so many that even we're afraid to go in and out?"
"Let's create security formula card keys so that traps won't activate for assistants carrying the cards. That way, only Oknodie, who tries to infiltrate without us seeing, will suffer."
"Oh, that's good!"

The assistants, who had mastered the art of laziness like their professor, created high-pass card keys without being asked and went home satisfied.
Unaware that first-year students were lurking in the bushes along their way home, eyes gleaming as they prepared to ambush isolated assistants.
