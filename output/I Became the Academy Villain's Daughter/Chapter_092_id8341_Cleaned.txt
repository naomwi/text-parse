# 92 - Slave Contract

"Oknodie, freshman. Indeed, your discerning eye was undoubtedly born for becoming a righteous thief."
"Me??"
"Would you like to become my chief disciple?"
"Chief disciple means becoming a graduate student, right?"
"Graduate student? In the sense of completing major studies and researching deeper understanding, you're not wrong."

I messed up.
I should have been just adequate in front of the professor, but I did too well and caught his attention!

"Absolutely not!!"

That's what I desperately want to say, but if I did, greater consequences would await.

"Young lady, do you realize your position? I, a professor, have shown you goodwill, and you dare not only refuse but also behave rudely?"

If I got such a response, I'd have to consider my grades doomed from that day forward.

"It seems too burdensome for me. I'm still young and quite timid."
"False modesty is also a worthy quality for a righteous thief."
"I'm too lazy to succeed. I have many classes to attend, and I'm at an age where I enjoy hanging out with friends!"
"When you become a righteous thief, all the world's treasures can be your friends. Stealing others' treasures is even more fun. That's the pleasure you could enjoy as my chief disciple."
"I'll think about it."
"Take your time until the next lecture."

Only after giving an ambiguous smile could I escape from Professor Bronze's lair.

'The efficiency of gaining skill experience is insane, but something feels off. There's no way the professor would be this kind.'

I've often heard about schemes to turn poor college students into graduate student slaves with just one meal.
This must definitely be an evil scheme to lure me with skill experience!
But what if there's another honey-trap event next week?
Could I eat and run?
What if I take the bait and become a chief disciple-graduate student?
With numerous concerns, I moved away from the research building.


* * *


Diligent students who packed their class schedules from Monday to Thursday enjoyed their freedom on Friday.

"I'm hungry."
"I could chew on rocks."
"That grass over there, doesn't it look delicious?"

The freedom to starve.
Without classes, there are fewer opportunities to earn points.
The students were filled with longing.

"I miss the custard dishes we had every evening."
"From now on, I won't commit the discourtesy of skipping banquets just because I'm full."
"I miss the hot, heavy petticoats. I want to hang out with friends I made at dinner parties."

Of course, these were the luxurious complaints of imperial nobles.
Nobles from the frontier who overheard these laments as they passed by snickered.

"Let them complain all they want. As if points would just fall from the sky."
"Ah, be careful! First-years down there!"
"What?"
"I dropped contracts worth 10,000 points each, but it would be terrible if you picked them up carelessly!"
"!!"

Points really did fall from the sky!
The imperial nobles, who were expected to eagerly grab the fluttering contracts, surprisingly showed cautious attitudes and put their hands behind their backs.
Though their minds were filled with luxurious past memories and complaints like they were in a flower garden, they were still nobles after all.
They had grown up with strict home education and private tutoring in noble households.
They had been repeatedly told never to touch suspicious contracts.

'Still, I'm curious.'

Arcadia felt curious.
She was the type who would board a warship in summer to cool off by blasting pirate ships with cannons.
Not thinking anything serious would happen just from looking at a contract, she reached for one as it fell.

"Don't!"
"Oknodie?"

Oknodie, who was passing by, ran over and pulled away the hand that was about to grab the contract.

"Dia. No matter how glad you are to see me, a young lady shouldn't lose her dignity by grabbing another person's arm in front of strangers."
"Arcadia, you were about to do something terrible. Those contracts have forced signature magic that makes anyone who grabs them stamp their fingerprint in the designated space!"
"What?!"

When Arcadia came to her senses and looked around, she saw a student who had inadvertently grabbed a contract frantically stamping their thumb.

"Freshmen, be careful! I accidentally dropped official seals that have legal effect under school law!"

A senior holding ten seals threw them out the window.

"Ahhh, my body is moving on its own!"
"Someone stop me!"
"This guy, why is he suddenly so strong? Hey, you come help stop him too!"
"Stay still. You might end up stamping your hand on a suspicious contract!"

Freshmen ran with their eyes fixed on the fingerprints.
Arcadia was shocked seeing the pitiful lower-class students.

"How could seniors do such a terrible thing!"
"Calm down, Arcadia. They're second-years. If we confront them carelessly, we'll only get hurt."

Students who were trying to stop their friends obsessed with contracts and fingerprints peeked at the contract contents.

"How can a contract be this professional?"
"Wait, they're giving points?"
"Isn't this actually a good deal? The method feels a bit unpleasant and suspicious, but it creates a way to earn points even if you're not good at studying."
"Just carrying 50kg of sand earns 5p for one meal?"
"It's like weight training too, isn't that good?"

While the poor lower-class students hesitated, the first student who touched the contract applied ink to their thumb and stamped it.
Not wanting to be left behind, other students started fighting over who would pick up the contracts on the floor.

"Dia. I'm also tempted by those contracts."
"Don't be fooled. How can you enter into a contract without knowing the market price of items?"
"That's not it. If I get that contract, I can find out in advance what materials will be needed when we become second-years. If I monopolize those supplies, I can name my price... hehehe."

Ah, Arcadia is scary!
When Oknodie made a frightened expression, Arcadia felt bad about being disliked.

"Oh, don't make such a scared face, Dia. I just recalled some experiences helping with family business. I'm still your kind and sweet big sister as always!"

Though regretful, I should give up on the contract.
After sending Oknodie away, I can get it later through the Western Noble Union's lower-class qualifiers.
I'm the only female student from the Western Noble Union who qualified for the upper class, but there are quite a few who qualified for the lower class.

[You have completed the event: Prevent Slave Contract (Arcadia).]
[Arcadia becomes a free person.]

I'm glad I stopped by, concerned about the random early-game events that happen around this time.
Arcadia almost became a slave!

That contract, while seemingly creating job opportunities for poor students to earn meals, is actually a complete scam.
Second-year Bellocasio.
A chaotic evil character from the 980th class, one year above the 981st, who exploits students according to the rules of his self-made contracts.
He sells the goods received from students to others at two or three times the price, and when students start complaining and rebelling, he reveals the terrifying reality.

○If Party B fails to supply goods within the deadline set by Party A, Party B shall pay double the penalty or fulfill the punishment.

Bellocasio assigns an enormous amount of supplies and short deadlines to suppliers who go against his wishes.
The right of refusal is limited to 3 times per year.
Supply requests can be executed once a week.
After 3 weeks, in the 4th week, the contract must be fulfilled - a devilish agreement.
According to the supply deadline by weight, materials over 100kg must be supplied within a week.
Students who initially thought this was reasonable because of "sand" are shocked when they see lists filled with rare items weighing over 100kg.

-I can't possibly find these things!
-Please give me a break just this once!

Even if they manage to find the supplies with difficulty, without any compensation clause, they must seek help from the student council.
Is it possible for first-years, who are already struggling with the storm of assignments coming next week, to find time to submit petitions to the student council, schedule dispute management, and follow procedures?
Usually, they don't even know such things exist.
Before that, after several minor delivery failures, clauses like *prohibition of disclosure*, *confidentiality obligation*, and *additional penalties* are attached one after another, and students who don't know the law are helplessly victimized.
Even noble students would shudder at being so badly caught, so what about commoner students who lack education?
They literally become slaves.
Instead of canceling the enormous compensation, they become supply slaves who provide all requested materials without pay for a certain period, barely guaranteed food and lodging.

'I should teach him a lesson later, but since he hasn't revealed his true colors yet, is it time to leave him be?'

I was planning to just find some stat stones buried near the beach and dig up some sand before returning, when I spotted Arcadia and Gisele talking near the dormitory.

"It seems the little lady has been building sand castles."
"My goodness. How can Dia be so adorable?"
"What are you two doing?"
"We're discussing business. It's probably still difficult for Miss Oknodie to understand."
"We got inspired by the seniors' business model and thought of a good revenue model we could use."

...I'm starting to fear what these people are planning more than the seniors.
What kind of commotion is Gisele, who caused an uproar with fake flags, planning this time?
