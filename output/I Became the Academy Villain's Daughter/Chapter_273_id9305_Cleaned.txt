# 273 - Reversal of Perception

The Academy has far more instructors than students realize.
To assist professors with their classes?
If needed, teaching assistants would suffice.
Unless professors personally brought in numerous instructors, they typically don't require many.
Perhaps to create jobs so upper-class students can easily earn points?
While that's partly true, it's not the whole reason.
Those involved know why there are so many instructors.

"If the transparent orbs containing sight magic break, your salary breaks too. Exercise extreme caution to prevent any damage."
"Yes..."

To spread the Dragon Headmaster's <Sight Orbs> throughout the Academy by attaching them to body parts or floating them in surrounding spaces!
In other words, they're mobile CCTV.
Since the Dragon Headmaster, who showed interest in everything, lost interest just as quickly, most sight orbs were monitored by Guardian and Grade Director Professor <Mahabharata> and his dedicated surveillance team, who collected information because the headmaster thought it would be wasteful to discard the orbs.

"A Jigoku first-year's bullet destroyed three windows. Property damage: -15 points."
"An Ishtar first-year rescued 12 kidnapped students. Requesting calculation of per-person allocation points for the entire organization."
"Wouldn't 50 be enough?"
"Understood. Rescue bonus: +600 points. Leader bonus: additional +600 points for Ishtar personally."
"A Magdonardo fourth-year threw a third-year who provoked them into a dimensional gate. Underclassman violence: -500 points. According to regulations, if they fail to return on their own within one hour, a rescue team will be dispatched to the identified coordinates, and costs will be charged."

From littering to property damage and school violence—a real-time manual point measurement department established to witness all kinds of incidents and provide feedback through points!
Not only was the number of administrative staff significant, but there were also plenty of instructors in the field to monitor students.

"Today, the fourth-years are relatively well-behaved, which puts me in a good mood. I still get headaches thinking about last week's incident caused by that idiot who opened a gate to the spirit realm instead of the demon realm while practicing dimensional travel for explosion magic."

At Mahabharata's words, the staff who had monitored that incident trembled.
Witnessing an accident that could have turned the continent upside down makes one wonder why humans only have one heart—it would be nice to have a spare to switch out.
Compared to that, the first-years' silkworm-ain extermination commotion was truly small, dainty, and cute.

"Next week, I should convince the headmaster to designate a Plant Friendship Week to appease the thoroughly angered plant-type spirits."
"Professor Mahabharata, what happens if you fail to convince him?"
"Plant-type spirits would supply massive negative energy, causing plant-type monsters to attack the Academy. If that's prevented, plants across the continent would transform into plant monsters and rampage out of spite."
"...Will the headmaster cooperate?"
"To be honest, he absolutely won't. He'd find the chaos more entertaining and might even fan the flames."

Mahabharata is essentially responsible for the peace of the Academy, its students, and the world!
While her attention was focused on devising ways to persuade the headmaster, one of the instructors' sight orbs captured a bizarre scene.

"Um, senior."
"What? Did Oknodie successfully exterminate the silkworm queen? If she also rescued the students the hero missed in the 5th layer, about 6000 points would be appropriate."
"That's not it. Oknodie is forming an alliance with the silkworm queen. Do you know how many points to award for establishing friendly relations with a different species?"
"...What nonsense is this?"

A veteran control officer who had been eavesdropping on Professor Mahabharata's conversation allocated Oknodie's video feed to his personal mana board screen.
For some reason, silkworm-ains—who normally swoop down from trees when they spot shiny metal weapons, shouting "I'll take this!" before grabbing them and disappearing—were circling around Oknodie, giving three cheers.

"...What bizarre thing has this kid done now?"


* * *


Rescuing the lighting equipment kidnapped by monsters.
...It would have been better if it were people.
Irene finally felt that enrolling in the Academy was worthwhile.
Her goal was to gain power from the Academy to save the harsh northern people who constantly suffered from demon invasions.
Although it was just lighting equipment rather than people, the fact that Oknodie had begun rescue operations gave her hope that the North might also receive salvation from her.

"Oknodie really cares about her friends. She's determined to advance deep into a dangerous monster habitat just for lighting equipment."
"Of course! Titosoga without lighting equipment isn't Titosoga!"
"What if it had been someone else's lighting equipment, not Titosoga's?"
"Hmm... I'd still go get it! Because I could give Titosoga a +2 lighting equipment!"
"+2...?"
"Oh, you don't need to know about being able to upgrade equipment when you collect the same item in multiples of two!"

So that's possible at the Academy.
This would be irrelevant in the North, where they use crudely carved wooden spears due to weapon shortages.
It must be a luxurious culture passed down in the wealthy Empire.
She probably didn't want to share this information with Irene, the Northern Grand Duchess.
While Irene felt more grateful for Oknodie's thoughtfulness, she couldn't help but continue her questions.

"What if Titosoga was kidnapped instead of the lighting equipment?"
"I'd rescue him!"
"What about someone completely unrelated, neither lighting equipment nor Titosoga?"

For the first time, Oknodie paused before answering and fell into contemplation.

"If there's a quest, I'd rescue them?"
"...What if there's no request or reward from anyone?"
"If I'm not busy, I might help out. But if it's too much trouble, I'd leave them be."

As expected, this child's view of human life is quite ruthless.
She would definitely rescue a friend.
She would consider helping an acquaintance.
For unrelated people, she wouldn't care unless they piqued her interest or she had nothing better to do.
Having learned what she wanted to know, Irene's expression darkened.
Currently, the northern people were unrelated humans who didn't interest Oknodie, and considering the attention she would receive from the Foundation and Academy professors after graduation, she wouldn't be free either.
The possibility of Oknodie joining Irene to save the North was virtually non-existent.
Somehow, she needed to win Oknodie's favor, improve her perception of northerners, and find something to draw Oknodie to the harsh northern lands.
If she couldn't find it during her studies, she would have no choice but to rely on the unreliable hero or the Grand Duke's son from the Western Return.

'How fortunate.'

Unlike her dire circumstances, the kidnapped students could be rescued from the cocoons while crying for help, solely because Titosoga's lighting equipment had been taken.

"Hmm. The viscosity isn't bad. It could help with developing unique skills."

Zuang, who cut open cocoons with a dagger to extract students, showed interest in the empty cocoons.
It was clear they had excessive firepower, from Hestia who menacingly swung her hammer to drive away the silkworm-ains, to Xing who didn't even consider drawing his sword.
Monster habitats typically have a high chance of extremely powerful boss monsters appearing, but they also had the extremely powerful Oknodie, who was far beyond a typical first-year.
There was no reason to be tense.

"If we've rescued everyone... should I freeze them all?"
"No! What if the lighting equipment freezes and breaks?"

Oknodie, who valued the lighting equipment more than people, prohibited Irene from deploying wide-area magic.
Fortunately, they didn't have to worry about cutting open all the cocoons to find the lighting equipment.
The silkworm-ains were gathered around an exceptionally shiny, large cocoon.

"Tsu-tsu-tsu."

The silkworm-ains requested help from their boss monster leader.
The large cocoon split in half, and the <Silkworm-ain Queen>, with more elaborate patterns than ordinary silkworm-ains, gently fluttered down to the ground.
When the Silkworm-ain Queen raised the lighting equipment high, a <Red Light That Raises Morale> turned on.

"Tsu-tsu-tsu."
"Tsu-tsu-tsu."

The silkworm-ains' wing-fluttering doubled in speed as their morale heightened!

"They're excited like primitive humans discovering fire."

Zuang's honest comment seemed quite cute, but if these creatures all sprayed <Paralyzing Powder> and engaged in close combat to prevent them from holding their breath, it would become troublesome.

"Oknodie. If we're going to fight, we need to strike first."
"Hmm. Wait a moment! Moths are naturally attracted to street lights... In that case!"

Oknodie chuckled as if she'd thought of something bizarre.
She approached the Silkworm-ain Queen and shouted.

"Silkworm! Do you know that the light from that lighting equipment will eventually disappear?"
"Tsu-tsu-tsu?!"
"If you return the lighting equipment, I'll help you get many more shining lights!"
"Tsu-tsu-tsu..."
"Look, if you do this, you can create more lights!"

When she attached a mana sticker with a lighting formula to a nearby fish tank, the entire tank emitted a blue light, creating a dreamy atmosphere like the deep sea.

"Tsu-tsu-tsu!"
"Tsu-tsu-tsu!"

The excited silkworm-ains gathered around the fish tank, fluttering their wings slowly like jellyfish, circling nearby.
Seemingly as satisfied as her subordinates, the Silkworm-ain Queen willingly handed the lighting equipment to Oknodie.

"That's ingenious. Negotiating with monsters like that—only a child's innocent perspective could imagine such a thing."

Irene silently agreed with Hestia's sincere admiration.
In the North, monsters were either killed or forcibly eaten as precious food.
Peacefully exchanging what each side wanted with such monsters—she had never considered the possibility or necessity.

"I can help make the lights last longer by regularly infusing them with mana. In exchange, help us freely gather materials in the forest!"
"Tsu-tsu-tsu."
"Heheh. What a nice queen. She says they'll leave us alone as long as we don't attack them first!"

Setting aside how they were communicating, a peace treaty with monsters was established almost instantly.

'...Perhaps what the North needs most isn't resources or talent, but negotiation skills to turn monsters that interfere with the war against demons into non-enemies.'

Irene's determination to win Oknodie's favor grew stronger.

"But don't let just anyone in, only people from our organization!"
"Tsu-tsu-tsu?"
"Don't kidnap and eat other people—make them infuse mana into the formula, then just suck out their mana and chase them away. That way, the formula will last longer, and humans won't exterminate you!"
"Tsu-tsu-tsu!"
"How to recognize our people? If they have black cloth tied around their forearm, they're our people!"

...Teaching them to use people as mana shuttles was a bit much, but it wouldn't affect their organization members—only other organizations' members or upperclassmen.
It was a wise decision that protected their material gathering location and prevented additional extermination by seniors, instructors, or professors due to human casualties.
From next year's 982nd batch of first-years, those who weren't selected by Oknodie to join the <Organization That Plays with Oknodie> would become mana shuttles without knowing why when they went out to gather materials, but that was the juniors' problem, not theirs.

"Want some iced tea?"
"I love cold things!"

The mutual attraction between Oknodie and Irene progressed smoothly along with the rescue of the lighting equipment.
