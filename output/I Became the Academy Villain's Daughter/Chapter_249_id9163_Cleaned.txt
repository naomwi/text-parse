# Graduating from the Academy by Luck - Chapter 249: Hey, Give Me Back My Ball

From ancient times, those who secure the high ground in battle are already halfway to victory.
This is especially true in dodgeball, where the rules dictate that you're out if you get hit by a ball and fail to recover it before it touches the ground or falls into someone else's hands.

"Class 981. There are many impressive juniors, living up to the reputation of being comparable to the greatest class in history."

The Four Heavenly Kings of the grade.
And this is someone who doesn't even rank among the top four.
Such strength that the absence of the Mesugakki Imperial Princess isn't even noticeable!

"But let's show them that our Class 980 is no pushover either!"

The White Paladin Ru.
When facing evil beings, he receives numerous combat bonuses, but against good beings like Irene, the Northern Duchess, he must compete purely on skill.
It's a battle where his strengths are stripped away while his opponent's are specialized.
If we're talking advantages and disadvantages, he's clearly at a disadvantage.
The compatibility doesn't match.
The terrain doesn't match.
The distance restrictions don't match.
A wizard versus a swordsman.
A court with lines that cannot be crossed.
But there was one peculiarity of the dodgeball that he liked.
The Blue Metal Ball with high mana conductivity.
The White Metal Ball with automatic shape restoration.
The Gray Metal Ball with tremendous weight.
Among these dodgeballs, each with impressive properties, the one that came into his possession was the Gray Metal Ball.
With its weight so heavy that even aspiring Knight Department students with inadequate training would struggle to lift it, generating proper speed with the strength of an ordinary Knight Department student is impossible.

<Justice Execution>
[The three divine aspects of the God of Good evaluate your request.]
[This battle is not a confrontation with the minions of evil.]
[This battle is not a step toward a good future.]
[This battle is a transcendental fight where the maxim of will does not violate the principle of universality.]
[The God of Good, Emmanuel, grants you strength.]

Transcendental.
The ability to recognize something innately, prior to experience.
Cognition that knows what is right without having to learn it.
The moment that sense connects to power.
The White Paladin Ru could tell.
That his ball could destroy a significant portion of the pillar even before throwing it.

CRASH!

Despite the destructive force that tore away more than half of the pillar, the ice pillar regenerated, emitting a fierce cold white light.
If this fight had any meaning, it was the clash of wills—the Duchess's will to not fall behind in the race for the lead, and the Paladin's will to not back down.
The will of the Duchess, who had been on battlefields from too young an age in the resource-poor North, was no less than that of Ru, who served three gods of good.
Conversely, Ru's will, steadfast enough in faith and heart to be noticed by three gods, could not overwhelm the Duchess's will.
As the pillar regenerated, an explosive offensive poured down from the top of the distant pillar.
The waves of Multi Ice Balls that had eliminated many second-year students froze the surface of Ru's holy barrier, robbing him of vision and eroding the ground beneath him, taking away his stability.

CRACK!

Eventually, the magic formula engraved on the Ice Ball activated as it burst on the ground, causing ice spikes to shoot up from beneath his feet inside the barrier!
Irene clearly demonstrated that her position as a military mage was not just a child Duchess's playful act to boost soldiers' morale.

"Good. I'm already excited about how much help that power would be in eradicating heretics and ma-in if it returns to the North. But not today."

The White Paladin Ru, who had also entered the Academy with a reputation no less impressive than the Northern Duchess.
The second-year students who knew his name couldn't hide their shock as Ru's momentum blazed even more fiercely.

"Is that the source of his reputation as the Organization Eradicator?"
"You know about Ru's power?"
"When one of the castles in the Southern Holy City-State Alliance was taken over by a drug operation using dark mana, there was a paladin who followed the trail found by a companion of the previous generation's hero and annihilated the organization. That White Paladin Ru accomplished such a feat at just 18 years old."

A drug organization that sold drugs mutated by the destructive properties of dark mana, turning humans into monsters.
On the day he destroyed the darkness that should never exist in the world, the lonely boy who walked the transcendental path with nothing but piety and everything to gain in justice earned his honor.
The title of White Paladin carried as much gratitude and expectation as there were citizens saved in the rescued city.

"Junior. I know you have people in the North you must protect."
"Knowing that, do you still intend to stand firm and not fall before me?"
"Stand firm? You're wrong. I am always moving forward. Toward the future that humanity should rightfully advance to, one step at a time."

Conviction leads to absolute self-affirmation.
Many people fail to gain conviction in themselves.
For economic reasons.
Due to lack of strength.
Because of racial limitations.
People rely on the power of faith to gain unrealistic strength while ignoring countless excuses.
Ru was different.
He had walked the path of faith simply because he realized that his path aligned with the path indicated by faith.

"Even if God does not acknowledge it, I have never once stepped into anything that I myself felt was wrong or unvirtuous."
"So I can be certain. This fight too is a step toward the future. There is nothing wrong with my path!"

The incoming Ice Balls were enveloped in Ru's immense holy power, trapped in a sphere of light that emitted a dazzling radiance.
Some burst in mid-air, reflecting particles of dazzling light in all directions like snowflakes, but the rest became massive restraining spheres that contained Irene's power.

"Come down. This is not a place you should be looking down from!"

With Ru's strike through the air, the light restraining spheres were unleashed all at once.
The spheres battered the pillar until they finally destroyed it, and beyond the released energy flowed diagonally, determining the path along which the destroyed upper part of the pillar would fall.
Waiting at the end was the White Paladin.
The owner of that title now emanated a weight and strength of a completely different dimension compared to when this confrontation began.

'He's on a different level. I can see why he's called one of the Four Heavenly Kings.'

Irene realized it.
This senior was truly strong.
Strong enough to make her think he was more powerful than all the advanced class students she had eliminated combined.
But he didn't know.
The weight of expectations carried by the name Northern Duchess.

"If we're talking about the weight of names, the name Northern Duchess is not light either."

Being compared in the first place was laughable.
Irene sneered as she stood on the tip of the falling ice pillar, gathering even more energy.

"!?"

She showed no fear of engaging in close combat against a paladin.
She didn't leap into the air, retreat backward, or employ magical techniques to endure.

"One hundred."

Because she knew.

"That was the number of Northern people who died every time I took one step back on the front line."

A hundred-man unit is the basic formation of the Northern Duchy.
Every time the front line wavered, one hundred-man unit was crushed and disappeared.
When she advanced, a hundred lives were saved; when she retreated, a hundred died.
She felt that immense pressure on every battlefield, always.
Sometimes she retreated, and there were even experiences of uncontrollable defeat.
Recalling the moment of great defeat when thousands of soldiers died due to her inadequacy was truly close to terror.
The moment when her heart broke and was crushed.
Even from such despair, she rose again.
There was no room for the power of faith.
The voices of those who remained.
The cries of those who wanted to survive.
Because there were lives she was responsible for.

"If moving forward is so good, then by all means, take that step. After you overcome this strike!"

Type - Ice Magic
Activation - Ice Flower
Specialization - Ice Sphere, Stacking, Single Point Condensation, Massive Explosion
Penalty - Freezing

An ultimate strike drawn out even at the cost of a penalty that would leave parts of her body frozen for a week, making even movement difficult until the mana-induced thawing was complete.
The massive ice pillar compressed and compressed again, and by the time it reached the end, it was all absorbed into a single Ice Ball.
The instructors, tense and ready to rush onto the court at any moment, knew this was a force that should not be unleashed—one that could easily kill a person and more.

'This is the moment of conviction.'

Ru realized.
If he could cut through that, he could take one more step forward.

'This is the moment of growth.'

Irene realized.
If she could freeze Ru's strike, her bitter cold would ensure that no one would dare take a step beyond her.

Close combat.
Collision.
In that moment when realizations crossed—

Swoosh

Oknodie, standing stupidly in the path of the charge, made an expression with an exclamation mark above her head.

'Why isn't she running away?'
'Is she planning to be cut down with me?'

Irene and Ru were bewildered.
Oknodie, who had walked right into the middle of the gap between the two of them, quickly reached out her hand in front of Irene in a split second.

Thump!

"!?"

Oknodie snatched the ball.
She said:

"I'll just borrow this for a moment!"

Borrow? My ultimate strike?
The deadly technique <Ice Flower> containing enormous power that no one but herself could possibly stabilize?
Stop it.
It's going to explode.
The explosion will start in your hands and ice spikes will burst out and consume your entire body.
Despite Irene's shocked gaze, Oknodie simply threw the ball.
Toward the infinite repetition zone where three balls were accelerating infinitely through the same section.

"I'll return it once I get that one out!"

Wait, you're using it to retrieve a ball...?
Then what about me?
Ignoring Irene's gaze full of indignation, Ru struck the approaching Irene with his scabbard instead of his sword.

"Ungh!"

Irene, clutching her abdomen and groaning, collapsed with a thud.
As she was transported outside the court, she was filled with indignation greater than pain.
