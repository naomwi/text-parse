# Enhanced Translation: Chapter 412 - That Mere Resolve 2

The third disaster pressured everyone with a difficulty level greater than the previous two disasters combined.

"The high-concentration mana vapor, worse than a mana storm, makes it impossible to see ahead even with Search Eye. This is going to be extremely dangerous."

"Without Irene's domain expansion, we'd immediately suffer burns. Ugh. We can't advance freely like this."

"We can endure somewhat with our power. It's just burdensome because the consumption rate is enormous."

In an environment with such limited visibility, they had no choice but to rely heavily on Intuition, which mobilizes all other senses.

In that respect, Irene, Dorothy, and Rockfell were fortunate.
While Irene's ice attribute power blocked the heat of the lava zone, Dorothy used her exceptional intuition to find the path.
They had formed a perfectly balanced party that grew stronger together, with properly distributed roles.

*Hisssss!*

"Ah, the vapor...!"

When steam suddenly erupted from a gap and threatened Irene's domain, Rockfell's sword extended like lightning, instantly slicing the ground to redirect the vapor flow in the opposite direction.

"I'll need to change my sword once we get out of here. Even with mana coating, the concentration of mana in the vapor is alarming—it's damaging my blade."

Through thorough division of labor, they helped each other survive.
If it weren't for this team, they might not have even attempted to venture beyond this point, even if they had made it this far.

"I wonder how far Zuang has gotten?"
"Who knows."

Irene didn't voice any hopeful outlook.
She had witnessed countless tragedies on the battlefield.
All they could do was hope they weren't too late.

"Dorothy. Speed up. The temperature is rising, and I can't maintain the domain for as long."
"If we rush, we might step into lava."
"...Alright. Just keep going as you are."

What if they had entered this place as second-year students?
Taking silent steps.
The sound of flowing lava.
Vision obscured by vapor.
As the three advanced step by step through the harsh environment, they imagined to themselves:
If they had just a bit more mana, would things be easier?
If they had improved their attribute affinity or resistance, surviving in extreme environments would be easier too.
Oknodie would probably be flying through this place.
Zuang would have received various training and would be confident enough to enter such a place alone.

'Frustrating.'

Though their thought processes differed, they all arrived at the same emotion.
Clearly, the gap hadn't been this wide until they entered the academy.
If anything, Oknodie had seemed somewhat weak compared to her rank as the top student of their year.
Now she was running ahead with an overwhelming advantage, and they were struggling just to keep up.
The professor asked them to search for Oknodie out of concern for her safety, but honestly, they wondered who would be helping whom.
If they had known it would be this dangerous, they wouldn't have rushed here in the first place.
Though no one said it aloud, by this point, they were more worried about Zuang, who was chasing after Oknodie, than about Oknodie herself.

'Oknodie will survive somehow, but what if Zuang gets into serious trouble just from chasing her?'

If they were too late, they might actually witness such a scenario.

"I need to use one big spell."
"One big spell?"
"I'll momentarily expand my domain's range. When the vapor is pushed away, don't miss the sight of the path we need to take."
"!!"
"Can you memorize it, Dorothy?"
"I absolutely must. A weak human who can't seize an opportunity when it comes won't survive as a forest dweller, let alone as a forest keeper!"

No matter how chaotic the terrain, it would be easier to memorize than Professor Weird's blackboard notes!

"Here I go."

Irene released mana into a semicircular domain, as if unleashing a sword strike beyond the hemispherical barrier protecting everyone.

<Ice Wave>

The 2nd-tier atmospheric magic Shock Wave.
She changed its attribute to ice, set its range to semicircular, and forcefully pushed away the heavy mana vapor permeating the entire space.
Beyond the sensation of drawing out mana came a feeling of weight, like heavy objects colliding with each other.

'This is tough. But... compared to the pressure of that mysterious giant monster, this is nothing.'

Having experienced it before, she had countermeasures to consider.
Turning all the high-purity mana against itself would require enduring fatigue so severe it would be cruel.
So she exploited it instead.
She deceived part of the mana.
I'm using my power to help you, she told it.

<Steam Explosion>

By mixing the vapor-filled air with Ice Wave and using the harmonized mana, she instantly detonated the high-purity mana within the vapor.
The energy spread at a speed exceeding the pressure of exploding steam, clearing the view cleanly beyond a radius of 200 meters, as if sweeping away clouds.
Within that cleared area, there was clearly a "safe zone."
If they could avoid the maze-like lava pits and flowing lava, they would find ground where they could catch their breath.

'Now I understand why there's a safe zone.'

A safe zone appearing in such an urgent situation was more desperate than an oasis in a desert.
As the vapor billowed forth again, obscuring their vision, the safe zone disappeared like a mirage.
But Dorothy, with hope in her eyes, led everyone forward with absolute distance calculations beyond mere intuition, stepping only on safe ground.
Without a single mistake or misstep.

*Hisss!*

Rockfell, who deflected erupting volcanic projectiles with his sword, also responded to all the hazards he had memorized while seeing the same view as Dorothy.
When they finally reached the entrance to the safe zone, they wondered:

"Did Zuang pass through here?"
"I don't know. I can't find any more traces."
"Let's catch our breath. Dorothy's intuition needs rest desperately."
"Then let me clear the view one more time before we decide. The exit might be unexpectedly close."
"Ugh. I hope so."

With her face drenched in sweat like melting ice, suggesting she had used considerable power, Irene raised one hand to cast a spell.
Zuang, who had poked her head out from a window in the safe zone's dwelling, offered advice with an indifferent expression.

"You'd better not use that magic anymore. The lava golem wandering in there might get seriously angry and come charging at us."
"Really? Thanks for the advice."

Irene, who had been casually conversing while preparing to cast her spell, abandoned her magic and began considering other options.
When she realized who she had just spoken with, she quickly turned toward the window.
Zuang, who had appeared as nonchalantly as her sudden appearance, brazenly closed the window.

"...Huh?"

Why is she coming out from here?


* * *


"I gave up. If you go further from here, there are not only lava golems but also plenty of monsters attracted by natural mana, including high-tier species that would burn to death even if I brought Logini."

Zuang had abandoned the attempt to overcome the mana disaster early on.

"Weren't you chasing after Oknodie because you were worried about her?"
"Do you want to go in then?"

Everyone fell silent like mutes with honey in their mouths at Zuang's question as she pointed beyond the safe zone.

"Stealth doesn't work there. The monsters' level is too high to overpower with skill. I was completely outmatched. At least with my current abilities, I couldn't pass through."

Though Oknodie, an expert in all kinds of cries, might have passed through with some strange cry rivaling "Muoooong."
Thinking it would be a funny sight even just to imagine, Zuang giggled.

"Haah. What a letdown."
"I know. We thought we had to go all the way to the end to rescue Zuang, but now that we've found her so easily, I feel drained."
"Should you have hidden somewhere more difficult?"
"Give me a break. When Zuang decides to hide, even my 'intuition' can't find her!"

Unlike Irene and Dorothy, who were sighing with relief, Rockfell, who was usually quiet and simply protected Dorothy from behind, spoke up.

"Did Oknodie break through that area?"
"I didn't see her."
"Then she must have passed through."

They felt even more deflated by the fact that Oknodie had disappeared alone somewhere far beyond, to a place they couldn't even imagine, than they had when they achieved their goal of rescuing Zuang.
The gap between them was now confirmed to be so wide that they weren't even allowed to catch a glimpse of Oknodie's back.

"Should we go back...? She might pop up out of nowhere like a stray cat after a day, looking for food first."
"The senior doesn't agree with that opinion."
"Which senior?"
"The senior who allowed entry to the safe zone."

[Green Zone]
[Additional Entry Condition: Anyone may enter]

Compared to the initial safe zones that wielded entry conditions like weapons to exploit others, forcing them to help with conquering the area, the lava zone's safe zone was incredibly generous.
Everyone, who had naturally assumed Zuang had shown them kindness, was shocked to learn that there was such a conscientious senior.

"I thought all students on leave were no different from demons who had lost their humanity?"
"I thought they would consider their peers' lives more worthless than flies if it meant earning points!"
"Honestly, I can't believe it. That such a conscientious senior still exists."

Even second-year students were full of nasty seniors like Bellocasio, the contract scammer.

"Haha. That's because they're pathetic fellows who can't survive without sucking each other's lifeblood. Strong ones like me don't necessarily reject those who can rise up."

The senior who emerged from inside the safe zone was a boy who looked remarkably fragile.

"Is he really a senior...?"
"Wow. Even I think I could knock this senior down with one punch."
"Don't be rude, Dorothy. You should have learned enough in the monster forest not to judge strength by appearance."

Tension crept into Dorothy's previously relaxed face.
The fact that the senior appeared weak was actually bizarre.
Even they had only made it this far by combining their strength and wisdom.
Yet this senior was alone in a safe zone in the middle of the lava zone.
It was as suspicious as finding a child wandering happily through the middle of the demon realm—you'd have to wonder if it was Oknodie.

"Senior, what are you doing here?"
"Affinity work."
"Pardon?"
"There's an enormous amount of heat here, right? Such a fire-attribute friendly environment is hard to find unless you're near a dormant volcano where magic tower mages reside. So I'm sitting here raising my fire attribute affinity and resistance as much as possible."
"For how long?"
"Thirteen years now, I think?"
"...What? No matter how you look at it, that's an impossibly youthful appearance!"

To have spent thirteen years here with a boyish appearance that could pass for thirteen years old!

"Usually, in student-on-leave exclusive zones, there's always someone unusual like me hiding. You'd better be careful."

Dorothy and Irene exchanged glances.
It was evidence that they had the same thought.

"Senior. Could you perhaps offer a small favor to your juniors?"
"Tell me."
"A classmate of ours named Oknodie passed through here, but we failed to track her with our abilities. We'd like your help finding her."

Before the senior could respond to Irene's request, Zuang shook her head.

"It's impossible. I already asked him first."
"He refused?"
"Not exactly. He said he doesn't particularly care."
"He doesn't care?"
"It's a small favor, so you only need to pay a small price."

The senior on leave held up one finger.

"Just give me one mana heart."
"What?"
"The thing inside your body. The mana organ created while mastering mana cultivation methods is called a mana heart."

Zuang looked at them with an expression asking if they would still accept help under these conditions.
Irene and Dorothy physically understood the hair-raising terror.
This senior hadn't left the entry conditions open for nothing.
He had left the door wide open for prey to walk into the safe zone of their own accord.
