# 307 - Time for Play

In the chairman's office of the Wiheomhae Foundation.
A wine tasting was in full swing at what would undoubtedly rank among the top places on anyone's list of residences housing the world's most dangerous individuals.

"The Wine of Wailing, presented by Monthly Gourmet as thanks for our donation. With each sip, the screams of souls swirl around my mouth—not an unpleasant experience."
"...I'm glad you're satisfied."
"Would you care for a taste?"
"I'm abstaining from alcohol, but I appreciate the offer."
"Oh, what a shame. Opportunities to taste such special wines and expand one's collection are quite rare."

Those who possess both wealth and power inevitably discover methods to grow stronger in this world.
Item collection.
Arms dealers collect weapons, monster hunters collect trophies from their kills, and art dealers collect artwork.
The chairman of the Wiheomhae Foundation possessed wealth and power on an entirely different scale from ordinary collectors.
His collection interests weren't limited to a single field but expanded across various domains with diverse collections.

"Acquiring existing items is simple, but beyond a certain level, one begins seeking new collectibles that have never existed before. In that regard, this wine holds considerable value."

As he swirled the liquid in his wine glass, small skull-shaped souls appeared and disappeared like smudges on a window.

"A wine of at least 5th-tier crafting difficulty, requiring exceptional torture techniques, kidnapping skills, soul extraction, and winemaking expertise. It's an atrocity that could only be glossed over with power. One might call it the Wine of Authority."

The chief secretary felt like he was losing his mind.
The screams and wails emanating from the wine glass that the chairman sipped with a smile were enough to chill one's blood.
What kind of mind could drink such a thing without batting an eye?
The chairman wasn't right in the head from the start, having willingly funded Monthly Gourmet's production of such an ominous wine.

"So, how is our spirited daughter faring with Papa's trial?"
"I just received a report from the cruise ship. The vessel's destination has been changed to the mansion on Quelarnia Islands, and there was a bloody incident involving an Apostle of Anlage."
"Did Jonna survive?"

The chief secretary couldn't immediately grasp the intent behind the question.
Why would he ask this?
The chairman savored the secretary's hesitation with the same gentle smile he wore while enjoying the Wine of Wailing.
The secretary found it unsettling.
He pondered why the chairman had prioritized Jonna's survival status.
Why was Jonna's survival more important than the fact that half the crew had died?
Why was Jonna prioritized over Oknodie, whom he had invited?
It was because there was a significant difference between Jonna's survival and death.
What was the difference?
Jonna had already experienced the young lady's death several times.
He had even dared to oppose the Foundation to save her.
If Jonna were alive, he wouldn't stand by while Oknodie was harmed.
Conversely, if he were dead, Oknodie's safety couldn't be guaranteed.

"Jonna, the two tails, the young lady, and all her friends have been confirmed alive."

Only after speaking did the chief secretary realize the implications.
The Apostle of Anlage serves the God of Corruption.
To this evil deity who forcibly awakens people's innate potential—Anlage—existing professions, routines, and efforts are irrelevant.
Their mission is to awaken new aptitudes that maximize a person's value and force them to pursue these new paths.
The Apostle's duty is to make people transform their lives to follow their true potential.

'An Apostle who has obtained a sacred artifact goes further, arbitrarily changing the owners of bodies with potential.'

They reassign superior bodies and aptitudes to superior souls.
The exchange of physical vessels and spiritual souls is permitted only because the god oversees it.
Jonna Wiheomhae is not a man soft enough to allow a demon contractor to take his young lady's place.

'With just one piece of information, he could examine the most significant conflict and its outcome.'

The chairman had been aware of all these causal relationships and clashes of desire from the beginning.
He was the mastermind who designed this scenario.
This made him even more frightening.
Did this man have not even a shred of affection for the child who called him Papa?
He had summoned Oknodie to a cruise ship where she might clash with such a dangerous entity.

"My, you seem to think I'm being too harsh."
"I apologize for my presumption."
"Haha. It's fine. Apologies between us are unnecessary. Aren't we in a relationship where we've each tried to take the other's life once?"

They don't blame each other because they know there's no trust.
This straightforward relationship terrified the secretary even more.
A relationship without trust, dependence, or regret can end and be severed at any time.
There were no constraints in this relationship.
He wasn't even receiving a salary.
No loyalty was demanded.
The chief secretary wanted only one thing from the chairman:
To obtain an opportunity for revenge.
The chairman also wanted only one thing from him:
To observe the humiliating spectacle of a youngster trying to kill him while dancing in the palm of his hand.
The secretary's very existence was merely an extension of the chairman's amusement.
The chairman's madness in exploiting even those who threatened his life didn't exclude the scholarship student Oknodie.

"The Wine of Suffering typically matures slowly over a long period. My taste isn't for short, stoic spirits, so you may rest assured."

...Is that supposed to be reassuring?
Considering his usual character, it probably is.
Of course, it's not reassuring at all.
How can one respond with "Thank you for slowly making me anxious" to someone whose preference is to inflict pain gradually to ripen screams?
The chief secretary, uncomfortable with the chairman's friendly smile directed at him, deliberately changed the subject.

"Did you believe Oknodie would be safe from the Apostle of Anlage?"
"I wonder."

His smiling face was clearly that of a gambler placing chips on words without expecting any particular outcome.

'Is this for real...'

As always, the chairman closed his eyes and savored the outcome with a somewhat shy, slightly proud, inscrutable smile, leaving the secretary dumbfounded.

"Perhaps I would have found some satisfaction if the child who fearlessly calls me Papa had been broken by the Apostle of Anlage. An Apostle who surpasses their limits and proves their worth as a new toy might qualify to be recognized as our new daughter."
"Is that what you expected?"
"Of course not. I hoped more that a new toy would last longer than recycling one that had served its purpose. Oknodie has performed beyond my expectations."
"Given Jonna's temperament, the possibility of defeating the Apostle of Anlage was slim, so Miss Oknodie must have done something remarkable."
"Remarkable? Heh. You still don't understand me as a person. While your obtuseness is somewhat endearing, is it appropriate for a chief secretary to be so dense?"

Wrong answer.
This wasn't what the man had been looking for.
The chief secretary reluctantly considered a possibility he really didn't want to contemplate.

"Could it be... you were interested in her ruthlessness?"

The chairman's smile deepened.

"A strategy that wouldn't care if a thousand crew members died as long as her friends were safe. I don't dislike a smart child who knows how to distinguish between human resources and friends. Thanks to her, the Apostle of Anlage was defeated."
"But in exchange, she's earned the enmity of a god by defeating their apostle. The God of Corruption won't take this lightly."
"Haha. A divine toy with talents beloved by all 24 deities, now hated? That's quite an exaggeration."
"...Do you regard Oknodie's talent that highly?"
"That child can become anything she sets her mind to. She surpasses the hero in popularity and exceeds the Northern Grand Duchess in severity. Her charisma surpasses the heir of the Western Noble Family. She's like a Joker that can be used anywhere in a deck of cards."
"...What do you want to make of that child?"
"Isn't that obvious?"

Nothing is as tedious as a question with a predetermined answer.
The chairman's face momentarily clouded with boredom.
A mistake.
A being who lives solely for interest.
The disasters that unfold to alleviate his boredom are no less terrible than the Dragon Headmaster's "Play."
Seeing the chief secretary tense up at his mistake, the chairman smirked.

"Parents naturally want their children to resemble them."

The chairman infused mana into the report of Oknodie's activities with a tap of his finger.
Following his gesture, a paper boat folded into the shape of a cruise ship plopped into the wine glass.

"Expensive screams that can be extracted with enough power may be rare but not unique. That's the difference between being Rare and Unique."

"Only those who know how to twist the world for their amusement and enjoy it approach uniqueness."

New items.
New collections.
The chairman's eyes, hungry for new amusements, followed the cruise ship's path as it appeared on the world map.

"I should reward my unique daughter who successfully completed Papa's directive. I can't welcome her and the friends she's brought to an empty mansion."
"I'll prepare a gate to Mansion No. 3 on Quelarnia Islands within three minutes."

The Foundation's chairman.
The meeting between the world's most dangerous man and the world's most dangerous child was drawing near.
