# Chapter 125 - Ominous Information

There are differences even among third-years.
The strong ones are truly strong, while the weak ones are so weak you'd wonder, "This is a third-year?"
The one who got shot by Jigoku's gun and fainted was among the weak ones.
In fact, it's only natural when you think about it.
How strong could students who earn points by taking daily patrol quests really be?
The true monsters spend that time participating in missions with much higher points or benefits at stake.

'Must be at Mob's level as a third-year!'

Objectively speaking, that senior was exactly at that level.

"A first-year beat a third-year?"
"Must be from the advanced class."
"No wonder they weren't intimidated when selling drugs."

The third-years muttered quietly, but thanks to my experience points in the "Hearing" ability, I could hear their conversation.
A moment later, Icarus, the squadron leader, flew over slowly, showing he had no intention of causing harm.

"Sorry about that, juniors. Our squadron member seems to have suffered from the heat."
"Don't come any closer. If you attack again, I'll report this to the student council!"
"Haha. It's nothing like that. I just came to retrieve that pathetically unconscious friend of mine, so cut us some slack."

Icarus had his other members retrieve the squadron member who had crashed into the tree and passed out.

"That blood stone—you'd better be careful with it. Anyone who knows its value will definitely covet it."
"I'll handle it myself!"

Seeing my vigilant attitude, Icarus shut his mouth like a civil servant afraid of getting a complaint education and left.

"How did you do that? Getting guys who only stop when shot to leave after just a few words."
"I borrowed the power of authority!"
"Authority?"

In Gift Academy, the student council's power is no less formidable than a royal decree from a kingdom.
For example, there was an incident where a princess violated school rules and was dragged to the student council.
When the king sent a lengthy threatening letter saying he would ruin the life of anyone who touched his daughter, the student council member reportedly responded, "Whatever, I can just emigrate~"
That senior is currently a third-year student of the 979th class and still serves on the student council.

'Compared to such seniors, those who just patrol are nothing!'

I could see admiration in Jigoku's eyes.

"You would've handled it fine on your own, right?"
"No way. Thanks to Jigoku using expensive elemental bullets, things were resolved easily! If they thought we were pushovers, they would've taken it by force and ignored any threats about reporting to the student council."
"Is that so? Hmm. Well, I'm glad they were reasonable."

Jigoku lowered his head and whispered in a subtle tone.

"Does this settle my debt from before?"
"You mean the magical beast storage incident?"

When I nodded to Jigoku, who was anxiously watching my reaction, his tense face brightened up.

"Oknodie, what happened? What did you say up there that made the seniors just leave?"

When I explained the situation to Hestia and everyone else who had arrived late, Rogini expressed concern.

"What if it's really dangerous?"
"Hmph. That won't happen."
"How can you be so sure?"
"I know this better than anyone in the world!"

Energy-containing materials are rare even within the Academy.
Blood stones are materials used for blood magic.
They're a new energy source discovered when someone thought of using the bio-mana contained in the blood of living creatures for magicians with insufficient mana pools.
If people are already illegally cultivating and consuming mana spore sacs, there's no reason they can't use blood stones too.
Moreover, unlike the widely recognized valuable mana spore sacs, blood stones are currently undervalued because people think such old-fashioned technology is outdated.

'An item that's easy to obtain but not valued properly. In other words, something that could hit the jackpot!'

Among the Academy's visiting professors, a blood magician will soon be added.
Perhaps the upper-class students have already started receiving education from the blood magician.
When that time comes, blood magic will become trendy as people with mana deficiency start drawing blood for magic, and the value of blood stones will skyrocket!

'Of course, such dangerous magic will be followed by safety accidents and stricter regulations.'

After a short while, blood magic will be heavily regulated with usage restrictions and qualification requirements to prevent accidents.
By then, the value of blood stones will plummet again.
But as someone with insider knowledge, I just need to sell before that happens. Even if I can't sell at the peak price, it should be enough to get a regular carriage pass when the price rises reasonably.


* * *


Rogini is an aspiring magic department student.
She attends lectures not only from Professor Weird, a master of nature magic, but also from several other professors.
As a promising junior, some senior teaching assistants viewed Rogini favorably, occasionally chatting with her and sharing various information.

-You're taking Professor Weird's lectures, right? You should be careful.
-Why?
-I heard that professor is testing whether feeding stones to fourth-years increases their nature affinity.

Hearing this rumor, she suddenly understood the strange rumors about Oknodie as well.

'So Oknodie isn't eating stones because she's lacking something, but to increase her nature affinity!'

Surely she's not just eating them raw.
Even in the Magic Tower, when odd research topics came up, they would approach them with surprisingly practical methods.
There must be magic to digest stones, special drugs or constitutions to adjust stomach acid levels, or arcane techniques.
Would they really just crudely feed stones without any of that?
To world-class Academy professors and fourth-year students?

'No way.'

They couldn't be that ignorant.
Common sense tells us humans can't digest stones.
Obviously, she had no intention of trying it herself.
But she did gain an insight.
Oknodie is truly knowledgeable about stones.
Especially since that child loves eating.
In such circumstances, how much more would she know and love edible stones!
Moreover, the item Oknodie obtained this time is a blood "stone."
It was something she wanted so badly that she recruited daily party members by offering the substantial sum of 50 points per person—enough to cover ten meals.

Whether she eats it or uses it for research, that's her business!

"The mosquitoes were pretty scary."
"...Do you realize my ears are still ringing from your screaming next to me?"
"What could I do? I was really scared! I've never seen mosquitoes that big in my life."

While the cowardly Zealot had an excuse, the sharp-tongued Echo got her points for free.

"How could you just stand there without lifting a finger?"
"If a boss hires the wrong employee and lets them leech salary, that's the boss's fault, not the employee's."
"If I had paid with my own money, I would have demanded you return every point. Oknodie is really too nice."

Echo, who had gotten 50 points for nothing, stuck out her tongue mockingly before leaving.
Anyway, since she had earned points, Rogini could also return in a good mood.


* * *


"What are Oknodie and Hestia talking about?"
"They said they're going to look for someone to buy the blood stone and test the waters."

Nise, a nun of the Thunderbolt Holy Divine Church who had accepted honey candy instead of catnip as payment after intense wage negotiations, answered while sucking on her honey candy.

"It's truly amazing. They actually executed such a reckless plan successfully, and even after encountering third-years, they returned safely without any casualties."
"Third-years are seniors, not monsters. Why would you think there would be casualties from fighting third-years, when we weren't even fighting mosquitoes?"
"Oknodie's demeanor seemed strange."

Arcadia recalled.
Oknodie, who was no longer the good child she once knew.
The rumors about Oknodie that kept surfacing after her good-child facade had been stripped away.
Even if Oknodie wasn't the ruthless assassin without blood or tears as rumors suggested, if she wasn't the good child Arcadia had known, she might possess some assassin-like qualities.
In fact, what Arcadia had worried about was Oknodie killing a senior.

"Have you heard about the Wiheomhae Foundation?"
"I know it as a scholarship foundation that nurtures talented individuals from countries around the world."
"Recently, there's a rumor going around that Oknodie might have been threatened to become a prostitute if she refused to be trained as an assassin by the foundation."

Students who attended the Eye-Training lecture had promised to keep it confidential, but Arcadia, with her keen sense for rumors, had uncovered the secret from those students.

"I was worried that Oknodie, having grown up in such a harsh foundation, might endanger her classmates or even kill a senior."

Nise stopped savoring her honey candy at this unexpectedly heavy topic.

"Are you afraid of that child, or are you concerned for her?"
"Both. I'm worried and afraid that she might walk down the wrong path."
"Why do you care so much about Oknodie?"

Nise's question was valid.

"There are many foundation scholarship students, children who must choose between becoming prostitutes or assassins, and children with tragic fates—not just Oknodie. What makes her special in your eyes?"
"At first, I liked her because she was cute, but as time passed, I saw her kindness."

Despite knowing the reality of the weak Firenze Kingdom, lacking in both cultural and military power, Oknodie spoke well of the kingdom and showed consideration.
She protected weak and oppressed students and wasn't afraid to stand against injustice.

Princess Arcadia of the Firenze Kingdom.
Hestia the Berserker, whom everyone avoided.
Mob the inferior student, whom no one paid attention to.

From the rumors she heard.
From her own experiences.
Oknodie possessed a pure heart that made it hard to believe she had experienced an unfortunate upbringing.

"Of course, purity isn't eternal. Her heart will gradually be tainted and chipped away by the foundation's manipulation, losing its true form."
"So you want to protect her. That child."
"Yes."

Nise could understand Arcadia's sincere confession.

"Do you understand?"
"I understand."

Nise nodded and replied.

"That the princess will soon suffer a great wound."
"...What?"
"I have information I bought with points from an instructor."

Nise said.

"Starting next week, each grade's academy evaluation scores and overall rankings will be posted."
"Scores...? What does that have to do with me or Oknodie?"
"I've received information that around this time every year, inferior students from the Wiheomhae Foundation cause incidents."

Among children from the same foundation as Oknodie, the most lagging ones cause trouble.

"It will be a turning point for both Oknodie, who is from the same foundation, and for Princess Arcadia who cares for her—a point where your academy life so far will change."
