# 205 - People Who Fear Tomorrow

Before Oknodie's finish line switcheroo could unfold, the first-years who had already successfully crossed the finish line pieced together the situation by eavesdropping on the upperclassmen's commentary from the "Jeneger's Tactics" lecture.

"Oknodie. She's truly terrifying."
"I know. Who would have thought she'd switch the finish line? We almost ended up in last place."

She had even made a contract beforehand.
That she wouldn't attempt the riding section until 100 students had passed through.
In exchange, the hero should proceed to cross the finish line and not return to cause trouble.

Other students could approach Oknodie.
But these two couldn't.
They were bound by the contract.

Suffering such a penalty from a terrifying child who concealed talent and skill rivaling a hero would undoubtedly lead to major regrets, whether in the near future or distant future.

"Did she target us from the beginning?"
"If she had switched it right away, we would have been completely helpless, but the fact that she waited suggests it was meant as a warning."

"Don't mess with her. That whenever she wants, she has methods to single out anyone she dislikes in the exam and make them finish last."
"...You're right. She's truly frightening."

The two were thoroughly intimidated as they watched the transmission crystal ball.

Criminal backgrounds.
Offender backgrounds.
Non-mainstream school backgrounds.
The pool of potential party members becomes severely limited.
Not to mention the various problems that come with rubbing shoulders with such people in the same party.

"...Let's stop openly antagonizing Oknodie."
"You want to dance to that child's tune?"
"If we can't end it in one go, the collateral damage is too severe."

Physically, they could kill Oknodie if they acted when professors weren't watching, but even while professors were watching, Oknodie could socially destroy them.

"Eventually, Oknodie will have to leave the Academy. That will be our opportunity."

Xing, who beats up passing students to make them find his mount, and Zuang, who returns blood-stained name tags with gratitude while instilling fear.

They needed a sage.
