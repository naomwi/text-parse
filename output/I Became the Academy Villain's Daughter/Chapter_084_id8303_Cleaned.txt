# 84 - The Difference Between Real and Fake

Hiding under the principal to escape the pursuing students was a good idea, but now I felt an even more enormous gaze upon me.

"You impudent little brat from the Wiheomhae Foundation. What are you doing under my nostrils?"

"Could I just hide a little longer...?"

"I won't stop you if you wish to be crisply roasted when I breathe fire from my nose while sneezing. You'd make a perfect snack before dinner."

Tsk. Seems like the principal's willingness to turn a blind eye only lasts about a minute.
I understand though.
It would be annoying if students kept running to hide around your face.
I reluctantly crawled out from beneath the principal's nostrils.
...But in that short time, the situation had changed dramatically.

"Oknodie is gone!"
"That's because you were lazy!"
"We were following Mesugakki Princess's orders! Are you insulting Her Highness by calling her lazy?"
"You're not the only ones with a princess! We have Princess Yaoi too!"
"Ha. Using a fallen princess who's lower in the succession order as a shield? You should be ashamed."
"Are you done talking?"
"Yes, what are you going to do about it?"
"You're all dead today. I'll fill your baskets with nothing but fake flags!"

The students who had lost their target began fighting among themselves, and the situation quickly descended into chaos.
Well, um.
Not my fault!
I stripped the jacket off a collapsed student nearby and slipped inside it.

'Whew, easy win.'

Just as I was planning to wait it out until the full 30 minutes had passed, someone picked up the jacket.
Oh no, I've been caught!

"Oknodie is here...!"

It was Zuang, trying to call the other students over in a loud voice.
I quickly stuffed a flag into her mouth.

"?!"
"I'll give you two more if you don't tell on me!"

Zuang narrowed her eyes like a flounder and held up three fingers.

"Fine, three!"

After putting four flags in her mouth and covering me back up with the jacket as if she was about to leave, Zuang suddenly stuck her head inside the jacket.

"Was what you said last time true? Have you really never taken the adventurer's night action lecture?"

There was no escaping this situation.
Inside the cramped jacket.
Eye to eye, close enough to feel her breath on my face, with my wrist firmly in her grip.
Pupils, breathing, pulse.
Zuang was using every detection method available, determined to catch any lie.
Assassins are skilled at detecting lies because they need to read their target's psychology and set up ambushes or create situations.

"Actually, that was a lie."
"I thought something seemed off."

I gave up on trying to deceive her.
Zuang made no attempt to hide her resentment.

"Why did you lie?"
"I didn't want Hestia to find out I was taking night lectures."
"Why?"
"I can't tell you the reason."
"You don't want her to hate you? You want to be friends with that gorilla-like muscular woman?"

Nod, nod.
I carefully nodded my head so the jacket wouldn't move from the outside, and a mischievous look appeared on Zuang's face.

"First Son O-cheon, now Hestia... Oknodie likes muscular people regardless of gender, huh?"
"That's not true at all!"
"Strong denial is just confirmation!"
"Yes, that's right."
"See? You admitted it!"

Are you a child, seriously?
I glared at her in annoyance, and Zuang giggled.

"Alright. I'll keep it a secret."
"Really?"
"You showed me something good at dawn, after all."

Since she's fundamentally an assassin with malicious tendencies, it's rare for Zuang to show kindness.
If I hadn't shown her the double-sided tape room, I probably would have failed to silence her, right?

"Are you going to keep hiding?"
"I'm confident in my hiding skills!"
"True. I was trying to hide in the jacket too because I was tired, but I didn't expect someone to be hiding here already!"

I think I know why~.
Excluding me, the shortest person in the Academy, and Princess Mesugakki who's done growing, Zuang is the next shortest person!
Given our similar height and build, it makes sense that we'd pick the same hiding spot.

"How's it outside? Has the hero's team lost many flags?"
"Not at all?"
"Then what is the hero's team doing?"
"Nothing."
"Are they just watching?"
"Yes."

That's not good.
They wouldn't show such composure unless they were quite confident.
It seems my hunch that all of the hero team's flags might be real has hit the mark.

"Then I need to shake things up."

I didn't want to be this underhanded against newbies who only live once, but there's nothing more embarrassing than losing first place because you went easy on someone!


* * *


The hero felt disappointed.
He had been willing to help Oknodie if she had asked for his assistance.
But she ran away without looking back and went into hiding, giving him no chance to help.

"That person really fights well."
"Yeah. Just looking at her strength alone, I'd want to recruit her as a warrior."

Compared to Jigokuna who was frantically running away after running out of bullets, or Lotto who was being dragged around by her hair, Hestia was putting up a surprisingly good fight.

Bang! Bang!

She rammed her shoulders with all her might and gripped her opponent's hands tightly with both of hers. In this fierce test of strength, the one who gave ground was Leape Life.

Instead of making the clichéd exclamation like 'This brutish gorilla woman, such strength without mana cultivation!', Leape activated her mana cultivation technique.

Pssshhh!
Sizzzzle-

Hot steam erupted from Leape's armor, and the temperature of the armor where they were gripping each other rose frighteningly.

"My iron plate armor and gauntlets start at 50 degrees Celsius and can go up to 150 degrees. If you keep holding on, those large hands of yours will be roasted red, you know?"
"What about your hands?"
"They're protected by my mana cultivation technique."
"That's cowardly."
"Why don't you just give up?"
"I don't care."

Even among apprentice knights her age, it was rare to find an opponent with such endurance.

"Don't be stubborn. Your main weapons were two-handed, right? If you lose both hands, how will you fight? With that disgustingly oversized body of yours?"
"That's not your concern."
"If it were me, I'd definitely abandon a warrior who can't hold a weapon. Charcoal that has burned out its embers and served its purpose is nothing but trash. In this state, Oknodie will abandon you, you know?"

Hestia snorted.

"Despite all the rumors surrounding me, Oknodie has never once shown any dislike. That honest and kind child trusted me and had me face you."
"150 degrees Celsius? The power of the Empire's three great meritorious families? I'm not curious, and I don't want to know."
"There's only one thing I'm curious about and want to know."

Her muscles swelled enormously.

"That's the sound of your screams!!!"

Her two fists squeezed with all their might, ignoring the intense heat.

"?!?!"

Crack! Crunch!

The steel gauntlets shattered with a crack, and the hand bones crumbled with a crunch.
If she pushed just a little more stubbornly, both hands could be completely crushed.

<Empire's Three Great Meritorious Families - Iron Plate Charcoal Ribs Ducal House>
<Life-saving Special Technique - Fire Storage Heat Pressure Bullet>

Click!
Whirrr- Bang!

A launch port emerged from the armor at Leape's wrist, releasing massive heat.
Steam comparable to a cannon blast erupted all at once, and the superheated air bullet forcibly separated Hestia with explosive power.

Throb.

If she had been even slightly late in using her family's inherited mana cultivation life-saving special technique, both fists would have been reduced to powder.

"Honor Fried Chicken. What are you playing at? Didn't we agree to fight together?"
"I clearly said it? We have a truce."
"What?"
"You must have been too busy fighting to hear, but Oknodie made a proposal to everyone."

Both Leape Iron Plate Charcoal Ribs, who had been fighting with gritted teeth, and Hestia, who had been buying time by fleeing diligently, received the surprising news.

"We've agreed to gather all teams' flags in one place and let everyone choose real flags at their discretion."
"Then why were we fighting?"
"Um... for everyone's entertainment?"

Realizing the gazes of the students gathered around watching, shame washed over her.
If she had at least won, it might have been different, but after a tough fight, she barely managed to pull back after using her life-saving special technique.

"That Hestia woman is no joke."
"She faced two heirs of the three great meritorious families alone."
"More importantly, that woman is a berserker, right?"
"So?"
"Don't berserkers get stronger when they bleed?"

Honor, who received the teachings of the divine spear, and Leape, who displayed ominous iron plate charging techniques.
Even against these two skilled fighters, Hestia didn't get a single scratch.
If she was this strong without bleeding, it was terrifying to imagine how powerful she'd be if she started bleeding and got faster.

"Good job, Hestia!"
"Sorry. I could only hold down two of them, so Lotto got beaten to a pulp by the rest."
"It couldn't be helped. Those two were strong opponents!"
"Was I... helpful?"
"Very much so. Thanks to you holding them off, I was able to persuade the other teams to reset the game."

When I threatened that teams who didn't agree to reset the flags would face Hestia, they reluctantly followed my proposal.
All the flags from the baskets were dumped on the floor, and everyone would simultaneously select real flags to collect.
For students who couldn't gather many real flags and had baskets full of fake ones, this was the last chance for a comeback, a great reversal.

"Oknodie's collected flags were among that pile over there. I remember clearly."
"Damn, everyone will be going for those. I wonder how many we can grab."

Unlike the anxious students nearby, Hero Ishtar calmly unbuttoned his wrist and rolled up his sleeves.
Most first-year students had made their own "marks" to distinguish real flags, like freezing the flags slightly or bending the flagpoles by hand.
However, there was another way to identify real flags without such post-processing marking.

'The fake flags made by Gisele look quite sophisticated on the outside, but there's actually a 13g weight difference.'

The weight difference due to different materials!

"Let's mix in all our team's flags too. We can pick them all back up anyway. Plus some from other teams as a bonus."

After collecting all the flags and taking first place, he would extend his hand to Oknodie.
Oknodie's collecting ability that gathered the most flags.
His own observational skills that could identify real flags.
Combined, they could overcome any lecture that came their way.
If his persuasion worked, Oknodie would become his ally.
If she wanted, he could even accept Hestia, whom Oknodie cherished, as an ally.

'Smart people advance faster than others even when starting under the same conditions.'

With three minutes remaining, as the flag collection began leisurely and he was calmly gathering real flags, the hero saw someone else picking up the real flag he had been aiming for.
Nimble and deft hand movements for such small, white hands.
A competitor who giggled mischievously and boldly grabbed other flags without hesitation.

'Could you distinguish them too?!'

Oknodie.
She too could distinguish the real flags mixed among the pile of fakes.
And she did it much faster than him, without even weighing them in her hands as he did.
