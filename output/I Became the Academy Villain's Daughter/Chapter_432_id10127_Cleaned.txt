# 432 - The Blind Spot of the Test

Honor Fried Chicken barely managed to get up, but he hadn't recovered enough stamina to continue the test.
Not only were his arm muscles trembling, but his palms were bruised from desperately gripping his spear and urgently needed healing.
He pulled out a healing potion he had been keeping in his pocket.

"Ugh. To think I'd have to use this precious thing in a place like this..."

Someone of high status, like a member of one of the Empire's three great meritorious families, is relatively free from the pressure of points.
They can bring in expensive healing potions and use them like extra lives.
Though the medical wing can treat any injury, making the potion normally unnecessary, right now with restrictions preventing access to medical care, it was worth paying any number of points.

"Poor guy."
"Should have chosen his opponent more carefully."

The second-years clicked their tongues.
But the timing was strange.
They hadn't said anything right after he got beaten, so why were they talking now that he'd taken out a potion?

"W-why isn't the recovery effect working?!"
"Because while you all were growing, I was growing too."

<Unwavering in Battle>
[The good deity Emmanuel will not grant another chance to enemies as long as you do not retreat.]
[In battles where you do not flee from the enemy, the enemy cannot obtain recovery effects through means unrelated to divine power.]

A new power bestowed by the good deity Emmanuel through a life of deep faith—recovery nullification!

"If you pledge to become Emmanuel's faithful priest, the deity will bestow grace upon talented youth. If you convert to the faith, you too can recover."
"...Someone who has converted to the deity cannot enjoy life as a noble, right?"
"Emmanuel does not deny a noble life. He merely punishes an unjust life."

Then a life as a noble would be impossible.
Just as a duck that doesn't paddle cannot float on water, a noble who doesn't know how to scheme cannot remain in their position.
If one had overwhelming power, such troublesome skills wouldn't be necessary, but Honor Fried Chicken wasn't that kind of powerhouse.
Still, he couldn't give up his life as a noble.
Even in that brief moment, he realized a loophole.

"Saintess Yupi. Please help me..."

Divine power can only be removed by divine power.
Saintess Yupi, who serves the <Beheading Golgotha>.
With her help, he could surely cut through the recovery curse.
The white holy knight Rudo couldn't hide his disappointment, looking like he'd just taken a hit.

"What do you think?"

Yupi turned to look at Ishtar.
The hero's response was cold.

"The Empire's three great meritorious families chose the Second Princess."

It was now an undeniable truth that Second Princess Mesugakki was obsessed with dark mana.
The hero had no intention of showing mercy to students who had sided with the princess.

"That's how it is. I don't feel sorry about it."
"Urgh... opportunities to put me in your debt are rare. Despite appearances, I've been trained in spear techniques by the Imperial Spear. When I grow at the Academy and return to my family, my position will be better than ever."
"What does that have to do with Ishtar? You'll be staying in the Empire's safe system while Ishtar will be challenging major incidents in the frontier—you'll never cross paths anyway."

Gritting his teeth, Honor proposed a condition.

"I'll provide the Spear Corps, my family's elite direct organization, free of charge for five years after graduation from the Academy."
"The Spear Corps!"
"Gisele, you know about the Spear Corps too?"

Oknodie seemed impressed that he knew, finding it endearing. Though bewildered by Oknodie's attitude, Gisele noticed the other students' suspicious looks and explained on their behalf.

"It's a private military organization made up of followers of the Divine Spear, each individual being a first-class spear master. They have combat power comparable to a regular knight order and are known for making great contributions in monster subjugation every year."
"How useful is that really? I used to hunt monsters on Rocky Mountain whenever I was bored."

At Son O-cheon's indifferent expression, Gisele realized his explanation had missed the mark.
These people needed a more intuitive comparison.

"Five Spear Corps spearmen can hold off one upper-class first-year student. Of course, those spearmen probably can't grow any further, but even at that level, they're considerable fighting power."
"Oh. Not bad."

Subordinates with such specs would be valuable.
But Ishtar's standards were even more severe.

"I accept. If I can use them regardless of whether they live or die."
"...So be it."

As Honor painfully nodded, Yupi held out a soul contract.
After completing the contract, he was finally free.

"...Who is the second weakest senior?"
"Who do you think?"
"Try to guess."
"You think we'd tell you just because you asked?"

Honor bristled at the second-years' teasing.
There was no reason for Gisele to answer even if Honor looked at him.
Seeing him being thoroughly humiliated, Cheddar Potato Pizza, another member of the Empire's three great meritorious families, pointed to one of the seniors.

"The senior sitting in the front row with folded arms pretending to be strong is the third weakest senior. Honor, you might be able to have a good match depending on the conditions."
"No way, how did you know?!"

Before the senior could finish expressing his dismay, Honor presented a challenge.

"Let's have a javelin competition. We'll throw spears from outside this line drawn on the floor, and whoever embeds their spear deeper into the wall wins—a penetration power javelin competition."

It was a cunning calculation by Honor—while he was hesitant to directly clash weapons, he was confident he couldn't lose in a contest of embedding a spear deeply in a single strike.
Contrary to appearances, the senior didn't have exceptional strength and was defeated, allowing Honor to gain 1,100 points, half of 2,200.

"Then I, Cheddar Potato Pizza, will challenge the second weakest senior."
"Since I have plenty of points, I'll end this quickly. I, Ref Charcoal Grilled Ribs, will also challenge the same target."

Even at half price, half of 1,000 points and half of 1,100 points are different.
Even 100 points can buy the cafeteria's set meal (5p) 20 times!
Cheddar and Ref's challenges made the students ponder.

Should they identify and challenge a new weak opponent?
Should they challenge a known weak opponent as quickly as possible?
Or should they risk defeat like Honor and challenge a strong opponent?

"Will Oknodie go after a big fish again?"
"Of course!"
"Don't you need to step up first?"
"If you have the courage to challenge Senior Mandela, I'd rather cheer you on!"

Unlike other students engaged in a battle of nerves to challenge even one turn earlier, Oknodie showed no signs of impatience.
A relaxed female student playing with her drill-shaped hair and whistling.
No one was willing to risk being held back a year by challenging the strongest person present, Mandela Castella, the second-year class valedictorian.

"Oknodie. How about a duel to the death where whoever kills the opponent first wins?"
"Sounds good! Seniors receive heavy punishment for harming first-years, so they can't win by conventional means!"

Encouraged by those words, Zuang pointed to a senior just below the Four Heavenly Kings of the year.

"Senior. It's a duel where whoever kills the opponent first wins."
"Did I look that easy to you?"

The second-year senior chuckled incredulously.

"You used your head well. Second-years get punished for killing first-years. The difference is huge—you can go all out with assassination techniques, but I can't. But you made one mistake."
"Choosing an opponent who talks too much?"
"If you run away without winning or losing, you'll never be able to leave here no matter how much time passes. In other words, your mistake was not setting a time limit."

With those words, the senior's form melted away like water.

"His form disappeared?!"
"Where did that senior go?"
"This isn't ordinary water magic. Transforming the body into liquid without any preliminary signs is closer to elemental arts."

Instead of struggling to find the senior, Gisele checked where Oknodie was looking.
Oknodie was excitedly watching the training ground attached to the homeroom classroom.

Splish splash...
Wet traces appearing on the floor with subtle sounds.
The senior's skill was impressive—moving such a distance in a moment without being seen.

Swoosh, thwack!

Even more impressive was Zuang's skill in detecting and throwing a hidden weapon without even looking at Oknodie.

"How did you find me? My stealth shouldn't be visible even with Search Eye."
"The higher-level the assassin, the less ordinary the target. I learned from my master how to assassinate opponents skilled in elemental arts. For instance, an elemental artist who exchanges their body with water from the elemental realm can control that water even if it evaporates."

As he spoke, opaque liquids in midair began to writhe and quickly turned green.

"That's when you spread poison. When the water an elemental artist has borrowed becomes contaminated, they're forced to return to human form until they send an equal amount of clean water back to the elemental realm."

The senior gasped from the poison as Zuang casually held a dagger to them, causing them to cry out in terror.

"I surrender!"
"Well done, senior. This is the antidote."

Though Zuang had won the match in a frightening yet convenient way, other students didn't dare try to imitate him.

"Only Zuang could do that. We don't have the ability to handle that kind of poison."
"Even assuming the main body is hiding in the elemental realm and only controlling the liquid, and returns when all the controlled liquid evaporates—how do you eliminate it if it's all scattered as gas?"

A senior with such an annoying ability that could evade capture for a day if they were determined to resist!
The fact that they could get caught in such trap abilities if they charged in ignorantly made all students fearful, leading them to make only safe and conservative choices.
But then something unexpected happened.

Thud!

"Ugh!"

Lotto, a fighter who had been expelled from the Imperial faction and crawled into Oknodie's organization.
She collapsed, wheezing after taking a punch to the stomach from the weakest second-year senior.

"Isn't that the senior who surrendered to Son O-cheon?"
"Talk about bad luck. How did she get hit by a lucky punch?"
"Still, they're tired enough to have disturbed breathing, so now we can win."

Three confident Imperial faction students who challenged next were all knocked down by the senior.
Students who realized something strange was happening stopped challenging.

"That senior acts like they're about to collapse but doesn't collapse at all."
"What's going on here?"

Amid the agitated first-years, Gisele, who had been watching Oknodie closely, realized she alone was smiling without being disturbed.

"Does the little lady know why that senior can endure with unexpected resilience?"
"Of course I do!"
"What's the reason?"
"I won't tell you. I only get cursed at when I give tips!"
"Then how about secretly telling just me?"
"That's fine!"

Oknodie cupped her hands around Gisele's ear and whispered the secret.

"Other second-year seniors are secretly casting recovery magic or buffs to heal them!"

Gisele's narrowed eyes widened.

"Isn't the match with the test opponent supposed to be one-on-one?"
"In principle, yes. But there's no rule saying you have to just watch your team lose, right? Helping secretly is fine!"

Especially if the second-years get along well, they're likely all united under Mandela Castella's command.
The few who passed earlier matches were either strong enough to overcome such buffs or secret support with one-hit techniques that couldn't be glossed over.
In other words, unless one was skilled enough to break through the rear support in one go, it would be impossible to pass this test by breaking through the second-years' secret support.

"So be careful about going into an information battle!"
"...You noticed?"
"Of course! In this match, the challenger can decide any method of competition. No one specifically said it had to be a physical fight, right?"

Someone like Gisele, whose specialty is information, could challenge with an information battle.
That is, if he was confident he could overcome the information secretly passed by the rest of the second-years on his own.
