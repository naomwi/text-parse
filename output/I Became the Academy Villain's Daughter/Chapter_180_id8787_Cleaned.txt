# Grateful Students

After Mob, I went to the lounge and distributed a whole armful of likability-boosting items, causing my friends' affection levels to rise rapidly.

"Sniff. Oknodie... thank you so much."
"Wow, to think you have this much stuff regularly... Waaah. That's so sad!"
"Oknodie. If you ever have any troubles, please tell us..."

What kind-hearted friends they are.
They're crying just because I gave them some gifts.
Is it because girls have such rich sensitivity?
But something seems strange.
When I was a guy and gave gifts, they would flinch, laugh awkwardly, and even get facial spasms.
After pondering briefly, I came to my own conclusion.
I guess girls naturally develop more girlish sensibilities when they're together!


* * *


Pain that isn't felt personally cannot be empathized with.
For the same reason, even though many students knew Oknodie had been abused by the Foundation, many still harbored slight resentment toward her.

"She's just naturally talented, isn't she?"
"The Foundation educated her because she has exceptional talent."

The difference in innate abilities.
The rationalization that she achieved more at a younger age while putting in much less effort than themselves.
Assertions born from jealousy and envy made them label Oknodie not as one of them, but as a cowardly talent-blessed person.

"Eat this and cheer up!"
"W-why do you have so many doping potions!?"
"Huh? I always buy this much."
"Y-you bought so many to give as gifts, right?"
"I always buy a lot!"

But now they found out.
That this girl was actually an effort-monster too.
That behind her shining talent was relentless effort that didn't even allow for sleep.
Children of prestigious families.
Children of masters.
For them, training from childhood became a daily routine.
But everything has its limits.
There's no child in the world who continues training all night from an early age just because they've developed a habit of training.

"It really was abuse..."
"I've been misunderstanding Oknodie..."
"I feel so sorry for her..."

Even the female students who followed Arcadia shed tears of repentance as the reality of the abuse was revealed.
At the same time, the hypothesis that all the rumors that had spread might be unexaggerated, raw truth without any embellishment rapidly gained traction.

"Sister Arcadia, please take this too!"
"Oh my. Hehehe. Thank you, dear. But what should I do with this? I don't drink doping potions."
"You can give them to the other sisters if you'd like!"

Yet that kind girl even gave potions to students who didn't think highly of her.

"Sob sob. She's truly a good person."
"That's why she could become the top student."
"I finally understand Lady Arcadia's heart. The Lady knew about this child's good nature all along."

Arcadia's faction students made a pledge.
They would become a shield wall to protect Oknodie.
But their opportunity to step up came sooner than expected.

"I don't even have the energy to eat lunch..."
"Ugh. Too many assignments..."

One day, in the midst of midterm exam preparations,
Arcadia's followers witnessed a scene of students gathering.

"What's going on?"
"Looks like a fight broke out."
"They have so much energy. Fighting at a time like this..."

Those raising their voices were students taking the "Basics and Understanding of Mana Usage" course.
And their opponents were students taking the "Basics and Understanding of Imperial Magic Studies."

Mandatory "magic" related courses that every student had to take at least one of.
Because there were many students, there were several mandatory courses and professors, and since students could choose one among many courses, not all students knew each other.
But it wasn't difficult to guess why they were fighting.

"Looks like the Frontier faction and the Imperial faction are fighting!"

Students taking courses from Frontier faction professors and students taking courses from Imperial faction professors.
Until now, even if they didn't get along, they had maintained a cold, indifferent relationship given the already difficult Academy life, but today was different.

"Aren't you sorry for the materials being wasted on your lacking talent and pathetic skills?"
"What? You're just counterfeits who bought your skills with money and family help rather than actual ability!"

They openly insulted each other, raising their voices, and eventually grabbed each other's hair and started fighting!
Surprised by the sudden brawl, Arcadia's followers quickly grabbed a student from Group A and asked:

"Hey, what's going on?"
"The professor gave us an assignment, and we were gathering materials when those guys said they got the same assignment and demanded we hand everything over!"
"How can there be such terrible people?"
"Help us too. Let's take this opportunity to beat up those pathetic Imperial bastards!"

The student who jumped into the fight with overflowing enthusiasm soon screamed in pain and crawled out with scratch marks on his nails and bruises on his skin.
Most of the students who ran away crying or surrendered after being beaten senseless were from the Frontier.

"Damn it! Their mana training method!"
"Both knight aspirants and wizard aspirants have accumulated mana like it's some cheat key."
"We can't win with our skills... Is this how it is? If you don't have power, you get your materials stolen and your grades drop?"

Students couldn't hide their frustration.

"Please help us!"
"You guys are followers of Arcadia, right?"
"If you're that strong, you should help us!"

Arcadia's followers couldn't muster the courage to jump in after seeing that spectacle.
They too have skills among the lower-class students.
Arcadia, a noble lady of a nation.
To be able to stay by her side after breaking through countless students who admire and adore her, one needs more than ordinary specs.
They all have name value, coming from relatively well-known families in the frontier or being children of high-ranking officials.
Paradoxically, that's why they couldn't step in.
The more they knew about the strength of the power they enjoyed, the more they knew how much stronger the power enjoyed by Imperial nobles was.
There is a clear difference in power between the Empire and the Frontier.

"I-I can't."
"We know. How strong they are..."
"The Frontier can't beat the Empire."

Some might wonder why the Frontier, with skills honed through practical experience fighting against barbaric environments, would lose to the Empire, but that's only knowing one side of the story.
While those in the Frontier spend time helping with territory management, stabilizing people's lives, or family affairs, those in the Empire hire professional educators with large sums of money to provide private tutoring for their children.
It was also common to pay huge sponsorship or investment fees to receive secret techniques from renowned experts at official guilds of famous combat class professions.

Even with classes, it's the same.

In outlying areas, most receive the <Street Fighter> class and build their skills through street fighting.
In contrast, in the Empire, they receive the <Dojo Martial Artist> class, learning techniques from officially licensed dojos and building solid fundamentals.
When a fight breaks out between the two, the result is almost always a crushing victory for the dojo martial artist.
Eight or nine times out of ten, the latter wins.
Unless you're a genius street fighter forged on the streets or a macho with innate, ridiculous physical abilities, that's how it usually goes.

"So we just get unfairly extorted without anyone to fight for us?"
"The instructors. Ask the instructors for help!"
"Let's report to the professors!"

A student with a black eye went to find an instructor, but the help they hoped for didn't materialize.

"Unless they physically restrain you from attending lectures, we don't intervene in disputes over assignment materials."

The instructors drew a firm line.
If fights escalate beyond a certain level, they would impose punishments, deduct points, and even imprison students in the underground dungeon, but fights that severe didn't occur.
Professor Weird, who assigned the task, had some words for the student who looked a mess:

"If you had diligently trained in nature magic, you wouldn't have suffered like this. Your training was insufficient. If you're upset, train harder in magic from now on."
"But I need the materials that were stolen to train hard in magic!"
"You still haven't given your best."
"Do I have some hidden potential or talent?"
"You're here in the professor's research room, not locked in the underground dungeon. In nature, when you fight, you fight prepared to die and cut off your opponent's breath."
"You want me to k-kill someone over materials!?"
"Maybe not to that extent, but you should be prepared to be locked in the underground dungeon if you want to secure lecture materials, shouldn't you?"

The professor's mindset wasn't helpful at all!
No one helps.
Even the Imperial students taking Professor Weird's lectures were the same.

"Help us. You're in the same situation as us."
"Give up. We're outnumbered by the Imperial faction from the start."

Students who gave up on their grades early because they knew the strength of their fellow Imperial faction members!
Even prominent students like Flame Wizard Rogini and Earth Mage Sand Cooker did the same, so the Frontier students had no choice but to sob and resign themselves.

"Stop!"

Imperial students who were taking materials from the storage room.
They were stopped by a confident voice.

"Who said you could take all the materials?"

Oknodie, the protagonist of ominous rumors who turned out to have an unexpectedly warm heart.
She stepped up to a fight everyone else had given up on.
Arcadia's followers became even more conflicted.
It was obvious who would lose.
But if Oknodie stepped in.
Somehow, it didn't seem like she would lose.

"How dare you hurt my cute friend Dorothy!"

The students thought:
Dorothy must have gotten involved in the mess and been beaten up.

"Look here. Dorothy's doodle that she drew on the textbook cover out of boredom has been crumpled under your feet. How heartbroken must she be!"
"S-stop... Oknodie, please stop..."
"No, Dorothy. For the sake of everyone who has suffered, we can't just let this slide."
"No, I'm asking you to stop killing me..."

Oknodie was furious, seemingly unaware that Dorothy was suffering for a completely different reason in real-time!

"Phew, that's a relief. We can trust that child."
"She trains so earnestly that she doesn't even sleep at night and drinks doping potions."
"With skills honed by the Foundation's child abuse at the level of co-valedictorian, she should be able to match even the Empire's early education."

But one of Arcadia's followers thought carefully and offered an opinion:

"But isn't it dangerous to let her fight? She handles dark mana."
"So?"
"Doesn't dark mana become uncontrollable if used too much, causing her to suddenly collapse?"

The followers' faces turned pale.

"If that news reaches Lady Arcadia's ears..."
"She'll hear that we didn't fight too..."
"And Oknodie's collapse will be blamed on us!"

They shouldn't have been happy that Oknodie stepped in.
Even if she wanted to fight, they needed to stop her to maintain their relationship with the Lady.

"We'll help too."
"Sigh. This is a special one-time assistance."
"Be grateful to the Lady. We're helping you because of her."

The followers reluctantly stepped in for Oknodie.
Better to get beaten themselves than to let Oknodie collapse.
This way, the Lady would know they tried to help Oknodie.
They also felt sorry for her.
Hadn't they learned from the doping potion incident?
How pitiful that child was.
Consider it repaying a debt of gratitude.
Frankly, if a real fight broke out, they'd get beaten, but they were still children of well-known powers in the Frontier.
If they compromised appropriately, they might be able to secure enough materials to save face.
Then they could set aside Oknodie's share.

"No! You all shouldn't get involved!"

But Oknodie had no intention of backing down.

"But we're saying we'll help you?"
"How can people weaker than me help anyone?"
"Ah, no, that's..."

They weren't planning to fight seriously anyway.

"What's this? Are you going to fight or talk?"
"You and your friends. Who's going to fight?"

The Imperial faction students stood askew, urging them on.
Oknodie fired a spell at them without hesitation.

<Water Creation Magic>

The Imperial students were suddenly drenched in black water.

"Argh!"
"M-my body is making strange noises!"
"Ouch, it stings. Gasp. T-this. Could it be poison magic used on humans!?"
"Oh. This tastes sweet?"
"Idiot, spit it out! What if it's protein dissolving and that's why it tastes sweet? Your tongue might be dissolving!"
"Is that what it is!?"

Dark water creation magic altered by dark mana.
The result was... cola!
Harmless, but the Imperial students who were shocked by the startling magic glared at Oknodie with fire in their eyes, and Arcadia's followers realized they were doomed.
Negotiation was off the table.
Now they really had to protect Oknodie and engage in a dogfight.

"Everyone protect Oknodie."
"Don't let anyone approach her!"

The second round of the brawl began.
Even if it was an unwanted fight, they had no choice.
At least Oknodie would be grateful, so they'd have to find satisfaction in that!
