# 258 - Human Landmine

Andersen Pretzel was feeling burdened deep inside.
The number one figure in the Western Nobility Alliance.
The leader of the provincial noble faction's male students.
The sense of duty to stand against the children of the Empire's three great meritorious families without backing down.
Each was a heavy burden.
But what truly made him feel pressured were the disastrously chosen landmine lectures.

"From now on, jump from the 30-meter model tower that causes the most fear in humans."
"What?"
"Whether your legs break or not, as long as you land, you pass this lecture's practical evaluation. Of course, if you believe in the 'Full Body Mark,' you can come down without injury."
"..."
"If you don't want to convert to the path of faith, prove that your body is an excellent warrior without divine power!"

The "How to Use Divine Arts Without Faith" lecture that forced religious devotion every class.

"Well, today you'll just sleep on the bare ground."
"Really? We don't have to hang from trees while infusing them with mana to avoid being swept away by floodwaters during heavy rain week?"
"On the contrary, today you'll need to try your best to conceal your mana. I've released worms that surge up and bite their prey when they detect mana on the ground."
"..."
"By the way, these worms howl 'KWAAANG' when hunting prey. If you hear a worm howl, it means someone's been detected, so you should wake up immediately and avoid the attack."

The "Sleeping Well Anywhere" lecture where they had never once slept comfortably.

"Since ancient times, military tactics have stated that retreat is the best strategy, and there's also a saying that hearing something a hundred times is not as good as practicing it once."
"...Why are there six healers attending our lecture?"
"Only those who have practiced retreat well can do it well. Those who choose the wrong escape route or have slow feet will face death due to poor study or weakness in practical application. To avoid repeating the same mistakes and improve, you need an experience that feels like death, right?"
"..."

And finally, the "Thirty-Six Stratagems of Running Away" lecture where they would scream loud enough to shake the forest if caught by the pursuing instructors.
Regardless of Andersen's terribly poor judgment in lecture selection, these lectures were unbearably difficult.
So much so that he desperately needed advice from seniors!

"Andersen. Isn't this too dangerous?"
"Lady Arcadia wouldn't understand. She doesn't know our desperate feelings since she's not taking the same lectures."
"Just looking at Andersen's course registration list, it seems it would be better for you to swallow your pride... but if that's what you want, I can't help it. I hope you achieve your goal."

While many Western Nobility Alliance students were withdrawing, the unfortunate nobles who had followed Andersen's course registrations stayed by his side until the end.
The number of foolish individuals who made the wrong choice of following Andersen was four.
Their eyes were all filled with fatigue, with dark circles covering their eyelids.

"Oh. Look at these first-years."
"They show some promise, don't they?"
"Eyes laden with fatigue. Muscles developed from being forced to survive despite possessing magical power. Even the killing intent naturally seeping out from their exhausted bodies and minds. They're like second-years already?"

Even the third-years who had been bullying first-years indiscriminately couldn't approach these promising juniors carelessly.

"If we mess with these kids, they might surpass us and take revenge before we graduate or get expelled."
"They're a bit scary."
"To think provincial nobles have more guts than imperial nobles. Tsk. This year's batch seems to have the strong ones coming from the other side."
"Barksaar was trying to mess with the first-year top student over there too."
"And he got eliminated by that first-year. Tsk tsk. What a disgrace to third-years. We shouldn't be like him."

Juniors who could be forcibly exploited for immediate gain, but whose rapid growth rate made them fearsome in the long run!
Thanks to such ambiguous evaluation, the Western Nobility Alliance members who had luckily remained unharmed were finally approached by the person they had been waiting for.

"Andersen? Could you be Andersen Pretzel, the second son of the Pretzel Grand Ducal family of the Deutsch Kingdom?"
"It's been a long time, Count Velvet. Should I call you Senior Velvet now?"
"Haha. Call me whatever you like. I heard you were enrolling this year, but I never expected to see you at the Grand Sports Festival."
"Arcadia would have loved to see you. I'm sorry we couldn't come together."
"That child is clever, so she makes fewer mistakes."
"...? That sounds like you're saying we made mistakes..."
"It means you wisely withdrew despite your insufficient skills."
"Ah. Are you complimenting me in front of my Western Nobility Alliance comrades, saying I'm better than Arcadia? Still, I'd appreciate it if you refrained from such comments in front of her. We're maintaining a good relationship."
"You've grown a lot. The boy who used to throw tantrums demanding special treatment wherever he went now cares about others' feelings."
"Senior, your skin is so fair and your expression so relaxed that it's hard to believe you're a third-year. I suppose it's thanks to your strength that sets you apart from other third-years?"

Andersen's eyes filled with expectation.

"Senior. I remained in the competition against third-years at the Grand Sports Festival despite the risks because I have an urgent favor to ask. As a junior sharing noble blue blood, I humbly request your help."
"Hoho. What kind of tantrum are you throwing this time? Are you going to tell my mother if I don't refill the guest snacks like when you first visited our family?"
"...That was when I was 14. Please forget about my gluttonous childhood."

The nobles who had followed Andersen exchanged glances, silently communicating.

'Fourteen isn't that young, is it?'
'That's older than Oknodie is now.'
'Be quiet. What if the young lord gets upset and refuses to help us?'

Senior Velvet chuckled leisurely and asked:

"So, little glutton, what are you craving this time?"
"I want to get exam materials for the final exams. If not for 'How to Use Divine Arts Without Faith,' 'Sleeping Well Anywhere,' and 'Thirty-Six Stratagems of Running Away,' then at least for the required common courses..."
"Did you get tricked by a disguised fourth-year? How did you manage to pick only such terrible courses..."
"...Are our course selections that bad?"
"You've chosen the most brutal courses available to first-years in their first semester."

At the word "our," Velvet looked at the barely acquainted juniors trailing behind with eyes full of pity, understanding why they were being dragged along.
Never having expected to receive such sympathetic glances from a senior noble, not a peer, their eyes grew hot with tears threatening to spill.
If only they hadn't followed Andersen, they wouldn't have had to endure such hardship!

"I'll tell you."
"Really?"
"This is why having good seniors is beneficial, isn't it? Especially since we're both nobles representing the West... I should bestow favors when needed."

Even Andersen was genuinely moved by this unexpectedly kind reception.
He had approached with nothing to lose, but who knew Senior Velvet would treat them so well!
He had been ashamed to face the noble comrades who had trusted and followed him only to experience disaster, but now he could finally save face as their faction leader.

"Stop right there!"

If not for Oknodie's appearance, it would have certainly ended happily.

"...Dumbbell? No, how...?"
"She stole it from a third-year senior!"
'I wasn't asking why the dumbbell is floating in the air, but how she knows flight magic...'

In any case, it wasn't the first time Oknodie's erudition had been out of sync with typical first-year progress.

"Oknodie. I know Arcadia cares for you, but this is a place for Western Nobility Alliance nobles to enjoy their reunion. It's not appropriate for an outsider to interfere."
"It's because of such soft thinking that you end up picking only landmine lectures!"
"What...!"

Andersen's face contorted as if stabbed in the solar plexus.

"Oknodie, that's too harsh!"
"Even if the Grand Duke is cursed with terrible judgment, you can't just tell him to his face that he's cursed. You're not a priest!"
"My goodness, Oknodie. Show some restraint. Even if the Grand Duke brings terrible disasters every time he opens his mouth and has the lips of destruction, calling him a walking human landmine is too much."
"Huh? I didn't say anything that harsh."
"Apologize anyway!"
"I'm sorry...?"

Amid the confusion, the Western Nobility Alliance members revealed their true thoughts while pretending to defend the Grand Duke's dignity, actually using him as a shield to strike him down!
Though she felt sorry for them, Oknodie, who believed in calling things as they were, tried to stop them again.

"Why are you so desperate to interfere with us?"
"Senior Velvet is an angel! He said he'd share the exam materials!"
"Don't tell me you want to hear them too? I suppose it would be fine for someone like you who has maintained Lady Arcadia's dignity."

The Grand Duke nodded in agreement with the Western Nobility Alliance members.

"As the leader of the 981st Western Nobility Alliance, I sincerely appreciate your dedication to protecting her. I'm willing to share the exam materials with you, so please stop interfering."
"I appreciate the sentiment, but you shouldn't accept those! None of you should!"

Oknodie pointed out what these childish noble boys, whose guard had been lowered by meeting a handsome and elegant noble senior after so long, had overlooked.

"What do you think he'll ask in return for putting you in his debt? The debt you'd be repaying isn't to a noble house, but to a third-year!"
"...!"

A debt to a third-year, not a noble.
The choice of words sent waves of terror through them.
Andersen and the Western Nobility Alliance members felt a chilling sense of reality as their warm fantasy shattered.
