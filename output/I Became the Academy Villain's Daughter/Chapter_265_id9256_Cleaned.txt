# Graduating from the Academy by Luck

## 265 - Let's Jump Off This Cart Right Now

Just like the daily quest <Professor Bronze's Underground Treasure Vault> increases in difficulty from the stairs to the outer door, inner door, and secret door of the treasure vault, the ingredient gathering dungeon also has varying difficulty levels.

For example, the difficulty of collecting the greasy fur of a Bomb Sheep is level 3 of <Professor Gordon's Monster Farm Beginner>.
The difficulty of milking a Minotaur is beginner level 5.
I was originally going to do it myself, but since they really wanted to try, what could I do!

"Come here, Mr. Spider!"

The ingredient I'm collecting is the Sweet Spider that spits out sweet silk.
Thanks to these spiders that improve the sweetness, aroma, and flavor of their silk to hunt insects that get tangled in their sticky webs while eating the delicious silk, humans can now enjoy tasty silk too.
When I cautiously extended a skein of thread, the Sweet Spider rolled all eight of its simple eyes carefully with an expression that seemed to ask, "What's this?"
There are mainly two ways to collect spider silk here.
One is to threaten the monster: "If you don't spin silk, I'll kill you!"
Or create an irresistible spot that makes the spider want to spin silk.
Of the two, I chose the latter.

"Look at this?"

When I showed a glass bottle containing familiar mosquitoes, the Sweet Spider stretched its legs frantically and jumped to cling to the bottle.
But no chance.
Zap!
Surprised by the electric formula I had carved beforehand, the spider jumped back in shock.
Like a pie in the sky.
A chicken on the roof.
The answer sheet in a professor's office.
Just like things that can be seen but not possessed make one more desperate, the spider tapped the glass bottle with its legs for a while before pulling away from the electricity that kept zapping.
The spider looked up at me as if begging, "Can't you do something about this?"
In front of the spider, I ostentatiously wound thread around a spool.

"?"

The Sweet Spider stared in confusion at the sight of me winding the thread around the spool.

"!"

The creature's eyes finally lit up with the joy of realization.
If I wrap it in silk, I won't get electrocuted!

Hop hop

The Sweet Spider jumped with joy and carefully wrapped silk around the glass bottle.
Whenever our eyes met, its round black eyeballs looked up at me as if being coy.
The mosquitoes inside the bottle poked the glass wall with their proboscises, complaining, "How could you do this after all our hard work at the sports festival?" but they too would get shocked if they touched the inside.
Truly a devilish trap worthy of a Dark Princess!

"Yah!"
"!!!"

Just as the spider, having diligently produced honey silk, finally climbed onto the bottle cap and danced with joy, I snatched the bottle away with my gloved hand.

"Kyaaak!"
"Heheh. Stupid spider!"

[You have over 30 Boldness experience points. You don't feel fear from the Sweet Spider's "Kyaaak."]
[You have over 10 Dark Electricity Generation experience points. The electricity generation formula is not consumed.]
[The poor Sweet Spider feels fear, despair, and frustration.]
[Deception experience +10]
[Fear Inducement experience +3]
[Bad Kid experience +3]
[Warning! Your Bad Kid experience is too high. If you fail to manage your experience to prevent further increases in Bad Kid, Bad Kid will evolve into Scary Kid!]

I gave the poor spider a candy to cheer it up before leaving.
It clutched the candy with all its legs like an egg, determined not to let this one be taken away, looking quite pitiful.

"Young Master, April. Are you done yet~?"

No answer.
I came out of the spider breeding area and peeked at the two of them gathering ingredients.

Tick-tock-tick-tick-tick-tick
BOOM-BANG-CRASH-BOOM-BOOM

The Bomb Sheep pen was like a fireworks display with colorful magic being shot by the Bomb Sheep.
When their colored wool was hit by magic, the Bomb Sheep's eyes turned triangular and they shot magic at other Bomb Sheep.
As the magic repeatedly hit surrounding Bomb Sheep, the place had apparently turned into chaos.

[Save the idiots!]
[April is trembling in fear in the corner of the Bomb Sheep pen with a food bowl turned upside down on her head like a helmet. Please save that stupid maid quickly!]

On the opposite side, an even bigger commotion was happening.

"Aaaack! Please, Oknodie! Please help meee!"

A Minotaur that had torn off its restraints was now trying to force the restraints onto Andersen, the young master, after placing him in the spot where it had been restrained!

[Save the idiots 2!]
[Young Master Andersen was deceived by the Minotaur's sorrowful cries and loosened its restraints, only to be turned into the victim. If you don't save that stupid first-year, he might be milked in reverse!]

Should I have just come alone...?
I was starting to regret a little.


* * *


In the end, April and Andersen failed to gather ingredients as instructed and only earned Good Kid experience points.
The two defeated soldiers were reduced to serving as Oknodie's assistants, handing over equipment or keeping watch—simple labor.

"Maid. You've had a tough time too."
"You look extremely exhausted as well, Young Master."
"I've never felt more grateful for my strength training than today. If I had been even slightly less trained, who knows what would have happened to me by now..."
"I agree. If I hadn't learned maid stealth techniques before employment, by now..."

The two shuddered as they imagined the bad endings.
Having gone through similar hardships, they felt a strange bond through their shared suffering.

"Was Oknodie always like this? Skillfully collecting ingredients from strange monsters?"
"I don't know much about Oknodie's past either. I've only been serving Oknodie for a few weeks since the Academy..."
"I see. But surely you must have heard rumors. After all, she's the foundation's top scholarship student."

April did have some idea about the rumors.

"They say that all the 'young ladies' raised by our foundation are extraordinary in their own way. While Oknodie particularly stands out, there might be other young ladies among the female students who have risen to great heights without our knowledge."
"Sounds like an urban legend. Does that apply to second or third-year students too?"
"It certainly does. Even just the scholarship students I managed..."
"There must be quite a number?"
"...I've said too much."
"Ah, sorry. I wasn't trying to pry. If it's uncomfortable, let's pretend I never asked."

The two maintained an awkward silence as they watched Oknodie at work.
Whoosh! Pat!
A skillfully thrown ring pinned a fleeing Wing Slime to the ground.
Oknodie collected plenty of bouncy slime jelly cut to perfect eating size with a knife and passed the sack over.
Andersen, carrying so many ingredients they barely fit in his magic backpack, loaded the sack onto the cart.

"The Wing Slime that was just caught is quite a rare monster. Would you like to know what infamous reputation it has outside the Academy?"
"I am curious."
"Those creatures hang from trees in high places where human eyes can't reach, and when a living being passes under the tree, they drop down with a splat and envelop the head. When the prey struggles in the jelly and suffocates, it becomes their catch."
"...That's quite a gruesome hunting method."
"Look how she easily subdues even such rare monsters and collects the edible parts. That's a level of skill that even I, from a ducal family, haven't learned."
"What are you trying to say?"

April glared somewhat hostilely, thinking he might be criticizing Oknodie.
Andersen waved his hands dismissively.

"Don't misunderstand. It's the opposite. I'm not criticizing Oknodie, I'm concerned."
"...I don't think she's as weak as to need concern from a male milk tank who nearly lost his chastity to a Minotaur?"
"Several years ago, during a year of severe typhoons, some Wing Slimes landed in the duke's territory. Just three Wing Slimes wiped out seven villages and killed the knights who went to subjugate them."
"!!"
"Can you say it's normal education for your 'young ladies' to handle such dangerous creatures so skillfully?"

That seemed too extreme.
April also hesitated a bit uncertainly before suddenly shuddering.

"What's wrong?"
"Just a moment."

April put her finger to her lips, requesting silence.
As she naturally approached and covered his mouth, Andersen caught her soft scent.
Feeling embarrassed for no reason and blushing, he heard April speak in a serious voice.

"Someone's coming."
"...!"

We need to hide.
The two looked around urgently and realized there was nowhere to hide except the cart.

"Oknodie, hide!"

After warning her, Andersen quickly hid himself between the sacks on the cart, while April stood there awkwardly, not knowing what to do.

"You're a maid, so you can just say you came to clean, right?"
"If I do that, my dedicated cleaning area will be changed to the monster farm."
"...That's truly a terrible punishment."

Andersen grabbed April's arm.

"!?"
"Stop talking and get in. We need to survive first."

Cleaning in a place like this would cut her lifespan in half.
Even for a maid as vigilant as her stiff tail, being assigned as a monster farm cleaning maid would be too cruel.

"Oh. Who collected so many ingredients?"

Whoever entered the farm discovered the cart and expressed great admiration.

"With this much, I don't need to collect anything else. I can just take this as is."
"!?"
"Attached the auto-driving formula sticker, attached the destination coordinate sticker, safety driving function... well, I guess we don't need that? It's not like first-years will be walking around here."

Clunk. Rattle rattle.
As the cart started moving on its own, the two people making eye contact between the sacks realized something had gone wrong.
Who came and where are they taking us?
A young face suddenly popped up beneath the two panicked people.
It was Oknodie, who had also hidden in the cart.

"Don't be too surprised, everyone."
"Do you know where we're going?"
"The teaching assistant for the <Understanding Monster Patterns> lecture is taking the ingredient cart to the lecture hall!"
"There's no such lecture for first-years..."
"Of course not."

Oknodie said calmly.

"It's a third-year lecture!"
"Aren't there no lectures during Religion Week?"
"Officially, yes?"
"...And unofficially?"
"If there's a naughty professor who didn't get the principal's permission, they might hold secret lectures!"

Andersen steeled himself.
Let's jump off this cart right now.
