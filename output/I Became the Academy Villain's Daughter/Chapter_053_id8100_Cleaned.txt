# 53 - A Lecture Even Veterans Avoid

After the 4th period Remote Weapons Mastery lecture that ran from 4 PM to 6 PM, Arcadia and I walked hand-in-hand to the cafeteria.

"That bow you were shooting made quite a sound, didn't it? Hehe. It was so satisfying to see those Imperial nobles trembling in fear."
"Arcadia, you looked amazing shooting that gun too."
"Fufu. I'm even better at shooting on deck, you know? Someday I'll show you how I turn impudent pirates who target merchant ships into fish food."

Arcadia, whom I thought was just multi-talented, surprisingly had expertise in marksmanship.
As we arrived at the cafeteria, painting gold on each other's faces with compliments, we found students lined up outside with dumbfounded expressions, sighing in despair.
Among them were Isabel, Gisele, and Son O-cheon, whom we had arranged to meet in front of the cafeteria.

"Why does everyone look like this?"
"How strange. I thought Oknodie would have visited the cafeteria during lunch and already known about this."
"I skipped lunch because they were serving the same menu as before!"
"I see."
"Hahaha! If we want to put the little mouse on a diet, we just need to serve the same food every day."

Instead of the unhelpful men, Isabel explained why the students were acting like people who had lost their homeland.

"Starting today, you need points to use the cafeteria. Students with few points are panicking."
"Ah, I see."

I thought to myself, well, this was bound to happen.
It was a disaster that had been foretold.
Didn't the Dragon Headmaster say it?
That points are everything at this Academy.
The free meals and provisions for clueless youngsters were just a service during the one-week admission preparation period.
From now on, you either earn points by actively participating in lectures or gather ingredients and cook for yourself.

"Let's go in."
"Are you sure? It costs 10 points for a single meal."

That's 30 points daily.
In just one month, an enormous 900 points would disappear.

"Tonight's dinner is sweet and spicy fried chicken."
"Chicken is always right."
"Sweet and spicy fried chicken is worth 10 points."

Isabel and Gisele nodded in agreement as we entered the cafeteria together.
We can't resist the luxury allowed only to honor students!


* * *


"There's a large forest near the Knight Department. Have you been there? They say there are tree fruits."
"Wouldn't it be better to raid the orchard in the Production Department?"
"That's managed by senior students who enrolled before us. Touch it and you're dead."

Students faced a dilemma: spend all their points on cafeteria meals, or save points for more valuable uses while finding alternative food sources.
Most decided to save their points and look for food elsewhere.

"Tch. At times like this, I envy those rich central folks."
"I feel you."

Unlike Imperial nobles who could earn additional points through donations, frontier nobles weren't so financially comfortable.
Students like Mob and Jaku, who barely made it into the lower class and came from modest noble families rather than prestigious ones, had to fend for themselves!
As they wandered around the campus like hungry hyenas, their noses were assaulted by a tantalizing food aroma.

"Meat?"
"That smell is insane."

Following the smell as if entranced, they found a large banner prominently displayed.

<Congratulations on your admission! Hungry?>
<Join the Hunting Club and hunt instead of starving!>

A large bonfire.
A whole pig roasting on a spit, turning round and round.
In front of it, a senior student was fanning with large leaves to spread the aroma farther.
These students had been starving since lunch.
No one had the mental fortitude to resist the smell of roasting meat, nor did they want to.

"Oh, freshmen, I see."
"Um, can we do this too?"
"Senior, I want meat!"

Next to the senior roasting meat, another senior who had prepared outdoor tables and folding chairs beckoned.

"Come quickly and sign up! Join the club and we'll let you eat meat!"

Mob and Jaku sat down as if hypnotized, filled out their personal information on the contract, and signed.

"Senior, there's a clause saying we have to pay a 1,000-point fine for each unexcused absence from club activities."
"Don't you want to eat meat?"
"O-of course I do."
"And what's this consent clause about possibly getting injured during club activities and not holding anyone responsible?"
"Just sign it!"
"I see!"

Students signing contracts as if entranced by food!
After the Hunting Club's cruel contract using roast pig as bait, students searching for food or wandering around the training grounds were exposed to numerous temptations.
The Hunting Club wasn't the only one that saw through the psychology of hungry first-years and tried to lure them.


* * *


"What club are you joining, Oknodie?"
"I'm thinking about it tomorrow."

Monday is a day dedicated to lectures.
There's no time to spare for clubs.

"My goodness. Oknodie, are you taking 5th period lectures too?"
"I'm going all out on Mondays!"

Isabel was shocked.

6-7 PM dinner time.
And then attending the 5th period night lecture from 7-9 PM.
For students who considered evenings as time for personal training, rest, and maintenance, this would be a tough, even shocking schedule.
But there's a loophole here.
The fact that I'm a veteran player.
If I already know everything I need to study, there's no need to spend time on studying or assignments after school.
I have enough points to eat at the cafeteria, and if necessary, there's Isabel the cook.
Time is what I have plenty of.
I can just pack my schedule with lectures!

'The problem is choosing a department.'

Monday 1st period: Homeroom.
This isn't a formal lecture.

Monday 2nd period: Developing an Eye.
This is a liberal arts lecture from the Adventure Department.

Monday 3rd period: Basics and Understanding of Mana Usage.
This is a liberal arts lecture from the Magic Department.

Monday 4th period: Remote Weapons Mastery.
This is a liberal arts lecture from the Knight Department.

That's right.
So far, I've filled all my lectures with liberal arts courses.
I've cleared out the liberal arts courses first, which can be taken regardless of department!
Out of 2 common (required) lectures, 3 department lectures, and 2 liberal arts lectures, I've completed both liberal arts lectures.
The remaining one could be a department lecture if I choose one among Knight, Adventure, or Magic departments.
Now I face the most difficult hurdle of selecting a department.

Knight Department.
Magic Department.
Administration Department.
Adventure Department.
Production Department.
Each department has different supporting characters who attend lectures together and occasionally cross paths.

Knight Department.
Main characters - Hestia, Xing, Jenya, Rockfell
New character - Son O-cheon

Magic Department.
Main characters - Irene, Andersen, Female Hero, Female Saintess, Imperial Second Princess
New character - Unknown

Adventure Department.
Main characters - Zuang, Jigoku
New characters - Isabel, Gisele, Dorothy

Among the three departments I've narrowed down to, the ones with the most main characters are the Knight and Magic departments.
Initially, I was aiming for one of these two.
Though I haven't told anyone, the class I'm aiming for is... Magic Swordsman!

'Short-range with sword, mid-range with bow, long-range with magic. Covers elemental attacks and self-healing. An overpowered class!'

If developed well, there's no more broken job than this.
A job that can overcome any unfair situation alone without relying on teammates.
The versatile class of Magic Swordsman.
It's the most stable job I've found as a veteran player.
The problem is that the companions in this playthrough are vastly different from the game.

'There's too much difference from the companions I usually befriend during the entrance exam.'

Normally, I pick one main character I want to befriend during the entrance exam, work hard to gain their favor, and become close.
Among the existing main characters, the one I worked on gaining favor with was Arcadia.
The problem is that this playthrough's Arcadia is such an unpredictable character that I have no idea which department she'll enter.

'And my other companions are all gathered in the Adventure Department.'

Knight Department or Magic Department to strengthen either the magic or swordsmanship of a Magic Swordsman.
Adventure Department where all my friends are gathered.
After intense deliberation, my heart finally leaned toward one side.

'What does efficiency matter to a veteran's pride? I should just do what I want!'

Want to learn swordsmanship and magic?
That can be developed sufficiently through self-study and building on existing knowledge.
Want to hang out with friends?
That's difficult unless I choose the Adventure Department.
While we'll see each other often as first-years, contact points gradually disappear as we advance in grades.

<Adventure Department>

Therefore, 5th period is Adventure Department.
I'll choose today's department lecture from here too.

<Basic Physical Enhancement for Adventurers>
<Basic Weapon Training for Adventurers>
<Adventurer Supply Studies>
<Adventurer Trap Studies>
<Adventurer Wilderness Cooking>
<Developing an Eye>

While these were the Adventure Department lectures available during the day, the list of available lectures at night was different.

<Adventurer's Night Activities>
<Sleeping Well Anywhere>
<Dealing with Nocturnal Animals>

The daytime outdoor activities like Basic Physical Enhancement and Basic Weapon Training, as well as indoor subjects like Supply Studies, Trap Studies, Wilderness Cooking, and Developing an Eye, were all gone.
Instead, new lectures on Night Activities, Sleep, and Dealing with Nocturnal Animals were added.

'That landmine lecture is still here this time.'

I wasn't the only student with a Monday all-in strategy. Other students lingering in front of the Adventure Department lecture hall sign excitedly said:

"Sleeping Well Anywhere? Is this a lecture where we just need to sleep well?"
"Wow. That's sweet."
"How can you resist a class where you just sleep and earn credits?"

Of course, I didn't even consider it and headed toward the lecture hall where <Adventurer's Night Activities> was being held.
The "anywhere" in Sleeping Well Anywhere literally means "ANYWHERE."
Sleeping in a makeshift tent set up over a pit crawling with venomous snakes without falling in.
Setting up a portaledge (cliff tent) and sleeping on a cliff face.

'Even as a veteran, that's a bit...'

I somewhat admire the freshmen who willingly attend lectures that even veterans avoid. Though I'm sure none of them know what they're getting into.
