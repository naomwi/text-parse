# 44 - Children Should Go to Bed Early

Lotto spoke with a terrified expression.

"I'm telling you it's true. That kid definitely said it before the power outage happened. That there would be a power outage today. How could anyone know such information at that exact timing?"
"Are you sure you didn't mishear?"
"No, I didn't! It was definitely caused by that child. If not by her directly, then something behind the scenes must have helped her!"

Lotto's peers exchanged glances.

"Lotto. When you're tired, it's best to get some rest."
"You look quite ill. Please don't come to our gatherings for a while and focus on taking care of yourself."

A polite way of telling her not to show her face at their meetings anymore!
Though she realized these people, with whom she had been acquainted and socialized since her days in the Empire, didn't believe her words, Lotto had no choice but to accept it. Her reputation was already in ruins.
After being used and discarded, protesting would only invite open ridicule.

"...Fine. I'll contact you when I'm feeling better."
"I wish you a speedy recovery."
'Cowards.'

She didn't believe such empty pleasantries.
Now that she'd been expelled from the faction, she probably wouldn't see their faces again.
They would gradually drift apart until one day they'd become complete strangers.

Vroom! Paang!

Late at night.
Lotto emerged from the training ground where she had been sweating to ease her stifling feelings.
A chilling premonition crept into her heart, which felt as heavy as her exhausted, sweat-soaked body.

"Who's there!"
"Hmph. Too slow."

She was caught from behind.
Even with a body tired from training, allowing someone to get behind her so easily...
This was no ordinary skilled person.

Pavat

A masked female student waved her hand in greeting.

"Is what you told your friends true?"
"...What do you want to know?"
"That Oknodie knew about the power outage."
"It's true. Everyone may think I'm making cowardly excuses out of shame for losing to a child, but facts are facts. I didn't lie."
"I believe you."
"...Really?"
"I saw it. The moment the power went out, that child used an impressive sword technique. She might fool others' eyes, but not mine."

The masked assassin Zuang.
Her eyes don't dim even in darkness.
Especially if someone used "mana," she would know.

'When I use my eye technique, I can see it. Even the movement of mana.'

The moment the training ground's safety guard disappeared, she precisely cut off a leg. That was her sword technique.
As soon as the lights went out, far from retreating or hesitating, she took exactly two steps toward her opponent and unleashed her sword imbued with sword energy aimed at the leg.
Her reaction speed at that moment was at a level impossible to respond to unless one knew in advance.

'My master said the scholarship students from the Foundation were mere small fry not worth being wary of...'

But that child is different.
Zuang twirled around and turned her back to Lotto.

"Still, don't go around talking about it too much."
"Are you threatening me?"
"Threatening? I don't bother with such troublesome things. I'd just cut you down and be done with it. This is merely a courtesy in return for the interesting story. A piece of advice for your own good."

Zuang warned her.

"Even if it's a facility for freshmen, you're saying there's an organization that can manipulate the Academy's facilities to cause a power outage at the desired timing."
"...!"
"If I were you, I wouldn't openly defy an organization capable of easily making a freshman disappear."

Lotto staggered and collapsed in fear.

"Ah, aeu, ah..."

I'm scared.
Help me.
She tried to reach out with those words, but Zuang, who had been right there, had already vanished.
They were on different levels.
Private tutoring with martial arts instructors arranged by her family. A manufactured reputation. The praise that elevated her.
None of it mattered before true masters.
Today, she felt the wall three times.

Oknodie.
Hestia who blocked her.
And the masked girl who was no less formidable than those two.

From now on, she would keep her head down.
Thanks to this humbling experience, Lotto resolved to live as a good person with newfound modesty.

Let's just sleep.
