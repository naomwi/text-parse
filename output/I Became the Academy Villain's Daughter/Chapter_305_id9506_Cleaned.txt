# <305 - The True Enemy>

The Apostle of Anlage.
A sentinel planted by the Chairman to protect the cruise ship and one of the 20 powerful individuals who were once invited aboard to search for the divine relic.
Jonna Wiheomhae was already aware that Low, the demon contractor who was the most dangerous among them, had awakened before boarding the ship.
This was because the <Sight Doll> possessed by the dark merchant Gisele had a two-way sight-sharing function that allowed the owner, Gisele, to peek through the doll's vision and vice versa.

Crack!

That's why when Low's mana barrier tried to push him off the ship as he was boarding, he was able to prevent it by raising the metal present on the ship.

"Your welcome greeting is quite rough. How do you plan to take responsibility if the young lady gets hurt?"
"You're being overdramatic. She won't get hurt with you protecting her, right? Besides... I'm just as interested in that young lady's body as you are."

To Leape and April listening nearby, it sounded like the flirtation of a pervert, but they couldn't bring themselves to show hostility toward either party.

'They're on a different level.'

Leape realized it.
Even though she was considered quite skilled within the Foundation, she was still lacking when compared to these two.
April, whom she glanced at from the corner of her eye, was barely managing to stand and maintain consciousness.

"Eww~ Are you a pervert, mister?"
"Please stay back, my lady. He's a pervert you shouldn't get close to."
"You don't intend to let me go quietly? Then... I guess I'll have to resort to this method."

When Low snapped his fingers, the magic circle engraved at his feet emitted light, and hundreds of crew members trapped in cages screamed.
The screams of the crew members who instantly lost their internal mana and life force, becoming <Mana Fuel> and causing aging, only brought a widespread smile to Low's face.

"Now, this is the beginning. Let's see who will be the winner to escape this ship."

Jonna Wiheomhae is strong.
Strong enough to defeat Professor Rave from the Academy.
He even has the record of being the first to attend the Academy as a proxy for the Foundation.
The face of the Foundation.
He has the skill and qualifications to represent the Wiheomhae Foundation, which is resented and hated by people all over the world.
At least, that's what the Chairman thought, which is why he has been using Jonna for important matters until now.
Therefore, defeating Jonna and taking Oknodie's body would be the ultimate revenge for Low.

'The Foundation's proxy. And the Foundation's top scholarship student. The demon contractor Low will kill the Foundation's present and future and make them his new vessel. Furthermore, if I seize the cruise ship they're so proud of, the Chairman will suffer tremendous losses.'

In an instant, everything the Chairman had invested in would turn to bubbles.
This level of revenge would be enough to repay the price of being bound to the cruise ship for over ten years.

"Now, my fuels, offer your mana to me. And pray that Jonna Wiheomhae falls as quickly as possible. Otherwise, you will all be squeezed dry and die as fuel for my battle!"

A cruel battle began with the entire ship held hostage.


* *


Low, who was squeezing power from two thousand crew members held hostage, was indeed powerful.
Even Jonna's metal manipulation couldn't overwhelm him when this skilled fighter, who had toyed with the first-year advanced class including the hero using minimal mana, gained massive amounts of mana.

Crash!

With each use of power, Jonna felt at a disadvantage.
The metal borrowed from the ship's internal components was being torn apart in real-time by Low's vibration waves.
Each time he used power for repairs, the remaining amount of metal equipment and components on the ship decreased.

'If I use everything, I'll have to sacrifice the hull next. If that happens...'

Even if he wins, it wouldn't be a true victory.
The ship would become immobile.
This meant the young lady couldn't reach the Chairman's mansion.
The indulgence that could become the young lady's spare life in the future.
That precious thing would be wasted due to his own inadequacy.
There was no way he could accept such a thing.

'I'll end this with a quick battle!'

Low saw through Jonna's impatience.
He consistently maintained his composure, directing most of his power toward <defense> and simply repeating the process of tearing apart approaching metal.
Even if he squeezed out all his fuel and exhausted his power, he knew that if the cruise ship lost its functionality, Oknodie wouldn't be able to complete the Chairman's orders.
Information isn't something only allies can obtain.
Low, as an enemy, had also acquired all information within the cruise ship through the crew members.
This included the fact that Oknodie had received orders from Papa to come to the mansion with her friends.

'No matter how much you want to end this quickly, I won't allow a short battle.'

Powers collided, <domains> unfolded, and magical light rained down, causing rupturing sounds in space.
The longer the battle continued, the deeper Low's cruel smile became.
Time was on his side.
Impatience was Jonna's burden.
The more urgent things became, the greater the burden and risk Jonna had to bear.
The advantage was steadily tilting toward Low.
There was only one variable.

"Oknodie."
"Zuang! Where were you hiding?"
"In the chandelier on the ceiling. We hid together at the Academy too, remember?"
"Ah! That was fun~"
"More importantly, what are you going to do? That man is unbelievably strong. Even the hero was no match for him. At this rate, your butler will be defeated too."
"I'm not going to do anything!"
"...Are you serious? Your butler will fall and everyone will be in danger. Wouldn't it be better to release the lifeboats and escape now? If possible, save Titoso too."

Of course, the lifeboats couldn't accommodate that many people. Most would have to be abandoned to fend for themselves.
Zuang hesitated to mention such a harsh reality.
Yet Oknodie just kept smiling.

"It's okay. Everything will work out somehow!"

He had won.
While Zuang's expression twisted at the carefree attitude of the only variable, Low's expression lightened.
Was it blind faith in her butler?
Or overconfidence in herself?
Faith always makes those who lack the qualifications to have it pay a tremendous price.
Just as they, who were once believers of Anlage on this ship, had done.
The only difference from before was one thing.
The powerful one was now himself, not the Foundation.
This time, he would make everyone in the Foundation pay the price of faith.
And he would take the body of that delicate yet bold child as his own.
Then escape to Gift Academy, the safest place in the world from the Foundation's pursuit and threats.
The hero candidate Kei or the others would also join forces if they inhabited the bodies of Academy students.
Together, they would become upper-class students, build sufficient skills and connections, and after graduating from the Academy, the Foundation would have to run away from them.

'It's not far off. I can begin to see all that future. Jonna Wiheomhae, once I overcome this single barrier, our faith will finally triumph!'

Anlage is the god of corruption.
The one who reveals what innate qualities people possess.
The best aptitude he revealed to him.
The quality of being a demon contractor granted him immense power.
The god Anlage told him.
To retrieve his divine relic.
Then that quality would grow even greater.
He had obtained the relic but was confined in one body with other selves, but now that he could freely exercise his power without anyone's interference, Jonna was clearly on the defensive.
The giant cabins rising in the center of the ship were gradually decreasing in height, layer by layer.
The same went for the ammunition in the armory and the cargo in the storage.
10% left.
That was the total amount of resources Jonna Wiheomhae could use without damaging the ship.
In contrast, he still had three chunks of fuel left.

[Magic Circle of Absorption]

The 500 crew members trapped in the second cage collapsed, vomiting blood.
Even after this, there were still 1,000 people left.
Plus, there were special students left as sacrificial offerings.
He had won.
Victory was already certain.
Even if Jonna squeezed out his power with the resolve to lose the ship's functionality in the remaining 10%, he couldn't possibly handle the mana equivalent to the next 500 people, and the 500 after that.
For Jonna to win, he should have honestly aimed for a quick battle targeting only himself, not killing all the other crew members.
If there was no mana to steal, no matter how much mana he tried to extract in a short period, the evaporating power couldn't all be handled, and any mana beyond Low's own limit would have evaporated into the atmosphere.

"Butler of the Foundation. Iron-blooded metal sorcerer. Your combat style, so soft it makes your title weep, has invited defeat."
"Do you really think so? Did you believe that I, Jonna Wiheomhae, would thoughtlessly compete with you in a position where I'm responsible for the young lady's safety?"

In response to the provocation thrown out of a desire to see a face stained with humiliation, Jonna unexpectedly retorted with confidence.
That confidence was irritating.
It wasn't arrogance or bravado, but a genuine belief in his victory that could be felt emotionally.
There was something.
Something he hadn't grasped.

"Our young lady has excellent future potential."
"...?"
"That's why everyone who sees her thinks this way: She'll be great in the future, but she's still far from it. That it will take time for that potential to reach beyond iron-plate level powerhouses."

That wasn't the case.
Jonna declared with a murderous smile that was once confiscated by the young lady.

"This is the delayed battle that the young lady wanted."
"A delayed battle? Against me, in the open sea, even with the resolve to lose the ship? What for?"
"The young lady is also versed in blood magic. Mosquitoes have already been circulating around the ship, sucking the blood of many people. And they've also injected a special secretion."

The quick battle was a diversion.
The more he hurried, the more Low also sensed Jonna's true intentions and tried to avoid it by delaying.
The strategy had already worked perfectly.

Thump.

The moment he made eye contact with Oknodie, Low felt that something had gone terribly wrong.
The mana he had just extracted by squeezing the life out of the crew members wasn't listening to him.
It was because the 'impurities' in the blood collectively raised their heads and refused his commands.

"Surprise!"
"You...! What did you do to the crew members' blood?!"
"Nothing much. Just played a little prank!"

Oknodie, who had been smiling all along while staying in place, kindly answered Low's roar.

"Did you know that when mosquitoes suck blood, they inject a paralyzing venom to prevent their prey from detecting that they're being bitten? I just enhanced that function a bit."

Beyond paralyzing the body, she added one more special function.

"I simply infiltrated a dormant mana-paralyzing poison that forcibly paralyzes all mana in the body the moment it receives a signal from my dark mana!"

Why would you do such a thing? What for?
I can't understand.
What benefit could there be in doing that!
The shock was momentary.
A terrifying truth struck Low's mind.
If there was a benefit, it was already there.
Right at this moment.

"You... designed this moment from before boarding the ship?!"

Low finally realized.
The opponent he was fighting now wasn't Jonna Wiheomhae.
The true opponent who had been steadily engaging in a numbers game while putting him on the surface.
The Foundation's top scholarship student and an extraordinary being that even the Chairman gave special treatment to.
The Dark Princess Oknodie was the true enemy he should have defeated.
