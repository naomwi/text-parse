# 177 - The End of the Internal Work

When a 0.1% chance stacks with another 0.1% chance, I've already reviewed how the Academy's humble abode gets demolished in the Gigantamax Mandragora event.
If such rare chances repeat multiple times, even more extraordinary things can happen.

The Academy's final day.

The moment when the Dragon Headmaster becomes disillusioned with the Academy.
It's the ending where, graduation be damned, the Academy gets completely demolished.

'A future that arrives when only students of a single personality type gather at the Academy, bringing either a tediously peaceful atmosphere or a storm of jealousy and infighting!'

It's not an event that happens easily.
With over two thousand students enrolling each year.
The event requires truly demanding conditions to trigger.
First, disaster events must drastically reduce the Academy's student population.
New enrollments must cease due to global instability.
Upperclassmen either graduate or get repatriated to their home countries.
It's an event most likely to occur during wartime.

'Though it's not impossible for it to happen in peacetime as well!'

With a veteran like me around, the chances of this happening in reality are extremely slim.
Unless we fail to prevent regional collapse events worldwide and the entire globe goes to hell, there's no reason for students to leave the Academy en masse.

But what if that rare chance actually happens?
What if the already diminished student body happens to all have the same personality type?

<Wealth Accumulation>

Another instinct possessed by the Dragon Headmaster beyond his publicly known greed.

<Enemy of Balance>

World peace.
Single order.
Permanent unification.
Absolute immutability.
The Headmaster becomes addicted to the poison called boredom from all unchanging things and hates it more than anyone else.

Imbalance. Chaos.

The moment the chaotic Academy image the Headmaster desires disappears, he grows tired of earthly amusements.
The Academy he cherished instantly becomes a burden that can no longer satisfy his entertainment needs.
Labor without reward.
Waste without value.
Time without meaning.
The moment he decides to end it all, the Academy collapses overnight.
The Headmaster doesn't just gracefully resign when he decides to abandon the Academy—he rams it with his massive body, crushes it with his feet, and burns it with his breath.
An ash-heap ending where he completely demolishes the Academy and leaves it in ruins!

"Headmaster!"

"What is it, human?"

"I want to become a new student!"

I assess the personalities of the remaining new students and quickly act out a personality that doesn't match theirs.

"I'm not a kind, boring, and timid child!"

The students remaining at this school are gentle, kind, and unmotivated kids who've lost their homelands and stayed at the Academy out of desperation and uncertainty.
If I act like a wicked, bad, and enthusiastic student, I can easily overcome this bizarre ending regardless of the extreme combat difficulty.

"Oh?"

"Of course, a fat-bellied lizard like you probably doesn't have the talent to teach me! But I'd still like to experience the Academy!"

"Well, look at that?"

As curiosity replaces the Headmaster's destructive urge, the Academy's nightmare finally ends.

<Total Annihilation Ending>

Except for the route of the worst 20-year graduate student in history who aimed to exterminate all living beings in the world.

"This is... impossible."
"Is that... Oknodie's master?"

My past self.
A macho muscle man with a gigantic, robust 2.3-meter frame.
A playthrough where, after being tormented for 20 years by a crazy professor who trapped me at the Academy, I decided to erase all living beings, declaring I would destroy this world.
A hell where no living creature could survive.
It's obviously impossible to face myself after 20 years of spec upgrades in that world of horrific recreated scenes.

Even four years is enough to become overpowered.
How could I possibly win against someone with 20 years of experience?

Emotionally, I understand completely.
20 years is truly crossing the line.
How could they think of enslaving someone for 20 years without even granting a doctoral degree?
I don't hate Imperial faction professors for no reason.
They're absolute garbage!

"Oknodie. 'Reset' through death. Quickly, before it's too late. Before that thing approaches...!"

Destroyer's urgent cry.
Behind his shouting figure, massive energy flies in.

<Kroit's Necromantic Field Type G>

The magical barrier Professor Sadako reflexively deployed shatters, sending both people flying simultaneously.
Destroyer rolls across the floor to correct his posture while Professor Sadako kneels, bleeding from her mouth.
My graduate student version, who put two professor-class individuals into a state of extreme tension with a single strike, approaches with burning rage and hatred toward the professors.

"Wait!"
"Die."
"I'll let you graduate!"

The <Primordial Destruction> spell that had been streaming from his fingertips changes course, grazing past both sides and splitting a distant mountain range into three pieces.
If you know the gimmick, you can block it this simply, but if you don't, there's absolutely no way to stop the 20-year graduate student route player strategy!

"Grad...uation?"
"Look here. I've brought back the professors from the frontier who were driven away by the Imperial faction professors!"
"Professor Sadako... Professor Destroyer...?"

The two of us using Korean, our original homeland language rather than the supposedly auto-translated isekai language.
True to being another version of me, the 20-year graduate student route me noticed the strangeness of the situation.

"What's the hottest coin these days?"
"Haenam faction coin!"
"What ability appears when Hiding experience exceeds 1000?"
"Absolute Stealth!"
"I see. You're not an NPC. An NPC that shouldn't appear has appeared, so this isn't a normal situation. Are you perhaps a manifestation of my subconscious and is this all an illusion?"
"It's the opposite! You're a manifestation of my subconscious and this is my nightmare!"
"Don't lie. That would mean that I, who've stubbornly stuck to the muscle macho big guy path obsessed with DPS, played as a female character whose head barely reaches my waist!"

Just like a veteran, my subconscious self quickly identifies facts he can't accept!

"No, think about it in reverse. If I'm real, then I subconsciously summoned a muscle macho big guy, but if you're real, then you subconsciously summoned a petite cute girl character!"
"That's ridiculous."

My other self accepted with extremely rational reasoning!
His fighting spirit disappeared.

"Oknodie. You can communicate with that monster...?"
"What language is that? Even I, a former member of a hero party that traveled the world, don't recognize that language."
"Ah, how should I explain this."

While I was wondering how to explain to the professors, Professor Destroyer boldly stepped forward saying there was no need to try.

"No need to explain. That appearance. It's definitely your master, Oknodie, whom I saw in the nightmare of struggle."
"What? I was under that kind of curse too!?"
"It's fine now. More importantly, about your master. How can we possibly stop someone with such ridiculous combat power?"

Although he's lost the will to fight for now, there's a chance he might go berserk if he suddenly realizes the Haenam faction coin has crashed.
I needed to implement a foolproof way to drive away my other self.

"It's simple!"

A possessor of power more terrifying than demon lords or dragons.
The end of the world incarnated in human form.
The experiencer and potential recreator of all tragedies.
Another me with the strongest specs.
And a 20-year graduate student.

"Write him a graduation certificate!"

I made the request that would repel him.

"...Oknodie's master will retreat if he gets an Academy graduation certificate? Why on earth...?"

While Professor Sadako, whose face is always hard to read behind her hair, might be confused, Professor Destroyer was understandably perplexed.

"Will that really work?"
"Yes!"

Professor Destroyer handed over a graduation certificate, figuring there was nothing to lose.
My 230cm self, holding the certificate, began to fade away like a ghost achieving enlightenment, with a warm smile.

"Don't ever become a graduate student..."

Clutching the graduation certificate to his chest as if it were the world's most precious treasure, my other self floated toward the sky, and the endless nightmare finally came to an end.
