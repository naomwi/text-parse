# 114 - The Effect Was Excellent

The member of the Red-Toothed Mushroom Illegal Cultivation Management Association, <Paper Company>, was determined to make up for his failure on the way to school.

While the third-years monopolized the Giant King Crabs, they began monopolizing shellfish—prey that first-years would target!

"If you want fish, pay points and buy them! Fishing is not allowed!"
"Hey you with the fishing rod! Put it away now or I might just cut your line with magic!"

Naturally, the first-years protested.

"Who are you to monopolize the fish! Do you want to die! I'll give you a kitty punch!!"

Paper Company responded cruelly to the cute resistance of Zenya, the cat-person who was jumping up and down in anger, and the first-year freshmen who raised their voices alongside her.

"Boys, our freshmen want to taste some spice. Give them a lesson!"
"Waves, rise up!"
"Water, surge forth!"

Waves rose on both sides of the 20-passenger fishing boat and crashed through the windows where Zenya and the freshmen were poking their heads out.

"Aaaah! The waves are crashing in!"
"Hold onto the windowsill tight!! If you get caught in the ebb tide, you'll fall into the water!!"
"Ptooey! Too salty!!"

Paper Company and his fellow cultivators laughed loudly.

"Wahaha! At this time of year, first-years are struggling just to memorize mana patterns and draw magic passes, let alone cast water spells. You can never win against second-years!"
"If you've figured out your place, obediently pay your points and buy fish. And tell us who the ice mage is!"
"Also tell us who owns the grill that fried my mushrooms! I'll give you one fish for free if you do!"

The freshmen, thoroughly soaked, made sad faces but still tried to muster courage and shouted:

"You think we'll tell you just because you ask?"
"You jerks. There are strong people among first-years too. We'll tell them about you!"
"The one who uses the grill is Leape Life, a guy who smells deliciously of charcoal-grilled ribs."

The lower-class students gave Zenya disapproving looks for selling out a fellow classmate to upperclassmen.

"He's an Imperial! He called Zenya, who's from the frontier, an uncivilized cat and said I'd be perfect with the male cat that yowls at his house!"
"What era does he think this is, making racist remarks from a century ago!"
"Those Imperial guys are really unpleasant."
"He deserves to have his identity sold out."
"And when I asked to see a picture, he showed me a baby that's only 1 year old! I can't forgive someone who treats Zenya like a pedo!"
"...That was the issue?"

While the first-years' feelings were complicated, Zenya proudly leaked Leape's personal information and received fish in return.
And now, after finishing explaining what had happened, she held out a fish to Isabel.

"So before we go, could you cook just one? Isabel is good at cooking."

Isabel, who had listened to Zenya's moving true story from beginning to end, replied with a blank face.

"No. I'm going to help Oknodie."

<Claim Shellfish Fishing Rights Event>
Mean second-year seniors are trying to monopolize seafood.
The thugs of the fishing zone.
The gangsters of the sea.
It's time to teach them a lesson.
Fight back for the first-years' right to survive!

Even without the event notification encouraging a fight, we couldn't leave them be.
But charging in recklessly wasn't the answer.
To teach those problematic seniors a lesson, we needed enough time to fight properly.
We couldn't end this with just a brief scuffle during breaks or transit times.
So I endured through the remaining lectures, waiting for evening to come.

"Oknodie, we're all ready!"
"I heard you're having a showdown with the second-years? We'll help too!"
"The third-year seniors lent us a battering ram because they thought it would be fun! If things get tough, we'll ram them with it!"

The battering ram that Dorothy borrowed from seniors taking other lectures after hearing about the situation during the third period <Basics and Understanding of Mana Usage> was a bit perplexing.

"Aren't we having a naval battle?"
"Still, wouldn't a battering ram be intimidating?"
"I guess that's true!"

Well, better than nothing!

"We'll help too. If there's a rule that allows shooting second-year seniors to death, we'll provide fire support. A third-year senior working as a teaching assistant procured a ballista for us."

Schola, the archery prodigy who attended the fourth period <Mastery of Ranged Weapons> lecture with me, brought a large ballista.

"...Thanks for the help, but if we shoot seniors to death with a ballista, we'll be disciplined!"
"What about arrows?"
"As long as we don't cause permanent injury, it'll end with just a light disciplinary action?"
"Then I'll just shoot with a bow."
"But aren't you guys from Group B? Why are you helping us?"
"Beyond the bad blood between the frontier and the Empire, we can't forgive second-years who interfere with first-years' food supply rights. Plus, Leape Life was attacked."

It seems Zenya's cowardly sale of personal information led to the Imperial faction joining us.
...If they find out the truth, those terrifying archers might target us instead, so we absolutely can't let them find out!

"So. How are we going to retaliate? Are we going to hide on boats on the water and open fire all at once?"
"No!"
"Since we brought the battering ram, how about luring them near the window and then charging the ram through the window to drop them onto the boat?"
"The seniors might die that way!"
"You're too soft."
"Right. If you say this won't work and that won't work, what do you want to do?"

Schola and Dorothy expressed their dissatisfaction.
As expected of archers, they both have quite the aggressive temperament.
In their minds, have the second-years become prey to be shot and killed on sight?
This is why ranged DPS players are scary.
It's a thought I have every playthrough, but when forming parties later, I must never forget to conduct personality tests for the archer position.

"How about an assassination plan? Sneak under the boat, make a hole, and sink it."
"Leave the escort to me. If we're discovered while making a hole, I'll block them with my life!"
"I appreciate the sentiment, but please hold back!"

There are too many students getting caught up in the enthusiasm like Zuang and Mob, which is troublesome.

"I'll try talking to them first!"

So we launched a boat and approached the 20-passenger fishing vessel the second-years were using.

"Seniors! Let's talk!"
"Hoping for a bulk purchase? I can't do much, but I'll give you a 10% discount!"
"We're not here to buy fish!"

A senior wearing a paper captain's hat made an unpleasant face and snapped:

"Then why are you here?"
"Please stop monopolizing shellfish fishing rights against first-years immediately! Otherwise, we'll go to the student council and report everything you've done today!"
"Oh. You know about the student council despite being a first-year? That's admirable, but what a shame."

The senior spoke arrogantly with a displeased expression.

"We conducted proper transactions with fair compensation. We set reasonable prices too. If you think the student council will take your side when you report us, you're mistaken."

There seemed to be no room for compromise.
But we had thought this through and decided to try resolving it through dialogue first.

"Then would it be okay if everyone found out that seniors were illegally cultivating red-toothed mushrooms?"
"You little... So you're one of the first-years who attacked our cultivation site."

The seniors' expressions suddenly turned fierce.
This is an event that happened occasionally even when I was a muscle-bound character, so I came to negotiate without much thought, but unlike before when they would back down out of fear, the seniors are being extremely aggressive.

"No! I just heard what the others said and came to mediate!"
"Mediate?"
"I'll keep your illegal cultivation of red-toothed mushrooms a secret if you stop bullying the first-years!"
"And what about our losses? The damage from our precious red-toothed mushrooms, which we were carefully growing to make magic spore sacs, being kidnapped and eaten?"
"Can't you let it go just once for your cute juniors?"
"As if we would!!"

A few second-years muttered behind the senior wearing the paper captain's hat.

"She is cute though."
"She's small but speaks very clearly."
"Hey, I have some jerky, want one?"
"What kind of jerky?"
"Squirrel jerky."
"I've already had that, so no thanks."

Hmph.
I actually have quite refined taste!

"Ugh! You're all getting soft. Why are you playing with this kid? They've damaged our business. There's no compromise until they compensate for that damage!"

The senior pushed me with his palm as I stood on the boat.
Yes, this was exactly what I'd been waiting for.
There are several ways to end violence in this world.
Either suppress it with greater force.
Or seek help from professors or the student council.

"Owww!"

Or overreact by spinning a full 360 degrees and falling down even when lightly tapped.

"You, you!! When did I hit you that hard! Stop exaggerating and get up quickly!"

When he roughly tried to pull me up, I kicked off the ground and flew backward about 10 meters, falling into the water with a loud splash.
After about 3 seconds in that state, Zuang and Mob quickly jumped into the water and lifted me onto the lifeboat.

"Oknodie has been attacked!!"
"What cowards! Using violence against someone who came for peaceful negotiations!"

A conflict resolution strategy I often used in my muscle-bound days.
The self-harm blackmail strategy.
The shortcut to resolving everything smoothly where only the opponent gets blamed, without having to pay fines to the student council or Academy for mutual assault charges using precious points!
...That was the intention, at least.
But perhaps because I performed it with a fragile child's body instead of a muscular one, the effect was far more impressive than expected.

"These trash!!"
"How could they find any reason to hit such a small child!!"
"Let's avenge Oknodie!!"
"Seniors or not, let's beat them all down!!"

My classmates suddenly became enraged and charged toward the seniors' 20-passenger medium-sized fishing boat.
Without distinction between upper and lower classes, frontier and empire, everyone was thoroughly angered and charged in.
...A brawl broke out.
