# 411 - That Mere Resolve

Though she always wears a mask, Zuang has such a special friendship with Oknodie that she unhesitatingly lowers her mask and reveals her bare face in her presence.
There have certainly been those who approached Zuang thinking they could become close to her too after seeing her gentle demeanor.
Irene was also one of the students who had harbored such thoughts.
The barren North is a place talented individuals avoid.
If one could attract even one more capable person, it would be a great help.
After all, the presence of a special-class assassin who can single-handedly assassinate an enemy general is no less valuable than an elite army of ten thousand soldiers.
But Irene abandoned that dream early on.
Because on a day when she went to speak with Zuang, she witnessed it.
The sight of Zuang slapping a classmate to the ground and so easily pressing a dagger to their throat.

"Don't ever appear before my eyes again. Next time, I'll watch the light fade from your eyes."

Zuang is an assassin.
She went through a completely different upbringing than ordinary students.
Of course, students who qualify for Gift Academy are not ordinary students either, but even among those exceptional students, Zuang was even more special.

'If I had to compare, she'd be similar to Xing.'

She has no hesitation in killing people.
The line of rules built by law and morality in her heart is much easier to cross than for other humans.
Because she is Zuang, she can say with certainty.

"How did you see that child's face, senior?"

A child with a doll-like, elegant appearance.
The masked Zuang doesn't have that image.
A blood-covered beast that could pounce at any moment.
Death manifested before one's eyes.
A child who summons visions of one's life flashing before their eyes.
A monster in human form.
There's no room for elegance there.
That is, if the person is truly a returning senior student meeting her for the first time.

"You knew Zuang from before, didn't you?"
"Huh... wow."
"Step back, Dorothy."

Dorothy gasps in surprise while Rockfell blocks her path.
As Irene coldly presses for answers, the senior with strength unbefitting a returning student smiles coldly at the first-years.

"Not bad? The rumors that the 981st class is different weren't unfounded after all."
"You still haven't answered my question."
"If your guess is correct. If I've known Zuang from before. What difference would that make?"

Irene answered while concentrating cold energy in her palm behind her back.

"Depending on what relationship you had with Zuang, you might try to interfere with us or harm us."

The possibility was especially high when recalling Zuang from right after enrollment, when she was almost completely cut off from interactions with others.
After all, even in jest, she wasn't the type of person with a personality conducive to making friends, nor did she seem to have any desire to do so.

Clap. Clap. Clap.

The senior applauded.
Like a parent praising a child for a job well done.
Like expressing admiration after watching an excellent movie or play.
But what appeared on that face was an expressionless mask, devoid of any emotion.
It was truly an "assassin-like" expression.

"An excellent deduction. As expected of the Grand Duchess of the North, the barren land of Ma where demon contractors swarm, your vigilance is quite remarkable. You're qualified to follow Zuang's trail."
"..."
"Don't urge me with such a frightening face. Men get hurt, you know? When they receive a woman's contemptuous look."
"Are you mocking me?"
"Of course. It's fascinating when an ant, incomparably weaker than me, shows hostility without fear. Lingering before my eyes without realizing it could be crushed to death."

The cold energy in Irene's hand grew even more frigid, and Rockfell widened his stance by half a step.
However, neither of them dared to make the first move.
Just as Dorothy had her intuition, they both had the instinctive sense of combat-class professionals telling them:

Attacking would mean certain defeat.
The moment they made a move would be their last.

The senior casually put his hands in his pockets.
He leaned against the wall at an angle.
Unlike the tense first-years, despite voluntarily sealing his hands and adopting a sloppy posture that put him at a disadvantage, the students' wariness didn't diminish.
A strength that made posture meaningless.
An overwhelming power that could threaten their lives at any moment with any method.
They belatedly realized they had stepped into the domain of such a powerful individual without even noticing.

"There's something called the Ten Great Thieves of the Continent. It refers to the ten thieves with the most outstanding skills in the world. But what if there's someone who's just barely the 11th strongest and wants to join the Ten Great Thieves? What do you think they should do?"
"...They would have to steal. The reputation of one of the existing Ten Great Thieves for themselves."
"Correct. Zuang's master, Elder Rung, did just that. He stole the life of the previous generation's 10th ranked Life Thief among the Ten Great Thieves, my master."
"!!"
"In other words, you've encountered someone who bears a grudge against your classmate."

To think that a person harboring resentment toward Zuang would appear in this lawless zone exclusive to returning students.
Their luck was so bad it made them want to cry from the injustice.

"Those who inherit the techniques of Bininbujeon carry all the burdens of their master. The techniques, the reputation, even the chains of grudges borne by the master's name."
"..."
"Reclaiming the name of Life Thief by killing Elder Rung is the fate I live with. If she gets in my way, I'll have to kill Zuang too. But that day is not today."

No one dared to complain as the man lit a cigarette.
Despite his expressionless face, they could tell from his bulging veins on the back of his hand that the man was exercising "patience."

"I'm still weaker than Elder Rung. If I kill his disciple, I'll be killed next. So I don't resort to schemes. I let Zuang go."
"Should I apologize for my needless suspicions?"
"No need for pointless lies. Though I do appreciate them, killing you would be troublesome too."
"..."
"I simply neither showed malice nor offered kindness to Zuang. When she chose a path to catch up to that child Oknodie, I closed the easy path that we control."
"We...? Who else is here besides you?"
"The Cheonaedanbyeok Investigation Team."

As the man tapped the ash from his cigarette, a strange mana wave traveled through the ground.

Kugugung.

The mana wave dug deep into the sheer cliff below, creating a large hole between the rocks.
It would be more accurate to say that it removed an illusion blocking a hole that had existed all along, rather than forcibly digging through the existing soil.
But beyond the side path, human presences could be felt.
All of them seniors on par with Irene herself, some at levels difficult to distinguish in terms of superiority.

"There are several paths to cross beyond Cheonaedanbyeok. The higher the path you enter, the faster you can reach your destination, but the danger increases dramatically."
"!!"
"Zuang took the highest path. I'll give you clever juniors a choice. Will you risk the danger to follow Zuang, or use the lower, more convenient path that takes more time?"

A ladder appeared between the hole in Cheonaedanbyeok and the ground here, which hadn't been visible before.

"It's not an illusion or trick. And that path really does seem safe. Though I mean that 'relatively' speaking."

Even Dorothy with her good intuition vouched for this.
After hesitating, Irene spoke.

"Who was the last person to pass through here before Zuang?"
"They never returned."
"...Then I'll take that path."

The man's hand twitched.

"Why? I thought I'd warned you sufficiently about the danger. Couldn't you trust my kindness?"
"No. I believe your words. Though our conversation was brief, you've never lied, even if you've concealed some truths. So I can believe you. Including that Zuang is in danger."
"How foolish. Do you intend to become friends with an assassin?"
"Perhaps."
"Even if she doesn't want that herself?"
"It doesn't matter. The North isn't a place fortunate enough to be picky about making friends."
"How calculating. Almost too pragmatic to even call it friendship."

The man sighed in exasperation and put the cigarette back in his mouth.

"Go as you please. While I'm still letting you go."

Irene gave a small bow and ran toward the path on the cliff.
Both Dorothy and Rockfell followed behind her.
Only after they were far enough from the unpleasant senior did Dorothy express her doubts.

"Why did we come this way, leaving the safe path behind?"
"Because this is the fastest way to face the danger. Based on the circumstances, Zuang followed Oknodie faster than anyone else. So Oknodie must be ahead. I'm not worried about her. But I am worried about Zuang who's following her."
"Because unlike Oknodie, she could get hurt?"
"Yes. If Zuang gets seriously injured or dies because of this, and Oknodie finds out, she'll surely be sad. She'll start moving around making sure no one can follow her. Then our plan to recruit Oknodie to the Northern Front will fail too."

So they would save Zuang.

"It's a selfish, self-centered reason. It has nothing to do with you two. If you want to go back because it's dangerous, you can."
"Not really? This level of danger exists even in the Monster Forest. I was just worried about you, Irene. If you have a reason to go this far, I'm actually relieved!"
"..."

What on earth is in that Monster Forest of hers?
Pushing aside the thought that had crossed her mind several times today, Irene quickened her pace.
But it was only when they approached the third disaster that she truly realized:

"Zuang and Oknodie went in there...?"

The zone exclusive to returning students.
A place where countless returning students risk their lives to find treasures.
Up to the second disaster, even novices are fine.
Returning students seeking treasures.
A den where the weak can band together or deceive those weaker than themselves, forcing them with strength and extorting them to survive.
But from the "third disaster" onward, the level was different.
This was not a place where such weaklings could set foot.

━━━
[Erupting Lava]
A lava zone where extremely high-temperature flames condense on the ground and periodically surge upward.
The desire for treasures burned hot, and the collision of desires created a hell where no one could survive.
━━━

Those who haven't resolved themselves to die.
Those who don't intend to challenge for treasures.
Those who don't possess sufficient skill.
Such half-hearted individuals cannot proceed beyond this point.

"Aren't you going?"

As if countering what such half-heartedness even means, Dorothy sticks her finger into the hot steam at the entrance, cries out "Hot!" and makes a tearful face.
Seeing this pathetic sight, Irene grabbed Dorothy's hand and opened her mouth.

Whoosh.

Dorothy's throbbing finger stopped hurting as Irene's breath, colder than frost, enveloped it.

"Don't be reckless. I might not have the capacity to help you inside."
"O-okay..."
"Let's go. Before Zuang and Oknodie turn to ashes while we're dawdling."

That mere resolve to risk one's life.
For the three of them, it was something they had harbored long before it needed to be mentioned now.
