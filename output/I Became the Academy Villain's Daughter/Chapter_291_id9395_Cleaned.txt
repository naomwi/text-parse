# 291 - Graduate's Appeal

The Jigoku Pirate Crew was extremely flustered.

"What are you all doing here?"
"Don't misunderstand. We're not suspicious pirates."
"And we definitely weren't hiding in the food container to steal provisions."
"We absolutely had no plans to use Jigoku's influence to extort the Foundation's supplies and steal valuable items!"

These guys have truly become full-fledged pirates.
I stared blankly at them in disbelief as Jigoku, who ruled as the leader of these pathetic rookie pirates, came walking out with a yawn, looking completely unbothered.

"Oh. It's Gisele."
"You're quite bold. Planning to rob the Foundation's cruise ship? Why attempt to stow away when you could have just accepted Oknodie's invitation?"
"Are you stupid? If we accepted the invitation and walked through the front door saying 'thank you very much,' we'd immediately get caught in the Foundation's surveillance network. That would end our pirating career before it even began."

The ruthless pride of a pirate willing to rob even his friend's father's ship without exception!

"This actually works out well. I was just about to start a mutiny on this ship. Would you care to join us?"
"What? Why would you start a mutiny when you were properly invited?"
"Because starting a mutiny when you're on a cruise ship is common sense."
"??"

Jigoku's face showed utter bewilderment, as if wondering if a great age of piracy had begun without his knowledge.
Both parties were equally perplexed by each other.
Jigoku only found peace of mind after reaching the conclusion that anyone who was friends with Oknodie couldn't possibly be normal.

"If it's such a pirate-like endeavor, I can't refuse to help. This ship seemed too powerful to rob completely anyway."
"Then I'll share information about our forces, enemy positions, and the current battle situation."

Gisele quickly got to the point.

"Currently, we're expanding our defensive line to the corridors of the first, second, and third floors of the cruise ship before the path to the underground food storage narrows. The first floor is secured with Sandcooker's earth magic, the second floor with Irene's ice magic, and the third floor has combat specialists guarding both passages."
"Why bother blocking both sides? Just break through one side and focus everything on the other."
"We're preventing a multi-directional siege to keep the control room accessible from the third floor from falling into the hands of regular enemy forces rather than their officers."
"So it's a trap to draw out the enemy officers."
"The left side is defended by the Black Knight Mob and Bikini Armor Warrior Pyoi, while the right side is fortified under Arcadia's command with the rest of our forces. If your group could take over the right flank on the third floor, it would ease the pressure on our lines."

The pirates openly showed their displeasure at Gisele's clear battlefield explanation.

"How could we possibly handle a frontline battle?"
"That's right. How could third-rate pirate lackeys like us, who only cling to pirates to earn credits, possibly handle the Foundation's crew members?"
"We're cruise-ship-container-cargo-thieves, not frontline-mutiny-combat-specialists!"

The pathetic reality of Jigoku's already unreliable pirate crew!

"Gyahahat! See? If you entrust the frontline to guys like these, they'll collapse instantly."
"Jigoku, weren't you among the top fighters in the advanced class? I recall you holding your own against the Empire's Three Great Noble Families during the final exams."
"I learned something after spending a semester at the Academy."

Jigoku proudly declared:

"It's a waste of bullets to shoot at weaklings!"
"Are you openly declaring a strike... I wonder if you have any intention to cooperate at all."
"Your mercenary tactics are flawed from the start for trying to use pirates in a frontal assault. Pirates have their own way of operating."

Strong against the weak, weak against the strong.
Pirates are a species that only gets motivated when robbing those weaker than themselves.
A pirate's mercenary tactic is to identify the enemy's weakness, swoop in to grab the loot, and make a quick escape.

"Now, third-rate lackey pirates. Go rob the auditorium where the non-combat crew members are gathered!"

The third-rate apprentice pirate students hesitantly asked:

"Won't we get in trouble for threatening civilians?"
"We're outside the Academy, Captain."
"You might be fine outside since you were already a pirate, Jigoku, but we have homes and families to return to..."

—BANG!

Jigoku shot one of the students without hesitation, then casually twirled his smoking revolver with a bored expression.

"Do I look like I care?"
"..."
"If you're worried about your home and family, either quit or steal enough treasure for everyone's share. Make that decision like a man."

In an atmosphere where saying "I can't do it" would likely result in ending up next to the fallen student, no one dared to quit being a pirate.
Even the Foundation's crew members were terrified by this lunatic with nothing to lose, and readily handed over their valuables and money whenever a bag was thrust in front of them.

"Why are you all so quiet while pirating? Are you really pirates?"
"Woohoo! This is too easy!"
"Robbery is the best, and armed robbery is the best of all! Pirates rule!"

The apprentice pirate students hastily put on an exaggerated act in response to Jigoku's suspicious gaze.

"Got everything?"
"Yes sir!!"
"Let's go. Get up quickly, you idiot."

The student lying on the floor said:

"But I got shot?"
"That was a rubber bullet."
"Ah, I see."

The student got up awkwardly and joined his comrades in carrying the loot bags.

'We've been tricked!'

The crew members belatedly realized they had completely fallen for Jigoku's [Intimidation][Deception] skills, but by then all their valuables had long been cleaned out into the bags.
The Jigoku Pirate Crew proudly returned to their container in the underground storage after leaving the auditorium.
One apprentice pirate crew member suddenly asked a question:

"Captain. How are we going to take all this loot back to the Academy?"

Since they couldn't possibly take everything off the ship anyway.

"You'll have to sort through it carefully."

Fortunately, they had students who had taken the useful "Developing an Eye" course for situations like this.

"Hey, Titosoga. Didn't you take the Developing an Eye course with Oknodie and Isabel?"
"Y-yes..."
"Come help us sort through the valuable items. You're not doing anything since you lost the spotlight anyway."
"Why are you scaring the poor kid?"
"Who said I'm making him work for free? I'll give him some of the leftover jewels."
"Actually, Titosoga is kind of cute when he's scared."
"Isabeeeel!?"

Though Isabel and Titosoga weren't reliable in combat, their discerning eyes and dexterity were quite remarkable.
From the pile of loot that had been haphazardly gathered and dumped on the floor, they sorted jewels with jewels, cards with cards, categorizing by type and arranging them in order of value.

[Mana Puzzle - Damage 3%]
[Gold Plating - Fake]

Isabel and Titosoga sorted everything so expertly they could pass for professional appraisers!
But even after sorting, there was still too much.
Having robbed an auditorium full of non-combat crew members from among thousands of crew members, they naturally ended up with a large quantity of items.

"There are too many expensive things to leave behind."
"Gisele! Let us borrow your magic backpack."
"I'm sorry, but all the space in my backpack is already allocated, so I can't help you. Let's think of another solution."

Gisele, who hadn't even had a chance to rest after repelling the attack, was trying to select the most valuable items when he spotted a blacksmith with a hammer among them.

"Who is that person mixed in with our students? The uniform looks like that of a cruise ship crew member."

A student who had returned from defending the frontline with Arcadia recognized the blacksmith and introduced him.

"Ah. We haven't introduced him to you yet, Gisele. This is Senior Ferguson, a 982nd batch graduate of our Academy and a senior from the Production Department. He's someone who uses the Empire's forbidden technology, <Reinforcement>."
"...Did I hear that correctly?"

Gisele became serious.
Jigoku didn't understand his reaction.

"Gyahahat! We can compress the loot with reinforcement and take it with us. You guys should have told me about this great method earlier."

While the pirates were excited about this idea that would increase the items' liquidity, Gisele couldn't share their joy.
The student who had introduced Ferguson asked nervously:

"Do you dislike reinforcement? Because of the Empire's scrutiny...?"
"That's not the issue. I was originally a black market dealer, so I'm not one to fear the law. But that man is someone to be feared."

Gisele pointed out what everyone had failed to notice.

"Do you remember which batch we are?"
"Um... the 981st batch, right?"
"Correct. And that senior claims to be a graduate of the 982nd batch."
"Huh???"
"That blacksmith, Ferguson, is claiming to be a senior who will graduate from the Academy next year—a year that hasn't even come yet. He's an unidentified serial reinforcement maniac."

Even Jigoku, who had been nonchalantly twirling his revolver with a bored expression, dropped his weapon in shock.
