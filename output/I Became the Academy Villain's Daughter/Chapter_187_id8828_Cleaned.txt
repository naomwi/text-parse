# 187 - Revenge

Isabel leaned against the bedpost, staring blankly out the window.

Flutter, flutter. A butterfly leisurely playing.
Woof, woof, woof! A flame hound barking diligently.
Oh dear, oh dear. Third-year students chasing after it pitifully.

Despite her busy days filled with assignments and exam preparations, she never missed Oknodie's meals, making sure to prepare them once a day.
Recently, she'd even started packing snack boxes, investing even more time.

'Was I too greedy?'

During combat practice, her consciousness suddenly faded, and when she opened her eyes, she was crawling on the floor.
It was a severe injury that could have permanently cost her an arm had it happened outside the Academy.
Thanks to the top-level medical facilities, her severed arm was now properly reattached to her body.

'Not that I can really feel it though.'

Even when she wiggled, her hand inside the cast just felt stuffy.
Was it really attached?
Would her arm fall off if the cast was removed?
Were the nerves 100% properly connected?
Her heart pounded involuntarily.

'It's about time I took a break.'

There were days like this before.
During her time with the Esonia Adventure Group, after finishing an adventure, she would collapse in exhaustion, her body battered.
The scent of soup tickling her nose.
The gentle tickle of warm sunlight.
With sparrows perched on windowsills in back alleys, cats on walls, and drunkards yelling in the streets as background, she would rest until the door opened.

"Isabel. How is it? The adventurer's life. Is it manageable?"

The captain with his rough beard.
A hedgehog-like man with the bad habit of pricking skin with his beard.
The feeling of that scratchy beard, the touch of his large, rough palms that could swallow her fist whole, the sensation of that deep, reassuring voice tickling her eardrums.
All gone now.
The same rest and freedom as back then, but now there's no one to take care of her.

"Hey. Are you feeling better?"

'No "people," that is.'
Since beastkin aren't people, they don't count.

"A hospital visit? That's unexpectedly thoughtful of you."
"Would I really seem so heartless that I wouldn't visit someone I hang around with all the time?"
"What happened to the girl who got hurt with me?"
"Seems that bikini armor has surprisingly good defense. She avoided vital spots and you were hurt much worse, so don't worry."
"I'm sorry, Oknodie. You were so busy, and I caused you to worry because of me."
"Aren't you sorry to me too?"
"Would you like me to be?"
"Sigh. The problem with this Academy is that the women are too strong-willed. I can't assert myself at all."

Son O-cheon was grumbling when a nurse chased him out, saying his voice was too loud and citing patient stability.
The hospital room grew quiet.
In her childhood, she enjoyed these silent moments thinking of the captain, but now they weren't welcome.
Perhaps that's why she became so immersed in cooking.
Bubble, bubble.
Chop, chop, chop.
The sounds of cooking helped keep unwanted memories at bay.

Rustle.
Step. Step.

When she sensed the careful presence.
Perhaps she smiled quietly at that sound for the same reason.

"Come in, Oknodie. Don't hang dangerously from the windowsill."
"...How did you know?"
"That's just how well we know each other."

Boasting made her feel a bit better.
Oknodie had a habit of treating windows like doors, climbing right over the sill.
She smelled of sunlight, different from the fluffy bedding—perhaps from touching the sun-warmed bricks as she climbed.

"Isabel. How did you get hurt?"
"They say I collapsed from fatigue during combat practice. I haven't been sleeping well lately. And I seem to feel the cold more."

Oknodie's face filled with guilt.

"Is it because of me?"
"Why would you say that?"
"Because you're tired from making my lunchboxes and don't have enough time to sleep. You don't have to make me food anymore, so please don't get sick."

Suddenly, Isabel remembered what Hestia had told her about Oknodie's friend.
There was a friend from Oknodie's Foundation trainee days whose body was lost and trapped in a plant, and Oknodie had pulled out just the soul to save it.

-She's been through a lot emotionally. Even without being told, you've probably done well, but please protect her from getting hurt by Academy life.

I'm sorry, Hestia.
I wanted to protect her too, but it seems I've already hurt her.

"It's not because of you, Oknodie. It's all because I was too greedy."
"...Alright. Isabel doesn't need to do anything, so please just rest well."

Was it just her imagination that Oknodie's retreating back somehow seemed frightening?

"Oknodie?"
"Yes, Isabel."
"I'd like you to remain a good child."
"Of course. I am a good child!"
"...Right. Take care of yourself."

Liar.
Isabel thought as she watched Oknodie leave the room.
Whatever happens is going to be serious.

'Where is she planning to vent her anger?'

If anyone was at fault, it would be herself for not taking care of her body.
Surely Oknodie wasn't planning to physically assault the opposing team from the combat practice?

"There's no space left in the infirmary."
"Set up temporary tents!"
"We're short on blood. Even with body acceleration, the blood loss exceeds production!"

Exactly 16 hours after Oknodie left.
The ominous premonition came true spectacularly.
The medical wing where Isabel was hospitalized was filled with so many new patients that they spilled outside, with an enormous number of injuries occurring.

'Oknodie... what on earth have you done?'

Isabel became afraid.
Afraid of not being able to stop Oknodie from walking down the wrong path.


* * *


I could tell as soon as I saw Isabel's condition.
This wasn't an accidental mistake caused by low stamina.

'Traces of mana remain.'

Every mana carries its own unique properties.
Magic from coastal village mages carries the scent of the sea, while magic from vagabond mages in filthy back alleys reeks of foul odors.
The scent embedded in the soul, the scenery held in the heart—these unconsciously mix into the magic.

[You have detected Professor Rave's mana residue from the traces left on Isabel's body.]
[Magical Studies experience +30]
[Tracking experience +10]
[Discernment experience +10]

Imperial professors who always have at least one unfair pattern per person.
I remember all their mana residues.
I don't know how they go astray and start being unfair.
I don't want to know either.
And I don't need to know.
If I'm attacked, I pay it back.
I just follow my own principles.

Professor Rave's lecture <Fundamentals and Understanding of Imperial Magic>.
Unlike the crude mana studies of the frontier, Imperial magic has one characteristic.
Enhancing power through the "specialization" of mana attributes.

For instance, with water creation magic.
If you increase cohesion to create a spherical form that envelops someone's face, it becomes <Suffocation Water Sphere> magic.
If you penetrate biomana and create water in the lungs, it becomes <Pulmonary Edema Induction> magic.

Magic that could save someone dying of thirst can also become magic that kills someone.

"I heard Pyoi collapsed with Isabel too."
"Wow. You even came to visit? You really are a good child!"
"...Not really. Today I'm planning to be a bad child."

Pyoi, another injured person I visited to confirm.

"Did you also feel cold before you collapsed?"
"Isn't that normal? Like feeling chills before you realize you're dying."

After hearing Pyoi's story about when she collapsed, I was finally certain.

[You have identified the magic used by Professor Rave.]
[Magical Studies experience +30]
[Tracking experience +10]
[Discernment experience +10]

The identity of the magic he used was <Temperature Conversion> magic.
The specialization used was <Rapid>.
Additional formulas were <Target Designation>, <Volatility>.
The resulting magic cast was 4th Circle <Designated Rapid Cooling>.
I could tell he had precisely deployed magic that quickly freezes a specific place, person, or substance, targeting only Isabel and Pyoi.
It makes them feel intense cold in an instant, freezing their bodies, and in severe cases, activating the sympathetic nervous system to raise blood pressure and cause collapse.
He activated the magic for an extremely brief moment, leaving traces that naturally disappear over time, making it impossible for investigators to catch Professor Rave even if they launched an investigation later.
Even if caught, what he used was simply temperature conversion magic.
If he denies it, there's no way to prove his guilt.
Truly an ingenious assassination attempt!

'Professor Rave... you've crossed the line.'

His method was good.
Threatening lives without using lethal magic.
Stealthy and difficult to trace.
Ingenious magic that allows deniability even if discovered.
But he touched someone he shouldn't have.

'I'll pay you back. I'll make your precious students feel the pain too!'


* * *


The healing mage at the infirmary couldn't hide her frustration in front of the tight-lipped Imperial students.

"Oh my. You stubborn fools. If you don't tell me how you got injured, you're only making things difficult for yourselves!"

The infirmary isn't some unlicensed treatment room in a dark alley where they'll heal you without asking questions about where you got your injuries or who the client is, as long as you pay.
They need to record detailed information about the student's identity, cause of injury, prescribed magic, and treatment progress.

-How dare you touch our child!
-Our child's lifespan has been shortened because they received unnecessary regeneration magic because of you. You'll taste the bitterness of the Imperial Supreme Court, mark my words!

While this is partly to prepare for possible treatment side effects...
The main purpose is to maintain treatment records for the healers' self-defense against lawsuits from parents!

"Leave this to me."
"Professor Rave. Do you know something about this? According to the system, all these children are students in your class..."
"Enough. I've come to assess the situation myself, so go have a meal while I investigate."

Professor Rave skillfully gave the healer some points and sent her away. Once the healer left, the students' expressions changed, and he asked them:

"Tell me what happened. Every single detail, without omission."

The tightly sealed lips of the Imperial students finally opened.
