# 282 - Desert Island Auction

What made Oknodie's claim so absurd was many things, but what perplexed Gisele most was this:

"Is it really okay to discuss a mutiny right next to the butler?"

If that person alone went wild, all the students on the ship would be tied up with rope like dried fish and locked away in a row!

"Don't worry. I am a butler and foundation representative who came to assist My Lady. I will not harm any of you directly with my own hands."
"Did you hear that?"
"Then we might have a chance. How exactly do you plan to carry out this mutiny?"
"I'll threaten to turn all the crew members' fingers, toes, and blood into <Points> if they don't steer the ship to a port with a transfer portal!"

Is this a mutiny or the beginning of the Dark Princess's evil journey?

"When will you do it?"
"After the point auction ends!"
"How many days until the auction?"
"One day!"

In Gisele's mind, routes, travel speeds, and sailing distances quickly took shape.
An ultra-luxury cruise ship equipped with a massive mana engine, heading to a deserted island three days away.
It shortened what would normally be a 14-day journey (avoiding dangerous sea monsters) to just three days on an ordinary large vessel.
This required exceptional navigational skills, top-tier ship equipment, and premium mana stones.
It proved that the Wiheomhae Foundation's personnel weren't just there to fill headcount or staff various facilities.

'It's somewhat frightening. Their level of preparation.'

Even the black market merchants could imitate something similar if they pooled their resources.
They had enough money to acquire a ship, and with money, gathering people was possible.
But finding items of the highest quality and trustworthy experts wasn't easy.
Wherever money gathered, there would inevitably be incompetent but greedy power-holders, con artists, and hostile organizations with ulterior motives.
The fact that the foundation, with its numerous enemies, could perfectly navigate a cruise ship of this scale was further proof of its strength.

"I've asked before, but I must ask again. How exactly do you know this information, My Lady?"

When Jonna questioned her, Oknodie tried to brush it off with an awkward laugh, but Jonna maintained a stern expression, saying this wouldn't work twice.
The little lady's pleading glances were subtly directed at Gisele, but he coldly ignored them.
Gisele was equally curious.

"Um... well... I saw it secretly."
"Where exactly?"
"In the navigation room."

Jonna paused before asking again.

"Are you saying you deceived my eyes, infiltrated the highest security navigation room, obtained information, and escaped?"
"Hehe."

...My Lady has become the Academy's spy.
Unforgivable!

Gisele had his hand in his backpack, ready to counter-attack in case Jonna charged at them with such accusations, but the butler's reaction was much more straightforward.

"You must have studied very hard at the Academy. You should be more proud of yourself, My Lady."

The butler's education was just as peculiar as Oknodie's response, and in the end, Gisele was the only one bewildered by the incomprehensible generosity of the foundation's people.


* * *


With information that only one day remained until landing on the deserted island, Gisele sold this information directly to the students.
Thanks to this, with just one day left, all students were completely absorbed in collecting points.

Booooooom—

As the ship's horn sounded and small boats were lowered from the cruise ship for the island landing, students who had purchased boarding passes with points gathered closely.
Of the 128 students, only 12 boarded the boats.
They were either representatives who had points pooled from their small groups or individuals with exceptional abilities.

"Didn't the princess go bankrupt?"
"Haaah~? As if I'd drop out that easily♡ There are so many crew members weaker than me♡"
"...I feel sorry for the crew members."

The noble Arcadia, daughter of a count, and the Empire's Second Princess Mesugakki.

"Oh, gorilla woman. I thought I had a monopoly on points earned through physical labor, but where did you get your points?"
"By setting new records in the training room."

The physically powerful Son O-cheon and Hestia.

"I won't waste the points everyone gathered for me."
"How boring. Then you'll divide whatever you win at the auction by N too, right?"
"You're one to talk. You also had points pooled from other kids."
"I didn't have them pooled. I'm the strongest, so I extorted them by force, offering to stand in for them."
"...Is that something you should be proudly admitting?"

Mob and Jaku, who had collected points from the weak.

"Bikini Warrior fighting!"
"Take me! Waaah."
"Thank you everyone~! I'll win at the auction and show you the greatness of bikini armor!"

Pyoi, who had gathered points by creating followers through seduction amidst the chaos.

"I'll split it with you, so stop nagging."
"Promise? You promise?"
"Oh, come on."

Rojini, the dark horse from the lower class.

"There are quite a few competitors."
"Don't let your guard down. Unlike us, they might join forces if necessary."

The hero Ishtar and the saintess Yupi.

"Where did Zuang get her points from?"
"The bathroom."
"...?"
"When people are desperate, they won't hesitate to spend points to pass through the corridor to the bathroom."
"Wow. That's amazing! I should try that when we get back to the Academy!"

The ruthless Oknodie and Zuang completed the group of twelve students.
Butler Jonna and Maid Leape each drove one boat as escorts for the twelve students.

"I hope they'll at least treat me like a slave. I'll give Countess Arcadia something to bite into."
"Hmph. This desert island event isn't for dealing with such trivial matters!"

The deserted island was far too large for fourteen people, including the butler and maid.
As soon as they landed on the island and watched the boats depart, Mob asked with a blank face:

"When does the auction start?"
"At this time every day."
"What? Did you come here knowing nothing?"
"Everyone just pooled their points when they heard about the auction..."

Jaku looked at Mob's armor with pitying eyes.
What foolishness to wear heavy armor on a hot, humid deserted island while participating in an auction where only one item would be revealed each day over several days.

"What's with that look suddenly? Staring at me with that unpleasant expression."
"Give up if it's too hard."
"Ha. As if I'd give up on a secret auction where treasures from the foundation might appear."

He had endured without using any of the luxurious facilities or delicacies, receiving everyone's help to get this far.
He wouldn't give up so easily.
Mob was full of enthusiasm.

Grumble.

Until his stomach growled.
He stuffed black bread, which he had brought for emergencies, into the food intake port of his helmet that only opened during mealtimes, chewing it roughly. The salt from the sea breeze permeated it.
The worst black bread—hard with terrible texture, and salty when dissolved with saliva.
Of course, the drinking water was limited to what he had brought.
At this rate, he couldn't afford to waste energy on training.

Clank clank

Mob set out to search for food with determined steps.

Clank clank...

He sat down on the ground with weakened steps and sighed.

"Rojini, you crazy woman... what the hell are you doing..."

Before his eyes, crimson vegetation burned.
Fruit-bearing trees collapsed in real-time as they burned.
The red mage Rojini responded shamelessly:

"I'm eliminating competition. If you don't want to starve to death, give up on the auction. If you didn't bring enough food and water to prepare for this situation, you don't deserve to enjoy the auction."

Students who had survived at the Academy by learning various survival techniques, even without being heroes!
Rojini was no exception.

"I'll take the first day's item out of sheer stubbornness."

Mob persevered tenaciously for a day.
He was determined to win at least one item, even if it meant spending all of his 250,000 points.

"I will now reveal the first item of the desert island auction. Today's item is <A Box of Black Bread>."
"I withdraw."

His determination ended as quickly as it had begun.


* * *


Jaku sensed that things had become troublesome.
He had already predicted Rojini's fire attack.
He understood the importance of food and water and had stocked sufficient supplies in his backpack.
Other students who had obtained information would have prepared adequately as well.
But now black bread was being offered as an item.
They were selling supplies for students who weren't prepared for a long battle.
Though it tasted terrible, having it might allow someone to last a day longer in the auction than others.

'That's only if I have points to spare for such things.'

He didn't know how valuable the upcoming items would be or how many points his competitors had brought.
It was a shame to spend points thinking about the long game.
If he made a mistake, he might end up returning to the ship with just a pile of black bread.
Having extorted a certain percentage of points from scholarship students to participate, returning empty-handed would make even the Primer doubt his abilities and look down on him.

"The starting bid is 1,000 points. Bids will increase by 1,000. Is there anyone who wishes to participate?"

Jaku made his move.

"Jaku, 10,000 points."
"10,000?"
"For mere black bread?"

Jaku had a winning strategy.

'Since this auction allows you to see the next day's item for each day you survive on the island, more valuable items will likely appear later.'

This meant that the longer one could endure, the more valuable auction items they could participate in.
To obtain truly precious items, participation wasn't determined by points but by how much food and water one had secured.
If everyone except him gave up due to hunger and thirst, he could monopolize all the later items at minimum points.

'Except for Gisele, who might have hidden an unknown amount of food in his magic backpack, I have no competitors.'

Just as he was confident in his auction victory strategy, Jonna, who was acting as the auctioneer, spoke:

"Leape, 20,000 points."
"...!"

The foundation's new tail.
Assassin maid Leape sneered at Jaku with contemptuous eyes.
Jaku bit his lip tightly and raised his hand.

"Jaku, 30,000 points."
"Leape, 40,000 points."
"Jaku, 50,000 points."

The points kept rising relentlessly.
Zuang, who was watching, spoke to Oknodie, who was putting pebbles in her mouth and spitting them out one by one.

"Aren't you going for that strategy, Oknodie?"
"The late-game strategy? No way!"
"Planning to get food from Gisele?"
"That's part of it, but if worst comes to worst, I can just steal from whoever wins the black bread!"
"..."

This declaration was enough to completely deflate Jaku's enthusiasm for the auction.
