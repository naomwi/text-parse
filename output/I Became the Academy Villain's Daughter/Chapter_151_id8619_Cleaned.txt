# 151 - Birthday Chosen by Jonna

As the sun set, the students who had been waiting in the visiting room gradually left with gloomy faces.

"Oknodie. Why don't you head back too?"
"Jonna promised he'd come. If I wait just a little longer, he'll definitely be here!"

A junior class student spoke with a worried expression.
His face seemed somewhat familiar.

"You've forgotten who I am, haven't you?"
"Hehe. Sorry."
"Maximus Mont Blanc. I told you my sister Max and the baby were helped at the airship dock."
"Ah, right."
"Max was planning to come too, but for some reason, many guardians who were supposed to visit couldn't make it, not just her."

It's unfortunate.
The Academy must distribute visiting tickets to the guardians of enrolled students.
Some guardians can't visit the Academy because they failed to receive their tickets, lost them for some reason, or had them taken by someone else.
But Jonna losing his ticket?
No matter how I think about it, it doesn't make sense.
Jonna isn't that careless, nor is he the type of incompetent butler who would just wait around blankly until the deadline passes.

"Hmph. Don't talk about Jonna if you don't know him."
"Sniff, you poor thing...!"

Despite my confident assertion, Maximus is busy wiping tears with the back of his hand.

"If you don't meet your butler tomorrow, come to the store. I'll buy you something for lunch."
"Wow, really?"

Still, I have no intention of telling him about Jonna's good qualities.
My butler.
The only trustworthy ally with 100% loyalty in the game world.
My exclusive possession who will never betray me.
What kind of person he is, what he's good at—I can't let others know. They might covet him and try to make him their butler.

"I'm bored. I'm hungry. This is a waste of time."

But it's getting difficult.
Being the only one left in the visiting room, stubbornly holding my position.
Watching my shadow tilt as the indoor lights flicker.

"Jonna... you're really coming, right...?"

Muttering to myself out of anxiety.
Even beginning to doubt whether Jonna's loyalty is really 100%.

"..."

Even accepting that games and reality are different.
Even feeling that I might just be an ordinary girl, not a special player.

I've reached my limit.

I clutched the whistle and awkwardly blew it. Wheek.
A messy sound with too much force causing the note to leak.
Normally, practice makes perfect.
The more you blow, the better you should get.
But somehow, I seemed to be getting worse.
It felt like a lump formed in my chest, and sadness welled up.

Come quickly.
Why did you make me wait so long?
With less than 10 minutes until the visiting room closes, tears welled up as I sniffled, calling that lying, wicked butler who hadn't shown his face.

"You're mean..."

What should I do if the caretaker comes to close the visiting room?
Should I use "Hiding" somewhere to conceal myself?
But then Jonna wouldn't be able to enter either—what then?
Should I use "Lock Picking" to open the entrance?
But what if they lock it from the outside?
Is it even possible to hide without being caught?
As I was seriously pondering these concerns, the smell of blood suddenly filled the air.

Tuk tuk.
Tu-du-duk.

Footsteps approaching, dripping something like rain.

Step. Step.

Dignified footsteps that never show urgency anywhere, steps that exude class.
Footsteps that make me want to hide behind curtains, behind wardrobes, always wanting to hide whenever I hear them.

Creeeeak.

When the door opened, I hid again today.

"I apologize, my lady. I was caught in an accident on the way, which delayed me."
"I missed you, Jonna!"

Not behind the curtain, not behind the wardrobe.
Behind the face of a good child with a bright smile.


* * *


"How did you get hurt like this?"
"There was negligence on the Academy's part."
"Let's go to the infirmary first!"

His appearance was terrible, with blood seeping from burst capillaries all over his body.
I was worried whether a person could survive after losing so much blood.

"It's fine. The bleeding has already stopped."

As Jonna took a deep breath with a look of realization, the bleeding truly stopped completely.

"Wow, amazing!"

So a first-class butler can stop bleeding just by willing it.
Jonna is truly an all-capable butler who can do anything!

"Still, your clothes are soaked with blood, so we should stop by the laundry room!"
"Hmph. Who do you think has been washing your sweat-soaked training uniforms and dirt-covered clothes from hiding in all sorts of places?"
"Don't tell me you're going to hand-wash with those injuries?!"
"...There's a modern convenience called cleaning magic."

<Life Magic - Clean>

A household magic that removes blood stains, dust, and various waste materials from clothes—a favorite among homemakers.
Though it has a high activation cost, it's essential magic for comfortable living.
If used incorrectly, it can remove decorations from clothes, cause discoloration, or even make medals attached to clothes or contents in pockets evaporate—a truly fearsome magic.

"Ah, this smell!"
"Just a small talent."

The Clean magic was enhanced with the pleasant scent of fabric softener.
This is practically a cheat key.
Regardless of gender, how could anyone not be attracted to someone with this smell—no, fragrance?

"Didn't Leape come with you? What have you two been doing? Did you miss me? Aren't you hungry? Have you been eating your meals properly?"

As I fired questions like a machine gun, Jonna raised the corners of his mouth just slightly with a grim face.
Even after not seeing him for a long time, that eerie smile makes my warm feelings disappear and sends chills down my spine.

"Leape was quite regretful too. I brought her gift for you instead. Don't worry, I've already obtained permission for possession and consumption."

A basket covered with white cloth adorned with red flowers.
Given Jonna's preference for cleanliness and his use of Clean magic, he wouldn't have left blood on the cloth unless he forgot.
Inside were a pouch full of candies, a memorization set of various sizes and shapes, a musical instrument, and a textbook.

"Wow. What's all this?"
"Leape's gifts. Considering your growth rate, she brought improved-flavor candies, assassination weapons necessary for Academy life, and a secret musical instrument."
"I see. So I hold this handle and strike with it like this? Like this!"

When I grabbed the neck of the mini-violin-like instrument and swung it around, Jonna raised his hand and stopped the swinging instrument in mid-air with just one finger.
His superhuman strength conveyed an unwavering comfort and stability reminiscent of a Simmons bed.

"The details are in the textbook, but please be careful as reckless swinging could cut your hand or cause the instrument to explode."
"...What? The instrument can explode??"
"I don't want to spoil the joy of learning, so I'll refrain from saying more. Rather than talking about myself, I'd like to hear about you—about your life at the Academy."
"I wrote to you about it in my letters."
"I believe you have much more to say than what could be contained in letters."
"Hehe. That's true."

It was strange.
Since becoming a child, I often found myself nodding off at night as drowsiness overtook me.
But tonight, as I excitedly talked about everything that had happened, I didn't feel sleepy even once.
Hehe.
Is this the power of the strong bond formed by 100% loyalty between a player and butler?

"I threw a stone with a swoosh, and the wall crumbled with a crash, so before anyone found out, I pulled out a nearby tree and planted it there, and no one knows there was originally a wall there!"
"In the cafeteria, it was hard to eat because some mean kids kept calling me bad, but Isabel makes delicious food, so I don't go to the cafeteria anymore, which is great!"
"And you know what? Tito-soga sometimes gets really brave, and if someone speaks ill of me, his eyes suddenly become sharp like Leape's, and he hits people with the lighting stand!"

As I was enthusiastically sharing stories, suddenly it became twice as dark as if night had deepened.
Looking up, I realized Jonna had raised his arm and placed his hand on my head.
His hand, which seemed to summon darkness that obscured even starlight, felt quite comfortable and pleasant, making me smile, but Jonna didn't look very happy.

"Jonna. What's wrong?"
"It's nothing. I just thought we should start obtaining support from the foundation in earnest."
"Will you give me points too?"
"Rather than that, I'm looking for ways to directly help your Academy life. I might seek cooperation from your friends as well."
"Ah, could it be a surprise birthday party? You don't need to throw me a surprise party!"
"Come to think of it, I don't recall hearing about it. When is your birthday, my lady?"
"My birthday?"
"According to Academy rules, guardians can provide special gifts to students on their birthdays, so this could be helpful."

Birthday, huh.
This is troubling.
Hmm.
I bit my finger and tried hard to remember.
But the answer was already determined.

"I don't know!"

How would I know the birthday when I'm not even a playable character with established settings, but someone who possessed an unfamiliar character's body?
As expected.
Muttering briefly, as if wondering what kind of pitiful human I am, the butler's face concealed emotions behind an expressionless mask, reminding me of the days spent at Jonna's mansion, which made me smile.

"If it's too much trouble, would you like to decide for me?"
"Decide... your birthday, my lady?"
"A birthday is a special day, right? Special enough to celebrate."

If it's such a special day.

"A birthday chosen by Jonna would be even more special."

Leaning my weight against Jonna's broad, firm embrace, the words came out naturally.
Not something I thought about before saying, but a spontaneous true feeling, thrust like a dagger.
Looking up with wide eyes to meet Jonna's gaze, I saw a small but significant disturbance in his face.
