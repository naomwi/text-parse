# <425 - Senior's Deal Condition>

The Grim Reaper's workshop revealed the owner's taste right from the entrance.

━━━
[Door of Life Plunder]
Those who cannot open the door shall become travelers of no return.
━━━

While the Workshop Street tests qualifications with artifact-grade relics, the Grim Reaper's workshop only grants visitor status to those who pass through the Door of Life Plunder.
A test of pride declaring that Workshop Street's standards alone aren't enough to recognize customers!

"Senior, which way do you want to enter? If you put your palm in the hole in the door, it will drain a large amount of life force, but you'll definitely get in. If you grab the doorknob, you'll have to overcome the suction magic to remove your hand!"

The Plant Club senior made a grumpy face and pretended to kick.
As expected of a third-year senior, his rebellious nature of disliking predetermined paths was impressive.
Yet his clumsiness in immediately stepping on landmines explains why he's not a fourth-year, doesn't it?

"If you kick it, the door will charge back at an even faster speed. Can you handle that?"

What kind of door attacks people?
At my incredulous look, I picked up a nearby stone and threw it at the door panel to demonstrate.

BANG!!

The part of the door hit by the stone shot out like a rocket, striking the empty space where the stone had been thrown from.
Even though I had activated <Quick Movement> immediately after throwing the stone, the speed was frighteningly swift!

"Well, the method isn't wrong. But for a person to pass through, you'd need to hit it really hard, and the larger the impact area, the fiercer the door's counterattack. Could you manage both evasion and breakthrough in a split second?"

The senior shook his head, face turning pale blue.
In the end, he obediently inserted his palm into the hole, had his life force drained extensively, and his face turned sallow.
Perhaps because he usually eats plenty of good food, he paid the large amount of life force the Door of Life Plunder demanded all at once.
As the senior trudged through the open door, now was my chance!

<Quick Movement>
<Forward Leap>
<Stealth>
<Boldness>

Just before the Door of Life Plunder closed itself after the senior passed through, I charged forward like a bullet.
Back in my <Strength-All-In One-Hit Character I Love Marine> days, I would forcibly kidnap party members, make them put their palms in the hole, throw them through, and charge in together with a crash!
But now with my smaller build, I could slip through the gap between the door and the senior without having to body-slam him.

"Free ride success!"

The senior, who had been trembling, threw punches at me.
As I lightly dodged them with swoosh swoosh sounds, laughter erupted from inside.

"This customer has sense. That wickedness of exploiting your companion, I quite like it."

[Grim Reaper's favorability has increased by 5.]

The workshop where the sound of hammer forging weapons had ceased.
The Grim Reaper senior, who had been contemplating with folded arms over an unfinished scythe, welcomed us.

"Are you worried about crafting materials?"
"I've gathered almost everything, but I'm a bit short."
"What are you missing?"
"Winter eye flower. Sun jewel. Honey watermelon seeds."
"Wow. You've gathered almost everything!"
"15-strength Farmer's Scythe. Sigh of the Field Mouse. New Moon's Membrane. Nasher's Claw. Giant Golem's Saw Blade. Eternal Snow Essence. Fallen Angel's Feather. High Blue Metal. World Tree Branch. Kraken's Tentacle."
"...Wow. You've barely gathered anything!"

Why is this senior here when he should be out collecting materials?
It seems this senior isn't following the regular story progression but is caught in an NPC growth-blocking event, commonly known as <Sim-ma>.

"Assassination. Slaughter. Crafting. What request have you come with?"
"I'm here to sell profitable crafting materials!"
"What materials."
"Ttoontoon plants and Spider plants! I have over twenty stems!"
"Impressive that they've grown so much."

Ttoontoon plants and Spider plants that grow individually would normally wither and die in a natural ecosystem before they can grow.
This happens because all surrounding plants instinctively sense that a natural predator has appeared.
They steal nutrients, block every drop of water, and don't even allow sunlight.
It's no wonder Ttoontoon plants and Spider plants are classified as ultra-rare plant mobs.

"Distance?"
"I brought them to the Academy to sell!"
"Show me the goods."
"Hey now. Senior, you're a fourth-year, how could I show you right away? Instead, I brought one stem of each."

I showed him the stems I had broken off right after planting the seeds, which I'd kept for crafting materials.
After confirming the items were genuine, the Grim Reaper senior nodded.

"Ttoontoon plants and Spider plants are excellent crafting materials that can be exchanged for several ingredients. The price?"
"One thousand points per stem, and I have forty!"
"I'll pay only one-tenth as a deposit. Guide me. If the information proves true, I'll pay the rest."

Although the senior only paid 4,000 points out of the 40,000, neither I nor the Grim Reaper senior were desperate enough to scam over this amount.
Since he didn't nitpick to lower the price, the cool transaction happened faster than expected.

"But senior, what worries do you have that keep you in the workshop when you're missing so many materials?"
"The subordinates I sent to break branches from the World Tree have gone silent."
"Oh no!"
"Those who went to get the Kraken's tentacles are also out of contact.
I don't know why, but it feels like the whole world is conspiring against me, preventing materials from being gathered."

Whoever is responsible is truly a terrible person.
To dishearten a senior as brave and patient as to take Professor Sadako's lectures!
The Plant Club senior, who could speak again now that we'd left the Workshop Street, carefully asked:

"If I compromise on materials, the achievement of Reaper Slaying will be further away, but I must also compromise with reality..."
"Excuse me, but why do you want to slay a reaper? Isn't that the Death Messenger employed by the Death God, considered one of the foremost among the 12 Evil Gods?"
"If by Death Messengers you mean those fools who collect lifespans to deliver to their master, then yes, we're talking about the same beings."

The Grim Reaper senior was a fourth-year senior with ambitious aspirations.

"Originally, the karma of life circulates through the food chain and ecosystem. The Thousand-Poison Frog eats the Hundred-Poison Snake, and the Ten-Thousand-Poison Insect eats the Thousand-Poison Frog. But if the Ten-Thousand-Poison Insect survives eternally, the accumulated poison would become a calamity that devours the entire world."

Because that much poison would transform the soil into a land of death just by breathing.
Someone had to kill the Ten-Thousand-Poison Insect to save nature before it became sick and collapsed.

"The reapers employed by the Death God eliminate beings that would become Ten-Thousand-Poison Insects in the natural world. Unfortunately, that includes humans who seek transcendence."
"If humans achieve transcendence and become gods, wouldn't that be good for the world's principles to increase by one?"
"The Death God considers the birth of such transcendent beings as the calamity of the Ten-Thousand-Poison Insect that sickens the world. At the same time, I hold this thought."

A moderately crazy person is treated as mentally ill, but an extremely crazy person inspires only awe.

"If the birth of transcendent beings and divine ranks is the calamity of the Ten-Thousand-Poison Insect, isn't the Death God himself the greatest calamity? The 24 main gods are the oldest transcendent beings, so someone must kill him, I say."
"Goodness. Senior, are you trying to become a God Slayer?"
"That is indeed my ultimate goal. I chose crafting a Reaper's Scythe as my graduation project as the first step toward God Slaying. After graduation, I planned to challenge Reaper Slaying before tackling the Death God."

But even that first step was hindered by inexplicable obstacles, so it's no wonder he's fallen into despair.

"Have you thought about asking Professor Sadako for help? She'd be thrilled if you offered her the corpse of a slain reaper!"
"You speak without knowing."

The Grim Reaper senior's dark-circled, black eyelids looked down at me seriously.

"Professor Sadako is more dangerous than the Death God's messenger, the reaper. That woman is an achiever of the Reaper Slaying feat who cut down the reaper that tried to guide her to the afterlife and remained in the middle realm."
"Wouldn't that mean she hates reapers enough to help?"
"Do you... want to seek help from a professor who wants to obtain graduate students she can exploit forever without them dying?"

Ah! That's a bit...
The Grim Reaper senior's fear has now infected me too.

"Never take Professor Sadako's lectures. I sense you have quite an aptitude for dark mana, which is exactly the constitution the professor would like."
"But I'm already taking them! I took them last semester and I'm taking them this semester too!"

The senior's face showed compassion, as if looking at a tragic protagonist who had chosen a foolish fate.

"Whatever you do, don't become a teaching assistant. Judging by your destiny, once you become an assistant, you'll be dragged around by Professor Sadako for life and beyond. Why did you take that lecture in the first place?"
"Professor Sadako has long bangs, right?"
"That's right."
"When a long-haired woman whose face can't be seen turns out to be an incredible beauty after moving her hair aside, isn't that an exciting situation?"
"..."
"That's why I took it!"

The Grim Reaper senior looked at me like I was insane.
The Plant Club senior also kept his distance from me with eyes mixed with shock and horror.
Why are they acting like that?

"Let's get to business."

We arrived at the open space quickly because he hurried his steps, not wanting to mix words with me.

"Are those the Ttoontoon plants and Spider plants you're selling me?"
"Yes!"
"You want me to buy that?"

A hero was stuck in one corner of the open space, and familiar techniques were deployed around the rare plant colony.

<Holy Mirror>
<108 Directional Formation>

"I tried to stop him?"
"You should have tried harder!"
"But I thought it would work. How was I supposed to know he'd get caught when he's a current hero with confidence?"

The malicious rare plant colony had learned the hero's magic.

"Not buying."

"Not buying."
