# 294 - The Professor's End

After entering the Academy, I thought there would be little I could do for my Lady now that she had made many friends and grown significantly in skill.
Oknodie was leaving my care, but in a different way than the Ladies I had regretfully lost before.
Jonna felt relief but also a sense of regret in one corner of his heart.

"Help me!"

My Lady relied on me.
Honestly, I was happy.
Happy that I could still be of help to her.

"Understood. This will be a good opportunity for observation. Leape, April. Guard the Lady."

At Jonna's call, Leape and April emerged smoothly from the darkness, taking positions on either side in defensive stances.
Their posture showed clear resolve to protect the Lady from any attack.
With them present, Jonna could proceed without worry.
Leape had the skills.
As for April... well, she could at least serve as a meat shield.

"Imperial professor. You've crossed the line by threatening my Lady's friend without invitation."

The <Metal Manipulation> ability, which I had only used to increase the weight of my Lady's training equipment, flowed through the ground.
The cold wave that had been rushing along the ground at a fierce speed was blocked by a suddenly rising metal wall.
With a thunderous boom, the earth undulated and cracked.
This was the typical phenomenon that occurred when the powers of two masters who had reached the pinnacle clashed.

A Silver-plate level monster with enough danger to warrant evacuation orders for an entire region.
A skilled individual who could single-handedly cause the kind of damage such a Silver-plate monster might inflict.

And not just any Silver-plate level.
Lower Silver-plates had a vague possibility of devastating a region someday.
Mid Silver-plates could threaten a region in the near future.
Upper Silver-plates would immediately devastate a region if not subjugated urgently.

Both Jonna himself and the professor causing trouble were at the Upper Silver-plate level.
In adventurer or mercenary terms, they were at the level called [True Silver], meaning silver among silvers—the top 0.1% tier.
Though not quite at the Gold-plate level of the top 0.1% dangerous individuals, they were certainly in the 0.2% or 0.3% range.
In front of such powerful individuals, even hero-level strength was not yet sufficient.
Someday in the distant future, heroes might catch up and surpass them, but at this moment, these two were practitioners who stood above heroes.

*Hissss.*

The hero and saintess were enveloped in pure white smoke.
Surrounded by extreme ice crystals, the two were barely managing to protect themselves.
Jonna passed by the crystal-encased hero and saintess with an indifferent face.

"What on earth is the Foundation's butler trying to raise?"

Professor Rave was incredulous.

"A mere butler with professor-class strength."
"Now you understand. You misjudged our strength. You stepped where you shouldn't have. And did what you shouldn't have done. Your head could fall off at any moment."

Jonna abandoned even the minimal courtesy of formal speech.
He made the professor realize the clear weight of reality.

"You will die. Unless you show sufficient sincerity for your rudeness right here and now."

Jonna Wiheomhae's pressure didn't yield even against a professor-class powerhouse.
Although he might pale in comparison to Destroyer, the retired hero who once served as a thief in a former hero party, he was by no means inferior to an ordinary professor-class individual.

"This is an uninhabited island in the middle of the ocean. A place overflowing with natural mana that responds to my ice magic. Foundation's butler, do you truly believe you have a chance of winning?"

With magic, once there is sufficient mana, the battle becomes one of mental imagery.
How efficiently can one awaken the principles of nature?
How powerfully can one harness nature's destructive force?
This was a battle between special-class magicians who had surpassed novice magicians who could only partially imitate nature, intermediate magicians who succeeded in recreating natural phenomena, and advanced magicians who could realize disaster-level phenomena.
Phenomena beyond disasters could only be realized through imagination.
The Empire had passed down their arcane knowledge to promising special-class magicians.

The opportunity to observe natural phenomena related to the birth and death of stars from cosmic astronomy.
Access to the latest magical observation instrument called the Nova Telescope.

Magicians who had experienced Nova and those who hadn't were selected by the Empire's will, and this was why powerful magicians with nothing to want for remained loyal to the Empire.
Of course, Professor Rave was a young magician lacking in both years and experience compared to the Imperial Court Magicians who had served loyally for decades and made numerous contributions.
Nevertheless, he had gained the enlightenment of Nova because someone had extended a hand to him.

"Insatiable greed and thirst for knowledge. Interesting, Rave. Will you pledge loyalty to the Academy in exchange for satisfying that desire?"

Rave had gladly taken that hand.

[Imperial Ultimate Magic - Nova Effect]
[Ice Attribute 7th Circle Ultimate Magic]
[Frost Nova]

And now, the power of a Nova-class grand magic user who had observed the destructive force originating from stellar explosions was being recreated in his hands.
Jonna acknowledged it.
Indeed, an Academy professor.
Rave's move was as impressive as he had thought.
But Professor Rave had made one misjudgment.

"For an ice magician, water is an element optimized for obtaining natural mana. An ice magician above the sea is nothing short of a nightmare. But there are other elements abundant here as well."

As Jonna drew up his power, Professor Rave began to see the butler's strategy.
The attribute of the mana puzzle rising in response around the butler was metal.
And now, this uninhabited island was piled high with the corpses of mineral rats whose bodies were made of Blue Metal, a type of rare metal that was in a different league from ordinary metals.

'Could it be that he knew from the beginning that Blue Metal rats would appear here and brought a metal artisan...!?'

Professor Rave realized.
The Foundation had agreed to the deal and provided information about Oknodie.
And they had positioned a somewhat ambiguous but sufficiently skilled guard as an escort.
While hiding monsters containing elements specialized for that guard's abilities in the underground of the island.
Unlike Professor Rave who exercised violence of scale with numerous mana puzzles, Jonna Wiheomhae pressed forward with the quality and purity of mana puzzles.

'Area-effect magic that disperses destructive power. To break through my most powerful magic head-on...!'

Unlike the common and abundant mana permeating the entire sea, the mana of Blue Metal rats possessed a purity of a different caliber.

'This is dangerous. I've depleted all the mana puzzles in the surrounding waters using Nova-class magic.'

The difference between low-quality and high-quality mana became apparent.
Mana that only has quantity is depleted after a single use, but high-purity, high-quality mana can be used repeatedly with good efficiency even in small amounts.
Though the thought process is lengthy, the conclusion is simple.
He was pushed back.
Rave's offensive had failed.

'I'll target Oknodie instead of you.'
'Try if you believe your head will remain attached until the end.'

Magic that clearly revealed their murderous intent toward each other.
Jonna's faith in his Lady and the maids was steadfast.
That strong belief planted doubt in Rave.
Could there really be some trick?
Maybe the maids were such incredible practitioners that they could block any attack.
And what about Oknodie, who was so versed in dark mana that she was called the Dark Princess?

'I cannot kill her. Even if it costs me my life.'

A moment of doubt created a tremor in the solid barrier of Frost Nova.
Jonna's iron-like will did not miss the small gap and broke through from the front.

'No, this is strange.'

Rave's eyes, which had been heading toward desperation, widened.

[High-Speed Thinking][Battlefield Analysis]

The cold brain of a magician derived the absolute value of mana destructive power that he had failed to recognize.
The mana puzzles of Blue Metal rats that Jonna had mobilized couldn't exert this much output.
There was something more.
The source of the elements that granted him power.
When he widened his view, the answer finally became visible.

"Just a little more, Ishtar."
"Almost there...!"

Ice crystal.
A large hole had been opened in the prison that had confined the hero and saintess by mixing the attributes of ice and metal.
The magic that had been deployed to stop the hero had instead become Jonna's power source.
The variable was there.
Just as Jonna was about to manifest a second grand magic, the crystal sphere finally collapsed.

"If I die, you're next. Do you think the Foundation would miss this golden opportunity to harm the hero?"

Rave shook the hero's heart.
He encouraged anxiety and instilled fear.
If the hero had any sense, he would threaten Jonna and Oknodie for his own survival.
If that created a gap in Jonna's grand magic, he could finally gain the upper hand.
This was Rave's plan for reversal.

"Do I care?"

The hero opened a door.
Holy Mirror.
The ability to transmit attacks through the image beyond the mirror.
The door was aimed at the back of Rave's head.
The hero's killing technique, fueled by rage, flew toward the professor.

"The Foundation has never harmed me first. Unlike your Empire."

As Rave's magic direction was thrown off while trying to block the hero's attack, Jonna's grand magic hit him.

*Crack!*

Rave's heart was pierced by steel thorns, and the center of his chest transformed into metal.
The professor couldn't hide his resentment as he glared at the hero.
The hero was too straightforwardly crazy for him to manipulate, and the price for not realizing this fact ultimately came back as death.

*Swish swish*

Just then, in the blurring edge of Rave's vision, he saw a shadow rushing in quickly.
Oknodie.
That terrifying child had finally begun to act.
Now that he was dying, she must be planning to kill the hero without thinking of any consequences or aftermath.
Serves them right.
Stupid girl, let's just die together.
As Professor Rave was about to mock the hero's end, a dagger was firmly planted in the back of his neck.

"I can't resist the finishing blow!"

...There wasn't just one stupid girl.

"Oknodie. Can I stab him once too?"
"Hmm. Sure! But I get to put in one more knife at the end."

Zuang and Oknodie chatted amicably while stabbing him with daggers.
The future of the Academy looks very dark indeed.
Professor Rave, having abandoned both hope for life and expectation of revenge, closed his eyes and perished.
