# Chapter 266 - Chaos

"April. What are you doing? We need to escape now."
"...I can't move."

April's face turned pale as her tail got caught in the sack.
Despite Andersen's attempt to pull with force, April's face contorted in severe pain.
Her reaction was so serious that he feared something would break if he pulled any harder!

'This is troublesome.'

Andersen agonized over the situation.
Just the thought of entering the third-year zone made his body tremble involuntarily.
Even as an advanced first-year student, he had struggled against the Minotaur that served as practice material for senior students.
Who knew what would happen if they were caught by seniors who casually used such monsters for lectures?
But with her body so rigid, she would get seriously injured if she jumped off the cart.

"Go ahead, Oknodie."
"Aren't you coming, Andersen?"
"I can't leave April behind."

The loyal gentleman Andersen, Grand Duke's son, resolved to stay until the end.
Oknodie wore a very surprised expression at his decision.

"What's with that face?"
"Hehe. It's just really unexpected. In any timeline, Your Grace has always had a cold and ruthless side. I thought you'd pretend not to know us, like with Lady Arcadia!"
"...I have nothing to say about that. I certainly did wrong by Arcadia. She was beyond my ability to save."

This time is different, the Grand Duke's son insisted.
But even April, who was receiving his help, inwardly disagreed.

'Perhaps a lowly minor noble could get away with anything, but someone of the Grand Duke's status should be careful not to let dirt touch even a falling leaf.'

A woman outside his circle, and a mere maid at that.
Moreover, she was disguised as a beastkin, so to others she would appear to be a cat-person. And he was helping someone affiliated with the Foundation.
If discovered, it would cause a scandal that would shake the Academy.
It would negatively impact his family.
It certainly wouldn't be good for Andersen's own future.
Yet he still offered help?
Because she was within his reach?
Because it was manageable?

'That can't be right.'

Though not embroiled in as massive an incident as with Lady Arcadia, this was equally dangerous for him.
Yet he still helped, which made April think there must have been a change in the Grand Duke's son's mindset.

"If you're doing this out of guilt, you don't have to."
"That's not it."
"Then let's just say it is, okay!"

Perhaps he was being kind because he felt sorry for not being able to help Arcadia.

'Or maybe he has feelings for me...?'

No way. That couldn't be it.
April shook her head at such childish thoughts.


* * *


The opportunity to jump off the cart had passed.
They finally arrived at the third-year exclusive zone.
The lecture hall for "Understanding Monster Behavior Patterns."

"This isn't it."
"Then where are we?"

Andersen was puzzled by Oknodie's words.
A teaching assistant who had parked the cart in an open space was making suspicious sounds with a flute.

Rustle.

Soon after, suspicious students began appearing one by one from a dim path.
Could their identities have been discovered...?
Were they planning to gang up on a first-year who dared enter the third-year zone?
Andersen tensed up as his imagination ran wild.

"The goods?"
"See for yourself."

Andersen trembled in fear.
A student approached the cart, cut the sack with a dagger, and swallowed some jelly.

"Premium quality. The cutting technique along the grain is extraordinary. Whose work is this?"
"Don't know. I just collected what was already there, scheduled for delivery."
"This works out well for us 'Foundation' 'scholarship students'."

The students laughed wickedly.
All three were surprised by those words.

"!!"

To think they would encounter senior Foundation scholarship students like this!
Andersen realized the situation was more serious than he thought.
While Oknodie and April were Foundation affiliates and might manage somehow, he was an outsider.
If discovered, he would certainly face terrible consequences.
His hand gripped tightly in shock.
April, whom he had been trying to calm earlier, now held his hand and gave him a reassuring look, silently telling him to stay calm.

'There must be more Foundation spies at the Academy than just the ones I know.'

April was aware that her responsibilities were mainly focused on the first-year lower class.
There might be other handlers within the same year, not to mention different years.

'What kind of operation requires so many senior scholarship students to gather like this?'

The Foundation's evil plan that even she didn't know about.
The thought of witnessing it firsthand made April's palms sweat.
Andersen and April, drawing comfort from each other's warmth as they faced their fears together.
They finally witnessed the true nature of the Foundation's evil plan!

"Finally, we won't have to wait for free meals anymore."
"If monster specimens go missing, it's the teaching assistant who gets in trouble, not us."
"Kekeke. It was a brilliant plan. Using sticker magic to disguise ourselves as teaching assistants and boldly stealing the specimens from right under their noses."

They were stealing specimens!
Exactly what Oknodie had been doing!

'I'm not sure if this means Oknodie is on the same level as third-years, or if the Foundation's thinking is all the same.'
'...I got tense for nothing. Come to think of it, the scholarship students I handle also understand the importance of points, so most of them live quite frugally.'

Though it seemed somewhat pathetic, Andersen reminded himself that this was no reason to underestimate them.
The mere fact that they were third-years in this harsh Academy was reason enough to be cautious.
April also remained vigilant, considering the possibility of deception.

"They probably never suspected that despite wearing uniforms, we're not even students."
"Kekeke. It's your fault for preventing us dropouts from eating school meals for 5 points."
"Wow, look here. There are even cockatrice eggs. This teaching assistant must have been really skilled? To steal eggs from a bird that burns you to death if you make eye contact!"
"Does eating those half-boiled increase fire resistance?"
"Should we throw them in lava?"
"You idiot, if they melt, no one except a lava golem could eat them."

Andersen wasn't sure whether to be surprised that these people were dropouts, or by Oknodie's skill in sneakily taking eggs from such dangerous monsters.

"Oknodie. The sack will be completely exposed soon and we'll be discovered too. Wouldn't it be better to come out voluntarily now and ask for forgiveness?"
"Just wait patiently a little longer. I sense other presences approaching!"

The third-year scholarship students who had been transferring items from the cart suddenly turned their heads.

"This presence...!"
"It's the student council! The Dropout Enforcement Division is here!!"

Andersen, unfamiliar with the student council's power, couldn't understand the senior students' reaction.

'Why are they so wary of each other? I could understand if it were instructors or professors, but they're just the student council.'

A thought only a clueless first-year could have.

"Fire the signal flare!"
"Over there! Dropouts who have illegally entered the enrolled students' zone are over there!"
"Wait, how did they know we're dropouts!?"
"Could there be a traitor among us!?"

The Foundation scholarship students fell into extreme confusion, noticeable even to Andersen who was eavesdropping from the cart!

"We'll identify the traitor later. For now, let's scatter... Argh!"
"Ahahahaha! To think you didn't notice Mandela Castella's ambush, seniors must have wasted their time at the Academy. You're so weak it brings tears to my eyes!"

'Did I just see that wrong?'

The distinctive laugh and characteristic drill-shaped hair.
There was no mistake.
That person was Lady Mandela, the second-year class representative he had seen at the sports festival's inter-year dodgeball competition.
In an instant, as the dropouts' escape route flashed, Lady Mandela appeared from thin air using "Short-range Teleportation."
A cheat-like technique that warriors who value positioning could only dream of.

"Ha, she's just a second-year! A surprise attack is one thing, but now that she's shown herself head-on, there's nothing to fear!"

A long-haired dropout senior levitated dozens of pebbles from the ground and fired them like bullets.
Following him, another senior with a shield tore off his fake uniform, revealed his armor, and began an armored charge.
Against the powerful consecutive telekinetic magic and armored rush technique, Mandela with her hammer as tall as herself looked as vulnerable as a candle in the wind.

"Now, let's escape while we can!"
"What? You don't want to watch the outcome of such an exciting battle?"
"Whoever falls, we'll be discovered next."

At April's words, Andersen reluctantly pulled out the sack to free her tail.
As they crept out of the cart, exclamations of shock came from behind: "No way!" "Impossible!" "To think that the charge that broke Professor Platon's statue would be blocked!"
...He was so curious.
He wanted to watch the fight!
As Andersen kept turning his head back, April grabbed his hand.
When he still couldn't focus, April took an even bolder action.

"!!"

Fingers interlocking.
At this lover-like hand-holding, Grand Duke's son Andersen stared blankly at April.

"Don't slow down. Lady Mandela's laughter is getting louder."

They might face worse consequences than if caught by Foundation scholarship students.
Despite her gruff voice, April, who was running ahead, resolutely pulled Andersen along even as she occasionally twisted her waist in pain.
Seeing April struggle despite her injuries sustained because of him, Andersen finally shook his head and regained his focus.

"Oknodie. April. I'm sorry to both of you. If I had noticed the dropouts' approach earlier, we wouldn't have gotten involved in this."
"Oh, that's fine. I managed to snatch something more valuable than all of today's gathered ingredients from the dropout senior who fell to Lady Mandela's surprise attack!"

What Oknodie pulled out from her magic backpack was a bundle of stickers with strange effects that the dropout impersonating the teaching assistant had been using.
