# 235 - Sleep Tight, Baby

The chairman tapped his finger on the support request sent by April, a smile on his face.

"Keeping the hero in check... So an evil villain has appeared to threaten a good child's daily life."
'Are you really the one saying that?'

The chief secretary cursed the chairman in his mind.
Of course, while keeping his head bowed.
The chairman was the type who could read one's emotions and innermost thoughts to their unknown depths with just a moment of eye contact.

"Oknodie's butler was definitely Jonna, wasn't it? A child with an unbending will, strong as iron."

He remembered.
The sight of Jonna in a frenzy after losing his "Lady."
The image of him crawling on the ground in pouring rain, not caring about his clothes getting soiled with mud as he recovered the corpse.
A strong and capable dog that rarely obeys needs a sturdy leash.
That's why he attached more fragile dolls to him.
With one loss came anger.
With two losses came despair.
With three losses came pleading.
Only after the fourth did he choose obedience, finally ceasing to bare his teeth at his master.
But he knew.
Even if he didn't lunge, didn't bark, didn't bare his teeth, even as he lowered his body and bowed his head.
The fact that behind Jonna's bowed head existed cold killing intent and a desire for revenge.

"Jonna Wiheomhae. Can you trust him? If he enters the Academy, he might try to hide with Oknodie under the Academy's protection."

The chief secretary offered his advice.
Though the chairman was an unsettling and frightening presence, he didn't hesitate to provide necessary counsel.
If he didn't, he would find himself ousted from his important position without realizing it, becoming someone who could never face the chairman again.
Just like the man once considered the next head butler.
Just like Jonna Wiheomhae had been.

"Hoho. The chief secretary's loyalty is quite admirable. As a reward, let me tell you something good."

The chairman picked up the teacup on the table.
Chamomile tea.
A healthy tea that calms the mind and reduces inflammation and stress.
The chief secretary had personally selected it.
His intention was to hopefully bring that madness-filled mind closer to the realm of normalcy.
The meticulously chosen chamomile tea, his masterstroke, was held in the chairman's hand before being placed next to a potted plant by the window.

"The Academy is the only den where those children can escape from me. But if that den tries to harm them, then the opposite will happen."
"Do you mean to make them flee from the Academy and return to the foundation's embrace?"
"It's human nature to want to escape an environment when under stress."

Splash.
The clear chamomile tea from the cup was poured into the potted plant.
Crunch, crunch.
The plant in the pot, having its leaves eaten by small insects, trembled on its stem.

"A clear mind is a symbol of health, but what about in reality? Would a child who survived alone in a plague-ridden village feel joy if they forgot their madness and regained their sanity?"
"...I think they would feel more tormented."
"They would suffer greatly, like this friend here. The act of objectively recognizing one's situation and facing reality is more painful for those in difficult circumstances."

Click. Click.
After cutting off a few leaves with pruning shears, the chairman applied disinfectant to the oozing stem and painted it with wax.
After meticulously finishing his brushwork, he put down the bonsai tools with satisfaction and returned the empty teacup to the table.

"It tastes good. Would you pour me another cup?"

...You didn't even drink it.
Suppressing his fear, the chief secretary carefully tilted the teapot himself to fill the teacup.

"The world cannot accept a child of the foundation. Especially one with the position of top scholarship student. Oknodie. The more exceptional she is, the more stress she will experience."

Because they fear the foundation.
And even more so Oknodie, who will become one of the foundation's people.
The chairman, now pouring the contents of the second teacup into an aquarium.
Gulp, gulp.
The fish that had been nibbling on seaweed trembled as if having seizures, then crashed against the aquarium walls.
After a moment of self-destructive behavior, the fish soon forgot what it was doing and floated around the water before nibbling on seaweed again.

"When that time comes, aren't you curious about what will happen to Jonna and Oknodie? Will they become bonsai trees that suffer and bleed until painted with wax, or will they become dull fish that forget their pain? Ah, personally, I prefer bonsai over fish."

Because dull fish don't know where their home is, but bonsai trees know they cannot escape.
Just like someone who had drunk chamomile tea, the chief secretary thought as he listened to the chairman's gentle and calm laughter.
The chamomile tea was a complete failure.
This man's madness had seen through his shallow intentions and played with him completely.
Now he was even beginning to fear.
What would this madman look like if he truly found "sanity"?
Was that even madness to begin with?

"...What will you do about the lower scholarship students from the foundation whose identities have presumably been exposed?"
"If the tail is exposed, we must plant a new one."

The chairman's face grew calmer.
The comfort in the smile drawn on his lips was even more so.

"There was an assassin maid who took care of Oknodie during her training, wasn't there?"
"Yes. Leape Life."
"Leape Life... Ah. I remember. The last sprout harvested while burning the fields of the Rozania Elf Territory."

A child with hair that held the freshness of green.
But there were limits to her potential.
Because in this era, those who aren't pure humans can never rise to mainstream positions.

"I feel uncomfortable and worried about sending an endangered species to a dangerous area, but if we coddle them too much, they won't grow properly, will they?"
"...I think so."
"Then let's send Leape to the Academy first. As a maid for now."
"What about the previous tail?"
"Hoho. Chief secretary. Do you know? You look like you need a cup of chamomile tea right now."
"......"
"I'm joking. Don't be so angry."

The chief secretary swallowed the sigh that was about to escape from his deep worries.
His stomach felt like it was going to ulcerate.
Perhaps he really did need chamomile.
But what difference would it make if his mind was calmed?
The chairman's will was firm.
If he didn't take action, the chairman would do it himself.
That would be an even more desperate future for the tail who had been diligently playing the role of a spy.

"I will issue the disposal order."

Let's at least show mercy so they can die as humans.
The chief secretary, carrying the order to kill, conveyed the instructions to the secretariat.
Remove the old tail and attach a new one.
It's a simple task, nothing difficult.
Even merciful at that.
The chief secretary thought.
Indeed, he didn't need chamomile tea after all.

The mosquitoes had sucked so much blood that a blood stone was inadvertently created.
Unlike the beginning of the semester, perhaps because students had increased their mana by eating, sleeping, and living in mana-rich areas, the blood stone was small but its radiance was more intense.

"Blood magic isn't really my style."

And the blood stone trend is already over.
What am I supposed to do with this?
While spinning the blood stone on my finger, I felt a burning gaze.

"Baby."
"No. Baby, you shouldn't eat things like this. No, no."

What is this child trying to become, eyeing such things?
I just put the blood stone in my pocket.
If I keep it, I'll find a use for it somewhere!
But somehow, Baby Mandragora's expression seemed full of resentment and dissatisfaction.

"Baby!"
"Oh, come on. I said no. Why are you being so stubborn?"
"Babyyyy!"

The angry Baby Mandragora extended its fine roots outside the bottle, pointing at the trash can.
Inside was the pouch that had contained the blood-fried powder I'd received from Senior Yumi.

"You saw...?"
"Baby."

You eat blood, so why can't I!
Give me some too!
Baby Mandragora throwing a tantrum over food.
But not a chance.

"Something terrible will happen if Baby eats this. I absolutely won't give it to you until you've grown up!"
"Babyyyy."

Baby Mandragora struggled, hitting the surface of the bottle with its fine roots.
While I was thinking about it, I poured the water from the bottle into a cup and added new special nutrient solution.

"Hmm. Let's see how much our Baby has grown this time."

Gulp, gulp!
I downed the water containing Baby's miraculous energy in one go.
The abundant energy flows down my esophagus into my stomach.
With this much energy...
My total mana has increased by a year's worth!

"Hmm. The growth rate is less than last month. Should I trim the fine roots and eat them next time?"

Baby, who had been splashing the water vigorously, suddenly stopped moving.
Shiver.
Baby Mandragora trembled and immersed itself in the nutrient solution.
It must be feeling cold because the water suddenly changed.

"Should I pour some hot water for you?"

Bubble, bubble.
Bubbles rose from inside the bottle.
The mouth on Baby's roots moved slightly.

[Baby]

It says this is just right.

"If you eat a balanced diet without being picky from now on, I'll let you eat blood stones later. Listen well, don't be picky, eat a lot, and grow big. Understand?"

Already excited at the thought of eating, it trembles again.
Just like a phone on vibrate mode.
Oh right.
If I'm going to collect blood at the sports festival tomorrow, I should go to sleep soon.

"Sleep tight, Baby!"

I turned off the lights and lay down on the bed.
For some reason, I feel like I'm going to dream about chewing on a crying Baby Mandragora tonight.
I hope Baby grows up quickly.
