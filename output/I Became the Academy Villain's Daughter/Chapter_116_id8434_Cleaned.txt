# 116 - The Seniors' Fear

Bellocasio let out a hollow laugh at the sight of the second-year students filling the infirmary.
Looking at the medical tags of the patients lying on the beds, he saw labels like "Frostbite" and "Cold Extremities."
When he flicked the tag with his finger, the back side revealed the patient name: "Paper Company."
Despite being marked as the figurehead CEO of the Illegal Red Tooth Mushroom Cultivation Association, unlike his skill at ordering around his colleagues, he hadn't successfully executed a single plan.

"I wondered where everyone went, and here you all are in such pathetic states."
"It's not our fault... those kids were just too strong! Ugh. How can students have such strong bonds between upper and lower classes...!"
"The upper class helped the lower class?"
"Without a doubt. We initially targeted the lower class. But suddenly, an upper class student intervened and froze the entire water area!"

Paper Company felt wronged in his own way.
Casting magic on moving objects is difficult.
Moreover, fixing them in place greatly depends on the mage's magical control.
You have to fix coordinates, suppress fluctuating values, and continuously concentrate forces that want to disperse.
Naturally, as the range increases, the required mana and mental energy grow, and calculations multiply.

"In one go, they froze fluid over 5cm thick and maintained it for more than 20 minutes. And it was powerful enough to create icy paths beyond the intended range!"

For the first time, surprise appeared in Bellocasio's eyes.

"As expected of the upper class. To think a first-year could put on such a performance."

Magic is a battle of intelligence and affinity.
Higher intelligence reduces the mana and mental energy required for spell calculations.
For spell formulas that can't be fully calculated, one can imbue mana with will, using affinity to guide the mana to take form on its own.
Either you need to be incredibly intelligent or have exceptional elemental affinity.
Those without these advantages who want to become wizards must desperately train in extreme natural environments of their chosen element for years.
If that's not appealing, they must diligently study spell formulas and work hard to increase their calculation capacity.
But it's only been about ten days.
How much could a first-year have learned in such a short time?
This had to be pure affinity power.

"You've had a rough time."

Bellocasio softened his expression.
Incompetence is a sin, but getting caught in a natural disaster is not.

"You... understand?"
"Despite appearances, I'm quite reasonable. I have no intention of throwing tantrums or demanding the impossible."
"Bellocasio...! I always thought you were just a jerk who ordered us around from behind, but you actually have a warm heart!"

Bellocasio decided to change his approach.
This ice incident confirmed it.
The first-year lower class students could be toyed with anytime.
The problem was the upper class.
Specifically, the two students who decisively interfered with Paper Company's operation.

"Group A's top student Oknodie. Northern Archduchess Irene. From now on, we focus on these two."
"Oknodie...! Because of that cunning girl, rumors spread that I'm trash who seriously beats ten-year-old kids. I'll never forgive her!!"
"Good. Don't forget that anger."

Coincidentally, Oknodie was also the one who interfered with Bellocasio's contract fraud.
To control the first-years like pawns, they needed to deal with Oknodie somehow.

"From today, investigate Oknodie. Discreetly. Try not to get caught. Can you do that?"
"I'll try my best!"

Paper Company steeled his resolve.

* * *

After leaving the infirmary, Paper Company heard several of his illegal cultivation colleagues announcing their departure.

"The upper class students are too strong, regardless of year. I have no confidence in winning."
"Between revenge and nonsense, I don't have time to keep up with lectures. I'm quitting here."
"Do you know how terrifying it was escaping to the infirmary while the Wind Walker magic on my feet gradually diminished? I never want to experience something so horrible again."

Paper Company couldn't blame his associates for deserting.
He still remembered the terror of having his stomach and shoe soles freeze in an instant.
In the blink of an eye, mana surged like a massive wave, freezing the water and creating a biting cold.

*Squish squish*

With each step, the rising cold made his socks barely prevent his skin from sticking to the floor, and he had to squeeze out every bit of his meager magic power to prevent the cold from freezing his feet.
Even when he thought he'd reached the escape zone, Irene would cast more magic on her way to school, forcing him to continue walking with tears in his eyes.

-No... I'm out of mana...!
-Hnngh! My feet are too cold!
-Don't touch the ground with your hands! What will you do if they get stuck?!

From the beginning, they were inferior students desperate enough to illegally cultivate red tooth mushrooms to increase their absolute mana.
It was inevitable that they would run out of mana and suffer frostbite on the ice that demanded absolute mana quantities.

"This is actually for the best. The disloyal ones have fallen away, but we're different."

Paper Company addressed the colleague who remained by his side until the end.
Second-year Knight Department <Box Chowder>.
Second-year Magic Department <Paper Company>.
Now these two must carry on the revenge.

"Box. Are your grades okay?"
"They're fine."

Box Chowder said.

"If I drop one class."
"Box...! That's too crazy!"
"This incident made me realize. No matter how much I train in martial arts or try to increase mana through shortcuts, I'm still helpless before a true master."

At this point, taking one more class wouldn't improve his skills.

"To defeat the strong, we must follow the hunter's method of finding and exploiting their weaknesses. Participating in this operation is practice for that."

Though he recognized his weakness, instead of collapsing in despair, he sought new methods.
Paper Company was inspired by Box Chowder's bold determination.

"Box. Let's make this work!"

The second-year inferior male student quietly built up his fighting spirit.

* * *

The investigation began with information gathering.
Paper Company offered snacks to other second-years whose faces weren't known, saying:

"I'll give you this if you go ask the first-years what they know about Oknodie or Irene, okay?"
"Why? Planning to beat up a kid again?"
"...I want to apologize!"

Most students scoffed at what they considered a trash person's excuse, but some gave him a chance.

"Having the courage to repent for dishonorable actions is a form of bravery. I'll help you take the first step toward clearing your disgraceful past. Hang in there, Paper Company."
"Th-thank you..."

Antenor, a popular second-year lower class talent with good looks, friendly personality, and excellent grades, quickly gathered the information he wanted.

"She has quite a good reputation, this Oknodie. Despite being only about ten years old, befitting the youngest top student, she excels in running, physical strength, archery, swordsmanship, negotiation, and writing speed."
"Ha. A real monster... What about magic?"
"So far, she hasn't shown significant magical talent. She might appreciate a gift related to that."

Though he planned to return the "gift" as an attack, Paper Company forced a smile and acted along.

"Thanks. That's somewhat helpful."
"Ah, but avoid giving cold resistance-related gifts if possible."
"Why?"
"I heard she once clung to Irene's back at close range while Irene was using freezing magic."

He knew he was weak.
He knew geniuses were exceptional.
But rarely had he felt such intense helplessness as now.

*Clatter.*

"Paper Company? What's wrong? You suddenly dropped your things. Did you leave the infirmary too early just to put on airs?"
"M-maybe."

Paper Company pressed his hand against his forehead, trying to calm his throbbing headache.
But he knew.
This wasn't the kind of pain that could be soothed this way.
It was jealousy.
The massive envy he felt toward incomparable talent was the reason for his headache.

'The ice magic was so intensely cold and penetrating that we could barely stand on two feet and had to be hospitalized.'

Yet Oknodie, much younger than them, had accompanied a frost mage at close range without ever needing medical attention.

'Still, she might have other weaknesses.'

Clinging to a glimmer of hope, Paper Company continued gathering information.
When he met Box Chowder at their contact point to compile the information collected over the weekend, he realized that the depth of despair flickering in Box Chowder's hollow eyes was at least equal to his own, if not greater.

"What information did you find that's got you looking like that?"
"I got Oknodie's course registration form."
"Was there a problem?"

Box Chowder pointed with a serious face to Tuesday and Thursday, 2nd period: "Adventure Tales of a Retired Former Hero."

"Do you know what this class is?"
"Never heard of it."
"It's a lecture taught by the world's strongest thief, 'Destroyer,' who served as an all-purpose rogue in the previous generation's hero party."
"W-what?!"
"Since he came to the academy, Oknodie has been the only student. It means she's been chosen by a former hero as a candidate for the next generation's hero party."

Even at Gift Academy, the world's premier educational institution, there are differences in rank among professors.
Naturally, a production professor who's the world's best at firing roof tiles won't have the same reputation as a combat professor who's the world's best at slicing off heads.

"Th-then Oknodie is..."
"Likely the most promising candidate to become the world's greatest thief in our generation."
"W-wait. Wait a minute. Thieves specialize in information activities too. Does that mean she's been aware that we've been digging up information about her these past few days?!"

Fear rippled in Box Chowder's eyes.
Paper Company's hands trembled, spilling water from his cup.

"That's not all."
"What else is there?!"
"There's also a rumor that she's an active assassin trained by some noble family."

Paper Company decided.
Tonight, he would sleep in the closet instead of his bed.
