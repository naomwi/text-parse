# 171 - The Curse of the Upper Classmen

"That Jaku guy, always saying unnecessary righteous things. What mental illness is he talking about? He doesn't even know the Foundation's circumstances."

Late at night, Primer, who had infiltrated the senior student area, grumbled to himself as he recalled Jaku who had hurled insults at him.
He knew that talking to himself made him look foolish, but he simply couldn't calm his anger.

'The Foundation's support is not fair.'

Just as the Academy regularly provides living allowance points, the Foundation also periodically provides scholarships and supplies to scholarship students.
Support funds and supplies distributed through the Foundation's hidden collaborators and support forces.
He had never thought it was tacky. Not until he heard certain news recently.

"You know Oknodie, right? I heard she has an amazing butler."
"They say he's a representative from the Foundation who leaves the child alone until late at night and only meets her briefly at dawn before leaving."
"Why can't her parents come directly? Isn't that too much?"
"She's an abused child."
"She's always smiling, but somehow it's scary. Like she might suddenly commit a terrible crime. If she's in a bad mood, wouldn't she extract people's souls and stuff?"
"Let's not get involved with her."

The usual malicious rumors about Oknodie. Among them was a story that couldn't be ignored.

'She met directly with a Foundation butler?'

Unlike the group trainees at the training ground who receive instruction collectively from instructors and seniors, dedicated teachers are assigned only to carefully selected children with outstanding talents.
That was the identity of those called "butlers" within the Wiheomhae Foundation.

'Filthy silver spoon brat.'

Those who receive special treatment just because they were born with slightly better innate talents!
Young ladies and young masters with butlers were objects of envy and simultaneously objects of hatred for the apprentice scholarship students who lived in groups of dozens or hundreds.

"What makes us any different?"
"I'm sure I have other talents that the higher-ups haven't noticed."
"Right. The instructor must have done a sloppy job checking."

The hopeful circuit of thinking that maybe I'm a silver spoon too.
The resentment that my inadequacy is somehow the instructor's fault.
Unable to vent their frustration on the instructors, they instead focused on the children with butlers who were surrounded by rumors.
The identity of one of those privileged young ladies and gentlemen had been revealed.
The only publicly acknowledged scholarship student certified by the Foundation.
Someone who doesn't hide their identity and therefore receives support openly through already exposed channels—an enviable person.

'I'm not the only one who dislikes you.'

Even as a Primer, a lower-class student, he wasn't aiming to be the top scholarship student without having something to rely on.

"Use this item to make an example of the top scholarship student. If you can't hold back someone who's ahead of you, you'll only hasten the time of your own disposal."

The secret coded message given to him during the last visit contained instructions to target Oknodie, along with an item to help with this task.
It was a portable curse storage device that could store and transfer the Academy's curses!

[First-year students prohibited]
[If entry is absolutely necessary, it must be accompanied by an instructor with permission.]

Entering the upper-class area with such an ominous warning sign made him feel unnecessarily nervous.

'I sense someone.'

Primer quickly found a hiding place and concealed his presence, recalling his Foundation training.
He heard several second-year students coming out of a nearby research lab, talking among themselves.

"Did you hear about the human model room? This week it's missing the right hand."
"So we have to pretend we don't have a right hand by keeping our arm inside our clothes? That's so annoying. If it were internal organs, there'd be nothing to hide since they're not visible anyway."
"I know, right?"
'...Internal organs? Human models?? Right hand???'

What on earth was happening in the second-year human model room?
It sounded like an extremely dangerous curse.
Maybe he could steal the human model from the human model room and place it in front of Oknodie to curse her into losing her right hand.
Though Primer was secretly intrigued, he immediately abandoned the idea when he heard that a professor had taken it for a lecture.

"My head hurts. Midterm preparation is too intense. Let's go rest in the garden."
"Ah, Big Stone. We shouldn't go there. I heard from a senior in necromancy that the dimensional seal has loosened due to the curse week, and it might be connected to the Forest of the Dead in the undead realm."
"Ugh, that's such a turn-off. What kind of academy has more places we can't go than places we can go, even in second year? I really feel like dropping out."
"You have to hang in there until third year to get recognition. Haven't you heard that if you tell people you dropped out in second year, you won't get any respect?"

The conversations of other second-years didn't contain much information about curses.
Even when they did, they were either too large, massive, or vague to be contained.
Deciding to give up on getting information here, Primer resolved to venture into a more dangerous area.

[Entry prohibited for students below third year]
[First and second-year students may die if they do not follow the escorting instructor's instructions when entering.]

"..."

Instead of strict security measures, there was just a single rope strung across the stairs leading to the third-year area, as if daring those with a death wish to cross it.
The sight of the ominous black-dyed sacred rope with bells attached, resembling something you'd find at a shrine, filled him with anxiety.

'What if I end up dying first while trying to get back at Oknodie?'

No.
He had to think in reverse here.
If even half the rumors were true, how could mere second-year area curses affect Oknodie?
She was already using first-year area curses for training purposes.

'I'll show her. The curses that exist in the third-year area. The true terror of curses!'

Gathering his courage, Primer crossed the black rope and entered the third-year area.
Unlike the second-year area where he had to frequently hide from students who were studying or researching during lecture hours, the third-year area was eerily quiet.
Though he had only climbed up one more floor, it felt like he had entered a place where no one lived.

'Are there really people here?'

When he peeked through the window of a lit classroom, surprisingly, there was no one inside.

'What the hell is this?'

As he backed away in confusion, suddenly a door opened right in front of him with a creaking sound.

"Aaaaah!"

A g-ghost!!
As he fled in panic, a human figure slowly appeared behind him.

"What's with that guy? Peeking into the classroom during invisibility lecture and then running away. Could he be one of those perverted stalkers I've only heard about who try to catch partial invisibility failures where underwear or bare skin becomes visible?"

Unaware of the third-year student's grumbling, Primer ran frantically until he hit a dead end.
According to the campus guide, each floor should have had the same structure, and there should have been stairs on the opposite side of the corridor, but the path was blocked in the middle.

'Why? A dead end? Why?'

Suddenly, handprints began to appear on the wall.
As the handprints extended like demons crawling out of a hellpit, and eventually even a human face began to form, his reason was paralyzed.
He had to escape.
Primer opened the nearest classroom door, went inside, closed it, and hid.

"What the hell. I was coming through the wall to tell him not to go in there, but he went in by himself?"

The senior who had come through the wall stood awkwardly, contemplating whether to open the door, then just walked away.
Of course, unaware of this fact, Primer paced around inside the classroom he should never have entered.

'What is this place... Ah, forget it. I don't need to know. Let's just open the window and jump out.'

Though his legs might get a little injured, it was a sacrifice he was willing to make if he could escape the third-year area.
As he tried to pull back the curtain and open the window in this creepy room full of mannequins, suddenly the mannequin next to him pulled the curtain back.

"!?"

Primer was so startled he couldn't even make a sound.
A statue spoke to him.

"Don't create light. This is an art room with a petrification curse, so if you expose it to light or go outside, you'll become a real statue for a while."
"A-are you a person...? I want to get out of here... Please let me out..."
"If you want to leave, pretend to be a statue for an hour to be recognized as one of them by the murderous statues, then quietly leave through the door. Otherwise, you'll be caught."

The statue's head opened with a click, revealing a real person's head inside.
Looking around at the third-year's words, he saw that the mannequins that had filled the art room had somehow gathered around him, holding weapons.
In the brief moment when he had pulled back the curtain and light had entered through the window, he had drawn their attention and nearly been killed.

'This is completely different from the lowest-level curses where you suffer alone just by entering a suspicious place!'

A curse with physical form that harms people unconditionally if you don't follow the rules.
Such things cannot and should not be contained in a portable curse storage device.
If he released statues that go berserk at the sight of light outside, not only Oknodie but all the first-year students might die.

"How do I get inside a statue...?"
"With decomposition and recomposition magic."
"I haven't learned that yet..."
"Then die."
"Please help me. Please..."
"Press the rescue button on your magic watch. You'll be fined, but the instructors will save you."

Following the senior's advice, he pressed the rescue button, and fortunately, instructors wearing black covers arrived at the scene, covered him with a black cloth as well, and dragged him outside.

"1,000 points deducted. It's the penalty for entering a dangerous area without permission, so consider it the price of your life and accept it gracefully."
"I'm grateful enough just to be rescued..."

After surviving the art room's curse through great hardship, Primer trudged back to the safe zone alone.

"Oh no, the curse storage device..."

He must have lost it in the commotion.
He had no choice but to consider that part of the price for his life too.
Primer's shoulders drooped even more as he walked back.


* *


"Oknodie, why are you breaking the ceiling tiles?"
"If you make a straight hole here at this time, an item that can store curses will appear!"
"Why would something like that be there...?"

Oknodie, Titosoga, and Hestia, the trio who had secretly infiltrated the second floor during the curse resistance exercise.
As they made a hole, laser pointer-like objects, sharp carving knives, and long fingernails came pouring down from above.
In addition to the curse week event items, they also obtained plenty of sharp objects for memorization and material items—indeed, the high-level player spot had good cost-effectiveness.

"That's a bit scary. I hear something trying to break through the hole to harm us."
"Because it's the third-year area!"
"Eek. Do third-years live with things like that...?"

Unlike the frightened Titosoga, Hestia, a mercenary with strong nerves, calmly pointed to the looted items first.

"Oknodie. I followed you because I was worried, but what are you planning to do with that item?"
"It's a shame if I can't work on curse resistance during sleeping hours. If I store curses in this and release them in my room, I can work on resistance even while sleeping!"
"Ah... I see..."
"Do you want to try too, Hestia?"
"I'll pass."

Even Hestia, who felt the need for curse resistance training, wasn't ready to go this far yet.

"Will you refuse to lend it to me if I ask later?"
"I didn't say that."
"You really won't give it to me? This is only for one person!"
"...Could you please stop talking about the curse storage item like it's a bowl of noodles you boil at night?"
