# 285 - Meanwhile, on the Ship

While Arcadia was shocked by the startling news, someone else on the cruise ship was equally surprised.

"Hello! Did your friend turn into a statue?"
"Aaaah! Put me down, put me down right now!"

Sand Cooker screamed in terror.

"What if you hold a petrified person horizontally and the weight causes cracks in their waist?!"
"Eh? It should be fine, right? They introduced it at the auction as an 'Absolute Defense Petrification Scroll.'"
"Absolute Defense Petrification Scroll?"

Sand Cooker, a yellow wizard, was deeply knowledgeable about petrification magic related to earth magic.
His scholarship was so exceptional that he could analyze and interpret magical structures just by looking at the patterns of formulas remaining on torn scrolls.

"Phew. No need to worry about a broken waist. The defense is so high that 'absolute defense' isn't an exaggeration. It won't even get a scratch if hit directly by sword energy or magic."
"Really?"

Pyoi struck Logini's arm with a shield, making a clanging sound.

"Aaaaargh!! Stop that insanity!"
"You just said it was safe."
"It's a matter of feeling! Even if I know intellectually it's safe, it's still terrifying to watch!"

How petty.
Ignoring Pyoi who was pouting with her lips pursed, Sand Cooker continued analyzing the scroll.
Only after a long time, during which the room's lamp grew dim, did he finally breathe a sigh of relief.

"Sunlight will allow movement again. Given how simple the de-petrification condition is, this scroll is meant to help survive on a deserted island for a few days. The petrification will wear off in as little as one day or at most 15 days."

Pyoi raised her hand and asked.

"What happens if someone uses it alone in a cave and nobody finds them?"
"I guess they'd stay there until sunlight reaches them...?"

Sand Cooker felt fear.
The Academy also treats people harshly, but with the Foundation, there's no visible bottom.
How cruelly they treat people.
How terrible the experiences can be.
He couldn't imagine what the "worst case" might entail.
Now he understood, whether he liked it or not, why the Academy was so wary of the Foundation.
Including why Oknodie, despite her bright and friendly demeanor, inspired fear in many people.

"I've decided to help."
"Help? Who?"
"The Northern Grand Duchess. While you were on the deserted island, she made a proposal. To start a mutiny on the ship."

Pyoi's eyes widened.

"A mutiny? Why?"
"I don't know. She said starting a mutiny on a cruise ship was common sense, so I thought she was crazy. But after glimpsing the true nature of the Foundation, I understand her feelings."

Anyone would want to leave this insane ship as quickly as possible, even if it meant starting a mutiny.

"There are thousands of crew members. Is a mutiny even possible?"
"Don't worry. Irene isn't alone. She has a skilled person by her side who was chosen by both Oknodie and Arcadia."
"Ah, I think I know! The man who uses a sword, right? An eastern swordsman named Xing with amazing skills."

Sand Cooker retorted, wondering what nonsense she was talking about.

"It's not a man, and they don't use a sword. The skilled person chosen by both of them is obviously Titosoga, the Light Bearer. Xing isn't even visible anywhere."
"Ehhh~? Was that kid so strong? I thought he was just a cute, clumsy idiot!"

Pyoi opened her drowsy eyes, unable to hide her disbelief.
She knew some rumors about Titosoga.
A lower-class student who was close to Oknodie, the top student of the year, and once a core member of Arcadia's faction, the largest faction in the first year.
An idiot who always dragged around an absurdly large spotlight on wheels, sighing heavily whenever he encountered stairs and struggling to carry it.
To think that such an idiot would be chosen by Irene to participate in a ship mutiny.

'Was he actually not an idiot?'

Her skeptical thoughts changed to "Amazing!" as she headed to the meeting point Sand Cooker had told her about.

"How much longer until the backup lighting arrives?"
"There are no backup lights to supply here because all the lights, even emergency ones, have been removed from the entire third floor!"
"Did some monster that lives in darkness infiltrate? Why are they taking all the lights? This is truly an ominous disturbance."

Just by eavesdropping on the crew members hiding under the stairs, she could tell.
Titosoga was causing considerable fear throughout the ship.
While he had acted like a harmless small animal around Oknodie and Lady Arcadia, it seemed Titosoga could display such terrifying skills when he wanted to!

'Oknodie really is amazing!'

To think she kept such an impressive friend with hidden powers.
Surely she must be hiding a bikini-armor-worthy figure under that baggy uniform.
Eager to pass on the excellence of bikini armor to this hidden talent as soon as possible, Pyoi searched the ship for traces of Irene and Titosoga.
When she arrived at the meeting point, she caught sight of the two people in the midst of their mutiny.

"Aaaah! I can't see anything!"
"Everyone put on blindfolds! If you look directly at the intense light reflecting off the ice walls, you might go blind!"
"Titosoga... our preliminary investigation described him as an unremarkable student on the verge of failing, but to think he was hiding such skills. The top scholarship student's eye for talent is truly frightening."
"This is no time for admiration! If we don't stop them quickly, they'll break into the control room!"

The unexpected combination of Irene and Titosoga was causing thousands of crew members to panic, filling the entire floor with dazzling flashes of light.
While the synergy between ice walls and lighting was impressive, Pyoi felt troubled.

"If you're not going to be at the meeting point, why set one in the first place!"

At this rate, the two would succeed in their mutiny while she contributed nothing.

-Huh? Bikini armor? Don't be stupid. Someone capable of leading a ship mutiny wouldn't wear the clothes of an incompetent like you.

...She might be ridiculed like this!
Pyoi closed her eyes tight and stepped into the corridor where the light was so bright people could barely see their feet.
Late to the party.
Stubbornness.
It might be pointless.
Still, Pyoi moved forward for the pride of the Bikini Armor Brigade.
Just as she gathered her courage, the situation changed dramatically.

"Your rampage ends here."

With a loud explosion, two people were violently thrown out from the control room door.
Irene, with her flowing silver hair that clearly marked her as the Northern Grand Duchess, took a battle stance with her pale skin, alongside Titosoga, the Light Bearer, holding a spotlight so bright it hurt to look at him.
...Honestly, the light was so intense that it was difficult to make out skin color or facial features.
As she was about to greet them cheerfully and subtly join the mutiny to increase her contribution, her hand stopped involuntarily.
The exposed skin on her back, thanks to her open bikini armor outfit, tingled with warning.

Dangerous.
Don't go out.

From her hiding spot around the corner, she heard footsteps leisurely walking out from the control room.

"A ship mutiny. I didn't expect to see students following in our footsteps from five years ago. How delightful. Don't you agree, Kildum? Kekeke. You're right, Uson. These youngsters show great potential."

A man talking to himself while rolling his eyes at empty space, repeatedly changing his tone, atmosphere, posture, and gait as if becoming different people with each switch.

"Who are you?"
"You? That's wrong. You should call us 'we.'"

The man introduced himself, or rather, themselves.

"We are the apostle of Anrage. A reckless challenger who boarded to obtain the secret records of the Order hidden by the Foundation, and a corrupted collective entity where spirit and flesh have been molded into one."

An entity as dangerous as Jonna Wiheomhae, or perhaps even more dangerous—a Foundation executive who appeared from the control room.

'Anyone can see that a villain who talks to himself with multiple personalities is dangerous!'

This was the thought of a terrorist who forcibly made people wear bikini armor.


* * *


Arcadia returned to the cruise ship with a mission to start a mutiny to stop the auction. She felt a strong sense of discomfort.
The luxury cruise ship was still ridiculously large and magnificent, but many of the colorful lights that showcased its extravagance had disappeared.

'Why is the ship so dark?'

To save electricity.
To reduce magic stone waste.
If that had been their intention, they wouldn't have prepared such a large ship in the first place.
Something had happened.
Her "incident detection" ability, naturally honed through countless incidents at the Academy, was continuously sounding alarms within her.

Thud

A paper airplane touched her shoulder.
Its trajectory was terribly crooked, but anyone who had experienced the Grand Sports Festival would know that the formula contained inside was more important than the airplane itself.
When she unfolded it, she noticed a basic code inscribed with formulas.
A basic cipher from formula cryptology, which even nobles without magical talent learned as general knowledge.
When she entered the cabin with the number written on it, she found statue Logini and Sand Cooker, who had returned to the ship a day earlier.

"As expected, Lady Arcadia with her high noble sense would understand!"
"Sand Cooker. I assume a Magic Tower wizard is looking for me to request help with Logini's solution. I'm sorry, but right now..."
"Ah, the statue is fine."
"...Really?"
"If you put him in sunlight, the petrification will wear off on its own."

She felt a strange gaze, and realized the petrified Logini's eyes were staring directly at her.
Out of idle curiosity, she placed her hand on Sand Cooker's chest, and Logini's eyes tensed.
A warning glare that meant "don't mess with my colleague"!

'...What can you do with just your eyes when your body is petrified?'

Arcadia, who had returned to the cruise ship on the same day she purchased auction items for the mutiny, was feeling stressed.
And now she noticed the arrogant glare from statue Logini, who couldn't move a muscle due to petrification.
In short, Logini was unlucky.

'Should I take out some of my frustration on him?'

As Arcadia smiled meaningfully while looking back and forth between statue Logini and Sand Cooker, Logini's eyeballs trembled violently.
