# 138 - The Third Event

Heavy.
Mob tried to recall when was the last time he sweated just from walking.
At least not within the past five years.
Jaku, his childhood friend who left after only promising to meet again.
Until their reunion, he spent every day except January 1st each year on training, hunting, and side jobs.
It was because of his pride—he didn't want to appear pathetic in front of his friend when they reunited.

"Whoa. Mob, what are you wearing on your body?"
"Training equipment."
"You're hardcore. This is a dormitory. Don't tell me you walk around your room wearing that too?"
"I want to take it off too."
"Impressive. You want to take it off but won't until you've trained your body to a satisfactory level, right?"

His roommates in the four-person room burst into laughter.

"Hey, but you don't wear that when you shower, right?"
"...I got a free Clean Magic pass from Professor Platon."
"Wow. You'd go that far to avoid taking it off?"
"But who's Professor Platon?"
"I don't see him in the Swordsman Department lectures."
"He's a professor for advanced class common lectures."
"Amazing. You're so dedicated to training that you'd even seek out professors outside your curriculum to ask for favors?"
"Wow. It's students like him who escape the risk of failing."
"Respect. You're a real man, Mob."
"..."

His pride, unbending for ten years, made his shoulders rise involuntarily.

'What the hell am I doing? I should be asking for help to take this off, begging to be saved.'

In his mind, an angel-winged version of himself whispered to stop this foolish training and ask for help.

'But Oknodie personally helped me with this—would it be right to ask another professor to remove it?'

Conversely, a devil-winged version tempted him with this thought, which also seemed reasonable.

"Damn it..."

Unable to decide either way, Mob couldn't sleep.
As he tossed and turned in bed, muttering in agony, his roommates thought:
What a tough guy—suffering so much but never considering removing his training equipment.


* *


The next day.
During regular class time, Mob picked up a bow with an exhausted face.

Creak.

His arms felt as heavy as a thousand pounds as he drew the bowstring.
For good reason.
The weight his body had to bear was much greater than usual.
Gloves and shoes, four pieces at 5kg each.
Bearing a total of 20kg was stressful.
Yet when he met Oknodie in the classroom, she was all smiles.

"How is it? Getting used to it?"
"Somewhat."

Having tossed and turned all night, his eyes were sunken and he occasionally seemed dazed. Seeing this, Oknodie said cheerfully:

"You're better than yesterday!"
"Really? It's only been one day."
"When you do something extreme, your growth rate gets a bonus. Thanks to the 'cannot be removed' status, you've got quite a high growth acceleration!"
"...What a dubious growth acceleration."
"Still better than failing, right?"
"...Well, yes. Thanks to you, I think I'll survive."
"Right? Aren't you grateful?"

Mob smiled wryly.
Was she that eager to hear thanks?
Or maybe she was worried.
Worried that he might resent her for not being able to keep up with her hardcore training.
Honestly, Mob himself thought that if it weren't him, most students would have quit such training immediately yesterday.
It made sense that Oknodie would worry.

"Of course I'm grateful. It's tough, but if it's rewarding, enduring this is no problem. I'll definitely repay this favor somehow."
"Great! Then keep your evening free."
"Oh. More training?"
"I ordered 5kg protective gear for your shoulders and knees. I lined them with fabric and prepared straps so you can adjust the position and take them off easily!"
"...You're adding more weight?"
"And I also ordered 5kg spoons and chopsticks for meals, which should arrive soon. You'll eat with those starting tonight!"
"...That's it, right?"
"Yep!"
"Sigh... Chopsticks come in pairs, so that's three pieces, and elbow and knee protectors are four pieces, so that's seven more pieces adding 35kg?"

What is this, full-body armor weight adaptation training?
Unable to hide his bewilderment, Oknodie added with an "Oh right!"

"Bring all your daily necessities in this bag!"
"...Why?"
"I'll have everything made in 5kg versions for you!"

Stop it.
Save me.
Mob tilted his head back and trembled for a while, trying to hold back tears that were threatening to escape.

"Are you that moved? Hehe. You don't need to be so touched by our friendship!"

I should never ask Oknodie for training help ever again.
Belated regret washed over him.


* *


While the lower-class students were desperately making last-minute preparations for Friday's exam, the upper-class students were enjoying a relatively peaceful week.

"Did you see the notice? Professor Platon asked us to bring both uniforms and gym clothes on Thursday."
"Swimming again?"
"If it were swimming, he wouldn't have asked us to bring gym clothes."

Though there was a slightly ominous feeling, compared to the grueling first three weeks, the fourth week felt more comfortable as they had adapted to Academy life, allowing them to smile with ease.

"Come here, everyone!"

Outdoor lecture area.
Today again, the statue-like professor appeared with a majestic shout, followed by rumbling earth and rising dust.
Wondering what he had brought, all sorts of animals appeared—horses, cows, donkeys, cheetahs, and more.

"Gyaaah! What are all these?"
"Weakling♡ Barbarian♡ Don't people from the frontier know even this~?"

Mesugakki, the Second Imperial Princess, giggled confidently.

"They're all things we can eat as meat. This must be a cooking practice session♡"
"Ah, a golem just appeared at the back of the procession!"
"..."
"Does that mean we eat golems too?"

Jenya, who was annoyed at being ignored to her face, gave a vindictive smirk and turned the tables on the Mesugakki princess.

"So the Imperial Family eats golems too? How impressive! If I find any pebbles lying around, I'll be sure to gift them to you as snacks!"
"D-don't be ridiculous! There's no way someone as noble as me would eat such things."
"Then who eats them~? After confidently saying they were for eating, are you now admitting you actually know nothing and were just making things up? Among beastkin, we call that a defeated bitch!"
"D-defeated bitch?!"

How humiliating.
Unable to accept such a degrading label but also unable to retract her statement, the princess floundered. Seeing the small figure clutching her head in distress, Irene, the Northern Grand Duchess, abruptly interjected:

"Maybe Oknodie will eat it."
"...Nya?"
"Oknodie eats stones."

Convinced by this plausible argument, the Mesugakki princess breathed a sigh of relief and returned to her group.

"Did you hear? They say you'll eat it."
"Hmph. I don't eat such crudely large stones. I have standards, you know."
"...So you do eat stones after all."

Son O-cheon was dumbfounded.

"Whoa, whoa. Everyone, pay attention. These animals weren't brought to attack you, nor were they brought so you could run away before being caught after I release them in three minutes. That's content for senior classes, so rest assured!"
"Are we going to have lectures like that in second year?"
"I really don't want to advance..."

Whispers.
Seeing the first-year students starting to chatter, Professor Platon's face hardened.

"Silence Magic!!"

With a powerful shout, the professor placed a small stone fragment on his thumb and flicked it with his index finger.
With a sharp sound, the stone fragments embedded themselves in the heads of the students who were chattering in fear.

"Ow!"
"That hurts...!"
"If you fear the power of physical silence magic, keep your mouths shut. This is lecture time, isn't it?"
"..."

Once the students, afraid of the professor's physical magic, quieted down, Professor Platon returned to a friendly tone and explained about the animals.

"These animals were brought for riding."
"Riding?"
"Haven't I always said? I remain in this professorial position to save my homeland by nurturing true iron men. An iron man must first possess a strong body."
"What does that have to do with these animals?"
"You're slow to understand. What have you learned from me in previous lectures?"
"Running and swimming."
"As an extension of that, this time we'll master riding skills."

Running. Swimming. Riding.
The triathlon set for nurturing iron men.
At that enumeration, Arcadia, who was from the same country as Professor Platon, turned pale.

"Professor. Don't tell me we're going to... do that for the midterm exam?"
"Hahaha! As expected of a proud descendant of the Kingdom of Florence. That's right. You will participate in the legendary sport of the Kingdom of Florence—the Iron Man Triathlon. Riding is the third and final event!"
"Why are they freaking out about that?"

In response to Son O-cheon's ignorant question, the clever Gisele answered.

"Each event might be manageable on its own."
"And?"
"The Iron Man Triathlon involves doing all three consecutively."

Students who had been out of breath just from one lecture staggered or collapsed upon hearing they would have to do all three at once.

"Don't be so scared."

Even newbies can find ways to survive harsh tests.
She gave advice to the frightened Isabel, Gisele, and Lotto who had collapsed nearby.

"Riding is about handling a mount. If you choose a mount that suits you, you can breeze through it. If you choose your mount well, it could be the easiest part."

Somewhat relieved that only two parts would be difficult instead of all three, Gisele sighed in relief.

"I suppose someone familiar with horseback riding would find it easier with a horse. But what happens if someone chooses something like a cheetah?"
"Well... you'd have to establish dominance first."
"......"
"A carnivore's pride wouldn't want to carry an animal weaker than itself on its back."

For reference, there are 44 students in the advanced class.
The professor brought 100 mounts.
Of those, only 10 are horses.
The rest are cows, donkeys, cheetahs, and the like.

"Now choose your desired mount. It will be your partner until the midterm exam, so choose wisely."
"Professor. What happens if multiple students want the same mount?"
"Why ask such an obvious question? The strongest one gets it after fighting."

This was one minute before the fighting broke out.

"Why is everyone fighting like that? They could just avoid coveting horses like I did."
"...Many people wanted the golem you're riding too."

Hestia, sitting on another golem nearby, chided her with an incredulous face.

"Oh, come on, that's not true. Then why didn't anyone challenge me?"
"They said they didn't want to fight a future demon lord or make enemies with a demon king candidate. Any idea what that means?"
"W-well..."
