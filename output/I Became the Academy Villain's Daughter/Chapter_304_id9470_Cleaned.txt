# 304 - The Final Vessel

The Demon Contractor Low.
He was someone feared by all the skilled individuals of his time, including the hero candidate Kei.
He was so dangerous that the reason the Apostle of Anrage couldn't normally bring out their powerful egos was solely to seal away Low's ego.

A demon—a being that crosses the line.
The target of a hero's subjugation.

Such a being had now surfaced.
Even the first-year students of the 981st class now realized the danger.

"A dark barrier that surpasses the dark curtain. Impressive, but the elemental advantage is mine. Everyone, support me!"

Ishtar was confident.
The hero candidate Kei had been stronger than her.
Yet the reason Kei couldn't defeat her:
Kei hadn't truly tried to defeat her.
The vessels he targeted were the weak first-years, not strong vessels that would strengthen his competitors.
This man would be no different.
If she tried to claim Xing's vessel for herself, he wouldn't touch the other powerful vessels.
That arrogance would give them time.
And soon, when Oknodie and Jonna returned, the one-sided violence of the Apostle of Anrage would end.

"Let me correct one misunderstanding."

Low denied Ishtar's belief-laden slash right from the front.
His hand, enveloped in pitch-black dark mana, grabbed the sword energy and twisted it, squeezing it out into the air.

[Disruption]
[Guidance]
[Doppelganger]
[Stealth]
[Compulsion]
[5th Tier Illusion Magic - Gesture in Darkness]

Divine mana exists to destroy dark mana.
The power of the holy sword scattered into the air, chasing false signals that dark mana was present.
As Ishtar's arm instantly weakened and trembled, attacks from upper-class students helping her rained down from all directions.

"We just need to steer the ship to the uninhabited island. It's only 10 minutes. If we hold out for 10 minutes, we win!"

Oknodie's auction wouldn't continue to the end, but the situation here was that serious.
Surely she would understand.
Such firm belief crumbled with just one glance and gesture from Low as he looked at these annoying pests.

"Kyaaah!"
"Argh!"

Irene's ice magic deflected and froze an innocent cabin.
Son O-cheon's body was thrown through the wall and disappeared.
Even the upper-class students' combat power was useless against his strength.
The strength he displayed clearly had "mana" as its foundation.
Without exceeding a certain level of mana cultivation technique, there wasn't even a sliver of hope for victory.

-Even with multiple egos, they share one body! Due to the efficiency of mana cultivation techniques, the attack power might be high, but the actual mana possessed isn't that much...

—Crackle. Snap.
Gisele's voice coming from the ship's speaker was cut off.

"Yes, you figured out my weakness. So what difference does it make?"

Low casually stretched out his hand.

[Telekinesis]
[Absorption]
[Levitation]
[High-speed]
[4th Tier - Forced Summon]

"Aaaagh!"
"Kyaaah!"
"No, nooooo!"

Several crew members were dragged into Low's palm from beyond the broken wall.
They screamed and writhed in real-time before becoming dry twig-like figures with their body moisture evaporated, sprawled on the floor.
They were barely breathing, but with their life force completely drained, it wouldn't be strange if they died soon.
The crew members were already familiar with this sight.
+5 Enhanced Lighting Rod.
A cursed equipment that absorbs enormous amounts of mana.
Victims of it looked exactly like this.
Low could absorb mana from perfectly healthy people as harshly as cursed equipment could, and if mana was insufficient, he could even absorb life force—a truly vicious power.

"Don't tremble in fear. I won't devour vessels with promising futures. I'm different from Kei. He was a coward who wouldn't share great vessels. I'm different."

It was the exact opposite of Kei, who had conserved his power to maintain his ego intact while taking strong vessels for himself and passing weak vessels to others.
Low intended to tempt everyone by allowing them all the opportunity to obtain powerful vessels so they could escape from the Apostle of Anrage's body together.
In reality, many egos wavered at the chance to gain more benefit than when cooperating with Kei.

"No need to be impatient. Hero, I have no intention of taking your body either."

Heroes are sensitive to malice directed at them.
Especially since her awakening as a hero stemmed from her indignation at justice failing to correct evil.
The power of the monotheistic Sophemia warned her.
That man, the Demon Contractor Low, was clearly a dangerous target.
No less dangerous than the Imperial Professor Rave or the Foundation's butler Jonna.
However, that danger wouldn't come immediately.

"You, could it be...?"
"That's right. What I'm after is an even more powerful vessel. A being with potential beyond even a hero. The vessel of the Dark Princess Oknodie."

It was an absurd claim.
Low laughed loudly at her expression stained with disbelief.

"Does it seem impossible? Because Jonna Wiheomhae is there? Not so. Certainly, at my current level it would be difficult, but after absorbing mana, things will be different."

Low stopped Ishtar, who had sprung up and charged at him, by simply deploying a mana barrier without even grabbing her with his hand.
Ishtar was shocked to see that the number of barriers he deployed was significantly more than before he absorbed mana.
This man—just by absorbing what couldn't be considered a large amount, even including life force, the number of mana barriers had increased at least tenfold.
Those numerous barriers layered together, pressing down on her sword.
Unable to withstand the pressure, Ishtar was violently pushed back, tearing up the ground.

"Don't spout bullshit. You won't lay a finger on Oknodie!!"

Hestia, consumed by madness, charged recklessly forward, enveloped in bright red fighting spirit.
Low momentarily admired her appearance, but ultimately it was just instinctual rage from someone who hadn't mastered mana cultivation techniques.

"What a pity. If you had properly learned mana cultivation techniques, you could have amplified that power even more. Still, you pass. I'll allow you to become a vessel."

A thin but long mana barrier instantly penetrated toward Hestia's head as she broke through several barriers.
Instinctively, Hestia raised her rage power to protect her head and block the intrusion.
However, the collision of energies in close proximity to her head was precisely Low's objective.

[Adhesion]
[Vibration]
[Amplification]
[3rd Tier Amplification Magic - Shockwave]

A basic magic used to clear camping grounds of annoying stones, twigs, or small fragments that would otherwise bother your back or buttocks when setting up a tent.
What would normally be a 2nd tier level magic combining [Vibration] with [Light Wind] or [Vibration] with [Range] could be applied like this in the hands of a high-tier magician.
By infiltrating a thin line between the mana and eardrum, the shock of vibration is transmitted along the eardrum to the brain.
The aftermath of the intense vibration: a concussion.
Unable to withstand the shaking of her brain, Hestia's consciousness was instantly and forcibly cut off.

Thud.

An earthen wall that emerged from somewhere pushed back the man who had so easily knocked down upper-class students one after another.

"Sand Cooker, stop your magic right now!"
"I'm pushing him back just fine. I can hold out, so don't worry."
"That's not it. He's absorbing it!!"

The moment the pale-faced Sand Cooker stopped deploying his magic, the sand wall crumbled and flowed down.
Darkness beyond the gaping hole.
Low peeked his head out from behind the students trembling with indescribable foreboding and asked:

"Are you afraid? If you're scared to this extent, you shouldn't have charged in the first place."
"Aaaah!"
"H-how did you get here so quickly?!"
"Didn't you know? If you change the direction immediately after absorbing magic, this kind of application is possible."

The mana of Sand Cooker remaining at Low's fingertips ejected sharp earth spikes.
Sand Cooker, hit by the spikes, screamed with his thigh impaled against the wall.

"Hmm... that was close. Still a failure, I suppose?"

A finger rising toward Sand Cooker's head.
Just as Sand Cooker closed his eyes tightly in fear, the floor collapsed with a rumble.
Black Knight Mob and Jaku, who had struck the ceiling with all their might from the floor below, were now regretting if this was really okay.

"That was quite good just now."

The two students, feeling a chill from Low who spoke with just his lips, had barely retreated when a woman shouted:

"Move aside. Unless you want to burn to death."

Rogini, enraged at Sand Cooker's crisis.
With her body not yet fully recovered from petrification, she forcibly raised mana and unleashed [Flame Projection].

"Did we get him?"
"You crazy bastard! What did you just do?"
"Aaaah, you insensitive jerk!"
"Ah, no. I was just curious."
"No matter what, there are things you should and shouldn't say. Don't you know about TPO? Time, place, and occasion!"

The Jigoku Pirate Gang minion who had spoken out of turn was scolded to tears and shut his mouth.
Though not entirely his fault, predictably, Low appeared very much intact, surrounded by mana barriers.

"You must have been quite upset about the earth magician's injury. You're truly a perfect match."
"W-what are you saying! I've never felt any romantic emotions for that guy. Besides, there's no way a marriage between the Red Magic Tower and the Yellow Magic Tower would be possible!"

Rogini, who had flinched on her own and shouted, was met with Low chuckling and extending his palm.

"You've really swallowed a big misunderstanding. I didn't mean that kind of match. I meant you match in being immature and stupid."
"P-pull your head back!!"

The terrified Jaku and Mob pulled back Rogini, whose body had stiffened.
From inside a deeply dug hole beneath the three who had fallen with a thud, the flame magic Low had absorbed rose as a tall pillar of fire.
Every magical attack was absorbed and returned as an even more intense or vicious counterattack.
Physical attacks were blocked by powerful mana barriers and instantly resulted in unconsciousness.
Low, who didn't hesitate—even eagerly—to knock out upper-class students and treat them as sacrifices, grinned.

"The synergy effect is good. This is enough for me to acknowledge you as extras. All of you win vessels."
"Huh~? Who decided that? If you've handled dark mana, be prepared to be controlled♡"

Kill yourself.
Low's hand, which had been rising toward his own neck at the Mesugakki Princess's command, suddenly twisted and shot magic toward her.
Jaku rushed in and rescued the surprised Mesugakki Princess.

"Are you stupid? A demon contractor has far more dark mana than an ordinary dark mana user."
"Mmm. Dark mana is useless..."
"Haha. Interesting characters keep appearing without end. What is this place? An amusement park?"

Low laughed out loud, each step accompanied by explosions and screams.
The battle that had begun hours before the start of the final uninhabited island auction had long since ended by the time Oknodie returned.

"I've been waiting for you. The final vessel."

When Oknodie, the butler, and the maids boarded the ship, rubbing their sleepy eyes, they were greeted by Low, who had come up to the deck to welcome them while keeping the crew members caged.
