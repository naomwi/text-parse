# Enhanced Translation: Chapter 167 - The Curse Commotion

After enchanting the fake ghost into the ring, my daily life started becoming noticeably inconvenient.

"Huh? I'm sure I left my stationery on the desk, so why is it on the floor?"
"Me too! The hairpin I put in my hair earlier somehow ended up in my uniform pocket."
"Maybe you're just forgetful~?"
"You think so?"
"Don't be so embarrassed. I forget things too sometimes, like when I got 40 points on a pop quiz!"
"...That's because you didn't study."

Students sitting around me during lectures getting caught up in strange incidents had become routine.

"Ugh. Look at this, Oknodie. My straw is wet."
"S-sorry."
"Why are you apologizing, Oknodie...?"

Zuang, whom I happened to meet in front of the cafeteria and was eating lunch with, pouted while touching the straw that the ghost had already sucked on.

"Now, agile movement is essential for vigilante activities. Today, we'll test your flexibility with pole vaulting, long jump, limbo posture, and arm extension while seated."

When it was my turn to prepare for a high jump, Lin, who had emerged from the ring in ghost form without anyone noticing, even lifted the pole slightly with her hand!

I was astounded by her persistent interference with my evaluation, but no way was that going to work! Pretending to take a running start, I moved 20 meters away, and while Lin couldn't grab the pole, I quickly ran and jumped over it.

[You have successfully completed the pole vault despite the ghost's interference.]
[Long Jump experience +3]
[Evasion experience +1]
[Leap experience +1]

Hmph! Trying to hold back a veteran player? Not a chance!

As I proudly snorted and taunted her, she appeared right in front of the pole when it was time for the next jump, which was 10cm higher.

Instead of subtly interfering, the fake Lin grabbed the pole with both hands and lifted it as high as she could above her head.

"What's that...?"
"Why is the pole floating up by itself?"
"Student using telekinesis magic. While I appreciate the strategy of interfering with other students to preserve your own score, be aware that obvious cheating will result in point deductions."

Professor Bronze's comment sparked controversy—is cheating okay as long as you don't get caught?? But the atmosphere grew serious as the ghost refused to put down the pole.

"Perhaps I wasn't clear enough. Put the pole back where it belongs."
"..."
"Ignoring a professor's instructions? Do you want to lose points?"

Since no one had actually cast telekinesis magic, the pole naturally wouldn't go down.
An angry Professor Bronze released a detection wave, attempting to identify whoever cast the telekinesis spell.
It was magic that detected mana caught in the wavelength to identify which sorcerer the magic was connected to.
A practical magic worthy of the world's greatest vigilante.
But since no one had cast any magic, naturally no one would be caught.

"It's not magic...?"

A pole floating on its own, not even by magic.
Of course, the classroom was thrown into chaos.

"What is that?"
"Why is it doing that?"
"Could it be a ghost?"

The fake Lin, startled by the mention of a ghost, returned to the ring.
Clang.
A coincidental timing that made it seem like it had understood and responded to the student's words.
Which it actually had.
Students who didn't know what was happening screamed "Eek!" and backed away in panic.

"Well. Since safety accidents could occur, we'll end today's practical lesson here."

In the end, the class was replaced with a theoretical lecture, but among the students taking the Developing an Eye class, strange ghost stories began to circulate.

"What could that ghost have been?"
"Perhaps it's the ghost of a student who failed at pole vaulting, didn't receive additional points, and starved to death?"
"Gisele, not everything that comes to mind needs to be said."
"Haha. Sorry. I just wanted to lighten the mood a bit."
"Still, it's creepy. I feel like it's a ghost that died in an unfortunate accident and harbors resentment, wanting to make students get hurt. Are you okay, Oknodie?"
"I'm fine! I'm physically strong, so I won't die even if I trip!"
"We're just worried about you."
"Young lady, I know you're independent, but if anything happens, you must ask us for help. Understood?"
"I will."

In the end, I even made Isabel and Gisele worry.
This troublemaking ghost!

"Are you really going to keep this up? People around me keep getting suspicious because of you."

The fake Lin silently crouched down, picked up the chain, and shook it, protesting that if I was annoyed, I should release the chain.
As if I'd listen to her silent protest when she causes so much trouble even while bound!

"Hmph! I'm never going to release you. Nyah!"
"..."

Like an impudent cat staring directly at its owner, the ghost looked straight at me without averting its gaze.
A battle of wills began between me and the fake Lin, like beasts trying to establish dominance.

Early evening, as the sun began to set.
Zuang and Titosogar were heading to the classroom for the Adventurer's Night Activity lecture.
Although the two usually chatted about various things, with the relatively more timid Titosogar typically doing most of the talking, today Zuang spoke first.

"Haven't there been a lot of strange things happening around Oknodie lately?"
"Oh, that? I heard about it too. I have a friend named Dorothy who went with Oknodie to the deep forest by carriage over the weekend. She said they found something strange there."

Flinch.
Zuang's shoulders trembled slightly at the scary story that emerged while they were on their way to an already frightening lecture.
Titosogar, focused on the conversation and looking straight ahead, didn't notice Zuang's fearful expression.

"Something strange?"
"She said it might be a cursed object."
"You can't kill a curse with a dagger."
"...Isn't your point of fear a bit odd?"
"But it's still scary. Many targets use curses to prevent assassins."
"Huh. I didn't know that."
"Your papa is a market deputy, right? It's good to prepare defensive measures with curses in advance. Targets protected by skilled witches aren't touched by assassins because they fear being cursed."
"What kind of curses can they get?"
"There are many. Curses that make you blind, curses that make your appearance ugly."

That's why curses are feared in the assassin industry.

"Are there such scary curses?"
"They're not common. I know that powerful curses have strict conditions for activation. Either the materials are extremely expensive, the conditions are very complicated, or they require tremendous dedication."
"Not much different from other fields. Ugh. Still, it's scary. I'd be afraid of accidentally getting cursed myself if I were protected by such curses."

Titosogar rubbed the goosebumps on his forearm.

"Do you think Oknodie is under such a curse?"
"Probably not that severe, but she does seem affected by something. After all, Oknodie is an assassin."
"Would the infirmary remove curses too?"
"It would be expensive."
"How are curses lifted?"
"Some disappear naturally over time, while others require special lifting procedures. The more potent the curse, the more difficult and complicated these procedures are, making them almost impossible to accomplish by chance."
"True. If Oknodie got a curse that could be lifted by eating a lot, she'd be cured in a day."

As they discussed curses, they finally arrived at the classroom.
Oknodie, who had arrived earlier, greeted them with a "Hello!" from beside the professor.

"...Hello."
"The professor is already here..."

Zuang and Titosogar's eyes clouded over.
Whatever curse Oknodie might be under, it couldn't be scarier than facing the real Professor Sadako, who would be terrifying even in nightmares.

"...Seems like you've developed an interest in curses."
"Yes... We thought Oknodie might be under a curse..."
"She's not. She's just been near a cursed object, so some of the curse's energy has transferred to her..."
"I-I see... But how do you know that, Professor?"
"The curse that might have transferred to Oknodie is likely residual from the curse waste used in my lecture..."
"Your lecture, Professor Sadako?"
"Black magic and curses are inseparable... You who have entered the path of darkness will have to learn about them in your second year..."
"We're here for the Adventurer's Night Activity lecture, right?!"
"...Let's begin the lecture..."
"Please don't evade the question..."

The brief chat only created anxiety about the future (second year).
But the next day, Titosogar realized that yesterday's conversation wasn't entirely in vain.

"Announcement for first-year students. Due to a contagious curse circulating on campus, please make sure to carry the 'Protection Charm' placed in front of your door when going out.
The Academy will not be responsible for any mishaps that occur due to not carrying the Protection Charm. You can check this notice on the first-floor bulletin board.
This was an announcement from the first-year girls' dormitory supervisor's office."

Ding-dong-dang-dong.
As the announcement ended with a bell sound, tremendous anxiety swept over Titosogar's timid heart.
What kind of curse could be circulating?
At times like this, he needed friends who knew about curses.
Zuang and Oknodie.
The two assassins would surely know.

"Zuang, it's me! Open the door. Tell me more about curses. I'm so scared!"
"...Don't call me. I don't want to get infected by the curse. You said you wanted to know about curses, right? Stay locked in your room too. I'm not moving an inch from my room this weekend."

The consultation with Zuang only increased his anxiety.
Now Oknodie was his only hope!

"You're worried about the curse?"
"Yes, yes. Isn't there some way to deal with it?"

Oknodie, who poked her head through the doorway, said with a puzzled expression:

"Just get cursed. Why are you afraid?"
"...What?"
"Once you get cursed and overcome it, your curse resistance increases. So the more curses you get, the better!"

What in the...
Titosogar was left speechless at Oknodie's cheerful explanation.
Did the Foundation make her do such things too...?
