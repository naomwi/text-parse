# 166 - What Happens When You Don't Lock the Door

I excitedly returned to my room with the ink and completed the magic circle I had been drawing.

With my veteran knowledge, I had already memorized the entire magic circle. Now with all the materials prepared and the mana I'd diligently collected by consuming Stat Stones, ta-da!

<Curse Magic Circle>
<Curse of Binding - Ghost Ring Binding>
<Curse of Restriction - Ghost Movement Range Limitation>

<Titoso Family Friendship Ring (Bound)>
Rank - Unique Grade 6
Description - A gift from Ghost Papa commemorating the unwavering friendship of the Titoso family. If the current ring wearer is unwilling, other individuals cannot gain effects from wearing the ring.
Effect 1 - Life gauge check
Effect 2 - Life transfer
Effect 3 - When within 20m of another ring owner, all abilities increase
Effect 4 - Instead of activating Effect 1, stores ghost cluster (fake Lin) in the ring
Effect 5 - Instead of activating Effect 2, prevents ghost cluster (fake Lin) from leaving beyond 20m from the ring
Appraisal Value - 150 gold coins, 15,000 points

Through a complex process, I bound fake Lin to the item and tightly restricted her movement range.

The item's rank decreased as a result, but that was to be expected considering fake Lin wasn't a high-combat ghost powerful enough to replace the life transfer function.

"...Is 20m too long?"

I placed the ring in one corner of the room and sat in the opposite diagonal corner, which was just barely over 20m away.

Swoosh.

Fake Lin emerged from the ring and tried walking on two feet, but kept staggering and falling repeatedly.

As the range restriction placed spiritual chains on the ghost's walking ability, she crawled awkwardly along the floor like someone who had forgotten how to walk.

Tug.

Feeling the chains pulling at her ankles, fake Lin eventually couldn't move any further.

"It's 20m! Don't wander too far because Xing will worry. From now on, you need to stay within that range, okay?"

Fake Lin looked up at me with an expressionless face that somehow seemed a bit angry.

Thump! Thump!

"Ow, ow! Don't hit me. Don't hit meee!"

Fake Lin was hitting my head with her fists.

The ghost's fists, which should have passed right through me, somehow caused real impact thanks to the life force she'd received, materializing with tangible force. Though she wasn't professionally trained in martial arts so it wasn't terribly painful, it definitely couldn't be called a love tap or cotton punch.

"Siiiing. If you don't sleep, you won't grow taller!"

Fake Lin defiantly lifted one leg, demonstrating the chains as if demanding I do something about them.

Of course, I wasn't about to release her, and I ended up being tormented by the ghost all night long.

But as I stayed up through the night, a random question suddenly occurred to me.

'Did I lock the door when I went out to get ink?'

Ah, whatever. It's probably fine.

If someone had come in, Baby Mandrake would have cried "waaah waaah" to let me know.

I'm sure nothing happened at all.


* * *


Hestia wasn't particularly skilled at cooking.

A mercenary's cooking always prioritizes quick preparation and high calories.

Their soul food is a hodgepodge stew made by dumping hunted game meat and whatever ingredients everyone brought into a pot and boiling it until everything turns to mush.

'I want to serve Oknodie a proper meal as a token of gratitude, but this kind of food won't do!'

Normally, with her Berserker class characteristics, she would have spent her Academy days lonely and friendless. But thanks to the protection of Oknodie, the class co-valedictorian, Hestia's Academy life wasn't too bad.

Even Lotto the fighter, who once picked a fight with her, maintained a cordial relationship as they were in the same group, and their other teammate Jigoku had come to acknowledge her as a comrade.

"Be good to her."
"Suddenly? What do you mean?"
"That little Oknodie seems to follow you around quite a bit."

Jigoku, the pirate hunter who roamed freely throughout the Academy wearing a pirate captain hat with a gun and sword at his side, despite his gruff personality, cared for Oknodie just as much as she did.

"I know. I've received plenty of help from Oknodie. That's why I want to treat her to a home-cooked meal."
"Oh? You can cook too? Let me taste it. I'll give you my review."

Hestia ambitiously showcased a dish she would prepare during camping when they had ample time and resources.

Not the barbaric hodgepodge stew where all ingredients are dumped into a pot, but a 'grilled' dish with meat cooked directly over fire!

Sizzle, sizzle.

The well-cooked meat gave off a strong gamey smell.

Though she had seasoned it with salt, she couldn't deny it was inferior in quality compared to the Academy's dining hall food.

"It's edible."
"Are you picking a fight?"
"Do you think Oknodie cares about taste? She'll eat it as long as it's edible. She even eats rocks."
"...You're right."

Encouraged by Jigoku's review, Hestia prepared meat skewers, grilling the meat outside in time for Oknodie's return.

'I want to see her eating food I made.'

She would have preferred to hand over the food in person, but Oknodie usually returned quite late.

Figuring she was probably out doing something strange that only she understood, Hestia was about to give up when a bone carriage led by a skeletal horse stopped outside the window, bringing with it an eerie atmosphere.

Oknodie stepped out of the carriage carrying a large sack, while a suspicious person sat silently in the dark carriage, seeing her and her friend off.

"...What on earth have you been doing?"

Though she had a mountain of questions, she worried that being too nosy might annoy Oknodie.

While hesitating, unable to decide what to do, Oknodie went out again.

'Sigh. I'll just leave the food in front of her door.'

This was a hallway for advanced class students, so surely no one would steal it.

Even if someone did take it, the taste would make them want to put it back after one bite.

As she placed the plate in front of the door, she noticed that Oknodie had forgotten to close her door properly.

"This girl is so careless. Where did she leave her mind?"

Thinking that after riding such a scary carriage, it was understandable to be distracted, she was about to close the door when she glimpsed something strange through the gap.

An unfinished, suspicious pattern drawn on the floor.

She shouldn't enter someone else's room.

Oknodie would surely be angry.

Despite knowing she shouldn't, humans often break taboos.

'I'll just take a quick peek. Really quick.'

With this poor rationalization, she opened the door.

What Hestia saw on the floor shocked her.

There was a magic circle.

In a room already piled high with ominous things, an unidentified suspicious magic circle.

'Oknodie... are you really trying to become a demon lord?!'

Hestia quickly entered the room and closed the door.

No one else should see this.

More importantly, this shouldn't be happening.

An obviously suspicious magic circle.

Could it be for summoning demons?

It gave her chills.

Judging by the cursed energy emanating from the room, this was no ordinary matter.

The magic circle was giving off such an eerie aura even though it wasn't fully drawn!

"Waah."

She heard the sound after regaining her composure.

In the dim room.

A suspicious bottle containing some kind of liquid.

A strange creature with a human face floating slowly inside.

Hestia covered her mouth.

Otherwise, she might have screamed.

'Oknodie... what on earth are you raising in your room?!'

Mandrake—an ultra-rare plant.

Even Hestia, a veteran mercenary, didn't know what a real one looked like.

Most mercenaries don't know how to safely harvest mandrakes.

Those who don't know what they look like are better off.

Those with half-knowledge try to harvest this expensive plant out of greed or attempt collection based on incorrect information, only to die with blood flowing from their ears.

Either live without knowing and avoid greed.

Or know and die from greed.

Hestia belonged to the former category.

To her, mandrake was an unknown creature.

Something unbelievably inhuman with a human face on a plant body.

"You... are you perhaps human? Were you human but got trapped in that body?"
"Waah."
"Say something!"

The mandrake raised its rootlets to point at something.

It was the plate Hestia had brought.

"This? You want this?"

Pat pat.

The mandrake tapped the water surface with its rootlets, urging her to place the plate in front of it.

After setting down the plate, Hestia watched the mandrake pick up and eat the meat skewer, firmly believing it must have been human.

"Did Oknodie make you like this?"
"Waah."
"No?"
"Waah."

Though communication seemed impossible, Hestia roughly grasped the mandrake's meaning.

A mercenary who can't read an opponent's next move or hostility by intuition when encountering a monstrous creature in foreign lands won't survive long.

<Function - Eye Measurement>
<Function - Guesswork>

It wasn't a monster created by Oknodie.

The mandrake's response suggested that Oknodie had rescued it.

But where did Oknodie bring such a horrific experimental subject that had once been human but lost its human form?

Hestia could only think of one possibility.

The visit.
The butler.
The Foundation.

A drama unfolded in Hestia's mind.

Late at night.
The butler who reportedly had a meeting with Oknodie.
Oknodie tilts her head and asks him:

"Where's the friend who was supposed to come with you?"

Oknodie nodding with an innocent face.
The butler smiling sinisterly as he takes out a bottle.

"Ah... you mean this?"

Before entering the Academy.
A prospective scholarship student raised in the Foundation just like Oknodie.
But unlike Oknodie who was accepted, the friend failed to get into the Academy.

This was the price.
A tragic result of losing human form.
Oknodie, unable to contain her grief, wails in despair.
The butler gives her a cruel warning:

"Don't go against the Foundation's will. You could end up like this next."

The butler leaves, and she is left to live with her friend who has become a monster.
And she begins searching.
Even if it means boarding suspicious carriages, she is determined to break the curse on her friend.

'The ominous cursed energy in the room, the suspicious magic circle, rushing out without remembering to close the door—it was all for her friend!'

Hestia struggled to hold back tears.
Clack clack.

As the mandrake put down the finished skewer on the plate, Hestia collected the plate and promised with a voice mixed with tears:

"You wanted meat? I'll bring you more next time..."

The mandrake splashed around excitedly, experiencing food other than culture medium for the first time in a long while.

Seeing its innocent behavior, Hestia finally shed silent tears and hurriedly fled the room.

"Waah."

And that's what happened in a single night because Oknodie didn't lock her door.
