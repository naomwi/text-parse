# 106 - I Reflected

Monk Myeongho had prepared himself.
Even for the task of cleaning up Oknodie's corpse.
It was just ahead.
The distinctive musty smell of fungal monsters reached him first, carried by the wind.
Monk Myeongho entered with firm mental preparation.
What greeted his eyes was a Clawman holding its nail-claws against Oknodie's neck.

"Aak!"

Oknodie screamed.
Monk Myeongho's hand shot out like lightning.

"How dare you!!"

With his face contorted fiercely like a hell demon, Monk Myeongho struck with a powerful blow.
Following his hand, the Clawman's body was reduced to a handful of flesh pieces splattered across the dungeon wall.
Fearing spore dispersal, he gathered the surrounding air with "Concentration" and smashed it against the wall in one strike, instantly killing the Clawman.

"Eh?"

Oknodie blinked, unable to process what had just happened.
Monk Myeongho's heart ached at her pitiful appearance.
How much mental suffering must this young one have endured, surrounded by vicious monsters?

"It's alright now. Do you remember me? I'm Monk Myeongho who supervised the first entrance exam."
"Why did you kill it?!"
"If you're worried about spores, there's no need. I eliminated it without leaving a single particle that might escape, in case you were being controlled by a fungal monster."

You can rest assured, he was about to say with a kind smile, but cracks began to appear in his expression.
Oknodie was looking at him accusingly, as if he had done something terrible.

"Why did you do that?!"
"...Was it not a dangerous situation?"
"Of course not!"

Had he underestimated the group's top student?
Oknodie showed no fear of monsters whatsoever.
Beyond fearlessness, she even seemed sad, as if she'd lost a pet.
What on earth had happened?
How could she display such a bold attitude?

"What exactly were you doing here?"
"I was getting a haircut!"
"A haircut...?"
"Geez. You might be fine as a bald monk, but my hair is long!"
"......"

Oknodie.
She had been using the Clawman as a substitute hairdresser.


* * *


Wild monsters are commonly thought to be heartless creatures that kill and eat each other on sight, but surprisingly, many enjoy symbiotic relationships.
For instance, Clawmen, whom bronze-plate adventurers typically face, have very clear power dynamics.
As weaker monsters, they flash their blade-like nails and rampage like Lu Bu, but before stronger monsters, they're quick to prove their harmlessness.
They insist they're good monsters.
That they'd be useful if not killed.
And the way they demonstrate this is through grooming services with their blade-nails.

"Perfect timing. Cut my hair a bit!"

The Clawman stood there blankly before turning to look at the Red Fang Mushrooms.
The Red Fang Mushrooms shook their heads and sighed, telling it to just do as told.
This was the result of humans being recognized as "stronger beings" by these creatures.

'After seniors captured and experimented on all of them, would they see humans as prey rather than predators? Absolutely not.'

In truth, monsters aren't much different from animals.
They cower before larger creatures, feel threatened by intimidating sounds, and grow anxious when behaviors or colors seem strange.
I guarantee that if Titosoga just held up a spotlight and flashed it, he could intimidate the top 20% of monsters worldwide with that spotlight alone.

Stone plate. Top 90%. Pushovers.
Bronze plate. Top 50%. Monsters.
Iron plate. Top 10%. Dangerous targets.
Silver plate. Top 1%. Regional evacuation order.
Gold plate. Top 0.1%. National disaster.
Platinum plate. Top 0.01%. Global crisis.

This is roughly how monsters break down by percentage.
Among them, Clawmen are at the level where you'd say, "Ah, from this point we can call them monsters."
They're dangerous, but once you show them the power difference, they obey like whimpering puppies.

"Cut just 1 centimeter more!"

Snip.

"Hey!! Who told you to give me a bowl cut?!"

When I shouted, its shoulders hunched up and it looked thoroughly intimidated.
Look at how it cowers, afraid humans might harm it. Typical bronze-plate monster behavior.

"How do you expect to survive in this harsh Academy like that? You need to cut hair well to get a job as a hairstylist, you know?"

If even dangerous monsters can coexist with each other, there's no reason they can't with humans.
I'm not just having it cut my hair because it's too long and washing takes too much time.
It's also to demonstrate these poor creatures' harmlessness to the Academy and help them get jobs.

'If I regularly tame monsters, I might get the Taming title!'

A well-intentioned effort for titles or achievements!
Regardless of my impure motives, it's mutually beneficial.
I get titles or achievements that grant stat bonuses, while the monsters avoid becoming experimental subjects for seniors and even earn wages.

"Aak!"

So they need to cut hair well, but these stupid monsters just cut hair crookedly without any care.
I was shouting in frustration when the monk appeared out of nowhere and blasted the Clawman to pieces.
That was the whole story of what I experienced.


* * *


"Have you finished your reflection paper?"
"Yes..."

In the end, I was dragged to the main building and found myself submitting a reflection paper.
The reason: recklessly doing dangerous things.
It's a clear rule violation for a student without professional taming training to attempt to train monsters.

"Mocking baldness is a terrible insult that could get you killed at the Academy. It's something you should reflect on even more than treating dangerous beasts as friends."
"Yes..."
"I hope you're sincerely remorseful, not just giving the right answers."

Monk Myeongho was clearly angry, continuing to admonish me even after receiving my reflection paper.
I ended up missing the second period "Adventure Tales of a Retired Hero" lecture, but fortunately, thanks to Monk Myeongho contacting them separately, I didn't receive any penalty points.

"As expected of an aspiring Adventure Department student, you've had quite the adventure."

Professor Destroyer personally came to the reflection room to say a few words.

"Monk Myeongho. May I take this child now?"
"Of course. I apologize for keeping your student."
"Not at all. I'll make sure to scold her thoroughly on your behalf as well."

Professor Destroyer took custody of me as if receiving a handover.
He looked down at me while stroking his rough beard.

"I'm sorry..."
"It's fine. You didn't really do anything wrong to me."
"Aren't you angry?"
"If I got angry over something like this, I would have killed Nyarlathotep a hundred times over with my own hands. He was truly a troublemaker."
"What kind of trouble did he cause?"
"He tried to experiment in the rapid cultivation room to see if breeding flying monsters with broken wings would produce offspring with broken wings, and ended up creating the earth dragon Drake."
"Oh."
"Don't be impressed. That Drake still devours 1 billion tons of grain annually in the western agricultural belt. It's fortunate that he created some foundation that earns a lot of money and pays compensation to farmers every year. Otherwise, the hero party would have been nationally wanted criminals."

This cycle's former hero Nyarlathotep seems to be living quite an interesting life.
But that foundation couldn't be the Wiheomhae Foundation, could it?

"Your friend is here. I'll stop the lecture now. You must have been quite shocked, so let her comfort you."

A great adult knows when to step back.
Destroyer put his hands in his pockets and went on his way after our brief conversation.

"Oknodie!"
"Ow-ow-ow-ow!"
"Do you know how worried I was?!"
"Ow-ow-ow-ow!"
"I heard you even let a monster cut your hair! There wasn't a pirate as crazy as you!"
"S-stop sh-shaking me-!"
"What if you had died? Were you planning to drive me mad with guilt?"
"......"

I think I'm about to suffer injuries I didn't even get from monsters.
My head is being shaken so hard I might get a concussion!

The student who watched Oknodie and Jigoku's reunion from around the corner quietly turned away.

"I see you're safe too, student. Please be careful not to have accidents falling into waterways in the future."
"Thank you, Monk Myeongho."

The lower-class student acknowledged Monk Myeongho's greeting with an awkward smile.
As the monk's concerned gaze left, the smile on the student's face turned cold.

'Bad luck. I thought it was a perfect opportunity to accomplish something without lifting a finger.'

Monk Myeongho frequently hovered around Oknodie.
Whether it was surveillance or protection, the situation made it impossible to make a move against her while he was watching.
Wanting to seize this opportunity, the student had distracted Monk Myeongho at the right moment, but Oknodie returned unharmed even after opening the monster storage.

'As expected, the Foundation's top scholarship student isn't easy to deal with.'

The anonymous lower-class student gathered their disappointed thoughts.
There would be plenty of opportunities.

'If it weren't for you, I could have been the most successful scholarship student in this batch. Whenever the opportunity comes, I will definitely eliminate you, Oknodie.'

The anonymous student's identity: a scholarship student from the Wiheomhae Foundation.
A competitor from the Foundation targeting Oknodie.

"Hooh. How interesting that a student harbors hostility toward that child."

And watching this student was Destroyer, walking upside down on the ceiling.
