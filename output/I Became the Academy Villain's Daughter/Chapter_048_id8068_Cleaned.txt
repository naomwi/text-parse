# <48 - First Practical Exercise>

Unlike what the professor had said about the <Developing an Eye> lecture being available to both first and second-year students, there were very few second-years actually sitting in the classroom.
Isabel felt an ominous warning from her adventurer's intuition.

"Why are there so few second-years?"
"It's still time to choose lectures, isn't it? I'm sure more people will come when the lecture starts."

As Gisele said, the number of students visiting the classroom gradually increased as time passed.
However, the vast majority of newcomers were first-years, and not a single additional second-year appeared.

"As I thought, they're not increasing."

Perhaps Isabel's anxiety was contagious.
Unable to hide his uneasiness, Gisele walked over to a second-year and asked.

"May I ask you something, senior? I noticed there are unusually few second-year seniors taking this lecture."
"Worried because there are only three of us second-years compared to all these first-years?"

When Gisele responded with a friendly smile and vague answer, the second-year senior Bigstone fiddled with the emblem on his uniform that symbolized his second-year status.
Senior, huh.
Since they too had been first-years until last year, the unfamiliar title felt strange yet desirable, making him want to show off a bit and delay his answer.

"I think I know why."
"Huh?"
"Could it be because this lecture has a bad reputation?"

Oknodie jumped into the conversation between Gisele and the second-year senior with a curious expression.

"Ah, no! Don't say such terrible things. The professor is here, what are you saying..."
"Then why are you sweating?"
"Who says I'm sweating?!"
"You are, senior."
"No, I'm not!"
"Could it be that only three people are taking this class because you all failed with F grades last year and have to retake it?"
"No! It's not like the professor gives stingy grades or assigns difficult tasks or has rumors about being dangerous!"

Several first-years who had been eavesdropping with interest left the classroom with disappointed expressions.

"And I'm definitely not trying to hide this from first-years hoping to have more people below me to boost my grade!!"

About a dozen students glared at the senior before leaving the classroom.

"And there's absolutely no risk of being treated as potential thieves or getting a bad reputation because the professor used to be a righteous thief in the Holy Central Empire!!"

Half of the remaining students hurriedly followed the first-years who had already left.
Most were Group B freshmen who lacked the courage to take a forbidden lecture that could block their path to success.

"Hooh. Bigstone. I never knew you were attending my lecture with such impure intentions."
"Aaaack! I've been caught!!"

He could have just kept quiet.
Why did he suddenly self-destruct?
Isabel inwardly thought what a strange senior he was.
Giggle giggle.
Finding his predicament amusing, Oknodie covered her mouth with her hand and laughed, just as Arcadia typically did.
Since Arcadia's gesture was itself an imitation of imperial noble ladies, it ultimately became an imitation of imperial nobility, but it looked cute regardless.

"Sh-should we leave too?"
"Oknodie is staying."
"If the top student from Group A can handle it, why should we care what the imperial folks think?"
"Central Empire."

A freshman sitting in the corner of the classroom corrected with a solemn voice.

"The term 'empire' refers to both the Central Empire and the Eastern Empire simultaneously. It's unpleasant to be treated as the same, so please make the distinction clear."

It was Xing, the Eastern swordsman who had displayed a remarkable presence since the entrance exam.

"Whoa, look at his expression. He seems really angry."
"Sorry. I'd be upset too if I were lumped together with Central Empire folks."
"Of course we meant the Central Empire."

Xing closed his eyes again and lowered the hand he had placed on his scabbard.
Only then did the tension that had filled the classroom subside.

"Don't misunderstand, you guys. Bigstone isn't mentally ill; he just accidentally inhaled potion fumes during preparation and is suffering from the <Truth Confession> status ailment that lasts for a week."
"That's not true! Actually, this is the friend I've been secretly in love with, and I've never confessed because of a potion effect nor been rejected! I don't feel suicidal and I'm not embarrassed to make eye contact!"
"...He's in this state."

This senior is seriously confessing everything.
But more importantly, could there be a chance?
She rejected the confession but they're still hanging out together.
The first-years' gazes were filled with curiosity.
While questions poured in about when he confessed, what kind of exams came up in the first semester midterms, and where the hidden vault was located, Isabel checked on Oknodie.

"How about you, Oknodie? Do you think this lecture will be okay?"
"Of course! The lecture might be difficult with stingy grades and a reputation that goes down the drain, but as long as it's fun, it's fine!"
"......"

Should she escape from the classroom for the sake of her own future, if not for Oknodie's?
While seriously contemplating this, Isabel realized that too much time had been wasted because of the second-year senior.

Ding-a-ling...

"That's enough chatting. It's time for the lecture to begin."

The lecture started.


* * *


The professor began with his self-introduction.
Bronze de estrada.
The name was written in elegant cursive on the blackboard.

"Bronze de Estrada. That is my name."

Ding-a-ling...

"As Bigstone mentioned, before being invited as a professor at the Academy, I was active as a righteous thief in the Central Empire."

Ding-a-ling...

"Some may doubt my qualifications, but a righteous thief is a profession that adventures through the empire's night streets. I am more than qualified to be a professor in the Adventure Department, so rest assured."

Ding-a-ling...

"Professor, the bell keeps ringing."

The bell, which had announced the start of the lecture, continued to ring without stopping.
Unable to bear it any longer, a freshman raised his hand and spoke.

"Ah. That's the way to escape from this lecture."
"Pardon?"
"While an eye can be developed by reading books, it can also be cultivated through intuitive practical exercises. Moreover, a righteous thief is a class that works on the ground. I plan to conduct a light practical exercise for you students taking this lecture."

Professor Estrada rhythmically waved the baton in his hand through the air.
Oknodie tugged at Isabel and Gisele's collars.

"?"

To the puzzled gazes of the two, Oknodie grinned mischievously and raised both hands to cover her ears.
Was she asking them to follow suit?
Perhaps she was in a playful mood typical of children.
But covering one's ears during a lecture seemed too disrespectful.
Just as Isabel was about to scold her, Professor Estrada spread his hands like an orchestra conductor and smiled playfully, resembling Oknodie.
The next moment, a commotion unimaginable in the world's premier academy classroom began.

Beep-beep-beep.
Ding-dong-ding!
Wake up. It's morning! Wake up. It's morning!
Rrrrrring. Rrrrrring.
Screeeech!
Whirr-. Whirr-. Whirr-.
Ding-ding-ding in your ears.
Beep-. Beep-. Beep-. Beep-. Beep-. Beep-. Beep-. Beep-.
Ding-a-ling...

"Aaaack!"
"My ears!"
"I'm going crazy!"

Bell sounds attacking from all directions like a bursting dam!
Terrified by the auditory assault, Isabel and Gisele also covered their ears.

"Now, students. Let me explain the practical exercise for our first lecture. Students who demonstrate an 'eye' by finding and deactivating the hidden bells in the classroom can immediately escape."
"Of course, students lacking the necessary eye will remain in the bell-filled classroom with me."
"For reference, if all bells are found, everyone can escape immediately. If you all work together to deactivate all the bells, I'll give extra points. But only to those students who stayed until the end to find the bells together."

The first-years instantly understood the meaning behind senior Bigstone's words.

"Isn't this pure sadism?!"
"No wonder the second-year seniors don't take this lecture!"
"Wait, we have second-year seniors with us! They must know how to escape..."

The second-years grabbed emergency escape axes, emergency lights hanging above the exit, and chairs, shouting:

"Objects, return to your original form!"
"Return to original form."
"Found it, this damn alarm clock."

Bang! Bang! Bang!

First-years were shocked at the sight of second-years pressing buttons on alarm clocks that had returned to their original form.

"The clocks even have shape-shifting magic on them!"
"Good grief. This is cheating!"
"Those things, we haven't learned about those yet!!"

The professor received the alarm clocks submitted by the second-years and nodded with a "Hmm" sound.

"As expected of second-years, not as sluggish as the freshmen. You pass. The retake was worth it."
"Indeed. We'll be going first, juniors."

The first-years looked at the seniors with betrayed eyes.

"Seniors! How can you escape and abandon your juniors?!"
"We were abandoned just like you last year."
"!!"

The malicious inheritance that continues through the innocent history and tradition that spans across academic years!
Among the dumbfounded first-years, only Isabel and Gisele had different expressions.

"Oknodie. How did you know about this?"
"Does your family perhaps also inform you about practical assignments, Miss Oknodie?"
