# 105 - The Stronger the Adventurer, the More Dangerous the Monster

Jigoku was confident.

"Gyahahat! You coward. Now that we're here, has your confidence suddenly disappeared?"
"Who knows what kind of monster might be inside when we open this."
"Monster? Who in the world would be stupid enough to lock monsters in a storage room! They're not even rare animals."
"This is Gift Academy, you know."
"...Good point."

Though Jigoku was full of bravado, even she had to admit that an academy like this might well have a monster storage facility.

"True. There were plenty of scholars who would ask pirates to catch rare sea monsters or strange indigenous species from archipelagos."

High-ranking officials occasionally make unusual requests to pirates—who fearlessly venture into dangerous areas that most countries avoid—offering enormous compensation in return.

"Jigoku... did you do such terrible things...?"

There are different types of pirates.
There are subsistence pirates who rob merchant ships to survive in desperately poor coastal villages, and there are demonic pirates who enjoy evil deeds and even deal in slaves.
There are also privateer pirates who work with governments to hunt other pirates and occasionally legally plunder enemy merchant ships.
Jigoku is a privateer pirate.
That's what I basically know, but the tricky charm of "Graduating from the Academy by Luck" is that some variable might have caused her to transform into a demonic pirate.

"What kind of person do you think I am?"

Thwack.

"Ow!"
"You need to be hit. How dare you lump me together with that trash."
"Ugh. I'm sorry."
"When I'm bored, I might shoot my gun or kick the legs of weaklings passing by, or even shoot between the legs of lower-class students because I want to see them tap dance, but I never touch slaves! Got it?"
"Yes..."

But wasn't I right?
Don't you think you're being too harsh? Is it just my imagination?
I have so many questions I want to ask, but I hold back.
Jigoku is stronger than she looks.
Strong enough to have the upper hand in a strength contest with Son O-cheon.
Actually, Son O-cheon is known more for his endurance than his strength.
Anyway, the fact that Jigoku, with less muscle mass than Son O-cheon, can win in a strength contest suggests one thing.
She's a practitioner of mana tempering techniques that dramatically increase the maximum power muscles can exert!
Considering that maritime mana tempering techniques are even rarer than land-based ones, it would be difficult to find someone who could surpass Jigoku in water attributes throughout the entire academy, beyond just the 981st class.
In maritime fields, she's ranked as 0-tier (1st-5th place).

"So, shall we open it?"
"Ahhh!"

Yet the reason Jigoku remains at 1-tier (11th-15th place) rather than 0-tier or even 0.5-tier in maritime fields where she could truly excel—
Her unpredictable, quirky, and self-centered bad personality was on full display right before my eyes.

Bang!

With the gunshot, the door handle creaked open.
From the dark monster storage room, slime creatures called "Sewage Slurpers" that crawl on the floor eating waste came pouring out.

"Hey, you were scared of these little things?"
"There's more coming!"

Behind them came small creatures with long, sword-like claws called "Clawmen," climbing along the walls.

"Ha, even with those, they're still one-hit wonders. You're overreacting."

Adventurers have ranks too.
Stone-plate, Bronze-plate, Iron-plate, Silver-plate, Gold-plate, Platinum-plate.
Sewage Slurpers are the most manageable monsters that stone-plate adventurers, the lowest rank, face when cleaning sewers.
However, storage facilities for monsters typically contain Sewage Slurpers by default.
They maintain hygienic conditions by dealing with the waste produced by other monsters.
The problem was the Clawmen.

'Those monsters usually aren't kept in the same storage as others because they injure other monsters?'

Though they're typically hunted by bronze-plate adventurers, they require isolation due to their aggressive nature.
Yet they weren't isolated and were placed in the same storage room.
Because something could control them.
Control the Clawmen's aggression.
Another monster in the storage room.

"Kyaaak!"
"Huh?"

Jigoku's face froze when she saw the fiercely howling monster that appeared behind the Clawmen.
A monster infamous even among pirates on the sea had appeared on land.
A monster with troublesome attributes of "Fungus," "Man-eating," "Parasitic," and "Gas."
The "Red Tooth Mushroom," a monster that grows from a mushroom stalk and tears apart living creatures with shark-like sharp teeth.

"Move, Oknodie! I need to shoot it dead right now!!"
"No! If you carelessly cut or injure a Red Tooth Mushroom, it will frantically spray spores everywhere!"

This is why fungus monsters are so terrifying.
If you rashly attack them, you'll be poisoned by the biological gas that spreads everywhere, and small mushrooms will grow both inside and outside your body.
Anyone who has heard the horrific accounts of Red Tooth Mushrooms growing by consuming the flesh of unfortunate creatures near a defeated one would have to agree.
The most common yet most terrifying monsters are mushroom monsters.
And that wasn't the end of it.

"Kyaaak!"
"Kyak! Kyak!"

Multiple Red Tooth Mushrooms came pouring out one after another.

Thud. Thud.

Beyond them, an even larger mushroom monster pushed its way through, filling the entire storage doorway.

"Mother-class Red Tooth Mushroom"

A higher entity that spreads its species by releasing spores on its own, even without being attacked.
A Mother-class Red Tooth Mushroom had appeared.

"Run away, Jigoku. Go call a professor."
"What about you?"
"I know the cry that makes mushrooms behave docilely."

Just as Giant King Crabs fear the cry of octopuses, their natural predators, Red Tooth Mushrooms also fear the cry of their natural enemies.
Even mushroom monsters, which parasitically grow on the bodies of their victims as if in revenge, have natural enemies.
"Fire Elementals," which make parasitism impossible.
They burn all spores with fire, and even if the mushrooms try to attach themselves forcibly, the elementals' bodies are made of fire, not flesh, making it impossible for ordinary mushrooms to steal nutrients—a hard counter species.
The most common fire elemental in the world is the Fire Hound!

"Woof! Woof woof!"

Startled by the cry of the fire-attribute dog, the mushroom monsters backed away.
Realizing that my words weren't a lie just to save myself, Jigoku gritted her teeth and turned to run.

"If you die first, I'll kill you myself!"
"Woof!"

As I answered with dog barks, Jigoku bit her lip hard and ran toward the academy's main building.
10 seconds. 20 seconds. 30 seconds.
When I thought Jigoku was far enough away, I stopped making dog barking sounds.

"Phew. Even I was startled!"

Looking closely, I saw the mushrooms were wearing fungus suppression devices.
With their internal spore-launching organs suppressed, they wouldn't release spores unless attacked first, and their aggression was controlled, showing no hostility.
Even the Clawmen, clicking their claws like scissors, were being controlled by the Red Tooth Mushrooms, their aggression regulated according to the mushrooms' will.

Trembling.

In fact, looking closely, they were afraid whenever they made eye contact with me.
They had been captured by humans, forcibly modified to prevent spore release, and locked in a dark monster storage room.
They might fear humans, but they wouldn't dare think of attacking.
Yet I pretended it was an emergency for a reason.

"Hmph. Jigoku needs to be taught a lesson."

It was to correct Jigoku's bad habit of touching things carelessly.
She almost caused a major incident by recklessly opening the monster storage.
We were lucky these were well-maintained creatures.
What if there had been truly ferocious monsters inside? A disaster could have occurred.
Even now, that's the case.
I recognized the mushrooms' condition and prevented an attack, which is why things calmed down.
If she had charged in with the one-dimensional thinking that monsters must be attacked on sight, she would have been heavily infected with spores before the professors arrived.
If she died before rescue came, it would be a dead ending.
Even if she were lucky enough to be rescued, she would have ended up in intensive care.
Points would have been deducted in chunks for treatment, plus penalty points for missed lectures.
If she saved both grades and time thanks to me, a little anxiety is a small price to pay!


* * *


Jigoku had never resented her sea origins as much as she did now.

'At worst, I thought it might be a mimic.'

Sea monsters become powerless when they come ashore.
She thought land monsters would be easy to handle, judging by her own experience, despite differences in degree.
Her arbitrary judgment led to an accident.
Dangerous mushroom monsters that should never be attacked.
And there were also aggressive Clawmen around.
Oknodie has to make crying sounds to drive away the mushroom monsters while facing the attacks of numerous Clawmen.
Until she returns.
Until the professors arrive.
Continuously, alone, at the entrance of these dungeons in this pouring rain.

"Help!!! Please, someone help!!!"

She ran frantically, shouting and requesting help.
The main building was too far away.
It had already taken 30 minutes to get here.
Even if running could reduce it to 3 minutes, that was still too long.
Whether Oknodie could hold out for 5 minutes, 3 minutes, or would make a mistake and fall in just 1 minute.
In real combat, nothing can be guaranteed.

'What am I doing feeling sympathy? I'm trash!'

Just last week, she had given Oknodie a lucky bullet out of pity for having received underwater training in the field at such a young age, yet now she had put Oknodie in danger with her own hands.
She wanted to cry.
She wanted to die.
But right now, she was the only one who could help.
If she, who needed to gather people to save Oknodie, was late, then Oknodie might really die or be seriously injured.

"First, please calm down."

A refreshing voice forcibly calmed Jigoku's anxious heart.

"You're... the examiner from the first entrance exam!"
"Do you remember me?"
"Monk!"
"...How unfortunate. I am Monk Myungho."
"Oknodie is in danger. She's surrounded by mushroom monsters and Clawmen that came out of a monster storage room that drifted to an area full of dungeons!"

Monk Myungho reassured her with a gentle smile.

"I will handle the rest myself. Student Jigoku, please request medical support from the main building, just in case."

The monk in loose-fitting clothes, who seemed like both a monk and a Taoist master with a mysterious aura, moved away dozens of meters at a time as if using a teleportation technique, disappearing from sight.
Jigoku, who had been watching the scene in a trance, finally came to her senses and ran toward the main building again.

'Oknodie can survive!'

Her heart was filled with hope and relief.
In contrast, Monk Myungho's face, as he rushed toward Oknodie, had grown cold.

'This is going to be a serious problem.'

Ironically, when encountering mushroom monsters, the stronger the adventurer, the higher the probability of death.
Weak adventurers don't even dare to fight and run away, and mediocre adventurers carefully retreat because they can't guarantee victory.
However, strong adventurers tend to fight anything they think they can handle.
Those who know the infamy of mushroom monsters wouldn't commit such foolishness, but what about those who don't?
They get covered with more parasitic spores and die faster.
Unfortunately, Oknodie was a student so overwhelmingly, remarkably, very much, dominantly stronger than other first-years that comparison was difficult.
She might burst a mushroom monster with a single punch and get covered in spores all over her body.
If that happens...
She could die in the tens of seconds before he arrives.
Monk Myungho quickened his pace.
