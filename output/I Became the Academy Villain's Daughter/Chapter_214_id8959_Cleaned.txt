All they could do was believe that the Foundation would send Oknodie back.

"...I'll look for a way."

"If necessary, contact the Esonia Adventure Guild and ask for help. If you tell the guild friends it's my request, they'll surely help."

The connections of Gisele and Isabel began to mobilize to retrieve Oknodie who had been taken by the Foundation.

"Miss Oknodie. Is that correct?"
"Where's Jonna?"

A stranger, not Jonna.

"You'll be able to see her later with Jonna."

Neither the butler nor the maid were my people.
This was getting annoying.

"Let's go."

She boarded a carriage where the outside wasn't visible.
When she pulled the curtain covering the window, she saw places that had been nailed shut with wooden boards.
Giving a look that said "why make such a stuffy carriage," the butler who wasn't Jonna answered.

"We've also reinforced the exterior with steel plates to prevent sniping."
"...It's suffocating."
"Please bear with it. It's a safety measure."

They traveled like that for an entire day.
The food they provided was even unappetizing black bread.
She could tolerate bad taste.
But it was hard to tolerate food that was not only tasteless but had long ago been added to her food catalog.
Her mood worsened considerably.

"We've arrived. Please get out."

Without any escort, she exited the carriage and entered a facility thoroughly protected by multiple barriers.
The adults moving around the facility were all dressed as clowns wearing masks.
They were members of the "Pierrot Mask Group," a confirmed mid-tier villain organization spread throughout the continent.

"Do you know where this is?"
"I do."
"Of course you wouldn't know. This is a subsidiary organization of the Foundation..."

Dull people have dull reactions.
Even his blinking speed was sluggish—this incompetent butler.

"You know?"
"The Pierrot Mask Group. They take in orphans claiming they'll help them make a living as circus performers, then train talented children as acrobats and sell them to their superior assassination organization, while using the rest as informants or feeding them to the circus animals, right?"

"Stop with the trivial talk and tell me why you brought me here."
"...The person who called for Miss Oknodie is the Supervisor who manages the Foundation's butlers. There's usually only one reason scholarship students meet him."

The fake butler tried to create an intimidating atmosphere with a deliberately menacing expression.

"When a scholarship student repeatedly fails to complete their directives and needs 'punishment.' In other words, when they're about to be punished."

If anger could stack, his arrogant words just now would have maxed out the stack.

"How unfortunate! I prefer giving punishments rather than receiving them."
"Don't maintain such an insolent attitude. Even if I don't mind, the Supervisor who came from the Eastern Empire will never tolerate a scholarship student who challenges his authority."
"The same goes for me. Think of that as the reason you're going back!"

With a bright smile, she lightly touched the butler's leg with her palm.

"Urghk!?"

Dark mana is similar to radiation.
Keeping a small amount won't endanger life, but exceeding a certain level causes the body to rapidly break down, as if suffering from "radiation exposure."
That's exactly what was happening to the fake butler's captured leg.
She raised and injected a lot of dark mana.
Just from that light process, he convulsed with seizures before collapsing with a thud.
If it had been Jonna, he would have easily broken free without being caught by malicious hands.
Indeed, he was pathetic compared to Jonna.

"Did you really not think this would happen while taking someone else's butler and making threats? Seriously."

What a trivial man.
She lightly stepped over the butler who was writhing and emitting black smoke, then called for the Pierrot masks.

"Would someone please take me to the Supervisor?"

For a rare outing, it wouldn't even take three days, let alone a week.
Boredom permeated her steps as she walked behind the mask who was cautiously leading the way.
