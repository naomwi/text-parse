Grand Prison, Basement Level 2.
Mosquito Master Yumi rubbed the rope marks on her wrists and pouted.

"Those petty jerks. Locking me up for two weeks without lectures just for stealing a little blood."

Her giant mosquitoes were a bit large, true.
But she only had them suck enough blood to keep people from dying.
Anemia? Dizziness? Falling accidents?
That was the fault of those who panicked about mosquitoes and fell while trying to stand up or run away.
Only fools scratch their itchy mosquito bites during practical training.
Are they planning to die in real combat by scratching their stomachs while swords are pointed at them?
Those idiots should actually be grateful.
They got to experience a stupid way to die in advance, so they could avoid making the same mistake in real combat.
The same goes for the professor.
It was his fault for not being able to handle a little itching despite his experience. Why go this far against a student just because he lost a bit of face?

"Mosquito Master Yumi. You have a visitor."
"For me?"

Did she have any classmates who would visit her?
Yumi tried to recall.

-That bitch who brings around disgusting mosquitoes.
-Enemy of humanity. Go die somewhere.
-I don't even want to call someone like you a third-year. You fourth-year-like bitch.

...There shouldn't be anyone.
All her classmates hated her.
When she went to the visiting area, the person sitting across from her was indeed not a classmate.
It was a maid with cat ears, dressed in a black and white two-tone outfit that emphasized her chest, exuding mid-battle energy. The cat-person maid kept her tail straight up rather than swishing it, and Yumi openly expressed her confusion at her appearance.

"Why is a maid here? Don't tell me you're bringing my belongings from my room?"
"Don't you resent having your freedom taken away because you possess an unpopular class?"
"What nonsense are you spouting?"
"Don't you want revenge on the professor, student council, and classmates who imprisoned you here? While you're locked in this damp basement, they're enjoying the youth of the Grand Sports Festival."
"Hmph. That damn Sports Festival. What youth? If they don't suddenly die from getting caught up in upperclassmen's mistakes, they should consider themselves lucky."

Yumi was wealthy enough without having to chase after points from the Grand Sports Festival.
The professor and student council had sentenced her to two weeks, demanding she pay 200,000 points in bail money earned from blood stone trading, but she was tough enough to use blood magic from mosquito-sucked blood.
She had the wicked determination to endure those two weeks and keep all her points for herself!

"I can get revenge after I get out in two weeks anyway."
"It doesn't matter if it's not now. Whenever you need help, visit the art room in the closed old school building. Someone who can help you will be waiting."
"Hmph. You're just here to recruit me into some organization with a weird name like the Bikini Warrior Squad or the Mein Conspiracy Group, right? Dream on. Not a chance. I won't wear bikini armor, and I won't wear the Mein Conspiracy Group's black high-leg costume either. Not to mention anything weirder."

Yumi drew a firm line.
She highly appreciated the skill it took to infiltrate the Academy as a maid.
No evil organization had demonstrated this level of skill before.
Still, she had managed well enough on her own without them.
She was determined to use her mosquitoes more discreetly next time, stealing just enough blood to cause sleep disorders without causing anemia. She didn't need outside help for her revenge.

"Ah, here you are! I was wondering why you weren't in your cell."

Suddenly, from above the maid, through the ventilation duct, a child wearing a pointed hat jumped down into the visiting room, her school uniform skirt fluttering to reveal her pantyhose. Unlike the maid who wore cat ears and a tail but somehow didn't quite give off the impression of a cat person, this child who landed without making a sound naturally incorporated cat-like qualities of a quiet hunter in her every step and movement.

"Are you with that maid too?"
"No, I'm not! I came to make a request!"
"I refuse any offer to join an organization in exchange for getting me out."
"Eh, that's unreasonable. Just stay in prison and lend me your mosquitoes for a bit!"
"...Wait. That cheerful voice and small stature... You're Oknodie, the one I traded blood stones with before, right?"

Oknodie nodded, dimples forming on her cheeks that looked so pinchable.

"You remember me!"
"I welcome a trading partner. But what do you want to do with the mosquitoes? And more importantly, do you know how to handle them?"

It was a fair question.
Without obtaining the rare Mosquito Master class, controlling those small, inferior creatures was not as easy as it looked.
Control magic requires the target to remain still until the formula is inscribed while they're subdued, but mosquitoes are incredibly fast and agile.
They don't stay still long enough for the formula to be inscribed.
Unlike ordinary small animals, their body surface area is small, so even for giant mosquitoes, you need to carefully inscribe high-level control commands in a limited area.
In terms of taming difficulty, mosquitoes exceed the difficulty of most rare mob tamers!
Despite this, they have low combat power and are primarily utility-focused, hovering between harmful pests and monsters. If even second-years struggle to control them, a first-year had no chance.

"Bring out one mosquito. I'll show you!"
"Your confidence is impressive. If you insist, I'll give you a chance."

A mosquito smaller than a finger flew in with an annoying whining sound.

"Bite!"

Whine!

Like a dog responding to its owner's command, the mosquito charged forward with a whining sound.
It folded its wings, changing its flight angle in a spiral to quickly dive into a blind spot.
From behind, it caused a miss, and in the moment the attack went astray, the mosquito got excited at the thought of plunging its long proboscis into pale skin.

"Paralyze!"

The ambitious mosquito's dream was cut short as it was hit by Oknodie's magic that paralyzed everything within a 1-meter radius, causing it to crash onto the cold visiting room floor.

"Hmm. An excellent Mosquito Master should also master methods to subdue mosquitoes."

Yumi nodded with a satisfied expression.

"A silent mosquito? So that's your preference, junior. A soundless mosquito isn't bad. You can create a blood thief that no one knows is stealing their blood."
