# 352 - The Weight of Resolve

'Was it only this much?'

Russo's legs trembled.
Though he poured a healing potion on his blood-soaked legs after their brief exchange, the trembling wouldn't stop.
The pain from muscles recovering from injury mercilessly assaulted his nerves.

'Even with the resolve to sacrifice my lifespan, I still couldn't overcome a true genius?'

Now he understood.
He understood those failing students who succumbed to the Foundation's temptations, even at the cost of accepting the deadly poison of Dark Mana.
Russo's life had never been desperate.
At least, not when he completed his second year and took the third-year advancement exam.
Back then, he lacked the resolve to sacrifice his lifespan.
He hadn't yet faced reality.
His dreams were sweet.
I can improve my skills during my leave of absence and pass the advancement exam when I try again.
Then I won't need to buy an advancement certificate at 100 times the price.
I've fallen behind, but it's not over yet.
All those self-comforting excuses vanished when he found himself at rock bottom.
He was nothing.
He realized this watching the backs of expelled students who left the Academy unable to bear their humiliation.
That he could be next.
That he was just another dropout.
The senior instructor's advice was the final blow.

"Stupid boy. Do you think others fail the advancement exam for years because they're idiots? Did you think the difficulty of the advancement exam wouldn't change?"

If he couldn't pass the first advancement exam, how could he possibly pass the second with its increased difficulty?
The third and fourth attempts would be the same.
Twenty-five years.
Earning points through enduring this overwhelmingly long time became his only path.
It was too far.
So distant he couldn't see the end.
It was too narrow.
One misstep and he would fall.

'Can I endure it? Twenty-five years.'

If only he had talent.
If only he had money to replace points.
If only he had encountered an opportunity to substitute for talent and points.
Meaningless hypotheticals piling up day after day.
On a day when even hope was dying, the girl approached him.

"Instructor. If you help with my business, I can share points with you... Does that interest you?"

Even if it meant receiving points earned through improper means, even if it meant turning a blind eye to injustice, it would drastically reduce his unbearably long period of endurance.
If this wasn't an opportunity, what was?

"Even if I tear up the contract, I'll still transfer the payment to you!"
"If you're lying, I'll just waste my lifespan for nothing."
"I'm telling the truth!"
"Do you know why scammers believe in the god of contracts? Because contracts torn by third parties lose their effect. A contract with someone powerless means nothing!"
"Ugh..."

He felt wronged.
Just when he had finally found his resolve.
For the first time since entering the Academy, he had demonstrated skills that could pass the advancement exam.
Yet that cruel girl had rendered even that attempt meaningless.
Sacrifice without points meant nothing.
Camella without a contract could never be trusted.
If a woman who deceived others even with contracts had no contract at all, how was that different from putting your hand in an alligator's mouth?
The Camella he knew was never that kind of person.
She used contracts even with her closest associates.
A fundamental distrust of humans lay at her core.

'So even when a weed blooms, it's still just a weed?'

His possibilities were slipping away.
His future was closing again.
Time spent was gone, and time remaining was diminishing.

"Don't be ridiculous. How much are you going to underestimate me?"

Through Russo's despair, he heard Camella's angry voice, teeth clenched.

"As the master of the Contract Seal, I command the forced termination of the contract with Russo!"
"Ah! Impossible. A double-cross here!?"
"Russo. Now I must pay a penalty for unilaterally terminating our contract."
"You...!"
"Yes. The contract is broken, but in return, a new contract of penalty payment exists. This is my goodwill toward your sacrifice."
"The penalty was all the points needed for the advancement exam. Why would you do something so reckless!"

Camella glared at Russo with as much disdain as she had for Oknodie.

"It's because of you."
"Are you... crying?"
"I know what state someone is in when controlled by a pet contract. Even when forcibly controlled, there's no obligation to fight with all your heart and strength."

That was the flaw in forced contracts.

"Yet you fought with your whole heart, even risking your lifespan. Did you think I would betray that sincerity?"

Camella, who used contracts because she couldn't trust people, had paradoxically grown colder toward people due to the distance created by those very contracts.

"After being betrayed by my parents, friends, and everyone around me in childhood, there was no trust for others in my world. I learned how empty even the most intimate relationships are without contracts."

The woman who trembled at betrayal felt a trust she had never received from anyone in her life.
Perhaps this was the sincerity that Camella had been seeking all along—Camella who had toyed with lovers using pet contracts that mocked human hearts.
She wanted someone who wouldn't hate her even with such a contract.
A childish thought she herself hadn't realized.
Russo had pierced through her tender inner feelings, her sadness that had been curled up abandoned and unexpected even by herself, with his life-risking struggle.

"This is a contract! A unilateral penalty payment contract formed by my tribute to your sincerity!!"

You trusted me enough to risk your life.
Then I too will trust you and stake my penalty payment.
Even if I never receive anything in return.
Just as Russo had resolved to sacrifice his life.

Drip, drip.

Blood beaded and fell from his forearm, scratched by fragments.
Sweat, more than blood, streaked across his face.
Still, Russo's eyes remained unwavering.

"The same goes for me."

Strength filled Russo's entire body.
Genuine surprise crossed Oknodie's face.

"No one ever needed me. No matter how unjust the purpose, this was the first fair reward I've ever received—a contract beyond what I deserve."

If Russo had taken Camella's first, then Camella had also taken Russo's first.
The trust between a man and woman who have exchanged firsts is not trivial.

"Twenty-five years."

Beneath the broken tiles, the floor engraved with automatic repair magic circuits vibrated violently.

"Even the student council never showed such faith and sincerity to a weakling like me. So just this once, it's okay. For a coward who couldn't stake his life on his own future to risk it for someone else."

Not just days or weeks.
Not such a trivial amount of power.
Months to years.
Burning his lifespan equivalent to 25 years with that much output.
This wasn't a fight to endure for six hours.
Camella had shown him 25 years' worth of trust.
To repay that, Russo had sincerely resolved to burn 25 years of his lifespan here today to defeat Oknodie.
That heavy resolve gave strength to Russo's charge.

<Heart Explosion Technique>
<Triple Explosion - Full Body Enhancement>

The blood from his violently racing heart accelerated his brain's thinking speed.
This wasn't just momentary enhancement of limbs or extremities with damage distributed throughout the body.
It affected the two most important organs in the human body.
The heart and brain.
Though he hadn't lived the cleanest life, if he lost even these—his body and health, his only capital, and the blood vessels and mana circuits that could cost him his tomorrow—

BOOM!

The accelerated afterimage sword no longer worked.
It couldn't deceive his brain function that observed every moment of sending out doppelgangers as if capturing them frame by frame in a photograph, his concentration exceeding its limits.
The wall that the pushed-back Oknodie stepped on couldn't withstand the impact and collapsed.
Some idiot who had been eavesdropping with his ear against the wall in the next room fell with the debris.

"!!"

It was an accident.
That was undoubtedly an unexpected situation for everyone involved—Russo, Oknodie, Camella, and the idiot caught up at the scene.
Russo had proven his resolve.
But that resolve was to risk his own life.
At this moment, continuing his attack on Oknodie would inflict irreversible serious injury on that idiot, and with high probability, certain death.

-Don't touch the first-years. No matter what happens. Not if you don't want to lose all your hard-earned points.

The senior instructor's warning flashed through his mind.

"Ah, aah..."

The pathetic sight of the idiot flailing on the floor with a stupid expression.

'Am I... going to kill him?'

This wasn't right.
This fight had 25 years at stake, but crossing the line would bring a lifetime of regret.
His muscles loosened.
His eyes twitched with indignation.
But not using his power would cause the forcibly drawn muscles to inflict greater shock inside his body.
He had to release it.
Russo extended his leg.
The greatest strike of his life, mixed with exaltation and despair, was directed not at Oknodie but toward the window.

Slash.

A crimson fighting spirit mixed with blood.
It was at the level of what people commonly call blade energy projection.
The limits of <Martial Artist> that had only managed to strengthen his striking power and stand equal with weapons despite numerous attempts.
He had reached the realm of <Blade Energy> or <Mana Coating> that could cut steel, and even beyond that, to the realm of blade energy projection.

<Wing Cutter>

The multi-layered security formula was cleanly cut and fell away with a single strike.
As a tree outside the window was neatly bisected, the roommates of the idiot, who were trembling in fear against the opposite wall, all gaped in unison.

"Huh? Wasn't there a barrier until just now..."
"I withdrew it. Even the handful of power allocated to the barrier."

Russo's fist struck the wall.
Thud.
Everyone was startled by the vibration traveling through the wall, but the tears flowing from his eyes silenced them.

"That was the extent of my sincerity. It shouldn't have been like this."

If he had maintained the barrier, they could have continued fighting in an environment isolated from the outside.
The mistake from a momentary misjudgment gave Russo bitter regret.
But what he didn't know was that his foolishness wasn't the only thing.

"Who said this was over?"

Whoosh. Russo caught what Oknodie threw.
He looked down at the potion in his hand.

"What's this trick? If it's pathetic sympathy..."
"Hmph. Don't talk nonsense. If you thought this would end quietly after crumpling a stagnant pond's dignity, you're mistaken."
"...What are you trying to do?"
"Drink it. It's not as high quality as what I gave to Xing, but it will strengthen your body's vital energy channels."
"You were my enemy. Why would you do something good for me? You expect me to believe this?"
"Don't want to believe me? Then I'll make you believe. If you don't drink that and fight again, I'm going to beat Camella from now on. Not enough to kill her, but enough that she won't be able to attend lectures for about a month, in a way that even healers can't help."

Still not going to drink it?
At Oknodie's provocation, Russo looked down at the potion again.

"Stop it! If you really do such a thing, it will be a loss for Oknodie too, and since the barrier has been released, the dorm supervisors and instructors will come anyway!"

Camella's urgent cry reignited Russo's directionless anger.
An empty potion bottle rolled on the floor.

"Ah... why such a foolish act..."
"Beliefs make people into fools. Even without that, this fight was unavoidable. Look at Oknodie's hand."

In Oknodie's left hand was a wand.
A weapon she hadn't used once in their previous fight.
Though Camella didn't notice, Russo clearly perceived it.
The wave of power spreading across the ground during the confusion.

"This place is already inside a barrier."
"A barrier? But someone just opened the door and left!"
"The conditions are the same. People who leave cannot return while the barrier is maintained. The internal space remains undestroyed and maintained. Only the range is different."

Camella looked out into the hallway and was shocked.
The idiot's roommate who had been running down the corridor screaming clearly disappeared from view the moment he took a step beyond the fire door of the dormitory hallway.
Russo continued with a grave expression.

"This entire floor is already within Oknodie's barrier."
