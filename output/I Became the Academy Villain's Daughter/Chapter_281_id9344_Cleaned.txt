# 281 - Stating the Obvious

Of course, Oknodie herself is a child who's good at hiding here and there, so she might be able to find good places to hide points.
Perhaps while pretending to be fair on the surface, she's secretly getting information from her butler who's always by her side.
Whatever the case, only Oknodie is doing well.
The other kids must be different.
Istar thought it was actually a good thing, remembering through the midterm exams how much damage her attempt to excel alone had done to her reputation.
The more luxurious time Oknodie spends, the more her friends who've become laborers will resent her!

"I'll take the scholarship."
"Me too."
"I'll take it too."

There are even students being released after accepting scholarships.
Those who've fallen into this trap and become scholarship students are unlikely to loyally serve the Foundation.
It just means they've gained more internal enemies.

'Their expressions seem rather carefree...?'

Many students show no signs of fearing becoming Foundation scholarship students, or they give off an air of "why would that matter now?"

'Aren't they afraid of people who would break fingers, pull teeth, and try to design others' lives as they please?'

The dangers of the Foundation come much more explicitly and quickly than those of the Academy.
The hero found it almost insane how casually these students were taking this, but then remembered they were lower-class students.
I suppose they're in the lower class precisely because they're such thoughtless, reckless kids.

"Istar. Take the royal guard and start tackling the facility challenges. Oknodie might extend her reach there once she finishes collecting points."

As Yupi said, she has much to do.
Other students besides Oknodie can certainly aim for the facility challenges too.
If they get ahead and secure those points, other students will be in an even more difficult position, accelerating the cycle of forced scholarship students resenting the Foundation.

"Everyone hurry. We need to collect points so quickly they won't even notice we've taken them."

On the afternoon of the second day of the cruise, the hero's royal guard began to accelerate their activities in earnest.


* * *


Unlike the students rushing about, the maids Leape and April knew from the moment they boarded that pointless luxury wouldn't be allowed, so they exchanged information with the crew members.
And they discovered that they were classified as "friends" who accompanied their lady, not as ship crew.

"This is the Chairman's test. What do you think?"
"It's not my place as a mere subordinate to speculate."
"Then let me tell you. This is what the Chairman calls a 'small experiment' to test important figures."
"...Has this happened before?"
"I was once drafted as a crew member."
"What happened then?"

Leape's expression darkened.

"Twenty followers of Anlage, the God of Corruption, boarded the ship. They had heard rumors that their cult's sacred treasure was hidden somewhere on the ship and gathered boarding points to obtain it."
"They were smarter than the students."
"That became their poison. Boarding points can be stolen by killing others. This clause became the catalyst for two different factions to kill each other."
"!!"
"These are Academy students. They won't cross that line, at least. If you can't kill everyone, you shouldn't kill anyone."
"They fought intending to kill everyone. How many survived?"
"Only one remained. There's only one spot for the owner of the sacred artifact. The Apostle of Anlage still works for the Foundation to repay the debt from that time."

April realized anew that she and Leape were maids of different calibers.
Their access to information was on completely different levels.

"Is there a prize this time too, something to tempt the students like the 'cult's sacred treasure'?"
"I've already looked into it. This time's bait is 'a Foundation executive.' The prize allows you to call upon any executive, including the Apostle of Anlage, as an assistant for Academy life."
"!!"

It's an astonishing prize.
In some ways, it could be considered even more valuable than the cult's sacred treasure.
Even the owner who obtained the sacred treasure after fierce competition is now just listed as one of the prizes.

"Isn't it dangerous? Revealing the list of Foundation executives to outsiders."
"Of course, the list of executives isn't provided. You either have to specify the exact name of the executive you want, or accept whichever executive they assign you."

April suddenly became curious.

"How strong are the Foundation's executives?"
"After joining the Foundation, the Apostle of Anlage killed three Apostles of the Sea God. All were individuals who interfered with the Foundation's procession."
"...How terrifying. To hunt down three apostles comparable to knight commanders or archmage masters."
"With such a brilliant track record, his name is known to those who should know it. The hero might not know, but the princess certainly would mention his name."
"Do you think they can collect more points than Lady Oknodie?"
"Who knows."

Somehow, she had this feeling.

"There are so many talented students among this year's freshmen."

The person April was watching wasn't the hero or the princess, but someone unexpected.


* * *


Gisele gave a bitter smile as he looked at the gleaming gold card.

"Points not worth collecting. Ten thousand points gone at once just to upgrade to a gold ticket."
"Hahaha! But I, Son O-cheon, already swept through the arcade points first. We're still ahead of the others, aren't we?"
"And we also earned many points by selling ten recipes to a chef who wanted them."

Son O-cheon and Isabel handed half of their collected points to Gisele.
They believed that Gisele, the smartest among them, would use the points most wisely.
Their prediction was spot on.

"What are the benefits of the upgrade?"
"Free use of gold-grade facilities and below. Permission to talk with senior crew members and access to missions. 20% discount on product purchases, among other features."
"Ah, I looked into that. Expensive facilities have gold or diamond emblems at their entrances."

Forest keeper Dorothy remembered all the distinctive features among the crowds and vast facilities, providing timely helpful information.
Those classified as "Team Gisele" gained access to premium information the fastest using their expanded privileges.

"I heard an 'auction' is starting on the deserted island?"
"More valuable items become available each day you stay on the deserted island... The most valuable items purchasable with points must come from the auction."
"If it's a deserted island, we'll need to stock up on enough food and survival tools to last a long stay. Leave the preparations to me, the explorer."

The Foundation's cruise ship had more to enjoy than expected.
Not just amusement facilities, but also the thrill of accessing hidden information.
Gisele felt his hobby desires as a black market dealer being greatly stimulated.

"I envy them. The wealth, planning ability, and organizational skills to create such a massive playground."

Indeed, they're as impressive as the world-renowned Gift Academy.
Perhaps this is a giant playground that the person Oknodie calls "Papa" provides for her.

"What are you admiring? If Oknodie's friends get into trouble, her reputation will suffer that much more. She's already feared as the Dark Princess, and if kids start saying they became debtors by hanging out with her, what will happen in the second semester?"
"That would indeed be problematic."
"If you understand, let's quickly gather points and go rescue those idiots."

Quite a few students from the Black Market Association had joined this trip.
Many commoners came along thinking they'd rather strengthen their connections with Oknodie than return to their hometowns without any backing.

"Now that we've gathered enough points, let's find students who are in trouble."

The commoner students from the Black Market Association were actually better off.
Students who were dragged away after indulging in luxury rooms without a plan recorded enormous negative figures, but the commoners' extravagances were modest.

"What luxury did I indulge in? I ate just one plate at the buffet before leaving!"
"I left half my drink unfinished!"
"I threw away yogurt without licking the inside of the container!"

Commoners whose extravagances amounted to such trivial consumption!

"From now on, look for points but be careful not to go into the negative again. You've experienced what harsh treatment awaits if you fall for the Foundation's temptations."
"Thanks, Gisele..."
"By the way, has anyone seen where Arcadia is?"
"The countess? She was heading to the grand theater."
"...The grand theater?"

Gisele was surprised.
Arcadia was with Titoso, wasn't she?
She had shown caution from relatively early on, recognizing the cruise ship's traps.
What reason could she have for using such a luxurious facility?
When he arrived at the grand theater, he found the answer.

[Grand Theater: Business Hours 9 AM - 6 PM]
[Free Viewing Hours: 11 AM - 1 PM]

Can't resist freebies.
Just as the free viewing time ended, crew audience members and a few student spectators came out mixed together.
Surprisingly, Oknodie was among the students, with an unusually frustrated expression and pouting lips.

"What's wrong?"
"Arcadia was one step ahead of me."

Behind Oknodie, Arcadia waved a gold ticket with a smug laugh.

"How did you get it?"
"During the play, there was a detective part where someone from the audience was chosen to ask questions, and I correctly identified the culprit and the trick, so I received this ticket as a reward. Hoho. It was a really fun play."

What's that all about?
Why are you the only one having fun?
Gisele, who had learned about the gold ticket as a reward for winning a nerve-wracking gambling match at the late-night casino, felt somewhat cheated.

"Oknodie. While collecting points is good, you should also think about helping other students. At this rate, your reputation is in danger."

Quickly shaking off his ugly jealousy, Gisele didn't waste this rare opportunity to meet Oknodie and offered necessary advice.
But Oknodie's expression remained completely nonchalant, as if he was stating the obvious.

"You don't need to worry about that!"
"...Are you planning to convince everyone to become Foundation scholarship students to get them out?"
"If we start a shipboard rebellion event, everyone can get off the ship without spending points!"

Isn't starting a shipboard rebellion common sense when you board a cruise ship?
Gisele was left speechless by Oknodie's confident assertion.
