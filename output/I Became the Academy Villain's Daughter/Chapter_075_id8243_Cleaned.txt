<75 - The Secret Room>

As they headed to the main building, Zuang and Hestia's anticipation grew.
Just leaving the dormitory required breaking through such a strict security system.
2:22 AM—a special time.
What could be waiting in the secret room that could only be accessed at this specific hour?
They were incredibly excited about the secret room where Oknodie was supposedly waiting.

'As fellow assassins, I might lose in courage tests, but I can't lose in stealth operations.'

Zuang was overflowing with confidence in her skills.
Ghosts?
Undead?
Professor Sadako?
Those might be a little... just a little bit too much.
But stealth operations were what she excelled at even without such obstacles.

'This time I'll show Oknodie exactly what I'm capable of.'

She may have often embarrassed herself as an assassin during the adventurer's night operation lectures, but now she'd show how amazing her assassin skills really were.
She'd show Oknodie that her assassin abilities were just as good as hers.

"Someone's coming. Quick, behind this tree."

While they were boldly walking down the tree-lined path after finding a gap in security, they sensed a group approaching from the main road.

"Your body's too big to hide completely."
"Don't scream and stay still."

When Zuang poked Hestia's body with her finger, Hestia felt pain like her bones were twisting.
Suppressing her scream with remarkable endurance, Hestia was about to get angry when she noticed her arm had visibly shrunk in thickness.
Seeing Hestia's surprised expression, Zuang smiled coldly with gleaming eyes.

"It's one of the secret techniques of assassins from the East. A body modification called 'Bone Contraction.'"

It shrinks and transforms bones and muscles.
A body manipulation technique that artificially mimics the effects of mana refinement.
A useful skill when you need to hide but your body is too large.

'That really hurts.'

Hestia shed a small tear.
Despite being a mercenary accustomed to pain, it was almost unbearable.
She could barely suppress her groans, let alone her tears.
She knew there were transformation spells in magic that could temporarily modify a person's body without pain.
Why would someone learn such a painful technique instead?

'Is this actually a torture technique?'

While she endured the genuinely torturous pain, something with a zombie-like staggering gait appeared on the main road where they had been standing.

'Z-zombies?!'

Zuang was terrified.
Zombies? Why? Could Professor Sadako have appeared?

"Late night lectures are driving me insane driving me insane driving me insane."
"Kill the professor. That's all I can think about..."
"Professor, I'm taking other classes too, please don't give us assignments that take a whole week to complete, I think I'm going to die..."
"If the professor takes all the awakening potion made by boiling cyan-green-red three elements in a 1:2:3 ratio over high heat for 30 minutes, then adding ground thistle stems and magical wormwood leaves and simmering for an hour, what am I supposed to do what am I supposed to do what am I supposed to do??"
"Kill the professor kill the professor kill the professor..."

Fortunately, it wasn't Professor Sadako.
It was just upper-class students with zombie-like appearances, overworked by the academy's brutal schedule.

"..."

This wasn't something to be relieved about—wasn't it more terrifying?
Is this the future of first-years?
Zuang seriously considered escaping the academy.
How would Oknodie react to this?

'Phew. They're just our poor seniors!'

She would definitely say that with a bright face.
That girl is a bit scary now.
The Oknodie I knew wasn't the real Oknodie.
What is the real Oknodie?
Does someone named Oknodie even really exist?

Creepy.

Goosebumps rose all over her forearms.

"Hey... it hurts too much... release it quickly..."
"Ah. Sorry."

The longer Zuang's bone contraction technique was applied, the longer the pain lasted.
Hestia's eyes were filled with resentment.

"Why do you have such a painful technique?"
"It was originally used for torture."
"...Do you want to die?"

So it really was a torture technique.

"What did you expect from an assassin's technique?"
"You'll get what's coming to you someday."

Zuang stuck out her tongue and took the lead again.
Bone contraction wasn't actually a torture technique.
It was just that her master saw the potential for torture in the original technique and developed it as such, then passed this torture version to her.

'There's no need to explain all that.'

They reached the main building amid Hestia's minor complaints.
Fortunately, the main building didn't have a laser beam security system.
That's because first-years who needed isolation for safety were in the dormitory, not around the main building.

'What exactly is dangerous for first-years in the academy at dawn?'

Until now, she had imagined Professor Sadako's zombies roaming around for lecture practice or assassins infiltrating to steal the academy's confidential information.
But now she wondered if the measures were to isolate first-years from the zombie-like upper-class students.
Or perhaps it was a rule established after some soul-searching to protect at least the first-years from professors who would mercilessly force them to attend lectures in the middle of the night.

'Either way, it's scary.'

They reached the main building entrance but hid nearby since it wasn't 2:22 yet.

"The seniors look really weak. Lack of exercise? Weak lower bodies? Something like that."
"...Don't you lack empathy a bit?"
"I don't want to hear that from an assassin who uses torture techniques on classmates."

The two of them saw groups of seniors passing by three more times.
Seniors who giggled for no reason and talked to themselves as they passed by.
Seniors carrying huge boulders on their shoulders, heading toward the forest beyond the main building for unknown reasons.

"Isn't that a monster?"
"Don't go, Hestia. The seniors will handle it."
"Are you scared? Why do you keep looking back?"

Poison needles stabbing into the nape of a monster that had burst out of the main building.
A senior cursing while dragging away the monster that had collapsed with a thud as the paralyzing poison took effect.

"I'm starting to get a little scared of this academy."
"Same."

Hestia and Zuang, who hadn't been on good terms, realized that in this unsettling dawn at the academy, they could only trust each other.

"It's 2:20 AM. Let's go in soon."

The two entered the main building lobby, sticking close together.
At exactly 2:22 AM, Hestia took Zuang's hand and climbed the central staircase to the second floor of the main building, as Oknodie had told her.
Hestia had been half-suspicious that it might be a joke, but...

"Huh? What's this?"
"It's true. There really is a secret room."

At the top of the stairs was a closed door instead of the second-floor lobby.

"Oknodie, have you been waiting long?"
"Wait, I don't sense anyone inside."

Ignoring Zuang's words, Hestia flung the door open.
Inside Room 222, numerous paper strips hung from nails covering the walls.
It felt eerie, like entering a shaman's room or a black magician's curse room.

"What is this place?"

Zuang examined one of the paper strips on the wall.

141-1: Professor Platon occasionally makes free "Protein Soup" for students who beg him.

"What is this?"

Flicking the paper with her finger in disbelief, she saw the back side as it spun around.

141-2: Cadets who consume Professor Platon's "Protein Soup" more than 5 times are destined to be dragged into the "Become an Iron Man" lecture in their third year—an unavoidable fate.

"...What is this, really?"

Good news on the front.
Bad news on the back.
Professors, lectures, objects, places, events.
Information about all kinds of elements.
It was too creepy to laugh off as a joke, and even more chilling if true.

"Ah, you all came early?"

2:22:57 AM.
Oknodie closed the door, speaking in a cheerful voice.
Zuang jumped like a startled cat.

"W-w-what is this! Oknodie, why did you lure us to this suspicious room!"
"I don't remember inviting Zuang? I just told Hestia about this room!"

Hestia confirmed this.

"Oknodie wanted me to come here because she was bored at dawn. I'm just sorry I made Oknodie wait here alone for two days already."
"No, I never waited!"
"Oknodie is always so kind-hearted. Hiding the truth to spare my feelings, not wanting me to feel bad about making her wait without sleep."

From Zuang's perspective, Oknodie's awkward smile made her wonder if she had actually fallen asleep and really couldn't come.

"So what is this room for?"
"It's the Room of Two-Sided Strips!"

That's not what I asked.

"...Right, the Room of Two-Sided Strips. But why does this room exist?"
"It's a room where seniors collected secrets of the academy for their juniors!"
"So there are actual victims who experienced all this? There's a senior who was dragged into the 'Become an Iron Man' lecture in their third year just because they drank 'Protein Soup' five times?"
"If you don't believe it, why not ask Professor Platon directly tomorrow?"
"No thanks. That's scary."

Zuang thought to herself.
Judging by the personalities of the professors described here, they probably wouldn't let a student who discovered their secrets go easily.
They might even force them to sign a soul contract stating: "I will take the 'Become an Iron Man' lecture in my third year. Of course, I will keep this secret."
