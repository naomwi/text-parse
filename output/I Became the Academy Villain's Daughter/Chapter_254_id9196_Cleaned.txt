# 254 - Level 4 Dark Ball

The few remaining Holy Mirrors of the hero twisted their paths with lightning-fast reactions, enough to warp the trajectory of the ball!

In the chaos, I retrieved the ball, infused it with all my might using the Dark Mana Overload, and launched it.

He seemed to sense the danger of this ball, but it was already too late.

'If you wanted to block it, you should have done so while I was triggering the Dark Mana Overload.'

There are certain finishing moves that cause havoc once activated.
Like the Demon King's <Instant Death Ray>, the gods' pathetic <Heresy Declaration>, or the Dragon Headmaster's <Academic Warning>.
The Level 4 Dark Death Ball was inspired by those unblockable finishing move patterns.

"Urgh...!?"

The hero's eyes grew desperate.

<Full Power Release>

They say even a worm will open its mouth wide and bite your ankle when stepped on—the cornered hero's resistance was no joke.
The remaining Holy Mirrors simultaneously emitted intense light, displaying tremendous output beyond anything I'd seen before.
A total offensive that poured out all remaining finishing moves without any thought of conservation!

'Even Kasia would have been eliminated if she had to face this much attack alone!'

Yet even amidst all this, he had hidden an insidious trap.
Among what appeared to be ordinary enhanced finishing moves, one blade of light with a different color flew in sinisterly.
When the secretly hidden 100-stack finishing move suddenly appeared, even I, a veteran player, was startled by the hero's thoroughness.

KABOOM!

The force was so powerful it pushed me back!
The thick explosion made me cough repeatedly, but through the smoke, conflicting emotions emerged as the shape became visible.
Not a blade strike, but a ball.
As I saw the slowly advancing ball, I realized.
The Level 4 Black Ball had overcome the hero's 100-stack ankle trap.

"Heheh. My finishing move won!"
"You stopped a strike that even I would struggle to block...?"

Powerful attacks always have gimmicks.
The hero's gimmick involves storing attacks through mirrors, amplifying stacks by passing attacks between mirrors, and launching surprise attacks from unexpected angles or multiple directions.
Storage, stack amplification, and firing.
That's the essence of Holy Mirror.
My gimmick isn't nearly as complicated.
It's as simple as a child's random scribble on paper.

Colors become closer to black the more they overlap.
The same applies to attributes.
Fusion of attributes.
Multi-attributes starting from dual attributes.
Simple water becomes more powerful when mixed with wind, and even incompatible water and fire can be utilized to create scalding steam that damages respiratory organs.
Multi-attributes are an inevitable rite of passage on the path to the pinnacle of all magic.
Anyone who reaches the summit eventually attempts it.
The attempt to integrate all attributes they know.

Of course, it doesn't include all attributes in the world.
It only aims for the maximum within one's knowledge.
Even then, due to lack of ability, resources, and various practical difficulties, some attributes are excluded and compromised.
Magic Tower Masters grandly call such incomplete attribute magic "Ultimate Magic."

'I'm not ready to contain everything yet either!'

There's no attribute I don't know.
But my body hasn't matured enough.
I don't have a 230cm body stronger than steel to withstand countless attributes, nor do I have mana as vast as an inexhaustible sea.
I've compromised.
More than the Magic Tower Masters did.
It probably falls short of standing shoulder to shoulder with Ultimate Magic.
But if it's just below that.
If it's at the level of Arcane Magic, a technique that aspires to the ultimate.
Even this small dodgeball can adequately contain it.

"Level 4 Dark Ball."

Category - Dark Magic, Life Magic, Enhancement Magic, Arcane Magic
Activation - Gathering
Specialization - Omnipotence, Cancellation, Absorption, Tracking, Neutralization
Penalty - Regression of mastery upon defeat

The original <Gathering> magic isn't anything special.
Gathering dust during cleaning.
Gathering items left in laundry pockets.
Among practicing magicians, it's considered a rustic magic that only married magicians learn, something housekeeping maids might use in daily life!
In fact, even Leape once used this magic to find snacks I had secretly hidden in the cupboard and locked them all away in the snack box.

'But for a veteran player, there's no more broken magic than this!'

All magic or functional skills improve in efficiency, power, or performance the more they're used, gaining experience.
Just as <Hiding> unlocks the additional function <Stealth> and <Observation> unlocks the special effect <Auto-Analysis>, magic also dramatically improves in performance.
However, some magic has the secretive and amazing characteristic of steadily accumulating invisible experience points even without visible experience gain.

For instance, "collecting" food encyclopedias.
Or "collecting" insect encyclopedias.

Gathering, and gathering more.
Magic that secretly gains experience through filling blank encyclopedias with knowledge, understanding, and experience.
That's precisely what this <Gathering> magic is.
People say the difference between a child struggling to gather cookie crumbs and a housewife effortlessly matching chopsticks from a utensil holder is simply skill.

But they don't know where that skill originates from.
That's why the power only increases as much as what you've collected and experienced throughout life.
Merchants' and nobles' children are good at gathering not because of strict home education in organization, but because they've done extensive collecting.
But I know.
That encyclopedia collection affects it.
So without separate practice, my power was guaranteed because I was more dedicated to encyclopedia collection than anyone else.

I elevate this further with the amplification power of <Dark Mana>.
By combining the various attributes possessed by a "veteran player" in appropriate forms like putting together a puzzle, I can activate an even more powerful gathering.
As a result, a pitch-black gathering that shouldn't exist in this world was born.

Level 4. Dark Ball.
Its effect: absorbing all attributes one possesses.

Swordsmen often misunderstand.
Thinking attributes are only handled by magicians.
But there's also something called physical attributes.
Piercing attribute.
Slashing attribute.
All techniques activated by wielding weapons have attributes.
And there are applied attributes created from combinations of all these attributes.
The flash slash attribute that cuts faster than a normal slash.
The heavy strike attribute that crushes stronger than a normal impact.
Even in physical applied attributes, there exist extreme techniques that delve deeply into a single path or arcane attributes that combine multiple attributes.
And among them, there's no attribute I don't know.
Even if it's the hero's attack.
No, especially because it's the hero's.
That's obvious.
What kind of veteran player would I be if I didn't know the skill composition of the main playable character?

"I've attacked this much. Why won't it stop!"

That's why all the attacks the hero desperately poured out were neatly separated by attribute and absorbed into the dodgeball.
The ball approaches slowly, creating increasing despair the closer it gets.

Haha!

The Dragon Headmaster, who realized the principle of this ball, laughs saying it's very omoshiroi, that he's dying of amusement—in inverse proportion to the hero's face darkening with agony.
So delicious.
So satisfying.
This is dodgeball!
Just as I was thoroughly satisfied, a voice awakening the hero's spirit was heard.

"Snap out of it, Ishtar!"
"Yupi...?"
"Did you start this fight to show the greatness of Dark Mana and make them submit? That's not it!"

Light returned to the hero's eyes.
The hands that had been desperately unleashing various sword techniques stopped.

"That ball absorbs power. But there's no ball in the world that can absorb power infinitely without conditions."

Offering unnecessary advice.

"Take this!"
"Eek!"

When I threw a stone out of frustration, the saintess who was standing with her shield was pushed outside the line and eliminated, disappearing from the court.
I removed the saintess, but the hero's eyes, now regaining their composure, stared intensely at the power elements constituting my technique with mana-infused magic eyes.
It's not such an immature power that it could be deciphered by a lower-grade student, but Ishtar is certainly not at a lower-grade level either.

"Dark Mana, I see. Dark Mana is adding rotation through the force of opposing attributes pushing against each other."
"Hmph. What difference does knowing that make? Just get eliminated already. Bad hero. Shame of the first years, spy of the second years!"

I hoped he would get upset by my colorful insults and lose his insight in a war of words, but befitting the cheat hero chosen by the One True God, determination filled his eyes.
He figured it out.
Just by briefly examining the weakness in the <Gathering> that I, in my inexperience, had deployed.
It's the effect of the hero class job characteristic that increases the probability of gaining insight by 5000% when cornered.

"Just as mirrors have a limit to how much they can hold, unable to charge beyond 100 stacks, your Dark Ball also has a limit to how much power it can gather."
"Ugh."
"If one type of power accumulates excessively, then that power will dominate and break the ball's balance, right?"

If only he had stopped there.
As expected, the hero easily jumped over the veteran player's trap.
Instead of feeding it slash attributes by swinging his sword, he shot only attributeless energy without any properties, feeding energy like a mother bird feeding her chicks.
The Dark Ball, with all sharp intent and dangerous attributes subsided, was overwhelmed by the colorless energy that covered the entire ball.

Thud.

As a result of the colorless energy growing so large that even the <Tracking> specialization technique contained in the ball couldn't exert its power, the ball simply dropped to the ground.
Using the principle that softness overcomes strength, he completely neutralized the ball by reversing the amplification effect of Dark Mana.

"Falling to your own power... To reverse-engineer the 'containing' attribute of Holy Mirror right before my eyes like this. You really are a frightening child, aren't you?"

The hero's eyes settled calmly.
Somehow I could tell.
Despite having wasted a lot of power earlier and used great strength to stop the ball, the hero at his most exhausted state was paradoxically the most dangerous.

"Tch. I have no choice. I really didn't expect you to block it, but since it's come to this, I'll finish this once and for all."

The eyes of the hero, who had found composure, trembled again.

"It's still not over? Don't tell me... you have Level 5 left?"
"Ah. Level 5 is too much. Not with a first-year's body."

The hero sighed in relief.
Isn't it a bit too early to feel relieved?

<Level 4 - Dark Ball>

Another Dark Ball began floating through the air.
However, this ball wasn't heading toward where the hero stood.
The hero's gaze followed the direction the ball was heading.

TSUPABAABABA!

Waiting in that direction was an infinite acceleration repetition zone making tremendous noise, where the ball, now barely visible, was passing through in flashes.

"...Oh, no."

A clever gimmick where the ball's nature changes based on the most absorbed attribute, turning the high-powered finishing move intended to stop the ball into a threat to oneself.
I'm really excited to see what happens when that ball touches that terrifying infinite acceleration repetition zone.
