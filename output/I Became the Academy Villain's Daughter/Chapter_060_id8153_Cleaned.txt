# <60 - A Lecture That Kills Your Appetite>

Young aspiring adventurers aren't very bright.
The Adventure Department was known as a place for underachievers, so their simplistic approaches were to be expected.
That's why he had no interest in giving mundane lectures.
He'd avoided giving lectures by placing restrictions that made it difficult for even advanced classes to attend, and for the same reason.
After all, as a former hero, his lectures should be rare and exclusive—not something just anyone could experience.

'The headmaster said there would be at least one interesting student, and he was right. Oknodie. This student is interesting.'

Destroyer felt a sense of freshness.
To think that a mere freshman would provide a perfect 100-point answer to the secret of the "Never-Decreasing Sheep."
If this child had been an active hero, she might have reached the answer much faster than he and his companions did 15 years ago, solving the root of the problem.

"Do you want to hear the truth?"
"Of course!"
"Then I'll tell you."

Destroyer recalled the truth he had discovered five years ago and began to speak about the traces of slaughter hidden behind the pastoral daily life.


* * *


Fifteen years ago, the first person to request help from the Adventurer's Guild was a shepherd who had lost his sheep.
The culprits were other shepherds.
In a remote village with little food, they secretly killed and ate sheep, making it look like the work of monsters. Adventurers who followed the traces eventually gave up, either catching insignificant monsters or returning empty-handed.
Meanwhile, the shepherd who committed the crime grew bolder.
He killed the shepherd who owned the stolen sheep and hid his corpse inside a sheep's skin.

Wild animals were the first to notice the crime.
The smell of corpses hidden among the sheep.
That's what frightened them.
The scent of death suggested the presence of a predator hiding among prey.
Even wolves starved to the point of showing their ribs didn't dare to raid the abnormal sheep pasture.

After seasons passed and years went by.
The culprit targeted another shepherd's sheep and killed their owner.
The vacant positions were filled with new refugees who had fled from the lord.

No matter how long you raise sheep, the compensation is meager.
It's hard to find merchants who promise proper prices to shepherds in remote villages.
For this reason, it was easy to convince others that missing shepherds had simply sold their sheep and left.

Suspicious shepherds repeatedly called for adventurers, but they all just searched the wilderness beyond the village for monsters that didn't exist.
Eventually, all the shepherds fell victim, and the bizarre cohabitation of sheep and corpses continued.
The sheep didn't bleat, didn't graze, and couldn't even walk straight.
The villagers, hungry and poor, cooperated with the murderer who had become the village chief, receiving food in exchange for keeping the secret.
Thus, the chief raised his "sheep," and occasional survivors who tried to escape without their sheep disguises became meals.
Then he would send requests.
Asking to find out why sheep were disappearing.
And so more "sheep" would come to the village.
That was the secret of the "Never-Decreasing Sheep" that lasted for ten years.

"What a distasteful mission that was."

Now he understood why the reward varied each time.
It depended on the possessions of the sacrificial "sheep."
The Adventurer's Guild had been completely fooled.
Over ten years, the guild had formed the impression that this was an easy request that sometimes yielded minor monster kills and always paid well despite requiring some legwork. How could they have seen through it?
Countless novice parties visited the village of sheep.
Some earned money and left, while others disappeared and became new sheep.
The long history of the sheep village finally came to an end when Destroyer exposed it.


* * *


"Aren't you scared? A tale of adventure formed by human malice worse than any monster."
"It's creepy, but it was interesting!"
"...Interesting? A story where many people died?"
"People usually think adventurers just hunt monsters or explore unknown regions. I was happy to indirectly experience something uncommon in the Adventure Department."
"Why does that make you happy?"
"It's like finding and punishing a *monster* hiding in human society, like exploring the *unknown within the ordinary*. It made me think that heroes are adventurers too, and adventurers can have hero-like adventures!"

Destroyer smirked at Oknodie's genuinely excited expression.

"Cheeky kid. You might not have what it takes to be a hero, but you've got the qualities of a good adventurer."
"What's that?"
"Not being afraid."
"Ugh. What's that supposed to be? That doesn't sound impressive at all."
"People often get scared. When they lack the skills to solve a problem themselves, when they're overwhelmed by ugly truths they don't want to imagine. When they fear approaching the unknown."

Destroyer reached out his hand to the fearless girl.
When she extended her hand for a handshake, he slapped it away and retrieved his paper using telekinesis.
He warned the embarrassed Oknodie.

"Still, you need to learn a bit of fear. If a mere freshman walks around fearlessly, who knows where you might become a sacrificial lamb."

With that malicious story, he waved his hand dismissively.

"The lecture is over. Since your answer was correct, this explanatory lecture ends here. I've added some points as a reward for being a good student, so don't starve and go to the cafeteria."
"Thank you!"
"Oh, by the way. I heard they're serving lamb skewers for lunch today."
"......"
"Hehehe. Eat plenty. If you can."

The lectures are interesting, but the professor's personality is terrible.
With that sentiment clearly written on her dissatisfied face, Oknodie left the classroom.

"With expressions that transparent, she would have died."

The former hero had a backstory he didn't share in the freshman's first lecture.
The village chief whose identity he uncovered wasn't just an ordinary person who became addicted to human flesh for survival.
Though that might have been true at first, over time, the chief met a wandering black mage who coveted his corpses and exchanged them for magic.

Anti-decay.
Walking gait of the dead spirits.
Curse of silence.

Through various spells, he maintained the sheep more conveniently.
After his identity was revealed, it got even worse.

Sheep uprising.
Breath of decay.
Corpse bomb.

Magic that could turn ordinary adventurers into bloody pulp in an instant came in succession.
In the ten years since his inexperienced past, it wasn't only the hero who had grown stronger.

"It would be troublesome if a promising talent rushed out overconfident and died carelessly."

To prevent such a sight, the headmaster principally doesn't allow first-year students to do practical training.
But wouldn't experiencing an attack "in a dream" indirectly not count as practical training?
Destroyer went to find the alchemy professor's laboratory.

"You want a first-year to gain indirect experience through dreams?"
"Yeah."
"You, who was desperate to avoid giving lectures?"
"That's right."
"How unusual. For you to find it so entertaining."

The alchemy professor licked her lips.

"If she's that clever, should I try to lure her into my lectures too?"
"Back off. She's still a first-year."
"She could skip grades."
"Shut up. Don't touch someone I've already claimed. Have some business ethics, you witch."
"How stingy. It's not like she'll wear out if we share."
"So when will you make her have this dream? During the next lecture?"
"I need to teach my lecture during lecture time. Why would I make her dream then?"
"...Don't tell me."
"I'll make her dream it tonight."

After witnessing the tragedy of an entire village through ten years of waiting, he had made a promise to himself.
To take care of things as soon as they came to mind.

Hidden lectures pay well.
But the price was also steep.

"Oknodie. Are you feeling sick?"
"No."
"Then why aren't you eating your lamb skewers and giving them to me?"
"I just don't feel like eating them."
"Too greasy?"
"I just feel weird about sheep now."
"I understand. Sheep are kind of cute."

Isabel understood Oknodie's pickiness.
She understood, but she took the lamb skewers that had been moved in front of her and placed them back on Oknodie's plate one by one.

"Still, eat them all. If you want to grow tall, you need to eat a lot."
"I think 2m 30cm would be too tall for a woman. I'll be satisfied with 2m 20cm."

Making ridiculous excuses, Oknodie moved the skewers back to Isabel's plate.

"That's still ridiculously tall, you know?"

Looking at Oknodie with annoyance, Isabel couldn't easily return the skewers, wondering what if she really did grow over 2m from eating them.

"What are you two fussing about? If the little mouse doesn't want to eat, just give them to me."

Son O-cheon snatched the plate and stuffed the lamb skewers into his mouth.

"I see you still have an appetite."

Gisele added a comment.

"What? Do you dislike lamb skewers too?"
"Have you already forgotten whose lecture awaits after lunch?"
"Ah. The headmaster."

Son O-cheon put down Gisele's plate that he had been about to grab.
Even the gluttonous monkey demi-human lost his appetite at the thought of attending the dragon headmaster's lecture.
