# 156 - Reassurance

Arcadia found a very bold way to help.

"From here to there, they're all friends I invited. This should be enough to ease your father's worries!"
"Eeeek!! There are so many people, too many people!!"

Titeomillio was overcome with terror.
I was trembling with fear as well.
I had mentioned needing ten friends, asking for help.
I did bring it up, but...
The line that wrapped around the courtyard and extended all the way to the shade tents set up outdoors consisted of over a thousand people.

"Hello, Princess! You look amazing today as always!"
"Fufu. Is that so? Frill, that frilled bracelet on your wrist looks quite stylish."
"Hehe. It's designed after my name, so I have to show it off everywhere!"

Titeomillio's friend candidate number 1, Frill.
After chatting with Princess Arcadia with a honey-dripping expression, she approached Titeomillio, who was sitting in a chair trembling in fear at the fate awaiting him.

"Hello, Titeomillio! I heard everything from the Princess. Let's go meet your father together!"
"Huh? Oh, um, okay..."

Like a criminal being forcibly escorted, he trudged reluctantly toward where Titeomillio's ghost papa was waiting.

"Hello, Father. I'm Frill, Titeomillio's friend."
"Oh my! To think our Titeomillio has such a cute friend. Why didn't you tell me sooner!"

Ghost Papa was thoroughly satisfied.
With every word Frill spoke, he showed enthusiastic reactions, openly displaying his contentment, pride, and joy.
It was enough to make one think she should become a real friend out of guilt.

"Fufu. What an interesting father you have. Let's stay close friends from now on, Titeomillio."
"Y-yes...! Thank you, Frill!"
"Bye-bye~"

As Frill waved goodbye and trotted away, Princess Arcadia raised her flag before there was time to feel the lingering sentiment of parting with a new friend.
Then the next young lady, holding ticket number 2, approached with her combat-ready drill hairstyle.

"Waiting number 2, Canelian Truffle. I'm here to be your companion for the day at Princess Arcadia's request. I'm the daughter of the ruling family of Truffle City in the Southern Holy City-State Federation."
"Whaaaat?! Really? I'm also from the Southern Holy City-State Federation. My father is the mayor of Canele City!"
"I heard you carry expensive toys around, so I see you do have financial means."

She seems aristocratic and intimidating.
But overcoming that first impression, the two unexpectedly found they were from the same region, lived in similar areas, and had much in common. They conversed like real friends.
When she was introduced to his father with a gentle smile, Titeomillio's ghost papa was extremely pleased.

"At this rate, we'll reach ten friends in no time. Thank you, Arcadia!"
"Don't thank me yet. You're about to go through quite an ordeal."
"Pardon...? What ordeal?"
"You may not know this, but the life of a social butterfly is truly exhausting. Always maintaining a smiling face, dealing with people who approach you wherever you go, never having a moment to let your guard down."
"Um, Arcadia? I just need to be recognized as having ten friends..."
"Do you think all these students who took time out of their day to come here would simply accept 'Thanks for helping, but we're not friends anymore' and walk away?"

Arcadia smiled a frightening smile that tasted of social bitterness, like a lich wielding wicked wisdom.

"They'd become enemies, not friends. 'Who do you think you are to reject us? You think you're so great? We'll see about that.'"
"Eek!"
"Or you could just become friends with everyone. Titeomillio has voluntarily chosen a very difficult life. But since you've gained many friends as you wanted, you'll accept it gladly, right?"
"Your smiling face is scary, sister...!"
"Oh my. Don't be frightened, Ti. It's not like I've been finding it difficult to manage all my followers lately, or that I need to start filtering people out, or that I've marked you as a secretary to help manage people?"

...She's marked me!
I'm confirmed as her secretary-designate!

"Still, this will be a valuable lesson for that child. If you keep avoiding people because you're afraid of dealing with them, you'll never learn how to interact with them."
"So you had a plan all along, sister?"
"Of course. Some people simply want to curry favor with those in power, some want a faction to rely on, and some are starved for human connection. Learning to distinguish between them comes with experience in dealing with many people."
"You're testing Titeomillio to see if he can handle such an important role?"
"Something like that."
"How can you trust Titeomillio with such an important task?"

Dealing with people isn't easy.
Not only is it difficult, but it's also hard to trust someone with the task.
A representative might subtly include people related to their own interests on the list, or accept bribes to keep quiet about those who might cause harm.
Even if Arcadia had difficulty balancing her studies and network management, such a position of responsibility would be coveted by all her followers.

"Think about it. Titeomillio doesn't want benefits; he just needs friends, right?"
"That's true."
"And his reason is very touching and heartwarming—not wanting to worry his papa."
"That's right."
"If he's a talented individual who has repeatedly proven to have a good heart, chosen by you who knows how to select friends carefully, he'll surely be helpful once he learns how to choose people."
"Ah, I see... Wait, what?"

Everything sounded good except for that unnecessary addition.

"What do you mean I select friends carefully?"
"Oknodie only likes good people, don't you?"
"Zuang is good but also bad, isn't she?"

The same goes for Hestia and Gisele.
Haven't I become friends with anyone I needed to, regardless of their disposition?

"I'm not talking about good and evil as people perceive them, or the threshold for extreme behavior. I'm talking about the difference in how one treats others."

Good people and bad people.
Arcadia has her own criteria for distinguishing between them.

"Still, I'm worried. I wonder if that timid child can endure to the end while sensing people's malice."

We would soon find out about Arcadia's concerns.
The first two people passed the test in succession, but...

*Current friend count - 3/10

Frill and Canelian Truffle.
After the first two were counted, not a single name among the hundreds that followed was recognized as a friend by Titeomillio's ghost papa.


* * *


Titeomillio felt burdened.

"What's the big deal about being friends? Just chat once and you're friends!"
"Huh? Y-yeah, that's right. Hehe."

Overflowing social butterflies.

"You, what's your relationship with Princess Arcadia?"
"Well, the Princess and I greet each other when we pass by..."
"That's no relationship at all. What do you do that makes the Princess pay special attention to you?"

Curious children from noble families.

"Hey, can you put in a good word for me with the Princess? I'll give you this, okay?"
"B-bribery?! I can't accept such things! I don't have the authority or qualification to guarantee I can say anything good about you to Princess Arcadia."
"Are you saying my gesture isn't enough? You're really greedy."
"That's not what I meant!"
"Fine, whatever. If you don't want to, just say so. Why do you have to make me look like the bad guy? You're really nasty."

Even students who couldn't believe he was simply recruiting friends.

"Haah. I'm really exhausted..."
"Are you alright, my child?"
"I'm fine. Papa, you must be tired too..."

Titeomillio could tell from his papa's unhappy expression that he wasn't the only one disappointed by the impure motives of others.
He didn't think the Princess was doing this to torment him, but he did feel a bit resentful. However, if he complained in front of his papa, it would only cause more worry.
Since he couldn't achieve the opposite of his goal to ease his papa's concerns, Titeomillio smiled brightly.

"Look, Papa. I'm a total social butterfly, right? I have so many friends!"
"I see... But this papa is worried. I wonder how many of these shallow relationships will stand by you when you're truly in need."
"It's not like I only have shallow friends. Even if I meet a thousand people, I'd like to believe it wasn't wasted time if I can make even one more true friend."
"You can even say such mature things. Our baby Titeomillio has really grown up."
"I was surprised to see you today too, Papa. I always thought you were just fussy and embarrassing, but you've shown me your mature side a lot today."

Thank you. Please continue to be friends with Titeomillio.
Even such a common phrase was rare from the doting papa she knew.
Perhaps she wasn't the only one who had grown.
Papa had grown too.
Just as she was trying to be a good friend to others, her papa had been working to become a proud father who wouldn't be embarrassed to show himself in front of others.

"Thank you, Princess. For creating this wonderful opportunity."
"Is your father satisfied?"
"Not completely, but he seems satisfied enough."

While Princess Arcadia and Titeomillio were talking,
a light suddenly flashed from the alley.

"Huh? That's where Papa was..."

When the surprised Titeomillio and Arcadia ran over, they found Oknodie holding a letter.

"Oknodie? Where's Papa?"
"He asked me to give you this!"

When Titeomillio opened the letter, inside was a message of encouragement for her future school life and a pair of rings.

-These are friendship rings to cherish your friendship.
-Share them with your closest friend.

After thinking for a moment, Titeomillio held out a ring to Princess Arcadia.

"Princess, it may not be enough to thank you for your help, but would you accept this ring?"

As Titeomillio shyly offered the ring, Princess Arcadia smiled awkwardly, noticing Oknodie's expression.
A look of shock spread across Oknodie's face.
Like a player who just missed an event reward right before their eyes.
It was naturally shocking, but Titeomillio's gift wasn't over yet.

"Is it okay if I don't give it to Oknodie? The letter says to give it to my closest friend."
"That's fine. I'm giving the other one to Oknodie!"
"What...? Then what about your share, Titeomillio?"

Titeomillio fidgeted with her hair and shyly confessed her feelings.

"I think I'd be happier if my most cherished friend and the Princess I'm grateful to wore these rings and thought of me."
