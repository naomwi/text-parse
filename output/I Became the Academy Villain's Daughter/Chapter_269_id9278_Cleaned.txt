"Thanks to you, I've been saved many times. I won't forget what happened today. Oknodie. And April. You too."

Andersen parted with firm resolve.
Today, he had felt numerous moments of shame.
During gathering, a moment of carelessness nearly got him harvested by a monster instead.
While chatting and neglecting external surveillance, he failed to notice the approaching student on leave sooner.
He lacked the skill to retrieve April's trapped tail from the cart without being discovered.
During Senior Mandela's attack, he was barely able to survive.

'I have a long way to go.'

His complacent mindset—that this level of effort was enough, that just struggling through difficult lectures meant he was working harder than the class average—was fundamentally overhauled.

'If I can't save people in need with my own strength, I have no choice but to rely on others.'

He had to accept help from a child, from a woman weaker than himself.
After graduation, he would need help from his family.
Help from the Western Noble Alliance.
This way, he would accumulate countless debts and favors.
The more debts one has, the less freedom remains.
Unwanted relationships and time commitments would pile up, gradually eating away at his life.

'I'll become stronger. Both body and mind.'

The Andersen heir's power-up event ignited his passion with "Great Success."


* *


With his changed determination, he saw his surroundings differently.
Andersen noticed the changes among the students.

"When times are tough, only those close to you will help. Wake up from the dream that heroes will save you."
"The same goes for the Imperial Princess. Of course, Her Highness is kind and doesn't easily abandon her subordinates, but there's a limit to how many people can benefit from being on her team."
"In the end, only peers at a similar level can realistically help each other."

First, there was a noticeable increase in "5-person teams."
Lower-class students, learning from the culture that had emerged in the upper classes due to the Dragon Headmaster, banded together in groups of five to combine their strength.

"Young Master Andersen, this is surprisingly beneficial. We share gathering duties for materials needed in lectures so we don't have to take from other students. Conversely, our numbers make us less likely to be robbed."

The 5-person team structure had become standard practice, even recognized for its effectiveness by the noble heirs of the Western Noble Alliance.

"Look, gathering still takes time. It's more efficient to buy points from the Dark Guild and invest that time in training and studying. That way, you earn bonus points in lectures and make more points—it's a shortcut."
"On the flip side, if you happen to gather more materials than planned, the Dark Guild is the only place that will buy them for points. It's a waste of time to go around selling them yourself."

The Dark Guild's influence grew as it handled material purchases and sales, along with the customers using their services and the increasing number of lower-class students helping with operations.
The Dark Guild faction, led by the upper-class quintet of Gisele, Isabel, Son O-cheon, Dorothy, and Rockfell, had effectively become the largest power bloc among first-years.

"What's the big deal about 5-person teams? Our Western Noble Alliance should aim higher."
"Do you have something in mind?"
"Like the Dark Guild, we should consolidate our power and expand our influence."
"Ooh!"
"Splendid, Young Master!"
"Do you have any specific plans?"

Unlike the students who simply expressed admiration, Yelchin Brownie, the fourth-ranked member of the Western Noble Alliance, adjusted his tie with arrogant eyes as he reviewed the current first-year power structure.

"There are many first-years who have formed factions besides the Dark Guild. The 'Camela Brigade' with 25 members using strange 'Pet Contracts' to gain followers. The 'Hero's Guard' with 30 members, formed because the hero felt the need for supporting forces. The 'Jigoku Pirates' with 15 members, formed to rob the annoying increased numbers of small fry. They all have clear goals or means of solidarity."

The Camela Brigade had forced loyalty through contracts.
The Hero's Guard had selfish intentions to gain benefits from the hero.
The Jigoku Pirates had the wicked intention of taking materials and points without effort.

"To be frank, our Western Noble Alliance is merely a social club for western nobles, and even that has lost many members since Imperial Princess Arcadia's downfall, hasn't it?"

The Alliance, originally 15 members strong, had dwindled to half that number—just 8 people.

"What are you saying, you who rank fourth among just eight people?"
"If we're down to eight, your skills must be halved too."

Yelchin's face crumpled at the criticism from the male students who had taken the second and third ranks in Arcadia's absence.
Though factually correct, the other popular nobles weren't bringing up these facts not because they were unaware, but because they were temporarily setting aside unnecessary facts to maintain morale.
In other words, Yelchin was arrogant but socially clueless.
Still, Andersen defended him.

"Don't be too harsh. Yelchin is right. Everyone has been uncomfortable mentioning it, but our Alliance has shrunk significantly since the beginning of the semester. But that's not all we've experienced."

These eight people shared something in common.
A powerful advantage that remained even after dozens of followers who had supported Arcadia or themselves had fallen away.

"The lectures we attend are the most difficult among all first-years."

These eight elites had devoted themselves to training to avoid injuries during lectures—an unintended hell training.
Their bond had become much stronger than the original Alliance's.

"The new motto of our Alliance has already been decided. Training. Without training, we cannot endure the lectures. This will be true not just for the first semester but the second, not just for first-year but second-year as well."
"So we're going to transform into an organization that emphasizes training?"

Andersen affirmed Yelchin's question.

"That's the plan. Additionally, after completing a certain period of training, we'll verify our strength while boosting morale."
"Are we going to have a snack party at the school store?"
"Those 1-point jellies are delicious."
"Are you stupid? Those are slime jellies."
"Ugh!"
"No matter how commoner-friendly this Academy is, your eating habits are too plebeian. Maintain noble dignity as a member of our Alliance."

The male student who had eaten the jellies became dejected at Yelchin's scolding.
This time, Andersen stopped Yelchin.

"No, such pretensions are unnecessary."
"Young Master...? You who valued dignity above all—what are you saying?"
"Dignity comes from strength. Until now, we've borrowed the prestige of noble houses and mistaken it for our own strength. But in reality, we're just ordinary first-years."
"...!"
"What's wrong with a first-year eating jellies to save points?"
"I was just curious about what commoners eat..."

Even the jelly-eating student mumbled in confusion, but Andersen addressed that too.

"Look at reality. The difference between us and those 'commoners' you speak of isn't that great. Compared to the gap between first and second-years, or first and third-years that we saw at the Grand Sports Festival, it's insignificant."

Yelchin, the jelly-eater, and the other students finally changed their expressions.

"Don't think you'll automatically become second-years and third-years as time passes. Gift Academy is merciless. Those without qualifications cannot advance. Failing to advance and graduate—isn't that the true shame for nobles, the real loss of face?"
"Young Master is right!"
"So it's decided. Since we prioritize training, we lack time to gather materials. Therefore, we must obtain missing materials from others. However, buying with points could lead to a long-term shortage of points needed for advancement."

Yelchin asked reluctantly:

"Surely you're not suggesting we become pirates too?"
"No. We will serve as a vigilante group."
"Vigilantes...?"
"We'll use force to stop those who try to steal materials for profit, like the Jigoku Pirates, and take half of what would have been stolen as our fee."
"...If we take that much as payment, some might not be grateful even after receiving help."
"It doesn't matter."
"...Are you serious, Young Master?"

Andersen had seen it.
How pathetically the students on leave had been beaten from behind due to lack of strength.
How desperately he himself had struggled just to survive.
Compared to such shame and humiliation, taking half as payment was nothing.
In time, everyone would realize.
That leaving even half was actually generous.

"Eventually, this 'gratitude fee' of half will become the power that increases the number of applicants hoping to join our Alliance. So work hard. Build enough strength that no one can complain when we take half."

While Yelchin and the Alliance nobles were convinced, Andersen privately felt this was still insufficient and grew anxious.
The reason he didn't pursue greater ambitions was his awareness of the "Foundation Scholarship Students."

'Even we have created such a profit model and begun to build strength. How impressive must Oknodie's structure be by now, with her connections to upper-class students?'

If we were to clash with them by force, could we win?
Not yet—we would still lose.
Therefore, we must not lose public support.

"And when you see cleaning maids on campus, don't carelessly throw trash and treat them respectfully."
"Is it really necessary to be so considerate to mere maids?"
"Are you objecting?"
"N-no, not at all. Please don't make such a frightening expression... it's scary, Young Master."
"I trust you'll keep it in mind."

Let's build a force that won't lose to Oknodie's, and one that won't embarrass us before April.


* *


While Andersen was strengthening his organization's discipline, Oknodie received an unexpected proposal from April.

"Miss Oknodie, wouldn't it be good for you to start leading your own faction?"
"Eh. That sounds bothersome."
"Other first-years have felt the need to grow since the Grand Sports Festival and are quickly banding together. You can't keep wandering alone and relying on Mr. Gisele, Lady Arcadia, and others around you forever. When they're not available, you'll waste tremendous time and effort."
"Ah-hah. So you're saying I should gather errand-runners to handle troublesome tasks for me?"
"...That's not exactly what I meant, but I suppose you could see it that way."
"Well, as it happens, I already have some errand-runners who can't complain no matter how I use them."

Oknodie pulled out a piece of paper from her pocket and waved it around.
The paper contained a "List of Foundation Scholarship Students."
