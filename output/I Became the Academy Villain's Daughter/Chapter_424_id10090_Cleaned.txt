# 424 - Fourth-Year Senior

One of the third-year seniors set up "Under Construction" signs around the open area to prevent underclassmen from approaching.
They even added warning messages that approaching out of curiosity and casting spells could expose them to magical waste, potentially increasing the types of magic the thorn grass could use.

"That's quite a creative idea. We were just going to put up signs and leave it at that."
"If it gets any stronger here, it'll develop such vicious combos that even fourth-years won't be able to handle it. At that point, we'd have no choice but to go to the professors or student council..."

That was an outcome none of us wanted.

"Please take Department Head Orcatch to the infirmary. And make sure he keeps his mouth shut!"
"Got it."

Another senior took charge of handling the injured Senior Orcatch.
Since injuries weren't particularly rare at the practically-oriented Gift Academy, keeping him quiet was the main task.

"What should I do?"

The last remaining third-year senior from the Plant Club asked.
This senior's task was already decided.

"Please come with me to the fourth-year area!"
"I got stuck with the most dreadful job..."

The senior complained but didn't refuse.
Knowing how dangerous fourth-years could be, they wouldn't send a first-year alone.
Despite bullying Eungae, they showed restraint and made excuses not to send me alone—they weren't as bad as I thought.

"Be careful. The department head said fourth-years facing graduation projects are extremely dangerous. Getting involved with them could result in sudden astral projection or being thrown into another dimension—it happens all the time."
"Yes. By the way, senior, don't step on those stairs ahead! They'll send you flying to the Abyss!"
"Why didn't you say that earlier?! You nearly gave me a heart attack!!"

The third-year senior clutched their chest after avoiding the forced teleportation magic circle disguised as stairs.
Seeing a female senior with a large chest doing that made me feel annoyed rather than aroused for some reason.
I wonder why?

"So which senior are you planning to visit? There aren't many fourth-years. Especially those who are willing to negotiate rather than just taking what they want from underclassmen by force."

The Plant Club senior was smarter and more knowledgeable than they appeared.

"The skilled ones joined the student council. They might demand hush money using fines as leverage, but they won't make deals."
"I know that much already!"
"Also avoid seniors who are too skilled. They might not care about the student council and just try to extort you."
"That's obvious!"
"Well, there are scholarship students among the fourth-years too, so this probably isn't news to you."

Come to think of it, the Foundation is kind of annoying.
Veterans accumulate knowledge through trial and error over many iterations, but Foundation connections let you know everything right away—isn't that basically cheating?

"Ugh. The pressure difference is tremendous just one floor up."

Masters carry vast amounts of mana.
The density of their domains can be so high that novices might faint just by stepping in.
People in the world call this charisma, pressure, or presence.

'It's no wonder I feel crushed, like I'm suffocating from mana.'

The fourth floor of the Production Department building, where fourth-year Production Department students with more raw mana than even the Knight or Magic Departments gathered, was a mana-congested area where unconsciously expanded domains collided.
Just from the mana waves emanating from the unconsciously colliding domains, the third-year senior's expression darkened.
Their heavy breathing and trembling legs resembled a wild animal instinctively backing away after recognizing a power difference.

"What are you going to do now? Find some senior lounging around in the fourth-floor break room?"
"First, I need to buy information from the fourth-floor instructor!"
"Will the instructor come just because you call?"
"Not if I just call verbally."

After crossing the no-entry sign and reaching the fourth floor, I placed my hand on the door of the "Homunculus Cultivation Room" which had an additional warning sign. Suddenly, a person emerged from the wall.
They reacted like a parent seeing a baby rushing toward an electrical outlet with chopsticks.

"Step away. That door must not be opened."
"Yes!"
"I heard the 981st class was exceptional, but I didn't expect it to include this level of madness. That is not an area you can freely enter. Go back."
"I'll go if you grant me one favor!"
"You want me to grant a favor in exchange for your safety? Are you in your right mind?"
"Negligent management is your responsibility as an instructor!"

As an instructor guarding the entrance to prevent fearless students who ventured to the fourth floor from entering the cultivation room, this was something they couldn't ignore.

"Sigh. I hope your request doesn't cross the line."
"Please give me a list of names and locations of fourth-year Production Department seniors who have free time right now!"
"Do I look like I have enough free time to run around collecting fourth-years' location information?"
"You can gather information from other instructors using your magic watch!"
"Perhaps I should just use force to send you back to the first floor?"
"I'll give you points!"
"How much were you thinking of offering, customer?"

I generously gave 10,000 points.
In this urgent situation, this amount should be enough for the senior to gather information by distributing their own points.
Thanks to that, the information was quickly collected.

"Phew. Fortunately, there are two seniors who seem somewhat normal."
"Let's go see Grim Reaper!"
"You want to see the most dangerous-looking senior without hesitation?! Why don't you reconsider?!"

The Plant Club senior complained fearfully.
I guess even third-years don't know everything about the world.

"Senior, think about it. The Four Heavenly Kings of the Demon Lord's Army have survived despite exceptional graduates emerging every year. How strong must they be?"
"Um... stronger than graduates, I suppose?"
"Can you imagine how insane seniors who claim they'll subjugate them must be?"
"I, I see your point."

The senior reluctantly nodded.
The fourth-year instructor listening nearby seemed satisfied with my reasoning.

"Then let's go to Grim Reaper senior's workshop!"

Workshop Street was a hidden spot accessible after crossing the special barrier of the fourth-floor main building.
It was more accessible than the library, but entry conditions were the issue.

━━━
[Those who wish to enter Workshop Street must prove they are worthy of creating or possessing a masterpiece.]
━━━

The method of proof was simple.
You just needed to show an artifact of Relic grade (+10 enhancement) or higher on the inspection platform.
Of course, with my Backpack Backpack, this was an easy test to pass.

━━━
[Qualification verified.]
[Unqualified companions shall be cursed with silence and untouchability.]
━━━

Those who couldn't possess or create a single relic couldn't enter Workshop Street, and even if they tried to enter with a companion, they couldn't speak or touch anything inside.
It was a practical special barrier reflecting the artisans' unwillingness to let unqualified people waste their time.

'Then why did you bring me?'

The Plant Club senior expressed their grievance through their eyes.
Well, I did have my reasons.
Sometimes the barrier conditions change, demanding things like "lock your guarantor in a cage before entering."
After what the seniors did to Eungae, I wouldn't have felt any guilt locking them in a cage.
You're lucky you didn't end up in a cage, senior!

"Oh, an unusual customer this time. A first-year who already bought a relic possession permit? 981st class first-year Oknodie. You must have a lot of points."
"Who are you, senior?"
"Workshop Street's exclusive instructor. I handle customer recruitment on behalf of the artisans for students who enter here. What product are you looking for? Or do you have a production request?"
"I came to meet Senior Grim Reaper!"
"Oh..."

The instructor smiled and took out candy from their pocket.

"Take this candy and think carefully again. Consider whether what you're looking for can only be obtained by meeting Grim Reaper. You have your whole life ahead of you—do you really need to make such a foolish choice?"

The Plant Club senior's eyes narrowed into triangles, giving me a sharp look.
Their expression clearly said: I knew that senior was dangerous from the name alone.
But that won't stop me!

"I've thought it through. For us, it has to be Senior Grim Reaper!"
"Sigh. I clearly tried to dissuade you. Any unfortunate incidents that occur from meeting Grim Reaper are the customer's responsibility, not mine, understood?"

I dragged the reluctant senior who refused to follow and arrived at Grim Reaper senior's exclusive workshop.
What kind of senior is this person?
The discount notice at the entrance made it clear.
