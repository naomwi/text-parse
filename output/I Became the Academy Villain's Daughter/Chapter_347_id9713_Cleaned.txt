# 347 - Overflowing Sense of Justice

After even the trusted Dead Cat was defeated by Oknodie, the contract scammer Bellocasio had given up on targeting first-years.
Now he's back.
Targeting Jaku, who isn't even an upper-class student.

"You... are truly impressive. Using disguise techniques just to establish a slave contract."
"Haha. I thought any student who hadn't taken Professor Pinkberry's 'Basics and Understanding of Disguise Techniques' lecture would definitely fall for it. This is a victory of thorough preliminary research, first-year."
"You're wasting your time. I'm neither as competent nor as useful as you think."

Despite Jaku telling him to find another slave, Bellocasio just smirked.

"It's no use. What I want is an investigation of a first-year organization. Rather, I need someone smart like you who attracts less attention from others while still being a first-year."

Jaku had an ominous feeling.
This guy, could he be trying to find out about the "Organization That Plays with Oknodie"!

"The organization you'll investigate is the 'Camella Brigade.'"
"..."

Fortunately, it wasn't Oknodie's organization.
If it had been, Bellocasio would have been hastening his own demise.
The organization that plays with Oknodie is, in a broad sense, a subsidiary of the Foundation.
It wasn't an organization that a mere second-year with no backing could investigate and remain safe.

"I thought they were keeping to themselves quietly. Did they cause some trouble?"
"Their quiet existence is the problem. They're taking what's mine and expanding their influence, which is annoying."
"What did you have that they took?"
"Slaves."
"...You're brazenly absurd."

From Bellocasio's perspective, investigating the Camella Brigade was perfectly reasonable.

"The secret to my power growth is contract fraud. Normally, a first-year wouldn't be allowed to target second-years, but my influence has weakened due to opposition from Oknodie, Gisele, and Arcadia."

The so-called unfair snowball effect had greatly reduced his power, and the remnants that the Dark Association couldn't absorb were snatched up by Camella's forces, who even used the same contract fraud methods as him.
From Bellocasio's perspective, he was angry at Camella for taking slaves that should have been his!

'Setting aside this senior who's like an evil slave owner, this information would be useful to Oknodie too.'

Dealing with Bellocasio's forces, which had already been defeated once, would be no challenge a second time.
Helping a little wouldn't cause much trouble for Oknodie.
But being manipulated by others isn't exactly pleasant.

"If it's an investigation, you second-years could have done it better. Did you really need to use me?"
"Second-years are busy."
"First-years are busy too."
"We're busier than you think."

A slight impatience appeared on Bellocasio's face.

"Second-years are starting to see the terror of 'failing' before their eyes. Unlike first year when we could advance with point loans, we've hit our loan limits and can't borrow anymore."
"Then why not just avoid taking loans?"
"Do you think fools who believe they can advance just by studying can earn enough points to pay advancement fees? Even those with enough points use Student Council credit loans to accelerate their growth."

Jaku listened carefully to a story that would apply to him in a few months.

"Second-years who have already taken loans must diligently study to accumulate credits, busily complete requests and challenges to earn points, and advance through their own efforts."
"...What's the problem with that? Isn't that how all students should advance?"
"That obvious path becomes impossible due to the harsh lecture difficulties and academic schedules we suffer through."

Jaku realized something quite unexpected.

"For such a pressed second-year, you seem to have enough leisure time to use first-years as information investigators."
"I've always had a knack for enchantment and I'm also a believer in the god of contracts. Actions that wouldn't be profitable for other students are manageable for me in my spare time."

Whatever the reason, if students are normally struggling just to keep up with lectures and assignments, they wouldn't have time for contract scams. As much as he hated to admit it, Senior Bellocasio must be among the exemplary students in second year, a high achiever who keeps up well with the curriculum!
From Jaku's perspective, who didn't think he had that much extra capacity, it was a gut-wrenching realization.

"After hearing that, do you think I'll spend my precious time helping you?"
"Hmph. You will have to. According to the prohibition clause in the contract, if you don't fulfill the request, you'll have to pay a penalty. You wouldn't want to cough up a whopping 10,000 points, would you?"
"..."

10,000 points.
For a point-rich person like Oknodie, it might be pocket change they could willingly give away, but for someone like him who saves on every meal, it's an unmanageable sum.
However, it's not so impossible to pay that he needs to run to the Student Council for help immediately.
The Student Council would probably consider 10,000 points a sufficient life lesson about not carelessly touching things that belong to others.
It might be difficult to get proper help.

"I have no choice. I'll look into it right away."

But he wasn't going to take this lying down.
Unaware of Jaku's sinister thoughts, Bellocasio set a meeting place and time.

"Are all first-years either monsters or weirdos...?"

At the appointed meeting time.
After just 48 hours, Bellocasio, reuniting with Jaku, turned pale.


* * *


Bellocasio's contract came with several restrictions.

━━━
□Bellocasio's Contract
3. Restrictions.
①Confidentiality: You must not inform others that you've been contracted. If violated, penalty fee is due immediately.
②Duty of Diligence: If you don't spend at least 1 hour per day on investigation, it will be judged as unwillingness to repay, and penalty fee is due.
③Penalty Payment: The penalty is 10,000 points; contract obligations disappear upon payment.
④Performance Principle: If investigation results are insufficient after one week, penalty fee is due immediately.
⑤Forced Loan: If you lack sufficient points, you must immediately take a Student Council credit loan to pay.
━━━

Confidentiality. Duty of Diligence. Penalty Payment. Performance Principle. Forced Loan. Light but non-negligible restrictions interconnected like well-fitted links.

'As expected, the restrictions are minimal. The Student Council would have overlooked this level.'

The Student Council, mainly composed of third and fourth-years.
Though he'd heard of their existence occasionally, he had a feeling they weren't exactly proper people.
Due to the small area of the paper airplane, the text was tiny enough to require a magnifying glass to read, but if it had been written on a large poster-sized paper, it would have been a detailed and suffocating contract that was frightening just to imagine.

'Whatever the case, it means even Senior Bellocasio has a higher level of contract than this. If I were caught in such a contract, I'd really feel like killing myself.'

Still, the paper airplane contract wasn't that bad. Instead of seeking help from the Student Council, Jaku first approached the "Organization That Plays with Oknodie."

'If I act differently than usual, someone might notice something suspicious and realize I'm under contract.'

Jaku approached the other scholarship students who were greeting each other in the organization's rest area.

"Good to see you all. How are your assignments going?"

The scholarship students looked at each other in disbelief, shocked.

"Did that rude Jaku just greet us?"
"Did he hit his head somewhere?"
"..."

Behavior different from usual.
While it was good to display uncharacteristic friendliness, the scholarship students, feeling an indescribable uneasiness, immediately left the lounge to avoid Jaku.

'Tsk. Such perceptive fellows.'

Without some level of friendship, they wouldn't be willing to help.
Jaku went to the training room where several organization members were sweating and dedicating themselves to practice.

"Your skills have improved a lot. At my level, I couldn't handle your growth."

Jaku's blatant praise, when he normally wouldn't offer compliments to others even with a knife to his throat!
Anyone who knew him even slightly couldn't help but feel uncomfortable with such a statement, and gazes poured in from all directions.

"Poor guy. You must have been thoroughly scolded by the professor. That's why you're so dejected, right?"
"I understand that feeling. When a project I worked hard on got criticized, I really felt like crying."

Jaku felt unexpectedly moved by the warm consolation from Sandcooker and Rogini.
Though he unintentionally received very heartfelt comfort, that wasn't what he was looking for!

"Jaku. On days like this, don't train and rest well. Sometimes you need a day for a change of mood. Let's rest together by running 10km!"
"...Can you call that resting?"

Jaku snapped back to his senses at Mob's pathetic consolation.
He changed his plan.

"Have any of you heard about contract scams run by someone named Camella?"
"Oknodie once mentioned it was a good stock to hold onto until next year."
"..."

The very first answer was ominously bad.
Clearly sensing a landmine that shouldn't be touched from Mob's answer, Jaku tried to change the subject, but Sandcooker, whose training mood had been broken, took the bait.

"What's up with those guys?"
"They do something called pet contracts, and I wanted to warn you not to stupidly fall for them."
"Pet contracts?"
"Apparently they write contracts that allow them to forcibly demand compensation for helping with studies or assignments, like mentors."
"What? That sounds like a very healthy contract. I thought it would be like the apprentice wizard magic tower residence contract where you pay 85% of your annual income to the magic tower."

Is that the kind of contract wizards make?!

"Those are quite generous terms. The Red Magic Tower took 90%."
"Isn't that too much?"
"In exchange, we get free meals."
"I see. That's fair then."

Mob showed doubt at Sandcooker and Rogini's tear-jerking story.

"Then do pet contracts also include profit distribution? Since they blatantly call it a 'pet,' I have a feeling there must be some extremely harsh conditions."
"I guess so? Anyway, those fools who agreed to such suspicious contracts deserve what they get."
"That's true. Fools who can't even secure 5% or 10% profit deserve it."

Having become accustomed to unfair contracts in the magic tower, Sandcooker and Rogini criticized the pet contract victims without realizing they were in similar situations!

"Don't be like that. They might have been tricked without knowing. This might actually be an opportunity."
"An opportunity?"
"For us to help them."

Jaku had his own calculations.
If a selfish person who only cares about himself suddenly speaks with overflowing justice, it would look genuinely suspicious.
Anyone would suspect he was under mind control or being forced to act against his will, and soon realize he was being dragged around by a contract.
Jaku's attempt to indirectly reveal the contract!
Mob grabbed his hand enthusiastically in response to his calculated sense of justice.

"Damn, I believed in you, Jaku!"
"?"

Sandcooker patted Jaku's shoulder.

"You turned out to be a really decent guy, huh?"
"??"

Rogini fiddled with the brim of his wizard hat and smirked.

"Not bad. Oknodie was planning to use them later, but your sense of justice couldn't wait that long? Alright. I'll help you just this once, specially."
"???"

With Jaku, who had suddenly become someone unable to contain his overflowing sense of justice, leading the way, the trio went to find Oknodie.
Jaku was drenched in cold sweat.
How did things turn out like this?
No, it's still okay.
Oknodie said she was planning to use those guys later.
If Oknodie stops them, this absurd situation could end immediately.

"Oknodie. For Jaku's courage alone, please allow us to fight so Camella pays the appropriate price!"

In response to Mob's cry, Oknodie answered refreshingly.

"Well, it's a shame to lose the reward I was saving for later, but I've already enjoyed plenty of benefits at Papa's mansion, so I'll concede. I'll help you too!"
"!?"

Due to Jaku's unintentional sense of justice, an organizational conflict between the "Camella Brigade" and the "Organization That Plays with Oknodie" began.
