# Crossing the Finish Line

Professor Platon is an ego-item from his very species.
It means he's a being who has abandoned being human.

"A healthy mind dwells in a healthy body. So I thought to myself: if I possess the healthiest body and transfer my consciousness into it, I could have a healthier mind than anyone else!"
"Behold! This is the ultimate healthy rare metal body I created by spending my entire fortune. It's a body that can regenerate even if pulverized by the principal's sincere punch."

As his body emitted light and sparkled, Zuang muttered, "Titoso would love to see this."

"I'd like to enlighten you freshmen about the joy of dwelling in a healthy body, but rare metal is expensive! Even if you brought a million points, it wouldn't be enough!"

I don't need it.
Despite the students' genuine looks of rejection, Professor Platon revealed his lecture theme without concern.

"But there's a way to have a healthy body even with your inferior organic matter. Develop your physique through intense exercise and increase your recovery rate with abundant meals!"

The students' eyes sparkled.

"Abundant meals?"
"Will we get food?"
"Can I eat two portions?"

Professor Platon answered enthusiastically.

"Of course! Not just two portions—you can drink ten portions if you want."
"Woooow!"
"Professor Platon is the best!"
"The conscience of the Academy! A living intellect!"
"The greatest professor who abandoned being human! So cool!"

Professor Platon skillfully accepted the flattery pouring from all directions with a smiling face.
He raised his hand and asked:

"Do you want to eat?"
"Yes!"
"Do you want to eat a lot?"
"Yes!!"
"Then run. Run with all your might toward that mountain and return with less than 10% of your stamina remaining before the lecture ends in 10 minutes. That's your first mission."

When Professor Platon patted the students' shoulders, fist-sized muscular statues appeared on their shoulders, sticking like gum.

"Ugh, what is this?"
"Too much muscle. Gross."
"Is this the professor shrunk to one two-hundredth of his size?"

These miniatures were a kind of avatar corresponding to the professor's minor copy. Their role was...

"Oknodie's remaining stamina is 90%."
"Gisele's remaining stamina is 82%."
"Son O-cheon's remaining stamina is 96%."
"Isabel's remaining stamina is 88%."

A condition meter.
Or rather, a remaining stamina meter designed to plunge students into despair.


* * *


On the way to Mount Omine.
While friendly students ran together at the same pace, others suddenly accelerated and climbed the mountain with all their might.
Most stamina fools belonged to the latter category, and Isabel was freshly impressed by how unexpectedly strong Oknodie's stamina was.

"They're already out of sight."
"Indeed they are."

After briefly feeling left behind, Gisele and Isabel realized their thinking was too pessimistic.

"Gisele's remaining stamina is 75%."
"Isabel's remaining stamina is 84%."

Their stamina wasn't decreasing.
No, it was decreasing, but it was lasting much longer than they expected.

"Is it really possible to lower stamina below 10% in this test?"
"It will be. You need to rapidly deplete your stamina at a considerable speed. Either sprint at full power for over 10 minutes or exhaust all your strength until you can't move a finger."
"This is maddening. Isn't this lecture too advantageous for physical types?"
"No. The more accustomed to training a student is, the more disadvantageous this lecture becomes. Overwhelmingly so."
"Are you saying this while I'm dying here beside you?"

Isabel expressed envy at the sight of Gisele dripping with sweat.

"I envy your lazy body."
"Are you mocking me? Even someone as good-natured as me gets angry at times like this."
"I'm not mocking you. Your stamina just dropped again in that time."

The Professor Platon avatar sitting on Gisele's shoulder, aware of their gaze, said:

"Gisele's remaining stamina is 74%."
"The rate of decrease!"
"That's what I envy."

Gisele realized.
Since he rarely exercised, his stamina decreased easily with just a little movement.
But for stamina fools whose daily life was exercise, a lecture focused on pure stamina depletion required a completely different dimension of total stamina consumption and effort.

"Then Son O-cheon and Miss Oknodie..."
"Will have a difficult lecture. More difficult than all the lectures they've had so far combined."


* * *


Dorothy was confident.
Outside the Academy, her profession was forest keeper.
Though an apprentice, she moved through dense forests as if they were her home.
While forests and mountains were different terrains, the skills for running through forests could be partially applied to mountains.

'Hehe. This is kind of fun.'

The feeling of leaving behind those who were stronger but lacked technique was truly the best.
She felt especially good when overtaking nobles who said things like, "Impossible, keeping up with mana cultivation body enhancement techniques without any aids!" or "That's not human speed," or "To lose to a mere provincial weed-picker."

"Dorothy's remaining stamina is 45%."

Considering her starting stamina was 90%, she had consumed exactly half her stamina in one hour.
If she used the remaining half on the return journey, she could perfectly deplete all her remaining stamina.
The plan was perfect, but reality was different.

"Huff, huff... too exhausting...!"

The closer stamina gets to 0%, the more difficult it becomes.
Numerically, 45% when near full capacity and 45% when approaching bottom are completely different dimensions of physical burden.
Without superhuman willpower, squeezing out the last drop of stamina to 0% was virtually impossible.

'I came too far!'

She envied the nobles she had proudly passed earlier and began to resent herself.

'Wait? Come to think of it, aren't there people I haven't seen once since the start?'

Dorothy realized.
There were students who had gone faster and farther than her!

'No matter how badly I do, at least I'm not last!'

There's a sense of relief in knowing there are people worse off than yourself.

"Argh! Overtaken again!"
"Why is she so fast?!"
"Please trip!"

She snickered at the desperate cries of the imperial noble students as she passed them.

"Dorothy's remaining stamina is 8%."

After running with all her might, Professor Platon welcomed her with hearty laughter in the clearing.

"Excellent! You overcame the difficulties of the return journey and arrived with your stamina below 10%."

She realized.
Even faster than when she was running up the mountain.
That she had grown during the return journey.

"Am I first?"
"Yes, you're first."
"I did it!"

Though she wanted to eat a lot after running hard, after sweating so much, she didn't feel like eating anything at all.
As she caught her breath and watched people arriving at the clearing, she could see the imperial nobles she had passed earlier rushing in from the distance.
But strangely, the closer they got, the slower they moved, until they couldn't reach the finish line.

"Eeek!! What is that?!"
"Didn't I tell you? You must arrive at the finish line with less than 10% stamina remaining. Those students have more than 10% stamina, so they're getting help with stamina reduction."

The miniature avatars of Professor Platon on the shoulders of students who had been lazy and were casually returning from near the starting line suddenly grew in size.
Students who collapsed under the weight, unable to move a single step, were effectively prevented from entering the clearing!

"Oh my, I'm dying."
"We're almost there, Mr. Gisele. Just walk a little more."

Students with weak stamina collapsed face-down on the clearing ground as soon as they arrived on trembling legs.

"Where were you two running? I don't remember passing you on the way."
"We just circled a nearby hill hundreds of times."
"Such a method...!"

Isabel, who had learned clever tricks from Oknodie's entrance exam strategy, had found a loophole.
Since the condition for returning to the clearing was simply having stamina below 10%, they minimized the return distance by using up all their stamina nearby.

"Was Oknodie doing repeat runs too?"
"No. She ran ahead at the front from the beginning."

Students who had run ahead began returning one by one, but most collapsed under the growing avatars near the clearing when their stamina exceeded 10%.

"What?! It was definitely 9% before I arrived!"
"Mesugakki's remaining stamina is 11%."
"My stamina recovered just because I slowed down a bit?!"

Led by the Imperial Second Princess Mesugakki, countless others were desperately trying to deplete their stamina through repeated running, push-ups, and other exercises after discovering the loophole!

Thud- Thud- Thud-

The students who were sweating and struggling turned their heads toward the rumbling sound coming from the other side of the mountain.

"Think I'll let you go in first?!"
"Aaaah, a crazy monkey is throwing rocks!"
"Heh. Struggling is pointless. If there's a way to eliminate everyone but myself, I know it better."
"S-sticky trap?! You cowards. Do you really have to go this far?"
"You started it first!"
"Fly up!"
"Ah, that guy used flying magic!!"

The competition among the leading group, who ranked high in stamina even in the advanced class, was on another level.
Flying rocks aimed at those running ahead, sticky traps to bind the feet of those following, and students taking flight to avoid them.
The situation was complete chaos!

"Everyone move. Unless you want to freeze to death."

Irene, the Northern Grand Duchess who didn't want to get involved in the struggle, created an icy path that froze the entire terrain with one hand on the ground.

"Irene's remaining stamina is 4%."
"Just like an uncivilized barbarian from the North. She must have planned her stamina usage and poured it all out here."
"Ugh, so cold!"
"The ice wall is too big."
"It's too slippery to climb over!"

Irene's ice magic created a slide that allowed her to pass through conveniently while blocking those following with a wall they couldn't cross!
The initial attack by imperial students trying to eliminate all provincial advanced class students had evolved into a fight between both sides, but Irene's major offensive brought everything to a messy end.

"I was really lucky... I'm truly glad I came in early."

Dorothy shuddered at the sight.
No matter how confident she was in running, if there was a crazy woman freezing the mountain as she came down, Dorothy would have just slipped and fallen repeatedly on the ice.
But something seemed strange about Irene sliding down to just in front of the clearing.

"Why are there two avatar copies on both her shoulders?"

The reason became clear when Irene arrived at the clearing.

[You clung to the Northern Grand Duchess's shoulder for over 1 minute without being detected.]
[Hiding experience +10]
[Stealth experience +3]

There was one student who had stuck close to Irene's back during the confusion and came down with her.
Just as Irene arrived at the clearing and took a step, a quick-witted trickster rolled on the ground and threw herself into the clearing—it was Oknodie.

"Ta-da! Arrived!"
"Oknodie's remaining stamina is 10%."

Irene turned to look at Oknodie with surprised eyes.


* * *


Northern Grand Duchess Irene.
She was a battle-hardened field mage who had trained through countless battlefields.
Unlike most talents who were admitted based on their "potential," Irene was already a somewhat trained expert despite being self-taught.
She had been proven in actual combat.
There wasn't a commoner in the North who didn't know her name, nor a demon who didn't fear her.
That's why she always had to be prepared for assassination attempts.

<Frosty Ground>

She creates a thin layer of ice on the ground so that footsteps always make a sound.

<Cold-Blooded Space>

She freezes the surrounding air to prevent anyone from daring to approach.

<Battlefield Intuition>

Above all, her trained wild instincts instinctively detect an assassin's approach.
All three of these trained detection methods had been completely neutralized and penetrated.
By a carefree little girl who proudly thought "I did well!" and jumped into Isabel's arms, making the muscle-sore Isabel suffer even more.

'What kind of harsh infiltration training must one receive to display such skills?'

Irene unconsciously bit her lip as she imagined the unfortunate upbringing this child must have had.
