# 276 - A Bad Smile

I wasn't surprised.
It wasn't the first time NPCs acted on their own.
But this was a bit much.

"Why is Leape trying to kill April and Jaku?"
"...My Lady. I apologize for showing you such an unseemly sight after not seeing you for so long. However—"
"That's enough excuses."

As I stepped past the two and stood blocking the way, Leape's pupils trembled violently.

"You mustn't do this. The Foundation's orders are absolute. There are no orders that go unfulfilled."
"Then today will be the first exception! I said I wanted to see Leape and Jonna, not that I wanted Leape to fight with April."
"The Foundation dispatched me, an assassin maid. If I fail to carry out my orders, they'll send someone even more dangerous than me next time."
"That's fine."

I had confidence in my abilities, but there was something else giving me confidence too.

"I'm Papa's daughter after all!"

The chairman of the Foundation.
The head of this run's powerful secret organization that rivals the Academy.

"My Lady, you don't understand how dangerous that person is."
"No. I understand perfectly well."

Until now, I've been keeping my distance.
It wasn't time for that event yet.
But the final exams are just around the corner.
Summer vacation starts the week after finals end.
I can't keep turning a blind eye forever.
So I thought about it.
Which NPC from the runs I know corresponds to the chairman?
In "Graduating from the Academy by Luck," where fixed and variable elements coexist, the villains who appear as main characters have stronger fixed elements the more central they are.
Even if organization names change, faces and appearances differ, or perhaps even genders change.
There are three worst villains that remain unchanged.
Just as there are chapter bosses within the Academy, these act as season bosses with a broader scope than the chapter bosses who transform the environment outside the Academy.

The Revolutionist of the Holy Central Empire.
The Agent of All Gods.
The Head of the Organization.

All three are the root causes of triggering or directly causing regional events on a national scale.
The influence of the Wiheomhae Foundation is comparable to these three great evils that affect the entire continent.
The Foundation's chairman, Papa, must certainly be one of these three: the Revolutionist, the Agent of All Gods, or the Head of the Organization!

'Usually, having an antagonistic relationship with a season boss is natural, but this run is special.'

Without the power of one of the three great evils that wreak havoc on a global scale, the Wiheomhae Foundation's influence in this world would be incomprehensible.
Currently, due to lack of information, it could be any of the three.
But the three great evils have something in common.

'A great villain is one who sticks to their principles!'

They're not the kind of trash that makes you think "This is a season boss?" – the kind that throws away promises, has no principles, and has no self-imposed restrictions that could be called their own line they won't cross.

"Leape treats me too much like a child. I have enough power to decide the fate of a maid's life as I please~!"
"...You've changed a lot since I last saw you, My Lady. I'm not sure if this should be called growth."
"Though I haven't grown any taller!"

Still, asserting myself to Leape isn't difficult at all.

"Both of you, follow me. I won't tolerate you two having a catfight out of my sight. We need to take care of those cute tails of yours!"
"...The Foundation people's obsession with tails is truly baffling."

April followed awkwardly, closing her legs and walking stiffly.

"What are you doing? Aren't you coming?"
"...Am I allowed to come too?"
"Of course, you're involved. Both Jaku and Primer."

With a thud, someone fell from the tree.
It was Primer, who had fallen on his backside in surprise at being discovered.

"I don't have to help you up, do I?"
"I-I can go! I can walk on my own!!"

I took everyone to the communications room.
Of course, I called the Wiheomhae Foundation.

"This is the secretary's office of the Wiheomhae Foundation chairman. Please state your business."
"Hello! This is Oknodie, Papa's daughter, calling again. I find it troublesome that you're ordering the disposal of my maid without my permission."
"...I'll say this only once. Whatever you're thinking, dispose of the maid. That is certainly the path where you, Miss Oknodie, will suffer the least loss."

What?
The head secretary talking back?

"Never mind that, just let me talk to Papa!"
"I have clearly warned you."

The head secretary hung up with a heavy sigh.


* * *


The chairman's office of the Wiheomhae Foundation.
The chairman's hand, which had been scanning the northern demon-occupied territories on the world map visible through the window, stopped at the blackened, scorched lands.
The area was smaller.
At least five times smaller than he had expected.

"The assassination of the traitor hiding in the demon realm ended in failure, unfortunately."
"This information hasn't been detected by the Academy yet. Our scholarship student Magdonardo, who committed the act, has only received minor penalties as it was judged to be a simple training accident."
"Well, this alone is beneficial. There are creatures in this world as tenacious as cockroaches."

When he switched the map settings, temperature readings across the continent appeared.
The extremely high temperatures centered around forested areas indicated outbreaks of plant-type monsters like flame flowers or flame vines.
It was evidence that the spirits, thoroughly angered by high-level fire magic that had leaked into the spirit realm from the Academy, had not been properly appeased.
At least once a month.
The worldwide chaos delivered directly from the Academy like a monthly event was no longer even a joke.

"Shall we hire water and ice mages to protect our hideouts, facilities, and supplies?"
"It's a trap."

A kind and benevolent smile formed on the chairman's face.
The smile of this handsome man, possessing both youth and insight, might have seemed charming even to other men, but the head secretary felt a chill in his heart as if a knife had been plunged into it just by looking.
The chairman's smile was never a good sign.
Even more so considering the fact that he was always smiling.
Because he was always a dangerous man.

"It's an attempt to identify those who possess the intelligence network capable of holding and overseeing the continental map in their hands. The Dragon Headmaster's cunning is truly something to be acknowledged. Huhuh. Though an enemy, he's quite an interesting opponent."
"...I apologize for my shortsightedness. Your insight is always admirable, Chairman."
"As my head secretary, what strategy would you devise based on my advice?"

This was a test.
Evidence that the chairman, who enjoyed playing with and mocking people's hearts, had made him a toy.
If he failed to meet expectations, the toy would be treated harshly.
A toy without affection could be grabbed by the arm and shaken roughly, and if an arm came off, it could be hastily reattached to play with before being replaced with a new toy.
Every thought, every word had to be careful.

"...I would leak this information to the Revolutionist of the Empire."

The chairman nodded slightly, suggesting it wasn't a wrong answer.
Gaining confidence, the head secretary continued.

"The Revolutionist, as one of the three great evils, knows that our main enemies are not just the Empire and the gods, but also the Academy. They won't openly show movements favoring ice mages. But they will actively use this information as a catalyst for revolution."
"For example?"
"They will favor fire mages instead. At this time when the fire demon's energy is strengthening across the continent, fire mages are at their peak power. Good for destruction rather than salvation."
"Wouldn't it be better to sell the information to the Agent of All Gods, who is obsessed with destruction?"
"Not necessarily. The Revolutionist also needs funds for revolution. They will sell the information we leak to the Agent of All Gods. The Agent would boldly use water and ice mages without worrying about the Academy's reaction."

The chairman turned around.

"The head secretary is an excellent model student. As always, you don't miss the model answer."

Not bad.
But it meant that once again, he had failed to satisfy the chairman, and the head secretary felt diminished.
Could there be an idea in this world that would satisfy that demonic man's heart?

"...I forgot to mention. There was a call from Oknodie at the Academy."

Perhaps in this atmosphere, he might be less angry.
With a small hope, the head secretary relayed what he had heard from Oknodie.

"Children are greedy. They don't want to let go of anything they have in their hands. I don't think such childlike innocence is bad."
"..."

What wind had blown to make this man say such pleasant, heartwarming things?
It wasn't with good intentions.
The head secretary was certain of this internally.
His suspicion was soon confirmed.

"Grant permission."
"...!"
"Don't you want to see? When a child holding handfuls of candy has too much to protect with their own hands, what will they throw away first?"

The chairman spoke with his usual smile.

"Tell her. Next week, after the final exams, during summer vacation, we plan to give a special gift to the children and maids who visit the Foundation. Tell her to bring all her friends, preferably without missing a single one. Huhuhuh."

It was truly a bad smile.
