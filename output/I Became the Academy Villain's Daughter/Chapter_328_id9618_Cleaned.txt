# Chapter 328 - Private Operation 3

Walking back to the dormitory with a gloomy face after leaving the office, Arcadia sensed something suspicious.

"I'm not joining any club."
"That's a misunderstanding."
"I'm not giving away any points either."
"I have plenty of points myself."
"And I'm not accepting any proposal to attack a professor, knock them out, and pretend the assignment never existed."
"...Are there really idiots who do such things?"

Arcadia finally stopped treating the person like a peddler she was trying to shoo away and looked directly at their face. A student wearing a robe pulled suspiciously low spoke.

"Follow me if you want to save Oknodie."
"!"

She turned to follow the beastkin student who had offered information she simply couldn't ignore. The student led her to an eerily quiet warehouse area so remote she wondered if such a secluded place even existed within the Academy.

"Who are you? How do you know how to save Oknodie?"
"I'll answer all your questions at once. Wait."

Arcadia realized that not only herself but other students with familiar faces had been summoned to the warehouse area.

"It's been a while since the cruise ship incident."
"Who gathered all these people?"
"Could it be Oknodie?"

However, strangely enough, students with strong combat abilities were noticeably absent. Neither Hestia, Irene, Zuang, Jigoku, Ishtar, nor Titosoga—none of these reliable fighters were present. She thought perhaps she was the only upper-class student randomly called, but that wasn't the case either. Even lower-class students with strong combat abilities like Rogini or Sandcooker hadn't been summoned.

They had deliberately gathered only weak students.
Or rather, students they perceived as weak.
...This felt suspicious.

"I don't like this. Everyone should leave..."
"It's already too late."

As students hesitantly tried to leave the warehouse, a group of robed students pushed their way in. Animal ears poking out from beneath their robes and tails protruding from beneath pants or skirts revealed their identity without needing explanation.

"Beastkin...?"
"That's right. We are beastkin. Beastkin who have many grievances against you humans."
"We don't particularly think badly of beastkin."
"We didn't particularly think badly of you humans either. But it was you humans who betrayed us first."

The male beastkin student angrily pointed at Arcadia, who had stepped forward as the representative.

"You will be sacrifices for our Beastkin Revival Association's criminal declaration. You must pay the price for daring to raise an army and invade the Great Forest beyond the human boundary."
"Wait a minute. I really don't understand what you're talking about. Invasion of the Great Forest? That couldn't happen in today's world. Besides, it has nothing to do with us."
"Our homeland was almost invaded without any justification. If you want to blame someone, blame your own kind..."

The male student's speech couldn't continue to the end. One of the people standing behind him in a robe stuck a syringe into the back of his neck.

"Who... who's betraying... why, fellow kin...?"
"Fellow kin? Don't make me laugh."

The male student who pulled back his hood removed the animal ear headband he was wearing.

"This is the Foundation's order. Die quietly, Mordor."

Mordor, a Beastkin Revival Association scholarship student.
His true identity was a Foundation scholarship student from the beastkin selection.
Mordor couldn't hide his sense of betrayal.

"I'm... a scholarship student too... why...?"


* * *


"Oknodie demanded we strike against the Beastkin Revival Association, but isn't that quite the coincidence?"

The Chief Secretary, who had no desire whatsoever to agree with the Chairman whose humanity was questionable, found himself aligning with him just this once.

"It is indeed a coincidence. Haha. I sometimes wonder if I gave birth to a child I don't even know."

The Chief Secretary asked:

"I think there's a possibility that Oknodie might have made the suggestion knowing that the Foundation is behind it."
"What do you think, Chief Secretary? About ignoring that child's advice and leaving the Beastkin Revival Association alone?"
"Since it's an exposed organization anyway, leaving it alone would only make me uneasy. Moreover, if their planned operation has been leaked, it's even more dangerous."

The Beastkin Revival Association had planned to issue a mass killing order targeting regular students to eliminate Oknodie's position and drive scholarship students out of the Academy.
If that had been discovered, Oknodie wouldn't have taken it quietly, and the professors who valued her wouldn't ignore such a report.
The connection between the Beastkin Revival Association and the Foundation would be exposed anyway, and the Foundation's subordinate organization would be cut off.

"That's wise. In Go, the chess of the East, there's a saying: Don't cling to stones that are already dead."

The Chairman and the Chief Secretary were in agreement.

"Begin the disposal. After the semester has started, so the Academy students can watch clearly."

Main Stream Chapter 2 - The Criminal Organization Attack Incident.
It was the moment when the victims became spies from a criminal organization rather than regular students.


* * *


Foundation scholarship student Primer responded brazenly even in front of the professor who came to investigate.

"Those beastkin bastards were going to kill innocent students. What would have happened if I hadn't stopped them? I did nothing wrong."
"...There are mitigating circumstances. However, killing a fellow student and being released without any punishment might make the Academy's regulations seem like a joke. Please understand that detention will continue at least until we can verify that Primer's statement is true."

Primer willingly accepted such a minor punishment, considering it nothing significant.
When he first received the Foundation's order, he was honestly dumbfounded.
The faction within the Foundation that wanted to eliminate Oknodie was very displeased with Primer for not fully completing his mission.
He thought they had issued an unreasonable order to dispose of him, but looking at how things were unfolding, the target the Foundation was aiming for was behaving suspiciously.
Primer, a master of betrayal, quickly assessed the situation.

'Is the Foundation trying to cut ties with the Beastkin Revival Association?'

While pondering Professor Rave's attack motive, Primer realized something.
Even Oknodie had only warned the Imperial students who were against her by injuring them rather than killing them.
Those who were trying to cross a line that even the top scholarship student hadn't crossed were fools blinded by revenge.

━━━
Eliminate Mordor, a Beastkin Revival Association scholarship student, just before he implements his plan to kill other students.
━━━

The Foundation's order clearly specified the condition.
Dispose of him right before he crosses the line.
Mordor might have survived.
If he had suppressed his desire for revenge.
If he had tried to assess the situation rationally.
All possibilities that never materialized.

'Don't take it too personally, friend.'

It's not my fault you died because of your own stupidity.


* * *


Arcadia went to find someone who could explain the incident that had occurred the previous night.

"Jaku. Since you're a Foundation scholarship student, you must know the whole story, right? Is the Academy's official announcement true that both the perpetrator and victims were all affiliated with the Foundation?"
"It's true. I can roughly guess the cause too. It's the result of Primer succeeding in a 'directive reset' between himself and Mordor, who had received an excessive order due to accumulated failures."

The Foundation's directives become more difficult and dangerous with each failure that accumulates.
For things to escalate to murder within the Academy, they must have failed quite a few directives or messed up some important ones.

"What benefit does the Foundation get from doing this? They've only exposed the identity of their own scholarship students. Six more spies from the Beastkin Revival Association were caught too."
"The scholarship students who were planning the massacre with Mordor were attached to a different tail than mine."
"By different tail... you mean the Foundation's contact person?"
"Yes. You've studied the Foundation well."
"When you have an information-savvy colleague nearby, you learn a lot of information whether you like it or not."

Although it felt unsettling, the incident was already over.
Arcadia was trying to forget about it when the very next day, she heard the full story of the Foundation's staged play from Oknodie, who had returned to the Academy.

"Di! You're back. Are you hurt anywhere?"
"I'm perfectly fine! More importantly, Arcadia. Did you receive my gift?"
"Gift?"
"I issued the order to kill those evil beastkin who were going to torment everyone!"
"...!"

An order issued for one faction of the Foundation to kill members of another faction.
The strange source of that directive was now revealed.

"The order came from you, Di?!"

Di is a good child.
Not a bad child.
But if she issued a kill order to save us, should we consider her good or bad?
I don't know.
I really can't figure it out.
In her confusion, she noticed Xing smirking behind Oknodie.

"Think simply. You owe her your life. What else matters?"
"...That's right. Di helped me again this time."

To have doubted such a child, even momentarily, thinking she might be bad.
Arcadia felt intense self-loathing.

"Di. I won't doubt you anymore. No matter what happens."

Oknodie beamed with joy as Arcadia embraced her.

"Really?"
"Of course."
"Liar."

Such a resolute statement of distrust.
Arcadia was startled.

"Why... would you say that?"
"Because Arcadia hasn't reached 100 affection points yet."
