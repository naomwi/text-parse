# Chapter 275 - I Really Don't Like This

The hand gathering laundry into the basket moved with increased urgency under the pouring rain.

'Don't get too excited, stupid April. Just because people are treating you a bit better doesn't mean you should get cocky and forget your place.'

Even a Foundation spy would be fired if they failed to fulfill their duties as a maid properly.
The disposal of a spy who had outlived their usefulness was only natural.
On rainy days, one must bring in the laundry.
Even this simple task was a matter of life and death for April.

In a world where simply placing a laundry basket and clothes on a mana board engraved with cleaning magic would do the job.
Hand washing without using magic stones was reserved for expensive laundry items with separate mana formulas that could be damaged due to interference between different formulas.
Not only was meticulous washing required, but any damage incurred would be deducted from April's salary.
If she caused damage beyond what she could afford and needed financial support from the Foundation, their view of her would become even more severe.

'The Wiheomhae Foundation is an organization where even a single mistake can come back as a fatal order.'

The world isn't filled only with kind people like the Grand Duke Andersen Pretzel, who is nice even to a beast-woman (which she isn't), or the head scholarship student Oknodie, who is generous even to a lowly Foundation spy.
Compared to the vastness of the world she had seen and experienced, the significance of Andersen and Oknodie was still incredibly small.
Compared to those small people, a more familiar presence made footsteps in the mud.

Splash.

With just one footstep, her brain quickly analyzed the information.
The weight was shifted to the left foot, and the time between steps was exactly one second.
The mechanical stride of a killer who, once taking a step, never hesitates and moves forward to achieve their goal.
She remembered it.
There was only one person under her who could walk like this.

"First-year Jaku."
"Is this the maid's main job? Seems bothersome."
"As I must perform my duties as a maid right now, if you have business, please come back later."

Normally, Jaku would have quietly waited until she finished her work or simply collected his orders and left.
But today, Jaku didn't maintain the unchanging implicit promise of silence while waiting for her.

"Run away."
"...I don't understand what you mean."
"The Foundation's new tail has arrived at the Academy."

April, who had been holding laundry larger than her height in her arms, hesitated as her hand moved toward the laundry basket.

"Is it a trap?"
"I was asked to lure you out."

The sound of pouring rain intensified.
Their shoes gradually sank into the mud as the rainwater rose.

"The Foundation's decisions cannot be reversed. A man like you who threw himself into dark mana prepared to die should know that fact. Yet why show mercy to a discarded tail instead of taking the opportunity to gain favor with the Foundation's new tail?"
"A bad child has developed a wrong habit. The habit of unnecessary meddling."
"You'll die."

April spoke her honest thoughts.

"If you don't fix it, you'll be next."
"I'll keep that in mind."

Beyond the laundry, though his face wasn't visible, April imagined Jaku wearing a cold smile, letting her words go in one ear and out the other.
He was a decent man.
A student with promising prospects, though not as much as Andersen.
So she couldn't trouble him any further.

"May I ask you to deliver one last message?"
"By all means."
"Tell the dog-person that even if Happy is clumsy at maid work, she's good-hearted and intuitive, so she can find other jobs easily."
"Are you planning to die?"
"If I don't go, more people will get hurt. Including you and all the first-year scholarship students."

Oknodie was among them.
She couldn't cause such trouble to someone she was indebted to.

"Please guide me to the place where you were supposed to lure me."
"...You really are a foolish woman."

Jaku led April to the meeting place he had been told about by Primer.
As the sound of rain intensified, a chill rose from her body as it lost heat.

'What a shabby maid uniform.'

The color is black and the fabric thick so that dirt and dust won't show, yet it has no waterproof function—she had always thought it was an outfit she didn't like.
Still, it wouldn't be long now.
The time of being forced to wear clothes she didn't like would soon end.
Just as she finished preparing herself mentally, she felt the sound of rain suddenly cut off with each step.
A small soundproof area that isolates external sounds.
An assassination preparation tool commonly used by the Foundation when eliminating targets.

"Pleased to meet you, Maid April."
"The Foundation's new maid has such bright manners."
"I commend you for coming here willingly. I am not an ordinary maid."

A green-haired woman with eyes colder than the pouring rain opened her emotionless eyes through her rain-soaked dark green hair.
In her hand, which had moved to her chest, were three sharp, thin needles.

"I am the Foundation's assassination maid. From the moment I was dispatched, a cleaning maid like you who has only completed basic combat training had no chance of survival."
"I know. You don't need to try so hard to break my resolve, Maid Leape."
"...Information has leaked. Where did you hear my name?"

A slight irritation appeared on her face, which had been unpleasantly calm.
April gave a small laugh at the face that showed fatigue at having more people whose mouths needed to be shut.

"Who do you think called you here in the first place? I was the one who sent the request for combat maid support to the higher-ups."

The two maids stared at each other without avoiding each other's eyes.
Like the scene of prey and hunter encountering each other.

"Did you want to commit suicide?"
"I was simply fulfilling my Lady's request."
"...!"
"I want to meet Leape and Jonna. Not just once a month, but much more frequently. That was the wish Oknodie asked of me."
"What did my Lady bestow upon you to make you this determined?"

Combat maid support request.
April knew what that meant.
The Foundation never plants two tails in the same place.
If a replacement comes, she would be disposed of.
Oknodie's request was essentially saying that she was no longer needed and asking for a capable maid to be called in her place, even if it meant her death.
Nevertheless, April had done it.
She wrote the support request that was tantamount to her death sentence.
And sent it.
Because she knew.
A tail whose identity had been discovered would be disposed of sooner or later anyway.
Still, she didn't feel bad toward Oknodie.
The average tenure of a maid was half a year.
She had realized from traces of her predecessor that successors never even see the faces of their predecessors, so there was no such thing as a handover.
To someone who had resigned herself from the beginning to the fact that she could die at any moment, even Oknodie's sentence felt like something that was bound to happen.
Yet Oknodie had allowed her something.
Interaction with a man who looked at her with special eyes.
The chance to experience the kindness of Grand Duke Andersen.

"What was meant to happen, happened. I received excessive kindness beyond what I deserved. That's all."

A hint of acknowledgment appeared in Leape's emotionless eyes.

"What a shame. Your strong spirit is such a waste. If you had come under my direct command, I could have personally trained you as a combat maid."
"I'll gratefully accept even just those words."

End it now.
She had no regrets.
April closed her eyes and quietly assumed a prayer position with both hands folded in front of her chest.
Waiting for death, she thought.
The assassination maid she had only heard rumors about.
A professional who doesn't even make a sound when throwing weapons.
Being killed by the Foundation's high-ranking combat unit was quite a luxurious end for a mere cleaning maid like herself.

Clang!!

Because of who she was, her surprise at hearing a sound that shouldn't have been heard was even greater.

"I can't do this after all. I can't let you die like this."
"...You've really done it now."

Jaku, who had guided her here.
The one who should have simply lured her and left, had now blocked Leape's strike, clearly showing hostility toward the Foundation.

"Do you intend to default on the debt you owe to Oknodie?"
"I'll pay my debts. I just didn't specify when."
"You're the typical example of a garbage debtor."

Despite her words of condemnation, a faint smile appeared at the corners of April's lips.

"Jaku. For the sake of the guardian who recommended you as a scholarship student, I'll warn you once. Do not interfere with the Foundation's disposal operation. Before you get disposed of too."
"You're saying ridiculous things."
"...This is the death you've chosen. Don't blame anyone."

Leape gripped a dagger in reverse grip instead of throwing needles and assumed a charging stance.
Jaku's back, which was blocking April, trembled slightly.
The clear difference in skill.
It was the result of sensing an uncommon strength.

"Should I thank you for dying with me?"
"Who's dying together with whom? Don't misunderstand. Things won't go as you wish."

Jaku's gaze turned toward the entrance of the sound barrier.
Following his gaze, the eyes of both maids, Leape and April, widened simultaneously.
There stood Oknodie with an extremely displeased expression, her cheeks puffed full of air.
