# 35 - Agent of the Foundation

Jonna felt proud.

'You've done much better than expected, My Lady.'

Honestly, he hadn't expected this much from her.
At first, his expectations had been surpassed by disappointment.
Initially, he had harbored the irreverent thought that even the Boss's eye for talent wasn't always successful.
Various mental disorders and a small stature barely reaching his waist. Her face showed some promise for the future, but he couldn't imagine what such a girl could accomplish.
Was the Boss planning to assign her tasks that only a female could perform?
He had even entertained such cruel thoughts.

"Training? I'd love to!"

Oknodie overcame all his doubts and concerns with her abilities.
Even while barely getting proper sleep.
Even while unable to shake off the bad habits formed from living as a street vagrant.
Yet she persevered steadfastly.
She recognized the value of the opportunity given to her.
And she was grateful.
To the Boss who gave her the opportunity, and to him.
She called the Boss "Papa" and relied on him, and she depended on him as her protector, often trying to hug his legs.

'Whenever I look at My Lady, I'm reminded of a flying squirrel.'

She frequently hides and observes people from somewhere, but when she hears someone calling her, she comes running and throws herself into their arms.
She was definitely different from the many foolish scholarship candidates who rejected outstretched hands and threw themselves away.
She was on a different level even from the bottom-tier scholarship students who barely passed.

'That's why I'm more worried.'

A cute flying squirrel to the master she follows like a mother.
The moment she steps into the harsh wilderness, the flying squirrel loses its freedom.
From cats as fierce as tigers to all kinds of birds.
There are many predators that would eat a flying squirrel.
Oknodie is no different.
Though she may be stronger than ordinary scholarship students of the Foundation, she will still be labeled.
The world is wary of the Wiheomhae Foundation.
As much as the Foundation despises the world.
It will be a heavy burden for a young child.

"May I have a moment to speak with My Lady?"
"Go ahead."

With Minerva's permission, he was granted a deferment from group assignment.
A time as precious as gold.
Jonna opened his mouth carefully.

"Listen carefully, My Lady. I was taken in as a scholarship student by the Wiheomhae Foundation."
"Wow, really?"
"What do you know to react like that?"
"You seemed to want me to be surprised."
"Please listen seriously. You are standing at a critical crossroads that will determine your future life."
"??"
"The Foundation has provided you with a butler, maid, training facilities, and ample funds. This is not pure goodwill toward you."
"Then what is it?"
"They will demand compensation. Someday, when they think you can no longer climb higher, they will demand your devotion at the position the Foundation has determined for you."
"Huh. I see."

An innocently bright expression.
As expected, she doesn't understand her situation.

"However, if you reject the Foundation's sponsorship, you can be a free person."
"Me? Reject someone willing to vouch for me?"
"Please consider it seriously. The Academy has shown goodwill toward you, who entered with good grades, despite the large cost that must be repaid."

Examiner Minerva.
This was the document he had handed over when he came as a guarantor.

*Child to Academy Agreement
-Gift Academy will pay all debts owed by candidate Oknodie to the Wiheomhae Foundation.
-The Wiheomhae Foundation cannot exert any kind of influence over candidate Oknodie.
-If a Gift Academy examiner determines that the Wiheomhae Foundation has exerted influence, the Foundation can be forced to pay a corresponding price.

If My Lady Oknodie just signs this document, she can escape from the Foundation's embrace.

"The Academy will handle all costs and procedures and create a new identity for you."
"Jonna...?"
"You just need to take this pen and sign."

Please don't ask.
Please don't ask anything and just take the pen.
He prayed and prayed again.

"Then what happens to Jonna?"

...Indeed, gods are not to be trusted.
Gods don't listen to the prayers of the weak.

"As a member of the Foundation, my contact with you would also be considered an exercise of influence. So I would have to say goodbye to you. Forever."
"Then I don't want that! How could I abandon my butler? That's impossible!"

Is it because he was chosen by My Lady?
Or is it because she walks the same path as him?
Even knowing that My Lady's life was heading down an irreversible path, a deep sense of fulfillment welled up in his heart.

"You don't yet know the despair of having someone else determine your life. Even if you like something, even if you like someone, you must set those feelings aside."
"But I just need to keep climbing higher, right?"
"...Do you think you'll be different?"
"It's okay. I'm a player."
"A player...?"
"I'm Papa's daughter, aren't I? And the Foundation that helps me is ultimately Papa's Foundation, right?"
"...I suppose that could be said."
"Then it's fine."

It wasn't a decision made after deep contemplation.
It was a childish thought that someone in their formative years might have.

"It's natural for a child to help their Papa, isn't it? Taking over the family business might be cliché, but as long as I'm not forced to do just that for my whole life, I can help out."

She just wants to think of herself as special, to elevate herself as someone greater than others.

"I don't know what Papa and the Foundation want, but I'm strong. It won't be easy for them to demand something from me. Because I'll keep getting stronger!"
"...I see. Indeed, you are strong, My Lady."

That's why he wanted to protect her even more.
He tried to send her away to protect her.
He pretended to give in to the Academy's pressure.
He showed her an opportunity to leave the Foundation's embrace.
Yet if she wouldn't fly away.
If she refused to leave the open cage, then all he could do now was protect her.

"Then as of today, you will enter Gift Academy as a scholarship student of the Wiheomhae Foundation. I, Jonna Wiheomhae, as the Foundation's proxy representative, will be your guarantor. Do you agree to this?"
"Of course I agree! My butler is on my side, right?"

My Lady Oknodie pulled out a necklace she had kept hidden inside her clothes.
Attached to the necklace was the whistle he had given her, telling her to use it whenever she was in trouble.
She hadn't called him many times until now, but it was evident that she had cherished it.
How many times had she fiddled with it?
How long had she kept it close to her heart?
In her eyes, as she proudly held up the whistle he had given her at the inn, there was an almost burdensome level of trust.

"Nothing my butler suggests could go wrong. You always give the right advice. I don't like that you make me eat only healthy food and nag too much, though."
"You flatter me too much."
"So are we done with the boring stuff? I have so many stories to tell you. At the entrance exam with Mr. Gisele and Mr. Son O-cheon..."

Now she belongs to the Foundation.
Even that smile might someday belong not to her but to the organization.
If she fails to fulfill the Boss's wishes.
And thus, with an even higher probability, Oknodie's dreams will be crushed as well.
Despite knowing all this.
Despite knowing all that future, the human named Jonna Wiheomhae simply listens to My Lady's stories and quietly stays by her side.

-I heard rumors that you caused problems regarding the previous "My Lady's" disposal...
-That's enough.

Even Leape, an assassin from a different command chain, knew about the major incident he had caused.
If he repeats the same mistake, this time he will surely be disposed of by the organization.

'I cannot help you, My Lady. I'm telling you that if you and the organization part ways, I will become your enemy.'

Yet why does she trust him with all her heart like this?

-I see. You must have a "next My Lady."
-Which number was I?
-Can I be your last My Lady?

Don't recall it.
Don't be consumed by it.
The agent of the Foundation, My Lady's butler, is not allowed to falter.
The sticky feeling of blood under his feet.
The sensation of flesh being cut transmitted through his fingertips.
Remember the sight of a heart growing cold.

"...so anyway?"
"My Lady, I'll hear the rest of your story next time. It seems my allotted visiting time is up."
"Aww. You're leaving already?"
"I'm sure we'll meet again soon."

He had spent too much time.
After finishing the conversation and returning, Minerva asked.

"What about the contract?"
"She refused. As of today, My Lady is a scholarship student of the Foundation."
"I see. That's unfortunate for both parties, Guardian."
"Then may I assume that My Lady will be assigned to Group A?"
"Of course. Now, if you'll excuse me."

On the way out of the counseling room.
Oknodie asked.

"When will I see you again?"
"Guarantors or proxy representatives of guarantor institutions for Academy students can only visit the Academy once a month, on designated days. I will visit you when the time comes."
"I don't like that... I need to have my butler by my side every day. This is weird."
"It's the Academy's regulation, so there's nothing we can do."
"I hate this reality patch."

Jonna straightened the wrinkles in My Lady's clothes and left her with some words of advice.

"Please refrain from using such incomprehensible expressions in front of others. Students of high status or those who care about formality might look down on you or bully you."
"Don't worry. I've already made lots of friends. I've even become close with big sister Arcadia from the Kingdom of Florence."

The sole female representative of the Western Nobles Union.
Despite her high pride and calculating personality making her difficult to befriend, she had already established a personal relationship.
His surprise was momentary.
He realized it was natural given My Lady's charisma, which had enchanted even him into giving her his heart.
Yes, perhaps this My Lady would be alright.

"Then, until we meet again, please take care of yourself."
"You too, Jonna!"
