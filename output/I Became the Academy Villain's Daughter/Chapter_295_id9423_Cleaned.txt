# Enhanced Translation: Chapter 295 - The Frightening Child's Inner Thoughts (Or Not)

A professor at the Academy had been murdered.

Whether this was an unprecedented incident or not, it was certainly an extraordinary situation. It was serious enough that it could potentially trigger a full-blown conflict between the Foundation and the Academy.

The hero belatedly felt regret. Should I have just let him live?

"Oknodie..."
"Yes?"
"Y-you're not planning to kill us too, are you?"

When his blood had been boiling, he'd acted without thinking and made the professor suffer first, but now that the situation was over, he finally realized it might be his turn to be threatened next.

Hearing the hero's trembling voice, Oknodie's face bloomed with a bright smile.

"Of course not! The hero has so much to do in the future. You need to diligently kill regional bosses that appear during local events, kill the Demon King, kill the Emperor, and so much more!"
"What exactly do you think a hero is..."

There was no killing intent from Oknodie.
But what about the butler?
The Foundation's expert who had just made the greatest contribution to killing the professor turned to look at the hero and the saintess.

"Ishtar..."
"Relax. He won't kill us."

There was no intent to kill.
He realized this fact the moment their eyes met.

"I have no intention of harming Miss's friends. Please rest assured."

Jonna himself said so.
But his warning was just beginning...

"Just remember one thing: the moment must never come when you are no longer recognized as friends by our Miss."

If that time ever came, they might have to face that Jonna with their own strength—the monster-like expert who had taken down an Academy professor-level fighter head-on.

Even though the hero himself had helped, it was a battle that made him think Jonna might have had the upper hand even without anyone's intervention.

The world is vast, and there are many strong ones.
Not only did he need to save his companions, but the hero himself still had a long way to grow.

Above the uninhabited island that had become a mana vacuum due to continuous use of high-level magic, natural mana of all attributes gathered, creating a brilliant aurora of five colors.

A scene that would be revered as a natural wonder by ordinary villagers, but to skilled adventurers, it signaled that a terrifying clash of powers had taken place.

She would not stop training until such sights became familiar to her.
With today's scene etched in her heart.

"So when does today's auction start?"
"In 15 minutes."
"What?! You're still going to continue the auction after all this?"

For a brief moment, they had witnessed a battle between those far stronger than themselves.
They were mentally and physically exhausted from the tension, knowing they could have died if they had been caught up in it.

It would be natural to want to return to the cruise ship together and rest comfortably, but that Oknodie was acting as if nothing had happened, smiling without a care.

'How frightening. That mental fortitude.'

Was it not surprising for a Foundation child to be attacked by an assassin, even if the opponent was an Academy professor?

"Um, there's something that's been bothering me that I wanted to ask... Didn't the auction supervisor say they wouldn't intervene in any incidents that happen to participants during the auction?"
"The current saintess has a good memory. That's correct."
"But you just intervened. To protect Oknodie. If the auction supervisor didn't break the rules, then you could intervene because the auction was over, meaning the auction has already ended, right?"

Hearing his childhood friend's clever observation, Ishtar belatedly realized what Yupi had intended by claiming the auction was over.

'Yupi wanted to end this unreasonable auction quickly before our weaknesses came up for bidding.'

Jonna conceded readily.

"According to regulations, that's correct."
"Phew. Then we just need to wait for the ship to take us back."

But their opponent was the Foundation.
Jonna's subsequent response was enough to fill Yupi with dread.

"This time is different. I determined that I failed to provide Participant Oknodie's 'One-time Assassin Detection Service' adequately, so I upgraded it to a 'One-time Assassin Elimination Service.' Therefore, this procedure doesn't qualify as grounds for auction suspension."
"Assassin Detection Service upgraded to Assassin Elimination Service...?"

The veins on Yupi's hand bulged as he gripped his staff.
Ishtar noticed that his childhood friend's reaction was an attempt to hide his extreme tension.
It was no different from how he had behaved when facing Professor Rave just moments ago.
Jonna's threat had disappeared.
Yet Yupi still felt it.
The same level of fear as when facing Professor Rave.

"Calm down. We can't help it if the auction continues. Oknodie was just that thoroughly prepared."

Jonna further explained the process of upgrading to the Assassin Elimination Service.
The level of preparation—anticipating the attack and setting up defensive measures that even the Foundation's butler couldn't detect—was honestly impressive enough to acknowledge, even coming from an enemy.

"Ishtar... this isn't just about that."

Yupi had seen something beyond such honest impressions.
Unlike herself who was straightforward and simple, Yupi had the qualities of a clever advisor, and Ishtar had benefited from his insight many times before.
It was largely thanks to Yupi that her often unreasonable plans were carefully executed.
The initial confrontation with Professor Rave was the same.
Even when Ishtar did something as crazy as turning an Imperial professor into an enemy, Yupi followed without complaint.
He strengthened their defenses and bought time for the Foundation and Oknodie to join.
He also naturally guided the flow of battle so that the professor's countermeasures would temporarily seal them rather than kill them.
If someone like Yupi was this afraid, there must be a reason.
When Ishtar showed she was ready to listen, Yupi whispered quietly so only she could hear.

"That professor... Oknodie deliberately provoked him."
"What?"
"The incident that became the first point of conflict between you and Oknodie—when students were collectively trapped by ice trap magic in the abandoned school building. The person Oknodie provoked by causing that incident was that very professor."
"!!"
"Oknodie might have been planning from long ago to bring the hero and the Imperial professor to this place and create this situation."

Goosebumps rose on Ishtar's hand.

"Even so, that's going too far."

Yupi's words were generally correct.
Knowing this, she still wanted to deny them because the weight of his statement was too heavy.
Yet Yupi didn't take back his words.
He didn't laugh it off as a mere feeling.
Instead, he showed even greater fear, saying that now things finally made sense.

"The Foundation's butler said that Oknodie bought the 'One-time Assassin Detection Service,' which seemed like a bad product, because she already knew about the attack. Knowing about it means she essentially intended to kill him. You saw how advantageous the butler's abilities were on this uninhabited island."
"Metal attribute mana puzzle!"
"Oknodie who summoned the professor, the butler who prepared to eliminate him, and Oknodie's 'Papa' who set the stage—they're all in this together. The Foundation has been planning this big picture since the midterm exams."

It was vast.
The breadth of vision was on a completely different level from their own, who had been struggling just to keep up with the current events.
Not someone who follows the game, but someone who designs it.
The boldness to prepare and execute their own plans even within the Dragon Headmaster's Academy.
The actions of Oknodie, who carried out one axis of the Foundation leadership's brutal plan without anyone noticing until today, and the meaning behind it all.
Everything was too vast.
Almost unbelievably so for the inner thoughts of a child younger than herself.

'What a frightening child.'

She became afraid just imagining what kind of evil thoughts might be hidden behind Oknodie's smiling face.

"Don't forget. We've been caught up in the Foundation and Oknodie's plan too. And the fact that we were present when the Imperial professor died."
"What... does that mean?"
"It means many things. At least what I can understand right now is... if the Academy learns about today's events, they'll consider us potential allies of the Foundation. And the Foundation can use this fact to complicate our efforts to gain sponsors for the hero's party."

Despite such a terrifying scheme unfolding, the auction would continue in just ten minutes.
An auction where items that might be Ishtar's or Yupi's weaknesses could appear.
Their vague anxiety soon materialized in the upcoming auction.

"I will now reveal the sixth item of the uninhabited island auction. Today's item is 'Sponsorship of the Hero's Party.'"

The difficult future Yupi had predicted became reality in just ten minutes.
