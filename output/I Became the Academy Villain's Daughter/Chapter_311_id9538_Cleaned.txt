# 311 - The Chairman's Banquet

There are things you cannot see until they are pointed out.

The Chairman's mansion, which had seemed like a fun training theme park to everyone, was actually no different from a military training facility.

The cheerful mood vanished like a lie.

No anticipation or excitement remained.

The students' imaginations ran wild.

Children brought to an island isolated from the outside world at a young age.

Forced to survive for set periods on each floor that grew increasingly dangerous over time.

Children lacking skill and talent died.

The surviving children barely clung to life, but only briefly.

As survival days increased, the time spent on the next deployment grew longer and longer.

Only after the number of children who survived the harsh training dwindled to single digits did the training end, and the remaining ones were selected as foundation scholarship students, or as maids and butler candidates.

This place might be where such cruel things actually happened.

"Oknodie, you said this was your first time here, right?"

"Yep!"

Then there must be facilities even worse than this somewhere in the world.

Everyone could silently guess the words Isabel couldn't bring herself to say.

Poor thing.

"Oknodie... want me to turn on some mood-lifting lights?"

"Hey. Want to try shooting a gun?"

"Merchants feel better when handling money pouches. I'll lend you a gold coin pouch if you'd like."

Of course, the recipient of this inexplicable kindness was simply overjoyed and accepted everything with pleasure.

Those who had only seen the bleak training facilities were finally freed from them.

Yet the students' expressions had grown dark and serious.

Where the place was didn't matter.

What mattered was what awaited them there.

As they thought about the facility's horrors, their thoughts eventually reached the person who had created all these installations.

Oknodie's Papa.

The Foundation's Chairman.

What kind of being was he?

How dangerous was he?

Was meeting such a person truly a wise idea?

No matter how they thought about it, it only felt dangerous.

"I feel like I'm walking to the gallows of my own accord."

At Zuang's complaint, Son O-cheon hesitated at the dining room door with an expression that looked ready to get indigestion.

Even the butler guide, who they expected would mock such behavior, checked with a thoroughly tense air whether his clothes were wrinkled or if there were any mistakes before knocking.

"I have brought scholarship student Oknodie and her friends to the dining room."

"Tell them to enter."

The voice that came through was unexpectedly fresh and clear—a young man's voice.

A long banquet table.

Place settings laid before each seat.

Gisele's narrow eyes quickly began extracting information about the Chairman.

Though shaped like a luxury suit, the clothes wrapped around his body were a type of magical armor with such tremendous magical power that it was dazzling—overlapping mana reactions too numerous to fully detect.

Such monstrously specced clothing could never be off-the-rack unless it was the work of the imperial court's exclusive tailor and blacksmith.

The chestnut-colored hair and neatly trimmed eyebrows indicated the presence of servants who assisted with grooming.

If he could procure enough food to fill a banquet table on this remote island, he must have an exclusive chef too.

He employed tailors, hairdressers, and chefs—the top three professions assassins most liked to target.

The probability this was the real person, not a proxy, increased further.

Above all, the smile that hadn't left his face once since his appearance testified to his composure.

'That's not a smile for kindness.'

Animals smile when they see prey.

This man's smile was no different from an animal's.

Even if not viewing them as physical prey, he was smiling in anticipation of satisfying some sadistic desire, some mental need.

"It's a pleasure to meet you all. My name is Jeil Wiheomhae. Though not comparable to the great families or backing forces of those of you doing great things in society, I modestly run a small foundation—I am the founder and chairman of the Wiheomhae Foundation."

Because he possessed tremendous power, he didn't flaunt but lowered himself.

Rather than letting his guard down, he was the type who made others let theirs down.

The sort who handled power in an extremely insidious manner.

"I imagine you have much to say and ask, but first, you must be tired from the long journey. Let's fill our bellies first."

Chefs brought out freshly cooked dishes in a steady stream through the maids.

Butlers brought wine bottles sloshing with liquid, submerged in ice water.

Hot dishes and cold wine.

A place made all the more unsettling and eerie by the contrast between the Chairman who maintained his smile and the servants who watched every move with extreme tension.

A banquet began where only the Chairman and Oknodie smiled warmly.

Even a single small roasted pig had days of aging, seasoning using rare ingredients, taste and aroma filling the interior, and pleasure enveloping the mouth as basics.

Though there were many types, no dish was carelessly made, and all delighted those who ate them.

"Damn. What is this, the Last Supper? It's disgustingly delicious."

"Idiot. Don't talk with your mouth full."

"Ugh."

At Son O-cheon's tactless remark, Isabel firmly stepped on his foot from beside him.

The Chairman laughed heartily and spoke with feigned magnanimity.

"Don't worry. When sending off death row inmates, I prefer having them taste life's bitter lessons rather than sweet dishes."

"Meaning if you'd planned to kill us, you'd have treated us to a full course of torture."

"Hahaha."

Even at Zuang's sarcasm that it sounded that way, the Chairman smiled warmly.

Several people felt chills at how he never corrected that Zuang's words were wrong, and glanced around nervously before quietly setting down the food they'd been bringing to their mouths.

"Does everyone have no appetite?"

Oknodie wasn't swept up in that atmosphere.

With a face that looked happy enough to die, the hand holding the plate never stopped.

She bit off large chunks of meat with impressive skill, inhaling the flesh in one gulp leaving only bones.

Even for fish dishes with many bones, she used Dark Mana to raise only the bones, then gobbled up just the flesh.

A talent for gluttony that went beyond hearty to nearly miraculous!

'Certainly, the strong are different somehow. To calmly empty a bowl in this suffocating atmosphere.'

Gisele felt oddly competitive and picked up the utensils he'd set down.

Watching Oknodie's eating habit of never leaving food once she started a meal had surely played a part in his decision.

Perhaps mustering that courage could be called lucky.

"It seems you all find receiving unconditional goodwill burdensome. Well, the Academy dining hall takes points, and the outside world takes currency, so eating free meals based solely on being my daughter's friend might go against social norms. Then let's do this."

The Chairman pulled out a mana board.

"The boarding points modeled after the Academy's points are worthless to me. But they certainly have a clear purpose. Through what preferences my invited guests have and how they collect points, I gather information about a person's survival methods."

Familiar faces appeared on the mana board.

Oknodie. Ishtar. Irene.

All faces of students who had boarded the cruise ship.

"Miss Irene's meal frequency is twice daily. Her distinctly Northern survival-oriented eating habits are notable—small portions, and even if tasteless, guaranteed high calories."

"!"

"Her main point income source is hostage exchange. Like a war mage accustomed to battlefields, she favors military methods. A splendid life philosophy showing maturity beyond her years."

The time spent on the ship had revealed her life.

As undisguisable unease rose on Irene's face, another name was called.

"Miss Hestia's meal frequency is five times daily. Regardless of type, she ate large quantities whenever opportunity arose, showing a wild eating habit different from the Northern Grand Duchess."

"..."

"Her main point income source is record achievement at training facilities and victories in duels with crew members she's confident against. A splendid mercenary's life philosophy of making victory over others in competition her life's work."

Though unspoken, it was clear that others present at this gathering, and perhaps even the remaining students who weren't present, had all had their information extracted and organized this way.

Gift Academy teachers extracted and evaluated student information to assign grades and determine advancement, progression, and graduation.

Then what was the purpose of the Wiheomhae Foundation's Chairman extracting and evaluating information about his daughter's friends?

"As you can see, I'm extremely interested in my daughter's friendships. So until the day you leave the mansion, those who wish to eat, please tell me your stories. Trivial or precious, either is fine. I'll give you food in return commensurate with your tale. Of course, stories related to my child would be even better."

On the surface, it was the meddling of a handsome young parent who seemed extremely interested in their child.

But when trying to fathom the true intent, the inner meaning, it was a statement that only gave chillingly eerie feelings.
