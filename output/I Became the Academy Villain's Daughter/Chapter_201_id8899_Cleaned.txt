# 201 - The Reason for Sending Ahead

Oknodie was preventing students who weren't friendly with her from crossing the river.
This suspicion soon proved true.

"Oh, you're all such lazybones! Why are you still here instead of crossing over?"

The group of five—Hestia, Jigoku, Lotto, Dorothy, and Rockfell—were trying to cross the swirling river surface by dipping their bodies in the water.
As soon as they stepped in, Oknodie's cheerful voice rang out from somewhere, as if she had detected their presence.

"Oknodie?"
"Where are you speaking from?"
"From across the river!"

Her voice sounded so vivid, as if she were having a conversation right next to them. By now, they didn't even have the energy to be annoyed.

"Gahaha! That's amazing. What a talent."
"Oknodie. I need to talk to you for a moment. Can you listen?"

Unlike Jigoku who was genuinely impressed, Hestia addressed her seriously.

"If you're trying to stop me, don't bother. I have absolutely no intention of forgiving those who've only chosen to do bad things!"
"I heard you laid magical traps on the riverbed so no one could cross."
"I told you it's useless!"
"Could you let just us pass through?"

Instead of scolding her for behaving badly, Hestia prioritized passing the test by latching onto Oknodie's power.
Dorothy, watching from behind, was somewhat disappointed.
Hestia often showed signs of caring for Oknodie, resembling Isabel.
She had unconsciously assumed that Hestia, like Isabel, would try to guide Oknodie onto the right path.
Of course, forcing others to do what you can't do yourself is something only a self-righteous person would do.
Dorothy herself equally wanted to pass the test by using her connection with Oknodie if possible.
After all, wasn't the Imperial faction doing the same thing—blocking paths and attempting to control passage whenever possible, while letting their own people through or helping with the control?

"No problem!"

Naturally, Oknodie permitted them to pass.

"Wait. I don't remember giving permission to you people."
"Are you really going to be this petty among fellow upper-class students?"
"There were many opportunities to live peacefully together as students at the Academy. During the past two months, you squandered those opportunities."

Oknodie warned Honner Fried Chicken, who was trying to get a free ride, in a cold voice.

"You're free to follow if you want. That's your choice. But I wouldn't expect my abilities to help you too."

While those friendly with Oknodie dove under the surface and passed through easily, the Imperial faction students remained stuck.
Honner Fried Chicken regretted trying to control the situation, but seeing the group of second-year students approaching from beyond the stairs, he realized there was no time for regret.

"What is the hero doing? She broke through the first section just fine!"
"She should have crossed by now."
"Has anyone seen the hero?"
"Not here."
"Wait. If the hero isn't here, doesn't that mean she's already crossed the river? After all, it was the hero who broke through the magical traps Oknodie set up to block the path."
"So what? The fact that she's not visible now only means the hero prioritized her own grades and is driving her mount toward the finish line."

Unlike Honner, who deliberately chose to do malicious things to torment others and confirm his authority, Cheddar Potato Pizza, who responded to his words, had strong self-love.
He had enough self-confidence that he didn't need to push others down to reaffirm his position, and thus didn't care about relationships with others.
For someone with such individualistic tendencies, the hero's direct path to the finish line was reasonable and not particularly a betrayal.

"If you need someone to rely on, find someone else."
"Tsk. In the end, only the Second Imperial Princess remains."

Now their only hope was the Second Imperial Princess Mesugakki, who possessed both military and political power, supported by the Empire's three great noble families.
They didn't want to appear to be dragged along by imperial authority, but enduring a moment of humiliation was better than receiving terrible grades in the mandatory midterm course.
Of course, this only applied if the princess was willing to take them in.

"Huh? Take care of your own grades. So~ pathetic."
"Hasn't Your Highness always set an example by looking after your fellow students' assignments?"
"Assignments and exams aren't the same thing. Don't you even know that? How stupid~"
"...The more of us who pass, the more hands and feet Your Highness will have to attend to your convenience. We will serve you more actively than ever before."
"Kik kik. Are you planning to combine hands and feet to have more than ten? That many would be gross. If you're going to be my hands and feet, you should know how to follow properly♡"

The Mesugakki Imperial Princess jumped into the river with a mocking laugh.
It would be nice if she drowned.
Contrary to Honner and Cheddar's curse, the Second Imperial Princess Mesugakki popped her head out on the opposite side of the river.
Fraud.
Conspiracy.
The Imperial students' worries only deepened as they faced Oknodie's trial, which those who used underhanded methods couldn't pass.


* *


Yupi asked her close friend:

"Are you sure this is okay? Leaving Oknodie like this."
"You heard her. She promised with her own mouth."
"I'm asking because it's so suspicious."

Ishtar and Yupi had gone all the way to the river, determined to settle things with Oknodie.
The two, prepared for a decisive battle, were deflated when Oknodie willingly opened the path.

"Please go ahead."
"You're willingly giving up the lead position?"
"I have something to do here!"

This child is hiding something again.

"What if I say I'll defeat you here and pass through?"
"You'll regret it! The others who've followed right behind you will head straight to the goal line, happy for the opportunity."

In response to Oknodie's words, a reply came from behind the stairs, from the rear of where Ishtar and Yupi stood.

"If that's your strategy, I'll play along."

Arcadia, the Northern Grand Duchess, with a voice that made the surrounding air drop several degrees even without being frosty.
If only she had appeared alone being cooperative with Oknodie, they might have tried to overpower her, but several others followed behind her.
Casia from Group C, carrying crackling electricity.
The rest of Group C trailing behind her.
If Oknodie kept them tied up while Casia and the others passed through in a line, the first through sixth places would be determined.
For Ishtar, who wanted to achieve the sole top rank among first-years beyond the joint top rank in the entrance exam and recruit promising talents, giving up the first place was a huge burden.

The hero isn't all that great.
We could do it too.

What if such opinions started to form?
Of course, she knew.
In terms of skill, only Oknodie could compare to her, the hero.
But even flimsy rumors could tarnish her reputation and name value.
The Empire's cunning nobles wouldn't miss this opportunity to reduce their support for her and manipulate her for their family's convenience.
The thought of having to deal with all that political strife and annoyance just because she couldn't get first place in a midterm exam made Ishtar feel immensely tired.

"The place to settle things with you doesn't necessarily have to be here."
"Right?"
"But I can't just pass by after seeing such an ominous power that controls monsters. You've provoked my duty as a hero."
"Then how about making a light contract?"
"Contract?"
"Saint Yupi, your companion, can establish a credible verbal contract by receiving divine certification and declaring a divine word."
"What kind of certification are you asking for?"
"I won't attempt the riding section until 100 people have passed through here. In return, you should go pass the finish line now. And don't come back to harm me."
"...A contract without restrictions is meaningless. What's the penalty for breaking the promise?"

In response to Yupi's question, Oknodie cheerfully exclaimed as if she had thought about it from the beginning:

"The saint who serves the Golgotha of Beheading has an extremely powerful 'Severance' function, right? If I break the rules, I'll gladly take 10 times the 'Severance' damage from Saint Yupi! In return, if you and the hero break the promise, you'll also take 10 times the 'Severance' damage from me."
"!!"
"Why are you setting such conditions? What benefit do you gain by sending us ahead and staying here to send 100 people to the third section first?"
"Hmm, I don't think you need to know that!"
"What?"
"I'm saying I won't tell you. If you were in my position, would you spill all your thoughts and desires to someone who dislikes you?"

Yupi glared at Oknodie and said:

"It's not worth hearing. We can just ignore such a suspicious proposal."
"...Let's accept it, Yupi."
"Ishtar!"
"I know too. That Oknodie has some suspicious scheme. But fighting here won't settle anything, and we'll only lose out on our grades."

The side that breaks the contract takes 10 times the damage.
Taking the firepower of a critical hit in a normal strike.
In a battle between powerful individuals, it's safe to assume that no one would violate the contract while shouldering such a risk.
Oknodie had shown a strong will to fulfill the contract.
In the end, Ishtar and Yupi concluded the contract and began the third section's riding test.
...More precisely, they wanted to start and were eager to begin.
But they couldn't.

"Instructor, where are our mounts?"
"My mount is a stag with beautiful antlers."

At the test site's mount storage.
The instructor replied indifferently to the two who were bewildered by the empty storage:

"I don't know."
"...Pardon?"
"They're somewhere in the forest."

The instructor pointed to the forest beyond where they needed to ride and said nonchalantly:

"Completion of the riding section is measured by riding a mount from the starting line to the finish line. If you can't find your mount, your record won't be recognized."
"Why aren't the mounts in storage but scattered somewhere in the forest?"
"Because the person who came first destroyed all our pens."

So that was it.
She made the contract with divine certification to buy time and do what she wanted.
This bad child.
Cunning little one.
Such an evil Dark Princess!
Ishtar and Yupi realized they had been thoroughly tricked by Oknodie's scheme and stomped their feet in frustration.
