# <108 - How Good Is This?>

Dragons are commonly regarded as the wisest creatures in the world.
With lifespans reaching thousands of years.
Transcendent power and intelligence.
The ability to freely create or destroy entire nations if they so desire.
Few beings could compare to the wisdom of dragons except for immortal gods or demons who challenge the gods.
When such a dragon uses magic to find a hidden presence, no secret can escape detection.

<The Eye of Entelecheia That Observes All Things>
<Conatus-based Complete Reinterpretation of Reality>

Dazzling particles of light flood the lecture hall, scanning the entire space and dismantling all kinds of hidden, distorted, and perception-deceiving structures, imprinting them in the principal's mind.
In the face of such harshly advanced magic, Oknodie hiding in Gisele's cloak was instantly discovered.
As a bonus, even the mana spore sac being digested in her stomach was detected!

"This little mouse. You'll eat anything with mana in it, whether it's a stone or a spore sac!"

How was I caught?
Surely he won't make me spit out what I've already eaten, right?
...Though I have a feeling he might actually do that?
Oknodie's gaze was filled with such wariness.
Reading her impudent thoughts, the dragon principal was even more flabbergasted.
That ill-mannered child from the Foundation showed not even a speck of respect toward an educator.

"Do you think that mouse-tail sized bit of mana would even register in this body of mine? I have no intention of extracting it, so stop looking at me with those insolent eyes."

Students sometimes make mistaken assumptions.
That the Gift Academy is a magical realm beyond humanity and the principal is some sort of disguised demon lord.
What started as a money-making scheme has somehow continued for 981 years, but unlike the early days, he now genuinely dedicates himself to teaching.
Having his capabilities as an educator doubted is unpleasant, even if it's due to the ignorant prejudice of insignificant beings.

"Today I shall make you look at me with respect in your eyes."
"I will show you the most excellent way to prepare mushroom dishes in the world!"

With a gesture from the dragon principal, several Red-Toothed Mushrooms floated into the air.

Squawk!

The mushrooms were killed instantly with a simple neck twist.
The spores, which are the most problematic aspect when dealing with mushroom monsters, weren't even released.

"The first step in cooking mushroom monsters is to coat them with magical energy while they're completely subdued."
"If you seal them so not a single spore can escape, you can cleanly dispatch them by twisting their necks or inserting a knife."

The students' expressions crumbled unpleasantly.
Either because they were disgusted or because they lost confidence from the first step.

"Different mushroom monsters require different cooking methods depending on their color and shape."
"Yellow-Toothed Mushrooms are best for savory broths, while Blue-Toothed Mushrooms should be added last to stews and stir-fries to preserve their texture."
"Green-Toothed Mushrooms with their good texture are often used as meat substitutes or for frying."
"The grand Red-Toothed Mushroom is a nutritious and texturally rich ingredient used for main dishes. Today I'll make Red-Toothed Mushroom Stew and Red-Toothed Mushroom Soup."

As the mushrooms began to emit a delicious aroma similar to grilling meat, the students started to salivate.

"Are you all crazy? Those are monsters."
"Right. And they're those notorious mushroom monsters."

Average adventurers could never cook these, and one shouldn't dare to treat them as ingredients without a mushroom monster cooking license. It was dangerous cuisine. As infamous as Red-Toothed Mushrooms were, the wariness toward the principal's cooking increased significantly.

"There, it's complete."

The principal's cooking skills were truly impressive.
His ability to produce various cooking utensils from thin air was amazing, and his skill in controlling water and fire through magic was impressive.
However, the students were reluctant to eat the resulting dishes themselves.

"Now, I'll allow the first volunteer to taste it."
"Why is no one coming forward? Are you doubting this principal's cooking skills?"

The students' faces had already fallen.
So this was why the principal was cooking.
No wonder the malicious principal seemed unusually docile today; he was setting them up for this dangerous lecture assignment.
Their thoughts, which were all being read, were all along the same lines.

'If I eat that and get infected by spores, he'll criticize me for not trying hard enough to eat without getting infected.'
'As experienced second-year students, we might get points deducted for being foolish enough to eat suspicious food when told to.'
'It's food made by that principal. Who knows what trap is hidden in it.'

As his irritation grew at the students' suspicious thoughts...

"Then I'll eat it!"

The fearless little mouse.
The ill-mannered child from the Foundation.
Oknodie stepped forward with a bright face.


* * *


"Oknodie! How could you volunteer to eat such dangerous food? I'll cook for you as much as you want later, so please apologize to the principal!"
"Please listen to Isabel. No matter how much you wanted to try new food, that's too reckless."
"Listen to the adults, little mouse."

As soon as she expressed her willingness to taste the food, Isabel, Gisele, and Son O-cheon—her first companions in this other world—immediately tried to dissuade her.

"It's okay. I won't die from eating that!"
"You might not die, but you'll be parasitized by spores."
"Even if you receive treatment, if even a tiny spore remains, you'll need long-term intensive care."
"Listen to the smart ones."

Their concern was understandable, but it was needless worry.
The dragon principal didn't seem like someone who would cook poorly.
That principal was surprisingly serious about cooking.
Sorry to the mushrooms, but mushroom dishes were delicious.
Above all, opportunities to collect mushroom dishes for the cookbook were rare, given how difficult they were to prepare.

"I'm really fine. It's not the first time I've eaten dangerous food!"

Later on, I'll need to build up resistance by eating 1,000 types of poison dishes to drink Hydra Poison Soup, so a mushroom dish that won't cause problems is nothing!
She meant to reassure them, but for some reason, the students started sobbing.

"Why is she so pitiful?"
"Is food so scarce in the borderlands that they eat such harmful products...?"
"Oh, it's a misunderstanding! Even in our borderlands, cases this severe are uncommon. Unless there's a great famine or you're extremely poor and can't find food..."
"You don't necessarily have to be extremely poor."

Zuang remarked casually.

"Assassins typically consume small amounts of poison or harmful foods from a young age to build immunity. Oknodie's school of training must be particularly harsh."

Tears welled up in the students' eyes and rolled down their cheeks.

"Waaah! Poor Oknodie!"
"...I feel somewhat sorry now. Even in our maritime nation of Florence, we've only thought of assassins as unpleasant people, but to think they grew up enduring such hardships..."
"At least she won't get arteriosclerosis."

All eyes turned to the student who made this atmosphere-breaking comment.
The student was even more flustered by the attention.

"What? Red-Toothed Mushrooms contain ergosterol, which is good for preventing arteriosclerosis. You need to consume an appropriate amount to keep your blood vessels healthy."

Horner Fried Chicken, as a member of one of the Empire's three great meritorious families, had accumulated extensive knowledge about mana refinement methods for maintaining a healthy body.
The knowledge accumulated by the Fried Chicken family for generations helped them take meticulous care of vascular health.
Red-Toothed Mushrooms could be used as medicine in small quantities.
As physical training advances to higher levels, the process of 'refinement' becomes important.
In the process of improving a polluted and diseased body to a healthy state, using various rare ingredients for health maintenance naturally led to the accumulation of such miscellaneous knowledge.
These accumulated differences in miscellaneous knowledge became family secrets.
Horner Fried Chicken had inadvertently revealed one of his family's secrets.
However, there weren't many who would appreciate or use this information.

"Do Imperial people have no blood or tears? How can you say such things in this atmosphere?"
"Don't assume all nobles are like that. The Fried Chicken family tends to be that way. It has nothing to do with our Iron Plate Charcoal Galbi family."

To those who didn't properly understand the medicinal effects of Red-Toothed Mushrooms, it seemed like a ruthless comment from someone who didn't understand the plight of the poor borderland assassin.
A new rumor quickly spread among the upper-class students.

The heartless Fried Chicken family who don't understand human emotions!
The Fried Chicken family are psychopaths who raise chicks by hand, then cut their throats, remove feathers and entrails, and fry them in oil when they become chickens!
It was an unfair reputation for the person concerned, but it was all the same to me.
