# 335 - Pre-Exam Experience

The first-year students had been increasingly troubled lately.

"Haven't the lectures become too difficult?"
"Yeah. And suddenly there's an insane amount of assignments."
"It's hard enough just keeping up with the lessons..."

The lectures had become insanely difficult!
During the first semester, they could always create leisure time if they put their minds to it.
Unlike the first semester when they had time to check out clubs, wander around looking for food, and do whatever they wanted, from the second week of the second semester, all lectures became generally more difficult.

"Until now, they've been going easy on us because we were baby first-years, but now that the first semester is over, the professors are finally showing their true colors."
"How can a person eat, attend lectures, do assignments, prepare lecture materials, and work on group projects in just 14 hours? This is a nightmare!"
"I want to go home. Back in my hometown, I could train at a leisurely pace for 5 hours a day, spend two or three hours helping with the estate, and spend the rest of the time doing whatever I wanted!"

Dorothy cursed inwardly.
Those dirty talented bastards.
They complained, but in reality, they had plenty of time to spare.

'Even commoners have to consistently put in 14 hours a day just to make a living.'

How many commoners lived lives where they woke up at 6 AM when the sun rose and returned home at 9 PM when the moon was out?
Rather, Dorothy found it chilling that there were so many students talented enough to enter the Academy after training for just 5 hours.
If they consistently followed the curriculum demanded by the Academy, they seemed highly likely to become even more outstanding talents than herself, who had advanced to the upper class.

"I should take a class with Oknodie too."

Dorothy's eyes were drawn to one particular lecture on Oknodie's schedule.

━━━
Saturday, 1st period, 9 AM-1 PM.
Professor Weird's "Wrath of Nature" lecture.
Magic Department elective, 3 credits.
━━━

It was a new lecture by Professor Weird, who had already helped her in the first semester with "Basics and Understanding of Mana Usage."

"...You'd better not."
"Hestia?"

Hestia, who had overheard her muttering, advised her to reconsider with an extremely tired face.

"Listen to what happens when you take the same class as Oknodie..."

Hestia told her a story that was sufficient to change Dorothy's mind.


* * *


Hestia was honestly glad that Oknodie had added many lectures.
Thanks to that, Oknodie had registered for a Knight Department elective, and Hestia had the opportunity to take the same class.

"Gentlemen. Welcome to the battlefield."

━━━
Wednesday, 3rd period, 2 PM-6 PM.
Knight Department Professor Rare Grills' 3-credit elective.
"Surviving as a Commander on the Battlefield."
━━━

"Hmm hmm."
"Oh, Hestia! What brings you here?"
"Just. Somehow I wanted to take this."
"That's strange. Hestia doesn't usually become a commander. Has a mercenary captain route opened up?"
"Route?"
"Nothing!"

Oknodie's odd comment made Hestia's heart flutter, wondering if it meant she was a gallant and reliable woman who could lead a mercenary group.
Unlike her silly thoughts, Professor Rare Grills did not allow students to chat.

"━FOCUS!!!"
"Ugh."
"Gah."
"My, my ears..."

Ziiing.
The professor's voice was so loud it caused ringing in their ears, startling not only Hestia and Oknodie but other students as well.

"This lecture has been prepared to increase the survival rate of those of you who will be appointed as battlefield commanders by nobles in numerous countries or who will command small units participating in battle. Carelessly joking around and distracting others is prohibited."

The second semester was definitely different from the first.
The professor's strict personality, the seriousness of the lecture content, and the students' compliant attitudes.
All of these were elements that would not have been easily accepted in the first semester.

'If this were the first semester, there would have been students who would have talked back, saying "Who do you think you are to speak loudly to a noble heir?" and gotten beaten up.'

Of course, even now in the second semester, no student would commit such a crazy act at the Academy, where even seniors far above them behaved properly in front of professors.

"Since there are students attending this lecture for the first time today, let's do a brief review. In today's wars, victory and defeat are determined by the deployment of high-tier forces. Therefore, the role of large-scale forces is to blockade, lure, or protect high-tier forces. Commanders help with these operations."

This was something Hestia already knew.
Large-scale wars are divided into extremes.
Cold wars where both sides amass forces and try to conceal the positions of their high-tier forces.
Chaotic battles where they try to find and kill the opponent's high-tier forces, spilling blood across the entire battlefield.
There are battlefields where not a drop of blood is shed, but there are also battlefields where once blood starts flowing, it leads to the annihilation of entire units.

"Therefore, if you are a commander, the matter you should always prioritize is this: Never, under any circumstances, set foot on a battlefield where the enemy's high-tier forces outnumber your own. If you have already entered, be sure to evacuate from places the enemy high-tier forces might target."

Was it jealousy because Oknodie was choosing additional lectures?
Due to many students protesting, the additional registration period was extended, and another student who came to attend the new lecture raised his hand.

"War is waged to win, but if we're so scared and just keep running away, won't we fail to win wars we could win and lose even more badly in wars we're going to lose?"
"The questioner should state his name first."
"I apologize. 981st batch, 1st year, Jaidas."

Jaidas!
Hestia's arms tensed up.
A famous holy knight from the Western Region known for the Western Guild Alliance.
One of the "New Three Powers of the 981st Batch" along with Irene and Hector, and according to rumors, he possessed extraordinary capabilities comparable to Northern Grand Duchess Irene.
Despite both being mercenary backgrounds, the popularity of Hestia, a Berserker class, and Jaidas, a Holy Knight class, was worlds apart.
For Hestia, who secretly harbored an inferiority complex toward Jaidas, this was an exciting opportunity.
A chance to defeat Jaidas with her own hands!
However, Jaidas only glanced at Oknodie, not Hestia.
As if Hestia didn't even exist.
But just as Jaidas seemed to ignore Hestia, there was someone who looked at him as if he were a neighborhood dog.

"What nonsense from a foolish freshman!"
"...!"
"A holy knight is a follower of God. Do you not even realize that the battlefields you've experienced were comfortable playgrounds provided because they feared the name of God?"

Oknodie nodded beside her.

"True masters do it raw without faith!"
"At least the top student of the year has the right mindset."

Jaidas trembled with anger.

"The fact that you are strong, Professor, does not invalidate my experience."
"Are you uncomfortable being evaluated by me, Rare Grills, just because you've gained some fame in the three Western countries? You don't even know your place!"

The professor burst into laughter and then flashed a chilling gaze from under his helmet.

"Originally, you would only experience this in practice during the midterm and final exams, but today I'll make a special exception and move it up. Everyone, pay attention. This is the trial you will all face on exam day."

At Professor Rare Grills' gesture, teaching assistants in armor set up several large mana boards with grim expressions.
What appeared on the largest central mana board was a three-dimensional tactical map.
Military formations and scales of various symbols and sizes appeared throughout the map.

"Is there a challenger who wants to compete with Jaidas?"
"I will step forward."

Hestia volunteered without hesitation.

"Hmm, I want to try too!"

With Oknodie joining, making three participants, the professor nodded.

"Let me explain the test method for 'Surviving as a Commander on the Battlefield.' From now on, you can input commands for your units on the tactical map."
"You are free to decide what actions to take with your units each turn, and the test ends when the commander is killed. The students who die last or survive until the final turn will be the winners of the test."
"Also, the blue army will be commanded by my faithful assistant Gracio, and the enemy army will be commanded by me."

Jaidas looked at the tactical map with suspicious eyes.

"If everyone's position is shared on the map, can't we just run away from the enemy to survive?"
"The explanation of the rules is over. Individual turn actions will be controlled through your personal mana boards, and only the results will be displayed on the central main mana board."
"How are the unit size and initial starting positions determined?"
"Normally, before the test begins, you could choose options such as position selection priority, scale expansion, or recruiting skilled personnel with points earned from midterm assignments or additional points, but since no one has earned any points for this test, unit selection will be done in order of first semester rankings."

Jaidas made a sour face.
He felt that the professor was deliberately trying to make things difficult for him.
If units were chosen by ranking, the upper-class students Oknodie and Hestia would choose better units first, and he would start at the greatest disadvantage.

━━━
Battlefield Briefing
Enemy invasion forces have been detected in the border region.
The Border Guard must cooperate with the Holy Knight Order and the vanguard forces to defend the fortress.
All units that survive for 30 turns until reinforcements arrive will receive a survival bonus.
━━━
Unit Selection List
Falcon Cavalry (300 light cavalry, 30 mounted archers), Front Line Risk Level 3
Regular Infantry Unit (1,500 spearmen, 1,500 shield bearers), Front Line Risk Level 3
...
Border Guard (1,400 elite garrison troops), Front Line Risk Level 5
Peasant Conscript Unit (2,200 peasant soldiers), Front Line Risk Level 6
...
Otos Penal Unit (1,200 prisoner infantry), Front Line Risk Level 8
Thunderbolt Heaven Divine Church Holy Knight Order (10 holy knights, 50 apprentice holy knights), Front Line Risk Level 8
━━━

Jaidas had already marked two options.

'The safest places are the Border Guard, which strengthens defenses along the walls, and the Falcon Cavalry, which is prepared to ravage enemy territory. These two.'

Other places, even with larger numbers of soldiers, would either be used as arrow fodder in the upcoming war or easily become targets in the main engagement.
Among them, he had to find the next best option.
After all, Oknodie and Hestia wouldn't leave those two for him.

"Next. It's the third person, Jaidas's turn to choose his unit."

Looking at the remaining units, Jaidas was greatly surprised by the unexpected list.

"What's this? Did they make a mistake?"

The Falcon Cavalry had been chosen by Hestia.
However, Oknodie had not chosen the Border Guard but the most dangerous Penal Unit.

"It's a service! We should be kind to newbies!"
"...I don't understand what you mean, but I can tell you're looking down on me. I'll show both you and the professor."

Jaidas chose the Border Guard without hesitation.
And so began the three students' second semester midterm pre-experience.
