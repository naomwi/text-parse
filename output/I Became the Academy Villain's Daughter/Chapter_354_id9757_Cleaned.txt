# 354 - The Principal's Deception

"Instructor Russo committed the crime of injuring a first-year student, which normally would warrant removal from his teaching assistant position and at least six months in the Grand Prison. However, considering that the victim was Oknodie, a first-year with abilities beyond her year, and that Russo was one-sidedly beaten, I hereby declare him not guilty."

Professor Mahabharata, also known as the Dragon Principal's final mercy, concluded today's trial by saving yet another instructor.

"You did it. I never thought I'd feel grateful to Oknodie."
"I agree. Though things turned out opposite to what we expected, it's remarkable that you weren't imprisoned for kicking a first-year student."
"What will you do now?"

Camella's meaning was clear without asking.
Russo himself had been thinking the same thing.

'If I wanted to... I could possibly advance to third year now. It might be difficult during the current semester, but definitely possible next year.'

Camella's brilliant mind, which had secured the labor of countless students by exploiting their hearts through pet contracts, must have reached the same conclusion.
Despite her pretense of indifference, Russo had already noticed her hands clutching her skirt behind her back.

"Do you think I'd betray you now that I have the ability to advance to third year, unlike during my impoverished days?"
"...Don't mock me. If you're going to leave, just go. I have no intention of begging someone whose heart has already left."
"Of course I'm not leaving. Did you think I'd run off with the penalty fee like it was pocket change? If so, I'm rather disappointed."

Russo gently took Camella's arm.

"The trust that didn't break even against Oknodie won't break overnight."
"Instructor Russo..."
"Rather, I should be asking you. You must be aware of the attention now focused on Camella's faction after such a major incident? If you continue exploiting contracts as before, the student council will come for you. How do you plan to run your organization from now on?"
"More mercifully and more strictly. I'll show mercy to the contractors while placing restrictions on myself who benefits from the contracts."

The operation of the contracts would remain the same.
But the purpose had changed.

"From now on, I plan to become Madam Camella, primarily focused on matchmaking. I have confidence in my ability to see through people's hearts, and those I've matched are satisfied."

Camella's faction had transformed from exploiting couples' affection to genuinely connecting couples and receiving their cooperation!
Though an unexpected marriage agency had suddenly emerged in the Academy, Russo thought it was for the best.

'Since she's saying that, let's give her one more chance.'

The watchful eyes of the student council in the courtroom gallery turned away.
With this, Camella had survived just like him.

"So what can I help with? Unlike before, it doesn't seem like you need me to create safe zones away from the instructors' watchful eyes."
"What a disappointing thing to say! In a world with few trustworthy people, you should be my safe haven."

Camella bravely leaned against Russo's arm.
After flinching momentarily, Russo pulled her head to rest on his shoulder with trembling hands.

-If you don't manage Camella properly, you know what happens, right? Next time, you'll have to play with me for 12 hours!

It would be a lie to say that threat hadn't influenced his current decision.
But such sweet moments were something he'd want to enjoy regardless of anyone's requests or threats.
Compared to wasting 25 years of life waiting for a promotion, this felt like real living.

'Keep your promises. Both Camella's and yours, Oknodie.'

As the heartwarming scene unfolded, another person left the gallery—Jaku, the man who had initiated this entire situation.

* * *

"So... if I heard correctly, Camella's faction was completely destroyed by your organization but managed to survive through a fundamental transformation?"
"That's what happened."

A mark appeared on Bellocasio's contract, forcibly indicating complete repayment.
Of course that would happen.
After providing such incredibly valuable information.
Bellocasio was dumbfounded.
Normally, such information couldn't be obtained just because one wanted it.
To thoroughly inquire about the circumstances surrounding a major event related to an organization's rise and fall would require the qualities of a competent spy, worthy of serious career consideration.
Yet Jaku had accomplished this difficult task.
By directly using Oknodie's organization to destroy Camella's faction once before allowing it to barely survive.

"Are all first-years either monsters or lunatics...?"
"With this, my contract debt is gone, right?"
"Is that even a question? Of course it's all paid off. The contract is terminated."
"Says who? That's not fair. I'll formally object to the God of Contracts."
"What?"
"The value of the information I provided far exceeds the debt I owed under the contract. If I don't receive compensation equivalent to the difference as change, I'll request the God of Contracts to evaluate the unfairness of this contract and demand punishment for Senior Bellocasio."

Suddenly, the victim and perpetrator of the contract had switched places.
Even the God of Contracts seemed to think "he's not wrong" and readily accepted the proposal.
As a special clause appeared on the glowing blue contract, inscribed with divine language, Bellocasio could only sigh.

'This threat is surely not the end of it.'

They destroyed Camella's faction just because someone touched one of their students, while also freeing them from the contract's influence.
Bellocasio realized that if he got on their bad side any further, he might become the next toy for "the organization that plays with Oknodie."
He felt Oknodie's unspoken threat through Jaku—accept this gracefully.

"I should never mess with you 981st batch again. I don't understand why every scheme against you fails."

Watching the bitter figure of a man whose pride as a crime consultant had vanished, Jaku pressed on.

"So when do you plan to pay the change?"
"Afraid I'll run off with it? Fine. The God of Contracts is watching, so I'm not crazy enough to try that. I'll give you one right now."

With the attitude of "take this and get lost, you filthy bastard," Bellocasio revealed one piece of information like a quest reward.

"The Dragon Principal. Seems quiet these days, doesn't he?"
"Indeed."
"That's because he's busy tormenting the upper-year students. But that will end soon too."

Jaku's expression soured.
Bellocasio's face, which had been crumpled like he'd bitten into a bitter persimmon, now showed a sense of vindication.
Definitely not a good sign.
Something was coming.
A future that wouldn't be good for the 981st batch first-years.

"What's going to happen?"
"I don't know. The principal is capricious. But I know what you should prepare for."

Bellocasio pointed to the magic watch.

"Access the lecture boards for the classes you're taking and check the registered assignments. Then submit all your assignments in advance this weekend."

What? That's just ordinary friendly advice.
Is this person actually a tsundere who can't help honestly?

Swoosh.

The special clause that had been glowing on the contract faded, its effect apparently fulfilled.
It was as if the God of Contracts acknowledged that Bellocasio's words constituted sufficient "change."

"It's a festival!"
"A festival?"

I immediately recalled upon hearing Jaku's report.
The second semester's trap event at the Academy.
What seemed like a rest week for lucky, good students mixed in among regular weekly events would transform into a maddening trap.
Or rather, it had been a trap from the beginning.
A fake festival event that catches every newbie who shows up.

-Professor! Can we really take a break?
-Of course.
-We won't lose points for not attending?
-That's a needless worry.
-But why are lectures still being held?
-The professors need to be in the classroom for those who don't want to rest during this time made for resting.
-Haha. You must get very bored then, since no one will show up.

The professor smiled warmly at my naive rookie-days comment.
And then the naive rookie got blindsided.
Students who had heard advice from seniors diligently attended the professors' lectures, and all accumulated assignments had to be submitted the following week.
Needless to say, the content taught during that period would appear on exams, causing the value of lecture notes to skyrocket.

-I said good students could rest, not that poor students could rest too. This is the price of overestimating yourself.

The naive rookie finally realized through this event that Gift Academy also teaches about the various injustices and absurdities one encounters in society!

"It started around this time last year too. The principal's vicious fake festival!"
"The festival... isn't real?"
"Oh, the festival is real. But if you believe everything's fine and let your guard down to enjoy it, you'll be the only one who suffers!"

Enjoy a week of freedom from all lectures during the festival days.
Jaku shuddered, realizing the impressive slogan actually meant "(for model students only)."

"But it might be fun! Every year, seniors from different batches experience the festival, but with different themes!"

In this world, there are beef festivals that delight the palate and mosquito-killing festivals.
What theme would this festival have to trick the newbies?

"This week's event is the 'Exorcism Festival.' Students who cannot exorcise non-material beings are advised to focus quietly on their studies."
"..."

A festival with no deceptive meaning appeared, like a bug in the system.
When they should be holding a real festival to encourage slacking off, they've openly set such an obvious trap with that kind of announcement—who would actually go around enjoying exorcism?

"If you diligently perform exorcisms, you can earn treasure-grade items as rewards based on your achievements. Additionally, if you complete exorcism challenges from each faculty, you can earn faculty merit points, so I recommend trying at least once."

The members who had gone on the boat trip exchanged awkward glances with me and each other. We already have things, so we don't really need more, right?
The ring that's been buzzing since earlier is also concerning.
It's a desperate rescue request from fake Lin, asking not to be exorcised.
Come to think of it, I'm worried about fake Lin being treated like a wild ghost.
What if she gets accidentally exorcised while wandering around? Wouldn't that ruin her relationship with Xing?
Alright, decision made.
While others play, we'll just focus on studying hard!
