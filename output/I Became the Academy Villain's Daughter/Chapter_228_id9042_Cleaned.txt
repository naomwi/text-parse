# 228 - Secret (Not Really)

Arcadia was saved with Oknodie's help.
The Sebiche family narrowly escaped collapse.
The public's cold treatment toward her also became confused.
Now that the family hadn't fallen...
Should they continue to ignore Lady Arcadia as before?
What if she made a splendid comeback?
Unlike many who feared her and were watching her every move, Arcadia had no interest in dealing with those who had betrayed her.

'I survived with blood on Oknodie's hands.'

This was a debt.
A debt equivalent to having her life saved from a crisis where her family could have been ruined, she could have been expelled from the Academy, and beyond that, she might have been hunted down by those coveting her family's wealth, captured and enslaved, or even killed.
If that was the case, she too must repay an equal debt.
Rather than turning a blind eye to the suspicious darkness surrounding Oknodie with excuses like "it's the Foundation's business" or "that girl will manage somehow," she would face it head-on and uncover the truth.
And she would steadily pull her from the darkness of the shadows into the light.
This was the only way to repay her debt.
From the moment she defined it as such, this became her determined method of repayment.

"Gisele. Despite my shamelessness, may I ask you to provide information to help Oknodie?"
"It seems you feel indebted in your heart."

Indeed, he was sensitive to people's hearts.
Unlike Canillien Truffle, who had gauged Arcadia's complex feelings through a long conversation, Gisele saw through all her emotions with just that one sentence.
It was remarkably quick, even considering he knew the circumstances.

"I understand your feelings, but you're still in a precarious situation, my lady. The Sebiche ducal family has lost its ducal title and been demoted to an earldom, hasn't it?"
"...Still, the external crisis for our family has stopped. And the family can't do anything harmful to me, as I'm presumed to hold their slave ledger."
"But you haven't regained your position as faction boss. You stepped down after being disappointed by your followers. Those who believe you've turned against them will become even more hostile to overcome their fear."
"That's not difficult. With the information I gathered while acting as faction boss and using the children's relationships, I could shatter any hostile faction into dozens of pieces."

Arcadia had excellent political skills.
Though she was kind and considerate, the very fact that she could maintain such "kindness" along with her high title meant she possessed inherent strength as a person.
For someone who isn't clever but wishes to live kindly, either they or their guardian must know how to wield extraordinary power.
Gisele liked such kind people.
Those strong enough to maintain goodness in positions where evil would be expected naturally shine brightly.
Though her light might not match Oknodie's, Arcadia was still admirable enough for Gisele to respect her as a partner.
With that respect in mind, Gisele granted her request.

"Very well. If I tried to break your stubbornness, my back would bend first."
"Please stop calling me 'my lady' when I'm no longer a duke's daughter."
"Should I call you Lady Countess then?"
"My name is sufficient."
"As you wish, Lady Arcadia."

After exchanging light banter and smiles, Gisele took out a notebook from his pocket and tore out a page.

"Please take this."
"What is...?"
"It's the results of my investigation into the 'Pierrot Mask Group,' the Foundation's subordinate organization that Oknodie was summoned to for her last mission."
"...I was planning to start by investigating the Academy's Destroyer professor, considering the possibility that Di received a dangerous mission from there."
"Trust me. That avenue is thoroughly blocked by the Thieves' Guild's information network. With your current level of intelligence resources, you'd be tracked down quickly."
"Is there really no way?"
"The Thieves' Guild's skills are overwhelming, as they were responsible for information support for hero parties even in the demon realm. Even underworld informants would suffer great losses if they clashed with them."

Let alone with the diminished power of the Sebiche family, now reduced to an earldom.

"I understand."

Arcadia gracefully accepted this and examined the notebook. Her expression hardened as she looked at Gisele's notebook pages.
After seriously reviewing the contents, she held up the note and asked:

"What is this record?"
"As you can see, it's a record of the Pierrot Mask Group's national tour route and the villages that were destroyed along their path."
"...Do you think Oknodie's hometown is among these?"
"There might be something even worse."
"Worse?"
"Villages destroyed by Oknodie's own hands."

The countess's hand moved surprisingly quickly to draw the gun from the holster on her thigh.

"...You. How can you say such things about Oknodie? Such terrible words about a child you believed was kidnapped by them!"
"Why do you think this shouldn't be mentioned? They're an organization that kidnaps children and raises them as killing machines. Have you never once considered they might test Oknodie's 'loyalty'?"
"!!"
"Please stop. You're too precious to dig into such matters, Lady Arcadia. If you knew how filthy the information that comes into my possession can be, you'd wish to be blind and deaf."

This wasn't an attitude of looking down on a well-bred lady.
Gisele sincerely believed she couldn't handle information worse than this.

"Don't be ridiculous. I'm not such a coward that I'd turn away from uncovering darkness out of fear when I owe someone my life. The name Sebiche might tolerate turning a blind eye to injustice, but the name Arcadia cannot."
"I feel uncomfortable for having provoked you with unnecessary words. I hope you won't get too deeply involved. There are limits to what we can touch right now."

Seeing that Arcadia was mentally tougher than expected, Gisele tore out another page from his notebook.

"Find this student."
"Jaku. First-year lower class student. The student who experienced dark mana overload during the mana response test. Can this student help track the Pierrot Mask Group's atrocities?"
"Yes."
"Who is he that he can access such information?"
"He's a Foundation scholarship student. Like Oknodie, he's likely someone who may have dirtied his hands."
"!!"
"But you must be extremely careful in approaching him. If you approach carelessly, the Foundation might silence you, or he might act unpredictably."
"Don't worry. I have no desire to lose the first lead I've finally obtained."

Arcadia turned away confidently, telling him not to worry.


* * *


To learn about someone, it's good to know their usual movements and which facilities they frequently use.
After figuring out their routine, the next step is to approach naturally and establish a friendly relationship.

"Excuse me! Is Jaku here?"
"Jaku? That guy should be in the training room."
"Lucky him... Jaku's getting attention from another girl."

The lower class students glared at Jaku, who was just leaving the training room, with envious and jealous eyes.
Jaku wiped the sweat from his forehead, pushing back his curly hair with a towel.
Several female students passing by either stole glances at him or gave each other thumbs up as they walked by, making the male students' gazes even more piercing.

"Excuse me!"
"...What?"
"Are you Jaku?"
"Follow me. If we talk while receiving those guys' glares, we'll end up with holes in our bodies like being hit by a rain of arrows."

Jaku led the way to a relatively secluded outdoor terrace from the rest area on the first floor of the training building.
The breeze was cool and the scenery was nice, but few people used it because of rumors that second-year students occasionally appeared there, making it a perfect spot for private conversations away from prying eyes.

"What do you want?"
"Actually, I came at someone else's request. May I ask you a question?"
"No."
"Aww, just one please?"
"...Fine, just one then."

A female student carrying a strange lighting stand.
With her cute appearance and playful personality, Tito Soga easily unlocked the door to Jaku's heart.

"First, please accept this letter!"

The sender of the letter Tito Soga delivered was, of course, Arcadia.
After opening and examining the contents, Jaku realized he had become entangled in something both troublesome and dangerous.

"Sigh."

With a sigh of complicated feelings, he scratched his curly hair. His gaze fell on Tito Soga, who was sitting across the table looking at him with sparkling eyes.

"Aren't you done with your business?"
"That letter. It's a love letter, right?"
"...Don't you know the contents?"
"I was just asked to deliver the letter. Still, it's the first time I've experienced the countess asking me to deliver a letter to a male student."
"It's not a love letter. Don't be silly."
"Aww, that's disappointing."

Tito Soga pouted briefly before smiling brightly and saying cutely with her hands clasped:

"Oh right! She told me to ask this question after you received the letter, so please listen."
"...What now?"
"If you had a chance to repay your debt to Oknodie, would you be willing to help?"

What a suspicious person.
Writing in the letter that she knows my identity.
Sending an innocent girl to make it seem like she has no ill intentions, stirring up my feelings.
Still, it was somewhat relieving.
At least my identity hasn't been discovered by someone who wants to use it for evil purposes.

"Of course. If it weren't for her, I would have been expelled from the Academy before even taking the midterm exams."
"Great! Then come with me to meet the countess!"
"I don't want to."
"Aww, why not?"
"Are you seriously asking that?"

Tito Soga.
Cute but really stupid.

"You're asking me to go discuss plans to help Oknodie in Oknodie's presence? What kind of torture is this?"

This was the problem.
The letter's sender tried to hide her identity, but the person delivering it kept saying "the countess's letter," revealing the source.
And then bringing the absolute last person who should be present to a meeting about helping Oknodie?
Is she messing with me?
That's what it feels like.

"Oh! I'll pretend I didn't see anything and you two can continue talking comfortably!"
"...How would that work? What were you thinking, really?"

In response to Jaku's protest, Tito Soga smiled shyly like a harmless small animal.

"I felt embarrassed going alone to deliver a letter to a boy! So I came with my best friend Oknodie."

It was truly an innocent and straightforward reason.


* * *


"...So you brought both Jaku and Oknodie?"
"Yes! I did well, didn't I?"

Arcadia's fist trembled at Tito Soga's innocent expression.
