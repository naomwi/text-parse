# <415 - Comrade or Burden>

In the safe zone, Gisele's group quickly reviewed information they had collected and analyzed.

"How about fried insect shells from the Bug Disaster Zone?"
"Rejected. Oknodie may compromise on taste, but never on cleanliness. Please exclude all bizarre cooking ingredients."
"Oh, senior! A senior from the safe zone pointed out a few places. They say the white petals growing in these fields are edible?"
"White cosmos petals grow outside the student-only zone as well. We should also exclude ingredients that might already be included in the foundation's secured ingredients."

All information from Isabel and Son O-cheon was rejected.
Though they had somewhat underestimated Oknodie because they always saw her eating something, it wasn't easy to narrow down destinations based on Oknodie's food encyclopedia collection status.
She had collected and eaten so many things, and had others stored in her food bag, that ordinary ingredients couldn't attract Oknodie's attention.

"For someone who looks like she'd eat fried cicadas, you're surprisingly picky, little one."
"Shut up. Filter more strictly before I actually make something and force-feed you."
"Hahaha! You could just not eat it?"
"If you don't eat, I'll never cook for you again."
"...Isabel's words don't sound like jokes at all. That's scary."

With Isabel and Son O-cheon helping gather information, those with anxious hearts like Hestia and Lotto couldn't demand to move forward recklessly.
They realized that moving blindly without Gisele's information network wouldn't yield good results.

"Take this. Information about rare ingredients that only grow within magical disasters."

Among those helping despite their impatience was Jigoku.
Gisele hesitated when he noticed bloodstains on the paper she handed him.
Seeing her look that seemed to say "don't act like a nerd by asking about the source or it won't be fun," Gisele nodded.

"This is very helpful. I'll identify ingredients that Oknodie hasn't yet accessed but would definitely want to target, considering the difficulty of accessing these rare ingredients."

<Pressure Herb> - A rare ingredient with the unique property of being compressed under high pressure in enclosed spaces with pushing traps. Due to its quality improving through natural compression, it has decent rarity, but its easy accessibility in the early stages means Oknodie likely already collected it.

<Flame Flower> - A rare ingredient that grows in the Flame Disaster Zone where fire rains from the sky instead of water. Though difficult to obtain and access, it can be found outside as well, so the foundation likely already provided it in her backpack.

<Bouncy Meat Cube> - A rare ingredient created by an ancient wizard who pitied animals sacrificed for meat. Information suggests these famous meat cubes, ranked among the top gourmet items, are bouncing around somewhere in the <Ant Nest> zone beyond the consecutive disaster area. With high acquisition difficulty, unlikely to be provided by the foundation, and located further along their path, Gisele's eyes lit up.

"This is it. If we rush to the Ant Nest zone and acquire the Bouncy Meat Cube first, we could meet Oknodie."
"We're already the most behind among the search parties. And what about the additional time spent on information gathering?"
"We need to catch up with Xing's group who took a shortcut ahead of us."

Not an easy task.
Even Xing and Mob had help from second-year students' collective barrier when taking that chaotic shortcut, and now first-years would have to follow it alone.
Breaking a limb would be considered a minor incident on such a dangerous path.

"The shortcut mainly required aerial maneuvers. It's impossible for someone heavy like me."
"Fighters can't use magic. I can't either..."

Hestia and Lotto declined.
Gisele and Isabel couldn't possibly manage either, with all their luggage, unless they planned to rain items from the sky.

"The Thunder God Church's heavy armor weighs no less than Hestia."
"Are you stupid? Who in their right mind would take a path where your feet don't even touch the ground?"

Nise, the Thunder God Church nun, and Jenya, the beast-person fighter, clearly drew the line.
When essentially everyone had expressed their refusal, only one person stepped forward.

"I'll go."

Now that Jigoku was revealing her true abilities, she was certainly capable of challenging the shortcut.
As Gisele hesitated about burdening just one person with everything, a hairy hand patted his shoulder.

"I'll go too."
"Mr. Son O-cheon?"
"What? Don't trust me?"
"This isn't a difficulty level to challenge on a whim."
"I'm challenging it because I'm confident."

There was no way to stop someone who insisted on going.
Gisele sighed and handed over a ring.

"This is a Ring of Flight. It should save your life in an emergency."
"Oh, this will be a great help. You nerd, have you been keeping such good items all to yourself until now?"

Gisele removed his gloves and took off the rings he was wearing one after another, handing them over.

"Next, in order from top to bottom: Ring of Life Maintenance, Ring of Poison Resistance, Ring of Five-Color Resistance, Ring of Strength Enhancement, Ring of Swiftness, Ring of Accuracy Correction..."
"No, you madman. How am I supposed to remember all this?"

Due to Son O-cheon's intelligence issues, he ended up taking only the most important few from the surprisingly large number of rings.
Everyone looked at Gisele with astonished eyes at his tremendous wealth, but Gisele's gaze was fixed only on Son O-cheon's departing back.

"If you're that worried, why didn't you stop him completely?"
"He decided to do this himself. How could I break his resolve to save a comrade? All I can do is lend him expensive magical tools."
"Hmm... is that so?"
"Yes, that's it."
"I have one more question."
"Go ahead."
"Those rings, were you secretly wearing them under your gloves since the entrance exam?"

Gisele's slightly opened eyes closed into a straight line like a lock.

"That's a secret."

Gisele returned to his usual squinty-eyed mode.
Isabel gave him a suspicious look before turning away.


* * *

"This is too much for you. Don't be stubborn, give up."

Though Jigoku might typically say such things with her fierce demeanor, she didn't actually rebuke Son O-cheon like that.

"Hey, wouldn't you move faster without me?"
"Want to be abandoned? Monkey boy. Move your legs instead of wasting time talking nonsense."

After running silently through magical disasters for a while, Jigoku suddenly spoke.

"There were often guys like you in the pirate crew."
"Monkey beast-people?"
"As if such rare beast-people would be there. I mean hard workers."

There was a reason she didn't abandon Son O-cheon and let him keep up despite her frustration.

"Pirate work is dirty, difficult, and hard. A damn 3D job. The country might spare your life if you pay tribute and convert to being a private pirate, but it doesn't change the fact that it's a fundamentally lowly profession. There's only one type of person who truly dedicates themselves to this job even when they're well-fed."
"What kind of people?"
"Those blinded by revenge. People whose eyes are so fixed on vengeance that they can't see the brilliance of gold coins, no matter how many treasures they possess."
"But I like gold coins."

Jigoku cursed with her eyes.
If this clueless guy provoked her one more time, she'd just hit him once and leave him behind.

"The great pirate who once guided me on the path of piracy said that those who chase things that shine brighter than gold coins are all fools. He said he makes such fools his comrades and never abandons them."
"Is that a recruitment pitch?"
"Even if you're not a pirate, you're one of the few I acknowledge. Even now, you're risking your life to follow me despite knowing your skills are lacking."
"..."
"Don't fall behind. I don't want to move forward leaving someone whose spirit has been broken."
"Hahaha! Who do you think I am? Son O-cheon of the Rocky Mountain never gives up."

With someone as incredible as you moving forward, how could my spirit be broken?
Son O-cheon thought to himself.
Jigoku stepped onto the shortcut that Xing's group had broken through, amazingly without barriers or artifacts, using just her body.

<Great Pirate-Style Mana Training Method>
<Pirate's Footsteps>

Jigoku handled with her bare body a speed that was challenging even for Son O-cheon with artifact assistance.
Like turbulent waves, she shook but deflected all impacts, always placing her foot exactly where she wanted in the end.
She spread her large cape like a sail, never losing her dignified spirit.

'This girl suffered serious injuries requiring chest bandages during the lecture with Oknodie, yet she can still demonstrate this level of movement technique.'

Jigoku's fighting spirit made Son O-cheon's heart race.

'This is how a true man should be!'
"Hahaha! You're a good man!"
"Suddenly?"
"Would you be satisfied if I called you an excellent man?"
"Gahaha! You're talking nonsense."

Son O-cheon finally drew laughter from Jigoku.
Without losing their forward momentum despite the friendly atmosphere, they succeeded in catching up with Xing's group.

"Hey, Xing! Gisele found the place where Oknodie is likely to be."
"Good. Lead the way."
"By the way, where did all the second-years go? Scouting ahead?"
"They fell behind, exhausted after using all their mana on the barrier. They were annoying, so I left them all behind."
"......"

Oknodie obsessed with rare ingredients vs. second-years who came looking for Oknodie but were abandoned after using all their mana.
No matter how you look at it, shouldn't they be more worried about the second-years than Oknodie?

"Don't misunderstand. I don't abandon people anytime. I cut off the tail to avoid being tracked."
"Tracked?"
"One of the Unregistered Challengers spotted us and started tracking."
"Unregistered Challengers?! Those are the extremely dangerous guys Gisele mentioned!"

Unlike Son O-cheon who was shocked at the mention of Unregistered Challengers, Jigoku glared at Xing with a fierce face.

"So you're saying you abandoned the second-years to shake off the Unregistered Challenger. How could you use and discard seniors who squeezed out their last mana for you? Is that what someone who claims to protect Oknodie would do?"
"One comrade, Oknodie, is enough to help me with my revenge."
"Damn pedophile. You don't deserve to stand by Oknodie's side. At least Oknodie wouldn't throw away people who support her."
"Then why are you here?"
"..."
"You lost your persuasiveness the moment you stood in front of me instead of beside Oknodie. Isn't being a good kid who cares for comrades just something you want to believe about yourself?"

Xing's statement, with his unnecessarily improved discernment from the Developing an Eye lecture, exposed aspects that Jigoku didn't want to recall.
However, their emotional battle couldn't continue.

"Everyone, please stop. I understand how you feel, but now is not the time for us to fight among ourselves."
"What nerve do you have to speak, Mob, when you agreed with Xing's methods?"
"Ah, my precious juniors. Don't fight. I've caught all the second-years you abandoned."

Even Jigoku, who had been furiously angry just moments ago, turned her head sharply toward the voice.
Zip, zip, zip.
With a sound like dragging a sack, a senior appeared.
In his hands were not sacks but second-year students being dragged along the ground, held by their hair.
The suffering appearance of the second-years was serious enough to make even Xing and Mob, who had abandoned them, turn pale.

"I spared the first-years because they're too green, but I had a feeling this would happen. If your comradeship is so strong, why not prove it?"
"...What do you want?"
"One artifact per person. At least treasure grade. Hand them over. If you refuse, I'll kill you all and take all the 'lost items.' Isn't that a generous offer?"

The most notorious figure among the three Unregistered Challengers.
The murderer Urgas had appeared.

'Little mouse. What on earth are you doing in a place like this? I hope you're alive...'

Son O-cheon clenched his trembling hands, trying to hide his fear.


* * *

At the entrance barrier to the Student-Only Zone.
Professor El Draco, who had been forcibly keeping the barrier from closing, gaped in shock.

"What the hell? I sent people to bring you back, and you come out alone?"
"Huh?"

At that same moment, having finished her business and feeling satisfied, Oknodie exited the Student-Only Zone—a fact that none of Oknodie's search party was aware of.
