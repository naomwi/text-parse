To be honest, the first lecture was more difficult!

A shepherd who skins sheep and stuffs human corpses inside.
A human troll who trains monster trolls with drugs and exploits them.
Though the contexts are similar, the difficulty is easier.
Suspicion of humans.
Distrust was already planted in the first lecture.
It's naturally much easier to find the crime of someone you've been suspicious of from the beginning than to find the crime of someone you never suspected at all.

'But somehow it feels different.'

While the Academy's incidents stem from "difficulty," Professor Destroyer's adventure tales originate from fundamental human "desires" and "malice."
There are many malicious characters at the Academy too, but people as evil as that are truly rare.

'What kind of adventures has Professor Destroyer been on since 15 years ago in this playthrough?'

I feel a bit anxious.
If I don't graduate from the Academy with excellent grades, does that mean I'll have to go out and live in such a harsh world?
After hearing just two adventure stories, my romantic notions about countryside villages have been completely shattered.
If I went down to farm in the countryside, I'd have to worry about whether strangers get captured by villagers and spread as fertilizer—that's the level of civility we're talking about.
And it's not just about being careful around villagers.
A passing black magician could obliterate the entire village overnight.
For a comfortable isekai life, even after graduating from the Academy, I'd need to accumulate wealth, fame, and power to sustain myself for life while enduring the harsh cost of living in big cities.

'For that, I need to diligently collect points.'

The result of which is the flags I've laid out and displayed in the principal's classroom.

"Flags for sale!"

The upper-class students looked at the flags with gloomy faces, repeatedly licking their lips.

"Damn. How many are there?"
"Living life all on your own, huh."
"This is depressing."

It's nice that everyone comes to look, but...
Wouldn't you like to buy some?

"Buy some flags!"
"Wow. There's even a purple flag."
"Buy some flags!"
"I really want one."
"Buy some flags!"
"Wasn't the accordion pork belly at lunch today delicious? Though it was outrageous that they charged 10 points for an extra piece of meat."

Seriously, no one is buying.
As tears welled up in my eyes from frustration, the chatting students panicked and fumbled.

"Ah, don't misunderstand. We're not trying to make fun of you."
"Then why isn't anyone buying?"
"Because we've all spent our points."
"What? Was the accordion pork belly that delicious?"
"It was tasty, but that's not where we spent everything. We used our points to buy flags too."
"But no one else has as many flags as I do!"
"One person does."

Just then, someone entered the classroom.
His face was so bright that I finally understood what people meant by "radiating light"—it was Gisele with his completely empty bundle.

"Miss Oknodie. You're a bit late."
"Hmph. Mr. Gisele. Everyone says they have no points. How many flags did you sell to make them like this?"
"I sold out completely. I meticulously sold every last fake flag."

If I had known this would happen, I should have sold all my remaining flags to Mr. Gisele!
Thanks to him, our team's total number of flags has increased tremendously.

But this situation has become quite ridiculous.
The fake flags that Gisele released.

"Our team has 112 flags!"
"Hoho. Don't underestimate the wealth of the maritime nation of Florence. For the team led by me, Arcadia, buying 100 flags is nothing!"
"Don't underestimate the wealth of the Empire. Our Fried Chicken faction has secured 122 flags!"
"Even 112 was too few!"

That's right.
The overall number of flags has increased.
Almost to the point of inflation.

"How are you planning to clean up this mess after selling so many?"
"I merely sold items according to the probabilities I disclosed in advance."

Gisele's kind smile makes me freshly realize that he's truly a black market dealer.
More than half of the flags held by other teams are fake.
The probability of collecting only real flags among them is practically non-existent.

"Excuse me, Oknodie. You just said you're selling flags, right?"
"Oh? Would you like to buy some?"
"If possible, I'd like to buy three."
"Then that'll be 100 points each, so 300 poin—mmph!"
"Oh my. Miss Oknodie made a slip of the tongue. It's 1,000 points each. A total of 3,000 points."

Gisele, who was standing next to me, covered my mouth and quoted a ridiculous number instead.
The student who had inquired about purchasing openly expressed her dissatisfaction.

"The person I'm dealing with is Oknodie, not you, Gisele. Don't butt in from the side and raise the price."
"Miss Vanilla. The value of goods varies depending on the circumstances. Moreover, these flags are 100% guaranteed winners without any probability games. It's only natural that their value would increase."
"Would you mind not calling me by my name so casually? I have no reason to listen to such a lecture. And who are you to Oknodie that you can interfere so freely?"

Obviously, he's my teammate!
As I was about to answer energetically, Gisele covered my mouth again.

"Miss Oknodie is still a tender-hearted child. It's difficult to say she fully understands the importance of commerce. I'm helping her as her advisor to ensure she profits."

Even so, 1,000 points per flag is too much.
Gisele isn't someone who wouldn't know the value of points, so what is he thinking?

"A thousand points is excessive."
"Then unfortunately, these flags will remain without an owner."
"Kuh... I'll buy them. 3,000 points is fine!"

But she's actually buying them?!
Flags aren't worth that much!

"Hmph. I thought Miss Oknodie would react that way."

Gisele said with a smile.

"Before the sale, could I have a moment alone with Oknodie?"
"I don't know what your business is, but make it quick. The lecture time is almost over, and we don't know when the principal will arrive."

Vanilla grumbled but waited patiently.
Keeping some distance from her, Gisele spoke in a low, measured voice.

"Miss Oknodie, you said before that the value of a flag is 100 points, correct?"
"Yes."
"That value is probably based on a careful examination of the benefits obtainable from flags and their quantity. The actual value of one flag is indeed close to 100 points."
"That's right!"
"That's the problem. For some reason, Miss Oknodie knows and is confident about the value of these items, but others are not."

Now I understand what Gisele is trying to say.

"Since others don't know how expensive these items are, they'll buy them even if the price is greatly inflated!"

Collect flags.
That's all the principal said.
What rewards come from collecting many flags, how precious those rewards are.
What penalties come from collecting fewer flags, how serious those penalties are.
No one has any of that information.
Unlike the actual value of 100 points per flag.
The value of flags can vary tremendously depending on each student's imagination and fear.

"Even considering that, isn't 3,000 points absurdly expensive? Especially since everyone's flag count has become a mess because of you, Mr. Gisele."
"The number three sounds strange in a situation where no one knows how many real flags anyone has, but Miss Vanilla surely has her own calculations."

The team Vanilla belongs to is the team of the 3rd Imperial Princess, considered a fringe and leftover group even within the Imperial upper class.

"You mean Yayoi's team?"
"...Are you referring to the Imperial 3rd Princess by her name?"
"What's the big deal? We're classmates at the Academy."
"You have remarkable courage to speak that way about an Imperial Princess, no matter how powerless she may be. Sometimes I envy Miss Oknodie's boldness."
"You might improve your courage if you attend the adventurer's night action lecture. Would you like to join me?"
"While tempting, I must decline. I've already selected all the lectures I wish to attend."

Unlike the 2nd Princess Mesugakki who goes around with followers, the 3rd Princess Yayoi has no followers, friends, or outside supporting forces.
