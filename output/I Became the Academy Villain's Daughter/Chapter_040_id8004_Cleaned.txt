# <40 - I'm Dying to Know>

"Isabel, there's someone I want to get closer to, but I don't know how to become friends with them. What should I do?"

Isabel, who was gathering ingredients with me in the nearby forest, walked straight into a tree.

"Um, Isabel?"
"Don't worry about it."
"Your forehead is really red though?"
"Never mind that. Who do you want to become friends with?"
"It's a secret!"

Isabel couldn't hide her disappointment.
I guess I'm being a bit harsh, considering she's spending her precious day off gathering ingredients with me.

"Well... it's a secret, but I'll tell only you, Isabel. Just don't tell anyone else, okay?"
"Of course."
"There's this person who usually stays cooped up in one place and only has one friend besides me. But they're so close with that friend that I can't get any closer to them."

Isabel smiled knowingly.

"I know the type. Someone who gets so close to one person that it becomes difficult for others to befriend them."

A soulmate.
A best friend.
It's hard to get close to someone who already has such a close friend.
That's what Isabel explained.

"In those cases, you should become friends by talking about their best friend. That's how the men in the adventure guild became friends."
"Really?"
"And they like talking about hiking."
"What?"
"Fishing isn't bad either."

Social skills learned from the harsh social environment of the Esonia Adventure Guild: bringing up hiking and fishing as conversation topics!

'But Moon can't go hiking or fishing...'

It's not helpful, but I'll keep it in mind.
Anyway, I just need to spark interest by talking about their interests, right?


* * *


Gisele declared with serious eyes.

"Miss Oknodie doesn't have any friends that close to her."
"I thought so."
"Unless she's made a new friend without Isabel knowing, it must be someone we already know."

Isabel owed Oknodie a debt of gratitude.
Since Gisele, who shared her concern for Oknodie, had requested it with additional compensation, she consulted with him once a day about Oknodie.
Gisele's perspective, shaped by his experience as a black market dealer rather than a conventional profession, was surprisingly insightful, and Isabel often sought his advice.

"It's most likely Miss Dorothy."
"Dorothy?"
"She's in the same advanced class of Group A. Previously, Oknodie helped her when she was betrayed and abandoned by her teammates at Minerva's gate."
"So that girl passed the advanced class..."

Honestly, Isabel was surprised.
She admired Dorothy's persistence, but the harsh realities of life couldn't be overcome by determination alone.
Isabel couldn't find any special qualities in Dorothy that would guarantee her success.

'A difference in judgment.'

While she prided herself on her ability to appraise wild ingredients and excavated items as an adventurer, her ability to judge people fell far short of Gisele's, the black market dealer.
Wasn't she the one who failed to recognize how kind, upright, and pitiful Oknodie was when they first met?

'I knew it, but to this extent...'

Even though they stayed in the same women's dormitory, even on the same floor.
Unlike herself, who had no interest and knew nothing about Dorothy, Gisele had much more information despite living in a different dormitory. It was somewhat shocking.

"Don't blame yourself too much. Gathering information about people connected to Oknodie is just an occupational habit as a black market dealer. It's a gloomy hobby that takes a lot of time and can't be casually shared with others."
"That's not true. I've benefited from it. I think it's a perfectly admirable hobby."
"I'm glad you see it that way. By the way, that girl hasn't used the automatic laundry magic circle even once in the past few days, so she might smell a bit."
"That's going too far, even for you."

I'd prefer if it remained at the hobby level.
One step further and it's nothing but stalking.


* * *


Finding information about Dorothy wasn't difficult.
It was thanks to the help of another person who knew everything about the female students in Group A.

"Dorothy? Ah, that girl who carries a bow. The movements of Group B have become suspicious again, so I suggested that Group A members stick together, but she said she wants to be alone. Perhaps it's because of the shock of being betrayed by her hometown friend."

Arcadia, the only woman in the Seogwiyeon.
Unlike her first impression as a strict and difficult person, she showed responsibility befitting an advanced class student and noble by caring for the safety of Group A's female students.
She was as helpful as Gisele whenever Isabel lost track of Oknodie's whereabouts.

"Could I know Dorothy's room number?"
"It's 120."

As Isabel returned to the dormitory, she saw the back of a male student at the entrance who looked familiar.

"What are you doing in front of the women's dormitory?"
"Are you Oknodie's colleague?"

It was Rockfell, the man who had once betrayed Dorothy.
He was staring at a curtained window, lost in thought.

"I'm waiting for Dorothy to accept my apology."
"How rigid of you."
"I did something terrible."
"If you knew that, why did you do it in the first place?"
"At that time... I wasn't in my right mind. I thought I should at least pass for everyone back home, rather than failing along with Dorothy."
"I would never forgive you if I were her."
"..."
"Don't look so down. I'm on my way to see Dorothy anyway."

Rockfell urgently raised his arm to grab Isabel's shoulder but stopped mid-air.
Did he have the right to grab someone after what he'd done? Could he make such a selfish request now?
Unable to shake off his hesitation, he withdrew his hand.

"What a wimp. How can a man be so timid?"
"...Are you going to help me?"
"I can at least tell her that a creepy guy has been waiting outside the dormitory for days."
"It's shameless of me, but please."

Isabel lightly knocked on the door of room 120, where Dorothy was staying.

"Who is it?"
"Isabel. Oknodie's colleague who helped you in the forest."
"...Come in."

Creak. The door opened just a crack, barely enough to fit a foot through, revealing her anxious state of mind.
As Gisele had said, there was a musty smell characteristic of someone who hadn't washed their hair for days.

"You managed to pass."
"When I touched the instructor in the black hat, I got an enormous amount of points. About 1,800 points?"
"How is that possible?"
"Unlike the applicants who started the game of tag after resting for a day, the instructor had been accumulating tag points from the moment the test began."
"...So that's what they meant when they said you might pass if you're lucky?"

With such rules, the first person to touch the instructor could receive all the points the instructor had accumulated since the start of the test in one lump sum.
If Oknodie had known this from the beginning, she could have touched the instructor alone, made a comeback, and easily passed through the magic circle.

"The points were reduced to one-hundredth, so I actually got 18 points, and I even got -50 points for not arriving on time. But thanks to that, I didn't give up and kept going."

What would Oknodie have done?
Could she have monopolized 1,800 points instead of just 18?
Considering that 1 point from the entrance exam was converted to 100 points, it was an opportunity to gain 180,000 points.

'She knew but didn't aim for it.'

Isabel knew why.
It was because she was worried about her colleagues.
Wasn't she the one who gave up immediate admission with the top special privilege from the first gate because she was worried about everyone who had been with her?
A girl so kind and pure it seemed foolish.
At the same time, a girl raised as an assassin in a noble family, suspiciously capable.
The more Isabel thought about it, the more her heart ached.

"Did she ask you to do something?"
"Not exactly, but I came to make a request."
"It's not about that idiot outside, is it?"
"That's part of it."
"That fool Rockfell needs to suffer more. I'm still not over it. Do you think I'm being too harsh?"
"I don't care. I wouldn't forgive him either. A man who betrays once has no reason not to do it again. He's not fit to be a colleague."

For romance, she might keep a reasonably handsome Rockfell by her side, but as a colleague or marriage partner, it was absolutely impossible.
Rockfell, who had betrayed at a critical moment, was the epitome of an irresponsible man.
She couldn't trust him, nor did she want to.

"If it's not about Rockfell, what did you want to ask?"
"Oknodie consulted me because she wants to become friends with you."
"Huh? With me?"

Dorothy's eyes, which had been filled with gloom, suddenly showed signs of life and surprise.
A mischievous smile spread across her face, and Isabel immediately realized something.
Even Gisele's information could be wrong sometimes.

"That's not it?"
"Of course not. I haven't seen her once since passing. Are you sure you're not confusing her with someone else?"
"I've just wasted your time. I'm sorry."
"Wait. I'm interested now. Tell me what this is about."
"I'm not sure if telling you is a good idea."
"Trust me. I'm an advanced class student who somehow passed the entrance exam on my own, even if I got some advice. A forest keeper's tracking ability is quite reliable, right?"

Dorothy was so insistent that Isabel decided to humor her and tell the story.

"That sounds like the friend next door."
"Next door?"
"I've been observing my surroundings from inside my room, afraid there might be some dormitory test even after entering."
"...If you've returned to civilization, you should adapt. Why are you making things difficult for yourself when you have a comfortable single room in the dormitory?"
"Tch. You think I wanted to? I couldn't help it because I was anxious."

Getting back to the topic, Dorothy shared what she had witnessed.

"A few days ago, a strange maid visited those two rooms. I remember because she only went in to change the bed sheets for rooms 111 and 112."
"The bed sheets?"
"She was a very strange maid who even tried to eavesdrop by putting her ear against the wall outside."
"...Eavesdrop?"
"Something seemed off. Could there be something special between the muscular woman living in room 112 and Oknodie? Sorry, that's the only person I can think of that Oknodie might care about."
"No, that's helpful enough."
"Oh, and sometimes kids from nearby rooms put their ears against that wall to eavesdrop. You might learn something if you ask them."

Bed sheets exchanged only for two rooms.
A maid eavesdropping.
Nearby residents paying attention to sounds.

'Ah, I was wrong. Now I'm curious too.'

I'm dying to know.
If I don't uncover this secret, I won't be able to sleep tonight.
