# 93 - Food for Thought

What's the scariest type of exam question during school days?
Is it multiple-choice questions that don't even tell you whether there are two or three correct answers?
Or questions from completely outside the curriculum that make you want to bang the professor's head against the wall?
I, Oknodie, am certain.
The scariest questions aren't those—they're the ones without answer keys at the back of the test paper!

'980th Generation, 2nd Year Contract Scammer Bellocasio.'

What this person does would be terrifying enough to make a beginner cry, but for a player who's gone through multiple playthroughs and learned the strategy, it's not that threatening.
You can predict how they'll manipulate the student council, and sometimes you can even create sparks to pit them against other dangerous students.

'I know not just one but five solutions!'

But this situation is different.

"The items in this contract are quite diverse."
"Still, I can roughly gauge which supplies are needed for which lectures. We could do something similar using Seo Gwi-yeon's connections."
"The noble lady's capabilities are impressive. The issue is which lectures to monopolize. Depending on our choice, we could indirectly manipulate certain students' grades..."

Arcadia and Gisele.
A combination I've never seen despite multiple playthroughs.
The appearance of Gisele, a completely new character.
This person is trying to create a synergy of versatility by connecting with Arcadia, who should be an icon of incompetence.
From their discussion, it seemed like a new villainous duo was about to emerge at the Academy.

"What are you two doing on a weekend afternoon?"

Peek.
As I poked my head over the windowsill to ask, Gisele and Arcadia immediately reached for the table and swept their plans and pens to the floor with a crash.

"We were stretching."
"Ehhh. That's a lie."
"Good children are supposed to respond, 'Wow! You were stretching!'"
"Wow! You were stretching!"
"Well done."

They look just like parents caught in the act who tell their child they were just wrestling. It's such a pitiful sight.
They're trying so desperately to cover it up that I decide to let it slide just this once.

"Di. Aren't you going to the beach to build sand castles today?"
"There's a giant king crab alert in effect."

Giant king crabs can cut through iron swords with their pincers.
Even Arcadia seemed to realize this wasn't a good excuse and suggested another location.

"It's such a nice day, how about going hiking?"
"I don't have sunscreen."

If you go hiking on a day like this, your skin will burn!
Arcadia, who had been staying indoors to avoid the sun, had nothing to say to that.

"Then, I've heard rumors that there's a library somewhere in the school, but no one has ever found it. How about looking for the library?"
"Why do you keep trying to send me somewhere else? Am I making you uncomfortable being here?"

When I pretended to look dejected, Arcadia gasped, clasped her hands in front of her chest, and fidgeted anxiously.

"Not at all, Di. How could I ever be uncomfortable with you? It's just that we're discussing secret business matters, so the timing isn't good."
"I know about business too!"
"Really? Then I'll give you a simple problem. If you solve it, I'll allow you to listen to our conversation."

<Arcadia's Test Event>
Arcadia has considerable business acumen.
If you pass her test, you might have the opportunity to gain significant benefits.

"Okay!"
"First, let's assume you've decided to enter the snack market that you love."
"I like chocolate teddy bear cookies!"
"Hehe. Yes. You want to make chocolate teddy bear cookies, but if you make too few, they'll sell out and disappoint customers. If you make too many, you'll have leftover cookies, right?"
"I'll eat all the leftovers!"
"Then the existing bakery that dominates the local market will spread rumors that your shop doesn't do enough business to sell all your cookies even if you stay open all day."
"Can't I just beat them up?"
"There's a better way."

Arcadia smiled maturely and said:

"You analyze the competitor's sales, flagship products, customer age groups, and peak sales hours, then target those age groups and time slots with special events."

Arcadia filled a chart with numbers showing customer age groups, peak usage times, popular products, and average sales, then twirled her pen and handed it to me upside down.

"Write in 500 words or less how you would target which age group, time, and product to enter the market share competition with minimum investment for maximum profit."

This is too serious.
I held the pen, put it to my lips, and thought hard for a while before carefully writing my answer.

I might be able to memorize and repeat answers, but there's no way I could solve a problem that requires genuine intelligence.
Such intellectual powerhouses might exist somewhere in the real world, but I was generally the physical type who got by with my body.

"Oh, by the way. If you go to the annex, you'll find Isabel and Son O-cheon."
"What are they doing there?"
"They failed the test earlier and are feeling depressed."
"..."

So it's a gathering place for the defeated.

"You too?"

So I ended up here.
At the gathering place for the defeated.

"This is why I can't stand those brainy types. They look down on people unless it's their specialty."

Mr. Son O-cheon, you look down on people whenever physical strength is needed. I was about to say something but decided against it. What's the point of fighting among fellow losers?

"Isabel, aren't you frustrated?"
"Not really? Thinking about that stuff just gives me a headache. In exploration teams, there are scholars who decipher ancient texts, and there are swordsmen who take care of rusty-armored zombies that jump out."
"What did Isabel do?"
"I was the exploration captain's personal assistant."
"Wow."

Being the captain's assistant sounds like a next-generation ace?
Even Son O-cheon looked at Isabel differently.

"I thought you were just a kind person who cooked meals, but you actually have unexpected talents?"
"...I won't make dinner tonight."
"Hahaha. I'm joking, joking."
"I'm not joking."
"...I was wrong! Please forgive me!"

Son O-cheon, who often receives demerits for his violent behavior, can't feel secure no matter how many points he has.

"Anyway, since it's the weekend and we have some free time, want to look for the library together?"
"Isabel, are you looking for that too?"
"I heard that starting next week, we'll be flooded with an alarming amount of assignments. I don't think we'll have the luxury of wandering around looking for a library we don't even know the location of."

That's true.

"Isabel, are you the type who values the process or the result?"
"Suddenly? If I had to choose one, the result is more important."
"Really? I thought exploration teams would value the process of going on adventures to find treasures and overcoming trials and hardships with companions."
"If the treasure's already been taken after all that suffering, we starve."
"Ah."

Survival is a major concern.
How pitiful would it be to go through all that hardship and not make any money, not even enough to complete your collection?
Above all, this is a world where monsters exist.
Once you leave the village, there are goblins shooting poison needles in the mountains, and wild animals have leveled up at least one size due to survival competition with such monsters.
Civilians find it difficult even to visit other villages, and the food they eat is always the same food from their own neighborhood.

'That's why when someone catches a pig and butchers it, the village holds a festival, or people cheer as if a golden bell has rung when someone pays for others' drinks at the tavern.'

In an era where survival is difficult, there's no beauty in the process.
So I decided to share the result first.

"The library."
"Yes?"
"If you order books from the shop owner in the corner of the main building's first floor, he'll lend them to you."
"What??"
"Then you don't have to look for the library."
"No, no, no. Wait."

Isabel staggered and leaned against the wall.

"Why would a shop lend books?"
"There's a Western food section in the shop, right?"
"I don't know why there would be such a thing in a shop, but... so?"
"Books are food for thought!"
"...Really? Because of that?"
"If you don't believe me, go see for yourself."

That's the response I get even after telling her.

"It's 1 point per day. If you return the book by the deadline, you get your points back. If you miss the deadline, you'll be deducted 10 times the points per day, so make sure to return it on time."

Isabel couldn't hide her bewilderment as she held the textbook in her hand.
A shop selling Western food meal kits that automatically cook when placed on a mana board.
Students sitting and eating hamburger steak meal kits or shrimp risotto meal kits paid no attention to her as she awkwardly walked past them with her book.

But if all you need is to borrow books for professor-assigned tasks, using just this place would be more than enough.
