# 253 - The Hero Who Can Never Be Defeated

Kasia knows.
She knows that the Gift Academy, known as the world's finest educational institution and considered the representative educational institution of the Advanced Type, actually "purchased" test subjects from the research facility.

"Instructor, I want to go to the store."
"Denied. If you wish to engage in personal activities outside the curriculum, use the nighttime hours when students aren't around."
"It's just the store."
"Even at 'just the store,' you possess the ability to electrocute and kill every student who sets foot on the same ground as you. You still lack credibility."

Her dedicated instructor Alexo, who monitors her every move, is equipped with electrical resistance technology, counter techniques, and defensive artifacts.
He even has the right to execute her on the spot in case of emergency, legally certified that killing her would not constitute a crime.
While she suffered physical pain at the research facility, at the Academy she suffers mental anguish—seeing free students before her eyes while constantly being reminded that she is different from them.

'They're no different from the research facility. Only their methods of inflicting pain differ.'

In other words, the Gift Academy, which everyone considers an Advanced Type educational institution, is by no means "advanced" to her.
This shattered Kasia's preconceptions.
She evaluated Ishtar, the current hero chosen by the only god "Sophemia of the Sun" and representative of the Advanced Type, not by his position but by his actions.

'A child like me. A hero who antagonizes Oknodie, hated by everyone.'

She understood that the hero's condition for tormenting test subjects, like the research facility or Academy, was "Dark Mana."
So what's different?
She too suffers greatly because she possesses a special "electric attribute" different from others.
Different attributes, different properties.
None of that means anything.
Those who are discriminated against are all the same minority.
Kasia saw her former self in Oknodie trapped in the Holy Mirror.
Her younger self, trapped behind the cold walls of the laboratory, touching the walls with eyes mixed with fear and curiosity.
It didn't take long for that curiosity to turn into terror once she realized those walls constantly emitted electricity and were a means to inflict pain on her.
And Oknodie would be the same.

"That's problematic."
"You... should have been a safe person without Dark Mana?"
"Enemies aren't distinguished by whether they possess Dark Mana or not. If you're going to torment Oknodie... then you're my enemy."

So she broke the mirror.


* * *


The hero laughed.
As if unable to contain his laughter.
Like someone who had lost his mind.

"This is truly amazing. I don't know how I'm so blessed with good people. Time and again, without getting tired, things keep appearing to block my way—it's almost admirable."

The hero's atmosphere was ominous.
Judging by his demeanor, it seemed like he might use some Dark Mana rampage technique.

"Be careful, Kasia!"
"That's my line?"

The hero spoke.

"Ah, forget it. I don't care anymore."
"...!"
"Whether someone interferes or gets in the way, I'll just clear them all out together."

Ishtar's holy sword once again emitted light.
Its intensity was at least twice as powerful as when he activated the Holy Mirror.

<Fatal Technique - Tempest Blade>
<Fatal Technique - Seven Vortex>
<Fatal Technique - Horizon Demolisher>

Fatal techniques added one after another toward the mirror.
Simultaneously, the sword strikes previously charged in the mirror were all discharged at once.

<13 Stack Amplified Windstorm Strike>
<14 Stack Amplified Windstorm Strike>
<12 Stack Amplified Windstorm Strike>

Who said swordsmen can't perform ranged attacks?
Newbie common sense was shattered as overpowered ranged attacks began to rain down.
Toward both me and Kasia!

'Can Kasia withstand this?'

This iteration's Kasia is incredibly strong.
If Arcadia went from jack-of-all-trades-master-of-none to jack-of-all-trades-master-of-all, then Kasia's electric ability random fluctuation gauge has maxed out to the far right.

"Hyaaah!"

<Ice Wall Creation>
<Steel Transformation>
<Sustain>
<Multi-Barrier>

The layered defensive walls she creates burst one after another against the fierce Windstorm Strikes.
Simultaneously, the natural mana rapidly depletes.
The Holy Mirror's absorption of surrounding natural mana to discharge as Windstorm Strikes is several times stronger than the wand's mana absorption.
A hero who can never be defeated as a first-year.
Now I truly understand why veteran players evaluate him that way.

"Ugh. This hurts my pride!"

While swordsman Oknodie could create afterimages with swoosh-swoosh movements and thrust skillfully in various ways, I wanted magic-user Oknodie to show the dignity of an archmage raining down magic like bombardment.
But contrary to my personal hopes, I'm struggling just to defend against the hero's wave of attacks, and now I'm even running out of natural mana to draw upon for defensive barriers.

BOOM!

Still, I somehow managed to squeeze out enough mana to block the final Windstorm Strike.

"I'm not letting my guard down. If the first wave is over, I'll just continue with the second wave of attacks."

The Holy Mirror once again emitted light and began to discharge sword strikes.
While I was struggling to block the Windstorm Strikes, some mirrors had been passing stacks back and forth, and now fatal techniques with amplified power appeared on the mirror surfaces.

'Wow. He's awakened the most vicious version!'

That technique—in the game, you'd only use it when fighting a chapter boss in the summer vacation of your second year.
Specifically, the chapter boss of "Chapter 4: Silent Death, Forest of Silence"—Professor Sadako's pet tree, Poppy.
It blocks vocalization to prevent skill activation and incantation, inflicts poison and curse damage as you approach, and randomly adds various curses when hit—a crazy tree.

For mages, it's about silent casting; for swordsmen, it's about embodiment—a chapter where you fight with high proficiency to overcome skill activation constraints. But the hero transcends common sense.
Holy Mirror.
He floats numerous mirrors at a distance, pours sword strikes into them, and fires them out from mirrors near the boss.
If a player challenges alongside the hero, just protecting the mirrors is enough to pull your weight—the hero participation route!
Even the overpowered crazy tree Poppy (not dead yet) would die crying tears of blood against this unfair pattern—I can understand Poppy's bitter feelings firsthand.

"Everyone, break the mirrors!"

But this unfair pattern was blocked by comrades joining in.
Starting with Gisele's shout, everyone launched attacks on nearby mirrors.

<Dark Shadow Dagger Technique>

Zuang elegantly scattering multiple daggers.

<Hand Axe Throw>

Hestia throwing hand axes to shatter mirrors.

<Throw>
<Throw>

Isabel, Son O-cheon, Dorothy, and others diligently throwing stones, ice fragments, and even dodgeballs.

"Ugh. Why don't our attacks break the mirrors in one hit?"
"Because there's a barrier field. You'd know if you took the Eye Training lecture. Even when cooking, you get a sense of proportion."
"What does cooking have to do with this?! Besides, Hestia breaks one mirror at a time with a crash! Zuang also creates lots of cracks with each hit."
"What obvious nonsense. It's because we're weaker compared to Little Mouse's dangerous friends."

The attacks from the intact mirrors were dispersed among everyone who was helping, despite their grumbling.

"Eek!"
"Ack!"
"Ugh."

First-year students falling out one after another with screams.
Advanced class students who held out until the end and hidden aces with skills too good for the lower classes collapsed in succession.
Thanks to everyone's sacrifice, the attacks directed at me weakened—now was my chance.

'Hmph. Since the mirrors have stolen all the natural mana around me, you think you don't need to worry about my attacks?'

I can read the calculation clearly.
The hero made a rational judgment.
But not a wise one.

<Rampage Technique>

If there's no natural mana, use the inherent mana stored in my body.
Isn't that common sense?

"The luxury of using sword and magic separately ends here."

I picked up Isabel's longsword that had been thrown my way with Son O-cheon's help as I rolled across the floor to dodge the hero's counterattack.
A weapon thrown this way just before she was eliminated.
Sword in one hand, wand in the other.
With full equipment equipped, my fighting spirit rises.
The kindness of these newbies who helped me despite giving up precious points is honestly touching.
This is why I've repeated this game countless times.
Though it has many unfair elements and heavily depends on luck.
The innocent hearts of the main and supporting NPCs living within it move people's hearts.

"This is enough resistance."
"No. Not yet, I can't fall like this...! When Oknodie helped me without any conditions, when she needs help, I must do something... ugh!"

In the chaos, Jaku, not wanting attention, pretended to block an attack while stepping on a line to get eliminated, while the diligently training Mob, wanting to test his limits, took sword strikes until his helmet turned backward, got hit about thirty times, and fainted.

"Kuk, have Oknodie and Kasia been enduring attacks like this all along?"
"...The numbers don't add up. If this were an assassination request, this ridiculous difficulty level would be considered falling into a trap."

Even Hestia and Zuang were starting to struggle with the onslaught.
Meanwhile, Ishtar's expression wasn't good.
The mirrors were being consumed faster than expected.

"...Someone's been secretly meddling."
"Gyahahat! Took you long enough to notice?"

Focusing his mind, Ishtar's perception caught the culprit.
Taking advantage of the confusion, the death pirate Jigoku had been silently casting soundless magic and secretly shooting to break mirrors!
With Jigoku eliminated, the named characters in the upper ranks of power fell one after another.
It was truly a situation where the hero alone was crushing the entire first year.
However, Ishtar's mistake was not dealing with them quickly enough.
Finally, Oknodie completed charging the Dark Mana rampage technique to a satisfactory level.

"You wicked hero, I'll teach you a lesson!"

A fatal ball beyond the rainbow ball's three-level spectrum.
The level four dodgeball, pitch-black with dark energy, moved forward slowly.
