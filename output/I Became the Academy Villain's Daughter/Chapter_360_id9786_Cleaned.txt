# Chapter 360: The Professor's Hospitality

The lower-class students, now freed from the ghost, expressed their gratitude enthusiastically.

"Thank you so much. Oknodie, if it weren't for you, we'd still be tormented by that ghost!"
"What were you experiencing in there?"
"It made us relive the most horrific experiences of our lives. It was truly traumatic!"
"That ghost bastard, having its limbs cut off was too merciful. We should have sliced it into sashimi."
"And what made it twice as terrifying was that the Skeleton Instructor kept tempting us, saying we could escape safely if we became skeletons!"
"...I, I was just trying to help in my own way."

Jaku selectively expressed outrage while ignoring the Skeleton Instructor.
Just imagining repeating the hell of his childhood—wandering battlefields and scavenging military rations—made him shudder. Yet these students had been forced to relive such terrible pasts.
Thinking that his close friend Mob must have experienced something similar made him feel quite sick.

"You all have strong minds. It's impressive how your expressions haven't crumbled after experiencing such horrors."

Perhaps it was because they were skilled enough to be accepted into the world's finest educational institution, despite being in the lower class?
Although they had grown somewhat pale, their spirits weren't greatly damaged, they could hold a conversation, and both their bodies and minds were relatively intact.

"What do you mean we look fine? We feel terrible inside!"
"My stomach is still churning."
"Do you have any idea how it feels to personally experience the horrifying sight of rice mixed with sweet and sour pork sauce?"

...These guys, was that weird food combination really the most horrific experience of their lives?

'They've lived such enviably easy lives.'

Since this wasn't something to say to hostages who had just escaped, Jaku turned the conversation to Mob.

"Mob, did you also experience eating rice mixed with sweet and sour sauce?"
"No. I saw Oknodie abandoning me, saying it was a waste of time to spend on a dull-witted dunce like you."
"That's certainly a nightmare befitting you..."
"After that, the Chairman appeared and turned Oknodie into a demon lord who slaughtered humans like insects, and I had to watch helplessly. Ugh... if only I had more strength, Oknodie wouldn't have become such a cruel child. She could have walked a more righteous path...!"

What kind of failed hero's dream did this guy have?
Unlike the absurdity of these two, the Skeleton Instructor got up quite normally and approached Oknodie.

"So what do you plan to do with this ghost now?"
"I'm taking it with me, of course! That was my intention from the beginning."
"Hooh. As expected of a student that Professor Sadako has her eye on. Can you recognize ghosts with high necromantic value?"

Can capturing a ghost for copying purposes also be considered of necromantic value?
Oknodie pondered briefly, then stopped pondering.
She was going to capture it anyway, so what did it matter?

Stealthily.
Rolling.

While everyone else was focused on the hostages and Oknodie was busy chattering, the hook ghost rolled its body through the gap in the open space.
This human is terrifying.
I've never encountered an item with a curse so severe that I couldn't copy it.
I'll be killed.
I need to escape from here right now.
As its back filled with desperation, Oknodie's small foot stepped firmly on it.

"Kweek"

With the child's surprisingly immense strength, the hook ghost was completely immobilized.

"I said I'd lift the curse, but who said you could escape? I have so many things for you to copy. I absolutely can't let you go!"

Keeeing.
The ghost made an adorable sound like a cornered animal, begging for mercy.
Having nearly died just from attempting one copy, how could it possibly survive having to copy such things repeatedly?

"Pfft. A veteran ghost wouldn't bother creating something like a strategy guide separately. The truly important information is all memorized in the body, right? This was a trap made for cursing!"

Fortunately, Oknodie wasn't planning to inflict such cruel torture.

"First, please copy Mob's armor! You were already making plenty of them, right?"

Mob's armor has considerable value.
As an item's value increases, so does its value as a cursed item, making it desirable even to ghosts.

Thud.

Seeing the cursed armor that popped out from the other dimension, Mob sensed an instinctive foreboding.

"O-Oknodie. What are you planning to do with the copied armor?"
"Hmm? When you have two identical armors, you obviously need to enhance them!"
"I was just starting to get used to this one!"
"That makes it even better! Just as you're adapting to the weight, increasing the intensity creates a more delicious burden on your body. And once you start learning the Mana Cultivation Method, you'll endure it even better."
"But then I'll have to constantly use the Mana Cultivation Method to endure it!"
"Then you'll just have to use it constantly!"

As Mob looked so depressed he seemed ready to dig a hole and crawl into it, Jaku approached him and whispered in his ear.

"Didn't you say it yourself? What would happen to Oknodie if you don't grow stronger."
"!"
"It's up to you."

Mob gritted his teeth and nodded.

"I'm ready. Enhance it."

Was Mob's determination unexpected?
The child who had been so intimidating toward the ghost approached with sparkling eyes and grabbed his hand firmly.
With flushed cheeks, Oknodie vigorously shook his hand up and down and exclaimed:

"Wonderful! For a mere lower-class extra who might not even exist in the original work to show such enthusiasm—honestly, I'm moved! Whether it's musical instruments, exercise, or training, the beginner phase is always the hardest, but you're following along so diligently that it really makes teaching worthwhile!"
"Y-you're too close, Oknodie."

Unlike Mob, who was uncomfortable with the proximity, Oknodie moved even closer and pulled his collar with her hand.
As he lowered his posture and bowed his head, seemingly pushed by her strength, Oknodie cupped her hands around his ear and whispered:

"They say when cursed items are enhanced, they prevent other cursed items from being worn simultaneously. That might automatically remove the bikini armor too!"
"...!!"
"Hehe. So keep working hard with your training. I may not be as good as Production Department's Professor Ferguson, but I do know how to enhance items, so I'll give you a +1 enhancement for free!"

Mob felt relief but also realized something terrifying.
If the hook ghost could copy cursed items, wouldn't it copy not only the armor but also the bikini armor cursed with equipment removal?
Looking closely, he could see several bikini armors buried among the mountain of copied items from the other dimension.

'I'm truly thankful it wasn't discovered!'

As embarrassed as he was by the bikini armor pressing against his skin under his regular armor, Mob gained a different kind of desperation.
He was determined to train hard and develop a physical condition that could withstand high-level weighted cursed armor and automatically remove the bikini armor!


* * *


While the event with Mob, Jaku, and the lower-class students ended with their rescue, my event was just beginning.

"I'd like to negotiate with Professor Sadako. Would you come with me, Instructor?"
"Gegelgel. Just say the word if you want to become a skeleton while you're at it."

There's no such "while you're at it," Instructor.

"Is this about the ghost?"
"Yes. I need to use the copier sparingly so it lasts a long time."
"Professor Sadako is indeed an expert in this field."

Given that she raises cursed pet trees, is there anything about curses that the professor doesn't know?
To prevent the ghost—cursed with a spell she knew how to cast but not how to remove—from dying outright, she needed the professor's help immediately.
Was a mythic-grade curse too strong?
Next time, she should lower the grade to a legendary-level curse.

"It's here."

The place the Skeleton Instructor guided her to was a massive underground cemetery located in a deep pit even more remote than the previous clearing, where no sunlight reached—the Catacombs, where the bodies of students, professors, and staff who had died at the Academy were buried, along with various monsters and heroes' corpses procured from different regions for practical training.

Thud. Thud. Thud.

As soon as the Skeleton Instructor infused his magic into the wall torches, all the doors of the catacombs opened simultaneously.
Dozens of layers of doors opened at once, revealing tombs containing bodies from low-access areas to high-access areas—a truly overwhelming sight.

Whooooosh.

From the depths beyond the doors, where even darkness feared to tread, came an eerie wind along with the presence of the dead that any living being would instinctively avoid.

"Oh. Our professor is in quite good condition. Allowing access all the way to the 38th gate of the catacombs—you should know you're very lucky."
"What happens if the professor is in bad condition?"
"At the very least, I wouldn't go in there."
"..."

I might be a little scared to go in there?

"Don't be such a coward. She only sucks the vitality from the living instead of consuming the spirits of the dead. Gegelgel!"
"But I'm alive! I have tons of vitality!"

As they were having this foolish conversation, the entrance door began to shake violently on its own.

"Is the professor urging me to come in quickly now?"
"I suppose so? If it were me, I'd wait about 60 more seconds."
"Why?"
"Because I'd gain another skeleton colleague."

It's scarier not to go in!
Before Professor Sadako became angrier, she hurriedly ran into the catacombs.
