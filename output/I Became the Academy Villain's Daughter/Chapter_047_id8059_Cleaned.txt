# <47 - How a Veteran Player Chooses Classes>

Though Mahabharata had a bad personality, he properly conveyed the information students needed to know.

"From now on, you'll be taking 'Common Courses,' 'Major Courses,' and 'Elective Courses' while attending the Academy."
"Common Courses are mandatory for all students with no freedom of choice."
"For Major Courses, you must select and take courses within your chosen department to fulfill the required credits."
"Elective Courses are classes you can freely choose according to your interests. They're purely for broadening your knowledge. Some students take them with the impure intention of converting credits to points, but I sincerely hope you'll take them out of pure academic passion."

Isabel scribbled notes and showed me her page.

Don't the electives look good? You can earn points from them.

I looked at Isabel with the eyes of someone watching a graduate student making poor choices, expressing sympathy for her upcoming Academy life.

It'll be tough for you later.
If I were you, I wouldn't take extra courses just to earn points.

Why?

The deductions you receive during class might be greater than the points you earn from the course.

...Really?
Do you think so?

I've given my advice.
Whether you listen or not is up to you.

"Major Courses differ by department. First..."

The Academy is divided into five departments.
Knight Department. Magic Department. Administration Department. Adventure Department. Production Department.
Knights. Wizards. Administrators. Adventurers. Producers.
Each department aims to nurture different classes.
Delving deeper, there are military knights who roam battlefields, domain knights employed by lords, royal knights employed by the royal family, guardian knights who protect specific races, and so on.
Depending on the goal, even the same profession can have different titles and required skills.

'Those classes are different from the world-class talents the Gift Academy aims to produce.'

The Academy still needs support.
That's necessary for its operation.
Most of these minor classes were created for show, catering to the various nations' expectations.
...At least that's what I knew from the game.
Based on what the Dragon Principal said, I can't shake the feeling that these are just inferior courses sold for money, designed for underachievers.

'The difference between lower and higher professions.'

Regardless of the Principal's comments, I need to understand that the seeds of success and the rest are separated from this course registration.
The Academy's ideal talent.
To graduate with top grades, one must obtain a special class and become a disciple who inherits the professors' world-class knowledge.
A true "Knight" who defines their own taboos and acts with righteousness is fundamentally different from a professional knight who merely hones combat techniques.
The same applies whether you choose to be a wizard, administrator, producer, or adventurer.
In short, if you only aim for easy credits and simple courses during registration, your future becomes uncertain.

"I'll give you an hour to think about which courses to take. Please consider the course plans posted on the magic watch's registration board."

Isabel asked me.

"Which department are you going to join, Oknodie?"
"What about you, unni?"
"That's not even a question. Adventure Department."
"Ah, right. You were an adventurer."
"Let's do it together. I think Oknodie would make an excellent adventurer."

I think so too.
If I, who knows many of this world's secrets, became an adventurer, just imagine how many treasures, dungeons, mysteries, and dangers I could discover.
The wealth and honor that would come with it makes my heart race just thinking about it, but there's a problem.

"I'm also considering the Knight Department."
"Knight Department? Ah, you did handle the sword well in the last battle. You're skilled with swords. You must want to learn swordsmanship, right?"
"Huh? Not really."

Think about it.
Female knight in form-fitting armor VS adventurer in the standard, completely non-revealing adventurer outfit.
Just looking at the balance, the former is overwhelmingly better.
How could I bear classes where my classmates are female knights?

"So you're going to abandon me?"
"...Ugh. Don't look at me like that. We can still meet in other classes."

When persuasion failed, Isabel changed tactics.

"The Knight Department would be too much for someone of your height. They probably won't even have armor that fits you."
"I might grow 30cm by next year!"
"No, you definitely won't."
"I will!"

The provocation naturally backfired.
I'm not taking the same classes as Isabel!

On the way to course registration, I held hands with Isabel while munching on the berries she gave me.
The corridor was becoming a chaotic scene with other students rushing to register.

"Hahaha! Out of my way, you little mice!"
"Waaaah!"
"That brainless monkey is ramming into everyone!"

Son O-cheon, the monkey-human hybrid acting like a human tank, charged straight toward the classroom.

"Gyahaha! Anyone who wants to get shot, come forward! I'll shoot anyone faster than me!"
"Aaaah, she's crazy!"
"W-wait! I'm waiting for you to go ahead quickly, why are you running toward me?!"
"No one's standing in front of me so I can't shoot anyone! Come forward quickly. And die!!"

The outrageous pirate Jigoku, running backward down the corridor, chasing fleeing students.

"That's impressive. Should we hurry too?"
"This pace is just fine. I don't want to get caught by the instructors and lose points."

We probably wouldn't receive as severe deductions as during the entrance ceremony, but causing a disturbance in the corridor could result in point deductions between 10 and 100 depending on severity.
Like getting soaked in drizzle without noticing, if you run around recklessly, you might experience the feeling of being caught by a speed camera and receiving a fine bomb.

"Is it okay to make your first course registration for adventure courses?"
"It doesn't matter. I'm not particularly interested in high-competition courses."

What concerns me more is Gisele following us in the same direction.

"Mr. Gisele, why are you following us to the adventure courses? As a black market merchant, wouldn't it be better to take merchant classes in the Production Department?"
"Hoho. Oknodie, you're still a child who doesn't understand. If you give desperate adventurers in the field a chance to quickly lighten their heavy loads, you can easily make over 1000% profit. Learning how adventurers think is even more valuable for merchants."
"...Are you a demon?"

So this is the kind of person who appears in games selling consumables at ridiculously high prices and buying loot for cheap!
Running around as a ticket black market merchant during ticket season and as a field merchant at other times.
What a diligent minor villain.

"This is the Adventure Department section."
"There's a guide sign over there."

<Adventure Department>

The long-awaited Adventure Department.
The available course list was as follows:

<Basic Physical Training for Adventurers>
<Basic Weapon Training for Adventurers>
<Adventurer Supply Studies>
<Adventurer Trap Studies>
<Adventurer Wilderness Cooking>
<Developing an Eye for Value>

Do you sense the ruthlessly cold reality?
That's right.
None of these include anything like Dungeon Exploration Studies.
It would be problematic if such a course existed in the first-year, first-semester basic curriculum.
If you really want to take it, there is a workaround.

'If you skip grades, you can take second-year courses. As long as you have enough points and credits, you can apply for grade advancement.'

You take classes with seniors a year above you.
It's a feature occasionally used by players aiming for speed runs or special strategies to unlock character-specific events or achievements.
Of course, as a veteran player, I've done it before, but I don't particularly want to do it now.

'It's not like humanity is facing an imminent crisis, and this playthrough has been quite fortunate overall.'

No national disaster-level events have occurred in the four frontier regions.
Without failing at disaster-level events, the apocalyptic world event <Imperial Invasion> doesn't trigger.
There's no <Hero of National Salvation> event that occurs when the Empire faces a crisis, nor the <Hero of Chaotic Times> event that takes place in the shattered west.
There's no need to rush through a time attack only to experience a depressing Academy life where "I didn't get an A-rank in today's class. Because of my stupid incompetence, tens of thousands of local residents will die."

'If I had entered the Academy during the war generation, I would have really experienced that, so I should be grateful.'

In short, this playthrough's continent is on the peace route.
There are threats, but humanity isn't particularly in a precarious situation, and there's no approaching enormous threat—it's a world of <status quo>.
In such a world, combat techniques don't develop much, but cultural development takes precedence.

'The trending technologies seem to be divine-related studies that lead imperial culture.'

I can roughly recall the related course list.

<Understanding the One True God of the Holy Central Empire>
<Understanding the 12 Great Gods>
<Understanding the Minor Deities of the East>
<Introduction to Non-mainstream Divine Arts>
<Using Divine Arts Without Faith>

There were some surprisingly interesting courses.
The last one was particularly so.

-If your physical abilities are exceptional, you can safely land from a height of 20m without the power of faith.
-What? You don't have such physical abilities? But you still want credits?
-Then believe in the god Mark and convert to faith, using the power of <Iron Block> to land on the ground. For reference, novice priests of the Mark Warrior Order are guaranteed at least a B-rank.

Would you rather trust Mark and get a B-rank, or take the test of jumping from 20m without divine arts through extreme training and technique?
Students who took this extremely manipulative course either tearfully became believers or firmly resolved never to convert to religion even if it meant breaking their legs, ending up in the medical ward.

"Hmm. A course on handling divine arts without divine power. That's intriguing."
"Grand Duke Andersen is going to take <Using Divine Arts Without Faith>!"
"Let's take it too!"

I only feel sorry for the freshmen who follow the wrong choice of the Western Noble Union's number one like moths to a flame.

"Oknodie. I've already chosen."
"Ah, the same as me!"

Isabel chose <Developing an Eye for Value>.
Compared to Grand Duke Andersen's choice, this course is a million times better.

"I chose this because I've already learned the other things through my adventurer life, but why did you choose <Developing an Eye for Value>? I thought you'd take the cooking course."
"I like eating food, not making it."

Of course, there's another proper reason.

"How unusual. Freshmen coming to take this course. Did you come knowing it's difficult to earn credits in a class taken simultaneously by first and second-year students?"

Leaning against the podium in the classroom was a female professor in a white suit with a silk hat, standing askew.
Her impressive chest, visible above her crossed arms, captured attention.
Despite giving the stingiest grades, having the most difficult lectures, and being located in the farthest corner of the Adventure Department, the reason I came here is right there.

Anyway, I already know everything from all the courses.
For a veteran player, the first criterion for choosing courses isn't easy credits, appropriate time distribution, or professors who teach well.

Difficult to earn credits.
Terrible schedule.
Even if the teaching isn't good, that's fine.

'I can't resist a D-cup glamorous female professor in a white suit.'

For a veteran player, everything is forgiven as long as the professor is sexy.
