# 121 - Changed Attitudes Around Me

Today, people's attitudes toward me suddenly became strange.

"Oknodie, you poor thing! Someone like you should save this whole wheat bread for when your points drop and you're starving!"

"Are you crazy? Why are you showing kindness to a corpse carrier? You might end up buried in a cemetery yourself!"

Suddenly, unknown students would approach to share food with me, while others would appear to stop them.

I caught Mob passing by in the hallway and asked him.

"Mob, why are people calling me a corpse carrier?"

Mob, who had been drinking water, spat it out violently and coughed.

"Who's saying that?"
"People passing by."
"I'll look into it after class."
"You don't need to go that far."
"No, I'm concerned about it."

Since he insisted on doing it, I let him be. He came back in the evening looking gaunt, as if he hadn't eaten dinner at all.

"Oknodie. There are strange rumors spreading about you. I'm just telling you what I heard, so please don't misunderstand."
"Okay!"
"People think you... have high dark attribute affinity because you've seen and handled many corpses. There's also a rumor gaining traction that you're a member of an assassin family."
"Ah, I see."

So that's what it was!
I thought something was strange.
I nodded as my questions were finally answered, but Mob still looked serious, as if nothing had been resolved, and cautiously asked:

"So... is it true?"
"What is?"
"The rumor that your dark attribute affinity increased because you carried corpses and worked as an assassin."

Well, I'm not sure.
If this were a game, I could firmly deny such rumors, but since this is reality, it's a bit confusing.
The deaths I experienced and the murders I committed as a player in this game weren't just one or two—I've even experienced "world destruction" at its worst.
If that were calculated as "my" affinity, my dark attribute affinity would be maxed out and then some.
But could the same result be possible if calculated as "Oknodie's" affinity?
Actually, what kind of character was Oknodie?
I don't know much about this iteration's character background.
I haven't obtained any information about my character's past since the Random Papa event.

"Hmm... I'm not sure!"
"Why are you saying you don't know? It's your life. Can't you clearly say it's not true?"
"But I'm confused!"
"About what?"
"Whether it's because of what I killed before or what I've seen this time."

Mob looked shocked as if he'd glimpsed a hidden truth about the world, then glanced around.
There was no one nearby who might be passing by or listening to our conversation.

"Oknodie. Don't say things like that anywhere else."
"Why?"
"Just don't say it, okay?!"
"Hmm... I don't want to!"
"You'll be in danger!"
"Me?"
"The kids are afraid of you. If they hear talk like that, they'll be even more scared. They might even tell their parents and ask for you to be expelled!"

His worry is excessive.
This is a country where even if a king requested the expulsion of a student, they would never comply.
In fact, the powerless Third Princess Yayoi entered the Academy with just one body double and no other support, so she might later be summoned by the Emperor when discovered.
Of course, the princess ignores this and stays put, while the Emperor schemes to take her away.
If I participate and play a role in that, the rewards are quite good, so should I manage my relationship with her?

"Oknodie. This is your life—think about it seriously!"
"Hmm... unless Papa calls for me, I won't be expelled!"
"Papa...? Oknodie's father?"
"Yes!"
"This Papa you're talking about is your real father, not someone who trained you as an assassin, right?"
"Yes, yes!"

In the Random Papa event, only my dad sends letters! So it must be my dad.

"Sigh... I don't understand what's going on. Anyway, be careful. Don't get hurt if the kids' attitudes change a lot."
"Mob, you're such a nice kid?"
"W-what are you saying! It's only natural to repay kindness, take care of children, and be kind to women!"
"Pong-pong?"
"Pong... what?"
"Nothing!"

I'm a bit worried about Mob's values—he's too soft-hearted despite not being particularly handsome.
He should be worried about himself, not me.
Mob was looking at me suspiciously, but when Zuang and Titosoga, who attend the next lecture with him, approached, he ran off with a final warning not to forget his advice.

"What's with him?"
"Could he be Oknodie's boyfriend~?"
"He's just a nice kid!"

Titosoga snickered as Mob ran away and tripped flat on his face in the hallway.

"He's cute!"
"Want me to introduce you?"
"Oh, stop it. No thanks!"

Right, why should I care how others react?
As long as the people close to me stay the same, that's all that matters!


* * *


After carefully observing Oknodie's usual expressions, actions, and attitude—her every move—Zuang came to a conclusion.

"Well, I worried for nothing."

It was natural for an exposed assassin to be feared or become an object of hatred and contempt.
Oknodie was already the subject of too much attention.
Zuang had thought that something like this would happen eventually and that the attention would be too much for a child to handle, but surprisingly, her attitude remained unchanged.
The only difference was one thing:
When she encountered people who feared or cursed her in the hallway, she would awkwardly turn away or take a different path to avoid meeting them.

"Don't you feel sorry for Oknodie?"
"Not particularly. It's something all assassins must endure."
"Is that so...? I'm not sure."
"That's because Titosoga isn't an assassin."

Titosoga, who wasn't an assassin but somehow ended up befriending two of them.
Suddenly, Zuang became curious.

"Aren't you scared?"
"Of what?"
"Of us."
"Oknodie and Zuang?"

Zuang blinked.
After a moment's thought, what appeared in Titosoga's eyes wasn't fear or disgust, but trust.

"Not at all!"
"Really?"
"You're much better friends than those disloyal students from the Aesthetic Appreciation class."
"You're a strange kid, Titosoga."
"Pfft. You're calling me a kid, Zuang? You're the third smallest child in the Academy."

What amazing courage—to trust and befriend assassins despite being such a coward that she faints screaming at the sight of undead.
What an extraordinary child.

"Titosoga. Don't ever change."
"Suddenly? Did something happen?"
"Not really."

If something happened, it happened long ago and ended.
The all-too-common story of an assassin: a child who became friends without knowing they were an assassin, then became a stranger upon discovering their identity.
A fate that her master couldn't escape, that she couldn't escape, and perhaps that her future apprentice wouldn't be able to escape either.
Once trained as an assassin, Oknodie couldn't avoid such a fate either.

'You're lucky, Oknodie.'

That she wouldn't have to suffer the wounds Zuang had suffered at her age.
No, perhaps that was more unfortunate.
If someone is going to betray you anyway, becoming strangers earlier would hurt less.

"Titosoga."
"Yes?"
"If you betray us, I'll kill you."
"What did I do?!"
"I'll kill you anyway."
"Hmph. If Zuang betrays us, I'll kill you too!"

I hope you'll remain Oknodie's good friend from now on.
Zuang sincerely wished this.
Because killing someone who was once a friend was not something anyone should have to do.

The magic I learned with high hopes to drive away mosquitoes unfortunately wasn't much help.
Dark magic was too dangerous to use carelessly before building up proficiency.
Even as a player, I had to be extremely careful with dark magic that could have different side effects each time, and venturing outdoors where mosquitoes dominated was quite frightening.

"Professor Sadako, could you please cast the pest-repelling magic circle for our return journey too?"
"Are you interested in black magic?"
"Not really?"
"How about handling undead?"
"Not particularly?"
"Find your own way back then."

Professor Sadako coldly sent the students away.

"So you know what happened? Titosoga was frantically pressing buttons to change the colors of the spotlight to drive away the mosquitoes swarming around it, but it just attracted more of them!"
"Ngae."

After much struggle, I returned to the dormitory and was telling the day's events, but the baby mandrake's response was lackluster.

"If you keep doing that, I'll bully you, okay?"
"Ngae."

As if saying "do your worst," the mandrake dipped its roots to hold a drop of water, then splashed it out with a flick.

"Tsk! You're going to get it!"

<Amplify>

I didn't want to cast Amplify on the mandrake concentrate, so I filled a cup with water and cast it there instead.
The water in the cup instantly turned black and began to bubble and boil.
The mandrake, terrified, pressed itself against the wall of its bottle, trembling.

"Hiccup."

What am I doing, threatening something that can't even understand me?

"I'm sorry. Were you scared?"

Feeling a sudden wave of self-awareness, I discarded the water and apologized.
After gently rocking the frightened mandrake to sleep, I lay down on my bed.

'Am I getting stressed?'

When I faced the Esonia Exploration Team including Isabel during the entrance exam.
When I clashed with the still-neutral Western Nobles Alliance.
When I got into trouble with the Red Tooth Mushroom Illegal Cultivation Management Association composed of second-year seniors.
This situation was different from those times.
People avoid students with high dark attribute affinity who might be dangerous.
It's a tacitly accepted social custom.
So those who discriminate against and suspect me aren't doing anything wrong.
Bluntly put, once you're labeled as a "potential mass murderer," complaining about unfairness is pointless.
You have to feel like a criminal without having done anything.

It's the fate of those with high dark attribute affinity.
When my character was discriminated against, I just accepted it.
But when I experience it directly, my chest aches.

Of course, it doesn't feel good.

-Want to talk to me about it?

The answering door seized the opportunity to speak.
Creepy thing.
I glared at the wall once, then turned to the opposite side and pulled the blanket over my head.
