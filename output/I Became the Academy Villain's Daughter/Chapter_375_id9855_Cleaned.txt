<375 - Library Expedition Team 2>

The patrolling instructor would be helpless if more than a hundred first-years, mixed with upper-class students, rushed at them—that was Oknodie's theory!

Once the common understanding spread that "if caught, we can just beat them up and leave," the students' fear disappeared. Dorothy, who was leading these students, also had one less worry and could guide them without burden. Zuang was impressed by the positive effect of this crude solution.

'I guess even assassins need to increase their strength.'

With enough strength, one wouldn't need to contort their body to perform difficult assassinations. A small but significant change began to sprout in Zuang's growth build.

"So what's all this enormous amount of supplies for?"
"Expedition supplies!"
"Are we going to war?"

Dorothy had several students pack enough food and water for 120 students to survive for a week if stranded, along with cave escape ropes, oil-soaked torches, weed-cutting daggers, multipurpose sticks, blankets, and more. The contents were packed so tightly that the lower-class students grimaced as soon as they put on their backpacks.

"It's heavy."
"Why is there so much stuff?"
"I used to be an adventurer outside, but even in my active days, I never prepared this thoroughly for A-rank missions."

Zuang inwardly sneered. Adventurers were beings whose existence was built on sacrificing human lives. These manual laborers, with their prevalent mindset of cutting corners, would never consistently pack supplies worth more than their daily wages. But now even these inferior beings were part of the organization, using their labor for Oknodie and hoping for some scraps to fall their way.

"Sing will be the supply captain who protects the lower-class students with heavy luggage!"
"Do I look like someone who would do such a bothersome task? Choose another position. I would gladly accept the job of cutting down instructors with my sword if they come at us."
"Sing, didn't you say you have an aptitude for being a shadow supply officer? Wouldn't it be good to try something that suits your aptitude?"
"I detest meaningless tasks."

Despite Sing's cold words, Dorothy responded confidently.

"You need to practice protecting people to improve your skills! Don't you want to protect Oknodie?"
"Remember this clearly. I'm only playing along with your little game this one time."

At Sing's chilling gaze, Rockfell, the high-speed swordsman who always stood by Dorothy, reflexively placed his hand on his sword hilt.

"That was truly terrifying killing intent."
"Ugh. I'm never asking him to do anything again. I've never met anyone as fierce as the growler from my neighborhood."
"Let's save the chitchat for later. Could you explain why you packed so much food and supplies? Everyone seems curious."

At Zuang's urging, Dorothy returned with a smiling face.

"Seniors said you never know what might happen in the Academy's unknown places. So I packed A-rank supplies assuming we might get stranded!"
"A week's worth of food? Isn't that excessive for a journey that would take at most two and a half days, from Friday evening to Monday morning?"
"What if we get caught in formations and illusions and drift into another dimension? Time could vanish completely! Actually, I wanted to pack enough for a month, but I held back because Oknodie is with us!"

Dorothy's prediction was that with Oknodie—known for her brutal class schedules—participating, they wouldn't spend such dangerous time!

"Are all your questions answered? Then let's go!"
"Where to?"
"To the store's back door!"

The Library Expedition Team departed.


* * *

Yui said:

"The store is the only channel through which books from the library are distributed. If we follow the trail backward from the store, we can reach the library."

With this simple but logical argument, Heathcliff's Divine Group's trust in Yui increased.

"So we're going to the store?"
"No. I heard a shortcut related to book distribution from the store assistant professor."

Book Club.
Deiphobus, the loyal retainer asking questions on behalf of the prince, was perplexed by the name written on Yui's map.

"Was there such a club? I've heard the names of many clubs and learned about dangerous ones, but I've never heard of a Book Club."
"First-years can't join it, and shouldn't even be encouraged to apply. The library is that dangerous, they said."

He began to regret whether seeking out such a place was a foolish choice, but the expedition team had already been assembled. Like riding a tiger, things had already progressed too far to back out now. If he tried to withdraw now, it wouldn't be Deiphobus himself who would be called a coward, but Prince Hector might be ridiculed by his retainers.

'I cannot tarnish the prince's dignity.'
"If we go to the Book Club, will they show us the next path?"
"Something like that. They said they would lend us the path the seniors use. We paid a hefty fee, but it's much better than throwing money at the wrong places."

The path to the library was beginning to appear, but even reaching the Book Club wasn't easy.

"Not that corridor. We need to go through here."

Yui pointed to a room labeled "Detention Room," which looked suspiciously dangerous, ignoring the perfectly normal path. The students were agitated, but Yui had her reasons for pointing to such a suspicious place.

"The corridor is monitored by instructors. If first-years set foot there, they'll be driven out immediately."
"Hmph. What would a woman merchant know? I'm no coward."

No sooner had one student suddenly rushed into the corridor than an iron stake flew from somewhere, hitting him and slamming him against the wall with a thud. The students, stifling even their hiccups from shock, nearly fainted when they saw an instructor appear like a ghost in the middle of the corridor and hoist the fallen student onto his shoulder.

'We must follow only the guided path!'

The inside of the detention room was as luxurious as a hotel, with one person lying on an expensive bed with shackles on their feet.

"Huh? Aren't you first-years?"
"Are you a senior?"

In this mutually surprised situation, the person lying on the bed was the first to regain composure.

"Wow. Not just one or two, but about thirty first-years wandering around at night? The 981st class really has great adventurous spirit."
"We are Hector's Divine Group, serving Prince Hector of Troy. May we have your introduction, senior?"
"976th class, Production Department, 3rd year, Tetrapos."

The students were surprised first by the fact that the other person was two years ahead of them, and then again by the fact that he had entered five classes before them.

"What on earth is such a senior doing here?"
"As you can see, I'm detained in the detention room. This is a room where one person must be detained for the rest to leave."
"Isn't that illegal?"
"If no one comes to rescue you, you can be released after a week. It's a minimal relief measure."

So they're not imprisoned forever.
The students were secretly relieved. They had begun to fear that in Gift Academy, it wouldn't be strange to find a few skeletons rolling around in the corner of a detention room.

"Well, detention room life isn't bad. At least here, food is free, and you can sleep comfortably. It's perfect as a hideout. I'm also hiding here temporarily from the student council's eyes."
"Student council...?"
"Oops. My tongue was too loose. I've told the soon-to-be-detained first-year friend various things to reassure them, but don't pay attention to the student council. There's nothing good for first-years to get involved with them."

The students were dumbfounded. Setting aside the student council, it seemed that one of them would definitely be detained.

"Why should one of us be detained? You're already detained here. You even have shackles on."
"Oh, these? They come off when there are two people in the room."

Click.
Tetrapos really unlocked his shackles. Seeing him free his hands and feet, Hector's Divine Group finally understood the situation and trembled in fear. This was... like sheep walking into a lion's den to escape wolves. And in a dead-end room with no escape route!

"So, what will you do? Want to fight me once and decide, or will you choose one person among yourselves to be detained? Either way is fine with me. I'm also curious about the skills of first-years."

The senior's confidence despite being outnumbered reminded them of the instructor who instantly took down a first-year with an iron stake. Deiphobus glared at Yui with resentful eyes.

"Did you have to guide us to such a place?"
"Don't look at me like that. Here, only one person needs to be sacrificed, but other places require more sacrifices."

In the end, Hector's Divine Group, excluding Hector, the loyal Deiphobus, and guide Yui, drew lots to select one person to be detained. The third-year senior Tetrapos personally put shackles on the first-year's hands and feet and said:

"Don't worry too much. There are also adult magazines under the bed."

This wasn't much consolation for the student whose grades were about to be destroyed.

Detention Room (10-person room).
Following Dorothy's guidance, they arrived at a room where ten people had to be left shackled to escape. Zuang thought Dorothy had made a major mistake, but Dorothy herself pulled out ten straw dolls from her backpack, which was twice as large as others'.

"Here, let's shackle these and go!"
"Dolls instead of people...? How did you prepare such things?"
"Huh? Doesn't everyone living in the forest have at least one path that requires offering sacrifices to pass? Having dolls to offer instead of people is common sense!"

At Dorothy's words, Oknodie also nodded and took out dolls from her backpack.

"Dorothy is right. The shabby dolls that seniors occasionally sell are meant for this purpose!"

Thanks to the thorough preparation of guide Dorothy and leader Oknodie, the organization playing with Oknodie was able to escape the ten times more dangerous 10-person detention room without a single loss.
