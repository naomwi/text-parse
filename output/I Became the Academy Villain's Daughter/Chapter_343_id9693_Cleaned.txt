From Professor Sadako and Professor Destroyer.

It had to be the work of Destroyer, who had once been active in the hero party led by the former generation hero <Nyarlathotep>, known as the strongest thief of his time.

"Fascinating."

Honestly, I never thought he would do something like this.
Why would Professor Destroyer support the Foundation?
But the existence of the answer sheet spoke for itself.
No matter how improbable the hypothesis seemed, this was the reality unfolding before me.

"Waaah."

Ignoring the baby mandrake who was oblivious to the intruder's presence and was strengthening its roots by poking them into the wall, I stopped flipping through the answer sheet.

'If they had stolen an answer sheet I created myself, I would have given full marks just to acknowledge their skill. But this...'

Was this truly Oknodie's own ability?
Or was it the handiwork of Professor Destroyer helping her?
The insight and wisdom to see through a professor's curriculum.
This was something that even a professor-level individual couldn't easily imitate unless they were a member of the former hero party.
The all-capable thief, Destroyer.
The possibility of this being his work had risen to the level of near certainty.

Of course, the handwriting was in the crooked baby-like script.
Unless a baby mandrake less than 10 years old had picked up a pen to write for her, this was undoubtedly Oknodie's handwriting.

This meant that even if questioned, Professor Destroyer had ample room to deny involvement.

"Was Oknodie's position more precarious than it appeared... Oh Primordial Heavenly Lord, why must this girl's fate be so tragic? Please bestow your compassion through merit."

"Isn't that a good thing?"
"It might have been seen that way if Professor Destroyer hadn't been recruited by the Foundation. But now that he has been recruited, the attack itself seems to have had a different intention."

Master Myungho completely reconstructed the incident.

"The Foundation was demonstrating that they could destroy anyone associated with Oknodie, regardless of their status, in an attempt to tame her."

"The only person who could commit such an act is the Foundation's chairman, Jonna Wiheomhae. Even the recent murder of a member of the Beast Revival Society, a Foundation subsidiary organization, must be seen as an extension of this effort to control Oknodie."

Professor Bronze reflected on her attitude.
The man she had despised as a "monk" had reached the truth through persistent observation and investigation, driven by deep concern for Oknodie.
A truth that she, as a vigilante, could have reached more quickly and accurately.
What kind of master was she to Oknodie?
As a teacher, her pride was wounded.

"I had no intention of developing shallow emotions like affection, but I should keep a closer eye on her. To prevent any more unsavory characters from circling around her."

I knew she had grown stronger since our boat trip, but to think she had even gotten one over on a hero.
Professor Bronze, secretly observing from a distance, was quite surprised by her disciple's actions.
But the real surprise began as she eavesdropped on the lecture.

"When former heroes set out on their heroic journey to destroy the evil plaguing the world, the first enemies they faced were insignificant monsters, but the first beings that could be called great evils were not monsters. Rather, they were humans."

Professor Destroyer taught using his past adventures as material.

Thirteen years ago, when the hero party returned to the capital after touring frontier villages and receiving appreciation plaques. Destroyer was talking about the first encounter with the three great evils that occurred twelve years ago.

"The guild leader was a merchant through and through. But our hero party leader, Nyarlathotep, couldn't miss the opportunity to earn funds for hero activities, so he displayed his merchant spirit and took on the request."

Nyarlathotep was truly an outrageous hero.

A small blue ticket.
