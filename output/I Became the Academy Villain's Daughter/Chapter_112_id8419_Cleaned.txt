# Graduating from the Academy by Luck

## 112 - The Way to School

"Take this─!"

I opened my eyes wide and shouted.
What's this?
As I blinked my eyes repeatedly, I realized.
I was talking in my sleep!
Feeling embarrassed, I naturally withdrew my fist as if I was just about to stretch and yawned, but I could feel an incredibly sharp gaze.
The baby Mandrake I had placed on one corner of my desk was looking at me pathetically.

"Aah, don't look at me like that! It's embarrassing!"
"Waah."

The Mandrake's gaze turned toward the wall.
I wondered what it was looking at, but it was staring at the fist-shaped dent in the wall.

Knock knock knock knock knock

The "Answering Door" living inside the wall knocked frantically to express its displeasure.

"Sorry..."

I don't know what kind of dream I had, but I vaguely feel like I was happily running wild, beating up this and that for the first time in a while.
Had stress been building up without me noticing?

"I'll call the janitor later and ask them to repair it. Really!"

Only after apologizing repeatedly did the knocking sound from the wall stop.
Really? It seemed to be asking suspiciously, so I decided to call as soon as possible.

Creak. Thud.

As I stepped into the hallway, the dolphin pants seniors who would normally be outside the main entrance breathing the morning air and preparing for exercise were staring blankly at the entrance.

"Hi, Oknodie."
"Oknodie, are you just waking up?"
"Look outside. It's totally insane?"

As the orange-haired senior said, the area outside the main entrance had become a sea.
The water was higher than the main entrance, and you could even see fish swimming in the current.
The heavy rain hadn't stopped and continued to pour, finally submerging part of the Academy.

"I guess jogging is out?"
"No way."
"If we go out now, we'll be deliciously eaten by a Giant King Crab?"

The senior girls aiming for the Knight department returned to their rooms with disappointed faces or began doing bodyweight exercises.

"Want to join us, Oknodie?"

The orange-haired senior asked as the others stretched their arms and legs.

"Hehe. I'd love to!"
"Then come over here."

She patted the waiting bench next to the main entrance, and I quickly sat down. Her delicate hand helped me get into a position with my legs stretched out.

"Now bend your waist and grab your toes with your fingertips! If you're not flexible enough, you can just lightly touch your toes, or..."
"Like this?"
"Wow! Oknodie, you're really flexible!"

When I was a muscle monster, I had given up on flexibility long ago because my body was too bulky, but now I have a baby body.
Instead of being a tank swordsman who takes all attacks head-on and breaks skulls, I'm aiming to be an evasion swordsman who deflects all attacks, dodges, and breaks skulls.
Expanding my range of motion and physical mobility through flexibility isn't a choice—it's a necessity.

"But I think you can go down a bit more? I'll press on your back. Tell me if it hurts!"

The orange-haired senior approached from behind and gently pressed down on my body, and I could smell a nice fragrance from her.
It was quite different from perfume or soap—a soft fragrance unique to the female body that made me smile involuntarily.
I even regretted not playing as a female character earlier, as this was a luxury I couldn't experience in the training room full of men.

'Job satisfaction 100%!'

My energy, which had been depleted after causing trouble with my sleep-talking, was fully recharged.

"Oknodie is amazing. She's not even aiming for the Knight department but comes out every morning without fail to exercise. I really want to bring her to our department!"
"I agree!"
"I wish my little sister I left at home was this energetic and well-behaved."

They laughed while patting my head, pinching my cheeks, and hugging me with their impressively soft chests, making me smile contentedly.

"Alright, I'm in a good mood! Follow me, seniors. I'll show you something good!"

The seniors chuckled, following along as if I was about to perform some cute trick or show them a special pretty pebble I had found yesterday.
Of course, I wasn't actually going to put on a show or show them pebbles.

"Do you see how the third-floor windows don't have curtains but do have curtain cords?"
"Yeah. It's really strange, right?"
"Right. The third-floor students asked the dorm supervisor to properly install curtains, but she just said no."
"But this is the lower-class dorm area. How does an upper-class student like Oknodie know about this place?"
"Are you here to warn us about the summer glare? How can you be so cute?"
"That's not it! I'm here to show you the proper way to use this cord."

This cord, which looks like a curtain cord at first glance, is actually an emergency lifeboat caller for disaster situations.

"This cord can be pulled when certain conditions are met. If the first floor of the Academy is completely submerged for some reason and you infuse the cord with magical power, a transportation device will appear. Like this."

I pulled the cord, and a lifeboat popped out with a "poof."
A 0/12 indicator appeared above the magically created lifeboat, indicating it was a 12-seater.

"Wow! A mysterious boat suddenly appeared."
"I was worried about how to get to class, but with this, we can make it to the lecture hall!"
"Oknodie, you're amazing~!"

I smiled again at the shower of praise coming from all directions.

"But if only students aiming for the same department get on, it'll be hard to reach the lecture hall!"
"Whyyy~?"
"Can't we just go with other seniors~? Let's have Oknodie come with us too. Hmm? Hmm?"
"Ahaha! I'll explain, so stop tickling me. If everyone has the same job class, you can't deal with different dangers, right?"
"Different dangers?"
"Like that."

The monsters that had backed away in surprise at the sudden appearance of the lifeboat seemed quite offended and came rushing in.
A large carp rammed the boat with its body, and a Giant King Crab used its sturdy pincers to puncture the boat and shake it violently up and down.
To top it off, a flying monster that had been perched on a nearby tree mistook the deflating boat for prey, grabbed it with its feet, and flew away.

Clack clack clack

The Giant King Crab that was carried along flailed its pincers in confusion.

"Ah, it's falling."

The Giant King Crab lost its grip on the boat and fell with a thud, creating a column of water as it splashed into the sea, then rose to the surface creating bubbles.
It was snapping its pincers toward the sky, clearly very angry at the flying monster.

"Spearmen need to block the carps' body slams, shield bearers need to block the king crab's pincers, and archers or wizards need to catch flying monsters and provide support."
"...What kind of school commute is as intense as going to a dungeon?"
"I really hate the principal."
"I'm losing confidence in attending this Academy..."
"But since Oknodie is cute, I'll let it slide!"
"I agree!"

The energetic seniors regained their spirits while patting my head, and I too was recharged by their touch.
This is what you call a win-win!


* * *


The Red-Toothed Mushroom Entrepreneurs Association member <Paper Company> came out near the first-year dormitory with a 20-person boat.

"Is everyone ready?"
"Twelve seats left empty, just as planned."
"We'll help everyone get to school except those who messed with the fish farm, and make sure they get ostracized. Never let the ice magic user or the one with the iron plate on board."

Second-years bullying first-years, strategy number one!
Discriminatory school commute strategy!

"We'll build goodwill with the regular first-years while bullying those guys. This way, no matter what we do afterward, public opinion will be that they deserved it, and the first-years will think they're being punished by seniors because they did something wrong."

Crime consultant Bellocasio suggested a method to win over public opinion while targeting their victims.
The association members thought it was a highly feasible proposal, so they brought the boat out early in the morning to transport first-year students, but something seemed off.

"Aren't there fewer first-year students standing by the windows than expected?"
"Yeah. Did they all give up on going to school and go back to sleep in their rooms?"
"This Academy isn't lenient enough to cancel classes just because of some rain. The 981st batch students are quite naive."

The second-year students diligently rowed closer, thinking they needed to wake up their foolish juniors.
They were firmly planning to make the first-years row on the way back, but there wasn't a single student to be seen in the empty rooms without curtains.

"There's no one here?"
"What's going on?"
"Ah, there's the dorm supervisor."

One of the second-year students knocked on the window of the passing dorm supervisor with an oar, looking dejected.

"What business do second-years have at the first-year dormitory?"
"We came to give our juniors a ride, but we can't see any of them. Where did they all go?"

The dorm supervisor looked at them with pity.

"They've already gone to school."
"What?! In these currents?"
"Isn't it too cruel to let the kids get swept away by the water, supervisor?"
"The cruel ones are those first-years with their sticky fingers."

The dorm supervisor answered coldly.

"To think they would steal my precious pet pebble. I raised it dearly for a year. Ah, François..."

The second-year students were quite bewildered.

"François? Did she just name a pebble?"
"What's wrong with that? A pebble is a harmless and docile pet that doesn't bother others. I heard one of the senior elves kept a hundred-year-old oak tree as a pet."
"Then there's nothing wrong with a tree either."
"Even if cicadas live in it?"
"...Damn tree-huggers. Just let them show up in front of our dorm. I'll set them on fire right away."
"Enough."

Paper Company, who had gone to the trouble of bringing out a 20-person boat, felt deflated at the thought of having wasted their effort.

"If there are no students to take, we should head back. It's time for us to go to school too."
"That's right."
"Sigh. What a waste of effort."

Just as they were about to pick up their oars and sit down dejectedly, a boat appeared at the window with a "pop" sound.

"What's that?"
"I've never seen anything like that in all my time at the Academy!"
"There's magic that creates lifeboats?"
"We experienced heavy rain last summer too!"
"But there were no boats like that then!"

The second-year students couldn't hide their bewilderment.
The dorm supervisor spoke to their disgruntled gazes.

"You never asked for a boat."
"......"
"I wasn't going to tell the first-years either since they didn't ask, but they're unnecessarily perceptive."

The dorm supervisor grumbled with discontent.
However, more than the supervisor's complaints, the second-years felt betrayed by the fact that such a convenient means of transportation existed but was never revealed to them when they had risked their lives to get to school.

"How could this happen!"
"You devil! Fourth-year! This is like a graduation assignment!"

While the second-years were arguing with the dorm supervisor, students climbed down from the window using a rope and landed on the boat.

"Why are you so late? We almost left without you."
"Sorry. I was preparing for an assignment."
"Leave the rowing to me, Oknodie."
"Heh. You found an interesting toy."

Oknodie, who had delayed going to school and waited, boarded with close friends like Isabel, Hestia, and Zuang, and departed.

"Catch them! Let's grab those guys and ask what's going on. If they don't answer, we'll sink their boat if we have to—"

Paper Company, who was pointing at Oknodie's boat, blinked repeatedly.
The small child and the large female warrior started rowing, and the boat created huge columns of water as it disappeared beyond sight at an incredible speed.

"How can they be so strong?"
"Their rowing speed is faster than ours?"

The second-year students left behind were dumbfounded.
