# 23 - I'm Perfectly Fine

How cute. While the examinees felt their hearts healing at the sight of the shy Oknodie, they also felt puzzled.
The brutishly large monkey beastman was one thing, but the female adventurer companion and the man with a decent beard didn't look particularly strong.
If it wasn't for her companions' help, how could such a small child have become the top scorer?

"I will grant a special privilege to Miss Oknodie who passed the first gate with the highest score. This privilege allows you to immediately pass the entrance exam without taking any further tests."

Envious gazes poured in from all directions.

"Congratulations."
"You're lucky, Little mouse."
"Go ahead and take it. You deserve to pass."

Gisele, Son O-cheon, and Isabel.
Everyone congratulated her.
Even Examiner Monk Myungho was waiting, wondering why she wasn't using the special privilege yet.
Such expectations...
Oknodie casually shattered them.

"I don't want to."
"Are you saying you won't use the privilege?"
"If I use it, only I will pass, right?"
"Yes, immediate acceptance is a privilege given only to the top scorer."
"I don't abandon my companions."

Her companions' eyes filled with emotion.

"How childlike."
"What a foolish thing to do."
"That's what makes her so pure."

The examinees each had something to say about her decision.
Some criticized, some praised, and some envied.
But there was one fact everyone agreed on:
This child is pure.
No one could deny that.
Which is why her next statement delivered an even greater shock.

"Unless I'm the one being abandoned."


* * *


Graduating from the Academy by Luck.
The NPCs in this game have an affection feature.
It's not just an entertainment feature attached to extras like the Girl in a Straw Hat who don't matter either way.
All main and supporting characters have their own affection parameters.
Friends who stick together like buddies, classmates who join forces on assignments—they all have affection levels.

'When affection reaches a certain level, the companion system opens up, right?'

The problem lies with this affection system.
Dungeons are typically long and difficult.
Choosing a dungeon that doesn't match the party members' skills, entering a dungeon where disliked monsters appear, experiencing negative events, or having poor companion synergy...

Affection decreases for all sorts of reasons.
And what happens when it decreases beyond a certain point?

Even if you build up affection diligently and become companions who enter dungeons together, there are times when you come out having had a huge fight, parting ways and becoming strangers.
If it goes beyond becoming strangers and reaches negative levels, you become outright enemies.
So no matter how much I want to stay together, companions sometimes betray me.

-You're not my friend anymore.
-Don't ever call me again.
-If I see you again, I won't let it slide.
-You're boring.
-Your muscles are too big. It's burdensome.
-Because you're unnecessarily large, we all get caught in weight traps! Don't even dream of going to dungeons with me!

Ah, it still hurts to remember.
But somehow, the expressions my companions are showing now contain the same shock I received back when I was the muscular big guy character.

"Miss Oknodie. Why do you think we would abandon you?"
"This little mouse... saying such unsettling things. I understand why the employer is concerned."
"Why do you say things that sound like you're used to being abandoned? At such a young age."

Ah. These people don't know that this is a game world modeled after a game, do they?
It would be difficult to explain the affection feature to them.

"Just because... that's how it's always been."

Just as chicken is delicious and Stat Stones are beneficial, companions parting ways is also natural.
Even if asked for a reason, it's inevitable that I'd struggle to answer.

"Hmm..."

Examiner Monk Myungho let out a long sigh.

"Please don't misunderstand. What I offered was not a punishment but a reward. If you don't want it, no one will stop you from taking the test with your companions."

[You have passed the first gate's stone tower building event with the highest score.]
[You did not use the top scorer's special privilege <Immediate Admission> given as a reward.]

As a result, we continued with the exam.

"That's annoying."
"What is that? Companionship?"
"Only an ignorant kid would do something like that."

The whispers of jealousy, envy, and criticism didn't stop.
People who said things like "that's too bad" or "how brave" with kind faces would turn around and curse just a short distance away.

"Ah- I'm hungry. Don't they feed us during the exam?"
"We have plenty of dried squid if you'd like."
"Give me some of that then."

A gluttonous monkey who doesn't understand others' feelings, just rubbing his belly and talking about food in a booming voice.

"Little mouse. Want one too?"
"Only if it's not one you've touched."

[You have consumed the common dish <Honey Butter Jerky>.]
[Consumption count increased (2/1000)]

I usually try not to eat the same dish twice, but I'll make an exception today.

"You're a friendly team."
"With this little mouse? Me?"
"With this noisy big guy? Me?"
"You look enviably good together. That's how my captain and veteran seniors were back in the Esonia Adventure Group days."

Isabel said her captain had died.
I don't particularly want to get friendly with this monkey beastman, but if our cook finds comfort in seeing us get along...

"Actually, we are friends. Mr. O-cheon is strong and tall. I find him reassuring."
"Oh. So that's how you've been seeing me? For a little mouse, you can say some pretty admirable things."
"That's a lie. Go die."

I ended up bickering with him again after he immediately got cocky when I gave him a little credit.


* * *


She's a deeply considerate child.
Despite having experienced an unfortunate past herself, she's considerate of adults like herself who are several years older.
Isabel thought that she wanted to protect this child, not just because she was her lifesaver.

'I've seen plenty of dirty and ugly things. But truly shining people don't succumb to their environment.'

There are gems that shine even in the mud.
Most are trampled by the greed around them, their light fading, and eventually even that dim light disappears.
But with luck, those who notice that light will pull the gem out, clean it thoroughly, and carefully polish it to make it shine brighter.

'She's quite considerate for a beastman.'

This beastman called Son O-cheon.
Though he acts indifferent, it's obvious he's trying to shield the child from voices that would taint her ears.
The man called Gisele is the same.
While the child's attention is focused on the beastman, he glares coldly at those who speak filthy words, keeping them at bay.
Good children and good adults.
A rare team in today's world.

'Adults who know gratitude are rare.'

Society teaches desire and selfishness.
It makes you physically experience that if you don't covet more and be more ruthlessly selfish, you can't survive.
If someone hasn't forgotten purity despite going through such times.
If they can accept a child's pure desire to simply be with their companions as it is.
That person is an excellent adult.
Gisele and Son O-cheon were sufficiently excellent.

"I'll cook for you sometime when I have the chance."
"Ooh. Really?"
"An adventurer's cooking. I'm looking forward to it."
"Can I make requests?"
"As long as I have the ingredients."

Perhaps that's why Isabel felt a desire too.
The desire to become a good adult.

"Do you dislike vegetarian dishes?"
"I eat pretty much anything."
"That's good."

The forest area on the way to the second gate.
Every noticeable ingredient was nothing but vegetables.


* * *


Just like before the first gate, the special scenery visible on the way to the exam site is a hint about the upcoming test.
This time, what stands out is the forest.
Unlike the rocky mountain that was cleanly split by the earthquake just a little while ago, this is a perfectly intact forest with no trace of earthquake damage.
Not an ordinary forest, that is.
The potential exam candidates I can imagine are all terrible.

Wilderness survival.
Hunting competition.
Forest escape.

If it's survival, we'll have to stay in this dense forest for days.
If it's a hunting competition, we'll have to compete for prey among the main and supporting characters who just got all riled up.
If it's forest escape, the environment gets harsher the deeper we go into the forest.

The common denominator.
This is the most important.

Whiiine-
Smack

Something flying crookedly past my outstretched hand.
Filthy blood thief.
There are mosquitoes.

"Little mouse, with hands that slow, can you even catch anything?"

Son O-cheon spread his pot-lid-sized hands wide and clapped them together with a loud smack!
What's with him, he missed too.
I was about to say something when I saw the mosquito escape his palms, but then I was startled.

Plop

The mosquito fell weakly onto a nearby bush.

"What was that just now?"
"If you clap fast enough, mosquitoes faint."
"Wow."

My affection level for Son O-cheon rose.
Right now, I could give him a candy I've already tasted.

"Want one?"
"Oh, candy? Nice."

I carefully opened my candy pouch and placed one piece on his large palm.
The size difference makes it look quite comical.

Crunch crunch

As soon as he popped the candy into his mouth, the sound of crushing it mercilessly echoed.
Rather than savoring the taste with his tongue, he seems to prefer the fun of crushing everything with his teeth.
His way of eating candy is completely different from mine.

'He really is hard to like.'

Candy should be rolled around in your mouth.
While I was grumbling internally and fidgeting with my candy, the monkey suddenly spat his out.

Smack
Crack

He spat it out so hard that the candy hit a tree and split in half.

"Ah! What are you doing?"
"What are YOU eating?"

Son O-cheon looked down at the candy pouch in bewilderment.

"This is poison candy, isn't it?"
"I'm perfectly fine though?"

Once again, the gazes around us became uncomfortable.
