# 2 - I'm Not a Poor Child

There's a pre-admission training system for Graduating from the Academy by Luck.
It's not the developers' consideration to help you improve your specs a bit before entering and make the early stages more convenient.
It's a time to grit your teeth and prepare because if you don't, you'll get kicked out before even being admitted, like failing at the entrance.

'I was amazing as a beginner.'

Training means practical experience, adventurer means growth.
Players who joined the Adventurer's Guild as if following quest guidelines, deceived by slogans that tempt novice players, and departed for the Forest and Cave.
More than 90% of them got completely destroyed in their first mission, either dying or returning in tatters.

'How is it possible that in a party of five, you all betray each other?'

A luck-based disaster party where party members betray each other at a certain probability (9.9%).
Thanks to a miraculous probability event where all party members betrayed each other, memories from that playthrough when I was dumbfounded resurface vividly.
Thinking about it again, compared to that truly legendary disaster of a party, grinding at the training ground is really pleasant.

"The facilities are nice."
"These are facilities prepared by the Master. Every year before the entrance exam, people are sent to manage them."
"Every year?"

The Butler made a face that suggested he was annoyed.
It's exactly the expression killers make when they create an unnecessary witness during an assassination contract, lamenting having to spill extra blood that didn't need to be seen.

"The mirror is in this room."

It's an obvious attempt to change the subject, but I actually welcome it.
I was curious about how Oknodie's body looked, since it's different from the appearance I had directly set up.

"Oh."

A full-length mirror set up in one corner of the training ground.
Looking back at me in the mirror meant for posture correction is a small girl with blonde hair.
A skinny thing that would roll across the floor like a bowling pin if kicked.
When I raise my palm, my face can be covered with one hand.
She looks pretty enough to be featured as a middle school beauty on TikTok.
Yes, middle school.
Not high school.
That's how young her physical age appears.
This is completely a child.

"Sigh."

As I look up, the Butler is looking down at me with a face that says, "What a pitiful creature."

"You've never looked in a mirror in your life, have you?"
"That's not true! I've seen mirrors plenty of times!"
"You don't need to pretend to be strong. That reaction was clearly from someone who didn't know what they looked like and was seeing a mirror for the first time."

No, damn it.
That's because it's my first time with this character.
I want to explain this misunderstanding, but the Butler doesn't even pretend to listen and strides ahead.

"I can roughly guess how you've lived until now. Let's have a meal before training."

I don't appreciate being treated like some charity case, but I kept my mouth shut at the mention of food.
This game has a collection feature.
When you collect various items, collection effects increase your stats, function levels, or rare item acquisition probability.

'The Cooking Book is something you can never collect enough of.'

While sitting at the dining table feeling bored, I noticed a shadow cast under the spacious table.
Time is precious until admission.
To avoid all kinds of bad luck, I need to raise my function levels whenever possible.

[You have hidden under the table for more than 1 minute.]
[Hiding experience +1]

With just my head peeking out from under the table, I spot a grandfather clock large enough for a person to fit inside with room to spare.

[You have hidden inside the grandfather clock door for more than 1 minute.]
[Hiding experience +1]

Hiding experience increases by 1 each time you hide in a new location for more than a minute.
When probabilities go crazy and elite boss monsters prowl where normal mobs should be, the only function you can rely on is this hiding skill.
If you don't raise it in advance like this, you never know when you'll fall victim to bad luck in the game Graduating from the Academy by Luck.

[You have hidden under a chair for more than 1 minute.]
[Hiding experience +1]
[You have hidden behind a curtain for more than 1 minute.]
[Hiding experience +1]
[You have hidden inside a wardrobe for more than 1 minute.]
[Hiding experience +1]

Where should I go next?
Just as I was thinking about an empty barrel, the wardrobe door opened with a creak.

"What are you doing in there?"
"Just looking for good hiding places..."
"......"

The gaze is piercing.
I belatedly realized how my behavior must have appeared to the Butler.
Can't sleep in bed, seeing a mirror for the first time in my life, hiding in a wardrobe when offered food.

"I'm not a weird person."
"I didn't say anything. Please sit at the table."

This is so unfair, seriously.
While grumbling to myself about this being worse than bad luck, I sat in the chair and saw beef stew that tickled my nose and delicious-looking baguette bread.
Unfortunately, it's not rare food that increases your stats just by eating it.
It's common-grade food that gives collection bonuses every time you eat 10 different dishes.

*gobble gobble*

Whether you eat quickly or slowly, the number collected is the same.
But you have to eat all of a given dish completely for it to be added to the collection.
So rather than savoring the taste slowly, it's better to eat as quickly as possible to save time.

"You eat very hurriedly."
"...Don't look at me like that. I just naturally eat quickly."
"You can eat slowly. No one here will steal your food."
"Really?"

It's troublesome even if someone says they'll just take one bite.
If someone else eats even a bite of my food, the collection judgment fails.
My wariness increases, and I practically hug the soup bowl as I inhale it, making the Butler's gaze even more piercing.

"Did you enjoy your meal?"
"It was delicious."
"Then I'll prepare the same dish tomorrow."

Ah. That won't work.

"Could I try different dishes?"
"...If that's what you wish."

I did it.
With this, I can easily fill several entries in my Cooking Book.
As I silently rejoice, the Butler clears away the empty dishes with a face that says he's indulging me out of pity.


* * *


The training began.

"This is basic physical training. The speed doesn't matter, just run around the outside of the yellow line in the training ground as much as you can."

After the Butler finished speaking, my head suddenly throbbed.

"Are you alright?"
"I'm fine. It was just a momentary headache."

After the pain subsided, a familiar event window appeared before my eyes.

<First Training Event>
Unlike beginners who mindlessly rush into mountains, fields, and caves, you have decided to train diligently.
As they say, well begun is half done—the results of your first training will greatly help your future training.
Follow the instructor's directions and proceed with the training program.

It's here.
The training event that can overcome insufficient luck.
A growth opportunity permitted only to the wealthy.

Originally, to utilize this event, I would have had to start with part-time jobs or adventurer activities to save money.
But now?
Money is the one thing I have plenty of.

*jingle*

I rummaged through my pocket and held out 1 gold coin, and the Butler stared at my hand.

"What are you doing?"
"Training fee."
"I am your Butler, not a private training instructor. You don't need to pay."
"...Oh."
"Put the gold coin away and start running."

Hehe, saved some money.
I might have heard a muttering from behind saying, "It seems you've never received kindness without cost..." but what's important right now isn't such muttering.
In a realistic isekai where status windows don't open, the only way to discover your abilities is through hard work.
Oknodie's body has surprisingly good stamina despite its frail appearance.
Judging by my instincts as an experienced player, although the appearance has changed, it feels like I've inherited all the stats I invested in.

'What a waste.'

That's why I'm even more upset.
The strength exhibited by a muscular monster and that of a delicate girl are different.
If I were going to invest in a body, it would have been better to have a slightly larger one, but unfortunately, the initial stats seem to have been heavily allocated to strength and stamina.

"That should be enough. Your basic physical fitness is surprisingly excellent."
"I'm pretty confident in my stamina. It's troublesome to be slow when chasing or being chased, right?"

As I boast proudly, somehow his gaze becomes even more pitying than before.
I don't know what he thinks I'm chasing or being chased by, but I can be certain he's not thinking of monster hunting and pickpocket-catching quests.

[You have run 30 laps around the track.]
[Physical training efficiency at the training ground increases by 300% for one month.]

"Please choose a weapon you know how to handle."

After stamina comes weapon skills.
Without hesitation, I picked up a sword first.
Glancing at the Butler, he stood there with a face that said, "Let's see what this greenhorn does."
So I picked up a bow as well.
Still no sign of surprise.
At a first birthday party, adults would get excited if a child grabbed two objects, saying "Wow, our child picked two items!"
Maybe because I'm not a baby, his reaction is disappointingly different from what I'd expect at such an occasion.
...Would he remain so indifferent if I picked up a wand as my final weapon?

"That's not a club."
"I know. It's a wand."

The Butler's face turned serious.

"Are you familiar with magical equipment?"
"Yes."
"Are you saying you've held a wand before?"
"Of course."

He clearly didn't expect me to pick up a mage's equipment.
Hehe, finally I'm getting some reaction.
Could he be surprised by my talent?

"Have you ever performed blood extraction or drug administration tasks on the street?"
"No, I haven't."
"Please be honest."
"I really haven't."

But his reaction isn't "Wow! Amazing!"
He seems very angry about something.

"Young lady. I strongly dislike lies."
"But it's true..."

Now that I think about it, there is something that might be catching me.
In my last playthrough, I did blood donation part-time jobs and clinical trial part-time jobs to gather initial funds because I was short on starting money.

"Ah. I did it before. A very long time ago."
"No matter how much you want to learn magic, learning from street magicians is dangerous."
"I didn't learn magic."
"Then where did you learn to use a wand?"
"That's..."

I learned it at the Academy.
But in this playthrough, I haven't even been admitted yet.
As my answer is delayed, the Butler's expression becomes even more severe.

"I understand. I will step out for a moment. Please continue with self-training while I'm gone."

The Butler left with the air of someone who was going to crack someone's skull before returning today.

"......"

Sorry, street magician.
It seems today might be your memorial day.
But you did mess me up once with an addiction potion.
Let's call it even.
Okay?


* * *


[Sword training efficiency increases by 300% for one month.]
[Archery training efficiency increases by 250% for one month.]
[Wand training efficiency increases by 200% for one month.]

"You've been diligently training during self-study. Well done, young lady."

The Butler's expression was calm when he returned.
I should pretend not to notice the smell of blood.
