# 217 - The One Who Sends, The One Who Takes, The One Who Plays Innocent

The Foundation representative felt like he was going to lose his mind.

Just when it seemed like they would take Oknodie away smoothly and everything would end well, this terrifying-looking professor suddenly started emitting murderous intent. The world's scenery distorted as sharp killing intent flashed from all directions, threatening to burn his eyes, pierce his heart, and slice his neck.

Even a mouse before a cat would feel better than this, he thought. In such overwhelming fear, all he could do was open his mouth with a face contorted in pain.

"The Foundation has no intention of antagonizing Gift Academy. Didn't we say you could take the child as long as you obtained her voluntary consent?"

"Voluntary consent, you say? Do you think you can shift all responsibility onto the child by passing everything off and turning a blind eye?"

Say something.
You stayed because you wanted to.

The supervisor turned to Oknodie, desperately hoping for some explanation, but his eyes widened in shock.

-So annoying!!! Want a taste of dark mana?
-Why so gloomy this morning?
-The clown mask gang irritated me by bringing the same menu they served last week.

Like the Black Dragon known as the Dragon of Tyranny, the most notorious dragon of the current era, this demon in child's form who demanded rare dishes for every meal and drained the branch budget was now blinking innocently with the face of an 11-year-old baby, then subtly averting her gaze.

'T-t-that damn girl. Is she shifting responsibility to us because she's scared of the professor!?'

She stayed even when we told her to go!

He felt so wronged he wanted to jump three meters into the air, but with the sense that one wrong move would get his throat slit, he couldn't even speak freely. Carefully straightening his posture to show he had no harmful intentions, he respectfully placed his hands together in front of his stomach.

Eyes cast downward.
Breathing carefully so as not to disturb.
Watching for cues, the supervisor spoke.

"We also hope that unnecessary misunderstandings will end and Oknodie can return to the Academy as soon as possible. We fully support the professor's wishes."

"Hmph. How amusing. You still have the energy to mock me after feeling my killing intent."

'That fucking Academy professor.'

I'm showing the utmost respect, and who's mocking whom? You're the one mocking us.

Suppressing the urge to curse to his face, the supervisor listened as the professor made his demand.

"I need to see how the Foundation has been treating Oknodie. Show me to the living quarters."

Despite the Destroyer professor's commanding tone, the supervisor led the way without showing any displeasure.

"This is our branch's night duty room."

"Where has Oknodie been sleeping?"

"We assigned one of these rooms to Oknodie."

The VIP room—equipped with the most luxurious items and a soft, comfortable bed enchanted with magic, reserved for high-ranking Foundation officials. It was true that they had given Oknodie this room.

That gangster-like brat was so terrifying.
Sleep well and maybe tone down that nasty temper a bit.

That was the mindset with which they had given her the room, but now the supervisor was relieved that this was one less thing for the professor to find fault with.

Well done, past me.
I really applaud your crisis management skills.

While the supervisor was lost in self-admiration at this life-or-death crossroads, Oknodie unexpectedly raised her hand.

"I didn't sleep here though?"

"What are you saying!? We definitely assigned you a room!"

"Silence. Don't interrupt Oknodie's words."

As the Destroyer professor's eyes widened further, the oppressive aura pressed down on the supervisor's shoulders.

Pop! Clang!
Crack.

In the display area where soft lighting illuminated a flower vase, the light popped and the vase shattered. The pressure simultaneously bearing down on his shoulders was enough to crack the side table. The supervisor's legs trembled as he crouched awkwardly, gritting his teeth and placing both hands on his knees to steady himself.

Despite feeling wronged, the supervisor couldn't utter a word, crushed under the professor's domain. The professor turned to Oknodie with angry eyes and asked:

"You didn't sleep here. Is that true?"

"Yes. Not yet, anyway."

Oknodie, who had been watching cautiously, added softly as if offering something to appease his anger:

"If the professor had come later, I might have slept here soon!"

"...So you were planning to make the child sleep in that bed decorated like a brothel room? Were you going to force the child to sell her body if she didn't follow your orders?"

"!!"

Oh no.
Strong regret crossed the supervisor's face.

It had seemed so natural that he hadn't even thought about it.
The high-ranking officials from the Foundation were obviously men.
It was expected that they would demand sexual entertainment.
It was normal to bring in prostitutes from nearby to supply them, so the VIP room's bedroom was decorated accordingly. Since Oknodie had never complained, it hadn't even occurred to him that there might be a problem.

Was this why she claimed she hadn't slept here despite sleeping just fine until now? Was she setting him up for this?

"This is unfair!"

The supervisor truly felt wronged.
If he had actually harbored ill intentions, that would be one thing.
It was true that the child showed remarkable potential.
But she was just a child.
There wasn't a single pervert in his branch who would lay hands on such a baby.
He wasn't spineless enough to meekly accept such a false accusation.

"Since our VIP room is typically used by men, I acknowledge that the bed may not be appropriate for a child's sensibilities. However, this was merely an administrative oversight. We had absolutely no intention of threatening the child!"

"Oknodie. Tell me yourself. Is what the Foundation representative saying true?"

"Yes! They never threatened me sexually!"

Oknodie answered vigorously, showing some conscience. She glanced around cautiously and added:

"...Not yet, anyway!"

"You conscienceless brat! How can you call yourself human!"

The moment the supervisor lost his temper, he felt his muscles tear and bones crush as one of his knees slammed onto the marble floor.

Despite tears streaming down his face from the pain that threatened to shatter his knee, the Destroyer showed no mercy and looked down at him with fierce eyes.

"Do not raise your voice. Do you wish to hasten your death?"

"Do you know how many rare dishes we prepared for that child! We truly did our best. We put so much effort into hospitality that it nearly bankrupted our branch!"

"Check it."

At the Destroyer's command, Thieves Guild members who had already surrounded the Foundation branch and were hiding nearby rushed into the dining hall, kitchen, and food storage.

"We found it."

"Multiple rare dishes confirmed."

"Price tags and packaging from local specialty rare dishes detected. Evidence of specialty products confirmed."

So they did feed her rare dishes, at least.
But they offered her a VIP room meant to treat her like a prostitute and tried to threaten her.
Who knows what tricks they might have played with the food too.

"Bring them."

The Destroyer examined the packaging, price tags, and other evidence.
The first few were quite decent.
10 gold coins each.
It was confirmed that she had eaten dishes that cost a considerable amount.
But whether Oknodie ate them or they ate them themselves was another question.

"Oknodie. Have you eaten Kira Kira Sprinkle Eel Grill?"

"Yes!"

"How about Hundred-Year-Old Lizard Tail Grill?"

"I ate it!"

"Ogogok Rye Porridge?"

"It was delicious!"

The verification started well.
From fun ingredients that made "ogogok" sounds when chewed, to hundred-year-old lizard tails that were softly reconstructed as they evolved and shed, to thunder eels that sparkled with electrical mana—all these foods were beneficial in terms of enjoyment, taste, and growth.

'These are gourmet-level dishes.'

Even at the Academy, such dishes required separate points and advance reservations as special meals. And that was with Gift Island's ridiculous prices that were 100 times higher.
Not a bad choice for outside dining.

But when one guild member rushed over with a ledger, what little trust had been building toward the Foundation representative completely collapsed.

"Oknodie. Have you drunk Screaming Soul Liquor?"

"Yes!"

"It's unacceptable for a child to drink alcohol in the first place, but Soul Liquor is especially problematic. Do you know what kind of alcohol that is?"

"It has souls that increase movement speed, so it was delicious!"

"So they even made you aware of its effects when they gave it to you."

Even the cruel disposition to deal with souls was naturally cultivated at the Foundation.
This situation was unacceptable.
It was a cruel act to make a child think it was normal to exploit souls under the guise of enjoying fine cuisine.

Crack!

The marble floor began to crack.
The Foundation representative screamed.
His knee was being crushed, and blood pooled on the cracked marble floor.

"Bomb Moss Soup is a forbidden food that can burst the esophagus and stomach, leading to death if the consumer's body isn't sufficiently developed. Did you eat this too?"

"It was tasty!"

As Oknodie licked her lips, the Destroyer gave the supervisor a look that said "how could you feed such things to a child?" The supervisor's patience finally reached its limit.

"ARGH!! You demon!! We didn't want to give her such things, but she was the one who demanded new dishes, refusing to eat the same rare dish twice!!"

"Oh, I was fine with it. I'm not picky and eat well."

"This girl...! Are you trying to commit murder by proxy!? You're planning to kill me using the professor's hand! Since it's come to this, I'll be honest. I actually hoped you'd be half-dead after eating that! I was planning to kindly send you to the Academy if you wanted treatment!"

The supervisor lost his composure and shouted at the top of his lungs, veins bulging in his neck.

"Do you know how much money it costs to buy rare dishes from distant places? The extra money spent to locate arcane chefs and purchase ingredients when dishes couldn't be bought directly? Branch funds weren't enough, so I had to embezzle my personal assets! For ingredients that couldn't be bought with money, we even risked the lives of those stupid clown-masked jesters to obtain them directly!!"

Huff. Huff.
Exhausted from his outburst, he glared at Oknodie as if to say "what are you going to do now?"
Oknodie was clever.

"Huh? What? I don't understand what you're saying, but it's so scary! Help me, Professor!"

Oknodie's perfect act of playing innocent and shifting all blame!
The effect was tremendous.
The supervisor's hand transformed underwater, donning claws that flashed sharp talons.

"That's it. Just die!"

Swoosh!
The supervisor leaped from the ground, extending his claws.

"What annoying resistance."

It was an insolent trick targeting Oknodie since he couldn't harm the professor himself.
Just as the Destroyer was about to crush his body alive to thwart this final struggle, Oknodie's body left an afterimage as she instantly approached the Foundation representative.

"!"

She's fast.
Too close.
At this speed, she might get caught up in it.

In the split second the Destroyer suppressed his power to prevent Oknodie from being crushed by his domain, the supervisor and Oknodie seized the opportunity and crossed paths even faster.

The claw extended with increased speed, and Oknodie dove inside to avoid it.
The outcome of the deadlock was clear.
Slash!
Oknodie's sleeve was torn by the claw.
The supervisor's attack merely grazed her.
Thwack!
Oknodie was different.
With a terrible sound, the Foundation representative's neck split open.
It was the result of slicing his neck with a hand coated in dark mana.
Gurgle.
The supervisor clutched his neck and looked at her resentfully, as if wronged.
He collapsed with a thud.

"What dangerous thing are you doing!"

The Destroyer was angry that Oknodie would have been caught up if he had been even slightly slower in withdrawing his domain.

"Haven't you learned that recklessly interfering with an expanded domain could seriously injure you!"

"I never learned that?"

The Destroyer thought carefully.
Oknodie was right.

"I see. That's taught from third year onward."

The Destroyer had confused how far the curriculum had progressed because Oknodie was so strong.
While he awkwardly closed his mouth, Oknodie wiped her blood-dripping hand on the corpse's clothes and smiled awkwardly.

'This is quite suspicious.'

The Destroyer stared at Oknodie with doubt flickering in his gaze.
Was this child really abused by the Foundation and rescued?
No matter how he looked at it, it seemed more like the Foundation representative had been abused by Oknodie and executed as the price of insubordination...

"Are we taking a carriage back?"

"There's an Academy-exclusive teleportation magic circle. We didn't use it when coming to avoid the Foundation's eyes, but we can use it directly on our way back."

"Wow!"

Oknodie maintained her natural, innocent face.
With a lingering sense of suspicion, the Destroyer headed to the teleportation station with Oknodie.
