# 200 - Area Control

"So now that you've already made an enemy of the hero, you're not hesitating to show your true colors?"

Hero Ishtar gritted her teeth at the recoil transmitted through her hand as she cut down the Mamok blocking her path.

<Holy Sword Release - Sacred Slash>

She cleaved the Mamok in two with a tremendous force that destroys the impure.

Even as the bisected Mamok let out an agonizing scream and scattered into particles of light along with the dark mana that constituted its existence, the darkness hanging over Ishtar's face did not lift.

"Yupi. You interfered with Oknodie during this exam, didn't you?"
"Don't worry. Scola won't make her move in Area 1, but in Area 3, when the mounted battle begins."
"We need to stop this."

Blood from a Mamok splattered across Yupi's face as she swung a massive guillotine, smashing one of the creatures to pieces.

The dark mana contained in mere monster blood could never invade the body of someone with the saintess class.
That was common sense.
The proper order.
The fundamental principle that must be maintained for the world to function.

Yet that fundamental principle was now challenging the divine protection barrier that guarded her skin, emitting ominous black smoke.

Yupi sensed something ominous happening that should never occur.
It was like the fear of a wise elder who foresees the approach of nature's wrath through years of experience.
Something terrible that should never happen was about to unfold here.

These Mamoks were too different from ordinary ones.
This phenomenon shouldn't originate from low-level magical beasts.

After blocking the energy that tried to dissolve her body and seep inside by drawing out her divine power, she frowned, raised her hand, and wiped the blood from her face.

*Hissss*

The grass withered and died where a single drop of blood hit the ground.
This level of dark mana concentration is only detected in the blood of high-level magical beasts.

Could the professors be behind this?
No, that's impossible.
Even if the professors were reckless, they had their limits.
Though their trials might seem unreasonable at first glance, professors never give challenges that students can't handle.

High-level magical beasts were different.
This was beyond what ordinary students could manage.
If non-combat students were subjected to a concentrated assault by these monsters, no one could guarantee how many corpses would be found in this forest.

"Even though Scola is a descendant of the divine archer?"

Even so, Scola isn't just an ordinary non-combat student.

"Shouldn't we be worried about the other students rather than Scola?"
"No. The other students will be fine. Scola is the one in danger. Today's Oknodie is different from usual. One of the chains that bound her actions while pretending to be an ordinary student has been loosened."

If it were the usual Oknodie who enjoyed academy life at a leisurely pace while holding back, such challenges and surprise attacks on her mount would be perfectly acceptable.
That girl would probably find such challenges "fun."
But what about now?
Would she still accept the challenge and think "that was fun, so it's fine"?
Or would she think "you're annoying, so I'll crush you"?

The duel that was stopped by Master Myungho.
A one-on-one sword fight.
Ishtar remembered that moment.

"Oknodie's potential... her true hidden ability, if unleashed, cannot be safely contained even by the skills of the top combat specialists in the advanced class."
"So if Scola accidentally offends Oknodie..."
"She might lose her life or suffer permanent disability equivalent to that."

Their hands moved more urgently to break through the Mamoks.

"The hero is amazing."

Though Lotto spoke, it was an honest impression that Hestia and Jigoku shared as well.

"There are so many traces of Mamoks being destroyed."
"That's because she's a hero."
"Dirty attribute user. I could do the same if I hoarded attribute bullets."

Oknodie had been holding back the second-year students at the stairs, buying time.
Her rampage ended when the Mamoks were swept away by Hero Ishtar's advance.
Of course, it was only a partial victory.
While Ishtar and her hero party member Yupi advanced, crushing Mamoks, other Mamoks followed Oknodie's command and passed through the stairs together.

"Kyaaaa!"
"Ahhh! There's still a Mamok hiding!"

Was this a Mamok that lost Oknodie's control during the retreat while the hero was rampaging?
Its mouth, which had been horribly distorted like a hell demon, and the branches that had turned sharp and pointy, now seemed much tamer and less threatening.

"What kind of trick did she use? Turning ordinary Mamoks into hell creatures that looked like they just escaped from the underworld."

Hestia felt uneasy.
Oknodie's power was too strange.
Logically, the amount of dark mana needed to enhance magical beasts is extraordinary.
Not to mention controlling them.
And she handled so many magical beasts.
This wasn't something that could be accomplished in just a day or two.
It doesn't make sense unless she possessed mana quantities beyond human limits or had extreme resistance to darkness, death, and necromancy attributes that rivaled a demon lord, allowing her to possess pure dark mana.

"Whoa, it's Hestia! I'm alive, I'm alive!"
"Dorothy? You were... Oknodie's friend, right?"
"Yes, yes! Let's go together. I thought I was going to die trying to get here after evading the second-years!"
"...I'm sorry. Could we impose on you?"

Dorothy, the cheerful and bright apprentice forest keeper who resembled Oknodie.
Rockfell, the gloomy and unsettling hidden blade master who always hovered around her.
With the confrontation between first and second-year students established, there was no reason to refuse the company of fellow first-year skilled students.

"Let's go together."

At the top of the stairs, students from the Imperial faction were forming ranks and indiscriminately attacking students trying to pass through, apparently intending to repeat what Oknodie had done.
This was why Hestia, Jigoku, and Lotto had been circling below, hesitant to cross the stairs.

"Can we challenge them with five people?"
"We'll have to try and see."

They couldn't rush into a battlefield that needed to be broken through with renewed determination while dragging exhausted bodies and gasping for breath.
The newly joined Dorothy and Rockfell took time to catch their breath and prepare before engaging in battle.

"Mamok? I bet she learned from that thing?"

Surprisingly, Dorothy, who had just joined them, seemed to have some insight about the Mamoks that had been bothering Hestia for quite some time.

"What thing?"
"Professor Sadako's cursed pet tree, Poppy. There's an incredibly crazy tree wandering somewhere in the deep forest of the academy."
"...You encountered such a thing?"
"Yes. Thinking about it now, Oknodie went to the forest to find that tree from the beginning. Selling blood stones to buy regular carriage tickets, going to the forest to collect materials—I think she planned it all to use Mamoks transformed like Poppy for the midterm exam!"

Everyone's expressions darkened as they heard more detailed stories about Poppy and Professor Sadako.
There was too much information.
It was beyond the level an ordinary student should possess.
The Foundation's help had to be considered a fact now.
These unsettling acts of enhancing and controlling Mamoks must be some kind of directive from the Foundation.
From the beginning, magical beast studies isn't an easy field to enter just to collect some materials and casually enhance and control creatures.
Even with matching attributes, incorrect application can disrupt the body's balance, making it worse than not enhancing at all.
Blood vessels can become blocked, cutting off breath, or the beast might attack the sorcerer if its violence can't be controlled.
To succeed in magical beast enhancement despite all these risks, and even control multiple beasts simultaneously, would require unimaginable trial and error and specialized teaching.

Early education. The Foundation had done it again.
Another schedule had been added to a dark page in Oknodie's past.

"I wonder if Oknodie has students she wants to eliminate? If she just wanted to set a record for herself, there's no reason to keep carrying around so many Mamoks."

Dorothy's question was valid.
Magical beasts are strong, true.
But skilled students can overcome them somehow.
The Northern Grand Duchess in Group A froze a Mamok whole.
The Hero in Group B crushed them with holy power.
The Lightning Sorcerer in Group C kept striking lightning until the Mamok exploded completely, regardless of whether the lightning flowed to the ground.

"Could it be a curse?"
"A curse?"
"Poppy, the tree Oknodie used as material for enhancing the Mamoks, was an ominous tree with lots of cursed waste hanging from it."

Somehow, the ominous feeling kept growing stronger.

Dark Princess Oknodie.
The Foundation's scholarship student.
That dangerous girl is deliberately up to something.

"What's everyone doing here?"

The first-year students who had arrived late finally understood why the other students were in a state of near-surrender.
Oknodie had blocked the path.
And she had increased the difficulty.
In a clever way that wouldn't violate the regulations.

"Honner. Don't waste your energy and come sit with us."
"The water is so clear..."

"These kids... they're all not friends with Oknodie."
