<292 - Landmine>

Academy seniors are dangerous.
They're the ones who experience professors' truly intense lectures—professors who are so gentle to first-years that you could say they're "putting on an act."
Countless cases exist where students become mentally and physically exhausted, turning into obsessive workaholics or even seeming to go insane trying to keep up with high-intensity lectures.
After overcoming such brutal lecture schedules and finally graduating successfully, these seniors could commit forbidden reinforcement techniques prohibited by the Empire, and everyone would simply say, "Hmph. That's just like our senior," accepting and even feeling reassured by it!
However, if the person isn't a graduate, that's a different story.

"Would a real graduate have any reason to lie about their class year?"
"No."
"Isn't it crazy for someone who's not a graduate to impersonate an Academy student? Why would they do that?"
"They're probably borrowing the Academy's authority to intimidate us."
"So that person isn't from our Academy?"

The students fell into confusion.
Gisele regretted his actions.
He thought he was maintaining his composure, but he had failed to tell only Jigoku privately, instead speaking loudly enough for everyone to hear, which increased the students' fear.

"Everyone, please calm down. I ask that you keep this matter confidential and leave it to me."

The lower-class students, not wanting to get involved in dangerous matters, tactfully kept their mouths shut and backed away.
Only Jigoku bravely stood his ground and asked.

"What are you planning to do? If you provoke him by asking about his identity, he might suddenly reveal his true colors and destroy you with reinforcement. You could die, you know?"
"...For now, let's entrust our reinforcement to him and think about it later. We also need the compression of our loot."

Would he reinforce items other than light stands?
That was his main concern, but Ferguson scolded him, asking what nonsense he was talking about.

"When doing reinforcement, would you reinforce more light stands or precious metals? Obviously, you'd reinforce precious metals much more often since they're easier to hide and less noticeable."

Ferguson was right.
Rather than conspicuously destroying visible objects like light stands through reinforcement, secretly reinforcing precious metals would be much more discreet.
Naturally, once a blacksmith with profit in mind ventured into the forbidden realm of reinforcement, they would undoubtedly focus more on reinforcing precious metals than light stands.

Clang! Clang!

With hammer strikes and dazzling light effects wrapping around the materials, piles of reinforcement materials vanished.
Even as mountains of sorted items evaporated, Gisele's eyes were analyzing the principle behind it.

'He's extracting mana puzzles that exist in the material and combining them.'

The hammering was no different from spellcasting by magicians who chant spells verbally or the eastern hand techniques developed for casting magic silently.
While it appeared on the surface to be hitting and breaking objects, in reality, much more complex and meticulous [disassembly][assembly][combination] was taking place beneath the hammer's movements!

'Even looking again, his speed is truly terrifying.'

Living as a black market merchant in the shadows of the Empire, one occasionally encounters reinforcement practitioners.
But no one had ever processed reinforcements at the incredible speed of the man before him.
His speed made it understandable why he would fearlessly impersonate an Academy graduate.

'His accuracy is no joke either.'

Ferguson, who had completed all reinforcement materials and compression in just one hour, returned his blacksmith's hand hammer to his work belt.

"There, that completes all the reinforcement!"
"...Your skills are impressive. I'm newly amazed. This is the first time I've seen a reinforcement practitioner of your caliber."
"Haha. You're a funny one. You're just a first-year student talking as if you've seen reinforcement practitioners before."

Should he ask?
What this man actually does.
Why he impersonated an Academy graduate.
His half-opened mouth closed again.
The world is full of landmines that shouldn't be touched.
Illegal businesses backed by high officials.
Talented children taken by the Imperial Family.
The Wiheomhae Foundation, which benchmarks the Empire's secret agent training program to produce their own scholarship students.
People with secrets respect others' secrets.
Because they know how terrible the consequences can be when those secrets are exposed.
The danger emanating from Ferguson was of that magnitude.

"If there's anything you desire, I'd like to offer you compensation."
"That's enough. I'm already fully satisfied with this excursion just from doing all the reinforcement I wanted. Haha."

'Excursion,' he says.
Gisele gritted his teeth and smiled off Ferguson's casual dropping of landmines as if begging to be stepped on.
Right now, there was someone else occupying Gisele's thoughts.

"What's going on? You said you'd handle it if we left it to you. Are you chickening out?"
"Honestly, yes, I'm scared. Jigoku, you must have sensed it too, right? That man's dangerous nature."
"That's what makes it good. Ah, if I had my own ship, I'd invite him as an exclusive blacksmith. Someone who can compress loot like this—wouldn't you absolutely want such a person on a pirate ship?"
"Please keep an eye on him while accommodating him as much as possible. We need to help him enjoy his 'excursion.' At least that way, he won't become our enemy."
"You, your mind is elsewhere. Is it Oknodie?"
"Are you not worried about Oknodie at all, Jigoku? I remember you also have a debt of gratitude to our little lady."

Jigoku stared at Gisele with an amused expression.

"'You also' implies that at least you have a debt to Oknodie. But there wasn't any occasion at the Academy where you would have incurred a debt to her, was there?"
"Then it must have been a debt from outside."
"Strange. From what our underlings told me, you first met Oknodie during the Ticket Exam. You even hired Son O-cheon as a bodyguard then. There wasn't any opportunity for you to incur a debt to Oknodie, was there?"
"Have you been investigating my background?"
"Why are you playing dumb? You're the one who's been most diligently investigating others among the first-years."

It really is landmines everywhere.
Gisele ignored the topic and moved on.

"Why aren't you worried about Oknodie?"
"Seems like even an information merchant doesn't know everything. Well, you probably didn't have enough time to extract information from our people. And that person wouldn't have been recklessly wandering around either."
"That person...?"
"Why am I not worried about Oknodie? Because there's no need to worry."
"Is there someone? Someone protecting Oknodie? If you're referring to the Foundation's butler, that's a miscalculation. That person is merely the Foundation's agent, and there are concerning factors in considering him Oknodie's loyal servant."
"Who said anything about Foundation people? I'm saying this because an Academy professor is there."

Gisele, who had navigated countless landmines as easily as breathing, couldn't avoid stepping on this one.

"Professor...?"
"From the start, aren't you overestimating me too much? Even for someone like me, infiltrating a state-of-the-art cruise ship's barrier with these ragtag apprentice underlings isn't easy. We were only able to infiltrate because a professor helped us."
"What kind of deranged professor would participate in smuggling while helping pirates with their piracy? Where is this person now and what are they doing?"

He had thought the Academy wouldn't simply stand by and watch as multiple students became entangled with the Foundation.
But now, during the height of the ship's rebellion, where was this professor and what were they doing without showing their face?

"Don't know. If they're not here, they must be somewhere else."

From Jigoku's indifferent response, it seemed the professor wasn't currently on the cruise ship.
If they had disappeared without being seen by the Foundation's crew or the ship's students, there was only one place the professor who had helped with the smuggling could have gone.

"The professor is on the uninhabited island...?"

Despite the serious situation that led to a shipboard rebellion, did they think Oknodie's condition was more dangerous than their own?
Strange.
That's an action no one would find reasonable.
The landmine detector in his head went off.
More intensely and seriously than at any time since entering the Academy.

'What's the situation on the island?'

Gisele immediately took out the mirror connected to his doll, not caring that Jigoku was watching.
On the other side of the magical tool, he could see Jaku and Princess Mesugakki boarding a boat, escaping from Blue Metal rats that were gnawing on everything in swarms of thousands, as if someone had disturbed something they shouldn't have.

'So that's where she was.'

The dwindling number of people at the uninhabited island's auction house.
Oknodie, who was always surrounded by crowds or in places no one could pinpoint, was now trapped in a specific location—the uninhabited island.
More than ever before, Oknodie was trapped in the auction house among fewer people, her location exposed.
By the Papa who invited her.
To the Foundation's scholarship students who knew this fact.

"The professor's name! Tell me the name right now!"
"Why are you making such a fuss?"

Jigoku grumbled but answered readily.

"He said it was Professor Rave, who teaches the Basics and Understanding of Imperial Magic."

It was the name of a professor who harbored clear murderous intent toward Oknodie.
