# 103 - Finders Keepers

There's no such thing as a transfer student at Gift Academy.
It's not because every other academy in the world is inferior to Gift Academy.

"What the fuck!! Headmaster!!!! How can an outsider who hasn't even gone through our Gift Academy's first-year curriculum just become a second-year student?!"
"This is an insult to our character!!! It's an insult to all of us who survived the hell of this academy's first-year curriculum!!! Arghhhh!!!"

It's because of the outpouring of protests from existing students, especially second-years, who feel unbearably wronged.
Protests born from the wicked mentality that others should suffer the same injustices they experienced!
Conversely, most second-years felt sympathy for the juniors who were about to experience the same hardships they had endured.

"Is that really a new student?"
"Isn't that some veteran who's been stuck in first year for like 5 years?"
"Come on, you idiot. The kid is barely 11 years old—how could they have been held back for 5 years? How old would they have been when they enrolled?"
"Wait, wasn't she a legal loli? Is that not true?"
"What the hell are you saying about a child, you sick bastard? Hey, everyone! Let's beat this guy up!"
"Ack! Ack! I didn't know! I was just asking!"

I feel a bit sorry for the senior who mentioned "legal loli."
Honestly, if I saw a first-year entering the secret training ground, setting up target distances perfectly, and conveniently using the automatic arrow retrieval function, I'd be suspicious too.
How many age-frauds are there in fantasy stories?

'There are so many long-lived races claiming to be 90-year-old elves who look 10 in human years!'

The 981st class shows a strong presence of humans, and considering that powerful individuals among non-human races typically don't enroll in the academy, there are no legal lolis in this class.
But if you broaden your view to include other classes, there are definitely legal lolis at Gift Academy.

'But strictly speaking, I've gone through thousands of cycles, so should I really be treated as this age?'

...Well, as long as I don't get caught!


* *


Zuang and Mob were lost, struggling to use the unfamiliar facility.

"Hey, freshmen. How did you even get here if you don't know how to use the facilities?"
"We read advice in the room with double-sided tape."
"What's that supposed to mean?"

Looking at the confused second-year senior, Zuang inwardly marveled.
So Oknodie had obtained information that even the seniors didn't know.
The thought of having access to information left by seniors above the second-years gave her a strange sense of superiority.
What a waste of age.
Lower information level than first-years!
As Zuang was feeling proud inside, the second-year senior grumbled and said:

"Just tell me what you came to do. I'll show you how to use it. Don't ask me to explain twice. I'll only show you once, got it?"
"Yeees."
"Ah, yes, I understand!"

The second-year senior looked at the extremely nervous Mob and smirked.

"I'm not going to eat you, man. Relax."
"Haha..."
"I won't."
"...Do other seniors eat people?!"
"You guys must have heard by now, right? About that annoying second-year who hands out scam contracts."
"Ah, the one who distributes errand contracts..."
"Watch out for that. If you get caught up in it, you'll spend the entire year doing nothing but gathering activities like a slave, and the year will fly by."

Zuang thought to herself.
This senior is quite kind.
Considering the harsh education at the academy, it was surprising that such a good-natured senior existed.
Of course, not all seniors were kind.

"This is ridiculous. First-years already enjoying the secret training ground. Back in my day..."
"Shut up. We were all first-years in your day too. Want me to tell everyone how you completely bombed your grades?"
"Come on, seriously. You want to embarrass me like that?"

A second-year student glaring with hostile eyes and grumbling.

"...If I bring that smart-looking first-year to our lab, would the professor praise me?"
"Hey, how can you call yourself human? How can you say such terrible things to an innocent first-year!"

A third-year student in the corner, emitting an ominous aura while spitting out evil plans.

"...The professor said not to touch the first-years. I must endure. Must endure. Must endure..."
"Wow, that senior is scary even to us. Can't someone chase them away?"
"No way. We're scared too."

Unlike the third-year with clear intentions, there was even a fourth-year student with an insanely unsettling atmosphere whose intentions couldn't even be guessed.
Just as the campus where only first-years used to roam was like a newbie hunting ground, there were many intimidating seniors in the same space.

"Hey, Oknodie. Do you have any tips for using this facility? I mean... *safely*?"

To Zuang's question, Oknodie answered with a bright face.

"I do! About three things."
"Three things?"
"First, don't get close to the seniors!"
"...And?"
"Don't talk to the seniors!"
"..."
"Finally, don't make eye contact with the seniors!"

What is this, ghost treatment?
I'm not still in the middle of 5th period class, am I?
I'm not wandering in the rain trapped in an illusion, right?
Regardless of Zuang's troubled feelings.
The cheerful Oknodie, who was irritatingly upbeat, focused only on shooting arrows at the target without making eye contact with anyone.
Maybe coming here was a mistake.
The terrified Zuang and Mob thought they understood why the school rule prohibiting leaving the dormitory at night was created.
Perhaps that rule was a minimal safety measure to protect first-years.
To put it bluntly, there might even be a rule for second-years and above that it's okay to do bad things to first-years wandering outside the dormitory after 10 PM.

"...Let's finish what we need to do and go back quickly."
"Yeah..."

And so the three first-years silently completed their assignment.


* *


Among the classes with assignments pouring in, there was one uniquely clean class without any assignments.
It was Professor Platon's class, "Advanced Physical Enhancement," held during first period on Tuesdays and Thursdays.

"Ughh. I had to swim for three hours in supplementary education over the weekend."
"If I added up all the water I swallowed, it might be as much as all the water I've drunk in my lifetime."
"Gahahah! You're all such weaklings, aren't you?"

Following the previous class, this class was also set up as a swimming lesson for students weak in water.
Unlike the last class where more than half the students had to practice how to float, this time most had mastered how to breathe while swimming.

"Therefore, today's class has increased in difficulty!"

Despite attending a class with no assignments, the students felt fear rather than gratitude.
No student could be happy attending a class filled with murderous intent, where the professor intended to finish them off with just one lecture and no assignments.
Above all, there was a particular reason why they couldn't swim right now.

"Due to the continuous downpour causing the river to overflow!"
"Is class canceled?"
"Is there an alternative class?"
"Wow! Swimming pool!"
"We will proceed with a class to cross the overflowing river!"
"We'll die!!!"
"Just because we're first-years doesn't mean we agree to everything!"
"Are you trying to kill the empire's precious treasures?!"

This was going too far. For once, everyone, regardless of group, firmly refused.
Even Casia from Group C was shaking her head in refusal.
The instructor in charge of supervising her was also giving a look from behind that said, "Are you crazy, Professor?"

"Well. What a bunch of weaklings."

Professor Platon grumbled.

"Back in the day, you know, when a professor spoke, students would tremble and answer 'Yes, sir!' with vigor. These days, even the headmaster has gotten soft with age..."

It was worse before?!
While the shocked students were processing this, this kind of class was problematic from my perspective too.

"Professor!"
"What is it, Oknodie?"
"Can we take a break today and work twice as hard on the weekend instead?"
"Twice as hard?"

My classmates nodded eagerly.
With giant crabs clicking their pincers, ready to attack anyone who entered, swimming there could really lead to multiple funerals.

"Fine! If even Oknodie, who always gets the best results, says so, and everyone else agrees, as a professor, I have no choice but to reflect my students' opinions."
"Yay!"
"We're saved!"
"Thank you, Oknodie!"
"But... you said it first? That you'd work *twice* as hard."

A contract with a professor is like a contract with the devil.
Today's happiness is tomorrow's misery.
The students began to tremble with inexplicable anxiety.

"Maybe it would have been better to take the class now?"
"Now that the Giant King Crab is gone, what if he brings even worse monsters to the next class?"
"I'm so scared..."

Whether they were belatedly anxious or not, the students were kicked out of the lecture hall.
While everyone was using their imagination to compete over whose idea of what horrific method of teaching awaited them on the weekend was the most terrifying.
The delinquent who had no interest in such trivial games approached me with determined steps.

"Hey, Oknodie. Do you have free time now?"
"Until second period."
"Let's go treasure hunting."

Jigoku looked around cautiously and whispered secretly in a low voice.

"I heard one of the seniors' warehouses was swept away in this downpour. You know the saying at sea—finders keepers!"

If we consider the flooded academy grounds as the sea, it wasn't entirely wrong.
