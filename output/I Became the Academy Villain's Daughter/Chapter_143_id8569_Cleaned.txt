# Dark Mana

The boulder that Mob had pushed with all his might was carried back onto the bridge by the teaching assistants, who wobbled as they walked with it in their hands.

"Professor, is this angle right?"
"You've put it on backward. Flip it 180 degrees."
"Oh, please."

The assistant sighed as he lifted the boulder into the air, adjusting his grip bit by bit to get the angle right.
Seeing his pitiful state, Son O-cheon remarked:

"It suddenly feels like a real student exam. They're playing around with something that was so difficult to push."
"They're sweating quite a bit though. It must not be completely effortless."
"That's because they're third-years! Fourth-years would be different!"

At Oknodie's cheerful exclamation, Son O-cheon and Gisele simply accepted it as the way things were.

"Place it down with the red line facing downward."
"Like this?"
"Good work. Though the exam has been delayed by five minutes."
"Ah..."
"If you're late again, I'll deduct points from your allowance."

As the assistant returned to the grassy area marked with a sign as the waiting area, Isabel noticed that the seat of his pants was stained with grass and dirt.

"I'm never becoming a teaching assistant."

While she was experiencing the harsh reality of third-year life, Isabel became aware of piercing gazes directed at her.
During her time as a member of an expedition, she had been exposed to such killing intent before.

Bandits waiting for the right moment to attack adventurers or explorers for their possessions.
Highway robbers disguised as merchant caravans encountered on remote mountain roads.
Monsters camouflaged as natural objects, holding their breath as they waited for exploration teams to approach.

It was no different now.
The crystal orbs floating in the air revived her sense of real combat that she had forgotten for a while.

"Gisele. What are those orbs?"
"They're the professors' monitoring orbs."
"...Showing killing intent toward a student who helped a failing student, instead of praising them—these professors are incomprehensible."

There's something strange about this Academy.
Much stranger than she had ever felt before.

"If you want to complain, you can do so later. More importantly, it seems the next participant is about to start."

At Gisele's words, Isabel pushed aside her uneasy feelings and turned her gaze toward the bridge.
A longsword slightly longer than an arm's length.
It seemed like a poor weapon choice for dealing with a massive boulder.

"Looks like he's nervous."
"Or perhaps he's confident."
"Little mouse, what do you think?"
"I see it in 3D!"
"What's 3D?"

Oknodie suddenly became dejected and lowered her head.

"Never mind if you don't know."


* * *


Her brilliant joke had failed.
She was freshly reminded of the fact that she had become a resident of a world different from the one she had lived in until now.
They couldn't share the same memes, the same humor, the same memories.
Though she knew more than anyone else.
No one knew what she knew.
While feeling the sorrow of being a possessed person, she noticed an unusual fluctuation of mana emanating from Jaku's sword at the bridge.

Mana.
The basic material unit for wielding supernatural abilities.

There are various types, but ordinary people can usually only wield one kind of mana.
To handle mana, one must complete a puzzle with mana of the same attribute, like filling one face of a cube with a single color.
The most common attribute that people possess is non-attribute mana, which doesn't display any particular property.

'That's why they say a swordsman's blade aura is basically formless and colorless, making it difficult to gauge distance.'

However, very occasionally, those who grow up in special environments, achieve unique accomplishments, are born with skewed attributes, or undergo special rituals can have color imbued in their mana.
That's why natives living in flame zones or snowy mountains can produce blade auras colored red or blue.
Regardless of color, the mere fact of being able to emit blade aura is remarkable.

'Even Mob only used his energy for physical enhancement rather than external projection.'

When one continues extreme training without giving up, those with mana try to use it to reduce the burden on their body.
That's the beginning of mana breathing techniques and the foundation of mana manipulation.
It's the stage where simple sword fights with clashing blades evolve into battles between mana practitioners who can cut through anything—wood, metal, or stone.

"You see that guy? His energy seems more unusual than that Mob fellow from earlier."

With the instincts of a wild beastkin, Son O-cheon sensed the difference in power.
Physical enhancement versus blade aura projection.
Though different fields, the latter was clearly stronger.
It couldn't be weak when concentrating all the mana that would normally flow throughout the body into a single weapon in hand.
However, unlike Mob who had complete control over his power, Jaku's sword created cracks in the boulder's surface with terrible ripping sounds.

"When did Jaku get so strong?!"
"Great, break it! Smash it to pieces so there's nothing left by the time it's our turn!"
"Wow. If Jaku destroys it, can we cross the bridge for free?"
"Go Jaku!"
"Jaku, fighting!"

Seemingly deaf to the first-years' cheers with their selfish intentions, Jaku swung his sword again and again.
The sword struck relentlessly, the cracks grew larger, and rock dust flew into the air as the cheers grew louder.
Even other spectators in the audience couldn't hide their excitement and joined in the cheering.

"This is dangerous."

Someone else had realized the danger that I thought only I would recognize.
The Academy's flag scammer.
The devil who had unleashed the poison of a ceilingless double gacha.
The dark merchant Gisele.

"You noticed it too?"
"The color of his blade aura is dangerous."

Blue blade aura indicates a water-attribute swordsman.
One might think they've just played in the sea a bit.
Red blade aura indicates a fire-attribute swordsman.
One might think they've played around a volcanic crater.
But "black" was different.
Black, the symbol of all negative energy.
The closer to pitch black, the greater the danger.
Darkness and despair.
Death and emptiness.
It's not an attribute born from a proper environment.
All black is a color leaning toward evil.
The demon king's kin, the demon race.
The demon race's underlings, the demon humans.
The demon beasts controlled by demons and demon humans.
It's the color symbolizing the source of all wicked power.
If one fails to control it even slightly, it's a power that will devour its user and lead to self-destruction—and a first-year is wielding it.

'Somehow nostalgic!'

There was a time when she was obsessed with dark swordsmen, so-called dark knights.
How many times had she unknowingly commanded the power of darkness only to face terrible destruction?
Yet, as the saying goes, old habits die hard—Jaku was demonstrating why she had repeatedly walked the path of the dark swordsman.

"The destructive power is no joke."

Its destructive power rivals fire attributes, and its penetration power rivals lightning attributes.
Its sustainability is no less than wind attributes, and its recovery power is no less than water attributes.
A power that even Son O-cheon finds impressive.
What makes this possible is dark-attribute mana.
That's why right now, Jaku's body must undoubtedly be under tremendous strain.

'A power that devours its user in proportion to usage time. He's already used it for too long.'

If he had little mana, he would have collapsed and fainted long ago, but for some reason, Jaku showed no signs of running out of mana.
If he lacked aptitude, he would have self-destructed and died, but even that seemed unlikely as his muscles just bulged more aggressively, with no signs of impending death.

"Son O-cheon, you still have much to learn!"
"Little mouse, what have you figured out that makes you so smug?"
"The loud noise and the boulder continuously breaking is evidence that he's not properly controlling his power. If the impact point was well-aligned, the force wouldn't disperse like that."

Abundant mana.
Sufficient aptitude to endure.
Control that fails to match these.
All three conditions were met.
Instead of the side effect of destroying the body, the dark mana was set to target and destroy the mind first.

"Graaaah!"

With a thunderous boom, the massive boulder shattered.
Cheers erupted from the lower-class students.
Dark mana continued to blaze along the drooping sword.

"Examinee Jaku. Congratulations on passing the test. You may now withdraw your energy."
"..."
"Examinee Jaku?"

Jaku's drooping head creaked as it turned toward the instructor who called his name.

"He did it! Jaku really broke the boulder!"
"Woohoo! With the boulder gone, we get to pass for free!"
"Thank you, Jaku! You're the hero of the Knight Department!"

The head that had turned toward the instructor suddenly whipped around to face the classmates who were cheering and calling his name.
I know what's about to happen.

Berserk rage.
Mass murder.
Indiscriminate slaughter.

A mild taste of the carnage that would have occurred at the end of the first semester by the hands of chapter boss Hestia.
The beginning of an incident demonstrating the danger of all evil-leaning beings and dark mana.

"Whoa, Jaku. It's dangerous to emit blade aura toward people!"
"Something seems off about Jaku's condition."
"His eyes are unfocused. Did he lose consciousness from using too much power?"
"W-wait. He's not withdrawing his blade aura. Isn't this dangerous?"
"D-don't come! Jaku, don't come this way!"

The students backed away in terror.
Jaku followed their steps.
Everyone retreated in a fan shape to avoid him, then began to flee.

Whoosh!

Burning even more dark mana, Jaku took a sprinter's stance as if ready to charge at any moment.

"Wait, Oknodie! Where are you going?"
"Little mouse, have you lost your mind?!"
"...No, it's better not to stop her here."

As expected, Gisele understood.
Unlike my two naive companions, Isabel and Son O-cheon, Gisele realized my intention and held them back from interfering.
With both the teaching assistants and instructors confused by the sudden situation, no one was there to stop me as I jumped down from the audience seats and landed in the testing area.

"Running into me—we're both lucky!"

I shouted loudly toward Jaku while pointing my sword at him.
At the same time, black mana blazed from the tip of my sword.

"That's... dark mana?!"
"I had a bad feeling about this since the eye-training lecture."
"So it's come to this, as expected."

Jaku turned toward me instead of the lower-class students.
Watching him, Gisele said:

"They say possessors of dark mana are drawn to other possessors of dark mana. Oknodie revealed her power to contain Jaku's rampage."
"What? Then isn't that a good thing? The little mouse isn't going to get hurt with her skills."
"It's not a matter of skill. It's a matter of perception."

I wish he hadn't noticed that much.
Gisele, whose perceptiveness was excessive, had even anticipated the consequences of revealing my power.

"Even a lower-class student with little power is currently failing to control it and causing problems. What would happen in an emergency if an upper-class student possessed such power?"

Isabel answered with a trembling voice:

"...A bomb. She'd be treated like a bomb that could go off at any moment."
"And a very high-performance bomb at that. One that might blow away the entire upper class."
"Then she shouldn't have stepped forward! Why did that idiot step forward?"
"Probably because she's too kind. That's our little lady's nature."
