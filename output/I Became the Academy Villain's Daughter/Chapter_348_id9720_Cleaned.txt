# Enhanced Translation: Chapter 348 - Organizational Conflict

A story where you know the beginning and end from the start is boring.
The same goes for games.

"That stupid streamer. Claims he was busy with real life, but his reactions are so fake. He's not even exploring properly—must've spent his break just playing games."

No matter how well you pretend to be playing a game for the first time when you've already played it before, it always shows.
Just like that streamer who had already seen the early graduation ending of "Graduating from the Academy by Luck"—the tedium of predictable days can't fool anyone.
A veteran player is someone who fights against that boredom, repeating the same game thousands or tens of thousands of times.
That's why unexpected events bring joy.
The 0.1% bad luck.
The surprise of rare probability.
Jaku's sudden action showed me that 0.1%.

"Bring the contract. Just this once, I, Rogini, apprentice wizard of the Red Magic Tower, will burn it for you."
"Ah, they're running!"
"Sand Cooker."
"I've already placed a transformation spell on the door lock. Anywhere connected to the floor or walls is the domain of an earth mage."

With their escape route cut off and their contract in danger of burning, the lower-class female students shouted fiercely.

"What did we ever do to you to deserve this?!"
"Jewel is my pet! He agreed to be my obedient pet in exchange for me filling in his lacking theory scores. We made a fair contract!"
"Woof woof."
"Just because he's barking like a dog doesn't mean he looks happy."
"Shut up! Jewel, I command you. Defeat them!"

As soon as the delicate-looking male student charged with his sword, Mob rushed forward in full body armor.
Jewel's sword repeatedly parried Mob's attacks, who was boldly taking the offensive trusting in his armor's defense, but each time Jewel's body shook violently.
The difference in physical training created a difference in the weight behind each sword strike, and skilled swordsmanship prevented perfect deflection, accumulating impact.

Tremble.

When he failed to parry due to the accumulated shock in his hands, Mob pinned Jewel's sword to the ground and twisted his wrist to subdue him.
The female student who lost her faithful dog and had her contract taken struggled, but Jaku mercilessly slapped her to subdue her and pulled out the contract.

"Here, burn it."
"You've become strangely proactive? There was still some reluctance left in you before."
"I realized I could just create and provide information myself."
"Information?"
"It's nothing."
"Ah. You mean information to report to professors or the student council after this is over? Without an explanation, we'd just be a gang that meddled in others' contracts and committed assault for no reason. You've thought about the aftermath before even getting involved—Jaku, you're quite thorough."

The more praise he receives, the more his expression becomes one of enlightenment, and the feeling that he's going all out regardless of consequences is all amusing.

"Congratulations. You're free now."

After the contract burned, Mob thought there was no reason to fight anymore and released his hold to help Jewel up. Jewel's fist struck Mob's helmet.

"What have you done?!"
"Is the contract not fully burned, so the command is still working?"
"Don't be ridiculous. Even without that contract, I was helping her voluntarily!"
"What?? But why??"
"A pet isn't human. So showing shameful behavior isn't embarrassing. The more May believed that, the more defenseless she became in front of me—how happy that made me!"
"Wait... this guy is way too honest about his desires?"
"While May treated me as a pet and I accepted it, no point redemption occurred, but I was willing to accept such a sweet contract anyway. When she bathed me saying pet hygiene was the owner's responsibility, I thought I could live like this forever!"

Mob said incredulously:

"Shouldn't we take this guy to the student council and report him for inappropriate relations?"
"Ahem. I suppose we could."
"What's so unusual about grown adults showering together?"

The lukewarm reactions from Rogini and Sand Cooker are also amusing.
These two were always rivals in other playthroughs.
Both apprentice wizards.
Rookies representing different towers.
With the support and pride of their respective towers on their backs, they would grow distant whenever they started getting close, hurting each other with words contrary to their true feelings until they became complete enemies.
If they formed a party with the player and went through dungeons together, suffering enough, they might become somewhat friendly—"decent enough skilled companions" at best.
But seeing them now as such an obvious couple after just one boat trip—even though Jonna and I handled all the dangerous work for them—really makes this feel like a special playthrough.

"If you like it that much, nothing has to change even without the contract, right? I'd say it's an opportunity to connect sincerely without using the contract as an excuse."

Jewel and May's eyes trembled violently as they looked at each other.

"Jewel... aren't you angry that I used the contract to make you do... things? I deliberately gave you tasks you couldn't complete to increase your debt and made you work so hard..."
"I knew. But I liked this relationship so much that sometimes I deliberately failed tasks I could have completed to avoid redemption."
"Jewel...!"
"May."

Only Jaku and Mob's expressions soured, having inadvertently made a couple honest with each other.
One after another, the Camella faction's contractors were defeated by the aggressive solo actions of these two angry individuals!

"Are there nothing but couples among these guys?!"
"I'm feeling good about doing something nice. Thanks to us, many couples have become honest about their feelings."
"Not all of them connected, though. Still, it's interesting. This Camella person seems to have tied potential couples together in the form of contracts—they all cared about each other quite a bit."

To the first-playthrough NPCs, it might look like divine harmony or the work of an amazing romance expert, but for someone who knows the truth, a future where you already know the answers isn't that surprising.
What's more surprising is Jaku cutting these ties before the couples became too accustomed to the domination and submission of their contractual relationships.

"How about you, Jaku?"
"I feel good. Breaking up all these couples. What about you, Oknodie?"
"I feel good too!"
"That's unexpected. I thought someone like you, who's loved by everyone, would be the type to like and support couples."
"Me? No way."

Even during my "ALL-IN-ON-STRENGTH-I-LOVE-ONE-HIT-CHARACTERS" days, I tore apart any hint of coupling between NPCs until my mid-level intermediate days.
The reason is simple.
They get weaker because they spend less time training while playing couple, their synergy effects only work between themselves, forcing constraints on party formation and reducing efficiency.
Yet when you actually form parties matched between couples, for some reason fights suddenly break out.
If they get the "Breakup" debuff, the party atmosphere becomes like a funeral home.
Sometimes they intentionally don't cast buffs on ex-boyfriends, or while tanking, they subtly direct mobs toward ex-girlfriends.
How inconsiderate!

"Couples shouldn't exist. They're nothing but a hindrance. Especially the weak ones!"
"Heh. That's right. The weak ones shouldn't be playing at love games."

There are couples who grow stronger together through the power of love, but they're extremely rare.
That's why Xing is working hard not to suffer bad luck!
Even a veteran player like me puts in this much effort, so I can't stand by and watch newbies flirting without knowing anything!

'If I can't have it, neither can they!'


* * *


After breaking up five couples, the remaining couples of the Camella faction launched a counterattack.

"The Dark Guild's warehouse caught fire. Fortunately, it was discovered early and the fire was successfully extinguished. The attackers are presumed to be members of the Camella faction."
"Hmph. With Arcadia around, a fire of this level is nothing. Using know-how from protecting warehouses and ammunition depots from fire attacks during naval battles, fires are quickly contained."
"They attacked our members who were returning after lectures! There were no casualties!"
"Who was there?"
"Titoso's illumination squad's blinding attack broke their formation, so we beat them up and chased them away!"

The Camella faction is making many attempts but achieving little success due to the difference in member skill levels!
But even if they're just a collection of small fry, they wouldn't become dangerous enough to be chapter bosses even with growth opportunities all the way through the first semester of sophomore year.

"This is terrible! My assignment was stolen!"
"Oh no... the dormitory door is open?"
"Kyaaah! My underwear is gone!"
"Want to borrow a bikini armor?"
"I don't need it!!"

While everyone's attention was focused on the power struggle, the Camella faction had committed burglary.
Even Hestia returned to the dormitory after training and knocked on the door with a serious expression.

"My mini golem was stolen. How's your plant friend, Oknodie? Is it okay?"
"Ngae."

The baby mandrake was sucking on its fine roots and tilted its head.
There's a subtle smell of blood, suggesting there was an intruder, but they were likely taught a harsh lesson by the baby and driven away.
But... the place where the assignment should be is empty.
How did this happen?

"Baby, where did the assignment that was here go?"
"Ngae?"

The baby looks confused, having seen nothing.
Just like a plant, it only fought within the range its roots could reach!

"As you can see, the baby is fine. But all the assignments were stolen..."
"I was just training and not bothering the weak ones, but now that I've been affected, I can't just stand by. Your assignment too. Let me help."
"Having a pet you worked hard to tame stolen crosses a line. Touching assignments crosses an even bigger line. Let's raid their dormitory tonight without sleeping!"

As I headed up to the lower-class students' dormitory with Hestia, I saw a dormitory supervisor in the hallway.

"Ugh. They requested security service. This is going to be troublesome."
"That's a thing?"
"If you pay points, supervisors or instructors will guard your room. Since it's a group room, if everyone splits the cost, it's not an unaffordable price. There's probably a special discount for first-years too."
"Then what do we do? The supervisor isn't just a supervisor for nothing."
"A frontal confrontation is burdensome. But there's a way."

If you spend points, you get protection?
If you spend more points, the opposite is also possible.

"I'll give you more points. Please step aside!"
"Only accepting immediate transfer."

I placed my magic watch on the mana board the supervisor held out and paid the points.
The supervisor, momentarily surprised at the balance, stepped aside.
When we opened the dormitory door from the front, the remaining members of the Camella faction, who had been staying awake all night watching the window in preparation for an attack, were startled.

"Oknodie? Did you defeat the supervisor...?"
"Messing with the baby fight, pets, and assignments crossed the line. Return them now! Otherwise, I'll beat you up!"
"What are you talking about? We couldn't steal anything from your room because of that demon-possessed crazy plant!"

These impudent newbies!
I was going to go easy on them if they confessed, but they're really asking for a beating!
