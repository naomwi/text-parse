# 176 - A Glimpse of the Divergence of Destruction

A curse is similar to a disease.
In the case of a fatal disease, it initially remains dormant, causing only mild symptoms, but when it enters the middle stage, it begins to deliver genuine pain.
It disrupts the body's vital signs, destroys the immune system, and finally enters the terminal stage, taking away life.

A curse has no transmission process.
Instead, to prevent easy detection of its source, only mild symptoms appear in the early stages.
As time passes, the symptoms gradually worsen, and when entering the terminal stage, it takes away life.

'But to think the death experience curse could become this serious. If I reported this to the academic society, the whole world would be shocked.'

A sight that might cause curse practitioners worldwide to surge in number.
The end of the human world unfolded in Oknodie's nightmare.

Guooooo.

A life that has slept for ages unleashes its fury upon the insignificant byproducts derived from its own fabric.
How on earth could a death experience curse manifest so extremely?
The curse he knew wasn't like this.
Usually, a random pedestrian suddenly attacks with a knife.
A merchant hands over a poisoned apple, causing collapse after consumption.
A seductive woman pulls a dagger from her thigh and stabs the back of the neck—that kind of curse.

A curse constantly analyzes.
When this person might let their guard down.
What kind of curse they might fall victim to.
It chooses increasingly lethal and threatening methods.
What emerges at the end is typically the strongest realistic threat a person can perceive.
Usually just a domain knight or an unidentified wizard.
The solution is simple.
A curse-breaking practitioner enters the dream and explains theoretically that real knights and wizards aren't invincible beings, and explains how excellent their own response is.
When the person accepts this, lowering the skill level of the knight and wizard while raising the skill level of the curse-breaker, the situation ends.
The curse's manifestation can be killed by force, allowing escape.

For it to grow and grow until a primordial giant rises.
