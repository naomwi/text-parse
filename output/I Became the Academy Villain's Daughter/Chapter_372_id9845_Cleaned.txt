# Chapter 372 - The Usual Occurrence

The 2nd Hat Lady wasn't just some poor student who had been trapped in the "Answer Room" and then released?
This unexpected connection surprised even me, a veteran player.

"Hmm?"

If she wrote the letters in that letter box, that means she was one of the Foundation's Ladies and one of Jonna's previous Ladies.
So the 2nd Hat Lady was a senior Lady!?

"Hmm. So this is how things can turn out!"

It's fascinating, but it all fits together perfectly.
Typically, character backstories, affection events, and unique storylines often involve hidden people or objects related to that character.
Like the story behind certain items or secrets that surrounding characters are hiding.
Even Zuang's "mask" is related to her unique storyline, right?
Having a previous Lady hidden around me, the current Foundation Lady—this setting is a perfect event for my unique storyline as Oknodie, a playable character.

'Looking at it this way, I really feel like I've become a game character!'

Honestly, it's fun.
There's a deep sense of fulfillment in becoming part of a world I've enjoyed playing through countless times.

"Is it okay not to tell Jonna?"
"About what, my Lady?"
[Please don't tell him yet. I don't want him to discover my identity. I don't want to be reclaimed by the Foundation either.]

I wonder what would happen if I told him.
But she doesn't want me to, so I'll respect that.

"My Lady?"
"It's nothing. I've made my decision. This is how we'll proceed."

My hand reached for the table and in one swift motion, I set fire to the letter box.

[Sudden event <Pandora's Box> completed]
[You chose not to open the box containing unknown dangers.]
[For the sake of peace, you turned away from catastrophe. A vague sense of foreboding that today's avoided crisis might someday bring another crisis briefly surfaces and disappears.]
[There is no reward for this choice.]
[Due to curiosity about the unread letter, you are afflicted with the status ailment <Insomnia>.]

Jonna looked at me with surprised eyes.
Zuang and the 2nd Hat Lady did the same.

"Why is everyone looking at me like that?"
"I thought for sure you'd open it, given the flow of things."
"Oh come on. Zuang, how can you be so clueless?"

When newbies are this blind, I can't help but lecture them from my veteran perspective.

"Listen carefully. If there's a linked quest and you have to choose between taking the reward without accepting the quest or accepting the quest but suffering a penalty, you should always choose the latter!"
"Quest? Choice?"
"I believe she's referring to the ancient wisdom that turning away from immediate difficulties for sweet immediate rewards will lead to regret in the future."
"As expected, Jonna is smart!"

That's exactly what I meant.
It's similar to the "wait" experiment with dogs.
How frustrating would it be if you couldn't resist eating one piece of dog food right away, only to find out later that your owner was hiding a whole handful behind their back?
You'd be so upset you couldn't sleep at night, whining in your kennel.
Linked quests have similar traps.
If you're blinded by small gains, you'll make the mistake of losing something bigger.
Sure, new scripts make my mouth water, but if I can endure, the reward will be greater!
Besides, either way is a new experience for a veteran like me.
Since the 2nd Hat Lady confessed that she wrote some of the letters, I can secretly learn about their contents, essentially removing the insomnia penalty.
Under such favorable conditions, there's no reason to make the mistake of opening the letter box!

"Are you sure? The Foundation won't sit still."
"It's fine. I can pretty much predict what Papa will do!"

Now that I know the Foundation is behind all these seemingly unconnected game events, I can roughly predict what's coming.
What events might be tied to the Foundation and threaten my academy life.

'The way to torment a professor is to torment the students who attend their lectures!'

It's time for the Foundation's mid-level ninjas—no, mid-level spies—to start operating.
My job now is to identify the Foundation's mid-level scholarship spies!
What should I do after finding them?
Should I quietly dispose of them one by one through "accidents" like in the game?
There was already content starting from the second semester of sophomore year where you had to identify and eliminate spy instructors from external forces or spy seniors from criminal organizations, so it's not entirely unfamiliar.
Well, that's not something to worry about right now!

"Anyway, are you satisfied now, Jonna?"
"My Lady... are you really okay with this?"
"With what?"
"Can you continue our relationship without harboring any suspicions, even if the past and flaws of the butler who serves you remain buried in darkness?"

I don't think I'll have any suspicions, especially since the 2nd Hat Lady who knows about Jonna's past is right here above my head.
Even if there's something the Hat Lady doesn't know, I'm not the type to get angry about character settings.

"Do you have any ex-girlfriends among the Ladies?"
"I have never engaged in inappropriate romantic relationships with any Ladies."
"Have you ever flirted with Ladies while helping them with weightlifting?"
"I would never joke about important training that builds the Ladies' blood and flesh."
"Then we're good! No problem at all."
"Still, I feel regretful for burdening you unnecessarily. Perhaps I am unqualified as a butler..."
"Don't make that face!"

For a butler to make such a despairing expression like a Titosogasu whose assignments got soaked in the rain.
It makes me feel like I'm some evil employer.
During my <I Love All-Strength One-Hit Characters Marine> days, the butler would sometimes have that gloomy face, which makes me feel particularly guilty.
I did occasionally ask him to gather materials I was too lazy to collect myself, or to buy delicious food from around the world with capital funds, or to hoard enhancement materials while avoiding the empire's eyes, but I always paid his salary well with bonuses on top.
I still don't understand why the butler acted that way back then.
I won't let Jonna make that face.

"Capable characters usually have less-than-perfect backstories. Even Irene, when she was young, accidentally killed a soldier by freezing him with her immature ice magic skills!"
"..."
"But she grew up to become a wonderful cool beauty, so we can look past such incidents. We also learn why she developed such a cold personality. Background settings are wonderful things, so there's no reason to dislike them!"

Zuang's gaze from under her mask feels extremely sharp.

"What?"
"Sigh. Whatever. It's foolish to even ask at this point."

Despite my mature attempt at consolation, Zuang's response was quite unsatisfactory.
Jonna's worries seemed to deepen as well.

"I see... I will engrave your words of encouragement in my heart like gold and jade. I pledge to serve you more sincerely from now on."

Jonna spoke with a face full of determination.
I'm glad he seems to have found his motivation!


* * *


'Coward.'

After advising him to make his own decision, this is what I see after he called me over following a night of contemplation.

"Leaving an important decision to a woman—what a cowardly man."
"Wuu. Zuang~ Don't speak badly of Jonna!"
"You're problematic too, Oknodie. Getting played by such a bad man. I'm worried about your future."
"Jonna is not a bad man!"
"Sure, sure. Whatever you say."

Zuang turned away primly.
Jonna stopped her as she was about to leave.

"I heard you're looking for a missing student named Dobi. If you find that student, you should visit the research labs of the peripheral professors."
"...Sigh. Your Foundation really knows everything, doesn't it? Did you know everything from the beginning and just pretend not to know?"

I'm starting to understand where Oknodie learned her habit of wielding asymmetrical information as if standing above others.

"Oknodie."
"What?"
"You can play with the hat later. Help me for a bit."
"Hmm... if it's only for 1 hour and 45 minutes!"
"What are you doing after 1 hour and 45 minutes?"
"Training functions when the cooldown returns!"
"Hiding again?"
"No. Observation!"
"You're training such strange functions. Why observation all of a sudden?"
"My observation and discernment skills increased tremendously while playing with Papa. If I raise them a little more, I might unlock a new function bonus!"

So Oknodie learned observation and discernment from that chairman...
This couldn't be more ominous.
But digging into that landmine is a task for later.
For now, it's time to find out which research lab is holding Dobi, who was the catalyst for all these events.

"Oh? The 2nd Hat Lady says she has a professor in mind!"
"Hat Lady?"
"The 2nd Hat Lady is the second consciousness residing in the Dark Aptitude Evaluation Hat brought from the Foundation! I imprisoned the 1st Hat Lady in the wall. Want to go see her? We could say hello and check how she's doing."
"Not interested."
"Hmph."
"How does that Dark Aptitude Evaluation Hat know which professor might have kidnapped Dobi?"

Oknodie, who had been holding the hat with both hands and thinking seriously, suddenly made an "I got it!" expression.

"Professor Weird kidnaps failing lower-grade students to his lab around this time every year—students who lack talent and perseverance for studies or training, with no expected achievements!"
"...What does he make them do after kidnapping them? I don't think such stupid students could handle the level of work a professor would require."
"Last year, one had to wake up spirits needed for practical training."
"Spirits? Spirit summoning requires quite a bit of aptitude. Wouldn't that be a talented student?"
"The person who wakes a spirit from deep slumber gets beaten up badly by the spirit, so he needed someone to take the beating instead!"
"..."

A lab with a punching bag slave? What kind of place is Professor Weird's research lab?
Zuang recalled the information she knew about Professor Weird.

-Professor Weird wears flowers in his hair!
-His figure is amazing...
-I wish all women dressed like Professor Weird. It's so nature-friendly.
-But his written exams are fucking hell.
-Ugh... he's really great but such an asshole professor...
-Professor Weird is recruiting first-years for his lab. Should I go? There probably won't be written exams there.
-Seniors told me absolutely not to go.
-Why?
-They say he makes you eat stones.

The expressions of students discussing Professor Weird all started with excitement and ended with agony.
Do I really have to visit such a professor's lab...
As Zuang's wariness toward Professor Weird began to outweigh her pity for Dobi, she fell into serious contemplation about whether she should go or not.
