# 67 - A Child Who Looks Pitiful

A cruel dryad obsessed with tree spirits, oblivious to human emotions.
A heartless demon who divides the vast blackboard into five sections, completes five pages worth of notes at once, and erases everything in less than a minute.
Even worse, a heretical note-taking hell that delivers utter despair by starting a second round of notes on the freshly erased board.

Professor Weird's reputation plummeted instantly.
The students trembled with betrayal.

"Imperial professors weren't wrong to call her an uncivilized vine-dweller!"
"Argh, I shouldn't have been curious about nature magic!"
"If only the professor's enticing underboob beneath those leaves hadn't seduced my eyes, I would never have taken this class!"

Students vented their frustrations to the point of awakening to regional discrimination.
Dorothy extended a helping hand to these pitiful students who had learned the dangers of beauty's allure through firsthand experience.

"Hey, did you all attend the third period lecture today? I'm selling notes for pages 1-3 and 2-3 for 10 points each. Want to buy them?"

The students shed tears of gratitude.

"I'll buy them! Please let me buy them!"
"Where did you get such wonderful notes?"
"I'm selling them on behalf of Oknodie. I didn't take these notes myself."

The students who had struggled to take notes finally caught their breath.
From the next lecture onward, they planned to attend in groups of at least five and implement a note-taking area division marking strategy, with each person responsible for a different section. This way, they wouldn't have to suffer the same ordeal again.
Since this was their first encounter with such a situation, they just needed to acquire the notes for this particular lecture that they couldn't properly record.

"Thank you. You saved us. Those Imperial jerks would never share their notes with us even if they finished taking them."
"I completely agree."
"But how did Oknodie think to take notes for exactly pages 1-3 and 2-3? Is it because she's the top student and so smart?"
"I don't know."

This was a mystery that Dorothy was also curious about.
So instead of retreating to her room as was her habit upon returning to the dormitory, she knocked on Oknodie's door.

"Oknodie has a full schedule of lectures until fifth period."
"Fifth period...? Are there lectures that late?"
"It's an evening lecture. I asked the instructor, and he said it's prepared for nocturnal students. But who are you?"
"I'm Dorothy. I've been indebted to Oknodie since the entrance exam. I live in room 120."
"Oknodie has many friends. I knew that, but..."

Hestia couldn't hide her bitterness.
Dorothy spoke to her in a bright and cheerful voice.

"You're Hestia, who moved into the room next to Oknodie's, right?"
"That's right."
"How was it? Being in the same group as Oknodie."

This wasn't what she had originally wanted to ask, but she couldn't help being curious.
Being in the same group as the top student Oknodie, who had stood out since the entrance exam.
From Dorothy's perspective, having indirectly experienced her extraordinary abilities in the "Basics and Understanding of Mana Usage" lecture, it was natural to be curious.

"First of all, it's intimidating."
"I figured as much. She's remarkable in many ways."
"Beyond that, I think she's pitiful."
"Huh? Oknodie? Why?"

If the youngest admitted student, youngest top student, and record-breaker in all sorts of "youngest" categories was pitiful, then ordinary people like herself would have to be third-world refugees.

"Haven't you noticed? That child always aims for nothing less than absolute perfection in everything she does."
"Isn't that natural for a top student?"
"It's not natural. Oknodie is only 11 years old."
"Even if she's bold for her age, early education isn't that uncommon. Some children are just precocious."
"You must not have seen it, but Oknodie used a killing technique against the Imperial fighter Lotto. And in Professor Platon's lecture, she took the position behind the Northern Grand Duchess."
"...That is a bit strange."
"Do you see the direction of her early education? This is evidence supporting the theory that Oknodie is an assassin. It's abnormal how she followed behind the ice mage, prepared to die with them."

Hestia viewed Oknodie's obsession with perfect results as a bad habit stemming from a wrong educational environment.

"That must be it. Being beaten if she doesn't produce results, surviving only if she's the best. If she grew up in such a vicious environment, it would make sense."
"That's child abuse!"
"Do you think people who raised a child as an assassin in a cold-blooded noble family would care about that? If you've ever taken on noble families' dirty work as a mercenary, you'd understand."

Hestia is a skilled berserker warrior.
As such, she had participated in a group mission with other renowned mercenaries from the Mercenary Guild to witness the darkness of noble families.
A massive underground facility.
Children chained by the neck and imprisoned.
Some trained as porters, some as sex slaves, and some as assassins—a horrific child abuse facility.
It was a terrible sight she never wanted to recall.

"W-wait. So Oknodie's skill in cooperation... is because she experienced such things?"
"Cooperation?"
"During Professor Weird's lecture today..."

Dorothy recounted what happened during the lecture.
The crazy professor's excessive note-taking and Oknodie skillfully taking notes of only the most valuable parts.
How she appeared to be staring blankly at the blackboard as if such trials were nothing new to her, but was actually lost in thought.

"Let's be good to Oknodie."
"Yes. We should."

Though Dorothy and Hestia had little previous interaction, they bonded over their shared belief that Oknodie was pitiful.
While this improvement in Hestia's social relationships wasn't exactly what Oknodie had planned, as long as the destination is reached, the path taken doesn't matter.
At least Hestia's chances of suddenly snapping and becoming a mass murderer had diminished.


* * *


Meanwhile, while Dorothy was busy selling note copies to other students for points.
Oknodie arrived at the Knight Faculty's outdoor classroom to attend the fourth period Wednesday lecture, "Mastery of Ranged Weapons."

"My forearms are still sore."
"I pulled the bow too much."
"I should have chosen spear throwing instead."
"Isn't a pistol the best? You just pull the trigger and that's it."
"So you're going to take the class with that crazy redhead?"
"Ah, that's a bit..."
"I'm scared I might get shot standing next to him."

The lower-class students, who had fewer required credits than the upper-class students, generally looked happier.
Just having a bit more breathing room between being tormented by professors with all sorts of personality issues was enough to maintain good complexion and mental health.

"Young pillars of the continent! Today's lecture will also divide you into groups according to your main weapon for training."

The reactions of the archery group students upon seeing Oknodie were divided into exactly three categories.

"Hello, Oknodie!"
"Are you using a longbow again today?"
"Amazing how you can draw it with those thin arms."

Group A students who honestly admired her strength.

"Oh, you startled me."
"Right. Oknodie was here."
"Aren't you scared you might get hit by something?"

Group B students who feared her gradually spreading notoriety.

"...She's just a child after all. Not someone to be so wary of, especially in this Mastery of Ranged Weapons lecture."
"Skola is right. We have nothing to fear."
"No matter how good Oknodie is, she can't beat Skola in archery!"

Descendant of the divine archer.
One of the most famous supporting characters among Imperial students for archery, and the "ADC" position commonly found in the Academy.
Skola and his group of followers formed the third group.

"Those guys, aren't they being disrespectful?"
"How dare they speak so challengingly to Oknodie."
"Oknodie, say something back to them."

And the person in question had no issue with it.

"Huh? It doesn't really matter, does it? Archery isn't exactly my specialty. I'm not that amazing at shooting. Hehe."
"No, anyone could see you were no joke!"
"That's right! The ability to draw a bow that even adults struggle with and hit targets accurately at just 11 years old isn't something just anyone can do!"

For some reason, the Group A students started getting angry on her behalf.

'This person was born to shoot a bow. How could anyone beat that?'

In any game, ranged specialists handle ranged weapons at an almost perfect level.
There are rare unpopular mine characters who have embarrassingly poor accuracy despite being in ranged positions, but at least the Skola I know isn't that type of character.

"Now, I'll explain the skills we'll be mastering today."

Until the instructor began his explanation, I was lost in my own thoughts.

'Well, it's only the second lecture, so all this fuss won't amount to much.'

If the first day was about hitting stationary targets, the second lecture would be about hitting moving targets.
While I'm not lacking confidence in my skills, it's not a lecture where I'd show off extraordinary talent.
I neither have the confidence to beat Skola, who's considered a tier-1 ADC in both rapid-fire and long-distance competitions, nor do I have any reason to compete with him.

"Due to the archery group's above-average skill level, we'll be advancing to the curriculum originally planned for the fourth lecture."
"What??"
"Don't worry, it's nothing major. Just a light obstacle course where you compete to hit as many targets as possible while moving—mobile shooting."
"Wow. We'll get to see whether Oknodie or Skola sets the better record."
"The score is worth 10% of the grade, but you can make that up with extra effort later, so just think of it as casually shooting arrows and having fun."

But now they're springing a surprise test worth 10% of our grade by advancing the lecture schedule.
The students who had been excitedly watching Skola and me realized that they too would be affected and turned pale.

"Why, why are you doing this to us!"
"We're still beginners!"

The instructor scratched his head while looking at me.

"Professor Evening Shooter said that the archery group could advance much faster in the curriculum since they're skilled, even with a child present."
"That's wrong! Look at Oknodie's arms. Can she even properly draw a bow with those delicate arms?"
"Look at her height. She'll trip trying to jump over obstacles and end up crying!"
"Don't you feel sorry for Oknodie?"
"......"

The ones using me as an excuse right now are the same ones who were praising me and encouraging a showdown just moments ago.

"I'm fine!"

Feeling somewhat annoyed, I raised my hand confidently to make an impression on the instructor.
