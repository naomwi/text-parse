# As the Game Begins

As the game began, balls rained down from the sky at random positions.

Hestia braced herself firmly on the ground, ready to catch the balls and determined not to be pushed back by the third-year seniors' interference. But she soon realized that no one was trying to stop her.

...Why?

Did she look so easy to beat that they didn't even feel the need to block her?

Was it because she was just a muscle woman who hadn't even learned mana cultivation techniques?

'That hurts my pride!'

Just wait and see.
I'll definitely catch that ball.

Hestia looked up at the ball falling toward her head and put all her strength into her legs.
But despite her firm resolve, she couldn't jump.
Not because the seniors' swamp magic had trapped her feet.
But because an even more absurd scene was unfolding before her.

Float, float
Whoosh, whoosh

Seniors were rising into the air one after another.
Some rode on broomsticks, others on magic carpets, and some even performed the feat of standing on sword sheaths.

"How is this fair!"
"Flying is cheating, seniors!"

The senior students responded to the first-years' chirping complaints with amused faces.

"What's wrong with flying?"
"Too cowardly to try?"
"There's no rule against flying, is there?"

True enough. The rules only specified that stepping on the line would result in disqualification, not flying in the air.

"But we can't fly!"
"Well, that's your problem."

A third-year senior openly mocked the speechless first-years.

"If you're upset, you should have learned flying magic. Did someone threaten you with a knife not to?"
"Did someone threaten you not to study ahead?"
"Let's just threaten them now. We'll kill them if they don't learn flying magic!"

The seniors were openly taunting the first-years through message magic from high above, where the dodgeballs could barely reach.
Demoralized first-years voluntarily stepped on the line to be disqualified.
Hestia too lowered her hammer weakly and stepped on the line.

* * *

Even the stubborn first-years who held out were soon forced to drop out.
The third-years attacked one-sidedly from the air while the first-years fell one after another.

"Stone Shower."
"Come on, kids. Try to dodge! One out of a hundred is a real dodgeball!"
"Hey junior. Sign this contract to join the 'Blood-Sucking Club' and donate 200ml of blood weekly, and we'll let you lose painlessly. Hurry and sign the membership form!"
"This junior looks pale and delicate—perfect for our 'Thousand Physical Sensations Club' to perform body modifications on. Join our club! If not... I'll break your arm!"
"Surrender! Surrenderrr!!"
"Sur... mmph!?"
"Cover his mouth!"
"Don't let him surrender."
"Block the supervisor's and student council's view. While we buy time with illusion magic, quickly get his fingerprint!"

The students who managed to get disqualified were the lucky ones.
At least they didn't suffer the terrible fate of being forcibly recruited into the third-years' clubs!

"Oh? This junior is wearing medium armor."
"Can't see any skin at all."
"This one should join our Thousand Physical Sensations Club too."

Mob, known as the Black Knight among first-years and usually proud of it, trembled in fear before the terrifying seniors' attention.

"S-seniors. I appreciate the offer, but I prefer individual training."
"What a good attitude!"
"Thank you... May I ask if it would be impolite to inquire whether I might be permitted to step on the line and surrender without appearing disrespectful?"
"You may go."
"Th-thank you..."

Mob's face fell.

"But could you please let go of my hand?"
"If you can break free from our restraint with your strength, I'll acknowledge that your individual training wasn't in vain!"

Mob felt wronged.
Why did he have to take a test of breaking free from the restraint of seniors standing on flying dumbbells in the middle of a dodgeball match?
And if he failed, he would be helplessly dragged away to join their club.

"Rainbow Total Attack~~!"

WHACK!
What saved Mob from his predicament was a familiar voice.

"Oknodie!? Wh-wh-what are you doing! Attacking a third-year!"

The third-year whose head had been bent 90 degrees to the side cracked his neck back into place and grinned.

"Ah, this one's got some real power."
"If we recruit this one to the Thousand Physical Sensations Club too, we'll definitely win the lower-grade championship."
"Then the student council will increase our club's support points."
"With those points, we can request newer equipment with more delicious weight than our current facilities."
"Points."
"Weight!"

The seniors who had been holding Mob released him and began flying toward Oknodie.

"Oknodie! Surrender quickly!"
"Geez. Where did you sell your common sense? I bought you time to surrender so you wouldn't have to receive weird training from those strange people. You're the one who needs to surrender, Mob!"
"Are you sure you'll be okay...?"
"Surrender quickly!"
"I'm sorry, Oknodie. Next time, I won't be a burden and will definitely help you...!"

The Rainbow Ball with its terrifying gimmick of infinite acceleration repetition could be stopped with minimal damage if the first hit was blocked.
The third-year senior from the Thousand Physical Sensations Club blocked the Rainbow Ball in one strike by channeling mana into his developed neck muscles at the moment of impact to enhance their performance.
This was strength on a completely different level from the "Dead Cat," one of the Four Heavenly Kings of the second year who had surrendered after losing his life several times!

"I surrender!"

Mob, who had finally made it outside the court, worriedly watched Oknodie who was now facing those terrifying seniors, wondering if she could safely surrender.
Despite the approaching seniors, Oknodie wasn't running away, seemingly planning to help other students as she had helped him.
Jaku tapped Mob's worried shoulder.

"Who's worrying about whom? Get a grip, idiot."
"The seniors look so strong."
"Well, that's true... But they must be like the Four Heavenly Kings of the third year to withstand Oknodie's attack. How could an ordinary third-year possibly take Oknodie's attack?"

To Jaku, a member of the Foundation, Oknodie was the top scholarship student at the pinnacle of all scholarship students.
She was an unprecedented figure in the Wiheomhae Foundation who openly revealed her identity.
She wouldn't reveal herself without absolute confidence in her abilities, and the Foundation wouldn't make her status as the "Top Scholarship Student" public without trusting her!

"You know nothing, junior."
"...Are you perhaps a fourth-year?"
"I'm a second-year. Relax."

Jaku, startled by the voice from a nearby spectator seat, genuinely sighed in relief.

"Barkssar, head of the Thousand Physical Sensations Club. That inhuman muscle monster is indeed from the advanced class of the third year, but he's far from being comparable to the Four Heavenly Kings of the third year."
"Is that really true!?"
"Yes. By the third year, there's no one in the advanced class who can't withstand an attack from that monster rookie, the Dark Princess Oknodie."
"Who are you, senior, to know such valuable information?"
"Bigstone. Member of the Alchemy/Chemistry Club. When you do club activities, you can't help but learn about the third-years. What kind of monster you need to become within a year to make it to third year."

While first year was simply about struggling to keep up with the eccentric lectures and curriculum, from second year onwards, students begin to feel the crisis of advancing to third year.

"You're very kind, senior."
"Kind? Hah! Kind people don't last in second year. You have to be mean or cunning. I'm telling you this because I hope talented juniors will get discouraged and drop out soon."
"...I withdraw my comment about kindness. You truly are cunning."
"Thanks for the compliment~"
"But second-years should know something too."
"Hm?"
"How dangerous the top scholarship student of the Wiheomhae Foundation really is."

Oknodie caught Barkssar's wrist as he flew toward her, spun him around twice, and threw him at the approaching Thousand Physical Sensations Club members.
Bigstone was just as shocked as Jaku had been.

"That's a monster wearing a child's disguise."

After rescuing Mob and defeating the brutish Thousand Physical Sensations Club seniors, things calmed down a bit.
The bizarre sight of muscular seniors flying around on flying dumbbells had been so shocking that the first-years who had been watching cautiously surrendered in droves.

"To think that Barkssar, the club president with superhuman strength who even receives personal guidance from Professor Platon, would be spun around like a windmill!"
"I can't believe it. This can't be real!"
"What were our three years for! Argh!"

It seems the third-year seniors' confidence was also shaken, but that lucky punch was a one-time thing.
Senior Barkssar wasn't underestimating her because she was a first-year, but rather holding back for fear of accidentally breaking a first-year.
Oknodie exploited that hesitation to achieve a swift victory using the principle of redirecting force.
The seniors who fell from their flying dumbbells all landed gracefully on the ground with proper falling techniques, but they were judged to have crossed the line and were all ruled out of bounds!
Now she just needed to make the remaining six students surrender or get eliminated to complete the event.
The six students were split into groups of five and one.
But... either group was quite remarkable.

"Imperial Second Princess Mesugakki. I'm impressed by your dark mana. Would you like to join the Dark Cult Club and enjoy the secrets of the dark society?"
"Ahhh~~!? A club that handles dark mana~~? Amazing♡ I really want to join♡"

Princess Mesugakki, who hadn't used any strength and was still full of energy after being eliminated.

"I've heard there are seniors who are the foundation of our Western Nobility Union. Even if we're going to leave, shouldn't we at least pay our respects?"
"The Young Duke is right."
"We should at least make an impression on seniors who have made it to third year."
"I wonder if they'll look favorably on us for making it this far?"
"It would be nice if they shared exam materials for the final exams, right?"

Young Duke Andersen and his noble entourage remained boldly in one corner of the court without any fear—six suicidal hopefuls who had lost all sense of danger.
