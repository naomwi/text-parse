<65 - This Is Not a Joke>

Professor Leibe, an imperial magic instructor.
He despised Headmaster Dragon's commoner-friendly academy management.
Despite the Empire paying enormous subsidies, the benefits were going not just to imperial nobles but also to frontier nobles and even to uncivilized commoners.

"This is unacceptable. I cannot allow wealth redistribution or advancement to higher classes. Especially not in Professor Leibe's magic theory lectures."

That's why he administered the test.
A pop quiz worth an enormous number of points.
A test deliberately biased in favor of imperial nobles.

"But what is this result?!"

Isabel - 19/20
Yupi - 18/20
Mesugakki - 17/20

Mesugakki, the Empire's second princess, raised with the Empire's strict education.
The saintess, chosen by the One God Sophemia and educated by the Papal Office.
These imperial prides were outranked by the name of an uncivilized commoner who was neither an imperial citizen nor of noble blood.

"I can understand the princess and saintess having heavy responsibilities, but how do you explain this result? Am I supposed to accept this?!"

Group A Average - 14.2
Group B Average - 13.7

Even in average scores, the Empire's pillars were outperformed by those worthless frontier students.
It wasn't that the 981st class of imperial students lacked ability. Compared to previous years, their average score was actually much higher.
In other years, imperial students scoring above 10 points would have been considered excellent.
For frontier students, scoring above 5 points would have been considered remarkable.
That was the problem.
An average score increase of more than double compared to other years.
This abnormal change made Leibe realize:

"The test was leaked."

There were no signs of intrusion.
Both the question and answer sheets still had their protective magic formulas intact.
Nevertheless, there was one irritating presence in this academy that made it impossible to guarantee security.

"Bronze de Estrada. That arrogant criminal playing vigilante. This was your doing!"

To humiliate an imperial noble professor to this extent would be a capital offense in the Empire.

"I will never forget today's disgrace."


* *


"Hey, you went through all that trouble to get the test paper, but then shared it with all Group A students. Isn't that a waste?"

Titosoga complained with a face that showed he was dying of regret.
Isabel quickly grabbed the test paper and hid it behind her back.

"You're right. It's too valuable to show you."
"Ah! Show me more! Quickly! I haven't memorized all the questions and answers yet!"
"Then just look quietly without complaining."

Isabel shared both the quiz questions and answers with all students taking the magic theory lecture.
Of course, she had Oknodie to thank for obtaining them, as she herself had received the papers from Oknodie.

"Are you sure? Giving me something this valuable. If you showed it to the imperial students, you could get tons of points."
"Among the people taking magic theory lectures, Isabel is my closest friend!"

Valuing friendship over profit.
While this was common sense in adventure parties, massive interests could break down such common sense.
Knowing that not all adventure parties in the world consisted of good people like the Esonia Adventure Party, Isabel found Oknodie's goodwill even more dazzling.
In a world full of trash who would sell out and betray their comrades for a few coins.
It was a miracle that Oknodie, raised as an assassin for a noble family, could maintain such a kind heart.

"...Thank you."
"Heehee. Don't mention it."


* *


Professor Bronze felt disappointed that Oknodie had obtained valuable test and answer sheets but gave them away to her friend for free.
The advantage of being a vigilante was that it could be very profitable.
Wanting her to experience this benefit, she brought valuable items for a practical exercise in distinguishing between genuine and fake goods.

"Try to guess which of these three papers is the real test. I'll give the test and answer sheets as a prize to the student who gets the most questions right."
"However, this test isn't just about finding the real one, but also explaining why the fakes are fake."

The practical test started with crude question sheets made of nonsensical letters, then progressed to sheets with different handwriting, different paper quality, and so on, with each subsequent sheet being closer to the original.
The students naturally learned the elements needed for "developing an eye" as they realized how many factors needed to be considered when determining the authenticity of an item.
However, there was one student who already showed talent close to a complete form.
Of course, that student was Oknodie.

"No way. We did a similar exercise in second year, and I can't believe I lost to a first-year student."
"Even matching a current adventurer's level isn't a bad result for a senior. You just had tough competition."

Isabel consoled Bigstone.
Unlike the first stage of finding the real item, which everyone passed, the difficulty increased steeply from the second stage.
By the fourth stage, the level had risen so high that even Isabel and Bigstone couldn't avoid elimination.

"Are you really a first-year? You're too good at this."
"I'm actually surprised by your skills, senior. I can't believe you're a student who failed last year—you have such a good eye."
"Of course. As the heir to the Rizna Trading Company, I have confidence in my discernment."
"That's amazing. Do you have any good tips?"
"When you have the embarrassing experience of becoming the heir to a trading company named after you, you'll study hard just to avoid the shame. Why don't you try setting up something like 'Gisele Trading Company'?"
"Haha. Your parents must really love you. But I'll pass on the embarrassment."

Bigstone's girlfriend Rizna was eliminated in the fifth stage along with Gisele.

"...Tsk. I thought I had confidence in my eyes."
"...I agree."

The taciturn swordsman Xing and the quiet second-year senior.
The two quiet men were eliminated in the sixth stage.
As a result, the question and answer sheets became Oknodie's, who passed the sixth stage.
No matter how skilled and talented the students were, it was practically impossible to find the original when magical forgeries had anti-magic spells on them.

"Then we can just cast a new spell! The forgeries already have so many spells on them that they won't have capacity for more, so the paper that doesn't accept magic must be fake!"
"That's correct. But Oknodie didn't cast any magic, so how did you find the real one?"
"Um... by instinct?"

Oknodie, who knew that the paper on the right was always the correct answer, gave a vague response, but Professor Bronze, who was already determined to make Oknodie her protégé, nodded in satisfaction, saying that good instincts were also a good quality for a vigilante.
However, the professor felt that Oknodie's attitude of not taking advantage of benefits and easily giving them away to those around her might represent the righteous aspect of a vigilante, but wouldn't satisfy the wealth aspect.

"Next time, I should investigate student relationships and bring items related to professors who have no personal connections with any students."

Unless he wanted to be known as an imperial noble who couldn't even keep his own test and answer sheets safe, he wouldn't spread rumors.
The next theft shouldn't be too difficult either.

"It might be good to get something that helps with growth as well."

Should I target the Chinese medicine professor's breast enhancement pills?
Or perhaps the alchemy professor's growth elixir that increases height?
Professor Bronze, who had the same playfulness as Oknodie but with the adult height and chest that Oknodie lacked, smiled happily as she thought about preparing for the next lecture.

After the second period "Developing an Eye" lecture, at lunchtime.
The dropout group that had formed during the Headmaster's Teaching lecture gathered in the cafeteria as promised.
Oknodie was shocked to see everyone had brought so many flags.

"Wow. How did you all manage to bring ten flags each?"
"Beat them up."

Oknodie blinked at Hestia's short and blunt answer, something that rarely fazed her.

"What?"
"We beat them up together."

Lotto also answered with a face that somehow looked relieved.

"You're joking, right?"
"I didn't beat anyone up."

Jigoku was proud.

"Instead, I shot them with my gun."

Though afraid to hear the answer, she couldn't help but ask.

"Shot what? Training dummies?"
"What else? Classmates who had flags."
"Whaaaat?!"
"Are you stupid? Why would I beat up and shoot training dummies in the first place? We were busy collecting flags."
"The beating and shooting is the weird part!"

Looking around the cafeteria, she noticed students with faces that were bruised and bluish as if they'd been hit by something.
As soon as they made eye contact, they made sounds like "Eek!" or "Huh!" while turning their heads away and trembling, making it obvious who had beaten them.

"Follow me right now."
"What for? We already confirmed you don't have any hidden flags."
"They all handed over their flags when I told them they'd get one bullet hole per flag if they didn't."

These were the violent answers from Hestia and Jigoku, who ranked among the top fighters of the 981st class first-years.

"We need to apologize!"
"To those weaklings?"
"Shouldn't they be apologizing to us? For being so weak yet being our classmates?"
"Are you only planning to stay at the academy for today? This time it was a combat-related lecture so you two had the advantage, but next time there might be a lecture requiring brains, and you might get destroyed."
"By those weaklings? Me?"

Jigoku let his imagination run wild.

"Are they going to hit me with an encyclopedia or something?"
"...There are also tests where you compete in knowledge against a designated opponent, and the winner gains points while the loser loses points."

In the end, the three of them quietly followed Oknodie to apologize to the students with bruises on their faces.

"I heard there was an incident with my team members last night. I'm sorry."
"Is that what you say after beating someone to this state...? Thanks to you, I can't even eat the karaage on today's lunch menu and have to eat porridge."
"Wow, that's really bad."

It's too much to beat someone so badly they can't even eat.
When she glared at her team members with fire in her eyes, Hestia bowed her head awkwardly.

"I'm really sorry."
"Then give back the flags."
"What? Why should I give back the flags?"
"You said you came to apologize?"
"I did apologize."

Who said anything about returning flags?
I only said I'd apologize.

"I'm just kidding. I'll give them back."

"For 100 points each."

This is not a joke.
