# 95 - King Crab Apocalypse

Homeroom time.
In the game, Professor Mahabharata was just a weekly event notification bot who announced weekly events and disappeared, but now that the game had become reality, he continued with his announcements.

"During the heavy rain week, some facilities may be closed due to flooding concerns. Additionally, access to the first floor may become impossible, so additional entrances by floor will be opened or closed according to water levels."
"I have a question... Which facilities will be closed?"

The cat-person Jena, with her cute nya-nya speech pattern, asked with drooping ears and a gloomy face.
Just as cats dislike water, cat-people also have water as their weakness.

"Consider the training building basically unusable. Not that you'd have time for training anyway, with all the assignments you have."
"Nyaaa... I miss the meadow field..."

Poor sad creature on a rainy day.
But it's okay.
My eyes are happy watching this!

"I hate this Academy... No pigeons to hunt, water everywhere, giant king crabs washing up from the shore... it's the worst..."
"Jena. Don't be so depressed. According to an old saying passed down in the Lightning Thunder God Church, lightning can strike even from a clear sky, so life is naturally unlucky."
"...How am I supposed to find encouragement in that...?"
"Worldly life is just a pile of ash with one handful of divine lightning. If you think of life as already ruined, you can accept and endure any hardship that comes your way."
"Hyaaak! Your sect's philosophy is scary!"

Laughter erupted around them at the nun Nise's comforting words that weren't really comforting.

"Are all religious people like that?"
"She must be joking to cheer us up."
"No. She was definitely serious."

One male student said with a straight face.
The surrounding students gave him reproachful looks.

"Yelchin Brownie. Read the room. Everyone's trying to lighten the mood, and you're ruining it."
"Still, I know what I know. Andersen Pretzel and I are certain, if no one else is."
"What would you two know?"
"We're students taking the 'How to Use Divine Arts Without Faith' lecture."
"Ah... that infamous..."

Faith-powered comfortable learning vs. Faithless extreme experience.
The notoriety of this landmine lecture had spread widely even among the upper classes, so the students expressed sympathy for the two.
Isabel, who had been eavesdropping on the conversation, suddenly became curious and spoke up.

"What does Oknodie think about churches?"
"Places to stack prayer skill points?"
"Pfft. What's that? It's not wrong, but you've learned it strangely."
"Oh right. Isabel, if you ever need to go to a church, try going at night."
"Try what?"
"If you break into the church's food storage, you can find tons of food blessed with divine power and disaster relief supplies, which will fill your food catalog tremendously!"

How about that? Amazing, right?
This is how veteran player tips can be helpful!

"...Oknodie. I'll make you a meat dish for dinner tonight."
"Really?"

Isabel patted my shoulder with sad eyes for some reason I couldn't understand.
When Isabel gets sad, she makes meat dishes for dinner.
I mentally recorded this new strategy information.
I should borrow a collection of tear-jerking stories from the school store later.

"Wait a minute. Didn't someone just say king crabs are coming from the shore?"
"It's true. Look out the window."
"King crabs really are swarming in!"
"This is insane. They look so delicious."
"I wonder if we can eat them?"

Slurp. A female student who was drooling wiped her mouth with her sleeve, blushing.
When you're hungry and living a poor Academy life, land-walking king crabs might as well be free meals walking right up to you.
But the student who bravely opened the window to approach the king crabs couldn't take his foot off the windowsill.

"Huh? Why do they keep getting bigger?"

The students, who had misjudged the size of the creatures by ignoring perspective, began to panic.
And for good reason.
Those were "Giant King Crabs."
They were already much larger than ordinary crabs as "King Crabs," but with the "Giant" prefix, they were even more enormous.
How could creatures that crack open the shells of all sorts of sea monsters and devour their flesh in the overflowing seawater be of ordinary size?

"Waaaaaah!! Close the window!! Look at the size of those pincers. We'll die if they catch us!!"
"They're so gross!!!"
"How can crabs be bigger than large dogs?!"
"They're big enough to feed a family of four commoners for a month if frozen!!"

Amid the nobles' shock, one commoner muttered timidly.

"Commoners don't even have freezers at home..."

Isabel cautioned Oknodie.

"Only middle to upper-class households have freezers with freezing magic. Oknodie should remember these social facts."
"Why?"
"Because saying things like that in front of commoners makes you look really obnoxious."
"I see."

Isabel and the other commoners seemed very angry, their eyes narrowing into triangles as they looked at the nobles.
Even angrier than Isabel were the giant king crabs, which charged at the building and struck the windows with their pincers with all their might.

Thud. Thud. Thud.

"King Crab Apocalypse!!!"
"We're all going to die!!"
"Kyaaaaaaah!!"
"Don't just scream! Stack desks in front of the windows!!"
"Waaaaaah!! Mommy!!"

Screams, shrieks, and crying from lower-class students in distant classrooms could be heard.
In the increasingly serious atmosphere of the classroom, Professor Mahabharata smiled brightly and said:

"No need to worry. These are protective windows. They have protective magic cast on them, so they absolutely won't break."

Rattle rattle rattle
Crash!!

No sooner had the professor finished speaking than something fell off, and the sound of giant king crabs' legs scuttling across the ceiling could be heard.

"Oh, the ventilation ducts."

So they only put protective magic on the windows?
The professor laughed at the pale-faced students.

"No need to be so serious. If we leave them alone, the seniors will take care of them. Many seniors hunt when there's prey around to save points."

The homeroom lecture ended in a chaotic atmosphere, with the students' main concern naturally being the giant king crabs that had infiltrated the school through the ventilation ducts.


* *

Dragon Principal looked down at the professors who had barged into his office.
The professors whose lectures had been interrupted were furious, raging without restraint.

"Principal! What kind of outrage is this? Today was supposed to be the third-year magic broom flying practice, but you've made the weather conditions this terrible!"
"My 'Learning About Fire Elementals' lecture is also in trouble. The fire beast I caught from the elemental realm yesterday is whimpering and peeing itself inside."
"Our 'Learning Horticulture' elective... Wait. When a fire hound pees, does it come out as water or fire?"
"It comes out as liquid flame."
"Oh, that's quite interesting. Anyway, our 'Learning Horticulture' lecture can't proceed as planned because of the king crabs invading the garden."

-So what do you want me to do about it?

"Take responsibility and resolve this situation!"
"Make the rain stop quickly."
"And send all the king crabs back to the sea."

The principal's dragon face twisted with spite like a mean seven-year-old child.

-I understand your grievances.
-Then let's do this.

The principal offered a solution.

-Cancel your classes.

"But then we'll lose our weekends."

-If you aspire to be called the world's best in your field, shouldn't you either overcome adversity and continue teaching, or cancel class and schedule a makeup session? Shouldn't you handle this much on your own?

The professors left the principal's office, grumbling at the principal who unfairly spoke only righteous words.
Professor Mahabharata, who had returned after finishing his homeroom lecture, saw the professors leaving the principal's office and smirked.

"The principal is being too harsh."

-I hear that every week. No matter what I do, two or three of them will always make a fuss.

Then why not just eliminate the special weeks?
Mahabharata kept this thought to himself.
Given the principal's petty personality, nothing good would come from saying it out loud.

-Think about it. How pitiful are the freshmen who grow up starving without points!
-It just so happens that giant king crabs are coming ashore for their spawning season, and they're absolutely delicious. I cracked open and ate a few yesterday, and they're real rice thieves.
-The kids should grow up eating such things.

"Ah. Such a compassionate intention."

It was the principal's own form of mercy—making it rain heavily so that students could easily catch king crabs that had come onto land as the shore was under a king crab advisory.

'I don't think the students will appreciate that.'

But just as professors have college students to boss around like slaves, dragons have guardians.
He was afraid that if he upset the principal, he might be assigned tasks like "Retrieve the pincer of a hyper giant king crab sleeping in an underwater pit" as teaching material.

"Indeed, Principal, you are truly a person of integrity! There isn't an academy principal anywhere in the world with such compassion, allowing students to hunt king crabs for every meal!"

Except for the fact that the giant king crabs were desperate to eat the students, and most students were weaker than the giant king crabs.
Well, from the principal's perspective, perhaps it could be said he was being generous... maybe.
