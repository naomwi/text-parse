# The Lonely Eastern Swordsman

The lonely Eastern swordsman, Xing.
Only silence and stillness linger in his path.

*Slash!*

When birds chirp, his sword flashes, and their lifeless bodies fall with severed beaks and wings.

*Buzz! Buzz!*

A cicada that woke too early due to unusual weather falls silent with a throwing knife embedded in its head.

*Thud, thud-thud*

A swordsman who creates only silence and stillness, leaving behind countless corpses of small animals and insects wherever he goes.
Classmates who witnessed his madness would close their mouths or turn away at the mere sight of Xing, fearing that eye contact or their chatter might disturb him.
After all, he had even killed another participant during the entrance exam—why would he spare a classmate!

'There he is.'

I set out on my own to find Xing, whom everyone fears.
Finding Xing isn't difficult.
Wherever students who normally love to chat are fleeing in terror or keeping their mouths shut, that's where Xing will be!

"Everyone run—! The third-year seniors' lab door is open!! If you don't hurry, you might be captured as test subjects!!!"
"Aaaah!! I don't want to be a guinea pig!"
"If you're caught, you'll become a clinical test subject! Everyone flee!!"

Not here.

"H-hey, first-year. You shouldn't go there right now."
"Why not?"
"During curse practice, a student got angry at a rude professor and cast a 'be kind' curse on him, and now the professor is showing his kindness by offering everyone jobs in his lab."

Not here either.

"Everyone run awayyy! A crazy swordsman is on a rampage!!"
"Be careful in the garden. They say a suspicious swordsman is cutting down anything that disturbs him, plants and animals alike!"
"We just came out for a picnic... and a beetle with severed wings fell into my lunchbox... waaah!"

There he is!

When I went to the garden where the disturbing rumors were spreading, I found the path covered with insects with severed wings.
A natural disaster in human form.

'No wonder he has no friends when he goes around doing that every day!'

While I felt sorry for him, I also understood. I knew Xing's character story.
Raising a character's affection level gives you opportunities to learn about their past.
Naturally, I already knew most of the main characters' affection stories.
Why Xing was possessed by a ghost, why he hated loud noises, why he chose solitude.

Empathy and understanding.
If I handle these two things well, perhaps peaceful communication with Xing would be possible.

"Hello, Xing!"
"You're noisy. Disappear, kid."
"......"

I already expected that he wouldn't return my greeting.
A character with good performance but major flaws.
Xing is the quintessential representative of this type of character.

[Miss Oknodie. Your friend still has some final self-restraint despite being possessed by a ghost. He didn't cut you down without question.]

Professor Destroyer's voice came through the short-range magic communication device in my ear.
But the professor is completely mistaken.

'Xing's habit of cutting things indiscriminately isn't really because he's possessed by a ghost!'

Xing was already prone to cutting things even before being possessed by a ghost.
But he has his reasons.
It's not that he's a pleasure killer who trembles with withdrawal symptoms if he doesn't kill something.

"Xing. You know what? I know who that person is!"
"!!"
"It's your younger sister, right?"
"You know about Lin?"
"She's someone precious to you, isn't she?"

Xing's younger sister, Lin.
A sister who was already deceased at the start of the game.
Lin was mute.
She was mistreated by her family for being mute.
And outside the family, there were constant dirty offers of arranged marriages, promising some benefits in exchange for giving away a daughter with a "defect."
Xing didn't want his sister to hear those filthy voices.
Still, they were family.
Still, they were members of the same clan.
As a genius swordsman, he devoted himself to the responsibilities, duties, and roles given to him by his clan.

Without realizing it was a scheme to separate him from his sister.
After enduring and enduring, the ending he reached was a bad one.

His sister died, and he was left alone.
The man who once wielded his sword to fulfill obligations forced upon him now wielded it to fulfill duties he had taken upon himself.
He cuts down anything that disturbs the silence and stillness.
He slices anything that interferes with contemplation and silence.
For his sister who couldn't even scream once.
To carve her pain into others.
The vengeful ghost who needed power for revenge came to the Western world's finest educational institution.

This was why Xing walked the path of a killer.

"Hey! Don't be so mean! I'm Papa's precious daughter too!"
"Must you experience pain before backing down?"
"Is it because of the pretty sister next to you?"

Whether she didn't hear his command to disappear,
Or had no intention of listening,
The ill-mannered little kid didn't listen.
This was the second time he had to deal with someone who wouldn't listen.

Silently.

His first troublemaker.
He felt the gaze looking up at him from beside him.
Someone who just stared at him with inscrutable eyes, just like before.
The owner of a gaze heavier than ten thousand words.
Someone he had once let go due to his own powerlessness.
A connection he thought he would never encounter again.
His half-sister, two years younger, born of a different mother.
The child who had died and was revived by the power of ghosts.
And now.
Dark Princess Oknodie, said to be the strongest first-year in Evil Force, had come looking for him.

'If it were any other student, I would have cut them down without hesitation.'

If she noticed something strange about his sister who followed him and reported it to a professor, this being who resembled his sister from his memories would be eliminated.
But what about Dark Princess Oknodie?
She might be different.
Didn't she herself have a terrible reputation that could classify her as an enemy of all?
Despite showing rough behavior due to a hint of anxiety, a vague expectation prevented him from swinging his sword.

"Xing. You know what? I know who that person is!"
"!!"
"It's your younger sister, right?"

The sword in his mind stood on edge.
Guardian Visitation Week.
No one could predict that someone younger than himself would visit.
This was genuine knowledge.
About Lin's identity.

"You know about Lin?"
"She's someone precious to you, isn't she?"
"..."

That was also true.
Xing cherished Lin.
Beyond the normal relationship of a brother caring for his sister.
They regarded each other as the only ones they could rely on in this world, becoming each other's reason to live.
There was a time when he cherished her more than anything else in the world.
Those were happy days.
Days when only he was happy.

He didn't hear the screams hidden in his sister's silence.
He was listening to too many unnecessary noises.
Empty fame.
Futile desires.
Responsibilities to bear.
Only after losing her did he realize.
What situation his sister Lin had been in.

"...How do you know that?"
"Ah, well... somehow?"
"Does this Foundation have ears in the Eastern Empire as well?"

His secret was exposed.
She had noticed his purpose.
Normally, he would have cut her down without hesitation.
After all, there was a clear possibility that she was an agent sent by his "enemy."
Cut before being cut.
Don't be swayed by useless thoughts.
That was the principle he had established for himself.
But this time, his hand wouldn't move.
This child was different.
He sensed it instinctively.
Someone belonging to a darkness even greater than his enemy lurking in the Eastern Empire couldn't possibly be a mere agent of the Empire's darkness.

"What do you want?"
"I'll help you! In return, please help me later!"
"How do you intend to help me, to help us?"

The tiny giant sneezed "achoo," wrinkled her nose, and answered.

"I'll make it easier to keep your sister!"
"...Keep?"
"Like confining her in a ring or something?"

...Confine Lin? In a ring?

"Oh, don't worry! We can also put a leash on her and chain her up so she can't come out on her own!"
"..."

For the first time, Xing doubted his instincts.
Whether it had been the right decision not to cut down this child.
Whether he should have followed the slight anxiety that a more powerful enemy might have appeared, rather than the vague expectation that she might help with his revenge.
