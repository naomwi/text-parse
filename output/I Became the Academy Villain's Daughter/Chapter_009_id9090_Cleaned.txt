# Enhanced Translation: Chapter 236 - A Mature Child

9 AM.
Normally, this would be the start of first period.
As I was heading to a place without students to begin another joyful blood-drawing session, someone called out to me.

"Hey, Little mouse!"
"Mr. Son?"
"Where are you sneaking off to now?"
"I just have somewhere to go for a bit!"
"You said the same thing yesterday and missed the desk-throwing event."
"Ah, I forgot about that!"
"Liar. There's no way someone like you, known as one of the academy's most sharp-minded students, would simply forget."

Son O-cheon had come looking for me with determination.
His face showed such resolve that it seemed he'd rather drink poison than let Oknodie slip away today.

"Are you feeling self-conscious? Because of those worthless people saying bad things about you?"
"What does it matter? I can miss a competition or two."
"The you I know would never do that."
"What do you really know about me, Mr. Son?"
"Lately, I've been thinking maybe I don't know much about you anymore. But I do know how much you love to play."

Son O-cheon's voice was filled with confidence.

"Stone collecting. Bug catching. Sandcastle building. Hide-and-seek."
"!"
"Despite everyone else training like their lives depend on it, you're always playing around like this. Yet you're telling me you'd skip desk-throwing? That doesn't make any sense!"

Well, when he puts it that way, I have no comeback.
Though it wasn't really just playing around.
Take the sand digging, for example.
Hidden-trigger sandcastle building, where you find items hidden somewhere in the sand faster.
I worked hard building impressive castles because of the gimmick where buried treasure chests would rise up into the sandcastle on their own, but it was misunderstood as just playing around.

"Then what was it? If it wasn't playing, did it have some other training purpose?"
"Ah, anyway. It's none of your business, Mr. Son!"
"Isabel is worried about you."
"Oh."
"Did you know she's been working hard making lunch boxes for you? She's been experimenting with various recipes as the 'Dark Chef' or whatever, and I've been helping her collect all sorts of ingredients from the forest."
"If someone had told me at the dormitory, I would have gone to find her..."
"Are you saying this while sneaking out through the ventilation shaft?"

Tsk. For a monkey beastman, he's surprisingly eloquent. I can't refute a single thing.

"Well, I understand you must have your reasons. So tell me."
"...Do I have to?"
"If you don't want to, you can watch Isabel suffer until the end of the sports festival, thinking you no longer want to eat the food she makes, growing more depressed by the day."
Ugh... what an underhanded tactic.
Pretending to be pitiful was supposed to be my specialty!
But for Isabel, I have no choice.

"Fine... I'll tell you."

Thinking of Isabel's distress, I whispered to Son O-cheon what I'd been doing all this time.

"I've been collecting blood."
"Blood?"
"With these."

Invisibility cancel.
At my signal, a squadron of mosquitoes that had been flying around invisibly revealed themselves.

"Mosquitoes?"
"Yep."
"You're using...?"
"Yep."
"Mosquitoes?"

I demonstrated for Son O-cheon, who kept asking in disbelief.

"Mosquito, bark!"
"Whine!"
"Mosquito, sting!"

The mosquito squadron extended their sharp proboscises and repeatedly stabbed the rock I pointed at.
Son O-cheon's dumbfounded expression as he stared at the rock now covered in tiny holes was quite entertaining.
Since his reaction was good, should I show him one more trick?

"Mosquito, fire!"

The mosquito squadron activated Bloody Boost and fired compressed blood like machine gun bullets toward the rock, leaving red trajectories in the air.
Unable to withstand the destructive power, the rock crumbled to dust, leaving Son O-cheon stuttering before he asked:

"So you used these to bite people?"
"Yep!"
"How many died?"

He was dead serious.


* * *


When Son O-cheon was going on about mosquitoes, I thought he might have been bitten in his sleep and lost his mind, but better safe than sorry, so I followed him—and there really were mosquitoes.
Not just any mosquitoes, but blood-thief mosquitoes following Oknodie's orders, secretly approaching students to suck and steal their blood.

"Aren't those kids being treated too harshly?"

One aspiring knight student unconsciously detected the invisible mosquitoes and repeatedly dodged their stings.
This thoroughly angered the mosquitoes, who fired bloody missiles in rapid succession. Startled by the stinging sensation, the student tumbled to the ground.
As soon as his hand touched the ground, the mosquitoes quickly sucked blood from his wrist.
I was speechless at the sight of the mosquitoes turning red as they fed.
It was a cunning hunting method, like primitive humans hunting mammoths or elephants with spears.

"More than the hunting method, I'm concerned about the targeted students. Look carefully."

At Gisele's attentive observation, Isabel and Son O-cheon realized something.

"She's trying to protect other students from harm."
"That little mouse... she was protecting everyone from behind the scenes?"

The girl they thought had run away because she was afraid of hearing criticism was actually protecting everyone in secret.
Isabel was already in tears.
Seeing Gisele patting Isabel's shoulder to comfort her, even Son O-cheon felt a lump in his throat.

"Little mouse, that's enough now. The Dark Society members and I will help you from here, so leave the rest to us and go have some fun."
"Huh? No way. This is more efficient, I'm telling you!"
"Don't talk about efficiency. The idea that sacrificing yourself makes everyone else happy is nonsense. If people can't enjoy the sports festival without a child like you sacrificing herself, they should quit the academy."
"Fine... But please keep a close eye around Arcadia. There are a lot of them appearing in that area."

A second wave of emotion washed over them.
She was secretly protecting Arcadia, who was openly treated as a has-been!
Even Son O-cheon quietly turned his head and covered his eyes with his hand.

"?"

Isabel took the confused Oknodie's hand.

"Let's go, Oknodie. There's still the paper airplane throwing event that you like. Let's do it together."
"Do you want to?"
"Yes, I really want to."
"Hehe. If Isabel wants to, I guess I have no choice. I'll play with you today as a special favor."

As the two walked away, Gisele spoke quietly.

"Mr. Son O-cheon. Catching the students that the mosquitoes were monitoring will require somewhat violent means. We might face disciplinary action if the student council catches us. Can you still help?"
"Of course. Don't you see how pitiful that little mouse is? No matter how strong that small thing is, it breaks my heart to see her not knowing how to enjoy life. If I can help like this, I welcome it."

The two men made a promise.
They would somehow deal with the troublesome students that Oknodie had been monitoring.


* * *


At the paper airplane throwing competition venue.
Lower-class students who came hoping for an easy win were dumbfounded by the scene unfolding before them.

"Why are missiles coming out of paper airplanes?"
"Aaagh! My plane got shot down!"
"These jerks have no sportsmanship... *sob* Did they really need to engrave flame projectile formulas on their planes?"
"Yeah, just escape with a triple acceleration formula."
"Don't you know how to engrave defensive formulas, you noob?"

Students faced a miserable competition that felt more like a science lab project competing with products worth billions!
The spectacular aerial show by these skilled competitors became even more tense with Oknodie's arrival.

"If even non-senior class students are going wild like that, how good is Oknodie going to be?"
"Maybe it won't be so bad? Oknodie might just be here to have fun."
"Has anyone seen Oknodie participate in any other events during this sports festival?"
"I think this is the first time her face has appeared in any of the events listed in the information booklet, right?"

Individual events weren't massive competitions where hundreds of students lined up at once.
Instead, they competed in groups of five to ten people at a time!

"Sigh. You guys know to back off when the strong ones show up."
"That flame magician Rogini, I thought she'd burn her own plane first, but she burned everyone else's and survived to take first place..."
"When it rains, you should take shelter first."

Students were eyeing the situation carefully and stepping out of the participation line.
Isabel, who had entered the line holding Oknodie's hand, felt guilty.

"Sorry, Oknodie. We should have waited and observed a bit longer before entering."
"It's okay. The more time I have to think about what formulas to engrave, the better."

Isabel felt even more apologetic.
What a clever and kind-hearted child.
Even though no competitors were showing up and everyone's wary gazes might make her feel intimidated, instead of complaining, she comforted Isabel with mature words.
It was hard to tell who was the child and who was the adult.

"Hey, first-year over there. Looks like you're having trouble finding opponents. How about competing with us? We can have matches among ourselves if we mutually agree."

While they were busy preparing formulas, an unfamiliar voice called out to them.
Looking up from their paper airplanes, the two were greeted by second-year students with emblems on their collars.
