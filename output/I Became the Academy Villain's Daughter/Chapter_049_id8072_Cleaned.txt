# 49 - Always Remember to Turn Off Your Alarms

Bronze de Estrada, known as Professor Estrada in the game and "Toxic Bronze Professor" on player forums.
Even Oknodie knew from experience how her lectures were conducted.

'No matter what, she always included a practical test of discernment in every lecture.'

When you experience it firsthand, it's unbearably annoying, but watching others suffer through it is incredibly entertaining.
The first lecture is most likely the object hide-and-seek.
A mission where you must find alarm clocks disguised as ordinary classroom objects and disable them to escape the classroom.
Naturally, this game favors second-year students and veteran players with experience, while putting first-year students and newbie players at a disadvantage.

"A designer bag with the Emerald Company logo in the classroom seems suspicious. This must be an alarm clock disguised as a luxury bag."
"Stop! That's my book bag!"
"Such an expensive luxury item as a book bag?! T-then this flower petal light fixture must be a frivolous luxury item unrelated to academics. It's an alarm clock!"
"That's my light fixture! My sister said with this, I'd be popular at any academy I attend!"
"What? Your sister set you up for failure?!"

Gisele was stuck dealing with first-year provincial nobles' possessions that defied common sense!
Even as a player, I find it ridiculous.
Students carrying items that make you wonder why they'd bring such things, and they even spent precious points to get permission to possess them. It's heartbreaking.
Even with my abundant points, I find it wasteful. Newbie players seeing this would develop an instant hatred for nobles and start looking for pitchforks and guillotines.

'Tsk tsk. Poor kids.'

At first, their family members would provide support without knowing better, but they'll soon realize the gold-to-point conversion rate and cut funding while shuddering.
Nobles who become tight on points will regret spending enormous amounts on such trash, but points once spent never return.
Even without intervention, nobles who spent points on luxury item possession permits will be hitting the ground with regret.
Pitchforks and guillotines for inferior students who'll be naturally weeded out?
They're not corrupt officials or anything—just pitiful children.
We should save pitchforks and guillotines for the truly corrupt.

Gisele, who had been suffering through these bizarre item challenges, asked in a half-sobbing voice.

"You've researched the equipment of other classrooms in advance. Excellent preparation."

"I don't care either way, but would you please find the clocks quickly? The longer you procrastinate, the longer my head rings."

Then don't assign such tasks.

'I guess everyone who could find one has found one by now?'
