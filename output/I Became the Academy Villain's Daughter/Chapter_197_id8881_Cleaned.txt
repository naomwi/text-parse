# Chapter 197: The Triathlon Begins

The operation had failed.

"I have no excuse, Saintess."
"No, it was my mistake for not anticipating the Dark Guild would have such capabilities."
"I'm grateful for your generous forgiveness, Yui."

Yui understood.
Gisele.
For a commoner, his resourcefulness was extraordinary.
He was doing business in partnership with Princess Arcadia.
The number of followers who dreamed of such an opportunity and orbited around her exceeded three digits.
Among them, those worthy of even a proper conversation could be counted on one hand, and the actual elite guard who received Arcadia's call and assisted with her affairs numbered only in single digits.
Gisele was a man who couldn't be underestimated, having been chosen as an equal "partner" among those single-digit elite followers who formed her inner circle—something even they couldn't achieve.
But that shouldn't mean the hero's failure.

"But you're not giving up so easily, are you?"

When a predatory yellow light flickered in Yui's eyes, Skola flinched. Whether he was ashamed of being overwhelmed by the saintess or ashamed of his failure.
Biting his lip and steeling himself against the humiliation, Skola bowed his head in submission.

"If there's a chance for redemption, please grant it to me."
"The practice is over. Next is the midterm exam. Show me your specialty in the next advanced class test."
"...Saintess. As I mentioned before, I cannot directly target someone."
"I'm not asking you to attack Oknodie herself. There's something else, isn't there? A target you can shoot."

At Yui's clarification, Skola realized what target she meant.

"You mean to target Oknodie's golem?"
"There's no rule against attacking vehicles. This academy is quite lenient with regulations."
"The golem is a rock creature. And I'm an archer."

Skola hesitated.
Oknodie's extraordinary talents, the protective forces surrounding her, and her close relationships with powerful individuals.
Anyone targeting her would need to seriously reconsider whether they should continue pursuing her after considering these facts.
Yui understood Skola's psychology.
What a cowardly man.
But his skills were no lie.

"And you're the disciple of the Divine Archer. I've heard the Divine Archer's arrows can penetrate any metal in the world. Can't the disciple pierce even rock?"

Yui's provocation, aimed at his pride, hit its mark.
Skola's eyes changed.

"I'll show you. Just how far my arrows can penetrate."


* * *


Tuesday, 5:40 AM.
The advanced class students gathered at the foot of the mountain much earlier than usual.
This was because they needed to start the exam six hours earlier than regular lecture time to complete all three events of the triathlon.

"Oknodie is so lucky. Her mount is so obedient."
"You're one to talk, Dorothy. You're quite skilled too! And you chose a horse that everyone envies as your mount!"
"What about our Rudolf?"
"How is he? Rudolf's condition?"
"I don't know. He's been depressed, like he's sulking... I think the other horses stole his feed."
"...That's a serious problem!"
"What was that hesitation just now?"
"...I was thinking about how to comfort you!"

Oknodie was so brazenly lying.
Watching from behind, Isabel nudged Gisele in the ribs.

"Will she be okay? Oknodie."
"Though her excuse is clumsy, Dorothy won't notice. As we saw in the entrance exam, she has a naive personality that's vulnerable to deception."
"I'm not talking about that—I mean the exam. The stares from all around are no joke."

Besides Skola, who had reported Oknodie's feed theft to the professor at the mount storage facility the other day, gazes were pouring in from all directions.
Both Mesugakki's second princess and her faction, as well as the faction admiring the Ishtar hero.
Many students were eyeing Oknodie with burning determination to check her, as she was expected to dominate the competition from the outskirts.

'I'll grab even the hem of your skirt!'
'Oknodie. I won't let you ride that golem.'
'Let's see who's relatively incompetent in this opportunity♡'

Their intentions were plainly visible from their expressions alone.
Contrary to Isabel's concerns, Gisele was completely unconcerned.

"I understand your worry, but there's no need."
"It's obvious she'll be targeted by everyone."
"We're not in a relaxed enough situation to worry about others."
"Will we be targeted too?"
"We're not the only ones being targeted. Professor Platon has completely deceived the entire advanced class."

The ground trembled as sand particles and gravel bounced up.
Something in great numbers was approaching.
Thud. Thud.
The sound drew closer.
In the early dawn, insects stopped their cries and birds ceased their chirping.
Boom boom boom... THUD!
Eventually, even several trees collapsed.
When the procession that had been approaching from beyond the rocky area finally emerged from the forest, they could finally see what was causing the sudden commotion.

"Students?"
"Look at their collars. Those are second-year emblems."
"W-what! That impossible number!"

Even at a glance, there were over a thousand students.
And every one of them had eyes filled with fighting spirit.

"You lazy bums. Finally here."

As Professor Platon chided them, a knight in armor emerged from among the students with clanking sounds.

"This year will be different. I've trained them rigorously for this day."
"Hahaha! How pitiful, Andrew Conrad. This year's advanced class has skills that rank among the top in the history of advanced classes. You have no chance of winning."

The two professors engaged in psychological warfare.
Who was this unfamiliar professor, and why had the second-year students gathered at the first-year advanced class's exam site?
A vague sense of foreboding.
The cruel truth they all wanted to ignore.
When the instructors leading the second-year students approached and stood before the first-year advanced class, everyone was forced to face the reality they had been trying to avoid.

"The first-year advanced class midterm exam will be held simultaneously with the second-year lower class students."
"Whaaaat?!"
"The exam will be conducted over 12 hours from 6 AM to 6 PM, and scores will vary depending on the records and completion of three events: running, swimming, and riding."
"Wait a minute, we're first-years! How can you suddenly make us compete against second-years without prior notice!"
"Silence is golden, and here is gold. In the name of the golden god, I command: seal the mouth of the poor."

The instructor threw gold foil from his pocket into the air, and a golden X-mark appeared on the mouth of the first-year advanced class student who had protested.
As the student opened and closed his mouth in confusion, then grabbed his throat in distress, the instructor coldly said:

"Penalty for disrupting academic administration, 10 points deduction. Keep quiet for 5 minutes."

The students, more afraid of the point deduction than being unable to speak for 5 minutes, shut their mouths tightly.
Isabel glanced at Oknodie and noticed she had already covered Dorothy's mouth—knowing Dorothy's personality, she was exactly the type to speak up and receive penalties.
...As expected.
Just like a child who knows everything.
Perhaps the foundation had secretly informed her about this exam too.

"For your information, your opponents are second-year lower class students taking the advanced physical training course. They're pathetic fellows trying to compensate for their lack of skill with the power of faith."

Despite the blatant criticism, the second-year students lined up quietly, burning with hostility rather than loudly protesting to the instructor.
Their seriousness was on a completely different level compared to the first-year lower class students before the mana verification test.

"These pathetic ones have one special rule. If they acquire your first-year advanced class nameplates, they will automatically receive the highest grade for this exam."
"!!"
"Your grades won't decrease if your nameplate is taken. However, I promise you'll experience the disadvantage of wanting to deeply regret having stupidly lost your nameplate."

Competition within the advanced class.
Beyond that, the massive participation of second-year lower class students.

"Additionally, third-year students taking the 'Jeneger's Tactics' course will also participate in this exam. Not as running competitors, but as third-years who are permitted to indirectly intervene to predict and realize their predictions of grade rankings."

In the distance, senior students riding brooms in the sky waved their hands.

"I believe in you, my destined one! Hero Ishtar!"
"Gisele! I believe in your strategy. Wash away the disgrace of being a reverse bettor!"
"Show the power of the Imperial Three Great Merit Families!"

A midterm exam where first, second, and third-year students participated simultaneously—a large-scale event with grades at stake, reminiscent of a sports festival!

"When the whistle blows, the first-years will start first, and when the second whistle blows, the second-years will follow. First-years, you'd better run for your lives."

The instructor's mouth corners rose to such an extent that it seemed not just hideous but frightening.

"Because if they catch you, you'll be in for a rough time."

Gulp!
The first-years moved their Adam's apples in tension.
6:00 AM.
When the scheduled competition time arrived, the whistle blew.
The triathlon.
The exam, which was quite different from expectations and promised to be much more difficult, had begun.
