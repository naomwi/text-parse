# 402 - What Awaits Beyond Prejudice

The Jigoku Pirate Crew was originally a group of weaklings crawling at the very bottom among the 981st batch's lower class.

"Wow, how can you seriously have zero points? Do you guys even eat?"
"Well... we get free meals for doing dishwashing part-time in the kitchen..."
"I-I don't have enough time to study, so instead of dishwashing, I buy 10-day bundles of 1-point black bread from the store when they're 10% off..."

Even Salyak Pirate Jigoku thought it would be inhumane to prey on such pitiful fellows!

"You all, become pirates!"

Jigoku took in these ragtag misfits as crew members.
At first, they were completely useless.
Jigoku did everything alone while they barely managed to follow behind.
There was no reason for Jigoku, a member of the advanced class, to invest time and effort into nurturing such novices.
However, Jigoku's perspective was different from others.

'All Gift Academy entrants have overcome competition with talented individuals from around the world. Even if they lag in rankings, their potential isn't necessarily rock bottom.'

In other words, even the weakest rookies available at the Academy possessed far superior qualities compared to pirates one could find outside.
Moreover, qualities don't all bloom at the same rate under identical training and testing methods.
Those whose direction aligns with their training develop faster than anyone, but those whose talents point in a different direction from the educational approach require much longer trial and error.

-Little pirate. If you want to survive at sea, there's something more important than becoming strong. It's not giving up on survival.

Back when she was still a weak little pirate.
When she was weaker than the current Oknodie.
She started as a lowly laborer doing the dirty work for pirates, stealing knowledge with sidelong glances about everything a pirate needed to know.
If she had settled for that and given up on further growth, she would have spent her life as an insignificant pirate and died as a mere underling.
But she persisted tenaciously.
And finally, she encountered an opportunity to become a combatant.
When the gunner who handled the gun in her place fell to enemy fire.
The moment she grabbed the gun, aimed at the enemy, and hit them with a bullet, she realized.
This was her talent.
She had finally found the "right direction for growth."

"My knowledgeable minions. Today is not the day for your true value to bloom. But if it's just firing cannons, even your insufficient talents are more than enough!"

For pirates who could handle magic—which required much more precise aiming and hitting targets within tight timeframes than cannons—observing and targeting enemies was simple.

"Port side, fire three cannons at three-second intervals!"

Enduring the eardrum-splitting roar with mana cultivation techniques rather than earplugs, they began firing according to precise instructions.
The battery seized by the Jigoku Pirate Crew hit the enemy vessel with surprising speed, and continued hammering it into tatters.

"The gun barrels are overheating!"
"Then pour mana on them to extract the heat!"
"This is an operation to extract only the heat from the gun barrels without detonating the surrounding shells. Make a mistake, and everyone around the battery will be immediately disqualified!"

Despite being a sufficiently tense situation, the Jigoku Pirate Crew members weren't afraid.

"Ha, that's as easy as eating cold rice!"
"It's not like we're fighting those monstrous foundation executives from the cruise ship."
"Even if we mess up, we just get disqualified—that's sweet! A mere test where only our grades are at stake is nothing to us who've faced real life-or-death situations!"

Other students who had joined the weekend lecture with light hearts, hoping to earn points while avoiding academic competition, were completely overwhelmed by the Jigoku Pirate Crew's spirit.

"A-are they crazy?"
"If we fail the test, we can't earn any points!"
"Using heat magic to extract heat instead of cooling the gun barrels with ice magic—undertaking such a reckless act, are they trying to fail the test?!"

But nothing exploded.
When a student was on the verge of failing the operation, Jigoku placed a hand on their back.

"Don't chicken out. Think about the distance to the ammunition box. Even if heat leaks out from a failed heat manipulation, just blast it away before it reaches the shells!"

<Heat Manipulation>
<Fireball>

They channeled the uncontrollable energy into magic and forcibly discharged it into the sea.
The Jigoku Pirate Crew's cannons, with their reload speed dramatically improved, resumed firing much faster than the enemy fleet's batteries.

Boom! Boom!

"We did it."
"We blew up their battery."
"The points we earn before engaging in close combat will be our overwhelming victory."
"Yahoo!"
"Long live the Jigoku Pirate Crew!"
"Hurrah!"

These rookies with insufficient talent gained the peace of mind to endure until they could develop their abilities through experience.
It didn't matter if they fled when at a disadvantage or acted cowardly while seeking achievements.
Because on the path guided by Jigoku, there were definite rewards, results, and the belief that they wouldn't be abandoned.

"The captain is really amazing."
"I'm glad I joined the Jigoku Pirate Crew!"

The Jigoku Pirate Crew clearly had the upper hand.

"Huh? Why is the ship heading toward the enemy vessel?"
"Professor, please steer properly!"

Until an obvious anomaly occurred on board.
Jigoku immediately picked out two subordinates and stepped forward.

"Nancy. Brodo. You two follow me. Without securing the right firing angle, we won't have a chance to achieve merits no matter how excellent our gunnery skills are."

It was a sudden anomaly.
The professor's intention to create variables rather than allowing easy achievements was evident.
If they were so confident in their gunnery, the professor was likely giving students a chance to handle navigation as well.

Whoooosh...

Inside the bridge where the helmsman should be steering the ship, white smoke was continuously escaping.
Jigoku immediately took out a handkerchief and poured rum on it.

"An unknown gas has filled the interior. I hear groaning from inside, so it's not an instantly lethal toxic substance. But there's a risk of explosion, so cover your nose and mouth and enter without triggering any magical reactions."

Upon entering the cabin, they saw one instructor slumped against the wall and another rolling on the floor.

"Ughhhh..."
"Urrrgh..."

The crew members quickly examined and observed the physical condition of the two groaning instructors.

"Impact injury! The jaw is dislocated!"
"Stabbed once in the side with a dagger. There's an impression where the shelf was crumpled after a protective spell was cast when their posture collapsed, but even that was destroyed in one blow, and there's a mark where the palm struck above the heart."
"What size?"
"Quite small. Like a child's palm."
"A child...?"

A child on a ship like this?
Jigoku felt a chill.

"Use the fallen instructors as shields immediately!"

There was only one child who could do something like this that came to mind.
No sooner had Jigoku's warning dropped than the lid of a cabinet in the bridge flew off and struck one crew member.

"Eek!"

The crew member who fell back, unable to withstand the force carried by the lid despite using the instructor as a shield.
Another crew member quickly threw a pouch full of powder and burst it.

<Paralysis Powder>

Paralysis powder with different properties from the gas.
In an environment already filled with gas, the paralysis powder would spread even faster.
As the crew member briefly rejoiced, Jigoku ran toward them at full speed.

"Duck!"
"Huh?!"

A shadow that charged forward as if completely unaffected by the paralysis powder.
Unlike the crew member who froze in momentary intimidation by the enormous figure that easily exceeded 2.3 meters, Jigoku wasn't fooled by the illusion that cast a large shadow with the basic <Light> spell and confused directions with the <Mirror> spell.

Crash!

A powerful kick with force comparable to an exploding shell blasted through the bridge wall—which could withstand cannon fire—instantly expelling the smoke outside.
In the now-open interior, the identity of the surprise attacker was finally revealed clearly.

"Oknodie. Aren't you taking this prank too far?"
"Prank? I'm being serious too!"
"What do you gain from doing this?"
"The highlight of piracy is a mutiny on board! If I forcibly take over the executive positions from the instructors, I can get bonus challenge points!"
"Gyahaha! You really have the makings of a great pirate. But if you're going to interfere with our crew earning merits, you won't hold a grudge if we become enemies, right?"
"Hehe. But it's already too late."

Crack.
The crew members realized in horror what Oknodie had broken and shouted:

"The paddle wheel is broken!"
"Ugh, our direction is... toward the enemy ship!"

This ship would forcibly collide with the enemy vessel.

"What are you going to do with all those points you're so desperately collecting?"
"Actually, I don't really need points. I already have plenty!"
"Then why are you doing this?"
"Well, that's, um..."

Oknodie made an expression as if something was difficult to say, then glossed it over with a smile.

"For fun?"
"...!"

This child.
Was she saying that even in an exam where everyone's grades were at stake, she would send all the students' grades to hell just for her own amusement?
Faced with this unimaginably cruel answer, Jigoku saw the figure before her not as a child fitting her small stature, but as comparable to notorious pirates infamous for their cruelty.
