# Graduating from the Academy by Luck - Chapter 204: Living as an Instructor

It would take more time for Xing and Zuang to arrive.
But I didn't want to keep losing my ranking indefinitely.
Friendship and grades.
The way to catch both rabbits at once was simple.
Just prevent others from getting grades!

"Isn't that the finish line?"
"How vicious."
"She's not even human."
"How can a child who's not even a third-year show such character?"
"Haven't you heard? First-years are saying her class is Dark Princess."
"What kind of class is that?"
"Princess of the Demon King?"
"That's insane."
"Looking at how the magic beasts are rising, it seems accurate."

The first-year advanced class imperial students and second-year lower class seniors were gossiping about me, but I paid them no mind.
Rather, I was concerned that I might be conducting the exam too mercifully with too soft a heart.
Right?
I hadn't physically eliminated the imperial students.
I hadn't taken their nameplates.
I was even leaving the students on the other side alone to secure 100 people to cross the river.

'If I were a third-year, I would have knocked them all down first and thought later, since who knows what mischief they might cause with so many people gathered.'

Compared to that, how merciful is it to let them complain freely without hitting them!
Still, as the test drags on, I'm starting to get tired.
I wish they would come quickly.
As I leaned against a rock near the riverbank, feeling bored about just standing around, I heard thumping sounds approaching.
The ground was shaking and screams could be heard, which surely meant...

'Has Xing arrived?'

I jumped up and turned around to find the golem had arrived.
The golem showed an expression of "I found my master on my own, aren't I good?" as it approached.
It lowered its huge head for me to pat, but it was so large that it blocked my entire view.

"Oh my. You should be mindful of your size. Do you think you're one of those baby golems Hestia carries around?"
"Kigugung."

The sound of its dejection was so cute that I gave it one pat.

"If you're bored, just drink some water!"

The golem gulped down water to cool its core while I, its master, lay sprawled on the rock to pass time.
Hmm... I'm getting sleepy.
Maybe I should take a quick nap.
As my head bobbed up and down drowsily and I swallowed my saliva, I noticed the gazes of students across the river following my head movements.

"Daddy's not sleeping!"

When I shouted with my eyes wide open, the sighs from across the river grew louder.

"She's toying with us."
"Ah. She might be a little cute."
"Get a grip. Would you be happy if all your grades were taken away?"

As I continued tormenting numerous students by holding their grades hostage, I heard thumping sounds again.
It must be another golem.
Perhaps Hestia's golem searching for the finish line? Or a wild golem that hadn't been chosen by a master?

As I glanced around, I noticed students across the river panicking and rushing into the river.
It wasn't because people had collectively lost their minds.
Nor was it because a rare golden fish or jewel fish had appeared, which had an even lower appearance rate than the 0.1% rare fish.

"Aaaah! A crazy murderer has appeared!!"
"How much blood is he covered in!?"
"F-first, attack! Let's take him down with ranged magic!!"

Like gunshots spreading in a circular pattern, blue mana residue bloomed consecutively around the magic casters.
The smoke rising violently like a fireworks display and the roaring sounds of various magics cutting through the air.
Instead of the screams that should have been heard from the target, there were distinct sounds of something being cut and split.

Crushing, splitting, tearing, shattering.

As I realized what the intense display of effects meant, even a veteran like me was left with my mouth hanging open.

'Consecutive parrying? Magic destruction?'

Consecutive parrying, deflecting magic attacks in different directions like deflecting an enemy's attack with a sword.
And between those deflections, canceling the magic manifestation itself by cutting the axis of magical power that forms the spell for attacks that couldn't be deflected.
This is definitely not a skill a first-year should possess.
It's a technique taught with significant emphasis even in the Knight Department.
A few weeks at minimum.
A few months at most.
It's a skill used in actual combat only after considerable practice and more practice.
Parrying magical attacks versus physical attacks requires a high level of insight into the reduction of effective parrying range and the workings and understanding of magical forces.
It's a skill for talented prodigies or veterans because the training required for skill activation is ridiculously high.

'Magic destruction would still cause damage to the caster!'

Just as cutting a bullet doesn't make the projectile disappear, magic also has inertial energy.
Magic that has already been fully manifested and fired from a distance will still cause a certain amount of damage to the surrounding space due to its aftermath, even if the spell is canceled.
With that much accumulated cancellation damage, it would cause significant harm on its own.

Stiffness.
Dizziness.
Reduced vision.

Despite suffering from various side effects and gradually becoming less responsive, Xing didn't stop even in a situation where he should have collapsed after taking a magic carpet bombing.
He finally closed the gap and began slashing the second-years, sweeping away seniors from the front line.

"R-run away!!"
"This is beyond our level!"
"Deflecting magic with a sword, where did such a madman come from?!"
"Is he a third-year?! Are we falling victim to a third-year disguised as a first-year?!"
"You might be able to avoid magic beasts with luck. But that guy isn't someone you can deal with by chance. To the river, we must escape to the river!"

The instructors, who had put down their popcorn and tensed up for fear of failing to rescue students, watched anxiously.
The chaos ended with over 100 seniors being caught by magic beasts and rescued by instructors after fainting, and almost as many students collapsing after being cut by a sword.

"Enjoying the show?"
"Huh! Zuang. When did you arrive?"
"With him. It wasn't bad teaming up."

Blood from someone was splattered on Zuang's cheek as she smiled.
When I raised my hand to wipe it off, Zuang flinched and stepped back.
This girl, really.
Why be embarrassed with something like this on your cheek?
I closed the gap and wiped the blood off her cheek with my hand.

"Now you're clean!"
"...You get too close too easily."
"Huh?"
"Nothing. So what about the exam? You were just watching here?"
"I was waiting for you two to arrive!"
"For us?"

Zuang looked confused.
Xing, who had fearlessly charged at the magic beasts, cut off all their branches, and pointed his sword at the core of the now-limbless magic beast, using it like a boat to cross the river.
When I explained to them why I had been waiting, they gave me incredulous looks.

"You're saying you'll send 100 people starting with friends because that's the requirement? Aren't you taking the exam too lightly? The professor must have prepared hard for this."
"Hmm... I don't think you're one to talk, Zuang!"
"Is that so? Well, I was also collecting nameplates like collectibles on my way here."

A handful of blood-stained nameplates came out of her inner pocket.

"Wow! With those, everyone from the frontier line would pass!"
"Should I return them to the kids who lost them?"
"Yes!"
"Hmm... is that what Oknodie would like?"
"Yes, yes!"
"Why should I give them back? Aren't you curious too, Oknodie? What penalty they get for losing their nameplates. What happens to those who reach the goal without their nameplates."

Zuang, who wanted to step on and tear apart weak classmates like wanting to step on an ant or tear the wings off a dragonfly—truly a sadistic instinct befitting a 0.1% unlucky evil character.

"Then we can test it on an imperial student."
"That's true. But I don't want to return them for free."
"Then take points for returning them!"
"Even if we make a deal, how would we meet them? The exam is still ongoing."
"I think that'll be fine. The finish line is right here, isn't it?"

Zuang giggled and tapped the finish line with her foot.

"Everyone's a fool. Wandering far away when it's so close."
"Shouldn't you go too, Zuang?"
"Why should I?"
"Because I destroyed the mount storage!"
"Doesn't matter."

Zuang took out something like a small flute from her bosom and blew into it.
At the sound that stimulated an ultrahigh-pitched range reminiscent of bat ultrasound inaudible to human ears, Zuang's mount came running from afar!

"Wow!"
"I trained it hard. What do you think?"
"Amazing!"

Zuang, who had struggled so much trying to put a leash on the giant hostile cat that raised its claws, had finally succeeded in taming it using a flute.
As a veteran, I feel a bit reflective.
To summon a pet without using physical or magical violence, just by blowing a flute—truly, the world of taming is deep and profound.

"I'll teach you this if you teach me the technique to change the finish line later? I think I could use it for assassination techniques where I leave valuables with a target and then switch them with bombs."
"Sure!"

The instructor's face filled with terror as he witnessed the exchange of techniques between assassins.

"Ah, Instructor. Perfect timing. Can we use the unconscious seniors?"
"Killing people will disqualify you."
"What? Of course. Why would we kill people in a first-year midterm?"
"...You'll also be disqualified if you kill people indirectly by teleporting them."
"Who would do such a horrible thing? I just want to place them across the river and fulfill the contract I made with the saintess."

The instructor nodded with an exhausted face.

"I apologize. Perhaps I thought too ill of you. That much doesn't violate the rules."
"Oknodie, what about the imperial kids who aren't unconscious?"
"We can just knock them out."
"You know, you're secretly quite mean."
"I don't think you're one to talk, Zuang!"

The instructor's complexion darkened further as he hovered around, preparing for any unfortunate incidents.

"They say eating candy helps when you're feeling down. Would you like one?"
"...No thanks. I ate enough popcorn earlier."
"You must be very upset. Your expression got even darker!"
"Eating that would make it even darker."
"Because you'd feel worse?"
"...Yes."

Fear filled the instructor's eyes.
Is eating something when you're upset that painful?

"Would you like me to pat your back?"
"Please just leave me alone..."
