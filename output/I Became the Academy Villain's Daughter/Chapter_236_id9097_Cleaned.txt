# Enhanced Translation: Chapter 237 - Oknodie Lacks Confidence

A big match between first-year honors class top student Oknodie and second-year seniors!
People who heard the rumors abandoned their events, even those who had been waiting in line, and rushed to the "Paper Airplane Throwing" arena.

"Second-years aren't that special, right?"
"I heard Oknodie crushed the second-year lower class seniors in the midterms."
"These people are different. They're from the second-year intermediate class."
"There's an intermediate class?"
"It starts from the second semester of first year. They gather students who did well in the lower class during the first semester and those who qualified for intermediate class in the entrance exam."
"Aren't they just misfits who couldn't make it to the honors class? Even if they're second-years, I think Oknodie will win easily if they're not honors class."

The second-years smirked at the clueless first-years' complaints.

"Quiet, juniors. If you don't know, keeping your mouth shut gets you halfway there."
"Not everyone who didn't make honors class is a misfit. Some have honors-level skills but ended up in intermediate class after losing to other honors students in exams."
"Some were demoted to intermediate class for receiving too many penalty points."

The first-years couldn't hide their confusion.

"You get demoted to intermediate class for penalty points?"
"Didn't you know? To stay in honors class, you need to pay points as a class maintenance fee every semester."
"Whaaaat?!"
"Without points, you can't maintain honors class status or advance from lower to higher classes. You can't even become a second-year without points."
"What do you do if you don't have points?"
"You attend supplementary classes during breaks to earn points and do everything the Academy offers that gives points."

As the first-years showed interest, the second-year seniors subtly lowered their heads and spoke quietly, drawing the juniors closer.

"Club activities give quite a lot of points. If you join our Mountaineering Club, you can earn many points while visiting nice places with professors. Want to join?"
"Hey, don't sell them false hopes. That mountaineering club is a crazy group that hunts boss monsters all over the place. Better join the Healing Camp Club instead."
"You're selling false hopes too. Your club has a name that sounds like playing in flower fields but you're just a medical support team that provides emergency treatment on battlefields!"

Crab Mentality.
Like the global common saying about crabs in a basket, these second-years were more concerned about others succeeding than their own well-being, pulling each other down.
As a result, the first-years, now looking disgruntled, refused to respond to any of their recruitment attempts.

"So who are these second-year intermediate class seniors challenging us, and how great are they?"

Students more interested in Oknodie before them than any club.
More precisely, students excited about the possibility of seeing the great Oknodie lose!

"Lycan is a master of barrier magic. No matter what attack formula Oknodie uses, it's impossible to shoot down his airplane."
"Jeiras is a master of energy magic. Jeiras's attack formulas, which possess no attributes, will force a mobility battle that doesn't allow attribute defense."
"Miini is unmatched in formula miniaturization, even honors class students can't keep up. She'll bring an incredible number of formulas that others couldn't even imagine."

In summary, there's a master of defense whose plane cannot be shot down, and to counter this, one must show exceptional mobility to avoid attack formulas.
And on top of these two, there's another unpredictable powerhouse—an extreme lineup!

"Seniors! Before we compete, how about we each place bets on ourselves?"
"Bets?"
"Each person bets 10,000 points on their own victory. With the referee certifying the conditions. What do you think?"

Instead of being intimidated or acting cute to ask for mercy against such a formidable lineup, she provokes them with a bet.
Second-year Jeiras's face clearly showed his wounded pride.

"I was going to go easy on you because you're a cute junior, but it seems I need to get serious."
"What about the other two?"
"Count me out... I don't have confidence in beating Miini."

Lycan, the barrier magic expert, backed out.
Miini maintained a haughty expression through her magic department's gold-trimmed robe.

"20,000 points."
"!"
"If you don't have that kind of courage, it's better not to start. My skills are extraordinary."

The crowd's agitation grew.

"How confident is that senior?"
"Isn't that too much? Trying to extort 20,000 points from a first-year."
"No, she's actually making her face reality. A bet of 20,000 points is too large for a first-year to accept rashly."
"It is a sum that allows one to back out while acknowledging the difference in skill level without losing too much face. Nobody would easily accept a 20,000-point bet regardless of skill."
"Is it actually merciful then? Still, it's disappointing... I really wanted to see Oknodie lose."

Isabel, with rising anger, rolled up her sleeves.
As blood vessels bulged in her arms, trained by wok-handling, fan-waving, and cooking, Oknodie grabbed her and stopped her.

"It's okay, Isabel."
"They're looking down on you."
"Then we'll make them bet points too, right?"
"...Are you confident?"
"Of course!"

Isabel boldly requested the supervisor who was acting as referee.

"I'd like spectators to be able to bet points and predict who will win."
"No."
"Why not?"
"It's annoying to calculate point distributions."
"...What if you take 1% as a commission fee?"
"I said it's annoying."

A supervisor lacking motivation no matter what!
He had his reasons for lacking will.

"I've failed the fourth-year advancement exam for three years now and I'm working as a supervisor to earn points. Do you know how many points are needed for advancement?"
"How many?"
"Ten million points."
"...What?"
"That measly 1% of the kids' pocket change won't change anything in my life."

Students were stunned by the shocking amount.

"How many gold coins is ten million points?"
"100,000 gold coins."
"That's insane."

It's a sum that commoners, who tremble at spending even one gold coin, have never even thought about in their lives, and even nobles can't manage such an amount unless they're high nobility.
A family would need to provide full support to barely make fourth-year advancement!

"If the supervisor isn't interested, I'll handle this."
"You are...?"

The supervisor's expression crumpled.

"Student council?"
"Festival management staff Nalbi from the student council. I'll certify it under my name. Commission fee is 5%."
"Do as you please."

As the supervisor backed out, Isabel immediately placed her magic watch on the mana board Nalbi offered.

[Isabel bets 17,800 points on Oknodie for first place.]

"I've bet my entire fortune. If you're confident in Oknodie's defeat, this is free money in your eyes, right? There shouldn't be any cowards backing out."
"W-why should we make such a bet? That's stupid."
"Right. If Oknodie happens to win by luck, we'd just lose for nothing!"

Hearing the cowardly complaints of the first-years, second-year Jeiras, who had been quietly watching the situation unfold, became angry.

"Are you saying that I, a second-year, might lose to a first-year by luck?"
"Th-that's not what I meant."
"It's just a figure of speech."
"Oh? Is your life only until the festival with nothing after? Want me to ruin your Academy life?"
"Eeeek!"
"If you don't want to die, bet on me or Miini. I won't let cowards who just chatter behind backs and back out when they need to take risks get away with it."

Students with even slightly more talent than others have experienced jealousy or envy at some point.
Jeiras had such experiences too, and he found it very unpleasant that first-years would freely disrespect Oknodie but try to back out when actual betting began.

"F-fine, I'll bet."
"I'll bet too..."

[Nox bets 3,500 points on Jeiras for first place.]
[Biden bets 2,700 points on Miini for first place.]

As points started coming in earnest, other spectators opened their wallets too.

Amazing odds for just one 100-point individual event!
The number of participants quickly increased too.

"If Jeiras wins, the points multiply by 3.55? I'll join in and help Jeiras."
"Then I'll interfere with Oknodie's airplane."
"Isabel joined to help Oknodie. I can do the same."

Oknodie had only one helper: Isabel.
In contrast, Jeiras and Miini each had two helpers.
Additionally, the two first-years Nox and Biden, drawn into the bet, joined with the intention of hindering Oknodie.
Ten people total.
For Oknodie, it was essentially a 2 versus 8 battle!

"Oknodie. What formula should I use to help you?"

As Isabel tried to strategize how to help her, Oknodie said:

"Do whatever you want, Isabel!"
'...Maybe she doesn't need my help?'

Hiding her hurt feelings, Isabel examined the number of formulas placed on Oknodie's airplane.
She blinked and rubbed her eyes with the back of her hand, wondering if she was seeing things.
But what she saw didn't change.

'First-years get praised for embedding just three formulas, but she's put more than ten.'

Even at a glance, it seemed far more than the number of formulas second-year Miini had placed on her airplane.
