# <101 - Chance Encounter>

After the grueling fifth period ended.
I wanted to sleep hanging from a tree without worrying about becoming crab food, but I had too many assignments for that.
The most annoying one was the "Ranged Weapon Mastery" lecture.
The shooting training assignment required hitting targets for a total of 1000 points each from distances of 50m, 100m, 150m, and 200m, meaning I'd have to shoot at least 400 times even if I hit only the 10-point targets.
To make matters worse, the bad weather meant the outdoor shooting range was filled with poor students shooting arrows while completely soaked in rain, even late at night.

'Not going there.'

I could ask an instructor for an escort to shoot arrows for safety reasons, but the instructors would insist on observing the dormitory curfew.
I could always sneak out again, but the time spent going back and forth would be wasted.

'This is when I should use the secret training ground!'

As you can tell from the double-sided tape room, the Gift Academy has many thoughtful considerations from seniors for their juniors.
Murderous assignments.
Inhuman professors.
Physically impossible remaining time.
The magic department seniors, feeling sorry for the overworked juniors, created a special training ground for their poor juniors.

'A private secret training ground available 24/7!'

Since it was only accessible from the Magic Department building, I headed toward the magic building through the downpour. Despite the late hour, I made eye contact with a first-year student.

'Huh? That's Casia.'

The frontier's Group A.
The empire's Group B.
The special Group C.
If the arrogant imperial students and the poor frontier students who suffer from regional discrimination form opposing sides, Group C is a presence that both sides find uncomfortable.
Those with unknown identities.
Those stripped of nationality.
The stateless.
Pseudo-humans.
A group of people from the shadows who are treated worse than beastmen, or are considered too dangerous—people who aren't recognized as human in society.
It might have been a group I could have belonged to as well.
Group C students must always be accompanied by an instructor, so an instructor was also waiting near Casia.

Tiptoeing.

Suddenly I felt like playing a prank.
I hid my body to avoid the window and tiptoed toward it.
And then... when I reached right in front of the window, I jumped up with both arms raised to surprise her!

"Mwoooong!"
"...Idiot."
"Huh? How did you know?"

She was definitely sitting at the first window by the entrance before I approached.
Making my attempt to surprise her by taking the long way around look foolish, Casia had moved to the second window seat and was mocking me as I popped out from the side.

"Just because."

Somehow, I felt challenged.

"Yah!"
"Idiot."

When I aimed for the second window, she moved to the third seat.

"Yah!"
"Moron."

When I tried to catch her off guard by coming out at the second window again, she was still at the third window.

"Just let me get you once!"
"No."

When I went to the third window and then circled back to the first window, this time she was at the second window.
Me, with shell game skills that could beat Professor Bronze, suffering a complete defeat against a Group C student?
It's frustrating, but at least I learned something.

'This iteration's Casia has high performance, huh?'

Casia's performance varies drastically with each iteration, but this time she seems exceptionally strong.
From a player's perspective, it's just a matter of saying "That's lucky" and moving on, but for the person involved, overly strong abilities make daily life difficult.

"You sensed me using bioelectricity, right?"
"...Aren't you an idiot?"
"The group's top student can't be an idiot!"

Casia is an electricity user.
She can emit electricity in a way that's closer to divine power than magic.
A well-developed Casia can grow to become a human power plant capable of replacing the electricity of an entire city.
If you need someone with lightning attributes, seeking out Casia is not just an option but a necessity!

"Aren't you going back to the dormitory?"
"Can't go back."
"Why?"
"Because it's dangerous."

For an ordinary student, that would mean the Giant King Crab is dangerous, but that doesn't apply to her.
When she says "dangerous," she means everyone except herself.

"If you're not careful, you'll die."

A ticking time bomb who can't fully control her own overwhelmingly strong power.
That's the concept of Casia as a playable character.

"Student Oknodie. There's not much time left before curfew. Please prepare to return to the dormitory."

As the instructor supervising Casia began to lecture, I continued on my way.

"Well, goodbye~"
"...Hmph."

Casia turned her head coldly.
A classmate I couldn't befriend until advancing to the next grade.
Befitting a late-game character with a different dimension of strength, her prickliness is also remarkable.

'Well, at least I've made her acquaintance.'

Without hesitation, I headed to the secret training ground.


* *


Casia had turned her head away, but she could vividly sense Oknodie leaving.

"Did you like that child?"
"Not at all."
"For someone who claims that, you played with her for quite a while."
"She's just better than you."
"That's disappointing. This instructor is sincerely carrying out surveillance and protection duties for the safety of the Academy students, yet instead of gratitude for my efforts, I receive coldness."

The instructor was joking, but Casia's eyes were colder than when she was dealing with Oknodie.
If her previous coldness was meant to push people away, her current coldness was that of someone wary of an enemy.

"The student you're protecting isn't me, but everyone else. I have no reason to be grateful."
"I'm making sure you don't carelessly use your abilities or do something dangerous that might turn your classmates into electrocuted barbecue, so you could say I'm helping you too."
"While planning to cut my throat immediately if I make a real mistake."
"I wouldn't cut your throat right away for a first mistake. I'd knock you unconscious so you can't move rashly and cause more damage, then cut the tendons in your limbs."
"...You're trash."
"Even trash like me is better than the humans from where you lived. You should always be grateful for that fact."

Casia is a fusion life form created in a laboratory.
A human consciousness was implanted into a non-human being.

-If a creature is difficult to control with human power, wouldn't it be easier to handle if we insert a manageable consciousness?

Life, mind, soul.
Casia, an electricity user born in a terrible laboratory that thoroughly used all human elements for experiments.
She has nowhere to return to.
She was sold to the Academy for a huge sum of money.
For her to leave the Academy would mean having to pay back the money the laboratory received.
The laboratory director would never let her live.
Perhaps a future worse than death awaits her.
That's what it means to become a Group C student.

A being with nowhere to return to, and who shouldn't return.
A being who cannot survive outside the Academy.

'I thought you were one of us too.'

Normally, she wouldn't even think of entertaining someone playing childish pranks.
Because it's difficult.
Dealing with students living normal lives.
Being careful not to let them die.
Every single movement is irritating and requires caution.
If she accidentally shoots a lightning bolt from her fingertips, it's an everyday occurrence for someone to be burned to death.

-Now, choose. Will you kill others? Or will you kill yourself?

Higher intensity.
Longer output.
The things the laboratory did to strengthen her abilities were unspeakably horrible.

'I thought a child trained in assassination to kill people would be in the same situation.'

Oknodie was both similar and different.
She had an adult who could vouch for her identity.
Just one difference.
That one difference determined their groups.
Oknodie became Group A, not Group C.
And she made many friends and became popular in the upper class.
The rumors and reputation about her are excellent.
She doesn't discriminate against lower classes or fear imperial students.
She freely mingles with imperial students, sharing meals, and when necessary, she stands up against injustice for socially vulnerable friends.

'Even her skills were extraordinary.'

Oknodie approaching from outside the window just now.
Casia had been watching her the whole time.
People have bioelectricity.
Most people don't know about it or feel it.
Even with training, most can only perceive it through skin contact or within the same space.

'I'm different. Because I'm half non-human.'

The non-human part of her body detects it.
The flow of bioelectricity operating in others' bodies.
Even through windows.
Even through magical barriers.
She can visually detect subtle bioelectricity through physical and magical barriers.
The first three times, she avoided Oknodie easily.
Evading Oknodie was no challenge at all.

'The fourth time was different.'

When she moved toward the right window, Oknodie followed.
When she moved toward the left window, Oknodie changed direction and followed again.
Oknodie was clearly reading her position.
She wanted to test her.

<Electrical Phenomena Manipulation>

Despite the instructor watching from behind, emanating killing intent and urging her to stop, she activated her ability.
She created a doppelganger with the same appearance as herself and placed it in position.
Beyond the window, Oknodie tilted her head and then looked at the second window.

"Yah!"

Not entirely confident, Oknodie poked her head out of the first window.
When Casia deactivated her ability, all that remained was empty space, herself having moved to the adjacent window, and the instructor silently pressuring her to end the prank.

'It wasn't electricity. She was detecting mana.'

Casia realized.
This girl was indeed special.
Next time they meet, she might greet her properly.
She liked her much more than her Group C peers who stuck together because of their similar circumstances.
