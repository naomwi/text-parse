# <426 - The Vast Gap>

"No way! I absolutely cannot give up Professor Sadako's lectures."

It's not just because I'm curious about the bare face hidden behind her bangs.
I'm also worried that Zuang and Titosoga, who attend the lectures with me, will cry "hiiing-ing-ing" tears, asking how we could run away without them.
Besides, Professor Sadako decided to stay at the Academy to continue her lectures because I was attending them diligently. If I leave too, there would be no one left to attend.

'If I run away, Zuang and Titosoga will surely notice and quietly escape too. Then the professor, heartbroken, will put down her teaching materials and retire!'

No matter how I think about it, that would be too cruel.

"That vicious colony appears to be your creation. Are you saying you won't give up Professor Sadako's lectures even if you have to take full responsibility for it?"
"I have no choice. I'll have to report to Professor Weird and pay the fine..."
"You're truly insane. Determined to get noticed by not just one, but two of the Academy's most eccentric professors. What made you like this?"

I answered sincerely.

"Fanboys naturally live life at a disadvantage."
"...What a hopeless, pathetic junior. It's regrettable that someone like you is my junior."

While grumbling, Grim Reaper senior pulled out the large scythe strapped to his back.

"Stop calling for help. I'll help you just this once, as a special favor."
"Wow! Grim Reaper senior, you're the best!"

This senior's personality type is 90% likely to be tsundere, and sure enough, he proved to be a tsundere senior this time too!


* * *


Ishtar detected an unusual fluctuation of mana near the open space.

"Senior. What's in there?"
"Shoo, shoo. Kids don't need to know."
"I am the hero Ishtar. If you're in trouble, I can help you."

It wasn't simply an act of kindness.
Since realizing how far behind Oknodie she was, Ishtar had never stopped working hard.

-Is Ishtar really a hero?
-Honestly, Oknodie seems more like a hero. Heroes abandon people while Oknodie saves them. How is that thing a hero?
-The one who actually defeated the demon contractor Low was essentially Foundation executive and current professor Jonna and Oknodie.

They criticize without knowing anything.
Ishtar gritted her teeth inwardly.

'That's just how heroes are.'

A hero who can save everyone isn't worthy of being called a hero in the first place.
The one true god <Sophemia of the Sun> doesn't recognize as her hero someone who settles for less than their potential and obsesses only with padding their achievements.
Mass-produced heroes selected as apostles of other deities might choose such complacent lives to elevate their deity and gather faith, but Ishtar was different.

'I must never stop challenging myself. Otherwise, I'll lose the name of hero.'

Therefore, she couldn't live a life full of dreams and hope, caring for the weak and advancing together with everyone.
The sun isn't an entity that advances with others.
It's merely an object of reverence that rises most brilliantly above everyone's heads.
Still, she realized the necessity of collective power.
That's why she organized the Hero's Guard and allocated the minimum necessary time for separate training.

'But it's not enough. Now I need to focus on my own training, my own challenges.'

She had recently discovered that the <Comprehensive Dungeon Theme Park> facility in the underground grand cavity contained numerous dungeons and monsters teeming with life.
She had submitted applications to explore those dungeons, attempting to practice by conquering increasingly deeper dungeons, but she hit the upper limit of accessible dungeons due to the restriction of being a first-year student.
While the Hero's Guard might be training, Ishtar herself wasn't.

'I've gritted my teeth through lectures too, but I'm starting to adapt to them now. Do I have to settle for this much this semester...?'

Just as she was reluctantly trying to compromise with reality, she felt that unusual fluctuation of mana.

"No way. If a first-year meddles around here, I'll be the only one getting scolded."
"Then I'll defeat you, senior."
"What?"
"If you're attacked and defeated by a stronger opponent, even that won't be your responsibility."
"Huh?!"

<Light Cluster>
<Mountain Summit Single Slash>

A name so grandiose it speaks of conquering a mountain peak with a single slash.
Yet this single-strike technique deserves its arrogance.
Jonna Wiheomhae, who subdues massive natural phenomena like hail with a single stroke.
The inadequacy she felt when she saw that man's back.
This was a technique she developed to shake off her powerless past.

'Third year. I have no intention of settling for just that level.'

The third-year senior who was controlling access to prevent safety accidents collapsed after receiving the hero's single slash.

'Wow, she's really crazy. Instead of resisting and getting hit more, I should just collapse right away.'

Thanks to the senior who collapsed with good sense, Ishtar, who had unintentionally defeated the senior with a single blow, gained even more confidence.

"Yupi. Skola. A perfect practice opponent has appeared."
"The mana quantity seems manageable."
"A plant monster? The compatibility isn't good, but I'll do my best."

As Saint Yupi raised her staff, a shower of light indicating enhancement poured down continuously.

<Expanded Vision>
<Improved Breathing>
<Enhanced Reflexes>
<Double Strike Granted>
<Double Charge Granted>
<Poison Resistance Granted>
<Sleep Resistance Granted>

Skola trembled at the buffs that were granted to him along with the hero.

'What an incredible speed of continuous buffs. With buffs like these, we can accomplish anything.'

Moreover, the hero was receiving three times as many buffs as him.
We've won.
Confidence overflowed even before the fight began.
Their mana quantity exceeded the enemy's.
They had powered up with buffs.
The threatening offensive that poured blade winds in all directions would inevitably be blocked by the hero's Holy Mirror-108 Directional Formation combo.

<Barrier Granted>

With Yupi's barrier applied on top of the 108 Holy Mirrors, they didn't even have to worry about the gate breaking.
Victory was essentially confirmed!
Until the early stages of the battle, the plant colony that was pouring blade winds from all directions against the hero's attack techniques seemed like it would be destroyed soon.

"Hero. Haven't you deployed too many Holy Mirrors? The attack techniques are being blocked by other Holy Mirrors, disappearing at the moments when they should be landing effective hits."
"It's not me."
"What?"
"Those aren't Holy Mirrors I opened!"
"!!"

No sooner had she finished speaking than a Holy Mirror opened behind Skola, followed by a slash pouring out from it.
As Skola dodged in shock, he finally noticed a Holy Mirror that had been absorbed into a spider web by the Spider Plant's <Pull>.
Amazingly, this plant colony had succeeded in absorbing the hero's technique and making it its own.

'So now we're fighting a monster that can use the hero's techniques?!'
"Skola. Destroy the Holy Mirrors without barriers!"

As Saint Yupi said, there was a way to distinguish them.
They just needed to destroy the Holy Mirrors without protective barriers.
But that wasn't easy.

"Hurry up!"
"I've been destroying them all along. But no matter how many I destroy, new Holy Mirrors keep opening!"

They didn't know that the royal jelly, which had appeared to be just nutritional jelly, was imbued with mana, and that by absorbing spirits, the mana recovery rate had increased as well.
That difference led to a miscalculation of power, ultimately resulting in the hero's defeat.

"Ugh."
"Ishtar!"

Thanks to Yupi's protective barrier, Ishtar avoided serious injury but was flung to the corner of the open space.

"The attack failed... Everyone run away."

This can't be blocked.
We need to flee.
But even escape was difficult now.

<Holy Mirror>
<Wind Cutter>
<Earth Splitter>
<Deadly Technique - Tempest Blade>
<Deadly Technique - Seven Vortex>
<Deadly Technique - Horizon Demolisher>

Holy Mirrors were continuously created around Ishtar, Yupi, and Skola.
Blade winds and deadly techniques poured out from Holy Mirrors that opened at close range.
The plant colony, which mimicked all types of techniques that used mana, was more formidable than they had imagined.

'If you open Holy Mirrors at close range, assuming they'll be destroyed anyway, does it become this difficult? More importantly, how can a mere plant execute deadly techniques!'

The sturdy body of the Barrel Plant had durability comparable to the hero's body, and the Spider Plant's enhanced mana control exerted the control needed to execute the hero's deadly techniques.
The most insignificant monsters that novice adventurers first encounter when leaving the village.

Goblins.
Slimes.
And plant mobs.

The plant colony, which she had considered to be merely an extension of such insignificant beings, crushed her pride by mimicking even the hero's techniques.

"I'll open a path. At least you two escape."

A special 100-stack deadly technique, which she had amplified by moving between mirrors without using it for attacks, shattered all the Holy Mirrors deployed around Yupi and Skola.

"Hold on a little longer. I'll call the instructors!"
"Yupi. You won't need to go that far."

Skola stopped Yupi, who was hurriedly trying to operate her magic watch.
Where Skola pointed, Oknodie stood with a thoroughly displeased expression, alongside a senior who wore an identical expression.

"Is the game over? Then move aside."

It wasn't a game.
We fought with all our might.
Yupi wanted to retort like that, but regardless of her will, her mouth closed and her feet were pushed away.

"?!"

It wasn't magic.
Yet it manipulated phenomena like magic.
Yupi felt fear when she realized she was already within a <Domain> that projected will onto the surrounding space simply by thinking, so naturally was it deployed.
It closed her mouth and pushed her back.
If it had tried to slit her throat and burst her heart instead.
Could she have resisted?

"The method for eliminating monsters with the talent for mimicking attacks is simple. Just blow them all away in one strike so they can't even mimic."

Great magic.
The power beyond heaven that she had witnessed on the cruise ship during summer vacation erupted from a single scythe.

<Tentative Name, Death Reaper Slash>

The plant colony was erased as if carving out space itself.
Not even fragments of materials remained as the distorted void pulled up the roots left on the ground.

<Reverse Slash>

With a strike that traced back along the trajectory and cut through the void again, all the roots of the plant colony were annihilated.
The formidable opponent that had given the hero's party such a hard time completely disappeared with just two scythe slashes.
Yupi and Skola could only follow the traces with dejected faces.

"If you can't accomplish this much, don't mess with plant colonies that have attack mimicry. Your clumsy talents cause greater disasters because assassination requests never stop when humans who should be killing each other get killed by monsters instead."

After calmly admonishing them, the senior grabbed a few falling stems from the air and left the open space.
Watching Oknodie's back as she politely bowed at the waist to see the senior off, Yupi realized.

'That must be... a fourth-year from the Foundation. So the Foundation has such masters even below the executive level...?'

The Wiheomhae Foundation is much stronger than I thought.
Meanwhile, Ishtar only has us by her side.
The Empire's capable individuals only provide the minimum necessary support to the hero who might threaten the Emperor's position in the future.
If she needs power, she must become stronger herself, and if she needs reinforcements, she must recruit them herself.
Meanwhile, Oknodie can easily borrow such talent through the Foundation's power.

"This is... a complete defeat."
"..."

Skola was right.
The gap was beyond imagination.
Her heart felt like it might crumble a little.
Even she, as the hero's companion, felt this level of powerlessness.
The person in question, Ishtar, must feel at least this bad, if not worse.
Yupi's gaze followed Ishtar's back as she lay fallen, unable to even lift her head.
Her hand tightly gripped the weeds on the ground.
What kind of face was Ishtar making right now?
Yupi became afraid to see that face.
