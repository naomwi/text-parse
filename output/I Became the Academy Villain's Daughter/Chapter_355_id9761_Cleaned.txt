<355 - A Diligent Daily Life>

Clank, clank.
Fellow classmates snickered at the sight of Mob walking with his heavy armor making noise again today.

"Wow, look at that weirdo. Why is he going to lectures during a week when we're allowed to rest?"
"Just look at him. Does someone who trains all day in full body armor look like he could relax like us? He's the type who'll be buried in his armor."
"Wasn't this just supplementary lectures anyway? Isn't he just an underachiever who doesn't know when to take a break?"
"Haha. That's harsh. He might start crying."
'I really want to cry, you jerks.'

Mob felt dejected.
While others were running around campus with exorcism equipment purchased with points from the faculty to enjoy the exorcism event, he was attending lectures.
Honestly, he had studied hard normally, so he desperately wanted to receive the principal's consideration, rest well, and have fun at times like this.

━━━
☆MANDATORY READING FOR ALL MEMBERS☆
All of us in the "Organization That Plays With Oknodie" should focus on studying instead of playing during this week's event since we play a lot normally!
If anyone gets caught playing, they might have to play a special game with me on the weekend!!
Even if you're playing exorcism games, NEVER get caught by me!!!
━━━

Oknodie's warning note, written in cute handwriting that showed she had worked hard with her small hands, conveyed an underlying sense of terror despite its appearance.
If anyone had the courage to go around playing exorcism games after seeing this, they would deserve respect.
Of course, Mob wasn't particularly interested in earning others' respect and had added another page to his image as a training maniac.

'Am I bitter because I can't play like others?'

That's how he felt at first.
But it was different.
Mob noticed the emotion he was feeling.
A different thought gradually emerging.

"Mob. You're attending lectures even on a day like today?"
"How unexpected. I thought a student like Mob might not show up."

Even the professors wore expressions suggesting they hadn't expected him to come.
Their faces seemed to ask why a student who was expected to drop out long ago had dragged himself to the lecture hall.
In truth, this wasn't the first time he'd seen such faces.

"First-year Mob. Why are you attending lectures in full body armor...?"
"...I have various circumstances."
"Magic armor requires substantial equipment points for use on campus without permission."
"This isn't magic armor."
"Let me see, according to the registered equipment application, that armor is... hmm, training equipment?"

He's just showing off.
He's being dramatic.
Let's see how long he lasts.
Gazes no different from his classmates poured down on him.
Even the professors who taught him were the same.
Not to mention himself.
There's a limit to persevering out of stubbornness.
He was tired now.
He wanted to take it off.
There were days when he was so exhausted he wanted to cry and beg to stop.
But Oknodie said:

"As expected, Mob is 'someone who can do it'! It's rewarding to teach the weight course. I wonder if Jonna felt this way about me?"

You are someone who can do it, she said.
You can endure, she said.
When no one else in the world believed in him, the top student in the year, Oknodie, believed in him.
The most outstanding first-year in this school believed in him.
His heart swelled.
He was even angry at himself for the thoughts he'd been having.
It wasn't the professors.
The one who truly taught him.
The one who believed in him and extended a hand to pull him up to see higher scenery.
It was the academy's shortest girl, Oknodie.

'I won't break. No, I can't break!'

Not for himself, but for Oknodie who believed in him, he would endure.
That single stubbornness supported his wavering, folding, weakening heart, helping him to straighten his crouched legs from the very bottom of his heart, to plant his feet and stand up again.

"Mob. You attended again today."
"Yes."
"Hmm. I thought you would."

The professor's gaze, which had looked at him with disbelief that his efforts wouldn't last long, that it was just showing off a little longer than others, had changed.
Among students who desperately needed rest more than anyone.
The professor finally acknowledged the diligence of a student who refused that rest and came to the lecture hall.

'I'm glad I'm wearing a helmet.'

Mob thought.
Honestly, tears had welled up a little.
He didn't want to show such an embarrassing sight to others.
Days that were painful, hard, and difficult.
The harsh academy schedule where just enduring drained his spirit.
But today, he was happy.
He felt like he had regained his initial determination after a long time.
At the same time, he could discover his stronger self anew.

"Mob, 65 points. An average score."
"Yes..."
"But it's improved quite a bit. Compared to the 22 points you received when you first took this lecture."

He remembered.
The moment when he was disappointed in himself for having a body as slow as his mind.
His pathetically dull talent that still couldn't escape the lower class even with the significant growth he'd achieved after all that suffering during the break.
But he didn't despair and continued to endure.
Finally, it had arrived.
The moment his efforts were recognized.

'I want to tell her.'

Like a child looking for their parents after receiving good evaluation at school, the first person Mob thought of was Oknodie.
After all, she was the one who had put him where he was today, who had made it possible for him to enjoy this joy.

"Oh, Black Knight Mob. Still playing with armor, I see."
"Excuse me, where is Oknodie?"

A student who had joined Oknodie's organization as a foundation scholarship recipient glanced at his watch and said:

"Lecture hall."
"After the lecture ends?"
"The next lecture hall."
"And after that?"
"Eat and then another lecture hall."
"..."

Ah, that's right.
Oknodie was taking a whopping 38 credits.
Compared to that, what he was doing was nothing.
He felt deflated somehow.
He felt somewhat ashamed for trying to boast about something insignificant to a child who was working so hard.
Expressing gratitude was too much.
Still, he made a resolution.
To not lose this elation of today and continue to devote himself to studying day by day.
If he did that, someday he would naturally meet Oknodie and have the opportunity to convey his gratitude.

"Emergency situation! Everyone in the hideout, come out!"
"What's going on? Whoa, you're covered in blood!"

But that opportunity came sooner than expected.
The student who had been telling him about Oknodie's schedule spoke in alarm to someone who had just entered.

"The exorcism-obsessed guys have started causing trouble."
"What does blood flowing from a normal person's forehead have to do with exorcism?"
"The seniors have taken all the ghosts to exorcise, so the first-years participating in the festival don't have enough prey to hunt!"
"Then isn't that even less related?"
"It's become a problem because they're beating up innocent people and claiming they're driving out possession spirits to earn faculty points!"
"...Those crazy bastards!"

That's a serious problem!
Mob shouldered his training steel spear.

"Lead the way."
"Oh, Black Knight Mob is coming too? That's reassuring."
"Idiot. Don't talk and go to the medical wing first."

The exorcism scammers had taken control of the connecting passage between buildings and were threatening classmates with exorcism equipment.

"You guys are going to lectures, right? Hand over some points on your way. Or do you want to be exorcised?"
"You jerks! What did we do wrong? We're just students who desperately need supplementary classes!"
"Shut up! Did you think we'd forgive conscience-less people who go study alone on a day when everyone implicitly agreed to enjoy the break? If you're not in your right mind, you're possessed by a study bug, and if you are in your right mind, you deserve more beating!"
"Ack! Aaack!"

Even to Mob, it wasn't a pleasant sight.
Students who, like him, had suppressed their desire to play and diligently came to attend lectures were getting beaten up.
Even the Jigoku Pirates wouldn't do something this cruel—it was truly a sight that made him want to curse.

"Attack the ones beating up students first!"
"Who are you guys? You're the Organization That Plays With Oknodie, aren't you? Are you interfering?!"
"Some of our members were among the students you beat up!"

In the chaos, Mob joined in, swinging a blunt wooden sword.
With his body enhanced through diligent training and the skills he had worked hard to develop creating a synergy effect, the same movements now had multiplied power, distorting his opponent's expression.

"This guy's pretty good!"
"You're quite skilled yourself, you thug who's more vicious than pirates."
"Ha. Of course! We who don't take supplementary classes are stronger than those who do."
"I acknowledge your skill. But you guys lack resolve."
"What resolve?"
"The resolve to train yourself diligently and grow, instead of exploiting others easily and comfortably."

Mob took his opponent's threatening stance head-on.
Then he stepped forward and grabbed the opponent's wrist with force.

"Ugh!?"

The wooden sword the opponent was swinging fell to the floor along with the twisted wrist.
The martial arts he had incidentally learned while studying Oknodie's acrobatics unfolded naturally as he twisted the arm behind the back, swept the legs, forced the opponent to sit, and stomped on their back.

"Your evil deeds end here. Unjust actions like oppressing classmates because seniors took away the ghosts to exorcise cannot be forgiven."
"W-what are you going to do with us!"
"I'll tie you all up and take you to the professors."
"Ugh. Are you planning to throw us in the great prison!"
"No. I'll ask them to tie you to chairs and force you to attend lectures."
"Argh! Just send us to prison instead. We don't want to attend lectures until the festival day!"

Students wailed miserably as they were restrained and dragged to the lecture hall.
Mob, having restored justice on campus, helped transport the prisoners to the lecture hall with a refreshed expression.

"Damn it. If we had worked with the Jigoku Pirates, we wouldn't have been caught..."
"Huh? We what?"

Students wearing shabby pirate bandanas looked up from taking notes inside the lecture hall.

"No way, why are you guys here? Don't tell me you were caught before us!?"
"No, we were just attending lectures?"
"Crazy. So we were really doing worse things than actual pirates."

The Jigoku Pirates, having returned from their boat trip, didn't particularly need the rewards from the exorcism festival either. Well-fed carnivores don't go hunting!
