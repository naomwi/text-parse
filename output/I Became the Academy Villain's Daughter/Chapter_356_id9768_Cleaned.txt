# 356 - Event Enhancement

Good intentions don't always lead to good results.
The activities of the organization that played with Mob and Oknodie were a perfect example of this.

[What is this?]
[Every single one of these kids is just studying.]

The advanced class was studying because they were mindful of Oknodie and the hero.
The lower class was studying because they were mindful of Oknodie's gang.
Even those who weren't part of either group ended up studying after receiving help from or being subdued by Oknodie's gang.
Dragon Principal, who had planned to burden the first-years with two weeks' worth of assignments after their fake festival revelry, became extremely displeased.
The fact that Oknodie, whom he had high expectations for, was the ringleader behind this study movement was particularly vexing.

"Isn't it admirable that the students are voluntarily studying so diligently?"

Dragon Principal snorted at Professor Mahabharata's attempt to defend them.

[With such a naive mindset, they'll just be used and discarded once they enter society.]
[I'll teach these first-years that the world isn't all about studying!]

'Wasn't this week-long event meant to teach them not to give in to temptation and to study diligently...?'

As always, Mahabharata suspected that discipline was just an excuse and that the principal's desire to have fun took priority. He couldn't help but feel sorry for the first-year students.


* * *


The first to notice the change in the Exorcism Festival were students who were hunting ghosts—the weak ones that upperclassmen had left behind out of guilt, considering them too insignificant to bother with.

"How's the back alley?"
"It's a goldmine. What about the drain?"
"It's crazy here too. They're suddenly pouring out everywhere."

Weak ghosts have a mechanism where they hide anywhere they can to survive. Just as hermit crabs hide in hard tin cans like they would in shells, ghosts tend to arbitrarily inhabit suitable objects left by students.

Taking advantage of this, lower-class students would place coins or straw dolls in dark, shady places, attend lectures, and then make rounds to find their hidden objects afterward.

Until now, even if they set up ten such traps, they'd be lucky to find one remaining after the upperclassmen had swept through. But suddenly, five out of ten coins were rattling and five out of ten straw dolls were moving on their own.

"The upperclassmen must have gone to attend a group lecture!"
"Wow, this is a once-in-a-lifetime opportunity!"
"Let's gather them quickly!"

The students frantically collected the moving coins and straw dolls from the ground and stuffed them into their bags.

"Huh? Wait a minute."

One of the lower-class students picking up coins noticed something strange. He definitely hadn't brought this many coins, yet somehow his coin pouch had become heavy with all the coins he'd collected.

"Whose coins are these?"
"Mine are right here. Don't leave your coins in someone else's spot."
"What nonsense are you both talking about? Mine are here too!"

No one had confused their spots. All three students felt a chill as they looked at their bulging pouches.

"Then whose coins are these?"

The jingling sound from the pouches no longer felt pleasant.

"L-let's throw them away!"
"Right. I don't want these creepy coins."
"I agree. Now's not the time to stick to our plan of taking these to where the upperclassmen performed exorcisms to piggyback off their remaining divine power!"

These students had come up with a scheme that would have earned applause from others for its cleverness.

"Are you going to throw them away?"
"Of course we're throwing them away! You just said we should and now you're asking such a random... huh?"

The shaggy-haired student was very confused when he spotted his skinny friend and chubby friend walking ahead of him.
There are three of us.
Then who's talking behind me right now?

"Why are you muttering to yourself... uh... uhh...?"
"Hurry up and follow... hyaaak!"

His friends suddenly let out a shrill scream and collapsed after turning to look at him.
Following their gaze, the lower-class student saw a ghost with a neck over 3 meters long, curved like a question mark, looking down at him.

"Are you going to throw them away?"
"Q-quickly, sprinkle the holy water!!"

This was dangerous.
This was no joke.
This wasn't some low-level ghost hiding in coins.
Rather, it was an entity that minted haunted coins, observing this habit—a ghost that lured people for some purpose, like someone luring ghosts for exorcism.

Pop.

The cheerful sound of opening a bottle cap.
As the student tried to sprinkle the low-grade holy water blessed at the altar, the long-necked ghost reached out and grabbed his arm.
Splash.
The holy water spilled all over the ground.
After witnessing their captured friend wet himself, the other two friends ran in different directions.
So that at least one of them could escape.


* * *


Everyone sometimes has such fantasies.
Wanting to see someone better than themselves beg for help.
Mob wasn't immune to such human feelings of jealousy and envy.

"Mob, please help us find Booby! We're classmates!"

But in his imagination, when his peers clung to him, they would plead to see his assignments, not approach him with sunken eyes, desperately requesting help in searching for a missing person.

"Wait, what are you suddenly talking about? Find him? Did he hide somewhere in the third-year area because he didn't want to attend lectures?"

The Oknodie Play Organization, with many scholarship students from the Foundation, naturally had information about upperclassmen that trickled down.
Even information like hands suddenly protruding from walls belonging to third-years wasn't particularly surprising within Oknodie's organization.

"It's the Hook Ghost. A ghost with a neck bent like a hook spilled the holy water and took Booby away!"
"What did you guys do?"
"We ran away. We knew right away. That was absolutely not a low-level ghost. It's something that upperclassmen should deal with, but for some reason it's loose in our first-year area."

So a fairly strong ghost had appeared.
But that didn't resolve all of Mob's questions.

"Why are you asking me to search for the missing person? I'm not a professor or instructor, just a fellow classmate."
"Mob, you're strong."

Strong? Me?
No matter how many times he experienced such honest evaluation from others, he couldn't get used to it.
Is that so?
Am I strong?
While he had diligently followed Oknodie's training, when he thought about the gap between himself and Oknodie, his own strength didn't seem very convincing.

"And the professors and instructors said they couldn't help because more urgent matters came up."
"Did the chairman of the Wiheomhae Foundation invade with troops or something?"
"Not that, but apparently Dragon Principal opened too many gates to the sound dimension, which accidentally opened the gates of hell too, so they're busy closing spatial rifts."
"..."

What a complete mess.

"The student council took over security activities, but there's an even bigger commotion in the upperclassmen's area, so they don't have the capacity to help us first-years."

So Booby, who was captured by the Hook Ghost, had been abandoned?

"Wait. Let me think about whether this is possible or not."

Mob immediately went to find Gisele.

"Mr. Gisele."
"What brings you here, Black Knight?"
"Do you happen to have information about the Hook Ghost in the Dark Association?"
"Yes, I do. We've received several eyewitness accounts and related information from within the academy."

It was a good decision to come to Gisele.
While his information might not be as suspiciously accurate as Oknodie's, Gisele's information was the next most reliable.

"If you're planning to exorcise the Hook Ghost, I suggest you reconsider. It's quite dangerous."
"Someone from my class who I know by face has been captured."
"You could just pretend you didn't see anything. You really have a personality that seeks out trouble."
"If Oknodie were kidnapped, I couldn't just stand by and do nothing. Such dangerous ghosts should be dealt with when possible."
"Haha. Our little lady? Kidnapped? Unless it's a kidnapper luring her with food, how could a ghost possibly kidnap Oknodie?"

Even if there were a ghost capable of that, they should immediately issue an evacuation order for the academy and flee the island through a dimensional gate.
After all, if Oknodie could be defeated by such a ghost, there's no way they could overcome it themselves.

"But I do like your kind heart. I'll give you this information free of charge, just this once."
"Thank you."
"Save your thanks. After seeing the confirmed information, you might not feel so grateful."

Gisele's kind face with its thin smile flashed sharply as he turned the mana panel.

"This is... quite something."

'I need Oknodie's help!'
