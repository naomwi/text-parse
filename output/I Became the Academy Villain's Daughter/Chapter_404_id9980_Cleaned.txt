# 404 - A Shiver of Strength

The second-year students who took the "Let's Try Growing Medicinal Herbs" lecture and Mandela Castella.
The third-year Production Department students who had to take responsibility for having their summons "stolen" and losing the baby Kraken from the isolation room.
Their challenge was how to exact meaningful revenge on Oknodie while avoiding the eyes of the professor and instructors in charge of the "Naval Combat ①" lecture.

"If an Academy professor sets their mind to it, people like us would be swept away in an instant. So we absolutely cannot commit serious crimes like murder."
"Then what's the point of all these people coming out for revenge, senior!"
"That's why we start by building 'justification.' We'll create an herb field for hydroponics in the path of their ship and release guard summons to be trampled by the ship."

If that happened, they would be the ones whose lecture was disrupted first.

"Even if direct attacks against Oknodie herself are impossible, retaliation against the 'ship' that disrupted the lecture would become a sufficiently acceptable situation."
"Ohhh!"
"Once we have justification, any interference in student affairs becomes even more dangerous for Professor El Draco. As someone who was forcibly brought to the Academy from his background as a great pirate to serve as a professor, that's a risk El Draco wouldn't want to take."

The Production Department senior turned to look at Mandela Castella with sharp eyes.

"This must be the plan you came up with, right?"
"I have no idea what you're talking about! As second-years lacking in knowledge, we simply follow the will of our third-year seniors!"

The third-year snorted at Mandela Castella's act of playing innocent while innocently fluttering her blonde twin-tails.
It seemed suspicious from the start that she helped build justification—apparently planning to shift the risk of conspiracy to the third-years in case something went wrong.
Sarpedon, the head assistant of the "Marine Biology" laboratory, didn't bother making an issue of it.

'The content of yesterday's conspiracy has already been recorded in the magical device. It's too late to back out now.'

There's no use trying to speak with two tongues in front of Production Department upperclassmen.
The recording magical device that prevents clients from playing innocent is an artifact passed down through generations in the Production Department.

"The moment the herb field is swept away, we attack the ship. Of course, our real target isn't sinking the ship but striking at Oknodie. We'll focus our attack range around Oknodie."

The plan was perfect.
Even the variables worked in their favor.

The Production Department third-years rejoiced greatly at this windfall that required no effort on their part.

"Focus more on summoning magic. While the second-years buy time, we'll perform the summoning ritual here to guide a powerful summon into an 'uncontrollable' state, causing an unavoidable accident!"

"It's too early to celebrate. If the summoning ritual fails, we're neither here nor there. Begin the ritual now!"

"Should we abandon ship and flee?"

Nancy and Brodo, executives of the Jigoku Pirate Crew who had followed Jigoku aboard only to be defeated by Oknodie.
As always in times of crisis, the two voiced the Jigoku Pirate Crew's policy of lightly throwing away pride and fleeing.
You have to survive to do anything!

"The captain would value safely escaping alive rather than dying defending the ship."
"That's right. Can't give up on the survival bonus!"

Jigoku readily admitted that backing out was the wise choice.

"Take the crew members and escape in the lifeboats."

"If you die, I'll name my first child Jigoku in honor of today's sacrifice!"

The executives left without even pretending to protest, coolly departing first!
Despite their cold response, Jigoku couldn't help but smile.
People with such straightforward personalities who aren't bound by pride or conscience are the ones who survive longer.

"There he is, a first-year!"
"Hey, move aside. We're not interested in you."
"Then who are you interested in?"

Despite Jigoku's brief words, the seniors showed no discomfort.
After all, grade level is determined by enrollment year, not age.
What matters is individual strength.
Jigoku was a member of the first-year advanced class, clearly stronger than the average second-year lower class.
Rather than risk the danger of treating such a person with contempt and hostility, the second-years answered his question straightforwardly.

"Oknodie."
"We're not trying to harm her personally. Just going to make her suffer a bit."

Mandela Castella.
