# 290 - The Little Rat That Snuck In

Zuang had been moving around the cruise ship while concealing her presence, deep in thought.
Just operating this cruise ship alone required enough money to cost the head of an Academy professor.
What was the Foundation thinking, making such an enormous expenditure?

In a world where money equals power.
The Foundation isn't an organization that enjoys luxury and waste.

'The Wiheomhae Foundation's motto is extreme efficiency.'

All unnecessary impurities and fallen dropouts face the fate of disposal through harsh "directives."
Only those who overcome difficult directives and prove their worth again can maintain their positions.
Interpreting it this way, this cruise ship journey clearly has something at stake equivalent to a professor's head.

'The point auction. Was their scheme to gauge Hero Ishtar's preferences and discernment at the auction, as she's destined to become a continental powerhouse?'

At first, that's all I thought it was.
After all, the only figures invited on this ship with massive investment value were the Hero and the Empire's Second Princess.
Only after the fifth day's auction ended did Zuang realize.
That had been her misconception.
The real powerhouse was someone else.
Oknodie.
The Foundation's top scholarship student.
A prodigy with the finest talent the organization had acquired, comparable even to the world's Hero.

"Would you all like to come over to my house?"
"If you don't want to, that's fine! But Papa has prepared gifts for my friends, which would be a shame to miss!"
"I don't know what they are, but I heard they're very special gifts! He said everyone would be delighted. He also said you'd greatly regret not receiving them in time!"

When persuading Dorothy, Oknodie had said:
Papa had prepared gifts.
She'd been asked to invite her friends.

That "request" was essentially no different from an order.
Who would consider it merely a request coming from a powerful figure who controls countless scholarship students across the continent through directives?

"Do you know how they raise elephants in the circus?"

Once, when Professor Sadako was teaching how to escape without falling when being chased by ghosts at night, she told a story to Zuang, who was exhausted and completely drained.

"Elephants sold to the circus from a very young age grow up tied to a stake. The young elephant cannot pull out the stake no matter how hard it tries, and eventually gives up and submits in desperation."
"I cannot pull out the stake. If I cause trouble, I'll be tormented with a red-hot poker. I don't want to experience pain without any results."
"That's how elephants remain tied to stakes even after growing to full size with sufficient strength. Without realizing their true power, living in fear of pain."

Titosoga asked with a bright face:

"What does that have to do with us?"
"The spirit chasing you now is the vengeful ghost of an elephant that lived tied to a circus stake and died crushed under a burning tent. If it catches you, you'll be completely flattened."
"Waaah!"

"..."

Regardless of Titosoga's foolish crying, Professor Sadako's story became a lesson.
Even elephants, which can easily kill humans by stepping on them with their full weight, can have their aggression neutered if raised by trainers from a young age.
Oknodie is experiencing the same thing.
She's being gaslighted by the Foundation.
Like an elephant tied to a stake.
Like an elephant that experiences pain when it resists.
Growing up bound to the Foundation, she probably harbors somewhere in her heart the fear of being sold to a brothel.
For someone like her, refusing Papa's "request" is naturally impossible.

The friends she brought along.
The Princess seemed to think the rest were just baggage and the real purpose was to draw out the Hero, but Zuang thought the exact opposite.
The Hero came along as a bonus, and the real targets were those close to Oknodie.
At least, that's what Zuang felt was the aim of the Foundation's chairman, Oknodie's Papa.

'Could threatening to sell her to a brothel really serve as a stake for Oknodie?'

That's impossible.
That child is strong.
Even if she truly believed those words, she clearly recognizes how useful she is, how strong she is.
She wouldn't have even the slightest concern that such a future of being disposed of as useless would befall her.
Then what is Oknodie's stake?
A stake is a person's fear.
It's a shackle that seals away even enormous power like an elephant's.
What is she bound by, what makes her hold back her power?
Zuang already knew the answer.

'Friends.'

Yes.
If the Foundation knows this fact too, then this is a place to firmly drive in a new stake.
It's a threat that Papa of the Foundation is imposing on Oknodie.
The friends she's worked hard to make over the past half year.
The Foundation already possesses all their weaknesses.
They can reveal those weaknesses to anyone.
They can completely upend a person's relationships at any desired timing.

"Oknodie."
"Yes?"
"Am I in the way too?"

I don't want to be an obstacle.
So tell me.
That you need my help.

"Hmm. Zuang is strong, but..."
"..."
"I wonder~?"
"..."
"Maybe you can, maybe you can't?"

Is she teasing me now?
When I glared at her with intensity, Oknodie flinched and quickly answered.

"Just stay and keep watching!"

I did it.
I've been acknowledged.
Zuang felt genuine happiness.
An assassin shouldn't have such expressive emotions.
You'll die if you can't conceal yourself.
But she couldn't hold back the smile that leaked out.

'I've stayed on the deserted island longer than Hestia, Son O-cheon, or even Gisele.'

Could I interpret this to mean that among the people Oknodie trusts and follows, I'm the most reliable?
That's definitely what it means.
Even if it's not, I'll interpret it that way.
Zuang's shoulders tensed with pride!
But there was something concerning about Oknodie's statement.

"Not 'protect me' but 'keep watching'...?"
"Yes. Keep observing!"

It's true that I've been chosen as a trustworthy companion.
But the nuance is different.
Not a comrade standing shoulder to shoulder, but a comrade in the sense of being permitted to watch her actions.

"Just hang in there as long as you can, then leave!"

She even thinks I'll drop out on my own at some point.
It means that while I'm better than the other companions, I'm still not enough to stand beside her.
My pride was hurt.
I'm the successor who inherited the only single-person transmission secret technique of the Life Thief, one of the continent's infamous Ten Great Thieves.
I can deceive not only the Hero's detection but also professor-class detection, move around in hiding, and even deceive that Jonna Wiheomhae's eyes, yet that's still not enough?
I know Oknodie is strong.
But isn't this too harsh?
I can't accept this.
Stubbornness.
Or what might be called defiance rose up in my heart.

'Hmph. I'll really just watch until you ask for help first.'

As Zuang slipped into the shadows, determined to extract an apology from Oknodie...

"Squeak!"
"...?"

Zuang noticed a little rat gnawing on a rock near the shadows.

[Blue Metal Rat]
[It constantly gnaws on hard substances and mana-infused materials to increase the purity and efficacy of the rare metals that make up its body.]

It was a rare monster she'd learned about from the monster encyclopedia in the Academy's magical beast studies lecture.
Finding one is like getting a free rare metal, which feels like a lucky find, but if you find two, you should be tense, and if you find three, you should run away.
The reason was simple.
Mana-infused materials.
The Blue Metal Rat's teeth target prey indiscriminately, whether living beings or objects.

*Stab*

Ignoring the resistance at the tip of her dagger, she quickly killed it, but cracks appeared from under the rock.

"Squeak?"
"Squeak squeak?"
"Squeak squeak squeak squeak squeak!"

Not just three, but dozens.
Sensing countless Blue Metal Rats from the island's floor, Zuang realized the true meaning of Oknodie's words about watching only as long as she could endure the auction.
This auction venue.
The longer time passes, the more dangerous it becomes to hold out on the deserted island.

"Hey, bookworm. Don't you feel disappointed about going back first?"
"I purchased the items that came up for me and escaped, so I have no reason to be disappointed."

Gisele spoke calmly in front of Son O-cheon, but he realized how lucky he'd been when he saw the scene reflected in the <Peeping Doll> he'd left on the island.
Blue Metal Rats.
If he had spent even one night on that island where such vicious predators were hiding, and they had suddenly emerged from beneath his feet, it would have been difficult to preserve his life.

'They detected mana and came out.'

The doll, scurrying around the island with its tiny doll feet, realized that the rats were gathering at the cave where combat had occurred between participants, and at the terrain where Rogini's embers remained.
If rats that escaped to the surface like that start following people's mana, it won't be long before combat between humans and Blue Metal Rats begins.
They might win one fight.
But if more traces of mana remain from that combat, and more rats crawl up from beneath the island?
It might become a situation where they're chased by so many rats that they'd beg to be sent back to the cruise ship.

'Perhaps our little lady sent us away first out of consideration for our weak combat power.'

While he appreciated the thoughtfulness, Gisele had his own concerns.
Oknodie had already engaged in combat with Princess Mesugakki.
This meant she had been selected as a "tracking target" by the rats.
At this rate, Oknodie and the Princess would naturally be chased by more rats than the Hero, tiring more quickly.
Without a clever solution, the next ones to cross over to the cruise ship on the lifeboat might be the Princess's team and Oknodie's team.

'Let's have faith. Our little lady can hold out sufficiently. We should do our best where we are.'

Using a merchant's eye and calculation, Gisele thoroughly searched the food storage with Son O-cheon to calculate the remaining food, checking the cargo list and quantities.
When they opened the food container, they found the Jigoku Pirate Gang sitting around a lantern playing poker.

"...It seems the little rats aren't only on the deserted island."
