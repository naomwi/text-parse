# 198 - How to Break Through the Professor's Trap

Gisele felt anxious.

'My thinking was too shallow.'

He had planned to mobilize outside connections to dig into the foundation's darkness for Oknodie's sake. But while designing the future, an unexpected obstacle had appeared right before his eyes.

"Gisele, don't fall behind. We need to pick up the pace here, even if it's pushing ourselves too hard!"
"I... know... Isabel!"

Gisele's stamina ranked at the bottom even among the advanced class students. To put it bluntly, he was dead last. Though he was naturally slow, he had a backup plan. Even if he came in last for this lecture, he could make up for it by earning enough credits in other classes!

But a variable had emerged.
The massive participation of second-year students.
Unknown penalties for having their name tags taken.
He might even lose additional credits.
For him, this was a painful mistake.

<Brooch of Composure>
<Breathing Correction Underwear>

The smuggled artifacts that should have lasted until the middle of the triathlon's first event, running, had already begun to activate one by one, flickering with light. As his confused mind calmed and his breathing eased, his heart paradoxically grew heavier.

"Isabel. Please go ahead."
"Son O-cheon already ran off on his own, but if I leave too, what will happen to you, Gisele?"
"At this rate, we'll just be doing the second-years a favor. Or would you prefer we both receive penalties together?"
"Ugh... that's cowardly. Making it impossible to help a comrade."
"Please complete the course for my share too. Helping me means not carrying the burden of knowing someone capable failed because of me."

Inside Isabel, the potential loss from staying with Gisele and her sense of loyalty were being weighed against each other. To her, who had been part of an adventure party since childhood following adults around, comrades were everything—a sanctuary to rely on.

"...Sorry."
"It's alright."

Isabel couldn't meet his eyes and looked at the ground as she put strength into her legs. Just as she accelerated to increase her speed, someone approached her side, kicking up dust.
She hadn't heard it.
She hadn't noticed.
Despite coming this close.
Even though they were running on two feet.
A presence that approached without making a sound, as if existing in a completely different space!

'A second-year!?'

When she raised her head in terror as if seeing a ghost, she found Oknodie looking up at her, blinking.

"Oknodie! Why are you still here? You should be competing with the leading group!"
"If I just left, Dorothy might be fine, but you and Gisele would definitely fail."

Isabel felt ashamed. This admirable child, despite having the skill to compete for first place, had come all the way back to the rear of the advanced class for her comrades, while she herself had decided to abandon Gisele.
But that was reality.
If they didn't give up, they would all fail together.
She could feel it even now.
The presence of second-year students pursuing them from behind.
If she relaxed her legs and slowed her pace even slightly, being caught was inevitable.
Those armed with determination far beyond first-years would mercilessly take their name tags and force them to fall behind to the back of the group.

"Ah, watch out there."
"A tree monster!?"

A branch swaying in the wind suddenly came down, blocking their path. After twisting her body in terror to avoid it, her field of vision widened, and she saw something.
Wriggle.
The knotholes in the nearby tree trunks were moving like human eyes!
Unlike during practice, the actual midterm exam had added elements that interfered with the race.

"If there are such obstacles, then the second-years..."
"Second and third-years already know about them. Only first-years are unaware of the obstacles!"
"...I won't ask where you got that information. Is there a way to prevent Gisele from failing?"
"Of course there is! That's why I came to help too."

Gisele recalled one of the rumors about Oknodie.
Mob, an aspiring knight from the lower class.
The story of a student nicknamed "Black Knight Mob" who never took off his armor.
His effort was admirable, but he had become a burden. Gisele didn't want to follow Mob's example.

"Little lady. If this is just an excuse to comfort me, don't bother."

To Gisele's prideful response, Oknodie simply bent her stubbornness.

"Should I carry you on my shoulders?"
"...I'll follow on my own feet."


* * *


Hestia felt annoyed.
She broke and smashed every branch blocking her way, so the tree monsters weren't what bothered her.
What troubled the frontrunner was, surprisingly, the second-year seniors who should have been behind them.

"We were definitely running at the front. How do these seniors keep appearing ahead of us?"

With her abundant stamina forged on the battlefield, she thought she would never be caught by the second-years behind them.
But after running for about 20 minutes, the situation began to change.
The students who had been in the lead were now harassed by seniors who suddenly appeared in front of them, jumped down from trees to pounce on them, or ambushed them from behind rocks.

"Gyahaha! The reason doesn't matter. There are just more targets to shoot at!"

Bang! Bang!
Jigoku pulled the trigger of her revolver with excitement.
Since forming a team in Dragon Principal's lecture, Jigoku had boldly cleared the way for them.
Hestia likewise sent seniors flying far away by punching or hammering them whenever they charged at her.
But there were too many.
Moreover, unlike first-years who would faint with a squeak when knocked down, the second-years were more persistent.

"Barrier activate!"
"Muscle Memory Falling Technique activate!"

The second-years used all sorts of defensive measures to prevent immediate retirement and continue the race.
Faced with such tenacity, Lotto, who had initially displayed the agility unique to martial artists, had long since become tearful after having her stamina completely drained.

"Eek! Please don't leave me behind!"
"Huh? Why should I care about a weakling's circumstances?"
"Jigoku. Don't be like that. This isn't the time for first-years to compete against each other."
"Tch. How boring."
"Bear with it, if only for Oknodie's sake."
"What does she have to do with this?"

Jigoku retorted indifferently.
Then she glanced around and asked quietly.

"Would Oknodie dislike it if we abandoned Lotto?"
"She probably wouldn't dislike it. That child tends to be rather merciless toward the weak."
"What? Then it doesn't matter."
"Still, she wouldn't like it either. She might learn from watching you. Do you want to make Oknodie become the bad child everyone arbitrarily talks about?"
"Of course not. I'll never do anything good for those who gossip like rats behind her back."

The three continued running through the seemingly endless forest, matching Lotto's pace despite Jigoku's reluctance.
Lotto, whom they had considered just a burden, presented an intriguing hypothesis to Jigoku and Hestia.

"I think we're going around in circles."
"What? That's impossible. We've memorized the course through physical practice many times."
"Wouldn't it be more convincing if repeatedly practicing the same course was the trap?"

In a normal race, this would be nonsense.
But at Gift Academy?
It was plausible!

"There they are! Three first-years!"
"Attack!"
"All-out assault!!"

As they ran off the path, occasionally encountering tree monsters and second-years, the three-person race through the forest began to change.
Several second-years suddenly emerged from nearby as if they had been waiting.

"We won, you brats!"

Jigoku kicked the fallen second-year's buttocks and burst into laughter.
As they continued through the forest, this time a dozen or so second-years appeared before them.

"I'm starting to run low on bullets."

Jigoku's laughter ceased, and she began to look disgruntled.
As they pressed on through the forest, this time twenty or thirty second-years appeared in a large group.

"This looks fucked."

Even Jigoku, who was happy just pulling the trigger, couldn't bring herself to do so given the alarming number of second-years lined up ahead.
And there was no guarantee that would be the end of it.

"...Can you climb a tree and scout for a moment?"

At Hestia's request, Lotto climbed a tree, grunting and moving her limbs with effort.
Her stockings covering sturdy thighs caught on the bark, tearing and developing holes, but Lotto didn't care about her stockings anymore—her stamina was completely drained from keeping up with the top runners' pace and engaging in successive battles.
After climbing to a high place and looking around, her impression was even more dismal.

"Oh my goodness."

A circular forest path.
In each sparsely scattered clearing in the center, second-years were waiting in ambush for first-years.
While the first-years were being lured into wandering the circular track, the second-years had quickly seized the exit routes.
Absurdly, the way out of the forest was an aerial staircase leading from the massive tree in the center of the forest to the river on the other side of the ground. Her heart sank at the thought of how to break through to get there.

"How is it?"
"It's structured like this."

After climbing down, Lotto used twigs to draw the forest structure and second-year ambush positions for Hestia and Jigoku to see.
There was indeed a reason why the 12-hour long competition had such an extended timeframe.
Hestia suddenly remembered.
Oknodie, who was weaker than herself in strength, inferior to Skola in archery, and always in that questionable second-place position in many areas.
The secret to her rising to joint first in the year wasn't outstanding combat ability but outstanding wisdom.

-Some of you might be stronger, have better techniques, or superior combat ability than those who passed. All of those are outside this examiner's interest.
-The wise advance before the strong, and if wisdom is lacking, one must have combat ability surpassing all others to advance. A score of 100 points is the proof of this.

The second gateway examiner Minerva had said it during the entrance exam.
Wisdom is more important than strength.
They had relied solely on stamina and ran blindly, but stamina without wisdom had actually made them run around the forest faster and farther than anyone else.
As a result, they failed to read the second-years' movements.
By the time they assessed the situation, they who thought they were in the lead had fallen behind.

"Is Oknodie trapped somewhere in the forest too?"
"I don't think so."

In the entrance exam, Oknodie had been the wisest among the participants from Group A's borderlands.
Hestia's thoughts were soon proven by an astonishing sight from across the forest.

"Huh? W-wait a minute. The forest is moving."
"What nonsense are you talking about? Do we look like we're in the mood for jokes?"
"I'm serious!"

While Lotto was scouting from the tree again with nothing else to do, Hestia quickly climbed to the treetop with her excellent jumping skills, and Jigoku with her sailor's sense from climbing ship masts, both much faster than Lotto.
Seeing this, Lotto felt dizzy with indignation, wondering what all her struggling to climb had been for, but the two had already shifted their attention elsewhere.

"Those are tree monsters. Why are they moving?"
"Look over there. Someone's leading the tree monsters, smashing through the second-years and clearing a path."
"Huh? That's..."

Hestia, familiar with battlefield dynamics, identified the most important vanguard, and Jigoku, who had learned vision enhancement magic during her pirate days, identified who it was.

"It's Oknodie."
