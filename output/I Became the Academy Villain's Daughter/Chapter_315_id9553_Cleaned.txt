# 315 - Shortcut

"Don't you want to see your reward?"

Xing picked up the scabbard.
Unlike Titoso, he was so quiet during lectures that people often forgot, but Xing was also a student in Professor Bronze's eye-training class.
He experimented with changes by sheathing his sword and swinging it, then drawing it and swinging again.
And he detected a meaningful difference.

━━━
<Scabbard of Silence>
Grade - Rare 6
Description - Reward obtained for staying on the 39th floor of the Chairman's Mansion for over 60 minutes and breaking through the floor. This scabbard possesses a special power.
Effect 1 - The power of the next strike increases proportionally to how long the sword has remained in the scabbard.
Effect 2 - The speed of the next strike increases proportionally to how long the sword has been sheathed without using magic.
Appraisal Value - 800 gold coins, 80,000 points
━━━

The item's effect, which he had appraised by instinct, was beyond satisfactory—it was remarkably impressive.
But it was also an item with clear limitations.

'If charged beyond a certain level, the scabbard would be destroyed by the power released when drawing the sword.'

If used without greed, it could serve as a supplementary item to enhance strikes multiple times.
However, with a completely different approach, one could raise a sword's power over a very long period and use it as a one-time killing blade.
On the other hand, a mediocre sword might collapse under the power enhancement that accompanies its drawing.

'What a useless yet precious scabbard.'

To demonstrate its true value, an extraordinary sword is needed.
Sealing such a magnificent sword just to use it well once would lead to a continuous weakening of combat power.
The conditions for bringing out this scabbard's true value are extremely demanding.

One must have the mental fortitude to resist drawing the sword over a very long period.
One must have a powerful enemy or goal worth such lengthy patience.
One must possess other spare swords for dealing with minor threats and daily use.

It's a scabbard unnecessary for someone living an ordinary life.
For such a person, sealing their main weapon would mean accepting considerable disadvantage.
If someone could survive for years with their main weapon sealed and still reach their sworn enemy, they might already be skilled enough not to need the scabbard at all.

'It's a troublesome scabbard that either puts you in danger through greed, or makes itself unnecessary through growth that matches your ambition.'

Though not exactly a cursed item, it's worse because it makes you willingly seal your main weapon.
It was quite a fitting scabbard to be offered as a reward by the notorious Foundation.

"Thank you. You saved my life."
"Hehe. What did I do? I just said a few words. It wasn't me who worried about you, so you should thank fake Lin!"
"..."

Behind Oknodie was the expressionless fake Lin looking up at Xing.
He didn't know what to feel toward this ghost imitating his little sister.

"Thank you."

The ghost quickly fled into Oknodie's ring.
Oknodie's pointed hat giggled and said:

"I scared it."

This is difficult.
Little sisters, whether past or present.
Xing was about to move to the next floor when his feet stopped before taking a step.

-Brother. I'm content just being by your side.

Xing looked down at the scabbard in his hand.
For some reason, he didn't feel like swinging his sword anymore today.

"Do you have any special plans later?"
"Just playing, eating, and sleeping!"
"I'll help you."

Though he was falling behind Irene, the hero, Zuang, and others who had already gone up, he no longer felt impatience or inferiority.
At least for now, he felt that not advancing was the right answer.

I take it back.
Following Oknodie was a very stupid thing to do.

"What do you think, Xing? Hidden routes are the tastiest way to progress, right?"
"Didn't you say we'd play, eat, and sleep?"
"We're playing right now!"

Oknodie's idea of play varies wildly.
Sometimes she engages in gentle activities like hide-and-seek or building sand castles, but occasionally she does things that make you wonder if they can even be called play.

━━━
Chairman's Mansion
B1F - Floor of Waste
━━━

A floor where broken and destroyed materials are discarded.
They're playing in a garbage dump where all sorts of junk is piled haphazardly.
Hygiene aside, the danger level was no joke.
Gik. Gigik.
A wooden doll with twisted joints crawls along the floor, flipping its limbs.
Whoong. Whoong.
A trap hanging from part of the ceiling spins freely through the air.
Among all kinds of waste from various floors, Oknodie fearlessly rummaged through the junk, knocking down anything that came at her.

"Hey. You're getting dirt on your sleeve."
"It's fine! I have butlers and maids!"
"So your premise is that it doesn't matter if you get dirty."
"Swords exist to draw blood, right? Clothes exist to get dirty!"
"I'd like your butler and maid to hear that confident assertion."

So what business does she have in this garbage dump?
In this dirty, dangerous, and suspicious place.
It's not a good place for a child to play out of curiosity.
There's a crucial difference between this place and the Academy's various facilities.
Facilities designed to enhance growth through dangerous environments always have <Instructors> standing by.
Ready to intervene at any time if necessary.
This place is different.
From the moment the butler and maid descended underground, all watchful eyes were completely cut off.
Not because it's a dirty, lowly facility, but because it's dangerous enough to be restricted.

"Have you ever thought about it, Xing? Why the Tower of Training exists?"
"To test and evaluate the skills of intruders, I suppose."
"For what purpose?"
"To cultivate skilled individuals to the Foundation's taste."
"This place wasn't originally a facility controlled by the Foundation!"

It was a thought-provoking story.
The tower wasn't created by the Foundation.
The tower existed first, and the Foundation became involved later.
This place isn't really a mansion after all.
The Chairman and butlers act so brazenly and confidently that it seemed people somehow managed to live in such a place, but it was all deception.

"A training facility that existed before the Foundation took control, in such a remote location... It wasn't created by someone or some organization in recent times, at least."

No country or organization has enough resources to exert influence in such a remote place.
We're in a Cold War era now.
Peace is maintained for the moment, but no one knows when that peace might end.

"This place exists for heroes to grow!"
"What?"

He had thought it was a relic of a past era.
But going that far back in time?
As he stood there in shock, Oknodie showed him some junk.

"Look carefully. There's a mana stone embedded here, right?"

A fragment of mana stone embedded in broken weaponry.
As time passed, the fragment gradually diminished until it dissolved and disappeared into thin air.

"It's mana dissolution. This Floor of Waste takes discarded waste from each floor, dissolves it, and converts it back into resources for reassembly!"
"You're fearless. With this level of dissolution power, even the mana in a person would gradually decrease."
"It's fine! I have lots of mana!"

It's not just about you being fine.
Well, I have plenty of mana too.
Xing gradually understood what she was trying to say.

"Mana dissolution. I've never heard it mentioned even at the Academy. Is this evidence of ancient technology from the hero-raising era?"
"Yep!"
"Let's say that's true. Then why did your 'Papa' occupy this facility? And what are you trying to gain in this dangerous underground?"
"Players only look for one thing. A shortcut to growth!"
"...?"
"Think about it. If the Foundation occupied the facility, they'd be conquering floors much higher than what students use, right?"
"I suppose so. Even that butler Jonna has strength comparable to professor-class."
"Then the test equipment on floors conquered by such strong individuals would be smashed too, right?"

This feels ominous.
The flow of this conversation gives off a very dangerous scent.
Xing tried to cut off the conversation, regretting that he had asked, but Oknodie puffed up her cheeks like a squirrel with a mouthful of acorns.
No choice then.
Accepting that he was already in too deep, Xing gave up as Oknodie continued with a bright smile.

"So, if we find high-floor waste among the discarded items on the Floor of Waste, an interesting trigger opens up."

A magical armor that looked hundreds of times more dangerous than an ordinary wooden doll.
Oknodie pulled out a full-body armor larger than herself that made metallic sounds when tapped.
She boldly led the way, carrying the armor.

"Now, let's go down!"
"What else is below this?"
"After collecting waste, you have to throw away what can't be used and recycle what can, right?"

A facility where various equipment operates along hundreds of massive lines.

"Come in too, Xing! There's plenty of room for both of us!"
"...That armor, if we get inside, don't tell me..."

Oknodie nodded with a bright smile.

"We'll be transported to the <Floor of Living Armor> on the 69th floor!"

They could go directly to the 69th floor of a tower where even regular butlers with instructor-level strength only reach the 50th floor.
