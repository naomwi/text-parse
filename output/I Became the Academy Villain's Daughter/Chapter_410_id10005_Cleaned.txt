# 410 - Hard to Chase

Irene had heard that the owner of the footprints wasn't Oknodie, but in a way, she had also heard that it was her.
It wasn't just them who had spent the semester staring at Oknodie's back.
She too had the desperate need to gain Oknodie's favor and make her an ally for the northern forces.

'Yet I couldn't tell the difference, while they could.'

Should she acknowledge and accept this gap in ability?
No, that was impossible.

"Even if it's not Oknodie, these are relatively recent tracks. The owner of these footprints might know Oknodie."

Dorothy showed interest in Irene's decision.

"Then we'll help you follow these tracks!"
"Wherever Dorothy goes, I go too."

Irene found it surprising that Dorothy and Rockfell offered to help her.

"You're not joining the others who are looking for Oknodie's traces? Honestly, when it comes to tracking skills, there's probably no one better than you among the first-years."
"Finding specific traces in a place affected by magical disasters is nearly impossible. It was the same in our forest! In situations like this, it's better to follow clear tracks."

...What kind of forest did Dorothy grow up in, exactly?
Irene deliberately ignored the suspicion that crossed her mind.

"But these tracks are incredibly technical, rivaling even Oknodie's skills!"

Dorothy gaped in amazement at traces showing someone had stepped on a tree and leaped 15 meters in a single bound.
This wasn't the only extraordinary movement.
The mud marks on the bent branch proved that the owner of the footprints had momentarily used mana cultivation techniques to perfectly distribute their weight on a single branch without breaking it, and even reinforced their "foothold" so it wouldn't break when they pushed off with force.

Perfect control over their body.
Free adjustment of balance and contact points.
Ability to extend that influence to surrounding objects.

Mana sword.
Sword energy merchant.

This was evidence of training and control that had reached such extremes that the internal mana and energy not only overflowed but could cause damage to the surroundings.

"Wow. How is this not Oknodie?"

Dorothy, who was quickly scanning the landing point of someone who had jumped from a tree, continued to express amazement mixed with bewilderment.

"Where did they land?"
"Over there. On top of another tree 50 meters away."
"!!"

The landing spot wasn't at a lower position from the branch they jumped from.
The destination was even "higher."
Normally, gravity would cause loss of force, and even with energy wrapped around the body to increase speed, the energy would be consumed, weakening protective power and lowering height.
The owner of these footprints directly violated these laws of physics, mana studies, and energy principles.
With overwhelming control, they suffered only minimal losses, defying gravity to fly this far and land on a higher tree.
It was so incredible that one might believe a magic missile had been fired rather than a person jumping.

"They reclaimed the energy expelled outward back into themselves to eliminate loss. Even in the northern military, there were few experts with such skill."

Irene recognized it.
This wasn't the level of a first-year student.
It was top-tier talent difficult to find even among the advanced first-year class.
At least in the field of physical enhancement, this was the skill of an incredible master that would be hard to match.

"We're still far from catching up. Their speed is amazing, so the travel distance will be just as long. Both of you, prepare yourselves to follow!"

Though Dorothy couldn't leap 50 meters freely from tree to tree like their target, she was just as nimble in her own way, tracking in two or three smaller jumps.
The pursuit continued without interruption.
Dorothy and Rockfell, from the most dangerous of the Three Great Forests, the Forest of Dimensional Rift Monsters, had mastered physical enhancement techniques for survival.
Irene, who had served in the military, had also mastered physical enhancement techniques to survive in the harsh icy soil of the north.
Though unintended, they had formed the optimal pursuit team of the most agile individuals!

"Huh? There's... nowhere to step here??"

After breaking through a pile of sandy dust, they faced a wide-open cliff.
Irene expressed doubt as they confronted a huge gap over 100 meters across.

"Did we lose the trail?"
"No. I've been tracking with fake steps and backtracking in mind, so there's no way I was fooled."
"Are there traces on the other side of the cliff?"
"Ugh. I can't see any."
"...You don't think they fell to their death?"

Honestly, that seemed more likely.
It wasn't an impossible gap to cross with a few tricks rather than a single leap, but...

Whoooosh!
Rumble rumble.

Air and mana being pulled down toward the bottom of the cliff.
Performing tricks while withstanding the suction of such tremendous magical winds was a different level of difficulty.
When magical winds blow through, the natural mana in the area becomes extremely thin.
With fewer mana puzzles available for magical formulas, the difficulty of casting spells increases dramatically.
To cast magic in such a place, a magician would need to carry many mana puzzles and have high mana purity to produce great power with small puzzles.
Otherwise, the spell would be canceled during casting or swept away by the mana winds.
Body enhancement, though less difficult than spellcasting, faced similar challenges for the same reasons.
Moreover, Dorothy could see the meaning of the traces left on this cliff.

━━━
[Heaven-Reaching Cliff]
A massive cliff with a 100-meter gap.
The magical winds blowing below the cliff look like the sword mark of a giant swung with tremendous will.
━━━

This was a sword mark.
A strike so powerful that its trace remained in the world even after many years.
Crossing through the trace of such a master's strike was absolutely not an easy feat.

"Dorothy. Is your intuition really pointing to the other side?"
"Yes. I'm certain!"
"Then Dorothy must be right."

Irene showed curiosity.

"You trust her intuition quite a bit for it to be just a hunch."
"Dorothy's intuition doesn't miss. If it did, she would have become monster food long ago."

The human brain warns of unrecognized dangers based on information perceived unconsciously.
In this respect, Dorothy's highly developed senses were more precise than others'.
Even without visible evidence, her brain had calculated that their target had crossed this place.

"Then I'll make a foothold."

Irene, with her unparalleled purity in ice attributes, formed a large ice bridge connecting the cliffs.

Whoooosh!
Rumble rumble.

Despite the violent winds immediately causing rupturing sounds throughout the bridge... it held.
If it didn't disappear instantly, they could run across and jump a much shorter distance than 100 meters.

"This is my turn. Hold tight."

Swift Rockfell.
The long-haired man who always followed Dorothy.
Irene had never seen his true abilities.
No, nobody had seen his true abilities.
Nobody had ever seen him exert his full strength.
Today, Irene finally witnessed his deliberately hidden capabilities.

'He's fast!'

It wasn't just his stepping speed that was fast.
Even while sprinting across the bridge that was collapsing from the magical winds, he distributed his energy so precisely that he didn't accelerate the bridge's collapse.
Near-perfect control.
Moreover, the natural mana left behind where he ran contained a clear "will."

-Quickly. Even faster.
-A speed no one can match.

There are stories that fragments of heroes' selves imbued in weapons used by great heroes speak as if they were living humans.
Though not at the level of communication, he momentarily inscribed his will into the atmosphere.
Witnessing this amazing talent, Irene realized:

'This man's peak might... even surpass mine.'

A survivor of the Monster Forest.
Was this the extent of his hidden strength?

"My guidance ends here. Jump."

The remaining distance to the other side of the cliff was half, 50 meters.
Still harsh.
It was a distance they couldn't cover in one jump from the trees, requiring two or three leaps.

<Mana Cultivation Technique>
<Hard Bullet>

Revealing his reserved strength, Rockfell shot straight like a bullet to reach the other side of the cliff.
He untied the rope at his waist and instantly infused it with energy.

Whoosh!

The rope extended straight like a ladder.
Dorothy stepped onto it and sighed in relief, wiping cold sweat.

"What about Irene?!"
"...Look behind."

<Flight Magic>

Dorothy's jaw dropped.

"That's not fair."
"I can't maintain it for long. Flight magic consumes too much energy."

The three safely reached the other side of the cliff.
Near the cliff were safety zones of various colors.
It was the perfect timing to recover depleted energy and regroup.
If only they could meet the entry restrictions set by previous users.

[Additional Entry Condition: Join the Heaven-Reaching Cliff Investigation Team]

"They're investigating the bottom of that cliff we just crossed...?"

Isn't that insane?
In response to Dorothy's spoken thought, an answer came from inside the safety zone.

"It is insane. If you don't have sufficient skill."
"!!"

Unlike the weak seniors who just stood guard at the entrance, this person had the same overwhelming presence as the powerful seniors they'd felt at the sports festival.
Someone with inexplicable capabilities who remained a student on leave despite possessing such power spoke to them.

"Chasing after the one who went ahead is even more insane. The next magical disaster is on a different 'level.' It might be better to climb down this cliff instead."
"Did you see the person who went ahead?"
"It was a girl with doll-like, elegant features."
"Doll-like... elegant?"
"There should only be one person among the first-years with such features."

Dorothy and Rockfell's faces hardened simultaneously.
Irene finally understood.
If that girl was the identity of the person whose traces were so difficult to follow, no wonder it was this challenging.

"Zuang."

The one they had been chasing was Oknodie's closest friend.
