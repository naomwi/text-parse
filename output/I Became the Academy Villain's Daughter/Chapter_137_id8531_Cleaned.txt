# Don't Be Fooled. I Know Better

The stupid hat eagerly revealed all the precious garbage, causing the classroom to erupt in chaos.

"What's wrong with everyone?"

"Is that so? I've heard that several deity candidates vying for the position of main god have promised apostle classes, and many legendary classes have opened up for those challenging great missions."
"I'm going to be a magic swordsman!"

Isabel said with a face that suggested she was dying of disappointment.

"Are you serious? Even explorers would switch classes without a second thought if they encountered one with such an ominous name in ruins."
"Did you dislike being an explorer, Isabel?"
"I wouldn't say I disliked it, it's just a job to make a living. Like how treasure hunters want to find treasures and make a big score, most explorers are people who want to find secret ancient legendary classes and strike it rich."
"Really?"
"Ancient. Legendary. Either keyword alone smells like a life-changing jackpot, and both appeared together."

Well, newbies love that kind of thing.
Like hidden classes or unique classes.
Those overpowered, one-of-a-kind, cheating professions.
I would have grabbed one too if there weren't any hidden traps.
The hidden risks are so great that as a veteran, I have zero desire to risk my soul on such dangerous classes.

"I like magic swordsman!"
"Sigh... what's so good about that magic swordsman? It's a class that's mediocre at both swordplay and magic."
"If you're good at both, it's doubly overpowered!"

Hit monsters with high physical resistance using magic, and those with high magic resistance using physical attacks.
And she's an adventure department student?
With this, there's no need for guides, trap disarmers, or one-trick thieves who just pick locks.
She could handle at least three roles by herself, or even more—a complete all-rounder character.

"Besides, why am I the only one being publicly executed while others don't have to continue with the examination?"

I looked around with a pout, inflating my cheeks with air to show my dissatisfaction, and Professor Bronze looked at me incredulously.

"I'm truly amazed by the boldness of this first-year Oknodie. You'll grow up to be quite a big shot."
"Of course! I'll be at least 2m 30cm tall!"
"...That's not the kind of big shot I meant, but I'll continue the examination for any remaining students who consent."

Students who wanted to participate raised their hands.
The classroom was nearly decimated except for Isabel, Gisele, and Tito Soga.
The only other notable student was Xing.

"Great figures attract brave heroes around them. Your courageous attitude is impressive."

Fortunately, the professor saw the attitude of those around me as courage rather than recklessness.

"Behave yourself."

The hat trembled at the professor's dagger that kept poking it as a warning.

"Administrative Department. You have the qualities to excel as a Dark Merchant class. With your cold-heartedness and ruthlessness, you'd also make a fine Military Merchant."

"Production Department. A born cook. With your skills, you could be a Dark Chef who could slip in a drop of poison without anyone noticing."

"Hahaha! What a coward. Weak-hearted, mediocre talent, and always conscious of others' opinions—you'd be perfect as a Maid. Why not take off that uniform and put on a maid outfit?"

The hat, which had been spouting whatever it pleased, finally shut up when the professor stabbed it with his knife after seeing Tito Soga whimpering with tears in her eyes.
The already cold atmosphere had grown even colder but lightened a bit thanks to Tito Soga who was just called a maid.

"Sigh. That hat must have done that on purpose, right? Dark Chef? Now I'll be remembered as someone suspicious who poisons food."
"Haha. In life, everything depends on how you look at it. From now on, there will be fewer people hanging around asking for Isabel's cooking, so that might be more comfortable."
"You certainly have an optimistic mindset, 'Dark Merchant.'"

Gisele, already a practicing dark merchant, took no damage.
Instead, he seemed to realize that the hat's insight was excellent enough to correctly identify his profession, which made him think the classes it assigned to me might actually be quite realistic possibilities.
Since then, his eyes have been crossing between strange expectation, worry, excitement, and enthusiasm when looking at me.

"Uncle Gisele. Just in case, if you suddenly show up with a legendary class advancement stone, I'll really get angry."
"Haha. Of course not. Such items are worth their weight in gold, and where would I get such merchandise? Unless I go looking for one with Isabel, there's no way to obtain it."

So he had thought about taking Isabel to look for one.
I'm truly glad I gave him a warning in advance.

"Hmph. Oknodie! He told me to be a maid. What if everyone starts calling me a maid and treating me like one from tomorrow?"
"Come tell me. I'll teach a lesson to any student who bullies Tito Soga!"

I tried to make a gesture of hitting her head with chopsticks, but I accidentally let go, and the chopstick spun around, shattering the ceiling light.
The broken light crashed to the classroom floor with a loud shatter, causing a round of screams.
Even amid the chaos, the chopstick, which was unnecessarily sturdy, bounced off the ceiling and walls with a ping-ping sound before finally stopping after vertically piercing through a student's desk.

"Oh! He called me a maid earlier! Oknodie, did you hear that? Are you getting revenge for me now?"

What's done is done.
I can't avoid losing points for property damage anyway.

"...That's right!"

After trying to take credit for it, I got a notification saying I made students tremble in fear.

[Fear Inducement Experience +15]
[Bad Kid Experience +2]

"First-year Oknodie. Be prepared to humbly accept the point deduction notice for classroom property damage that will soon arrive on your magic watch."
"Yes..."

There's an unintended expense, but if it means my friend Tito Soga can attend the academy without crying, isn't it worth it?
I didn't entirely dislike Tito Soga, who was sticking close to me and chattering away with admiring eyes.
Why hide it?
I have a weakness for beautiful women and cute creatures.
Just because my body became female doesn't mean my personal preferences changed.

'Still, there's such a thing as muscle memory, so I might also respond to things the original owner of this body liked.'

I wonder what Oknodie's original body owner liked?
I'm curious, but not that eager to know.
What would an 11-year-old kid like anyway?
Dolls?
Flowers?
Probably something like that.

"Administrative Department. A born Quartermaster at that. It suits your evil nature and ruthless personality perfectly!"

Everyone's attention turned to the hat, which had broken free during the commotion and was now evaluating the aptitude and talent of the last department student.
Xing, the last student being examined, grabbed the brim of the hat with a look that suggested the hat had gone crazy.

"You'd better think and speak carefully, hat. I am a swordsman who has slain over a hundred other swordsmen."

Honestly, even I thought Xing's appropriate department would be the Knight Department with a class like Assassin Swordsman, so it was really unexpected when the hat stubbornly assigned him to the Administrative Department.
The Administrative Department was generally filled with those who lacked talent for other departments but worked hard to make something of themselves, or with royalty and high nobility.
To put it bluntly, it's a department created so that even those without talent can at least hand out business cards saying they're Gift Academy graduates or dropouts.
It's perfect for saving face with various countries.
That's why there are particularly many imperial and royal family members in the Administrative Department.
The Mesugakki Imperial Princess would probably go to the Magic Department since she's exceptional and has talent for magic.

"That's why it suits you even more. Have you ever thought about it? A quartermaster who slaughters attackers targeting supplies and enemies."
"It's trivial work. What's the difference between escorting a merchant group and escorting military supplies?"
"That might be true for an ordinary quartermaster. But what suits you is 'Dark Quartermaster.'"
"...'Dark'?"
"When army morale hits rock bottom with deserters and casualties mounting, you're the quartermaster who sets out despite the risk of betrayal or supply routes being reported. You might end up killing more allies than enemies!"
"Hooh."
"You can even execute soldiers on the spot by abusing your authority, framing them for crimes like hoarding food or being informants. It's a class perfect for bloodshed!"

What's with that "hooh"?
He's really into something that suits him.
But even I can see that if someone like him becomes a quartermaster and shows up with a transport carriage, people would think, "Wow, this looks really intense, should we just let this carriage pass?"
No wonder they call it Dark Quartermaster.

"For advanced positions, there's Dark Munitions Commander. At the highest level, there's Shadow Power of the Military. A class that directly influences a country's military control can use mere swordsmen like disposable ants."
"That sounds interesting."

No, this is crazy.
It's fine if the person involved finds it interesting, but a nuclear bomb is about to drop on the Administrative Department where princes, princesses, and nobles chat and laugh together.
The Administrative Department, where students coast through based on their country of origin and support base, with lower-ranked students acting as subordinates and doing assignments for them—a peacefully rotten department.
Now imagine a lonely madman who draws his sword at the slightest provocation barging in there.
Every day would bring bloodshed, and eventually, the academy might have to call in assassins to fight Xing.

"Don't be fooled. I know better. That would be really boring, you know?"

I wanted to avoid interfering if possible, but for the sake of the academy's peaceful daily life, I couldn't let this inflated nonsense continue.
Xing looked at me with psychopathic eyes that made me believe he could have killed someone on his way to school this morning.

"What do you know, big-shot kid?"

I needed to be persuasive.
I needed an excuse he would believe, even if it wasn't from my own experience.
