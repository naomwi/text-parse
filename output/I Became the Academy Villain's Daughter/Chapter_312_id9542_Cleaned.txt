# 312 - The Chairman's Banquet 2

The tense atmosphere changed when one student naturally broke the silence.

"What's the big deal about being analyzed? Evaluating and being evaluated. It's as natural as breathing. Haven't you ever evaluated someone in your life?"

The speaker was Xing, the Eastern Swordsman.
He nonchalantly finished a bowl of molasses porridge, fish in five spices, and kung pao chicken before speaking, calmly meeting the gazes of the Chairman and everyone else.

"Are you stupid? It's scary because his intentions are suspicious."
"How amusing. You tremble in fear when you see someone potentially harboring hostility. Then shouldn't you, a frontier-born prisoner, live in constant fear?"
"You bastard... You think words don't hurt?"

Son O-cheon grabbed his staff, making the banquet atmosphere even more chaotic, but neither the Chairman nor Oknodie showed any intention of stopping them.
One found the confrontation interesting, while the other... was busy grabbing plates others weren't eating and wolfing them down.
While Gisele looked at Oknodie in disbelief, Xing continued his blunt observations.

"The Empire doesn't view beastmen and humans equally. They see beastmen as slaves they can exploit anytime. It's just that as society's morality has improved, they can't openly use them like before. But the killing intent remains the same then and now. Are you afraid of that?"
"Not at all. If anyone arrogantly tries to use beastmen as slaves in front of me, I'll crack their skull with this staff."
"That's it. Whatever one's intentions, the moment they act on them is when you intervene. The killing intent in the world of swordsmen is no different."

One can stare with their eyes, take a stance, prepare to draw a sword, and measure the distance between opponents.
When a suspicious person approaches, one might place a hand on their scabbard and widen their stance.
Vigilance is an instinctive area for survival.
However, once a sword is drawn, the likelihood of bloodshed increases.
Excessive caution escalates situations.

"If you swing your staff because someone gave you a suspicious look like they might enslave you, the Empire will gladly imprison you. They'll find a way to charge you with a crime and use you as a slave. Because you've given them justification."

Xing's barely indirect words contained a clear warning.
Stop making a scene.
If you don't want to be rushed to your death.
Son O-cheon flinched and glanced at the Chairman.
Though Son O-cheon had seen plenty of friendly smiles from Gisele, the Chairman's smile had a different quality.
The essence.
The distillation of emotion.
His body trembled as if facing an evil so vast he couldn't imagine it in his current state.

"Haha. What an interesting conversation. But if you don't share your own story, you can't have any food?"

As if saying: would you dare?
Tell a story to someone as suspicious as you.
Both Xing and Son O-cheon kept their mouths shut.
It would be better if everyone remained silent and avoided this uncomfortable time and place.
Breaking through this implicit silence, an unexpected person responded to the Chairman's offer.

"Then I'll do it♡"
"Princess Mesugakki. Are you serious?"
"Unlike that pathetic hero, I don't care what others think♡"

True to her arrogant mesugakki nature, Princess Mesugakki faced the Chairman's gaze without hesitation.

"I hate royal council meetings♡ The crown prince shows off, the ministers play along, and father just silently approves—it's no different from playing house♡"
"My, how sad. It must be boring to just watch situations unfold beyond your control. You've had a sad past, Princess."
"Is this story enough to get food?"
"For a common-grade dish, certainly."
"...Honor Fried Chicken claims to be loyal to me as a member of a three-generation noble family, but he's arrogantly aiming for my husband's position♡ I give him difficult tasks whenever we meet because it's annoying, but he just won't go away♡"
"Unfortunately, that's still common-grade."
"But that was quite important~? Isn't a young girl's love story rare~? And I'm a princess, too~?"
"As with anyone, hearing stories we already know is boring. Classics are enjoyable even when revisited, but Your Highness's romance still has a long way to go. If you were aiming for bonus points, a story about my daughter would have been nice, but it seems you don't have any suitable information either."

Even the confident princess was startled by this remark.
The Foundation had already obtained information about Honor Fried Chicken's flirtations while pretending to be Princess Mesugakki's confidant.
And they had no intention of hiding this fact.
The declaration made to her face was dizzying.
Fear spread across the table that the Chairman might know the students' daily lives down to unimaginably intimate details.

"If you don't have a specific order within the common-grade dishes, I'll provide a random common-grade dish."

While Princess Mesugakki hesitated, a butler brought a common-grade dish.
The princess lost her initial arrogance and picked at her food with chopsticks while watching the Chairman cautiously.

"What was she thinking in the first place? Anyone could have predicted that getting involved with such a creepy man wouldn't lead to anything good."

In response to Logini's grumbling, Sand Cooker displayed a wizard's intuition.

"You never know. She might have stepped up despite her fear because she wanted something."
"What could she want?"
"Maybe she was hungry?"
"Does that look like the chopstick movement of a hungry person?"
"...Maybe there was a dish she wanted?"
"That makes sense. She does look disappointed that the dish grade is low. And she seems hesitant to share a more valuable story because it feels uncomfortable."

While an awkward silence hung in the air, Oknodie, who had been diligently using her hands, put down her plate.
After cleaning her plate without leaving a single scrap, Oknodie looked around and cautiously opened her mouth.

"Is no one going to talk? Then can I?"
"Oknodie. Haven't you eaten enough? If you're still hungry, I can give you some food from my magic backpack."
"Gisele, are you stupid? When such a good opportunity comes along, it would be ridiculous to settle for preserved food. You have to seize opportunities when they come!"

She was a child with a big appetite and a strong desire for new foods.
Gisele decided to watch, unable to stop her from talking about her Papa.
With the interference gone, the Chairman also made eye contact with Oknodie.

"What story does my daughter want to tell us?"
"Hmm~ You've heard everything about what happened on the ship from Jonna, right?"
"Of course."
"Then I'll tell you something even Jonna doesn't know!"

Oknodie pulled out a point card worth 1 million points from her chest.

"You still had such an enormous amount?"
"Just donate it to the Bikini Armor Squad if you can't use it all!"

Despite Hestia and Pyoi's exasperated complaints, Oknodie smiled awkwardly.

"That would be difficult. This is actually a copy!"
"What?!"
"To be precise, I used this sticker!"

When Oknodie peeled off the sticker from the point card, all the point cards that had been in a bundle disappeared.

"That's fraud!"
"Oknodie... you used deception to trap Ishtar!"

Though Ishtar and Yupi shouted angrily, Oknodie responded shamelessly.

"Oh well."

Though the meaning was unclear, the intent to provoke was obvious.
Just as they were about to draw their swords in uncontrollable anger, butlers from all directions simultaneously drew their hidden weapons or took offensive stances toward Ishtar.
In an instant, the banquet table and feast became surrounded by instructor-level experts and even Professor-level expert Jonna joining in from among them.
The atmosphere was so deadly that if a sword were drawn from its scabbard, it wouldn't be strange if attacks rained down enough to reduce the banquet table to ashes!
Everyone turned pale at the magical energy response vibrating so intensely it made their skin tingle.

"Ishtar. I understand you're angry, but don't draw your sword."
"It feels like we'll get attacked too..."
"Listen to what the others are saying. Now is not the time for revenge."

Only when Ishtar removed her hand from the scabbard did the butlers finally stand down.
However, like alert guard dogs staring at suspicious shadows or prowling around, the butlers' gazes repeatedly lingered around Ishtar.

'There were three of them.'

Meanwhile, Zuang counted the auras of the powerful individuals she detected while stroking her arm to calm her goosebumps.
Jonna Wiheomhae standing near Oknodie, the middle-aged chief secretary standing next to the Chairman, and the head maid commanding the other maids.
Even amid the chaotic killing intent crackling in the air, the Chairman showed no signs of fear or hesitation.
At least three professor-level experts.
Perhaps the Chairman was even stronger than them.
As everyone at the banquet realized this fact for what felt like the umpteenth time and broke out in cold sweat, Oknodie calmly asked.

"So what grade is telling you about the point bug?"
"I'll give you one unique-grade dish."
"Yay! I did it!"

No one else responded to the Chairman's offer until Oknodie cleared her plate.
