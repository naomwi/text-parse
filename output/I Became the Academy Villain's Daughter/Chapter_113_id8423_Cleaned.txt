# Enhanced Translation: Chapter 113 - I Just Decided

"Oh, you're here, Oknodie?"
"Hello, senior!"
"That thing from last time, I put it to good use."
"Hehe. Me too."

"What's this? When did you two get so close?"

"A small mushroom that's enough for one person!"

"Oh, I didn't mean the quality was bad. It was small but excellent mushroom!"

"What do you mean 'of course not,' you trash? You wanted to show a barely ten-year-old kid your 'small but excellent mushroom'?"

"Senior, why are you suddenly crossing your legs?"

"Oknodie, why are you tormenting this innocent senior first thing in the morning?"

"But why is the classroom atmosphere like this?"
"You're asking because you don't know? Obviously because of the assignment."
"Ah, I see."

Due to Titosoga's unintentional trolling, the assignment now had both additional bonus points and penalty points.
Everyone had to go through the trouble of preparing reference books and official documents related to "Developing an Eye for Property Valuation" to avoid group penalties.
The noticeably awkward distance and chilly atmosphere among the first-years was due to the tension and wariness toward each other to avoid receiving penalty points.

"Ah, hello, Oknodie..."
"Titosoga, why are you standing at the entrance?"
"Ugh. Somehow it's hard to go in."

Well, the first-years had said some harsh things to Titosoga after the last lecture.

"Everyone! Titosoga is here, so apologize!"
"Aaaah, Oknodie! How could you announce it so loudly!"
"It's better to make up quickly in situations like this!"

If we leave things be, everyone will harbor grudges and eventually fight more insidiously. Fighting leads to more fighting, creating hostile relationships and making the Academy atmosphere brutal.
Although future fixed events will inevitably cause fights even among students in the same year, there's no need to create tension from the beginning of the semester.

"Why should we apologize?"
"We might get penalty points because of Titosoga."

The first-years grumbled, expressing their discontent.

"If you get penalty points, it's not because of Titosoga but because you were lazy and didn't prepare for the assignment!"
"Y-you! What do you know to say such things!"
"I'm the group's top student, so you should obviously listen to me about studying and assignments!"

At school and the Academy, grades are king.
"What's your class ranking?" One sentence is enough to establish the hierarchy!
The students reluctantly apologized while grumbling.
Neither Titosoga nor I expected sincere apologies.
It's something that even the proud Gift Academy students would at least pretend to do.
After all, a student's true enemy isn't the model student.

"Now, let's check how well everyone did on their first assignment. Take out the reference books and official documents you brought."

The professor with a handsome face and large justice pocket who cruelly works students to the bone is the real enemy!
The students each took out books and documents they thought necessary for checking nobles' property records.

A great thief must develop an eye for identifying what assets wealthy households possess!
...According to Professor Bronze's principle, students had prepared for the assignment in their own ways.

"Professor, I brought land registers to identify territories owned by noble families!"
"Well done, Vivian. Since local lords accumulate wealth and assets from land income without markets, understanding land is the most basic foundation."
"I determined that a lord's vassals are the core of their assets, so I looked up the vassal contract lists submitted to each country's central government!"
"Good work, Adolf. The vassals protect the lord's land. Understanding the command hierarchy is also important."

However, these students had only managed with basic document searches.
Titosoga, apparently quite upset about being bullied by everyone, had borrowed three reference books.

"I compiled a list of territories where wealth accumulates by examining books from rental agents who manage private storage of surplus agricultural products in each region, and I also created a list of territories where surplus yields aren't redistributed to farmers based on minstrels' accounts of regional festivals!"

Professor Bronze applauded for the first time.

"Excellent. The more a lord fails to invest in the welfare, convenience, and development of their territory, the more likely they've accumulated unjust wealth and the people suffer. You've shown a fine eye that doesn't miss the 'righteousness' of a noble thief."

The students, while annoyed that Titosoga was being praised, had to acknowledge his thoroughness.
Even Isabel, who had heard about borrowing books from the school store, hadn't prepared as thoroughly as Titosoga, which showed how impressive his effort was.
Even second-year seniors Bigstone and Rizna didn't receive as much praise as Titosoga.

"Does he only take the Eye for Value class? Why did he prepare so intensely?"
"Stop making excuses, Bigstone. You look extremely pathetic."

As the assignment submission and inspection continued, it was soon Xing's turn.
Everyone watched expectantly to see what this solitary Eastern swordsman had prepared, but Xing sat with his arms crossed and nothing on his desk.

"Xing, do you have nothing to show?"
"This assignment doesn't help me train my sword."

What incredible audacity to boldly declare to the professor's face that he prepared nothing because the teaching wasn't necessary for him!

"Wow. That's some serious charisma."
"Isn't he cool?"

Despite the seniors' admiration, Xing naturally received the lowest score and penalty points.
Finally, it was my turn, the last in order.

"Freshman Oknodie, did you also not prepare anything?"
"Of course not."

Few people can live with such a my-way attitude like Xing.
Even I, a player, don't have that level of audacity, so I obediently presented the books and documents I had prepared.
At most, three books.
Unlike the other students who mostly just brought a few official documents, I stacked up books and documents into a tower.

"This is a certified copy of the land register, this is the vassal contract list, storage rental status and surplus yield redistribution list, and I also brought precious metal sales records and auction records from each territory!"

Of course, I had memorized everything the NPCs had researched while taking this class and borrowed the exact same materials.

"Now I see why the top student is the top student."
"Wow. So that's what it takes to be number one."
"She has everything we brought."

There's a bit of guilt about stealing others' efforts, but the professor himself is a noble thief!

[You have most excellently completed the Eye for Value class assignment.]
[Professor Bronze becomes more convinced that he should make you his disciple.]
[You can advance to the class 'Noble Thief's Disciple'.]

...But wasn't the reward supposed to be different?

He's truly a wicked professor.
