# Chapter 61 - Lotto's Reasonable Suspicion

Students who had suffered through Professor Platon's not-quite-abuse still looked pale despite enjoying a three-hour break that included lunch time. Some hadn't fully recovered their strength, while others were simply dreading the upcoming lecture.

"How difficult will the headmaster's lecture be?"
"As the person who gathered all these outrageous professors, it's bound to be the most difficult."
"I heard Oknodie attended the second period lecture and came back fine."
"...I'm convinced. That child must be half-orc. Otherwise, that level of stamina is incomprehensible."
"I'd like to call that racially discriminatory language... but honestly, with that kind of stamina, it's natural to be called an orc."
"Right? Lotto, what do you think?"
"Huh?! Well, I'm not sure..."

Lotto inadvertently responded to friends who had spoken to her after a long time, then mentally cursed when she saw her friend's expression of immediate regret. They had spoken to her out of habit because she happened to be standing nearby. If they were going to regret it, they shouldn't have spoken to her in the first place. Or if they were going to speak, they should have controlled their expressions better. She keenly felt her position, which had plummeted after her confrontation with Oknodie.

"W-we should get going. Haha."
"Oh, I kind of like those seats over there. Don't you?"

As the female students awkwardly moved away, they lowered their voices, though not enough to prevent Lotto from hearing if she paid attention. They berated the student who had spoken to her.

"You idiot. Lotto isn't in our circle anymore."
"Why did you talk to her?"
"Sorry. The situation just felt like old times."
"What if we get marked along with her?"
"Right. The Mesugakki Imperial Princess looks down on Lotto for disgracing the imperial nobility's honor, and Oknodie probably doesn't have good feelings toward her either. We shouldn't associate with Lotto."
"I know, I know..."

If only she had known this would happen, she wouldn't have fought with Oknodie.
While she was regretting her choices, the group of four known as Oknodie's faction gathered in one corner of the lecture hall. Lotto, who aspired to join the Knight Department, remembered seeing Son O-cheon among them in several shared lectures. Son O-cheon was impressive, though not as much as Oknodie.

Mana Cultivation Method.
Mana Refinement Method.

Without these two arcane techniques for increasing mana capacity and improving constitution, he was an uncivilized barbarian who overcame everything with innate toughness, brute strength, and rugged stamina. He was a monkey beastman so genuinely strong it made one envious, proving that those born with sturdy bodies needed neither training nor discipline.

"Look over there. The top student of Group B."
"How can she look so stylish wearing the same uniform as us?"
"Well, one is a hero chosen by the gods, and the other is a saintess selected by one of the twelve zodiac gods. If they're loved by the gods, their charm must be on a different level from people like us."

Compared to undiscovered gems like Oknodie and Son O-cheon, Lotto—who had merely accumulated mana and improved her constitution a bit faster than others through private tutoring—had nothing left to show. With mediocre skills and status, Lotto wasn't openly despised, but she had no comrades with whom to share a word of affection. Rejected by Group B but too proud to join Group A, and potentially exposing herself to even greater hostility, she was a loner.

It was truly pitiful. Having to listen to Group A students' conversations to avoid hearing former Group B friends badmouthing her, and being lonely with no place to belong.

"I have arrived!"

"Kyaaak!"
"Uwaak!"
"It's really the headmaster!"

Beyond the students who were making a fuss as if a devil had appeared, a massive dragon's head appeared at the podium. Rough snorts heated the air and swept through the entire lecture hall, causing unbuttoned uniform jackets and skirt hems to flutter chaotically.

"This lecture is titled 'The Headmaster's Teachings.' I'll teach you the bitter taste of the Academy."

The headmaster's large mouth twisted into a sinister grin.

"Form groups of five people now. This lecture will evaluate all scores by group—it's a group project lecture!"

Lotto nearly burst into tears.
How could they form groups in the very first lecture?
What was someone like her, a loner, supposed to do?
Was this a sign that loners shouldn't pursue academics?

"Headmaster, what happens to groups that can't form five members due to insufficient numbers?"

The headmaster answered:

"Point deduction."

"!!"

Students' gazes urgently darted back and forth.
Lotto wanted to cry even more.
After receiving family support to enter the world's best Gift Academy, if she were to return home after just a few months with a failing grade due to point deductions...
How would her parents look at her, and how much would her sisters despise her?
Neighboring noble families would mock her as a deficient child, and their children currently attending the Academy would spread the story that she failed because she was a loner.
And the result at home?

"We'll find you a husband." Or "Marry this man. Otherwise, I'll put you in a convent for the rest of your life."

Her career as a fighter would end before it even began, and the inevitable fate of an arranged marriage to preserve her value as a "noble woman" would arrive. She might marry a powerful family's trash who beats women or has many wives. Or she might marry a man from a humble family with a protruding belly and ugly appearance, a pervert who looks at her lustfully but whom she wants to believe is a good person.
An unhappy future.
A severed career.
No freedom, only a future waiting as someone's wife, a child's mother.
The fate of a noble woman who had lost her value unfolded in her mind like a tragic opera.
She didn't want to be anyone's wife, mother, or nun.
Absolutely not.
She didn't want to face such a fate.

"Hey! W-would you like to form a group with me...?"

Though she spoke boldly at first, Lotto soon shrank back, and her former friends exchanged glances before shaking their heads.

"I'm sorry. We want to work with people we're close to. You understand, right?"

As expected, they were cutting ties.
The imperial nobles, armed with sociable smiles, were all telling her with their eyes:
Don't cling to us.
Don't talk to us.
Do you want to be embarrassed?
She felt anger rising at their cowardly behavior, turning their backs after encouraging her to fight.

"Imperial Princess Mesugakki! You know we only have eyes for you, right?"
"Hm~ Since you like me so much, I guess I have no choice but to hang out with you for a bit~"

The Second Imperial Princess Mesugakki faction.

"Hero. Your skills exceed even half the rumors. If you're willing, shall we work together?"
"Sure."

The Group B top student Hero faction.

"Shall we heirs of distinguished families join forces to overcome this group project crisis?"
"I refuse. Why would I join hands with a chicken family brat?"
"I treated you with respect as a fellow meritorious family, but you don't know your place. Potato Pizza family brat. If there's a group duel, yours will be the first to be eliminated!"

Two of the three great meritorious families of the Holy Central Empire.
The Chicken Family faction and the Pizza Family faction.
Amid the fierce competition, Lotto turned her gaze elsewhere.
If everyone in Group B had abandoned her, she shouldn't be bound by Group B anymore.
There would be no greater shame than returning home and ending her life and freedom as an individual if she became an outcast while only watching others.
If necessary, she must endure even collaborating with commoners!

"Y-you guys! If you want, I, the great Lotto who is skilled in combat, could become your teammate."

The frontier nobles in Group A would surely die for her offer as a central noble.
Arcadia, the leader of Group A's female students, was said to be interested in foreign cultures.
If she, an imperial noble, offered to join them, they would surely accept.

"Huh? Do you have no shame? There's no way we'd accept someone who insulted our cute Di."

Her seemingly foolproof plan immediately crumbled.
Not only was Arcadia, the spiritual pillar of Group A's female students, hostile toward her.
Andersen, the grand duke's son and spiritual pillar of Group A's male students, also spoke with an unpleasant face.

"Oknodie is the most important student to our western frontier. Do you think anyone in Group A would join hands with someone who made an enemy of her?"
"Why the hell are you deciding everything? Are all Group A members your subordinates?"
"Jigoku. You again? How about showing some cooperation for once?"
"Huh? You want me to shoot you?"
"...I was foolish to seek cooperation from a pirate."

Just when she thought she would fail to find even one teammate, she gained at least one comrade.
Not a woman but a man, not from Group B but Group A, and not quite normal but someone with a trigger-happy streak—but at least she had a group member!

"...I'll help too."
"Hestia! I-I said harsh things to you. Are you still willing to be my teammate?"
"It's not particularly because I like you. I just had nowhere else to go..."

Lotto looked around in alarm at Hestia's words.
Sure enough.
Most of the 44 advanced class students had already formed groups of five.
Even the mysterious Group C, rejected by both Groups A and B, had all five of their members together.

"Oh, what's this? Are we the leftovers group? Then who's our last member?"

In response to Jigoku's intrigued question, an unexpected figure tugged at her collar.

"Me!"
"Huh? Oknodie?"

One of the most popular potential group members had become part of the group of four outcasts.

"That concludes group formation."
"Those four who couldn't find all group members will lose 400 points as penalty!"
"And as consolation, I'll award 500 consolation points!"

"???"
"Yay!"

Oknodie was smiling as if she had somehow known this would happen.
Looking at her face, Lotto remembered something.
Even during their duel, this girl's backing was extraordinary enough to deliberately cause a power outage at the Academy training ground.

'Could she have known in advance about the additional points?'

Otherwise, why would she leave her usual companions to join the forest keeper kid and the man who follows him around, while coming alone to the dropout group?
It was a reasonable suspicion.
