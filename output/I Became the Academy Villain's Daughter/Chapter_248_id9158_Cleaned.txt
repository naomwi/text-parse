# 248 - Let's See You Catch This, Senior

The rainbow ball was far more sinister than the tri-colored ball that delivered the burning sensation of a flaming dodgeball, or even the six-colored ball with its spicy trick of flying in repeated intervals.

"She handles advanced control techniques as naturally as breathing."
"Is that truly a first-year's dodgeball?"
"Incredible proficiency in transformation magic. The quality of the mana formulas is extraordinary. She's properly harnessing the power of natural mana."

The professors, who had settled in to watch this rare major event, were startled the moment they saw the rainbow ball.

"Of course. Who do you think taught her?"

The professors were surprised for a different reason.
It was because of the presence of the flower-crowned crazy woman—no, the Weird Professor who usually skipped all these events—sitting in a corner on a swing made of vines, rocking back and forth with a creaking sound.
While Professor Rave, who taught "Fundamentals and Understanding of Imperial Magic," and other imperial professors couldn't hide their uncomfortable expressions, the professors from the frontier regions asked questions without hesitation.

"Do you teach mana control techniques to first-years?"
"Isn't that preparation too advanced?"
"I never specifically taught her that."
"Then why is it your credit? That's just raw talent."
"Because one of the spells that child is using was taught by me."

Vines grew rapidly at the Weird Professor's feet.
Peculiarly, the vines grew sideways rather than upward.

"Gravity manipulation."
"You teach such advanced magic to a first-year?"
"Until the final exams, I teach magic related to nature's survival strategies. Camouflage, poison, ambush, and such."

In the world, there are animals that hang upside down from ceilings to launch surprise attacks, and monsters that eat prey thrown off cliffs by suddenly levitating with gravity magic.

"Hmm. There are many promising students in this year's first-year class. Both Mesugakki and Oknodie."

A uniquely young and cheerful voice rang out among the professors.
It was Professor Pinkberry, the Academy's shortest professor at 144cm, who insisted on pink twin-tails but never got told to act her age—the perfect example of a legal loli.

"But why do I see disgusting mosquitoes near that child's magic?"

A clenched-teeth anger began to appear on Professor Pinkberry's youthful face.


* * *


While the six-colored ball's interval repetition would slow down after being caught with the body a few times, the rainbow ball could maintain nearly full power through gravity magic that constantly applied gravitational acceleration.
A ball falling from 3m height can be caught easily, but a ball falling from 30m is frightening to approach for those unfamiliar with it.
Not to mention a ball with gravitational acceleration from 300m or 3000m.
The moment Dead Cat first experienced the rainbow-colored all-out attack, he realized the rainbow ball's trick.

'The first catch is relatively easy. Once you miss, catching the returning ball becomes much harder.'

The ball gains speed as gravitational acceleration continuously adds up, then pops out at close range through interval repetition, trying to knock his body out of bounds.
Naturally, the rainbow ball had various magic formulas embedded to thwart any attempt to catch it.
This was a pure battle of skill.
A contest between the skill of placing formulas to trouble the catcher and the catcher's practical skill.
However, this time too, Oknodie's ball had the troublesome powers of "Transformation" and "Amplification" that changed the ball's characteristics at regular intervals, making customized defense impossible.
Truly a diverse all-out attack worthy of being called a rainbow-colored assault.

'I caught it.'

Dead Cat's skill was not lacking either.
At the cost of losing two life stacks, he managed to grab the ball relatively quickly.
But he couldn't throw it.

"Wow~! You caught it already. Then here comes the double rainbow-colored all-out attack~~!"

Not only was Oknodie's attack continuing, but... this ball had precisely engraved formulas that disconnected force application and space.
Even if he threw the ball toward Oknodie, gravitational acceleration would kick in again, tilting the ball back toward himself, and it would start endlessly repeating through the disconnected space.
It was like holding a boomerang that returns when thrown, or a guided missile with his own coordinates locked in.

'She engraved such vicious formulas to ensure it doesn't end with just catching the ball.'

Dead Cat felt powerless.
As long as this dodgeball game continued, there was no way for Dead Cat to win inside the court.

"I forfeit."

Dead Cat quickly folded. There was no point in wasting precious illusion stacks out of stubbornness only to become a laughingstock in front of students and professors.
His illusion stack wasn't a technique light enough to keep showing off in front of others multiple times.

"That's not fair!"

He ignored Oknodie's whining.
Next time, he would put that arrogant nose in its place with a no-rules confrontation outside the court.
But...
After leaving the court, he witnessed a scene that made him glad he had forfeited.
The balls that had been targeting him were now whooshing through the air.
Balls that kept accelerating as they repeatedly passed through disconnected space.

"Wow. Who's going to retrieve those balls?"
"Look at that speed. Not even telekinesis magic could get them out."
"Seems like nobody can retrieve them...?"

Suddenly, all three balls on the court were sealed in the space where Dead Cat had been.

"Wow. Dead Cat had it rough."
"That first-year is insane."
"Being the top student isn't for just anyone."

Students approached, pretending to be friendly.
Dead Cat, who was about to proudly ignore them with feline dignity, turned his head sharply.
These faces were all familiar.
This one from the advanced class. That one from the advanced class. Every single one was from the advanced class.
There were already too many students who shouldn't be outside the court.

"What got you?"

When Dead Cat asked, the second-year advanced class students sheepishly pointed to their respective defeat factors.

"This guy named Gisele unfairly threw pepper powder shaped like a ball, and when I grabbed it, it burst. My nose stung and my eyes burned so much I couldn't see."
"Dorothy is pure evil too. I managed to get Rockfell out, but she threw a dodgeball with spikes all over it. It hurt so much I dropped it."
"You know that kid wearing the pirate captain hat? I was having an intense shooting match with him, but suddenly the ball next to me started spewing electricity and I got shocked and hit by a ball."
"Didn't someone get ambushed by a kid with a dagger and get carried to the medical wing?"
"Dodgeball is really dangerous. Guns, ambushes—there's too much to watch out for."

Second-year advanced class students sharing stories of how they were eliminated by first-year advanced class students through extraordinary methods, as if they were heroic tales!

"You guys got off easy."
"What happened to you?"

One second-year student pointed to a towering ice pillar with a gloomy face.

"I couldn't even see their face, and something that might have been a dodgeball or an ice ball kept pouring down from the top of the pillar one-sidedly."

Estimated height: 50m.
Diameter: 5m.
The massive ice pillar was so tall that even looking up, you couldn't see the top.
Its imposing presence was reminiscent of a tower, as bewildering as if a strategic weapon had appeared in a children's game!

"Wow..."
"That's too much..."

Irene, the Northern Grand Duchess.
She was picking off second-year advanced class students one by one, displaying a presence second only to the students known as the Four Heavenly Kings.


* * *


Irene was honestly offended.
Oknodie?
Understandable since she was the top student.
Ishtar?
She had personally experienced the strength of the newborn hero.
But the other two were different.
Casia.
A quiet, seemingly unmotivated student from Group C whose daily activities were unknown.
Mesugakki.
Someone who attended the Academy with an attitude that seemed to belittle it, playing princess games.

'If such people are counted among the Four Heavenly Kings, does that mean I'm worse than them?'

Unacceptable.
She would clearly show those who dared to judge her as inferior.
One, two, three.
She threw ice balls indiscriminately, like bombing raids, at second-year advanced class students and regular students alike.
Some ice balls would stick to your hand if caught.
Some would explode nearby, sending ice shards flying.
Some would turn the ground into an ice rink upon impact.
Students fell helplessly against the barrage of ice balls with random effects.

Boom, boom-boom

Occasional explosions could be heard from the base of the pillar, but her expertise in ice magic properly utilized architectural engineering principles to ensure the structure could withstand until rapid repairs and reinforcements could be made.
As a result, those targeting the pillar and those targeting her at the top were equally wasting their efforts.
No one without the skill to destroy the pillar in one strike and bring her down to eye level could threaten her.

'Most of the advanced class students are already engaged with the first-year advanced class. The only ones who might have the capacity to face me are...'

CRASH!

A different level of impact shook the ground at her feet.
Turning around in surprise, she saw a brilliantly white senior holding a ball.
Lu, the White Paladin.
The opponent who had eliminated Mesugakki without anyone noticing had now set his sights on her.

'I didn't sense anything at all. To defeat someone with such ominous dark mana without me even detecting it...'

This was no ordinary senior.
A senior counted among the Four Heavenly Kings of the second year.
Yet he was underestimated?

Crunch.

The ice ball in her hand grew colder.
Such a powerful opponent—the first since the hero Ishtar.
How much stronger had she become since that day of powerless defeat?

"Let's see you catch this, Senior."
