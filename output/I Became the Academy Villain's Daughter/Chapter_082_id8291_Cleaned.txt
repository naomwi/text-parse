# 82 - Fake Flags

Vanilla ultimately failed to purchase the flag.

"I'm sorry, hero."
"It's okay. I was the one who asked for your help."
"I'll return the points I received as a down payment."
"The 10 points are a down payment from me. Regardless of success or failure, they're yours, so please use them for dinner tonight."
"Thank you!"

Ishtar couldn't hide her disappointment.
She had sent someone else to attempt buying the flag, fearing she might raise suspicions if she went herself, but the clever Oknodie had seen through the proxy purchase.
However, her disappointment was lessened by the equally important information she'd gained.

"To think Oknodie knew all the lectures Vanilla attends—her intelligence network is quite concerning."
"I was so scared I thought I would die! Please pat poor frightened Vanilla's shoulders, hero!"
"I'll pass on that."

Ishtar firmly grabbed Vanilla's shoulders with both hands when she tried to throw herself into her arms, coldly pushing her away.
She maintained an expressionless face until Vanilla left, pretending nothing was wrong, but once Vanilla departed with a dejected look, the corners of her mouth curled up slightly.

"Ishtar. What are you so pleased about?"
"Yupi, did you hear? Oknodie must be interested in me."
"...But she's an assassin."
"She's also my top candidate for a water-attribute teammate."
"When assassins gather preliminary information, there's usually only one reason."

Unlike the hero who was delighted that someone who had openly refused to be her teammate was secretly investigating which lectures she attended and who her classmates were, the saintess was tense.

"In my opinion... Miss Oknodie is definitely an assassin sent by some organization. One who's been ordered to assassinate the current hero."
"Yupi, aren't you worrying too much? Oknodie is just a 10-year-old child."
"Don't underestimate Group A's top student because of her age. She's a powerful individual comparable to you, the hero."

Skola, who had been on the same team as the two, supported Yupi's opinion.

"That child's archery skills were extraordinary. I believe she's easily the second-best archer in the 981st class. The first place, of course, belongs to me, descendant of the divine archer."
"See? If she's skilled in archery, she might be capable of 'sniping' too. We can't let our guard down."

Hero Ishtar's official party member was only Saintess Yupi.
Skola, ranked first in archery among the 981st class, believed he could secure a position as Ishtar's companion, but...
He flinched.
Ishtar, looking at Skola, seemed more interested in second-ranked Oknodie than in him, the first-ranked archer.

"Really? What was Oknodie's archery like?"

Skola recalled her archery skills.

"It was a moving target test race. First of all, she was quick on her feet and light-bodied. Her core muscles were well-developed, allowing her to put power into her arrows even in unstable positions."
"She was strong enough to skillfully execute power shots... Um, another notable point would be her wide field of vision."
"But she was naive enough to backtrack during the race to help classmates who were falling behind, running alongside them. In the end, she's just a child."

Having felt he'd praised her too much, he added Oknodie's weakness at the end.
It was meant to emphasize that he was a better archer than Oknodie, but contrary to his intention, the hero's expression turned cold.

"How disappointing, Skola."
"...Hero? You misunderstand! I wasn't trying to belittle that child...!"
"If not belittling, then you consider it common sense to treat a righteous and kind heart as childish. Does that mean you see me, a hero who values justice, as childish too?"
"Not at all!"
"You don't need to make excuses. If that's how you choose to live, I don't want to deny that fact. It's just that we're not compatible."

Skola felt sick to his stomach at the thought of losing his chance at joining the hero's party.

'After all, companions need to be physically strong.'

Meanwhile, Ishtar was in a good mood.
She had plenty of reasons to recruit Oknodie: the water attribute quota, the archer quota, and now bonus points for physical strength.
This made her not just the first choice but the zero-choice recruitment candidate.
She must have feelings for me too, since she went as far as investigating my social circle.
What a tsundere!

'I thought if I showed my competence as a hero in this lecture, Oknodie would stop pretending and honestly agree to become my companion... Too bad.'

This wasn't her only opportunity.
The hero decided to wait for another chance in the future.


* * *


-I have arrived!

In the end, Ishtar failed to overtake others in flag count with a last-minute comeback before the headmaster appeared.

-Put the flags your five-person teams collected into your respective baskets. I'll rank you according to the total number of flags.

Dragon Headmaster expected at most ten to twenty flags per team.
That's how it had been last year, and the year before that.
He never imagined he would see baskets overflowing with flags, some even stacked high like towers.

-Why are there so many flags?

The students secretly hoped:
If only the fake flags could be counted as real, their rankings would skyrocket with these flags!

-Someone has released fake flags.
-As many as three hundred of them.
-How audacious.

If a merchant's quality was determined by how many fake flags they could sell, Gisele would have been an excellent merchant.

□Arcadia Princess's Team(A) - Total 112

Fake flags everywhere!
Only the bottom two teams who couldn't afford to spend points, Gisele's team who had sold all their fake flags, and Oknodie's team who had many flags to begin with had competed honestly.
Everyone thought so, but there was still one more team that didn't possess any fake flags.

-Fake flags are obviously all invalid!
-If you confess now, I'll be kind enough to waive the penalty points in recognition of your sincere effort.

As the Dragon Headmaster offered leniency, students began removing flags one by one from their baskets.

"Huh? Wait. Was this fake or real?"
"I don't know. They all look the same."
"H-Headmaster. What happens if we remove real flags from the basket?"

-Naturally, they won't be counted.

"Then what if fake flags remain in the basket?"

-You should be prepared for penalty points.

The students turned to Gisele with resentment. They had poured their points into a gacha with no ceiling or 10+1 bonus, only to receive harsh penalty points in return!

"We need to find a pattern. What did the real flags look like? Is this one real? Is that one real?"
"Damn it, we're all doomed! Fried, you bought too many flags and messed up the real ones too!"
"You all joined in happily! I spent the most points in the first place!"
"Who cares! You're the team leader, so you take responsibility!"
"The cerebral watashi getting penalty points in the first week of Academy...? This is something I cannot include in my letter to my family!!"

Students were in chaos—some rummaging through flags, others unable to accept reality and wailing, and some grabbing each other by the collar.
Amidst the pandemonium, Mesugakki banged on the desk with a bang-bang sound.

"Attention!"

The imperial students who had fallen into confusion turned to Mesugakki. No cadet could ignore the call of the 2nd Princess.

"You all look so pathetic, like you've failed miserably. Isn't that too pathetic~? Losers♥ Failures♥ There's still a chance for a last-minute reversal♥"

Mesugakki came up with a shocking plan.

"If we mix fake flags into other teams' baskets too, all teams will receive penalty points~?"
"!!!"
"Such a brilliant idea!!"
"As expected of the Princess!"
"The teams without fake flags are Oknodie's team, Gisele's team, Andersen's team, and that team of five from Group C!"

As the situation turned unfavorable, Gisele quickly raised her hand and spoke to the Dragon Headmaster.

"Hasn't the submission already ended?"

-I told you to bring flags by Thursday, but I never said the deadline was when the lecture started.

If there was one thing Gisele overlooked, it was that the headmaster's personality was worse than anyone could imagine!

-In life, you'll be backstabbed by deadlines more than once or twice.
-A paper submission deadline might suddenly change from 1 PM to 2 PM due to unexpected schedule changes, and then mysterious assailants might attack the conference and burn all the papers during that one hour!
-Protecting your team's achievements despite arbitrarily changing deadlines.
-This is a valuable experience you must go through to survive not only the Academy but also the harsh society.

The example was strangely specific and plausible enough to make listeners wonder if it had happened to him.

'Did the headmaster change the time?'
'He might have burned them himself.'
'He probably burned the paper on "Whether Dragons Mate in Dragon Form or Human Form" because he found it embarrassing, and now he's pretending he didn't!'

While students looked at the headmaster with suspicious eyes, and one particularly short student with certain eyes, the headmaster announced the final submission deadline.

-The submission deadline is 30 minutes from now.
-Until then, you're free to burn other teams' flags, take them by force, or mix in fake flags.
-Judging will be done in 30 minutes, based only on the flags in each team's basket.

In short, for the next 30 minutes, teams with 100% real flags must defend them against their formidable classmates from the advanced classes.
Naturally, Oknodie's team, which had collected the most real flags, received the most attention.

'Oh? This might be a chance to make Oknodie honestly become my companion?'

Hero Ishtar's eyes also gleamed with opportunity.
