# Chapter 196 - Protection and Discipline

Scola had mentally prepared himself. He was stepping into the first-year restricted area where all sorts of ominous rumors circulated.

'If things go wrong, I might die. Was betting my life on 500 points too much? I feel like Yupi tried to warn me.'

The reality he'd heard directly from Yupi was even more frightening than the rumors, but by faithfully following the precautions, he fortunately reached his destination without losing his life.

Just as he was about to reassure himself that it wasn't as difficult as he'd expected, he noticed something unusual about the storage area.

'Are the animals on edge?'

Scola moved cautiously, suppressing his presence and footsteps as he explored the interior, alert to the strange atmosphere.

"!!"

And then he discovered it.
In a dark corner.
A golem's feed bin trembling with fear.
And Oknodie, picking out a small stone from a pile of rocks, muttering "Lucky~!" before suddenly swallowing it.

'Good heavens!'

He didn't particularly sympathize with the golem.
After all, he was the very person who had brought paint cans intending to frame the golem for bullying other animals, and had studied ways to provoke the golem into punching other animals.
But he never imagined there would be a crazy child stealing golem food.

'No, this is actually perfect. If I report this, I can deal a direct blow to Oknodie!'

He wasn't even curious about what she was trying to do.
Whether she stole the stones because they were delicious.
Or if she was threatening the golem.
Whatever the case, that child had infiltrated a restricted area and stolen a mount's food.
And she wasn't just stealing stones.
She was also tapping the forehead of a cheetah that was whimpering and begging her not to touch its food bin, then stealing the cheetah's food too.
This wasn't her first time stealing food.
This was sufficient cause to catch her in the act.

'Foolish girl. You've dug your own grave!'

Scola hurriedly found Professor Platon and brought him and the instructors to the "Advanced Class Mount Storage."

"Are you certain about this report? False reports that waste Academy staff time are grounds for point deduction."
"Don't worry. I saw it clearly with my own eyes."

The professor, instructors, and Scola the informant burst into the mount storage area, flinging the door open without hesitation.
But instead of Oknodie the organic-mount-food-thief, they found only her companions, wearing chef hats and distributing food they had brought to the animals.

"What are you doing?"

The donkey deftly licked away the rice ball that Isabel was holding while she stood there looking dumbfounded.

"We were bringing food for the mounts, sir."
"And what happened to the perfectly good feed that was here?"
"Well, actually..."

In this moment of crisis.
Gisele demonstrated quick thinking.

"Oknodie once had to survive on animal feed during her training at the Foundation. She felt sorry for the animals having to eat such tasteless food and suggested bringing them human food instead. That's why we snuck into the mount storage area without permission."
"Oh my."
"She ate animal feed?"
"What a pitiful..."

Even the instructors couldn't help but sigh at this story!
Isabel, holding the food, picked up on Gisele's quick-witted comment with an adventurer's intuition.

"I'm sorry. In the frontier, we have a culture of treating livestock like family and providing them with food. We've all felt bad about not taking care of the animal friends who have worked so hard carrying students on their backs during exam preparations. It wasn't just because of Oknodie."

A statement emphasizing their voluntary service!

"How exemplary."
"Their attitude toward bonding with the mounts is admirable."
"These kids are so thoughtful. Should we give them bonus points, Professor?"

Professor Platon scratched his head as he observed the happy mounts, then said:

"Give them bonus points."

The result was the exact opposite of what Scola had intended.


* * *


The sudden appearance of Gisele and Isabel.
This is what happened behind the scenes.
Oknodie had been caught red-handed stealing feed by Scola's infiltration.
Gisele already knew about this.

"Urgent news. A student from the advanced class, Scola, followed Oknodie into the first-year restricted area."

Gisele, who had stationed Dark Society secret operatives along the infiltration route to monitor people coming and going, realized that Oknodie was being followed when Scola was caught in his information network.

"Impressive skills. Please summon Arcadia and Isabel here immediately. They must come at once, regardless of what they're doing. This is top priority."

Scola's ability to successfully track Oknodie from a distance, deceiving someone more sensitive than wild animals, was remarkable.
But Gisele couldn't just stand by while Oknodie was in danger.

"Top priority? Why so serious? Did a third-year senior who escaped from the professors' laboratory hide in the first-year area?"
"Isabel. Please prepare a large quantity of food immediately. We need to save Oknodie."
"Oknodie? Did she get lost while playing in the mountains?"
"Her eccentric behavior of stealing feed at the advanced class mount storage has been discovered by a student from the Imperial faction."

The news was much more shocking than she had anticipated.

"Wait... how did you know about this?"
"I had people monitoring the situation in advance."
"You knew and didn't stop her?"
"You know our little lady wouldn't stop just because I asked her to. Next time, she'll act even more secretly to evade even my surveillance."
"This is ridiculous. We'll discuss that later. We need to save her first. When do I need to finish cooking?"
"Preferably within 30 minutes. I'll use the fake Academy ghost stories I've spread to delay Scola from calling a professor or instructor."
"I don't have enough time to prepare sufficient quantities alone."
"Perfect timing. Arcadia has arrived. She will provide the ingredients and necessary assistants. Feel free to direct and use them as needed."

This person has adapted too well to the Academy, hasn't he?
Gisele's methods were eerily bizarre even though they were on the same side.
Isabel figured she'd gain nothing but unwanted knowledge by questioning him further, so she focused on putting Arcadia's followers to work.

"You should be able to easily make salt-cured meat by mixing it well with clean, salt-washed hands, and shape rice balls. Hurry."
"Um... Isabel? I washed my hands clean, but all the salt disappeared. Is this magic?"
'Just put down all the ingredients and carry the sacks.'
"Go die."

Isabel was so flustered that her inner thoughts and spoken words came out opposite.
It was an oversight to forget that Arcadia's followers were nobles with poor life skills.
Nevertheless, they somehow managed to prepare the food in time, bringing it to the advanced class mount storage and distributing it to the mounts.

Clank

"Don't move! You intruders!"
"Huh? Professor. What I'm seeing is a bit different from what was reported."

When the professors, instructors, and Scola burst in less than three minutes later, her heart raced as if she might not live to see another day.
But the outcome was smooth.
Thanks to Gisele's quick thinking and Isabel's perceptiveness.
The appropriate excuses from the two saved Oknodie, who became an icon of sympathy.
Professor Platon wrapped up the situation warmly, giving them both bonus points and advice to get proper permission next time before distributing food.
When the situation was resolved, Isabel, who had been carrying the remaining sacks of food, opened one in a secluded spot.

Wriggle, wriggle...
Pop!

A small blonde head popped out of the sack!
Her slightly whitened hair from flour and her even whiter cheeks didn't diminish her cuteness but rather enhanced it.
Gisele wasn't fooled by that cute face and eyes, and scolded the mischievous girl.

"Oknodie. Why did you do something so dangerous? You could have been in serious trouble if we hadn't helped."
"I'm sorry..."
"Never mind the apology, just tell me why."

Even Gisele, who had a soft spot for Oknodie, was stern this time due to the seriousness of the situation.
In response, Oknodie whimpered with a "Hing" sound reminiscent of Titosoga.
Her gaze turned to Isabel and Arcadia, but the two made it clear they wouldn't give in to her aegyo this time.

"I'm sorry, Di. This is all for your own good, so please understand."
"You're 11 years old, practically an adult. Speak like a grown-up."
"Hing, I will."
'Ah, this is maddening. She's too cute.'
'...I must endure for Oknodie's discipline.'

Arcadia quickly covered her face with a fan and bit her lip to stop herself from speaking, while Isabel clenched her fists behind her back, suppressing the urge to hug her!
Realizing she had no allies, Oknodie reluctantly revealed her true purpose for infiltrating the storage.

"I wanted to increase my guidebook collection rate to get stronger before the main exam."

Guidebook collection rate.
Wanting to get stronger.
These incomprehensible words made no sense.
Children often live in their own world, but even considering that, Oknodie's words were difficult to understand.
Someone unkind might wonder if she was a bit slow!
But Gisele was on Oknodie's side.
Even as he questioned and pressed her, at the core of his heart was a desire to understand and help her.
Simultaneously, he felt sympathy for a poor child abused by the Foundation.
He filled the logical gaps with his own assumptions.

"Experiences from childhood, especially before important events, can become routines that are repeated as a kind of ritual even as time passes."
"?"
"Is that what happened to our little lady? Did you feel compelled to eat whatever you could before important exams?"

Oknodie nodded vigorously.

"So Gisele understands?"
"...Poor thing. It's alright now."

After hesitating, Oknodie ran into Gisele's outstretched arms.
Gisele stroked her head, gently dusting off the flour and wiping her face with a handkerchief.
His heart sank with complicated emotions.

'Those Foundation bastards. Not providing proper food to young children whose value hadn't yet been proven, making them eat animal feed instead. And she had to grit her teeth and eat it to maintain her energy for grueling tests just to survive. What a miserable past.'

How long would our little lady continue to reveal such a miserable past?
Why did the Foundation have to be so cruel to children?
Do they even have any humanity?

'This won't do. After this exam...'

His resolve hardened.
Outside Gift Academy.
He would use his underworld connections to thoroughly expose the darkness of the Foundation.
