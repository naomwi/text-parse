# 90 - The Professor's Interest

I woke up early in the morning to go exercise when a note fell from my door.

===
<Professor Bronze's Note>
The professor has sent you a note.
When a professor shows interest in a student, it's an omen of ominous events.
What terrible thing is about to happen?
===

There was also an event notification.
My head started throbbing automatically.
When I opened the note, my headache got worse.

There's a raccoon with sticky fingers in the garden.
If you're interested, come to my research lab.
-Bronze De Estrada

Bronze de Estrada.
The professor with a large pocket of justice had noticed the theft I committed yesterday.

"Wow. How did I get caught in less than 24 hours?"

Considering I woke up early in the morning, I was essentially caught within 12 hours.
I wonder if she knows who the culprit is?
This is terrifying.
I want to dig into the sand, bury my head in the ground and hide, but if I did that, the professor might sneak up behind me and say, "You've positioned yourself perfectly for burial," and start shoveling dirt over me.
Or she might misunderstand and a few hours later, I'd crawl out, put my clothes on the laundry magic circle, and go take a shower.
I need to be brave.
I went to the professor's research lab to check if my crime had been discovered.

"Do you like cookies?"
"Yes!"
"Too bad."

The professor smugly bit into a soft cookie and ate it all by herself.
There's a rumor among players that you can't become a professor without losing your humanity, and it seems I should start supporting that hypothesis.

'I need to stay calm.'

I'm a bit annoyed, but if I get emotionally rough, I might reveal my true feelings in response to casually thrown questions.
I tried to hypnotize myself internally to suppress my desire for cookies and my indignation.

'That must be a wasabi horseradish cookie. Or maybe a very sour vinegar cookie.'

The professor suddenly asked.

"Do you like tea?"
"...Can I have some too?"
"I was just asking out of courtesy."

What a rude person.
The way she takes a sip to cleanse her palate is really annoying.
It makes me appreciate what a generous person Arcadia is for providing free snacks and tea.

"Are you enjoying academy life?"
"Very much."
"Very much enjoyment?"
"Yes."
"That's good."

The professor put down her teacup and said.

"Soon, unpleasant things will begin."
"......"

Was this person always like this in the game?
I mostly only looked at her chest, so I don't remember.
I knew she had a weakness for beautiful people, but I never expected to be caught like this.
I reflected that from now on, I should remember people not just by their faces or figures, but also properly consider their personalities.

"I have three pieces of information."

Professor Bronze, despite her unimposing name, exuded an imposing atmosphere.

"But a thief must obtain what they want with their own hands. Pay an appropriate price for each piece of information, and I'll give it to you."

===

"Are you asking for points?"
"You can also pay with information of your own. Don't you have at least one? Some valuable information that would warrant a reward if shared with others."
"Hmm... I do have some, but..."
"Well then, tell me. What do you want?"
"I don't want to buy any information!"
"...Oh?"

A look of surprise crossed the professor's face.

"You must know that Gift Academy is no ordinary academy. You must be curious about the value of the treasure. And if you seek strength, you'd be tempted by skills."

Yet you don't want anything?
Don't lie.
The professor looked at me like she was admonishing a wary child.

"If you're afraid of not getting your money's worth, don't worry. I swear on the honor of the Empire's greatest noble thief that I won't shortchange you or deceive you. This offer itself is a favor to you."

But I really do know all this information already.
Weekly events appear in a fixed pattern on rotation, I'm the thief, and the way to receive skill training is to complete a professor's request.
But a child who knows everything lacks a certain cuteness.
Efficiency freaks have no style.
A true veteran player should occasionally know how to follow an inefficient route with finesse!

"I'm curious about skill training."
"The information fee is simple. Show me your skills. If your skill satisfies this noble thief, I'll teach you a skill of comparable value."

This is the system where if you've mastered a 'basic skill' with sufficient proficiency, they'll teach you an advanced version or a related skill.
It's something more suited to a training instructor at some training ground, but professors are actually an extension of instructors.
There are professors who mainly teach theory, but there are also those who teach practical knowledge, and even practical professors who teach skills themselves beyond knowledge.
Skills taught by Professor Bronze, the noble thief.
Of course, what's most tempting is her magnificent chest, but the psychological resistance I feel at the thought of my own chest growing is somewhat... well...
I may have been a 'child' in the past as a man, but I was never a 'woman.'

"May I borrow an empty cup?"
"If you wish."
"One more please."

The professor nodded.

"Just one more!"
"Ask for one more and you'll get tears."

Professor Bronze snapped her fingers in the air.
The chilling sound made me fear my skull would collapse and I'd die.

"Three is enough."

Perhaps fascinated with Oriental pottery, each of the three cups had different patterns engraved on them.

"Please put a coin in one paper cup and shuffle the other two cups. I'll turn around and not look."
"You want to play the shell game? Against a noble thief?"
"What do you think? Sounds fun, right?"
"You'll guess without looking. Can you do it?"
"Hee-hee. Just because I turn around doesn't mean I can't see, right?"

When I provoked her confidently, the professor took out a coin.

"This is a 777th anniversary commemorative coin issued by the Central Holy Empire in the year 777. Only 777 coins were issued, and it's known as a coin that brings luck."
"Wow! That's amazing."
"If you really guess correctly, I'll give you this coin."
"Really?"

This isn't an item you can just have because you want it.
Items related to luck are especially like that.
Most can only be obtained if you're truly lucky!

'I absolutely must have this!'

I turned around excitedly, and the professor casually said.

"Then I'll begin."
"Okay~"

The professor placed the coin in a cup with a clack.
Then she took the two cups without coins and shuffled them.

"Done."
"Hmm~ Where could it be~"

I'm trying to sound clever, but I already know.
I heard everything by 'sound'!

"You hid it here, right?"

The professor smiled with satisfaction.

"You're very good at this."
"Hehe. Give me the coin!"
"Yes. If you guess correctly two more times, the coin will be yours."
"What? That's not fair!"
"There it is."

In the corner of the table that the professor pointed to, a display appeared showing [Round 1: Challenge Successful][Round 2: ?][Round 3: ?]

"It was there from the beginning. Didn't you see it?"
"Urgh. If I get three right, you're not going to say it's actually best of 7, are you?"
"I won't torment you that much."
"Fine. Then it's the second round."

As I turned my head, the professor immediately said.

"Done."
"Huh?"

I didn't hear anything.
No sound of picking up cups, putting down the coin, setting down cups, or shuffling the two empty cups—it was completely 'silent.'
A truly soundless process that happened in an instant.
This professor, she unfairly used <Acceleration> with <Sound Dampening>!
I pouted resentfully as I turned my head, and the professor laughed.

"Try to guess. If you can still get it right."
"Hmph. Were you that tired of seeing a student's cute performance?"
"If you were merely trying to locate the coin through sound, I'd be a little disappointed."

It seems excessive to be so serious against a child, but I'm not asking for special treatment.
Saying "I'm a child, so go easy on me!" in a serious competition is something my veteran player ego won't allow.
Besides, 'hearing' was never the only way to find the coin.

"It's here, right?"
"You really are good at guessing."

It's a simple trick.
The cup hiding the coin stays in place, but the other two cups are shuffled once.
In this process, if you memorize a specific 'pattern' on the cups, you can tell which cup is in its original place and which cups have been shuffled.

For example, if the coin is in cup 1:
If cups 2 and 3 are shuffled, cup 1 remains in place, and cup 1's pattern can be found in position 1.

If the coin is in cup 2 or cup 3:
Cup 1 is shuffled with another cup, and its pattern can be found in a different position than before.

The fact that all three cups have different patterns was the first thing I confirmed when I received the cups!

The smile on the professor's face deepened.

"Next time, you should learn how to hide your gaze."
"...Did you notice?"
"Heh. Turn around now. We should start the final shell game."

I turned around, praying it wouldn't be what I feared, but once again I immediately heard, "Done."
Absurdly, three larger cups were covering the original three cups.

"Now. Find it. Which cup do you think contains the cup with the coin?"

This wicked professor used [Sound Dampening], [Speed], and [Covering] simultaneously in that short time.

"Surely you won't say that putting a cup over another cup is cheating? We never established such a rule."

Is this the dirty, unfair way of a noble thief?
The cruelty of taking a child's playful performance seriously.
I was chilled by the evil prowess of an academy professor.
