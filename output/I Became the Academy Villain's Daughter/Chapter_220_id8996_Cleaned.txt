# 220 - I Won't Cry

If Arcadia leaves.
If there's one less person to protect Oknodie.
Would it still be possible to protect Oknodie as before?

'That's absolutely impossible.'

Gisele was certain. More than half of the Dark Association members believed in Arcadia's reputation.
The moment she leaves, a massive hole would open in the information network surrounding Oknodie.
A hole so large that even trying to plug it with one's hands would be impossible.

'There's no time.'

He had to save Arcadia Sebiche.
For himself, for the Dark Association, and for Oknodie.
Before the formal petition arrives and Arcadia is finally expelled from the Academy.


* * *


After midterms ended, Arcadia had regained her leisure time.
She was enjoying her tea party as usual.

"Hoho. Today's treats are from my secret collection that I used to enjoy in my homeland."
"Wow! Secret collection!"
"Eight Flavor Cheese Buns. You can enjoy eight different sweet flavors in different ways with milk, eggs, and cheese."
"Wooow!"
"How does that sound?"
"I want to eat them!"
"Hoho. If you repeat 'Thank you, Sister Arcadia,' I'll gladly allow it."
"Sister Arcadia is the best! I love you so much!"

A satisfied smile spread across Arcadia's face.
This child was truly worth all her efforts.
Oknodie and the other followers would never know how many days she had lived frugally to save points to bring these snacks.

"Mmmmm~! The cheese flavor spreads throughout my mouth."
"Hoho. You recognize it, don't you? The true taste of the Eight Flavor Cheese Buns?"
"It's like biting into clouds of different colors!"
"Hoho. What an interesting expression. Even as a duchess, I have things to learn from Oknodie's understanding of fine cuisine."

As expected of a secret bakery creation from the Kingdom of Florence, its price was considerably high.
However, some people disparage it as a symbol of mixed culture without roots, calling it a pathetic food typical of the Kingdom of Florence.
Oknodie never showed any sign of such hurtful words or intentions.
Sincerely.
Purely.
She simply enjoyed the taste and was happy.
Arcadia's tired heart was truly healed by that purity.

'If only other children were as pure as this one.'

Oknodie didn't know how much disappointment and heartache Arcadia felt whenever her so-called followers said things that contradicted their actions.
She didn't need to know, and Arcadia would never let her know.
Purity is like an unstained blank page.
If you bring dirty things close, it gets stained, and eventually the clean white drawing paper becomes covered in filth.
You can wipe away the filth, but the stains that have seeped into the paper cannot be removed.
The heart becomes tainted.

'It's so strange.'

Oknodie should not have been able to remain pure.
The Wiheomhae Foundation.
If even half the rumors were true, it was an organization that exploited talented children through education bordering on abuse, used those who didn't meet expectations even more harshly, and sold rebels to brothels and slave markets.
Its malevolence was no different from demon clans who spread evil while pledging loyalty to the Demon King.
Despite not even being an organization of mages, they had a reputation exceeding them—perhaps they too were an evil organization under the control of mages.

The foundation's greatest masterpiece.
Top scholarship student Oknodie.
How could she possibly maintain her purity?
Arcadia had been watching her all along but still couldn't find the answer.
A child who knew more than she appeared to, yet simply wanted to be cute.
A child who could become anything she wanted if she put her mind to it.
What could be more healing than the gentle appearance of such a child?

She wanted to cherish this time a little more.
She wanted to enjoy tea parties forever.
She wanted to tempt her with new treats like raising a small animal, feed her, and tame her little by little.
Arcadia, who had been gradually realizing her usual gloomy desires without Oknodie noticing.
An uninvited guest burst onto the terrace, interrupting her time with Oknodie.

"It's terrible, Your Grace!"
"...Disturbing our tea time is too much, Titosoga."

This better not be nothing.
I'll give you a good scolding if it is.
Despite Arcadia's reproachful glare, Titosoga paid no mind.
Honestly, he was scared, but attending Professor Sadako's lectures twice a week had given even the biggest coward some courage.
But it wasn't just because of increased courage.
The fact that this cowardly Titosoga was holding back tears while saying he couldn't freeze in fear was a sign that this was no ordinary situation.

"There's a rumor going around that ledgers containing your family's dirty secrets will be made public. An urgent message from your homeland has arrived ordering an immediate severance of relations with you, Your Grace!"

Spring and autumn always end suddenly.
Summer arrives before you know it, and winter comes just as abruptly.
Like seasons scattered by the wind, the emotions that had been floating high sank in an instant.
Blood freezes.
Hands and feet turn ice cold.
A cold winter arrived in Arcadia's mind as she had been enjoying her tea party in the flower garden.



* * *



There is a communication room in the Academy.
Unlike visitations, it's a facility that exists to deliver information when there's a problem with a student's guardian or supporting power outside the Academy.
As a facility prepared for emergencies, the cost is enormous.
It would be better to pay a little extra to send someone for a visit.
Nevertheless, students occasionally use the communication room when news is so urgent that there's no time for a visit.
In that sense, the communication room, which would normally be empty, was now crowded with numerous students—a sign that something serious had happened.

"Everyone? If you've heard anything about my family, please let me know."

To Arcadia's anxious question, followers who would normally have answered immediately now looked at her and each other with cold eyes.
This was the true face of those who had been busy hiding their emotions behind smiling masks.

"I'm sorry, Your Grace."
"I have urgent business, so..."

She had been abandoned.
Since nothing was confirmed yet, her followers maintained minimal courtesy with honorifics and greetings.
But even from their attitude, Arcadia could tell.
You're finished now.
Your downfall is near.
Was playing duchess fun?
As if saying that, as if telling her that a different life from before would begin, they dispersed with subtle sneers on their faces.
There was not a trace of the respect or deference due to a faction boss who had protected the provincial faction from the imperial faction throughout their Academy life.

"What terrible people!"
"Don't worry, Your Grace. We're different from those traitors!"

The eyes of those who clung to her side and offered comforting words revealed cunning expectations.
Their greed was too shallow and light to deceive the duchess's eyes.

"Ah, I, I..."

As she stammered, unable to accept this sudden situation, someone called to her from behind.

"Arcadia. Someone's looking for you."
"Jigoku? What business do you have with me..."

Their relationship wasn't good.
Jigoku knew.
He knew that the reason pirates ran rampant in the Kingdom of Florence was because of the Sebiche ducal family.
At the same time, he knew that Duchess Arcadia was someone who faced her family's karma without turning away.
Privateers who fed off the government were still pirates in the end.
If the Sebiche ducal family hadn't existed, Jigoku wouldn't have had his livelihood shaken and wouldn't have become a pirate.
Arcadia belonged to that enemy family, yet was also treated as an oddity and heretic within her family for considering her family's past shameful.
It was natural for them to maintain a distant relationship.

"Your business partner with the squinty eyes is looking for you. He said there's nothing to gain by staying here and to find him as soon as possible."

In the midst of the Sebiche ducal family's crisis, Jigoku could have mocked and ridiculed her, but unlike the followers who quickly changed their attitudes, he responded with a businesslike manner.
He was simply delivering a message he'd been asked to convey.
That straightforwardness actually helped Arcadia regain her composure.

"Your Grace. We've heard some news too."
"Our family will protect you, Your Grace."

Girls making sweet, pleasant sounds.
Arcadia easily saw through their aim to absorb her position in the Academy and gently shook off the hands supporting her arms.
This was temptation.
Saying they would protect her in her predicament.
In exchange, give everything you have to us.
It was as cruel as relatives approaching an orphaned child to steal their inheritance, exploiting emotions.

"Thank you. I won't forget your consideration."
"Your Grace? Where are you going? Please, let's discuss countermeasures with us..."

Arcadia gently shook off those who looked down on her and pulled at her arms, speaking in a calm tone.

"The Sebiche name may be heavy, but the name Arcadia is not light."
"...!"

With that, she followed Jigoku with dignity, showing the strength of the human being rather than the family.
Someone who depended on family prestige couldn't have been this dignified.
But Arcadia was different.
She knew.
She knew of a child who could bravely navigate life without relying on someone else's authority.

'Compared to the unfortunate past Oknodie experienced, this is nothing. I can't cry over something like this.'
