# First Friend

Max and Jessie were quite pleased and intrigued by the appearance of a pretty child their age.

"Hey, why were you hiding in the ball pit?"
"Did you not want to play with the other kids?"
"Idiot, girls are shy! She was probably embarrassed."
"Ha, shy? More like a wild girl."
"What? I told you I'd teach you a lesson if you talk like that!"
"Ow, stop! You hit my bone!"

The children started bickering among themselves.
I'm envious.
I thought absentmindedly as I watched them.

"Are you two friends?"
"We're forced to meet because of our parents. Our families are neighboring baronies in our hometown."
"You're the one who says meaner things..."

The pair seemed to get along well—Max pouting with her lips jutting out after getting upset so quickly, and Jessie teasing her with "What, do you like me or something?"

"Sorry for startling you earlier. When I go to unfamiliar places, I prefer to hide..."
"Oh, I get that feeling. You want to run away when strangers bother you, right?"
"Are you also on your way to take the entrance exam?"

The two little ones tilted their heads in unison.

"Us?"
"We're only ten years old?"
"Aren't you?"
"How could a ten-year-old take the entrance exam?"
"It's difficult even for twenty-year-olds."

A look of disbelief appeared in the children's eyes.

"Are you on your way to take the entrance exam?"
"For real?"

I feel like I've made some kind of mistake.
When I carefully nodded, exclamations of "Woooow" and "That's awesome" followed.
This is a familiar look I've seen a long time ago.
It's exactly like the admiring gazes I received when I had rare stickers after the remastered Pokémon stickers were released and I collected them in my notebook.

"What's your name?"
"Oknodie."
"That's a weird name."
"How can a person be named Oknodie?"

My thoughts exactly.

"My brother is going to take the entrance exam when he's fifteen. I hope Oknodie passes instead of him."
"Why?"
"So I can make fun of him for losing to a ten-year-old."

I don't want encouragement for that reason.

Despite their questionable motives for supporting me, I found myself enjoying the conversation with the two children.
Before I knew it, the sun had set and it had become late at night, time passing without me noticing.

"Oh, we should get going now."
"Hey Max. Hurry and bring your little sibling."

Max picked up a child who seemed barely over a year old, who had been nodding off drowsily, as a device rang like a café vibration bell.

"Abubu"
"Hey, put that down. You can't take the ball pit ball."
"Abububaba!!"
"Ow, don't push with your hands!"
"Pfft. You're terrible at taking care of kids."

Max's face turned red at Jessie's mockery as she tried to take the ball from the baby, but the child desperately pushed back against Max while hugging the ball tightly.

"It's like an attachment object."

Jessie explained from the side.

"My mom said that children over 12 months old have attachment objects that they pour their attention and love into instead of their parents. Since there aren't any dolls or objects like that here, it seems the baby has made the ball pit ball its attachment object."
"Jessie, you're smart."
"Tch, compliments won't get you anything."

If she had a tail, it would be wagging furiously.
But that ball pit ball felt strange somehow.
I felt oddly drawn to it and wanted to check it out.

"Should I take care of it?"
"The ball? Why?"
"I still need to stay here longer."
"Are you here to babysit too?"
"Leape said she'd come if I waited."
"Leape?"
"That's my maid's name."
"She must be cute. But the baby is really possessive, will you be okay?"
"We'll see."

I made eye contact with the baby.

"Hello, baby?"
"Uwuma? Kyakya!"

The baby's eyes brightened and reached for my golden hair.
As it grabbed my hair, the ball pit ball naturally fell to the ground.
I easily got the ball, but I think a few strands of my hair were pulled out before Max managed to pull the baby away.

"Sorry. Our youngest is quite rough, isn't she?"
"It's fine. This much doesn't really hurt."
"You're very mature. Is that why you're taking the entrance exam? We're the same height, but you seem completely like an adult. If it were me, I'd be too annoyed to even smile."

That's because I am an adult.
Anyway, after seeing off Max, the baby, and Jessie, I picked up the ball pit ball that the baby had been holding.
The ball was shiny with saliva and felt oddly different in weight from the other ball pit balls.
To be precise, it felt weightless.
It felt like something else in the form of a ball pit ball.

This feels familiar.
I know what this kind of object is.

'Isn't this a Stat Stone?'

It's possible.
The Kids Zone was a place I never entered during my muscular male character playthrough.
It wouldn't be surprising if there were Stat Stones I didn't know about.

'I'll know if I eat it.'

I wiped the saliva off the ball with my sleeve, opened my mouth wide, and swallowed it in one gulp.
Gulp.
The swallowing was smooth.
My guess was right.
This feeling, it was definitely a Stat Stone.

'I wonder which stat increased?'

Once I enter the Academy, I'll be able to see my status window through Academy magic even without the player's status window function.
I want to enroll quickly.
As I was thinking this and turned my head, I met eyes with Jessie, who was covering her mouth with both hands.

"I, I just came back because I left something..."

Jessie was holding a small pouch in her hand.
In short, the timing was bad.

"Oknodie...? Why did you swallow the ball pit ball?!"
"Ah, ahh, this, this is..."
"We need to go to the healer, the healing teacher right away!"

Jessie grabbed my hand and pulled me along, grunting with effort.
My body with an estimated strength of 29 wouldn't normally be dragged like this, but fearing Jessie might hurt herself if I resisted, I relaxed and let myself be pulled all the way to the emergency room.
What if Leape comes back?
Well, I can just go back quickly.
It should be fine, right?


* * *


Leape, who had found a way to infiltrate the gambling den, returned to the Kids Zone.
Seeing the empty ball pit, she realized.
Her lady's chronic hide-and-seek habit had started again.
This time, she seemed to have hidden quite cleverly, but Leape was also an assassination instructor.
She was confident in finding hidden people.

"Hehe. I can hear your breathing, my lady. Are you perhaps hiding behind the curtain?"

Swish

"I guess not... You wouldn't make the trite choice of hiding in the wardrobe either, would you?"

Creak

"If you've hidden under the bed, taking inspiration from this being the Kids Zone sleeping room, I might even praise you."

Slide

"Today, my lady's hiding skills are quite impressive... This will be a good match. Hehe."

The solo game of hide-and-seek in the empty Kids Zone continued for quite some time.


* * *


The emergency room set up for emergency patients was equipped with various facilities and specialized healers.
There were experts in every field, from priests who used healing magic to doctors who performed surgical procedures, and healers who treated symptoms of various diseases.

'In the game, if you got injured and went straight to the emergency room, you'd be assigned a random doctor who might give you strange treatment or even completely incorrect treatment.'

A skilled priest would realign bones, but a lower-ranked priest wouldn't do that.
Knowing the risks, from my perspective, the emergency room was a shortcut to ruining your character, and Ezio, the lower-ranked priest on night duty, was a tormentor of broken bones and an approaching horror.

"You said your friend swallowed a ball, right?"
"Yes! Please do something quickly!"
"Hmm. You seem to be at least 5 years old, have you registered? Please place your palm on this mana board."

What's this? Is this a new feature not in the game?
When I placed my palm on the stone slab that looked like a plaster board, my hand sank in and left an impression.
Intricate magic circles rose above the plaster board, and familiar symbols like 'communication,' 'recording,' 'analysis,' and so on appeared in sequence, forming geometric patterns.
What kind of magic is this?

"This can't be!"
"What's wrong, doctor?"

Is this some kind of power level measuring magic?
Perhaps my combat power is actually 530,000 or something?

"To think that someone of this age is an unregistered stateless person not recorded in the International Identity Registration Magic Repository!"

It wasn't power level measurement, but identity verification magic.

"Whaaaat?! Could there be some mistake, doctor? Oknodie boarded an airship with a butler and secretary, and is even preparing for the Academy entrance exam!"

Hearing Jessie's explanation, the doctor stood up with a chuckle.

"Yes, it's more likely that the relay device malfunctioned and the search didn't work properly. Surely there can't be parents who wouldn't register a child nearly 10 years old."

The doctor left the room laughing, and shortly after, doctors with serious expressions could be seen through the transparent glass corridor outside, engaged in conversation.
Several nurses also came out, murmuring and glancing this way, then giving awkward waves when our eyes met.

"Hey, Oknodie. Did you really not register?"
"I don't know!"
"Huh? You've never used the identity registration magic circle that gives you a free talent assessment just by registering?"
"I don't know what I don't know. Nobody ever taught me about this kind of magic."
"Not even the butler or maid who came with you?"
"No."

Jessie's expression became as serious as the doctors outside.

[Ahem. This is an announcement.]
[A guardian is looking for a child. A girl about 10 years old with blonde hair, approximately 133cm tall, with a thin build, wearing an adventurer's set of women's clothes and a ribbon headband.]
[If you find this child, please hand her over to the nearest staff member.]

Oh no.

"Leape must be looking for me. I need to go back."
"Don't go."
"Jessie?"
"My parents told me that families who don't even register their children's identities aren't normal families. Your family isn't normal, Oknodie."
"Hmm."

I think so too.
But does it really matter?
They give me support funds and train me.
It's not easy to find a Papa who helps this much in the Random Papa event.
Personally, I'd rate him as one of the best Papas I've experienced so far.

"Papa isn't bad. I'm grateful to Papa. I'm always thankful to Jonna and Leape, whom Papa sent, for helping me with my training."
"Training? What kind of training do they do without even doing basic identity registration?"
"Like sword dueling? Sprint intervals where you alternate between running at full speed and walking? Shooting arrows at squirrels tied to targets or rabbits trapped in cages?"

Jessie's face turned pale.
Her focus trembled as if she were looking at an abused child from an inhumane household.

"Let's run away."
"What?"
"If they catch you, you'll be in big trouble again."

A sense of mission blazed in Jessie's eyes.
