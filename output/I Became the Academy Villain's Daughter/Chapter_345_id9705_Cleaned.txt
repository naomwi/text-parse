# <345 - Administrative Department Lecture Served Raw>

The Destroyer's story was shocking.
But as Ishtar mulled over his words, another person's story came to mind.

"That can't be right! The hero has so much to do in the future. Diligently killing regional bosses that appear during local events, killing the Demon King, and even killing the Emperor!"
"What exactly do you think a hero is...?"

Day 6 of the desert island auction.
Just before the sixth item, <Patron Forces of the Hero Party>, was presented, Oknodie had shared this chilling vision of the future during their brief conversation.
A future Ishtar had never even dared to imagine—that she, as the hero, would have to kill the Emperor.
It had seemed preposterous.
The Emperor and the Empire were the hero's patrons.
If not immediately, they would become potential backers providing the greatest support as she achieved more accomplishments for the Empire.
In reality, the hero had received help from officials and knights appointed by the Emperor until her enrollment at the Academy.
Why would she ever need to oppose such an Emperor?

Now, after hearing the Destroyer's story.
That firm trust had been shattered.

"The Emperor designed the atrocities of the past...?"
"He wouldn't even need to. The Empire has many soldiers seeking military service and no shortage of strategists who have become staff officers. Simply put, nothing happens in the Capital without the Emperor's approval."
"That can't be true. The Emperor of the Empire has been a longtime ally of heroes throughout history...!"

The Destroyer nodded.

"The Emperor is indeed the hero's ally."
"Then why would he frame the Revolutionist?!"
"And didn't he use his ally as a sacrifice?"
"...!"
"He 'usefully consumed' troublesome refugees and cleaned up undesirables in a series of processes that provided achievements for the hero. Since neither the Emperor nor the hero ever lose in this game, couldn't one say he fulfilled his responsibility as an ally?"
"That, such actions, how could...!"
"You mean how could they be tolerated?"

The Destroyer easily completed the inexperienced hero's unfinished sentence as if pulling words from his own mind.
A cold sneer formed on his lips, like the chilly wind that happened to blow by.

"The Emperor is the hero's ally and the father of his people. But not all parents are good parents to their children. Perhaps someday he won't be for the hero either. What Nyarlathotep did was closer to giving a new headache to the Emperor, who might become the hero's enemy, and buying time."
"..."
"At least that's what I wanted to believe back when I first realized the truth."
"Then now, what are your thoughts now, senior? About the previous generation hero, and about the Emperor."
"Try to guess. Your answer on the day the second semester ends will be the answer to the second semester final exam."

Though the lecture had ended, Ishtar and Hestia couldn't easily walk away.

"Ah, a red-spotted butterfly!"

Only Oknodie cheerfully ran off, pulling a butterfly net from her backpack to chase after the butterfly.
As if she clearly knew what she was chasing and why she was collecting it, enjoying it all.


* * *

Who determines the Three Great Evils?
The Empire's standards do.

'If classified as evil by the Empire, then the world calls it evil too, but the Empire's evil doesn't truly become the world's enemy... This lecture is too profound for first-years.'

After all the students and Professor Destroyer had left, Professor Bronze de Estrada finally took a step.
Her movements were filled with caution, like someone who had been holding their breath in front of a beast and could finally breathe.

'Yet this lecture gave me certainty.'

The Destroyer's lecture gave new insight to the novice hero and the Foundation's scholarship student.
That their true enemy might not be the Three Great Evils but the Emperor of the Empire.
It shifts focus as if protecting the Foundation Chairman, who is a member of the Three Great Evils and head of the Foundation.

'Destroyer. Is that why a man of your caliber sided with the Foundation?'

The Dragon Headmaster would surely say this was "interesting."
Asking for help wouldn't change anything.
What would she even request?
To recognize the Destroyer's true colors and reject him?
To stop the Foundation from checking the Emperor?
Or perhaps to ask him to assassinate the Emperor?
All futile hopes.
On the other hand, looking at Oknodie, she realized.
That child knew everything all along.
Had she heard the whole story from the Chairman?
How much did the Chairman know about the Empire and the hero?
What was the relationship between the Chairman and the Destroyer?

'To arouse such curiosity in me, you really are a bad man.'

Just as bad adults attract bad children, bad men attract bad women.
Professor Bronze decided.
It was time to be a bad woman again after a long while.


* * *

Recently, Mob has been feeling considerable anxiety.
The frequency of receiving Oknodie's training has been decreasing.
It's not that her interest has completely disappeared.
But it's become harder to find Oknodie hanging upside down from a window to tap his shoulder, jumping out of bushes with a "boo!", or popping her head out of a suddenly appearing hole in the ground.

"So that's why you brought me to this Administrative Department lecture that's not even in my field?"
"Stop pretending you don't care. Jaku, you're just as concerned about Oknodie."
"...That's true. I do owe her a lot."

Was it really so disappointing that the usually playful Oknodie now only occasionally asked about training progress instead of playing pranks?
While thinking his rival's determination to train harder like a candle with a lit wick was foolish, Jaku found himself partly agreeing.

'Getting Oknodie's attention isn't easy. Repaying the debt is even harder.'

Everyone who boarded the cruise ship owed her their lives.
The benefits gained from the ship and from the Chairman's mansion were enormous.
Just the +5 Reinforced Net Gun hidden in his chest was an incredible weapon that could trap an enemy in a net with just a pull of the trigger, making them flounder while being stabbed from outside.
Those who accepted Oknodie's invitation as "friends" each possessed at least one such treasure.
Conversely, mediocre treasures did nothing to repay their debt to Oknodie.

"I found this on my way here."
"Ah, I already collected a Rolling Snail. But I'll take it anyway. I'll go to the Production Department and exchange it for another monster! Hehe."
"......"

To think that rare collectibles that could be found by chance on the road weren't even enough!

So they help Oknodie, who is struggling with her heavy course load, by attending lectures.
This <Theory of Functional Monopoly> was the lecture chosen by Gisele and Arcadia to help with their plan.

-Don't overdo it. Oknodie might not need as much help as we think.

Mob was also concerned about what Hestia had said when they met during dinner before entering the lecture hall.
Of course, Mob ignored her words.
I became her disciple first.
I was the only one receiving teachings from Oknodie.
Hestia was one of the students who had taken Oknodie away from him.

"She's just saying that to separate me from Oknodie."
"...Stupid fool. A man who only obsesses like that won't end well. You have a proper justification, so stop saying such things."
"Justification?"

While not hiding his contempt, Jaku offered advice with the complex feeling that he disliked seeing his rival in such a pitiful state even more.

"The Administrative Department has different students than other departments. The Knight Department teaches military power, the Magic Department teaches magic, the Production Department teaches production skills, and the Adventure Department teaches various other functions. But the Administrative Department belongs nowhere."
"Then why does Oknodie bother coming to Administrative Department lectures?"
"The reality of official positions is that children of high-ranking officials eventually take their predetermined seats. The students in the Administrative Department are those who would rather enjoy their share of assets comfortably than struggle in other demanding departments."

Like that one over there.
Jaku pointed to a handsome young man who was receiving admiring glances from many female students.
Mob asked indifferently after seeing his face.

"Who's that?"
"...Are you serious?"

The male student who was pretending not to eavesdrop on their conversation also frowned.
Before Jaku could answer, one of the young man's followers raised his voice.

"How dare you disrespect Prince Hector, the rightful heir to the throne of the Kingdom of Troy? Are you out of your mind? There's a limit to ignoring a prince!"
"Enough, Deiphobus. Berating someone for not recognizing a prince of a small country is embarrassing in this vast Academy. Besides, as the Dark Princess's Black Knight, he probably doesn't care about princes of small countries anyway."

Though Hector pretended to be mediating, his insinuation that the other was a prince of a minor country made other students' expressions sour.
Even Administrative Department students shared the perception that they could coast through on status alone without entering the more challenging departments.
That's why Mob's behavior, which seemed to mock their weakness, appeared distasteful to them.

"...Jaku. What should I do?"
"Don't do anything. You won't have any chance of winning against a royal child trained in proper rhetoric if you try to argue while stumbling over your words."
"That's right! Arguments are for people who are good at them!"
"Oknodie!"
"I don't understand why everyone is doing such a nice thing by taking an extra lecture because of me. Hehe."

Nothing could be more reassuring than the small girl Oknodie who appeared with a mischievous smile.
Unlike Mob who brightened at her arrival, Prince Hector of Troy's expression had already hardened.

"What did the prince do during the break?"
"I expanded my influence and trained. We also cleared a dungeon as a group during the break."
"Is that all? Aha."

Oknodie nodded as if she understood.

"So you were playing house in a low-rank dungeon!"
"Why you little—!"

As soon as Hector's follower Deiphobus drew his sword, a thorn flew between the desks and struck the blade, sending it flying into the air.

"Quiet♡ Don't disturb the Imperial Princess's break time, you loyal dog of a country bumpkin prince♡"
"Gasp! Im-Imperial Second Princess Mesugakki...!"

Mesugakki, saying she had no interest in weaklings, didn't even glance at him and instead gave Oknodie a provocative look.

"What wind blew you into a lecture that only nobles attend~?"
"Why is a Magic Department student like you here, Miss Incompetent Princess?"
"Because one can never have too much cultural education♡"
"Same here!"

Hector became like a mouse before a snake amid the psychological warfare between these two top-tier first-year students.
To think that two heavyweight first-years would clash in what he thought would be an easy credit Administrative Department elective.
He had a feeling that this lecture, contrary to the idea that it would be advantageous for earning credits and points since Administrative Department students didn't care much about grades, might become extremely difficult.
