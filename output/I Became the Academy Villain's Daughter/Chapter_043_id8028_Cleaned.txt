# 43 - Today is the Day of the Power Outage

<Training Facility>
<Arena 3>

The arena was packed with Group A apprentice female knights wearing dolphin pants and Group B apprentice female knights covered in pantyhose. This was the first confrontation between the two groups who had been warily keeping their distance from each other.

"Oknodie told you not to call us?"
"Yes. That's exactly what she said."
"That little mouse..."

Son O-cheon glared fiercely at the arena.

"Did she not want to show us beating up an imperial noble?"
"...Isn't it the opposite? The opponent is an imperial noble who's mastered mana cultivation and mana tempering techniques. And it's Lotto, who's famous as a martial prodigy after receiving combat tutoring from martial artists."

Unlike Isabel, who found it absurd, Gisele raised his hand in agreement with Son O-cheon's opinion.

"Ms. Isabel doesn't understand. Miss Oknodie is stronger than we think. She won a sword duel against a senior examiner and earned a Platinum Ticket."
"Platinum Ticket?"
"It's a ticket that allows the holder to automatically advance to the next stage even if they fail once in the advanced class entrance exam. It was even a double-number ticket."
"The admission is already over, so why does that matter? Numbers weren't even mentioned in the entrance exam."

Gisele had received a triple-number Platinum Ticket from Senior Examiner Michael. Because of this, he had an interest in the numbers and after persistently extracting information from instructors, he got his answer.

"There are a total of 9,999 Platinum Tickets, numbered from 1 to 9999."
"With only two thousand students admitted, 9,999 tickets? Isn't that too common?"
"And once a ticket is issued, it won't be reissued until the student with that number dies. This ticket is no exaggeration to call a guarantee of talent."
"!!"
"Do you understand? The meaning of a double-number Platinum Ticket. Oknodie has been recognized as having talent equivalent to a two-digit ranking among all students in history."

Moreover, the ticket Michael possessed was numbers 15 and 143. He was recognized as having talent ranked 15th in history, close to single digits. Lotto's reputation as a martial prodigy means nothing in front of the shining guarantee of a Platinum Ticket.

"Now think again about what Mr. Son O-cheon meant by not wanting to show us beating up an imperial noble. Then you'll understand."

Only after hearing Gisele's explanation did Isabel comprehend the hidden meaning, like a warrior who had achieved enlightenment.

"She was afraid. Afraid that we wouldn't stay by her side after seeing her unchildlike strength."
"For a child raised as an assassination weapon in a noble family, the sight of adults fearing the child within her probably isn't an unfamiliar experience. She might even have 'practical experience.'"

She fears being hated because of her unchildlike strength. She doesn't want her comrades to watch the duel. Both are heartbreaking. Oknodie's terrible past too. And her mindset that might have stemmed from experiences of not being able to trust adults.

"...Hey, Gisele. Perhaps the reason why the noble family allows us to stay by Oknodie's side..."
"Please keep the rest as just thoughts. It's not something to be carelessly spoken aloud. There might be eyes planted anywhere in the Academy."

The darkness surrounding Oknodie was deeper than imagined. Isabel felt anger before fear.

Trust no one except us. Weak people die quickly. Show just a little strength and they fear her. They feel disconnected from her unchildlike demeanor. And they betray her heart.

Through experience, they shatter her expectations of relationships. And they control Oknodie's body and mind. Isabel feared the darkness of the noble family. She got goosebumps from the shadowy mastermind behind it all. But at the same time, she felt defiant.

'They underestimated us.'

Wondering if we could consider such an unchildlike child as a comrade and stay with her. How long we could stay by this child's side without leaving or hurting her. Anger welled up against the faceless, nameless mastermind.

'I'm staying with her to repay a debt; there's no way I'd turn my back on her.'

Isabel thought. Don't underestimate people. The adventurers of the Esonia Adventure Group are the kind who always accomplish what they set their minds to. And now, she had made up her mind. Another goal following her debt repayment to Oknodie.

'If the day comes when I face you who control Oknodie, I'll throw a punch in your face.'

As her resolve settled, the commotion in the stands died down. Oknodie and Lotto. The duel between the two advanced class students had begun.


* *


The instructor serving as referee spoke while manipulating a mana board.

"When the safety guard activates, the duel stops immediately. The winner will be determined by decision."
"Yes."
"You're lucky. Your bones won't break. But I'll still make you suffer enough."

Lotto stood opposite me, glaring with sadistic eyes. The signal announcing the duel sounded. Instead of rushing in as I expected, Lotto wiggled his fingers as if giving me a handicap.

'How amateurish.'

I could tell by looking at his eyes. It wasn't an attitude born from confidence. It was because he was conscious of the audience's gaze. Having entered an inglorious duel with a child, he probably thought he needed to show this much concession to save face. Losing would be humiliating, but winning would also bring criticism, so he was trying to salvage some dignity.

"Mr. Lotto. Did you know?"
"What? Getting scared now that we're about to fight? Going to beg for mercy?"
"On the sixth day of dormitory move-in, there's a power outage in the training building. When a power outage occurs, the safety guard stops working."

Lotto frowned.

"Playing shallow tricks. Did Group A give up the top spot to a kid because they were intimidated by such wordplay?"

It must be hard to believe. Predicting an accident that hasn't happened yet. If I didn't know, I wouldn't believe it either. But it's unsettling, right?

No matter what happens, the safety guard ensures only minor injuries. Perhaps because the possibility of losing that safety guarantee left no room to worry about honor or dignity, Lotto rushed in, taking steps.

Tswat-!

His body was light and agile. His kick had weight behind it and was powerful. His skills were excellent, showing the benefits of his tutoring.

But my weapon is a "sword." The range of a sword is longer than that of an unarmed fighter. The initiative, the advantage, is mine.

Kang!

A sharp sound that seemed impossible from the collision of sword and leg rang out. The audience stirred in surprise at Lotto's strength, but Lotto himself didn't look pleased.

"Hurts more than you thought, right?"
"You... that strength, could it be."

Yes. Have you noticed now?

"Are you a half-orc as your name suggests...?"
"I learned mana tempering techniques!!"

Ah. In the moment I got heated, Lotto's leg fiercely grazed the side of my face. His footwork might be unknown, but his provocation skills are no joke.

"I thought so. For someone so strong, you're not bulky like Hestia, that mercenary."

Lotto asked exploratively.

"Did you also learn mana cultivation techniques?"
"What do you think?"

Mana tempering techniques that allow one to maintain a beautiful physique while exerting strong force by compressing muscles with mana, and mana cultivation techniques that greatly enhance mana collection speed.

These aren't exclusive to nobles.

Nobles with practical experience and some high-ranking mercenaries. Descendants of martial families passing down secret martial arts. Hidden experts inheriting sole-transmission secrets.

There are various mana tempering and cultivation techniques in the world. And the Academy collects and teaches these techniques to students. As a player who has attended the Academy thousands of times, I naturally know these techniques. I can even research and master frequently acquired skills through experience alone.

Some techniques are too advanced for freshmen. That's why it's good to have a noble papa. If the Academy asks where I learned this, I can blame it on papa. And if papa asks? Then I can blame it on the Academy. Still, it's best not to get caught.

Until now, I've been restraining myself because of the butler's watchful eyes. I've been using it secretly, briefly, and stealthily. Lotto, facing me directly, noticed, but it's fine as long as the spectators don't know, right?

"Isn't your leg starting to go numb?"
"I'm still fine!"
"That's good. If your leg suddenly weakens and coincidentally the power outage happens at the same time, disabling the safety guard... Clang. Your leg might get cut off, you know?"

Perhaps imagining the specific description, Lotto's face turned pale.

"Do, do you think the Academy would let you get away with such a thing?"
"I'd be disciplined, sure. But how severe could it be? A pre-admission student who hasn't fully grasped the Academy's official rules. And it's a 'mistake' caused by a power outage during a duel."

Lotto's leg tensed excessively. Not missing the opportunity when his movements became stiff, I pressed the attack, and Lotto was instantly put on the defensive.

"Don't worry too much. Even if it really gets cut off, it can be reattached, right? The Academy has excellent treatment facilities and high-level resident healers."

Though trauma would remain. The hesitation to extend one's leg in crucial moments would be as good as the end of a martial artist's life. But that's self-inflicted. If he had been kind-hearted from the start, he wouldn't have picked a fight with Hestia, and wouldn't have had to duel me who stepped up to protect her.

Paat!

Just then, the awaited power outage arrived. In the darkness, I heard Lotto make a frightened sound, "Hik." Despite hanging out with an arrogant bunch, his scared sound is cute. But that doesn't mean I'll go easy on him. Didn't he say he'd make me suffer?

Swish

I swing my sword, intending to take off one of his legs. The sword cutting through the darkness was unexpectedly deflected with a jolt.

Kang!

No, how did he block this? Was he hiding his skills? Surprised, I changed the sword's path and swung three more times, but all were blocked. A foot with heavy weight behind it pushed back my persistently probing sword with a thud.

Pabat Pat

After rotating my body 720 degrees in the air and still not fully dissipating the impact, I lowered my stance as if drawing a circle with my legs immediately after landing, only then channeling the force into the ground.

My arms still tingling from the impact. Suddenly I felt pressure. In this posture, if the next strike comes aiming for this opening... Could I block it? As I tensed, imagining someone rushing at me in the darkness, the lighting magic in the room came back on.

'Huh?'

I was surprised when I saw my opponent. The skilled fighter I had been pressing in the darkness. It wasn't Lotto. It was Hestia who had rushed into the arena.

"That's enough. I can't let a child who stepped up to help make a big mistake."
"Sigh. How deflating. If it were me, I would have pretended not to see."

Perhaps realizing he had almost lost a leg, Lotto sat down, drained of strength.

"Match stopped! Due to the safety guard malfunction caused by the power outage, this duel is suspended!"

With the instructor's shout, the duel ended inconclusively.
