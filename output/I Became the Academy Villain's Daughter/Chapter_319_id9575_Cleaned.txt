<319 - A Special Promise>

Ferguson was not in a position to simply say "Yes, sir" and leave Oknodie behind.

"I can't allow that. You sent her to the Academy because you couldn't properly understand Oknodie's true nature, but now your thinking has changed. Do you think I don't know your scheme to raise that child within the Foundation's embrace?"
"I believe a child who was attacked by an Imperial professor needs time to heal her emotional wounds. If this fact becomes known, parents concerned about student safety won't only be at our Foundation."
"Are you threatening the Academy now?"
"Don't forget that the justification lies with the Foundation."

This wasn't a battle he could win with words.
But using force would mean failing to protect the other children and putting himself in danger.
He needed to find a solution through peaceful dialogue while there was still willingness to resolve things amicably.

"Then I'll speak with the child directly. Whether she wants to stay here of her own will, and what schedule she plans for returning. As an Academy professor, you understand my concern for a student, don't you? If you refuse even this, the Academy cannot simply let it go."

While resolving things with violence might seem faster and simpler, the most dangerous and troublesome blade often comes not from the hand but from the tongue.
Perhaps the Foundation wasn't willing to risk all-out war with the Academy either, as Jonna couldn't refuse this request.

"Follow me."

Training Tower.
Upon entering the facility, Ferguson felt an enormous magical field permeating the entire structure.
It was a force comparable to the massive magical field of the Dragon Headmaster that dominated the entire Academy grounds—something any professor entering the Academy would inevitably sense.

<Continental-grade Mana Boundary>
<Do not defy the will of the Lord - non obedite voluntati Domini>

The Dragon Headmaster, who could become a living god himself and violate the laws of the world, established an island in the middle of the sea and inscribed his own laws upon his territory.
Thus the Academy became a domain that even the 12 Good Gods, 12 Evil Gods, and the One God Sophemia could not easily intrude upon.
The pressure felt in this tower was no less imposing.

'Were the rumors about the Chairman's identity true?'

The location was also significant.
The Training Tower, created for the hero party.
A training facility created by the One God Sophemia for her chosen true heroes.
The Chairman and Foundation secretly occupying such a facility.
Perhaps the Chairman wasn't an illegal occupant but someone fully qualified to use this facility.

Whoosh.

An elevator—technology that spread from ancient facilities to all of humanity.
A convenience of civilization. As the doors opened sideways, Oknodie and the Chairman appeared.
It was an anxiety-inducing sight, like watching a small, fragile jewel rolling around in the hands of a massive demon, ready to break at any moment.
If you can't have it, wouldn't it be better to destroy it?
Seeing Ferguson's uneasiness, Oknodie made an openly disgusted face.

"Ugh. It's really him."
"Really?"
"The evil serial enhancer. The main culprit behind upperclassmen's bankruptcy!"
"What are you suddenly talking about?"
"Don't play dumb. Professor Ferguson, you run the enhancement service for upperclassmen!"

Some loose-lipped upperclassman must have blabbed to Oknodie, who was just a freshman.
Thinking that his first impression had been thoroughly ruined, Ferguson frowned deeply.
Do you even know what kind of person you're clinging to?
He desperately wanted to scold her.
The Chairman, seeming to understand Ferguson's inner thoughts, lightly shrugged and smiled.

"I heard you have something to say to my child. Since we can't keep a busy person like you for long, let's keep this brief."
"...Oknodie. If you miss the cruise ship back, there's no telling when you'll be able to return to the Academy. It's not too late—come back with me."
"No way!"
"Is it because of the false rumors seniors spread about me? If there's a misunderstanding, let's resolve it through conversation."
"I was suspicious from the moment you gave Titoso a +5 lamp stand. You showed the power of enhancement through early access, planning to fleece everyone once they become upperclassmen, right?"
"Why would you say such a terrible thing? I swear to the gods, I've never plotted such an evil plan."
"Hmph. I won't fall for it! You're mistaken if you think I can be easily fooled like Titoso!"

Is the rumor already spreading that such a difficult child is being groomed as a disciple by Professor Bronze and Professor Destroyer?
As a professor, he found the complaints of a child who was already on track to surpass fourth year and head toward fifth year graduate student status amusing, but as a student supervisor, it was more troublesome.
This distrust ran deep.
It was beyond his ability to resolve.
The Chairman smiled with upturned lips, as if mockingly.

'It seems pointless to try further?'
'...To have less credibility than the Foundation's Chairman, damn it. Did I set the high enhancement probability too low on the slot machine?'

Convincing Oknodie was impossible.
As he was about to give up, he noticed a student with an extremely uncomfortable expression standing behind the two.
A male student with a sharp presence like a sword, but unable to properly display his aura because he was intimidated by the Chairman.
It was Xing, a first-year student from the advanced class of the 981st batch.

"Xing. Will you also stay with Oknodie?"
"Yes."

Why is this kid speaking informally to me?
Ferguson's insides boiled with anger.
If the Chairman weren't here, he'd have no qualms about hammering that head without caring about appearances.

"If your way back disappears, your safety will be in the hands of the Foundation. If they don't permit it, you might have to spend your entire lives trapped on this island."
"No need to worry about that. There's a <Transfer Magic Circle> on the 60th floor of this tower, and Oknodie secured our right to go directly to the 60th floor through a deal with the Chairman."
"You mean you can leave this tower anytime without taking a ship when you want to?"

Nod.
Xing now just moved his head without even replying.
Veins bulged on Ferguson's hand.
That bastard—if he ever takes my class, I'll make him repeat 'I'm dead' until I feel better.
Suppressing his violent thoughts, Ferguson decided he had fulfilled his duty as a professor.

"Then I hope you return safely before the semester begins. As you know, if the first semester was from March to July, the second semester is from September to December, so you should return to the Academy by the end of August at the latest to attend classes properly without paying a fine."

Ferguson gave up on taking Oknodie and Xing back with him.


* *


Oknodie watched the cruise ship leaving the island through the tower window.
The Chairman asked her:

"Aren't you disappointed? Missing the chance to enjoy more of your vacation with your friends."
"Not at all! I'll see them again when the semester starts anyway!"
"Hehe. What if I don't allow you to return to the Academy? There's no professor to take you back now."

Oknodie stared up at the Chairman.
Then, with a somehow confident expression, she let out a high-pitched "Phee~" laugh.

"A big shot like Papa wouldn't do that."
"What correlation is there between me being a big shot and your Academy attendance that I'm unaware of?"
"Of course there is. Small-time villains don't know how to keep their word, but big shots have class. You need to know how to keep promises to qualify as a big shot."

Evil, but with style.
That's what makes a great villain!
Her bright self-assertion evoked a feeling of endearment.

"Where did you learn such excellent words?"
"Hehe. Just somehow? It seemed like the right thing."
"Excellent. Truly excellent. Oknodie, you might have a talent."
"What kind of talent?"
"The talent to be called a great villain of the continent!"

Xing's hunched shoulders twitched.
'What kind of conversation are this crazy father and daughter having as soon as the professor leaves?'

"Remember this. Ordinary people tell countless lies throughout their lives. Promises they can't keep. Empty boasts. The world watches and remembers such words. It says, 'This person is a liar who doesn't even keep their own word.'"
"Heh. That sounded just like Papa!"
"Hehe. Not just like Papa—I am Papa. Didn't you make an implicit promise to me as well?"
"Hmm~ I suppose you could see it that way!"
"Lies are bad. In times before qi existed in the world, there was no honor in the words of the strong, and they could renege on their promises to deceive the weak as much as they wanted."
"Is it different now?"
"Of course. The world gradually gives credit to those who don't lie and keep their promises. Internal energy, mana, divine power. Though different in kind, the size of credit grows."
"!"

Xing, who was eavesdropping on their conversation, became alert to the wisdom-filled talk that made his mind flash with clarity.
As if reading Xing's mind, the Chairman smiled generously.

"Xing. This will be a good lesson for you too, so listen carefully. Keeping promises with the world isn't just about keeping your word. Running at a set time every morning, repeating sword strokes ten thousand times a day—these are promises kept with the body."
"So my training had such meaning...?"
"Ah, that's about the dolphin pants sisters... Ahhh, ah, it's nothing!"
"Of course, keeping diligent promises in daily life isn't bad, but to gain great credit, you must keep great promises. Actually fulfilling a vow to complete revenge, or achieving a goal to eat one of each type of food in a region."
"!!"
"Slurp...!"
"So don't make commitments lightly. Don't easily harbor expectations for anything. If you commit to something, you must keep it, and if you expect something, you must achieve it. If you can't, credit will leave your side."

Is this how the great villain of the continent, the Chairman of the Foundation, viewed the world?
Though an evil being, perhaps there was something admirable about his attitude toward life.
Xing harbored such thoughts.

"In that sense, would you like to make a promise?"
"...What kind of promise?"
"Not to leave the tower until you climb to the 50th floor on your own feet. If you achieve this condition, I'll give you a congratulatory gift in my own way. However, if you fail to meet the condition, I'll help you with special training until you can climb the tower."

For Xing, who had overcome the trial of the 39th floor with Oknodie's help, it was quite an enticing offer.
But for a bet, it seemed somewhat soft.

"What do you gain by doing this?"
"Simple. You'll stay with the Foundation rather than the Academy for longer."
"...!"
