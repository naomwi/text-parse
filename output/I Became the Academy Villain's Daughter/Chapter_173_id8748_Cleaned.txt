# 173 - The Reason for Nightmares

Oknodie, found on the bed, had not woken up for several hours already.
She only mumbled things like "Don't kill..." repeatedly while sweating profusely and appearing to be in pain.
Each time this happened, the expressions of Isabel, who kept wiping the cold sweat from her forehead with a handkerchief, and Son O-cheon, who chased away onlookers gathering out of mere curiosity, only grew grimmer.

"Isabel. How is Oknodie's condition?"
"It's gotten worse. The nightmare seems to have intensified."
"That little mouse... Has she been suffering from such nightmares every night?"

Why couldn't they have noticed sooner?
Oknodie's suffering appearance was disturbingly severe, enough to cause regret.

"There's no direct physical abnormality. Rather, intense mental stress is reversely affecting her physical body."
"Does that mean treatment is impossible?"
"At least, it's not a condition that can be addressed with healing spells. In my opinion, it appears to be a powerful curse related to nightmares, so I cannot intervene carelessly."
"Can't you just cast a curse removal?"
"Attempting to remove a high-level curse recklessly could cause permanent damage to the mind. Active curses infiltrate the brain and hold brain functions hostage."
"Waaah."
"Is there no other way?"
"If we had a record of her healthy brain state on a mana board, it could help with treatment, but since this child is unidentified, we lack comparative information."

That's right.
Even though the foundation's proxy had certified Oknodie as the foundation's scholarship student, that didn't mean her identity records existed.
There was only the foundation's empty promise; the world had no information about the child named Oknodie.

"Hey, skinny. Aren't you a healer? If you're working for a salary from the Academy, shouldn't you be more helpful?"
"All I can tell you is that this student is under a very powerful curse. Without someone deliberately casting it, the possibility of contracting such a curse is extremely rare."

Oknodie was cursed.
By someone's malice.
Although Oknodie usually practiced curse resistance, she wouldn't have willingly accepted such a dangerous curse that might kill her.
There was a culprit.
Here in this Academy.

"Son O-cheon. There's no point pressuring someone who has no solution. Guard the entrance. The culprit might try to approach and cause more trouble."
"...Understood."
"Gisele, please call a professor. Any professor who might be able to help Oknodie."
"I'll return as quickly as possible."
"Waaah."

Isabel determined that swift action was more important than unnecessary arguments.
Both Son O-cheon and Gisele agreed with her judgment, so they carried out their roles without complaint, either going to find a professor or silently guarding the door.

"Hey, I heard Oknodie collapsed?"
"Is Oknodie okay?"
"We came to visit!"

Son O-cheon turned to the healer with a serious face.
The healer, perhaps feeling bad about not being able to help, assisted with controlling access.

"Oknodie's condition is currently critical, so visits cannot be permitted. If you're concerned, please pray for her."

Students who had just heard the news realized the situation was more serious than they'd been told, judging by the healer's grave demeanor.

"They say Oknodie was cursed."
"She usually went looking for curses in the bushes and deliberately got cursed."
"That must have been exploited. She let her guard down for a minor curse and ended up with a major one too."

In the gloomy atmosphere, Arcadia glared at the Imperial faction students.

"If anything happens to Oknodie, I will never forgive you."
"Why are you blaming us?"
"Do I really need to remind you that most students who dislike Oknodie are from your side?"
"There are more depraved people capable of such terrible acts from your frontier!"
"Waaah."
"Who was that just now! Who's mocking us like we're children? Are you saying people from the frontier have the intelligence of babies?"

The relationship between students was rapidly deteriorating.
Just because Oknodie had collapsed, peace was crumbling remarkably quickly.
Son O-cheon realized anew how greatly that little mouse had contributed to the Academy's peace.

"Enough! Do we really need to determine who the culprit is right now, right here in front of the corridor where the healer said Oknodie needs stability?"

The students fell silent, looking awkward.
Urgent footsteps added to the uncomfortable silence.

"I've brought a professor. Everyone, please make way."

Surprisingly, the first professor to arrive at the scene was Professor Sadako, whose eerie presence seemed like she had just stepped out of a horror film, capable of startling anyone's heart.

"Dark magic and curses have an inseparable relationship, like water and fish. Your decision to find me was appropriate..."

Professor Sadako looked at Oknodie's condition and placed a hand on her forehead.
Making cracking sounds while turning her head this way and that, the professor's examination looked more like she was observing a murder target rather than diagnosing a patient.

"I need to go in directly."
"Go in? Where?"
"Into Oknodie's nightmare."

The students who had been arguing loudly outside suddenly fell silent as if cold water had been thrown on them.

"I-I learned from my family... If you carelessly enter the mind of someone suffering from a mental curse, your soul could be devoured by the curse, leaving you in a vegetative state."
"It's too dangerous. The Great Forest Witch Grandmother also said not to interfere with people pulled into the dream world. Meddling incorrectly could make things worse."

Lotto, who received formal basic education from a noble family.
Dorothy, the apprentice forest keeper.
Though they lived in different regions and had different main classes, their awareness of taboos was the same.
Do not interfere with another's mind carelessly.
It's a dangerous act that could cost the lives of both the person suffering mental issues and the one trying to help.

"Professor. It's too dangerous!"
"Worry about yourselves before worrying about me..."
"Are you encouraging us because you're concerned we might collapse from worrying about our friend?"

What a kind-hearted professor, despite her scary appearance with long hair hanging down in white mourning clothes and joints that kept cracking!

"That's because you need to enter the nightmare with me."
"...Us?"
"Friends with deep bonds reduce the probability of being attacked by the unconscious. Companions are essential for safe mental diving..."

According to the professor, she intended to take Oknodie's close friends into the nightmare with her!

"We weren't that close though?"
"Right. No matter what we think, Oknodie probably doesn't consider us friends."

The students who had gathered after hearing the news stepped back hesitantly.
Arcadia, who had brought the most students, felt betrayed but also understood their feelings.
She knew most of these children were pretending to be friends with Oknodie to gain her favor and protection as the spiritual pillar of the frontier students.
It was pointless to demand genuine bonds from such children in the face of a potentially fatal crisis.
As they said, Oknodie wouldn't unconsciously strive to protect such artificial relationships.

"Lady Arcadia. I understand your feelings completely. You've been the one who cared for Oknodie the most."
"Isabel..."
"I'll go in. I can trust Lady Arcadia to take care of Oknodie."

Arcadia accepted the wet cloth Isabel offered with an apologetic expression.

"...I'm counting on you. Please save Di."

Isabel stood beside Professor Sadako.
Son O-cheon also pointed at someone.

"You and you there. You'll take over as doorkeepers."
"Me...? You trust me?"
"Me? But I'm a pirate?"
"What does being a berserker or a pirate have to do with anything? Oknodie mentioned you guys occasionally."
"Oknodie talked about me...?"
"Hmm. Really? Well, I do owe her, so consider this repayment."

Hestia and Jigoku willingly took over Son O-cheon's position.

"Next, I should..."
"Gisele. You stay outside in case something happens."
"Planning to exclude me from saving the little lady? I'm hurt."
"You're the most level-headed among us. If problems arise, you're the only one I can trust to handle things from the outside."
"Waaah."
"Understood. I'll stay. And..."

Gisele was about to point at a plant that had been bothering him for a while when he received a strong look from Hestia.

"...I'd appreciate if you could finish this safely without requiring my intervention."
"What? That's disappointing."
"That gentleman has a soft heart. I wonder how he ever got the dark merchant class."

When Isabel and Son O-cheon turned to the professor indicating they were ready, Professor Sadako nodded.
Chanting an ominous spell, the three people enveloped in pitch-black fog leaned against the wall beside the bed one after another and disappeared into Oknodie's mental world.


* *


"What is this..."
"It's Oknodie's mental world."
"Why is the little mouse being attacked by Academy students?"

The three people, now in the form of translucent ghosts, floated through Oknodie's nightmare.
Professor Sadako interpreted the dream's reality.

"A death experience nightmare realizes what the subject fears most. For that child, being hated by everyone must be the most frightening moment..."

Don't kill.
Now they understood the meaning of the words Oknodie kept mumbling in her sleep.
Even while being driven to her death by students.
Oknodie was desperately trying not to kill anyone.
As if doing so even once would be irreversible.

'She didn't want to become the foundation's killing machine. That's the battle she's been fighting in her nightmare!'

Tears welled up in Isabel's eyes.
