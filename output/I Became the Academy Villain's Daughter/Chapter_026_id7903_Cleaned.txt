# 26 - State Your Business

Hood quickly disappeared, climbing up a tree in an instant.
The remaining targets naturally became our group of four.

"That's the team with the top scorer from the first gate."
"That monkey guy looks dangerous too."
"This is too risky."

Most of the human hunters who came looking for people with points didn't dare target us.
Thanks to that, we were deeply engaged in casual conversation.

"I see the money I spent hiring bodyguards was worth it."
"Hahaha! If you're grateful, pay me more."
"Is there something you'd like to buy?"
"Good alcohol is expensive, you know."
"Alcohol?"

My ears perked up.
How could I not be interested?
The Cooking Book includes beverages too.
If beverages are included, there's no reason alcohol wouldn't be.
Better to drink when I have the chance!

"Oh, what's this? Does the little mouse want to try some?"
"I like everything related to food and drink!"
"Then would you try this?"

Son O-cheon pulled out a gourd bottle from his waist.
It was the same gourd bottle he'd been carrying at his waist all the way from Mt. Shinjeong through the airship and the first gate.

"You've had that forever. Wasn't that just water in the gourd?!"
"What are you talking about? Of course it's alcohol."

This heavy drinker.
How much has he been drinking all this time?

"Here, what do you think?"
"Ugh. The smell."

How much do I need to drink for the alcohol to register in my collection?
One glass? One bottle?
If it's in a bottle, do I need to empty the bottle, and if it's in a gourd, do I need to empty the gourd?
Honestly, I'm confident in my drinking capacity.
Before the capsule malfunction that caused my sudden death and forced me to get a ticket to another world, I used to drink heavily with my childhood friend Sia.
It was amusing to watch my childhood friend, who despite being a heavyweight drinker would always challenge me saying "Today I'll definitely make you drunk!" only to pass out first every time.
Sometimes she would drunkenly insist that I must have a lower alcohol tolerance than her for some inexplicable reason.

"......"

Hmm. I'm getting a bit nervous.
In the game, choosing "Drink" would automatically empty the container and a certain amount of time would pass, with a certain probability of getting the "Drunk" status effect...
But in reality, where it's purely about drinking capacity, there's no room for probability.
Oknodie's body is a child's body.
Children are usually weak to alcohol.

"Please refrain from giving alcohol to a child. It's not good for her health, and we're in the middle of a competition."

Still, alcohol is a candidate for my Cooking Book collection.
I should try it when I have the chance later.
Hmm... yes.
Preferably when that bearded man is asleep.
After the exam ends, I'll secretly steal and drink it late at night.

"Little mouse, aren't you worried about getting stranded? The leaders are already out of sight."
"We can just track them."

There are plenty of hints.

[Observation experience +2]
[Tracking experience +1]

[You found leaves stepped on by muddy feet.]
[Observation experience +3]
[Tracking experience +1]

[You found bushes and branches cut by a knife.]
[Observation experience +1]
[Tracking experience +1]

Thanks to the tracking mechanism that progresses smoothly as I discover hints through eye-tracking, my ability to identify and follow hints is quite useful in reality too.
To be honest, I'm just as confident in my tracking ability as I am in my hiding ability.

"Oh. Little mouse, do you have hunting experience?"
"Of course I do."
"With your size, did you hunt rabbits?"
"What are you talking about? I hunt monsters too. Sometimes even people—oops."
"People? You're joking, right?"
"O-of course I'm joking. Hehehe."

Phew. That was almost awkward.
Or maybe it already is?
The sudden silence makes the atmosphere heavy.

[The trackers flee in fear.]
[Detection experience +1]
[Intimidation experience +3]

Oh no.
I wanted to gain combat experience, but my slip of the tongue only increased unnecessary skills.

[The dangerous presence moves away.]
[Detection experience +1]

Thanks to that, the annoying enemies who were worrying about how to approach us gave up on trying to attack us alone.
It was an accidental benefit, but honestly, I'm glad.
Even if they're at Son O-cheon's level, exam takers with malicious tendencies who like to attack alone are quite dangerous.

"It's okay. That won't happen anymore."

Did she misunderstand something from my momentary thoughtfulness?
For some reason, Isabel patted my shoulder comfortingly.

"What won't happen? Oh, hunting? We'll have to attack if the situation calls for it. That's how we pass the exam."
"It's okay. The older ones will take care of that."

It's convenient for me, but that's not right!

"What if you get hurt, big sister?"

Isabel placed her hand on her chest with a touched expression and silently stomped her feet.
Heh, she's really innocent.
She doesn't even realize I'm laying the groundwork so the cook who should help me collect for my Cooking Book can't avoid difficult cooking by using injuries as an excuse.
She'd be shocked if she knew my evil thoughts.
After that, Isabel occasionally looked up into the distance or rubbed her eyes with her sleeve.

"Isabel."
"You can call me big sister."
"Big sister. Are you crying?"
"No. I have allergies to pollen."
"Ah. That must be tough."
"Yeah. It'll be fine soon, don't worry about it."

Isabel was not only foolish but also a liar.
There weren't even any flowers in this forest.
Yet she kept wiping her eyes afterward.

Oknodie's training became increasingly clear.
Poison resistance training.
Tracking training.
Sword training and archery training.
Not just animal hunting, but human hunting experience too.

'This is definitely assassin training.'

There's no doubt about it.
This is absolutely assassin training.

"Hunting isn't anything special. At that age, I used to hunt orcs too."
"Really?"
"Some cheeky bastards claimed they killed a deer just because they stuck one stone axe in it. I put that axe right back into their skulls."

Son O-cheon tried to lighten the mood.
Oknodie, now relaxed, laughed and played along.

"Ah, that's so annoying, right?"
"...Little mouse, have you sent a few people off too?"
"Not with an axe, but with a bow."
"......"
"N-no, I'm joking! Lying! Just kidding!"
"...Reeeally?"

Instead of lightening, the mood darkened further.
In Isabel's mind, Oknodie's tragic past was already unfolding.
A remote island.
Child slaves captured together.
Only one child could escape alive.
Children who ate and slept together forced to point knives at each other in a cruel trial for survival.
Oknodie trying to forget the terrible sensation of killing by pretending to be happy.

'This child is so pitiful...!'

Despite such an unfortunate past, her innate nature that hadn't completely disappeared helped me.
She probably didn't want to kill people but had to do it for her missions.
Otherwise, this optimistic child who gets along well with people wouldn't make sense.

"Let's all rest here before continuing. There's a stream, and this is the perfect place to cook the mongoose meat."

At the mention of meat, Son O-cheon and Oknodie scattered to gather firewood, drooling.

"Gisele. Just a moment."
"Yes?"
"You two are together to help that child, right?"
"Was it that obvious?"
"I've figured out what kind of situation the child is in. She must have had a very unfortunate past."
"Please don't make it too obvious in front of the child. She's trying hard to be cheerful. I want to protect her smile at least."
"I understand. That's why I want to help too."
"Are you serious?"
"I already owe you my life. You also saved my precious adventure party members. I'm afraid of whoever is behind that child, but I want to help as much as I can."
"If you're that determined, I won't try to dissuade you."

Gisele told her about the oddities of Oknodie observed during their journey, including the butler, the maid, and more.

"I see... So she was able to easily evade the guards on the airship."
"You seem to have had a considerable past yourself, Isabel."
"Oh, this?"

Isabel had been silently skinning the animal, removing the organs, and sorting the meat while listening to the conversation.
Noticing Gisele's gaze on her impressive butchering skills, Isabel replied awkwardly.

"I'm different from that child. I'm already 18 years old, and I learned these skills by begging my seniors to teach me because I wanted to be helpful to the adventure party."
"I see."
"From my perspective, Gisele, you're the strange one. You have an incredible number of items in that backpack. Didn't you apply insect repellent when she said she hated mosquitoes?"
"Well, my past isn't very honorable. I was a ticket black market dealer."
"A ticket black market dealer? It's surprising you decided to take the exam. One gold ticket would fetch quite a price."
"Haha. It hurts even more than that. I used a platinum ticket."
"A platinum ticket?!"

Even Isabel, who had collected tickets by visiting various examiners with her adventure party, had never obtained a platinum ticket.

"I have to ask, just to be sure—you're not staying by that child's side with some suspicious intentions, are you?"
"Of course not. She's a child raised as an assassination prodigy in a noble family. Meddling with that would be suicidal even for a black market dealer."
"...We've both come to know quite an extraordinary child."
"Indeed we have."

Whatever their pasts might be, their desire to save Oknodie was the same.
Isabel felt she had grown closer to Gisele.

"Are they dating?"
"Don't ask me, little mouse."
"Should we wander around for about an hour before coming back?"
"Hmm, should we?"
"You two. If you've gathered the firewood, stop talking nonsense and come over here."

Gisele called to the two people hiding behind the bushes with a smile.
Isabel couldn't help but smile at the two who were visibly disappointed.
