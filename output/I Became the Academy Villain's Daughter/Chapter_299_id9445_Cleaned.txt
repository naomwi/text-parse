# Enhanced Translation: Chapter 299 - A Parent's Heart

As I finished the seventh day's auction and lay on the auction house floor building sand castles, I heard a thunderous noise coming from the cruise ship in the distance.

"Hmph. Everyone else is having fun. Except for me."
"You could return to the ship now. Then you could enjoy yourself as much as you want."
"No way. I absolutely refuse. Why would I go back empty-handed after collecting all these points?"

Spending 90,000 points would be nothing compared to my remaining points.

"The auction house's safe zone is not open outside of auction hours. Please leave, my lady."
"Hmph. I hate you, Jonna."

As soon as I stepped outside, the Giant King Crabs that should have pounced on me surprisingly just stared blankly or simply passed by.
This wasn't due to the mysterious powers of Dark Mana or some achievement effect, but simply because they had other, easier prey to hunt.

"Leape. April. Since you participated as contestants, the same applies to both of you. Please leave now."

Leape stepped out of the safe zone.
The Giant King Crabs still stared intently at the safe zone.
When April cautiously placed one foot outside the boundary, the clicking sound of crab legs echoed from all directions.
When April pulled her foot back inside the boundary, the crabs seemed disappointed and flattened themselves on the rocks again.

"Was I being targeted as food..."

April quickly forfeited.
Knowing when to stay in the safe zone even at the cost of losing participant status was the wisdom of survival without taking unnecessary risks.

"Leape. Shouldn't you give up quickly too?"
"That would be difficult. The opportunity to gain participant status is extremely valuable."
"That's unfair. I thought I'd only be competing against students, but now Leape is still in at the end."
"Don't worry. I haven't collected as many points as you have."
"How many did you collect, Leape?"
"Shamefully, I only gathered 9 million points. As an assassin maid, I consider it disgraceful that I couldn't reach 10 million points."

...So 10 million is the standard for assassin maids!
That's quite threatening even from a veteran's perspective.
It's certainly surprising that she offered twice as many points as that hero.

"You didn't receive a scholarship, did you?"
"My lady. You underestimate me too much if you think I was just playing around with students."

I tried to avoid her hand as she moved to flick my forehead, but with a sharp snap, everything went white before my eyes.

"Ouch!"
"While I acknowledge your growth potential, you still have much to learn compared to fully accomplished experts. From my position as your servant, I'm delighted to have so much to teach you."
"Hmph. I definitely dodged that."

This is sorcery!

"Would you like to challenge me? For today, try to avoid my assassin maid secret technique 'Bug Squashing'."
"What do I get if I win?"
"I'll give you a rare-grade preserved food that you've never tasted before."
"Wow! That's exciting!"
"However, if you fail before the auction starts, you'll have to eat a tasteless common-grade celery dish."
"Wow...! Not exciting at all...!"


* * *


Unlike April the cleaning maid with her clumsy movements, Leape's movements were difficult to read.
She could read her master's movements anytime, anywhere, approach with silent steps that didn't disturb the senses, and before you knew it, you'd already been hit.
What kind of bewitching footwork was she using under that long flowing skirt?
Despite her modest appearance, what was the true nature of her cunning technique that could manipulate people's hearts?

[Your discernment ability is too low.]
[Your observation precision is too low.]
[Automatic skill analysis failed.]

Surprisingly, I failed to copy Leape's technique.
Unlike the academy underclassmen who freely displayed their skills without reservation, Leape's techniques were extremely secretive.
But even if she can deceive my eyes, she can't fool my brilliant mind!

"How about this?"
"I commend your ingenuity in planting a dark curtain on the ground and deliberately leaving an opening to guide my attack, then using the curtain to block my forehead flick."
"Hehe. I got praised!"
"That's 50 points."
"...50 points?"
"While this technique would work sufficiently against academy underclassmen, we're outside the academy now. Please understand that the evaluation standards must be higher."
"50 out of 100 points??"
"If you had added an adhesive property to the curtain, combined with the curtain's unique rapid deployment, it would have become a binding trap that could seal your opponent's movements."
"But this still prevents you from hitting me!"
"Have you forgotten? Today's goal is not defense but evasion. If your binding was so complete that evasion wasn't even necessary, I could have acknowledged it, but this is insufficient."

Leape stomped her foot in front of the ice barrier where the dark curtain was planted, and the curtain, detecting the adhesive mana, stretched out on its own.

"Even the same magic can produce completely different effects depending on how you apply its properties, and can be easily countered. Recognize that your opponent can also employ strategies like you do, and establish active countermeasures."
"Hmph. The difficulty is higher than in the original. Hardcore mode is too intense."

As a result, I ended up eating a tasteless normal-grade wild vegetable bibimbap.
The brown vegetables made my hands tingle when I ate them, and the purple ones made my fingers prickle, but I managed to expel the toxins with Dark Mana and was fine.

"I may have lost the bet, but the auction will be different!"

Just wait until something comes up.
I'll teach them a lesson!

"I will now reveal the eighth item of the desert island auction. Today's item is 'Unifying the Academy Cafeteria Menu with Nutritionally Perfect Lunch Sets for One Year'."
"!?!?!?!?"

How on earth could such a vicious item exist!?
That's too much, practically forcing me to give up food catalog collection for a year!

"The starting bid is 500,000 points. Bids will increase by 500,000. Is there anyone who wishes to participate?"

Jonna looked at Leape and me as he asked.
Leape seemed extremely interested.

"To be able to take care of my lady's nutrition—this is irresistible. I bid 500,000 points."
"No!! Losing the free food catalog collection is too great a loss. I'll block it with 1 million points!!"

April grumbled in disbelief.

"I wish the ones who dropped out earlier could have seen this spectacle of someone burning a million points on such an item."

The bidding price rose relentlessly.

"Leape. 1.5 million points."
"Oknodie. 2 million points."
"Leape. 3 million points."
"Oknodie. 4 million points."

Leape withdrew with a very carefree expression.

"Take it. I gladly yield this item to support my lady's bountiful meals."

I've been had.
Using my weakness for food to drain my points.
I knew the purpose of weakness items was to make points evaporate, but this really hurts to the bone.
But I also have doubts.
Is this item designed because I like eating new foods and hate eating the same food?
Or is it intended to interfere with my food catalog collection after discovering the mechanism?

'I don't know!'

Both Leape and Jonna are masters of the poker face.
With Jonna's perpetually stern expression and Leape's consistently cold face, their emotions were as unreadable as if they wore masks.

"I won't fall for it in tomorrow's auction!"
"I look forward to it."

Leape replied with a smile.
Whether it was a genuine smile or not, I couldn't tell at all.


* * *


The next day, the waters around the ship froze and fire storms raged amid explosions more spectacular than the day before.
The kids must be having fun.
Playing with fireworks and ice.
As I built sand castles on the beach and stared blankly at the cruise ship, Leape came dragging a Giant King Crab with a cracked shell.

"My lady is still growing. Shouldn't you eat dinner?"
"I'm not hungry..."
"Are you sulking?"
"Hmph. Who says I'm sulking? I'm not sulking at all! Hmph hmph."
"In society, that's what people have tacitly agreed to call 'sulking'."

Leape cracked open the Giant King Crab's shell, and delicious smells and steam wafted out.

"This is Giant King Crab shell innards fried rice that I spent three hours cooking to share with you."
"Wow. What kind of fried rice takes three hours? Did you use some secret cooking method?"
"It took one hour to start the fire."
"Did you spend two hours cooking it over low heat?"
"I spent two hours gathering firewood."

The entire island had been swept clean of vegetation—once by fire, once by ice, and once by metal.
It must have been difficult to find fuel.
As I felt Leape's sincerity, my resentment melted away like ice.

"Hehe. Leape is the best!"
"I've heard reports that you've developed many new hobbies. Is building sand castles that enjoyable?"
"Magnificent castles produce treasure chests! I can't resist the random loot from daily quest treasure chests!"
"...I'll help you."
"Suddenly? You used to scold me saying it was bedtime."
"Bad adults don't sleep at night."
"Then am I a bad child too?"
"To me, you're just my adorable lady."

After all is said and done, family is still the best.
Time spent with Jonna and Leape is always enjoyable!


* * *


My lady has many friends, but most are actually followers.
Even with true friends who have opened their hearts to each other, the skill gap is so great that she can't train together with them and ends up playing alone, building sand castles on the beach.
I thought it when I received the documents, but it's truly sad.
My lady, so lonely and isolated because she's too strong.
The fact that despite possessing such immense power and talent, she's really just a child who wants to play.
The situation where the Foundation conducts ominous experiments like this desert island auction, and my own position cooperating with them.

'One night should be fine. To indulge my lady's childish whims.'

Leape realized the parental feeling of worrying about a child no matter how much they grow.
There was no way she would seriously believe that building sand castles would produce treasure chests.
It must be something like a child's imagination.
Perhaps she read some strange picture book somewhere.
Still, I should play along.
That's how it is when playing with children.
Leape, using her assassin maid's dexterity to build sand castles.
As she was carving patterns into the castle walls with her dagger, mixing fire ashes and ice fragments to create a sand castle of fire and ice, a dazzling light burst from within the castle.
An excited Oknodie demolished the castle and pulled out the treasure chest hidden inside.
Seeing this, Leape had only one thought:

'Why is this real?'

A real treasure chest had appeared.
