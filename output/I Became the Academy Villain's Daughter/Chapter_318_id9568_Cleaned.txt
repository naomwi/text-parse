# 318 - The Academy's Observer

The Training Tower, now strictly controlled by the Foundation, was once sought out by countless adventurers dreaming of becoming heroes.

In this era where disaster-level monsters rampage and reshape the land—where what was once a continent has become an archipelago—the procession of adventurers has abruptly ceased, their names no longer remembered.

However, the Chairman, who discovered the Tower's existence through ancient documents, has been utilizing its true value: guaranteed enhancement up to +7. This was a major factor in building his current wealth.

'I thought I had fully grasped the Tower's true value. How arrogant that was.'

It was truly a shocking discovery.
How is the Tower maintained?
What happens to "impurities" unnecessary for maintenance?
These were concepts he had dismissed, conveniently attributing everything to magic, thinking it would somehow work out.

Oknodie had exploited this oversight, using the backpacks of all past adventurers who visited the Training Tower as enhancement materials.
These weren't ordinary adventurers either.
They were skilled enough to target the hero party's exclusive Training Tower, so their backpacks were already enhanced to at least +3, with some even at +5.
This was the result of a new "porter" position that became popular among adventurers, created by a hero who recognized the value of high-level backpacks for carrying loot.

"Hmm. What a shame. I should have left just one sticker."

Oknodie had used up all her stickers on guaranteed enhancements, and with so many backpacks to enhance, she had destroyed some materials through failed enhancements. Nevertheless, she had achieved her goal—her backpack of backpacks now stored numerous +10 backpacks.

"If you don't mind, may I examine the detailed specifications of that backpack of backpacks with an appraisal scroll?"
"Eh? No way! Information about something this valuable shouldn't leak out!"
"I'll trade it for unique-grade dishes."
"Hmm... three of them!"
"Agreed."
"But you have to look at it alone, okay?"
"I promise."

The most special backpack containing all magical backpacks.
Its detailed specifications appeared on the appraisal scroll.

━━━
<Backpack of Backpacks #2 (Artifact (+12), Soulbound)>
Grade - Rare Level 3
Description - A backpack made by enhancing accumulated backpacks from the Training Tower. Only backpacks can be stored in this backpack. Growth is halted due to the backpack's curse while equipped.
Effect 1 - Expanded Capacity (100 slots)
Effect 2 - Expanded Maximum Storage Weight (10 tons)
Effect 3 - Protection from 4th-tier and below physical damage
Effect 4 - Protection from 4th-tier and below magical damage
Effect 5 - Protection from status effects such as submersion, corrosion, etc.
Effect 6 - Complete weight reduction of items within the backpack
Effect 7 - Search function allows searching and retrieving contents
Effect 8 - Only backpacks can be stored in this backpack
Effect 9 - While wearing this backpack, height and weight will not increase
Appraisal Value - 10,000 gold coins, 1,000,000 points
━━━
Currently stored backpacks (32)
Backpack of Backpacks #1, Weapon Backpack, Armor Backpack...
━━━

...Incredible.
The specifications were truly impressive.
To bypass the <Specialization> penalty that restricts storage to one type of item, she created a backpack that could only store backpacks, which in turn stored other specialized backpacks.
There was no need to rummage through contents thanks to the search function.
The powerful penalty was offset by the special <Physical Growth Freeze> penalty!

'If the hero party's porter position were revived, they would carry a bag like this.'

This backpack alone would warrant giving a gold badge to a porter.
Of course, the penalty is not light.
Height doesn't increase.
Weight doesn't increase.
For someone whose growth is complete, this might seem like a negligible penalty.
But for a hero party that grows rapidly from a young age with high potential, it's a backpack that should never be worn!
That's why they would have someone else bear the penalty.
If there's someone willing to sacrifice growth and silently fulfill the role of porter, this backpack truly shows its worth.
Oknodie blocked such workarounds with the <Soulbound> element.
By adding what could be considered a penalty—soulbinding—to the enhancement effects that could be unlocked one by one, she increased the risk that had to be borne.
As a result, the effects of each backpack became even greater.
The greater the risk, the greater the reward.
It was a product that properly considered the exchange conditions of enhancement.

"Aren't you afraid of the price you must pay for carrying this backpack? You're still young. It may seem light now, but you'll feel the cost more as time passes."

The Chairman isn't foolish enough to not understand why he shouldn't create collection backpacks by type and store them in a backpack of backpacks.
Even for him, whose growth is complete, the danger this backpack poses was obvious at a glance.
The growth issue is actually among the minor concerns.

"What will you do if your painstakingly gathered collections are damaged by high-tier attacks?"

You would lose all your collections in one day.
Of course, there are two types of collection judgments.
Possession judgment and record judgment.
Possession judgment applies simply by owning something, but record judgment for collections is obtained by carrying the item on your person for 24 hours.
This backpack has the advantage of making it easier to obtain record judgment just by possessing it.
However, the possibility of losing everything at once is tantamount to bankruptcy.
Take food, for example.
While items might register collection effects when gathered, the food catalog doesn't recognize possession or record judgment unless you completely consume one serving.
There are many catalogs with strict judgment criteria like the food catalog.
If you lose collections gathered over a long time before obtaining judgment, the damage would be immense.

"Then I'll just keep enhancing it until it won't be destroyed even by high-tier attacks! This is an endgame item that I'll keep enhancing until the end!"

Fascinating.
This child's worldview is drastically different from others'.
Is it her own wisdom?
Or the wisdom of the Destroyer who became her teacher?
Either way, this wisdom is sharp.
If honed, it's impossible to gauge how keen its edge might become.
Perhaps it could even deliver a stinging blow to that Dragon Headmaster.

"By the way, Papa, why did you hire an enhancer separately when you have an enhancement facility? I thought you hired one because you couldn't climb to the 70th floor, but you came up by elevator!"
"I don't know what you're talking about. I haven't hired any enhancer."
"Huh...? Then who was that blacksmith enhancer who enhanced the Titoso family's lighting equipment on the ship?"
"A blacksmith named Ferguson. We're already verifying his identity."
"Ah!"

For the first time, the child's expression showed fear.
She hadn't shown fear when eating the Steak of Sorrow or when indirectly experiencing the Foundation's power, so this display of weakness aroused interest.
What was it about this blacksmith Ferguson?

"He's the professor who teaches <Enhancement Enhancement Bang Bang> in the Production Department!"

An Academy professor—that was indeed cause for alarm.
But this had become troublesome.
Just when he thought he had finally shaken off the Dragon Headmaster, two professors had infiltrated the cruise ship.
Reave was killed.
That was a gain.
But without Oknodie, he wouldn't have noticed the blacksmith professor's approach.
This meant a new professor had appeared who hadn't previously been listed among the faculty.
How did Oknodie know this?

"The chefs will be busy."

The questions kept multiplying.
It seemed like many unique dishes would be needed to satisfy all these curiosities that kept leading to more questions.

* * *

Ferguson recalled the Headmaster's summons.

-Board the cruise ship and watch over the children. One of the professors has been recruited by the Foundation.

The Headmaster's concerns had become reality.
He had noticed Professor Reave's stowaway presence early on.
Unlike Reave, who secretly boarded the ship, Ferguson had recruited a blacksmith working on the ship, forged his identity, and boarded officially.
The facilities he could use and the areas he could access were different.
This led to a difference in the amount of information available.

"Tsk tsk. They say early-onset dementia is rampant these days, but this is truly madness."

Ferguson brought out forbidden enhancement techniques and quickly threw the ship into chaos.
The moment he learned that a special cargo ship had first visited the uninhabited island and that Blue Metal Rats had been released, Ferguson realized the trap.
That uninhabited island favored metallurgists.
It was a trap optimized to take down Reave, an ice magic user, in a sea environment.
To aid the confusion, he enhanced the lighting equipment and helped the students run wild in a shipboard rebellion.
Reave took advantage of the chaos to cross to the uninhabited island, where he met his end.
However, even Ferguson hadn't anticipated what happened next.
The rampage of Anrage's apostle.
Thanks to Oknodie's arrangements, the apostle collapsed without fully demonstrating its true power, but Ferguson felt considerable dread at the fact that the students' souls might have been swapped with those of Anrage's followers.
As a Production Department professor, while he could exert considerable combat power by bringing out high-level equipment, he wasn't confident in stopping Anrage's apostle.
His plan was to enhance the body at the moment of the soul-swapping contract.
Deliberately using the destructive phenomenon accompanying <Enhancement Failure> to burst the souls was his only chance of winning, but sacrifice was inevitable.
Through the backflow phenomenon, some students would return to their soul vessels, but some might fall into comas and become vegetative.
Then they would need to call in a professor skilled in necromancy and soul-handling techniques.
If they were late, several students would die.
Still, he had done what he could.
Oknodie had leaped beyond Ferguson's plans and rescued all the students.

-In the worst case, save at least Oknodie.

Now he understood why the Headmaster had prioritized Oknodie over the heroes.
It wasn't about power but the depth of wisdom.
Such a child was now meeting with the Foundation's Chairman in his tower-like mansion, doing something for several days already.

'Should I request a bombing?'

As he was wondering if he should get Oknodie out of the mansion as quickly as possible, someone from the Foundation approached him.

"Professor Ferguson."
"Huh? You've got the wrong person. I'm a blacksmith."
"We've already confirmed that you're a professor-designate in the Production Department. Your identity as the Academy's observer has been exposed, so there's no need to pretend."
"...Foundation Agent Jonna Wiheomhae. If there's no need to pretend, then speak plainly. When will you release our kids?"
"Don't worry. The students are scheduled to return by ship today. I came to inform you of that."

Shortly after, as Jonna said, the students came out of the mansion.
It was suspicious that they all came out holding +5 enhanced equipment, but it wasn't the first time the Foundation had tried to recruit Academy students.
Having noticed his identity as an enhancer, the Foundation was probably showing off that they could do anything the Academy could do.
It was a significant reward.
Some even had +10 enhanced equipment.
After such an incident, the Academy couldn't just keep quiet and let it slide.
They would have to open the treasure vault a semester early, give the kids something to hold, feed them something good, and appease them before their hearts turned toward the Foundation.
Fortunately, the headache-inducing tasks would be handled by the Headmaster or other faculty members.
His role ended with bringing the students back.
Even in the worst case, securing just Oknodie's safety would have been enough.

"However, two students will return separately, so please understand this point."

Of course, the Foundation wouldn't do the Academy any favors so easily.
Among the students who returned to the cruise ship, Oknodie was absent.
