# 381 - Dorothy's Ingenuity

The closer Dorothy got to solving the quiz answers, the more she sensed the smell of blood and money drawing nearer.

'Yui. An outsider and friend from beyond who showed us—who only knew life in the restricted zone—what lay beyond.'

Dorothy's feelings toward her were truly complicated.
Yui was an enemy who tried to take Rockfell away.
She was a wicked woman who tried to steal her home.
But she had also shown her a new world.
Without her, Dorothy would never have known about Gift Academy or met Oknodie.

'If I meet her again...'

"If I meet her again"? What nonsense.
What would she even do if they met?
Her complicated feelings, which even she couldn't understand, were suddenly resolved when she arrived at the next quiz location.

'Ah. So that's how it is.'

Yui, together with students other than herself.
The honesty that had once convinced her to leave the forest that had been her entire world.
The guide who had led Dorothy out of the forest was now guiding students again.
That sight.
That scene.
She no longer felt envy or jealousy.
It just seemed indifferent, like someone else's business.

'I wanted to confirm it.'

That the kindness Yui had shown them in the Golden Beast Forest wasn't eternal.
That she wanted to realize she had merely been used, evident from the expression that clearly showed other intentions despite the help offered.
That Yui's goodwill no longer remained as lingering attachment or hesitation in her heart.

"Dorothy!"
"Yui."
"I know it's a shameless request. But I'm asking anyway. I need Oknodie's help."
"For what?"
"To reach the library."

Despite her words, the hand signals she made while pressing close conveyed a different intention.

Unbeatable opponent. Dangerous target. 10 o'clock direction, within 25m.

The target indicated by the hand signal was a third-year senior.
The person who carried the most blood scent that had been irritating Dorothy's nose.
Yui's eyes trembled with anxiety.
She was asking for help, but she knew too.
How shameless this action was.
The eyes of Hector's vassals also trembled with anxiety.
Because they knew they were hostile to the organization that played with Oknodie.

Would they readily help upon receiving a request for help from an enemy?
It would be fortunate if they didn't attack in return.
Some even regretted that the operation had gone wrong.
Honestly, Dorothy thought the same.
Her first thought was that they were fools.

'Even in the forest, we'd teach nasty beasts that invaded our territory a lesson!'

Would humans be any different?
She'd even heard that Hector from Hector's vassals had been bullying a child named Casia.
But when she saw people cornered, sometimes she wondered what Oknodie would do.
Oknodie was such an unpredictable friend.
A lady who knew everything and excelled at everything.
Someone who appeared cheerful but was also a loner with cruel methods.
Would she have helped or ignored them?

'Oknodie doesn't help weak people! Unless they're interesting. Would Yui make me have fun?'

No, she wouldn't.
Yui had ignored her for her own amusement.

'Sometimes Oknodie helps people even when they're not fun. Because they're useful!'

Could Yui be useful to me?
That might be possible.
Because Yui had plenty of treasures.

'Give me one treasure. Then I'll temporarily forget what happened and help you just this once.'

Dorothy pointed at the shoes that made Yui's feet lighter.
Yui bit her lip tightly, then nodded.

Negotiation successful.

For some reason, the third-year senior had become an enemy.
Dorothy pointed at him and said,

"Everyone, we need to defeat that senior to proceed to the next path!"


* * *


"I see. So this time it's that kind of test."
"A third-year substituting as an examiner. Even a third-year can't handle this many students."

Mob and Jaku, who had followed Dorothy, advanced with other lower-class students.
Mob didn't know, but this group included many members of the "Secret Scholarship Society," a secret organization within the group that played with Oknodie, composed of scholarship students from the Wiheomhae Foundation.
The reason was, of course, that Jaku was the second-in-command of the Secret Scholarship Society.
Considering that the nominal leader was Oknodie, Jaku was essentially leading them.
As scholarship students rather than ordinary students, they harbored insolent thoughts even before a third-year senior.

'If we push with numbers, what can he do besides get hit, even if he's a third-year?'
'Does a third-year not bleed when stabbed with a knife?'
'Let's just rush him.'

Thoughts too violent for students!
As they all drew their swords and charged, Hector's vassals shouted urgently.

"No!"
"We need to slowly drain his strength or we'll be the ones in trouble!"

The scholarship students sneered.

'Ha. What are they saying?'
'They asked for help.'
'Are they backing out? Defeated dogs.'

We're different.
We're members of Oknodie's organization.
We're even foundation scholarship students.

"Hahaha!"

In response to their first charge, fueled by such pride, Senior Tetrapos didn't panic or dodge but stood in place and burst into laughter.
Something was wrong.
By the time Jaku felt uneasy, the scholarship students' swords, blinded by the prospect of achievement, had already descended upon the senior.
Simultaneously, a violent roar like hammering metal rang out more than ten times in succession.

"Argh!"
"My hand!!"
"Ugh, it feels like I hammered the ground!"

The scholarship students clutched their hands, making sad faces or shedding tears.
The recoil was so powerful that everyone was busy grabbing their hands in pain.

"Ah, this is called 'Metal Skin.' It allows you to circulate mana through your body or skin to instantly overlay the defensive power of the strongest metal you understand and can structure, wherever and however much you want."

This was bad.
Despite all those sword strikes, the senior hadn't taken any damage.
Rather, only the attacking first-years had been damaged.
But Jaku sensed they couldn't back down.

"Keep pressing! If we let him counterattack, we're finished. If we stop attacking, we'll be the ones in trouble!"
"!!"
"Jaku's right. I don't think we can withstand an attack from a body with that kind of power."

As the scholarship students changed their expressions and charged again, Tetrapos nodded, acknowledging they were indeed first-years who had entered their second semester.
He had intended to leave them writhing in pools of blood if they had fled in fear after just one sword strike.

"But I can't play too much. Now that I sense the presence of a student council executive, I can't stay tied up with you. Playtime ends here."

"Don't get caught in the threads! Either dodge or deflect them all!"

Despite Yui's shout, threads densely filled the corridor, pouring down like a waterfall from Tetrapos, who had spread his hands wide.

"How are we supposed to dodge this?!"
"Aaaaargh!!"

The <Metal Skin> that had deflected swords with a clang was now applied to all the threads.
This wasn't something one could simply deflect.

<Charging - Single Point Thrust>
<Begram-style Iron Wall Defense>
<Terrain Modification - Fortification>

Some tried to deflect the enemy's attack with all their might, some tried to withstand by increasing their defense, and others created trenches using the terrain.
These different forms of resistance seemed to hold against Tetrapos's strike momentarily, but they were all swept away by the continuous onslaught.

"Ah, it hurts..."
"My, my head..."
"I feel like I'm dying..."

Among the students rolling on the floor half-dead, Tetrapos's eyes were drawn to the few still standing.

"Quite impressive! To withstand that."
"*cough* It's a delaying tactic. For some reason, that senior fears wasting time. And he can't kill us. So forget about dying and just endure no matter what."

Jaku, with one arm hanging limply, stood up with blood dripping from his body and spoke.
As Hector's vassals, who had arrived late, rushed to the side of those who had withstood the strike, formed ranks, and began to pull the injured back, an immense pressure was felt once again.

"Then, the second strike."
"He's using that much output again without any delay?!"
"Tsk. I thought they were reinforcements, but they're just more baggage."

Hector, who had taken the lead with his fluttering cape, momentarily received mana correction from the dispersing cape folds, holding more than the limit output at the tip of his sword.

<Troy Royal Family's Secret Kingdom Swordsmanship>
<Leading Sword>

A powerful swordsmanship that increases strength by the number of people standing behind one's cape.
Instantly, mana threads extended from the cape to all students, transferring power to Troy, making it stronger than when defeating a single root of Poppy.

"Oh? So there's even a royal child here?"
"This is the power of the rightful heir to the throne. Senior, you should have settled this before our numbers increased. You'll regret allowing us to join."
"In return for showing me something good, I'll show you a nice technique too."

Tetrapos's numerous threads gathered above his raised index finger, forming a single massive line.

[Stack][Stack][Stack][Stack][Stack]

All those threads, each with such terrifying power, were combining into one.

"Techniques that handle borrowed power, you know, aren't your royal family's unique skills."
"You stole the royal family's techniques!!"
"Haha. Don't misunderstand. Rather, royal families from various countries tried to monopolize this technique from the world. Because they couldn't allow 'revolutionaries' or 'usurpers' to wield that power and target them in front of angry citizens."
"I can't believe it!"
"It doesn't matter if you deny it. Even such forgotten techniques can be found in the 'library.' No matter how high you build the walls of your royal family, Gift Academy grants the power to break down walls."
"Still, there's no one behind you!"
"It doesn't matter. I can just take and use it. Besides... I have plenty of power I 'saved' from earlier."

Is he referring to when he faced the monster tree?
Hector couldn't believe it.
He had used such a powerful strike then.

'Even that was a strike where he saved his strength?!'
"I'll hit you just enough so you don't die."

Hector's gleaming white sword clashed with Tetrapos's bright red blood thread.
The immense pressure made it feel like his body would float off the ground and be thrown back.
It was overwhelming.
Even withstanding a single strike, just a moment of that strike.
As his body trembled on the verge of being crushed, energy from behind supported him.

"Mob, if that prince falls, we're done too!"
"I know...!"
"Urgh, lend your strength to the prince!"
"This is crazy. The treasure's energy is depleting so quickly, it's unbelievable!"

Mob, Jaku, Deiphobus, Yui.
Despite the combined strength of numerous students, the strike that couldn't be fully withstood crushed the light on Prince Hector's sword and sent him flying back along with the students who had been helping him with mana shields.
When Jaku, who had been writhing on the ground, managed to raise his body with difficulty, he saw Hector—whose sword had broken but whose body hadn't yielded—being kicked by Tetrapos's foot and flying past him.

"Now, juniors. It's time for a blood donation. After using power, I need to replenish it. Would you lend some strength to an unfortunate senior being chased by the student council?"

Just as despair was setting in at the approach of the senior extending his threads like spider webs, it happened.

"Stop!"

Dorothy, the strongest in the group who hadn't participated in the chaos, finally raised her voice.

"If you attack us, you'll end up breaking this too, won't you?"

What Dorothy had brought were the signposts scattered throughout the final gateway to the library expedition.
They were classified as Academy "property."
Tetrapos couldn't hide his bewilderment at the appearance of Dorothy, who had wrapped the signposts around her body like armor.

"Junior. I hate to say this myself, but you don't seem to be in your right mind. From the moment you pulled those out, shouldn't you be paying compensation for property damage?"
"If you hit me, you'll also have to pay points for property damage. Try hitting me if you can afford it!"

Thanks to Dorothy's ingenuity in deploying the unprecedented "point suicide defense technique," the confused Tetrapos halted his attack.
