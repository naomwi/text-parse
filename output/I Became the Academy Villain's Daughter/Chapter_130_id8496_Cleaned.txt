# <130 - A Reward for the Good Child>

Stat Stones increase a random ability by 1 when consumed.
However, Low-grade Stat Stones only increase abilities within the low-grade range.
This means there's an upper limit to the ability increase.
Oknodie's body had quite high potential.
Her basic abilities had already grown close to the upper limit of low-grade abilities.
These days, she was certain.
There was no point in eating low-grade Stat Stones anymore as none of her abilities could increase further.
Just a slightly larger stone.
Unless she ate a mid-grade Stat Stone, consuming stones wouldn't increase her abilities.
That's why she had been generous.

'Someone like Mob deserves to share my Stat Stones!'

Actually, she was curious too.
An extra from the low-grade class.
A youngster she wouldn't have even noticed in other playthroughs.
How far could this child grow if nurtured?
Could he continue attending the Academy even as a second-year or third-year student?
Regardless of possibility, she wanted to experiment.
She wanted to keep this child around for a long time.
So she showed him kindness.
But she didn't expect him to agree so readily.

"You're going to eat a stone?"
"I'm prepared. There must be a reason for whatever Oknodie asks me to do."

His mindset was extraordinary.
Mob truly possessed the compliance of someone who had transcended being an extra.
He was completely different from those who would say "How could I do such a thing!" or "That's impossible!" and back out.

"Then... see you at dinner time!"

Mob nodded with a solemn face, like a pet that had realized it was going to be slaughtered in two hours and had willingly accepted its fate.
While he was serious in his own way, from an observer's perspective, his tense appearance seemed both admirable and cute.

"How is it going? Is the education progressing well?"
"I'm going to teach him more this evening too!"
"Hehe. That's how Oknodie should be. Good, good. Please continue being a good child."

Arcadia, whom they encountered in the lecture hall, patted her head.
When Arcadia, with her large chest, raised her arms, her top clung to her body, making her upper body lines quite noticeable.
Feeling embarrassed about where to look, she averted her eyes slightly, but Arcadia misunderstood the reason for her shyness as being due to the head pat and enthusiastically continued with "Eih, eih!"

* * *

Unlike the warm atmosphere between Oknodie and Arcadia, the lecture time was grueling.

"Today we'll learn a new skill. Until now, we've learned <Aerial Shot>, <Quick Shot>, and <Power Shot>, but today's skill is a named skill."

The instructor loaded a bunch of arrows onto the target with a unique grip technique and fired toward the target.
With a whooshing sound, the arrows flew simultaneously and stuck into the target in a vertical line with a rapid tapping sound, causing the uninformed students to exclaim "Wow!" and applaud.
Mob turned pale.

"What are you doing, Mob? You saw it too! Look at that awesome shooting!"
"You idiot... why do you think the instructor showed us that?"

Mob's concerns soon became reality.

"<Beoeum's Linear Shot>. This is the name of the shooting skill you'll learn today."

Named skills require more complex operation and delicate control than ordinary skills.
They're harder to use, but once mastered, their power in specific situations is on a different level from ordinary skills.
The so-called <advanced course> skill!

"Beginners should start with three arrows at once and gradually increase the number. The effective range and accuracy will also be evaluated with increasing difficulty."
"Instructor. So how can we acquire this skill?"

An innocent student raised his hand and asked.
It was a pointless question.

"Well."
"...Pardon??"
"Diligently."
"Instructor??"
"Practice diligently for two hours."
"Hey, wait! Where are you going?? Hey!!"

Amid students who belatedly realized the reality and despaired, Mob silently picked up his arrows.
It was already decided.
No amount of protest would help.
Trying to place multiple arrows on the bowstring at once made his aiming time longer and his hands tremble.

Fwip-fwip-fwip

Arrows curved left, right, up, and down as they pleased.
Placing multiple arrows in a straight line simultaneously was not easy.
Even shooting from a much closer distance than usual, it was clear without saying that building proficiency would be an arduous process.

Fwip-fwip-fwip

As if mocking his struggles, Skola, known as the descendant of the divine archer, proudly placed magic arrows in a straight line on the target.
Skola kept increasing the number of arrows and distance without hesitation, and Oknodie quickly followed behind.
Though not as good as Skola, she improved rapidly as if she had practiced this skill for decades, regaining what seemed like a forgotten skill.

'This is the difference in talent. The gap between the low-grade class and the high-grade class that can never be bridged.'

This was it.
The gap between Oknodie and himself.
The difference was far greater than he had thought.
Effort alone wouldn't be enough.
Talent was such a terrifying thing.
Catch up?
No.
Even keeping from falling further behind was difficult.
This was no time to refuse any eccentricity.
That's why, as soon as the lecture ended, he secretly received the stone in a deserted back alley and swallowed it without hesitation.

'Ugh.'

It was painful.
The act of swallowing something that shouldn't be eaten.
The act of enduring the anxiety that one's body might be harmed.
The act of suppressing the urge to gag and the body's rejection.
It was almost like an act of self-harm.

"How is it? Do you feel anything?"
"My stomach feels bloated..."
"That's normal! Now we just need to test which ability increased by using your body."

They measured the time it took to reach maximum speed in a full sprint, whether the maximum speed had increased, and how long it took for the consumed stamina to recover.

"Your agility increased!"

This time, the speed at which he reached maximum velocity had improved.
It meant his acceleration had increased.

"Really?"
"Yes!"
"Just from eating a stone?"
"Yep yep!"
"Haha. Whatever it is, it's amazing."

He didn't understand.
But the records didn't lie.
The time was clearly faster than what had been measured before eating the stone.

"Do people normally get faster when they eat stones?"
"No? This is a special stone, that's why!"
"Thank you. For using such a special stone for me."
"You're welcome!"

Feeling infinite gratitude, Oknodie called out "Hand!" and made him open his palm, then took out a candy from her pocket and placed it there.

"Since you did well, have a candy! This is Oknodie's reward for being a good child!"

Strawberry flavor.
A taste that naturally brings a smile.
It's similar to the sweet scent that emanates from Oknodie.
She must smell like this because she always eats such sweet desserts.
It made him feel good.
Like he had learned one of Oknodie's secrets.

"Delicious, right?"
"Yes."
"I was actually saving this to share with Leape when we meet again, but I'm giving it to you specially!"
"Thank... you?"

As he tried to express his gratitude, his vision blurred.
Suddenly, a strong dizziness came over him and his breath was cut short.

"—? —?"

His head was spinning.
Oknodie's voice seemed increasingly distant.
Something had gone seriously wrong.
Was it the stone?
Or the candy?
One of them was definitely problematic.
Perhaps eating the stone was the mistake after all.
He groaned, clutching his forehead, and collapsed with a thud.

Blink. Blink.

Through his closing eyes, the scenery rushed by.
The wind cut across his cheeks.
Was Oknodie carrying him on her back?
She was so fast.
Incredibly fast.
He wanted to match that speed.
With that thought, his consciousness abruptly cut off.

* * *

"Are you awake?"
"Wh-where am I...?"
"The infirmary."

As Mob tried to get up, the doctor gently pushed his head back down onto the bed with a magic wand.

"Respiratory distress, dizziness, muscle paralysis. You've ingested quite a strong neurotoxin. Any idea what it might be?"

Poison?
That couldn't be.
What he ate wasn't poison.

"There must be some mistake. I only swallowed a stone, so how could it be poison..."
"How would I know? Anyway, the child who brought you here paid for the treatment, so rest well tonight and leave tomorrow."
"...That won't do. I won't be able to keep up with the lectures. Isn't there a way I can leave sooner?"
"The detoxification itself is complete, so you could leave right away if you wanted. I just gave you time to replenish your energy. Still, since you're already here in the medical wing, you might as well rest. The beds here have an effect that increases the recovery speed of those lying on them."

The doctor said what he needed to say and closed the curtain, telling him to rest as much as he wanted before leaving.
As he groaned and loosened his stiff body, a small piece of paper fell beside the blanket.

[The doctor said this much is okay and you won't die! See you again in two days when you're better! - OKNODIE]

So Oknodie had helped him after all.
As soon as his energy recovered, he left the infirmary.
It was quite late.
If he returned to the dormitory, it would soon be time to sleep.
He'd already had enough sleep.
His feet naturally headed toward the secret training ground.

"Your perseverance is commendable. I heard you were in the infirmary."
"I can't afford to waste an entire day."

Zuang, whom he encountered again at the secret training ground today.
A student from the high-grade class like Oknodie, a petite and pretty girl from the assassin class.
Unlike Oknodie who had a child-like innocence, Zuang, who gave off the impression of a dangerous killer, smiled coldly again today.

"If you collapse from mere training, shouldn't you rest more?"
"It's not like that. Oknodie helped me train, but my body was shocked and I collapsed."
"Oknodie did?"

Showing interest, he told her about what happened in the evening, and Zuang burst into laughter.

"Ahahaha!"
"Hey. There's no need to laugh that much."
"I just think it's amazing that you actually ate it because she told you to."
"I had no choice. I was that desperate."

When he retorted with an annoyed voice because she kept teasing him, Zuang wiped away tears from laughing and said with a voice still tinged with amusement:

"Do you know what kind of training you received?"
"Stone-eating training?"
"Wrong. Poison-eating training."

Zuang said with a face that suggested she found it hilarious.

"The problem wasn't the stone, but the candy."
"There was poison in the candy?"
"Haven't you figured it out yet? Oknodie gave you assassin training. The only training method she knows—the one she learned herself."
"...You're saying Oknodie regularly eats candy laced with poison that knocked me out for half a day after just one piece?"
"Her organization is quite vicious. And it wasn't just regular training—she would eat a poison candy every time she acted like a 'good child.' I'm thankful I learned under my master instead of Oknodie's organization."

There was something ominous about her tone.

"...What do you mean?"
"You're really slow, aren't you?"
"Please. I'd really like to know. I'm a slow learner and don't understand things easily."

Zuang, with her doll-like elegant face, frowned and poked Mob between his eyebrows.

"Don't bow your head. Friends don't do that to each other."
"S-sorry."
"When an assassin sees the back of someone's head... hehe. It makes them want to do things you wouldn't want to know about."

He gulped nervously.
It suddenly hit him.
What an assassin truly was.
What kind of world Oknodie and Zuang lived in.
He thought he understood, but what he knew was barely scratching the surface.

"I'll tell you if you want... but can you handle it without regret?"
"Of course. We're friends, right? Both you and Oknodie."
"You shouldn't answer so lightly. If your attitude toward us changes after hearing this... smooth talk won't help."

Her colorless eyes stared as if probing his inner thoughts.
Those eyes somehow felt a bit frightening.

"You might throw away our entire relationship so far. Do you still want to hear it?"

In Zuang's eyes, he sensed a darkness so deep that he couldn't claim to truly know the inner selves of the two.
When he nodded, he realized that the darkness before him—Zuang's darkness—was much lighter than Oknodie's darkness that she was about to reveal.

"The poison candy is probably a punishment from the organization."
"Punishment?"
"A punishment she has to swallow every time she wants to be a good child."
"What does that even..."
"Still don't understand? Or are you pretending not to? Hehe. Mob... your cute reactions are nice, but there's no use in running away. You asked for this."

He didn't want to hear it.
He didn't want to understand it.
Step by step.
As Mob retreated slightly, Zuang followed.
Thud.
When he hit the wall and slumped down, Zuang leaned in and whispered in his ear.

"Do you understand? For failing to torment an animal that needed tormenting. Or failing to hit someone who needed hitting. Or failing to kill a target that needed killing."
"A punishment given to a 'good child' who refuses training to eliminate resistance to violence and murder."

A sharp scent, different from Oknodie's sweetness, with traces of blood, seeped into Mob's nose.

"That's the meaning of the poison candy that an assassination organization gives to a good child."
