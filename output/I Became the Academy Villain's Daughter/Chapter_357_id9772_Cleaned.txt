# 357 - Someone's Terror, Someone's Photocopier

"So, Gisele, you're in the advanced class, right?"

Someone with extensive information, many subordinates, and an enormous amount of points. However, I'd heard rumors that his personal combat abilities weren't particularly impressive.
Though it might be a bit unscrupulous, if I fought against Gisele, could I somehow win?

"Haha. If you're looking for a monster like me, it would have to be a leader-class of a group-type species with many subordinates. Beyond just a pack leader of three or four or a tribal chief of dozens, it would be a named monster capable of generating monster waves that would seriously threaten fortress invasions."

How could anyone defeat something like that?
As he turned pale, Gisele drove the point home.

"Fortunately, you don't need to worry about that."
"Because... there's no monster as strong as you, Gisele?"
"Because it's expected to be a singular entity with monstrous strength comparable to such a horde."
"!!!"
"Among the advanced class students, I'd say it's on par with Hestia, Archer Schola, or Horner Fried Chicken."

Mob made his decision.
This was beyond his capabilities.

"Would Oknodie help if I asked her?"

Though Gisele always maintained a neat appearance with a wealthy, gentle merchant-like demeanor and narrowed eyes, right now those same narrowed eyes suddenly conveyed considerable pressure.

"The young lady is busy focusing on her studies. While your friends' situation is unfortunate, I suggest you seek help elsewhere."

Mob, pushed back by the intimidation, replied that he understood.
Gisele...
He always seemed to yield to Oknodie, so I thought he would eagerly help with something like this, but surprisingly, he could draw a clear line when needed.
Mob changed his mind about resenting him.
He couldn't blame him.
He was already indebted enough just for receiving free information.
Let's find someone else who can help.


* * *


"What?! Why should I fight such a scary monster? Absolutely not!"

Mutu Lotto frantically opened the door with a bang and fled.
Seeing how the doorknob was crushed and the door fell off from the force, Mob thought that with such strength, she could have accomplished anything if she had helped.

"Their origin?"
"They're friends from the frontier."
"Is one of them the eldest son who must inherit the family business?"
"One of them is."
"Then I must decline. I don't want to waste precious training time on a student who can't become a military asset for the North."

The Northern Grand Duchess Irene showed no interest either.
She seemed to think that surely no one would die in an event organized by the principal.
Unlike Mob, who was anxious about the slim possibility, Irene was completely detached since it concerned students she had no connection with, so her cold attitude wasn't strange.

"Has anyone seen Zuang?"
"She's an assassin. Even if you try to find her, she won't be visible. You'd have to leave a designated mark at a specified location and wait a full day just to make contact. Unless you're Oknodie or Titoso, lower-class students like us won't even see her face."

Zuang wouldn't even deal with lower-class students.
Even then, face-to-face meetings only happened when she was carrying out missions commissioned by students for points.
Curiosity mixed with fear arose about what kind of missions an assassin would accept at the Academy, but there was no time to wait for Zuang in this urgent situation.

'The golden time for a kidnapped person to return safely is 48 hours. I can't hope for Zuang's help when just making contact takes a day.'

If he reached out to Oknodie who was focused on her studies, Gisele wouldn't stand by quietly.
Honestly, they weren't close enough classmates to risk incurring Gisele's anger.

-Mob, you're strong.

Still, these students acknowledged him as strong.
He didn't want to easily give up on their friends.
Because if you're strong, you should be able to meet someone's expectations.
Lotto, Irene, and Zuang couldn't do it, but he was different.
As a fellow lower-class student, he understood their frustration.
They had nowhere to turn.
If professors and instructors didn't protect them, they were abandoned.
The student council prioritized senior students.
Advanced class students each had their own reasons for refusing to rescue a lower-class student who offered no benefit.

"Jaku. So about this..."
"You idiot. You're going to go yourself to save people you're not even close with?"
"Yes."
"And you're asking me to help you too?"
"Please."
"Are you stupid? It's true they have no one to rely on but you, but how long do you think their gratitude will last after you help them? You're risking serious injury or getting kidnapped yourself, and what do you get in return? Nothing."

It's as foolish as a civilian trying to protect a victim fleeing from a random serial killer!

"I still want to do it. Like you said, I'm stupid."

Mob thumped his armor.

"Oknodie was like that too. Helping me brought her no benefit, but think about what that child did for someone as insignificant as me."

Jaku's expression changed subtly.
Certainly, Mob had been lucky.
Thanks to catching Oknodie's eye, he achieved tremendous growth.
Of course, while Oknodie designed the training course to accelerate his growth, it was Mob who diligently followed it, so his effort and passion deserved recognition.
However, if Oknodie hadn't created that training course for him in the first place, such growth would never have been possible.
Unconditional kindness.
A donation without expectation of return.
Mob owed such a great debt to Oknodie.
Oknodie's goodwill, both a debt and a light to him, had fostered feelings of admiration in Mob's heart.

"Fine, I give up. Who can stop your stubbornness?"
"Thanks, Jaku! I knew you'd help!"
"Bring those idiots who lost their friend. They might be useful."

True to his secretly intelligent nature, Jaku had already come up with a plan.


* * *


Jaku set up a bait trap using the haunted coins of the lower-class students.

"Didn't Gisele say that? Hook ghosts place new cursed items near existing cursed items. We're using the monster's habits to lure it into a trap."
"What about the trap setup cost?"
"Those guys should pay for it. It would be unconscionable for us to cover that too."

The lower-class students who had come to ask Mob for help hesitated.
They came because they felt guilty about abandoning their friend and running away, but now they're reluctant to open their wallets?
Jaku openly mocked them.

"How pitiful for the kidnapped guy. He lost not only his life but his friendship too."
"Watch what you say! We were just worried because we have so few points!"
"We'll pay! We'll pay, okay!"

For the trap setup, they directly invited a priest from a religious order.

"So which order is this priest from?"
"The God of Harmony, he says."
"Then why does he look so displeased?"

Mob, who had already found a suitable location for the trap, was puzzled by Jaku's sour expression as he arrived with their classmates and the priest.

"I went to find him during his meal and saw him pouring sweet and sour sauce on his rice."
"Some people put sauce on sweet and sour pork. That's how you eat freshly served sweet and sour pork at restaurants."
"Not on the pork. On the rice."
"......"

Sweat beaded on Mob's face.

"Why on earth? What's the purpose of such a horrible act? Even in the Empire, people who play with food are branded as traitor families."
"The God of Harmony raises faith when you harmoniously mix things that don't go together. The followers of that god are truly pitiful."

The All-Food-Fulfillment Religion that serves the God of Harmony.
It was a religious order with such brutal faith verification methods that one might wonder how it could be considered a benevolent deity.

"Ho ho. Don't worry. Priests of benevolent deities don't force their doctrines on others. Except for the ghost that will step onto this sacred magic circle."

The students forced smiles at the priest's banter, his belly protruding like a mountain.
They didn't want to offend him, especially since he had expressed interest in watching first-year students exorcise a named ghost.

"As first-years, you probably haven't learned about curse studies yet, but since I've been invited to the Academy, I'll teach you something. Ghosts usually remain in this world due to strong attachments from their living days, and those that harm people are ones with deep grudges."
"Is it true that even dangerous ghosts can't move in the presence of divine power?"
"Ho ho ho. That's right. Divine power is divine power. When those things are nailed with divine power, they can't move a muscle."

Everyone's gaze toward the priest showing off his Gen Z slang grew more anxious, but the operation had already begun.


* * *


"That's great then! You got help from the priest. Why did you come to me?"

In response to Oknodie's question, Jaku grimaced, clutching his arm soaked with blood.

"That thing threw a pile of cursed items from outside the divine exorcism circle, and the circle's power faded. I'm never associating with people who eat rice with sweet and sour sauce again."
"What about Mob?"
"I don't know. I barely escaped myself. I saw the two lower-class idiots get caught and the priest run away, but Mob probably got captured too. That armor is heavy."

That armor, you made him wear it.
It was Jaku's ploy to make Oknodie help Mob by stimulating her guilt, but he was honestly skeptical.
Would Oknodie have enough conscience left?
What if she actually thought it was convenient to get rid of someone she found troublesome to raise?
As Jaku's imagination ran wild in negative directions, Oknodie's response exceeded his expectations.

"Then I'll go in just one day!"
"You need time to prepare thoroughly?"
"If Mob was captured, the bound curse armor will be copied too, right? It would be perfect to collect them as enhancement materials, so I should give it time to copy the armor!"
"..."

Is it really okay for Mob to receive training from such a monster? Jaku began to genuinely worry about his friend's future, though for entirely different reasons than before.
