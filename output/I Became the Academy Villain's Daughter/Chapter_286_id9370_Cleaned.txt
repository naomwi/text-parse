Especially not a noble lady from the Sebiche family, whose influence has waned.

In my heart? Is this a proposal? Is she suggesting we have children? Is she seducing me? Would I become a noble too?

Even his gloomy imagination couldn't overcome the weight of reality.

"I'm sorry. No, I apologize."

Sandcooker rejected the passionate noble lady's proposal, thinking it was beyond his station, yet feeling momentarily happy and satisfied.

"So if Rogini doesn't need help, what assistance are you seeking from me?"
"Oh right. Pyoi, who went to join the mutiny yesterday, hasn't returned. She must have been caught by the crew and is getting her bottom spanked."

Looking at Sandcooker's innocent expression, Arcadia figured out what was happening.
Because he always wore that prickly expression typical of magicians, it took her a while to notice, but Sandcooker was actually quite handsome—enough to make maidens swoon.
She remembered hearing rumors that the Magic Tower was full of even stranger eccentrics, and among them were terrifying masters who, having missed their prime marriage years and grown dark with desire, unleashed their hidden lusts on their disciples.

'The Foundation probably has magicians too, but surely they don't do bottom spanking.'

Whatever the Foundation does would likely be far more terrible and cruel.

Arcadia repeated what Oknodie had told her to say when troublesome situations arose.
For some reason, Sandcooker muttered, "Could there be common knowledge that the stuffy apprentice magicians at the Magic Tower don't know about...?" and reluctantly agreed.

"Was the cruise ship's blackout also because of the mutiny?"
"I think so. That Titosoga helped the Northern Grand Duchess."

"No use pretending? I already know Titosoga was some kind of secret weapon. It's impressive enough to wipe out all the lights."
"Huh? Um? Are we talking about the same Titosoga I know...?"
"Still playing dumb. I get it. It's supposed to be a secret, right? Don't worry, I won't run my mouth about it. I'll keep today's passionate events as memories in my heart."

What on earth happened to Titosoga?
And why is this man blushing unpleasantly and glancing sideways like a shy bride on her wedding night when I haven't done anything? And why do I keep feeling a burning gaze from Rogini's statue?

Irene remembered the conversation she had with Oknodie when Titosoga's lighting stand was plundered by silkworm moth people!
She was struck with inspiration seeing the abundant lights all over the cruise ship.

"Irene. I have a favor! Would you contract with me and start a mutiny?"

Irene was planning to win Oknodie's favor anyway.
Having received permission from the relevant party, there was nothing holding her back.
Titosoga, received as a bonus gift, nervously looked around, pretending to be interested in the lights on the lighting stands and fiddling with the pillars, but these actions gave Irene the crucial hint.
I see.
What Oknodie had said about Titosoga's lighting stand.
The lights overflowing throughout the cruise ship.
This ship existed to enhance Titosoga's lighting stand!

"Find me a production craftsman who can enhance items by combining two of the same material."
"Hmph. You've gotten fixated on something trivial."

Though Xing showed reluctance, he cooperated readily when told it was to help Oknodie.

"...When I asked you to find a craftsman on the ship, did you have to bring someone who looks like a criminal about to be executed, bound and with a sack over their head?"
"Be grateful I brought them alive."

Though the invitation was somewhat aggressive, Irene found it convenient when the craftsman, once the sack was removed, burst into tears and promised to help with anything.
Thinking she should occasionally adopt Xing's methods, she showed the craftsman several lights taken from the ship and Titosoga's lighting stand.

"Please enhance this. It's to help Titosoga, a close friend of Oknodie, the Foundation's top scholarship student."
"Enhance...? Am I really allowed to enhance? Are you saying I can commit this forbidden destructive act prohibited by imperial law?"
"...Why is enhancement a destructive act?"

The production craftsman, still tightly bound with rope, looked around and spoke in a hushed voice.

"Enhancement is a secret technique of production craftsmen that uses two identical items to strengthen the performance of one. Naturally, mastering it requires an enormous quantity of items, which is too burdensome for ordinary production workers to afford."
"So if they don't have their own items, they use other people's?"
"Didn't the Northern Grand Duchess herself bring perfectly good lights from the cruise ship to enhance the lighting stand? Imagine if such things happened repeatedly in public facilities, or if someone blew up their neighbor's property through enhancement. Think of the chaos and expense it would cause society."

It was dizzying just hearing about it.
In the resource-scarce North, what if soldiers selfishly used their comrades' weapons as enhancement materials for their own?
One soldier's combat power might increase slightly, but others who lost their swords would face dramatically higher mortality rates.

"Enhancement, despite being classified as production, is extremely destructive and cruel. There's a reason the Empire designated this technology as forbidden."

Was it too much for Oknodie to engage in such unreasonable behavior on a cruise ship her parents had sent her on to play with friends?
Such thoughts disappeared completely when Irene saw students who had fallen to the working class, either imprisoned underground and released after receiving Foundation scholarships to repay points, or diligently working as crew members.
If the other side crossed the line first, there was no reason students shouldn't cross it too.

'Perhaps this is an extension of the Academy's education.'

Socially unacceptable actions.
Outrageous ways of thinking.
The Academy encourages boldly committing them.
After all, one must survive to do anything.
Using all sorts of tricks, learning to counter others' tricks, and making even the most rule-abiding students understand how to avoid getting entangled in illegal activities or protect themselves.

That must be the difference between an educational institution and a private enterprise.
If that was the policy of the Foundation and Oknodie, she, as an invited guest, had no choice but to follow.
