<373 - The Professor's Laboratory>

The professor's laboratory.
Zuang had expected a lab bench with specialized equipment, bustling teaching assistants, and instructors everywhere.
Contrary to her expectations, Professor Weird's laboratory was an enormous botanical garden.

"Why join a plant club? This place is already a botanical garden. Couldn't you just become Professor Weird's assistant?"
"A club is something you join because you want to, but being an assistant might be something you're kidnapped into doing."
"You get kidnapped into clubs too. According to the contract, you're forced to work regularly or pay a penalty with points."

Zuang's observation was sharp.
Oknodie seemed to know the answer, but as always, she wore that hesitant smile, wondering if she should share what she knew.

"Never mind. I don't need to know that badly. So where in this botanical garden is Professor Weird?"
"Oveeeer there!"

When they first entered, it hadn't seemed so large, but now the ceiling of the botanical garden soared over 100 meters high, with an enormous tree standing at its center.
Like a circular stone staircase carved from rock, wooden steps protruded from the tree's surface, leading up to a large knothole that could only be reached by climbing quite a distance.
From there came an ear-splitting chirping sound that made Zuang turn to look at Oknodie.

"We're going up there?"

Just from the volume, it was clearly the cry of an enormous bird—enough to frighten even third-year students, let alone first-years.

"That's the main entrance, but there's a side door!"

Enhancing her eyes with magic, Zuang's vision improved like a telescope, magnifying objects in the distance.
Upon closer inspection, she could see a small rectangular mark between the tree bark that looked like a door.
There were even people—students or instructors, she couldn't tell—busily carrying things up and down those treacherous wooden stairs.

"Want to race to see who gets up first?"
"Sure."

Befitting a professor's laboratory, there were several security measures along the way.
Vine traps that wrapped around ankles, beehives positioned along the path, sticky platforms covered in adhesive, leaf platforms that crumbled when stepped on...
Forgetting they were in a competition, Zuang couldn't help but ask:

"Oknodie, does Professor Weird want his assistants to fall to their deaths here?"
"I don't think so! These must be devices to repel spies or intruders sent by other professors!"
"How do the assistants get in and out?"
"They probably learn through experience or from seniors!"
"I'm definitely never becoming an assistant."
"Hehe! Everyone says that! Before they get the points."
"...Are assistant positions that lucrative?"
"Quite good, I hear! It's more profitable to be an assistant during the semester than to take a leave of absence and become an instructor!"

I see. I was wondering what the difference was between assistants and instructors since their jobs seemed oddly similar, but it's about being enrolled versus on leave.
Gathering information that wouldn't hurt to know, Zuang shot out sticky threads from her hands and climbed up the tree wall.

"Ah, that's cheating!"
"There's no rule saying we have to follow the path."

Pleased with her successful trick, Zuang smiled slyly as she forged a shortcut.
Despite leaping over several wooden platforms at an incredible speed, Oknodie couldn't overcome Zuang's vertical climbing speed and arrived late.

"You used flying magic at the sports festival. Why not use it now?"
"If a flying object above a certain size is detected, the chirpy comes out of its nest!"
"...That enormous presence from earlier?"
"Yes!"
"The professor really has bad taste. Giving such a pathetic name to a giant monster."

If one said they were attacked by a giant monster nesting in a massive tree, it would sound like a veteran adventurer's tale of failure. But being attacked by something called "chirpy" sounds more like a bumbling third-rate adventurer's misadventure.
But thinking about it, this could be a deception tactic to prevent intruders from properly recognizing the dangers of Professor Weird's laboratory.
Who wouldn't let their guard down with such a pathetic name for a guardian?

"Anyway, a bet is a bet. Grant me one wish."
"Alright. Just give me one kiss?"
"Not a chance. I'll ask for something expensive later."
"Hmph."

Pushing open the side door, they entered a passageway filled with clear, refreshing air that cleared their sinuses.
Beyond the corridor were various laboratories with different magical protections lined up on both sides, one of which had its door slightly ajar.

"Hehehe, this girl's body is so tender. All that loving care was worth it."
"Urgh, help me here. She's struggling too much."
"Kekeke. If you don't eat nicely when your big brothers offer food, we'll put a gag in your mouth!"
"There, now you can't move. Stop being difficult and just eat what we give you!"

The content of the conversation was impossible to ignore. Were they taking advantage of the blind spot in the professor's lab to commit crimes against a female assistant?

'Hmm. How convenient.'

Zuang was pleased.
It had been a while since she'd found meat bags she could stab to death without consequences.

"Oknodie. Let's go in."
"Huh? Do we have to?"

Dragging along an indifferent Oknodie who thought it wasn't their business, Zuang barged into the research lab.
Expecting to find a fragile victim surrounded by men, naked with something inappropriate in her mouth, Zuang was extremely confused.
There were no women in sight, just male assistants clinging to a carnivorous plant with tentacles, pouring food into it.

"Kuhehe. Look at these magnificent tentacles. All that effort combining culture media was worth it, wasn't it?"
"Stop talking nonsense and hold the vines properly so they don't come loose! The thorn vines are hitting my back and it hurts like hell, you idiot!"
"..."

Devastated by the scene that was completely different from what she'd expected, Zuang quietly walked out.
Oknodie asked her:

"Were you that eager to see a carnivorous plant eating? Zuang, you have such strange hobbies."
"It's a misunderstanding. I went in thinking it was something else."
"A flame flower? An ice flower?"
"I thought it was a person and went to help. And maybe satisfy some personal desires along the way."

But there was still an unresolved question.

"Why are they raising such strange giant plant monsters in the research lab?"
"Probably teaching materials."
"They're not planning to use them for first-years, are they...?"
"Maybe?"

Oknodie pointed to a plaque on the research lab that had F4L3 engraved on it.

"It's a lab for low-risk experimental subjects that first and second-years can handle, so it should be fine even for first-years, right?"
"...Just out of curiosity, what's the danger level of this tree we entered?"
"B1U5."
"In plain terms?"
"A first-year can enter alone, but it's a dangerous facility that would require the combined power of five novice professors somewhere inside!"
"...Now I understand why some seniors seem a bit crazy. Let's just find Dobi quickly and get out of here."

Zuang had no desire to encounter anything more dangerous than a plant that beats people with vines when it throws a tantrum about not wanting to eat.

"But Dobi is a first-year, so he must be doing something less dangerous than those seniors getting hit by plants with vines, right?"
"Yes! Getting hit by spirits must hurt less than that!"

Wandering only through the corridors of Professor Weird's research lab—which by Zuang's standards could easily be classified as a dungeon—they quickly found their destination in a room tagged B1, indicating the lowest difficulty level.
There was Dobi, crouched in the middle of the room with his arms covering his head, being beaten mercilessly by about ten angry spirits surrounding him.

"...Who said it hurts less?"
"...Their damage must be lower! He just can't block it properly."
"Can we attack those spirits?"
"Hmm... I think it's better not to!"

Seeing Oknodie—who normally acted first and thought later—backing away, Zuang quietly put away the shuriken she had pulled from her sleeve.

"Spirit friends. I think he's been hit enough now. Can we take our friend with us?"

The furious spirits shouted in protest.

"Not a chance!"
"He didn't bring the offerings he promised for waking us up."
"He didn't even say 'Good morning, princess'!"
"And he didn't bring any fairy tales!"
"Such a bad person deserves to be hit!"

Their reasons for being angry were so childish!

"Hitting him won't get you what you want. The world doesn't work that way. Why not ask Dobi to promise to bring fairy tales to read to you later?"
"Lies! Humans always deceive spirits. If you want our trust, pay the price!"
"If he doesn't bring them, I'll come for Dobi's head. That's how an assassin makes promises."

The spirits' eyes dangerously sparkled.

"His life...?"
"Yes. It would be satisfying to kill him instead."
"Death to liars!"

Dobi, with a black eye, trembled pitifully.

"Do you really have to make that kind of promise...? I wasn't even the one who promised to read fairy tales..."
"You don't have to."
"Really?"

To the hopeful-eyed Dobi, Zuang coldly replied:

"You can just keep getting beaten. Want me to close the door on my way out?"
"No... I'll just do it..."
"You don't have to if you don't want to. Promises need sincerity for the other party to believe them."
"I'll do it... *sob*."

Through the Good Morning Fairy Tale Gift Agreement with the spirits, Dobi was able to gain his freedom.

"Why did you come here in the first place? It's common knowledge that a professor's laboratory is more dangerous than a wild dungeon."
"I didn't want to come either... But Professor Weird said he had documents to deliver to the principal and that I seemed more capable and smarter than I looked, then kidnapped me... *sob sob sob*."
"Ah..."
"But the professor isn't bad... He said I could go back after waking up the spirits. He even gave me 1,000 points. It was my fault for not knowing how to get hit without it hurting."

This kid has lost his mind in such a short time.
Zuang shook her head.
She would bet the Tito family's illumination squad that this point-blinded fool would soon voluntarily return to the laboratory.
