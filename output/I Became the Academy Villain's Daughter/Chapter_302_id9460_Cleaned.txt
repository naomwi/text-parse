# 302 - The Longer It Gets, The More Dangerous

Gisele recognized that the situation was flowing in an extremely dangerous direction.

"Can you defeat the hero candidate's ego?"
"It's difficult."
"Why? He's just a candidate while you're an active hero."
"Different times."

Ishtar couldn't hide her dismay.

"In my time, the monotheistic deity Sophemia chose me directly as the hero without selecting other candidates. So there was no reason for candidates to compete and adventure against each other. That's why my growth stats aren't as high as the Apostle of Anrage."
"So you're saying that man has accumulated such tremendous karma that it negates the advantages of being an active hero."
"Plus, techniques possessed by his other egos are mixing in. It's beyond my abilities to overcome."

The hero was stuck.
If that was the case, they needed Oknodie's help.
Let's move the doll left on the deserted island to request assistance.
That was certainly the plan, but...
The situation unfolding on the other side was no less dangerous.

'Are they trying to kill each other?!'

When he first saw it, he couldn't believe his eyes.
Ice fragments flying continuously through the darkness, and between them, the flashing blade of a dagger.
No sooner had she twisted her body in midair to parry the dagger than another pitch-black dagger that didn't reflect moonlight bounced off with a sound.
Harmless fragments and harmful daggers.
Daggers that reflected light and daggers that didn't.
Leape mixed feints with real attacks, adding danger to every movement and every strike.
One couldn't let their guard down just because they could see an attack, and if they relaxed thinking something was harmless, they'd be caught off guard by an unexpected strike.
Having to respond to every attack at every timing, Oknodie naturally lost her timing and breathing rhythm, and was one-sidedly dragged along by Leape's wave of attacks.

Clang-clang-clang!

Even knife fighting with daggers clashing in close quarters in the darkness!

'There have been countless allegations of domestic abuse surrounding Oknodie.'

Could the Foundation really be that cruel?
Maybe we misunderstood something because of Oknodie's eccentricity.
Such doubts remained somewhere in his mind as a measure of restraint.
That last bit of restraint disappeared.
The truth finally revealed was visible beyond the doll under the sight magic.
Her unnaturally familiar responses spoke volumes.
Oknodie had experienced such brutal real combat as if it were daily life during her time at the Foundation!

"What are you talking about?"

The voice belonged to Hestia, who had returned to the warehouse to rest after repelling the hero candidate's advance once.
Hestia's face quickly hardened as she was drawn to look at the sight magic by the sound of weapons clashing, befitting a mercenary.

"Is that Oknodie?"
"Yes, it is."
"Those crazy bastards."

What appeared to Gisele as merely a brutal fight looked even more dangerous in Hestia's eyes.

"These bastards are using mana."
"Pardon?"
"Those daggers, look carefully. There's silver contained in the blade."
"I thought it was from the moonlight."
"It's mana light."

War mercenaries live by bloodshed, so their temperaments are rough.
When conflicts arise, it's not uncommon for fights to escalate to the point where limbs are severed.
That's why, even when fighting with genuine animosity, they follow an unspoken rule not to use mana, to avoid crossing a line.
Anyone who breaks this rule is considered the scum of the earth.
They're treated like ticking time bombs and become outcasts among other mercenaries.
No matter how confident in their skills or how arrogant they might be, using mana is a line not to be crossed unless they truly intend to go all out.
Yet the assassin had casually crossed that line.
And Oknodie had countered it just as nonchalantly.
This meant that Oknodie's time at the Foundation had been even more brutal than the rough world of mercenaries.

"I'm going back out."

Hestia picked up a hammer instead of her broken axe and opened the warehouse door.

"Where are you going! You're exhausted. Your arms are trembling!"
"Oknodie is enduring a fight much worse than this. There must be something on that deserted island that she absolutely needs to obtain."

Hestia was furious.
At the maid driving Oknodie into a corner.
At her own foolishness for inwardly resenting Oknodie, wondering when she would return while going through such difficult times.

'I promised to protect you. What am I doing?'

A child who gave trust to an unbelievable berserker, became her friend, and showed her what friendship means.
It was shameful that she had harbored complaints after failing to protect such a child at her side and being eliminated in the very early stages of the deserted island auction.

"The hero candidate won't kill us. Fighting him is exhausting to the point of death, but that's all."

It's different from Oknodie's situation.
If it's just about enduring hardship, she can do much more.
No, she must.
If her pledge to protect Oknodie wasn't a lie.
Even if she couldn't protect her at her side, shouldn't she at least protect her friends?

"If you can't help Oknodie, stop just staring at the doll. Let's do what we can."

Hestia's point also brought about a change of heart in Gisele.
He didn't want to show it because of awareness of the Foundation, but it seemed he needed to reveal a bit of the true nature of the dark world's merchant king.

'Don't regret it, Wiheomhae Foundation. You crossed the line first.'

The stagnant shipboard rebellion, blocked by the powerful force of hero candidate K, began to change its course.


* * *


K felt annoyed.
The Apostle of Anrage is a being completed by twenty personalities trapped in one body.
Even if one personality is powerful, if other personalities pull that personality down, the ego is pushed out and loses control of the body again.
Like crabs in an open jar, they're all busy pulling each other down!
That's why K's escape required convincing the other egos that there was a way for them to escape this body too, that he would provide sacrifices to help everyone escape.
That way, they wouldn't interfere when he escaped into Xing's body.
Of course, the others didn't need exceptional bodies.
If he gave them such bodies, what if they destroyed his new vessel?
The grudges held by the egos united in the collective of the Apostle of Anrage were immense.
The longer they spent hindering each other's actions, the greater their resentment grew.
By now, they were practically mortal enemies.

'I was planning to fill 19 suitable vessels and proceed with the ritual.'

But a problem arose.
The students, despite realizing his strength, refused to back down.
Breaking them, crushing their spirits.
Despite using every means to repel the strong students, others emerged like substitutes and clung on.
Eliminating them would be easy.
The problem was the gaze of the other egos.

-It's unforgivable to subdue them and then let them go.
-Are you planning to monopolize the good vessel for yourself?
-In that case, we'll drive you out and continue the great ritual ourselves.

If they weren't going to be sacrifices, he shouldn't incapacitate them.
Rather, even if the students tried to charge until they fainted, he had to drive them away before that.
For the sake of what comes after the great ritual.
But that had become difficult.

[Holy Sword Release]

Hero Ishtar and Saintess Yupi.
Surprisingly, the selfish hero party that only cared about themselves began to help the students.
They fought fiercely with the intention of helping others who had nothing to do with them.
The hero's vessel was tempting.
He also liked that she was a woman of great beauty.
In life, it's better to be a woman than a man.
She would also receive less suspicion.
Above all, the fact that he, a hero "candidate," could become a real hero was attractive.
However, to subdue Ishtar, he needed to cross the "suppression line" of his output.

'I need to draw the power I use to suppress other egos into battle.'

If the first control line is maintained to avoid subduing, and the second control line is suppressed to avoid killing, then the third control line is kept to prevent other egos from opening and to maintain his turn.
No matter how inexperienced, she was still a hero.
To defeat her, he had to cross the third control line.
From K's perspective, who wanted to lead the great ritual proactively and escape in Xing's body, he obviously couldn't cross the third control line.
So he couldn't subdue the hero.
That gave courage to other advanced class powerhouses as well.
Students who could be subdued without effort if fought separately kept charging in rotation.
To prevent him from kidnapping other students.
To prevent him from taking other actions.
K felt increasingly anxious.
His allowed time was decreasing.
The power of other egos pulling him down was growing stronger.
As if she had eaten something wrong, even the berserker who had just collapsed was charging again with greater fury.
With decreasing control in an unreasonable fight where he couldn't and shouldn't subdue his opponents.
K was forced to make a choice.
Should he subdue all the interferers and perform the ritual, even if it meant giving strong bodies to competitors and risking having his opportunity stolen by another ego?
Or should he give up his chance and hinder others so they couldn't enjoy this opportunity either?
Or should he promise that everyone would get strong bodies and make a new start, and then knock them all down at once?

"This is the crisis you brought upon yourselves. I'll make you regret charging in without valuing your lives."

Just as K genuinely harbored killing intent, everyone on the ship lost their footing.
The cruise ship lurched greatly as it changed course.
The ship had started moving.
Toward the deserted island in the distance, where the fight was taking place.

"I told them to do something if they couldn't stop the fight, but they're planning to destroy the island?"

From Hestia's muttering, who was dejected even after her rage subsided, K realized.
Some crazy student had infiltrated the wheelhouse while his feet were tied and turned the ship's helm.
They were trying to forcibly end the deserted island auction and bring Jonna, another executive of the Foundation, onto the ship.

'No.'

If the auction ends, a skilled person who can stop his great ritual will return.
His ego as a hero candidate couldn't overcome this situation.

-Then hand it over.

Twenty egos.
The one sleeping in the deepest place, the being that all 19 egos had thoroughly restrained, whispered.

-If not now, none of us will have another chance.

K conceded.
As the hero candidate's ego sank, a new ego surged rapidly.

"Phew. It's been a while. Regaining the sensation of a human body."

Ishtar, who was about to cut down the fool talking to himself in the middle of an intense battle.
Her decisive strike was blocked by a barrier that spread out in front of the man's outstretched hand.

"A mana barrier? And it stopped my strike with a holy sword using dark attributes?!"
"Why are you so surprised, child? A demon is one who has corrupted even their soul to darkness. Is it not natural for someone who has pledged despair in the afterlife to possess such strength?"

Both the deserted island auction and the shipboard rebellion began to rapidly increase in danger.
