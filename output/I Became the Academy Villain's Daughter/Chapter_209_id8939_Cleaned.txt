# Enhanced Translation: Chapter 209 - The Switch

The Aptitude Assessment Hat glanced nervously at Oknodie and asked.

"Kid. How long do I have to keep doing this?"
"Keep scrubbing harder!"
"I've already polished the floor until it shines."

Oknodie narrowed her eyes fiercely and raised a finger.

"If you don't want to do it, just say so. I'll send you right back to Professor Bronze's box!"
"P-please, anything but that. Being tortured without question or explanation is too cruel."
"Well, who told you to doodle with precious materials? And you even tried to mess with valuable Stat Stones. If you were human, I would have taught you a real lesson."

The Aptitude Assessment Hat felt indignant.
I wasn't the only one involved.
There was an accomplice who agreed to share the mana stone!
He glanced at his silent accomplice who was keeping his mouth shut and playing innocent—the Baby Mandrake.
The Mandrake, who had been spinning in circles inside the flower vase, snapped back.

"Waah."
'You little bastard.'

While the Aptitude Assessment Hat was cursing internally, Oknodie was staring down at him intently.
The hat grew increasingly anxious.
Wasn't this child usually talkative?
When he saw her during lectures before, she was bright and chatty.
Today she's quiet, like a completely different person.
He couldn't tell what she was thinking.
That's what terrified him.

"Should I clean the windows too?"
"No. Instead, sit on this cushion for a moment."

Here it comes.
The Aptitude Assessment Hat sat down, feeling like he was sitting on a bed of thorns.
Oknodie began to speak.

"While you were busy cleaning, I've been thinking about what to do with you."
"Could you show me some leniency?"
"That's become quite troublesome. You know quite a lot about me, don't you?"
"I-I promise not to tell anyone about your status window and function window. I already told you everything I saw."
"I appreciate that. Especially the 10,000 points it costs to view the status window."

The hat cautiously asked.

"By any chance, the materials I tampered with yesterday..."
"Worth 1,200 points!"
"Phew."

So I have 8,800 points worth of life left.
His relief was short-lived.
The specific figure made his skin crawl.

'This kid can appraise the value of every item?'

It wasn't for nothing that she had a black market dealer as a friend and took appraisal lessons from a righteous thief.

"But I'm still uneasy about leaving things as they are in case something like this happens again."
"I promise it won't happen again."
"Can you swear on divine language?"
"Divine language for a mere ego-item like me? The gods wouldn't even acknowledge it."
"Then I have no choice. I'm uncomfortable with you secretly 'communicating' somewhere, but your status window feature is useful. Since I can't kill you or give you away, I'll have to dispose of you."
"D-dispose!? Don't tell me you're planning to make a custom box and put me in it...!"

Snicker. Oknodie laughed lightly as if she'd heard an amusing joke.

"Of course not."
"H-haha. It was a joke after all? I knew it from the beginning. Good children don't do such things."
"You'll just be dwelling in a different place."

Oknodie took a rope and tied the hat tightly. The hat felt a sense of dread and struggled, but a hat with rock-bottom combat power—even Professor Bronze acknowledged this—couldn't escape such rough binding.

"L-let me go! What are you planning to do to me!"
"Don't be so scared. It won't hurt."

With practiced hand movements, Oknodie drew a magic circle on the wall using ink.

The hat, who had been trying to comfort himself with the thought that a mere first-year couldn't draw much of a magic circle, felt his hope turn to despair as he watched the magic circle expand in real-time.

'W-what is this speed!'

He knew she had genius-level talent.
But that was just 'talent.'
One can't create something from nothing.
Learning must come before application, that's normal.
How could a first-year who had never learned about magic circles create one at such an impossible speed?

'No... there is one. There's exactly one magic circle this kid could have learned from, even if just briefly.'

The Talent Assessment Hat was shocked.

'That magic circle... is the one I drew!!'

Oknodie had watched intently as he drew the magic circle for communicating with the Foundation's emergency network.
The time was short.
At most, one minute.
Barely enough time to memorize it.
But to draw such a perfect magic circle...

'Did she understand the structure and mechanism of magic circles!?'

This wasn't simple understanding.
It was a skill only possible after engraving a comparative magic circle in one's mind and repeatedly separating and recombining it to create hundreds or thousands of one's own magic circles.
Was the name "genius" this terrifying?
Is such a thing truly possible for a human?
The weight of the word "genius," which he couldn't fully comprehend even after seeing it with his own eyes, finally became real to him.

"Ta-da! Complete!"
"But... what kind of magic circle is that?"
"A consciousness exchange magic circle!"
"Consciousness... what?"
"When you go in, make sure you reflect on your actions. Understood?"
"But... consciousness exchange requires a target or it won't work! With this one-sided application, you're just casting an exchange spell on a normal wall that will end up doing nothing!"
"Oh, don't worry about that. The exchange target is already in there."

The Stat Stone clicked into place in the magic circle.
The Stat Stone, containing enough powerful mana to permanently increase an ability score, became the power source that fully activated the magic circle.
Flash!
With a blinding light, he felt his consciousness being pulled out.
After feeling his consciousness being helplessly extracted from the hat, he noticed his field of vision suddenly widening while his body became extremely heavy.

"T-this!? I'm inside the wall!!"

At the same time, the hat where he had dwelled for so long slowly opened its eyes and shed tears of joy.

Ego-item, Dark Aptitude Assessment Hat.
Ego-item, Answering Door.

It was the moment when the consciousness of the two ego-items were exchanged.


* * *


Unlike the troublesome Mr. Hat, the Answering Door was a great help during the midterm exams.

"I brought you out because I'm grateful you told me about the exam format and scope!"
"I'm... so happy... I thought I'd have to live my whole life without a physical body, just passing through building walls."

Actually, this door shouldn't be brought out easily.
The main reason is that it's a useful tool that reveals exam questions, so it should be used wisely throughout the first year.
But there's another reason.
The door also has an unfair pattern mechanism that can pop out from anywhere.
Normally, the ego dwelling in the door is a student who was trapped in the wall due to an unfortunate accident.
If this gets twisted, its identity flips 180 degrees to become a sealed demon.
Taking it out because you feel sorry for it means you've released a demon with your own hands.

'But since I have plenty of dark mana, that's actually better for me!'

With dark mana, the being with more can exert strong influence over those with less.
A demon trapped in a wall, unable to gather mana and struggling, can actually be used like a servant.

"Was it hard for you? I can't promise you'll be able to move around freely, but from now on, you can go wherever I go!"
"Thank you so much..."
"But why did you help me? You were closer to Hestia before."

Unlike the first Mr. Hat with his sly, middle-aged man's voice, the second Mr. Hat replied in a careful, calm female voice.

"I overheard other dormitory students talking about Oknodie."
"About me?"
"Circumstantial evidence about how the Foundation might have abused and raised you. Gossip about you being an unpleasant child they didn't want to get close to. Things like that."
"Hmph. Don't listen to what weaklings say."
"I've thought this for a while, but Oknodie, you're really strong... I would have cried."
"Crying doesn't change anything, does it?"
"That's right. If I had known that, I might have graduated from the Academy without getting trapped in the wall during my student days..."

Oh. So this resident of the "Answering Wall" was a student.
The most common and safe probability was drawn.

"It might sound shameless coming from someone who just escaped with your help, but I have one favor to ask."
"Go ahead. If it's something I can do, I'm willing to help! After all, I want to be able to freely see the status window whenever I want, thanks to Mr. Hat."
"Tomorrow is the second guardian visitation. Take me with you to the meeting place."
"I was planning to keep wearing the hat anyway, so that's fine... but why?"

Is she trying to escape?
That would be problematic.

"I want to find out. Whether the Foundation people are as terrible as the rumors say or not."

Not escape at all—she just wants to help.
I feel bad for immediately assuming the worst.

"Alright! But don't make any sound, since Jonna might notice something's different."
"I won't. My goal isn't communication, but to see a bit of what the Foundation is really like."
