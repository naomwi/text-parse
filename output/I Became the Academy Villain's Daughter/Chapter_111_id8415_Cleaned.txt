# 111 - An Unexpected Storm

Bigstone, thinking he wouldn't graduate on time if he got too entangled with these dangerous juniors, immediately put on a gas mask and raided the red-toothed mushroom farm.

"It's not even shift change time, why are you already here... Ack! W-who are you?!"
"Be quiet!"

When Bigstone threw a vial from his chest to the ground, gas erupted with a poof.
The guard who inhaled the pink gas coughed a few times before clutching his throat and collapsing.

"Heh. That senior is surprisingly competent when he needs to be."
"Well, he is a second-year after all!"
"True. If he's made it to second year in this academy, I should give him some credit."

Zuang was genuinely impressed.
Among the groaning, collapsed red-toothed mushrooms, he wrapped wide pieces of cloth tightly around the wrists of three of them, and the monsters were easily dragged away.
The gas contained <Sleep> and <Diffusion> components, while the cloth had <Binding> and <Weight Reduction> spells cast on it.

"Senior Bigstone has quite a few tricks up his sleeve!"
"If I couldn't do this much, how would I survive the harsh second-year life? Especially with those crazy people running around using sword energy and bombarding with magic."
"That's true."

If you don't have skills, you need to be good at alchemy and enchantment to at least serve as support to survive.

"Now that I've given them to you, the deal is done, right?"
"Yep. I wrote down the location, so please check it!"
"Wait. I need to verify that the item is actually at that location before I hand these over."

Instead of immediately handing over the bound red-toothed mushrooms, he demanded verification.

"You're so picky. Despite being a coward."
"I'm picky because I'm a coward. I'm afraid of being tricked."
"It's fine. If my trading partner is anxious, I can accommodate that much. It's not a big deal anyway."

Above all, Senior Bigstone was someone I'd continue to see in the Eye for Value lectures.
If we didn't properly conclude this deal and rumors spread later, it would be awkward for both of us during class.

"There, see? You just need to go in here."
"...So you push the painting on the wall to enter the space behind the 'flipping wall.' You actually had the courage to attempt such a thing?"

Senior Bigstone seemed shocked that Zuang knew the secret of the art gallery.
Zuang asked with confusion:

"Is there something wrong with the painting?"
"If you mess with a painting protected by the art gallery's security magic, all the statues in here will come to life."
"Then we can just destroy them, right?"

Though it didn't seem like something an assassin should say, Senior Bigstone snorted and pointed at the statues.

"Those statues were supplied for security purposes. From what I know, some were even submitted as graduation projects by fourth-years."
"F-fourth years?!"

Having witnessed the danger of upper-year students at the secret training ground, Zuang finally showed fear.

"Oknodie. Did you know about this?"
"Huh? Of course I did. It's clearly written in the double-sided sticky note room. 'Many treasures are hidden behind the paintings in the art gallery, but if you touch them incorrectly, the statues will come to life!'"
"How could I possibly remember all that already?"
"True. It is a lot."
"Huh? Double-sided sticky note room? What's that?"
"Don't worry about it if you don't know."

Anyway, the deal was done.
Bigstone got his moonshine, and I retrieved the mushrooms to return to the third-year senior.

"They're a bit small... but I'll overlook that considering the quick delivery."
"Thank you, senior!"

When the senior declared forgiveness to this extent, a notification appeared that the event was successfully completed.

[You have completed the Equivalent Exchange event.]
[You acquire 1000 points as an early return bonus.]
[The third-year senior's trust level increases.]
[The trust level of the <Dark Cult> club increases.]
[Your current trust level is <Apprentice Member>.]
[As an apprentice member, you can apply for <Ritual Observation>.]

Suddenly, a Dark Cult route I had no interest in opened up.
I should avoid even talking to them since it's burdensome.
This senior seems unsociable too, so it should be fine if I just don't engage, right?
I bowed my head in greeting and was about to leave when the senior called out, "Wait."
Though I really didn't want to, I turned around with a forced smile.

"Yes?"

"...Eat this."

The senior placed a jelly candy on my palm.
Unlike the rough, coarse touch of their fingers, the jelly was soft and squishy.

"Wow! Thank you!"

Though they look scary and belong to a suspicious club, this senior seems like a nice person.
Right, even if they're a bit cruel to monsters, they're good people if they're kind to humans.
My internal trust level for the senior increased by about 50.

"Eat it now."
"Okay."
"Oknodie? Isn't that... a bit scary?"

Zuang was wary, but I didn't mind and popped the jelly into my mouth.
It was a chewy jelly with that distinctive junk food taste that you have to chew thoroughly.

"...Is it good?"
"Yes!"
"If you join the Dark Cult, you can have more..."

As I was seriously considering it, Zuang poked my side repeatedly with his finger.

"Idiot. How can you be tempted by food?"
"Hmph. But it's delicious."

Due to its nature, the Dark Cult has few regular meetings and public assignments.
This is because they only promote trusted apprentice members to full membership once every few months, allowing them to participate in real rituals and engage in full club activities.
Most members don't even know each other's faces, as the secret organization is traditionally structured as a cell organization.

"...You take one too."
"Eck. No thanks. I'm not joining."
"I'm just giving it to you."
"Then thank you."

Zuang also popped the jelly into his mouth, and his eyes widened.
He clenched both fists, not knowing what to do, and stomped his feet, suggesting it really was delicious.
While I was familiar with junk food taste from Earth, for Zuang, a native of this world, the texture and taste must be even more unfamiliar and fresh.

"...Zuang. You haven't forgotten what you said to me, right? You won't join just because they give you food?"
"Tch. I know. I'm not that stupid."

The Dark Cult recruiting apprentice members with jelly.
Zuang, who almost fell for it but barely escaped.
What an adorable club and an adorable friend.

"Do you always hand out jellies like this?"

Since I didn't remember seeing this in the game, I casually asked, and as expected, the senior shook their head.

"So you gave them to us because we're cute?"

In response to Zuang's confident statement, the senior formed a circle with their thumb and index finger.
The senior moved the circle in front of Zuang's forehead, who was feeling smug, and delivered a flick.

"Ouch!"
"It's not because you're cute. It's because I felt sorry for juniors taking the same professor's class..."
"The same professor?"

The suspicious-looking senior who appeared to be a proper Dark Cult member.
There was only one professor who came to mind.

"...Professor Sadako?"

The senior nodded.
Zuang's expression visibly soured.

"What class of Professor Sadako's are you taking, senior?"
"<Understanding Structural Conflicts and Response Strategies with Undead Summons>."
"...Wow. Just the name sounds awful. Why are you taking it?"
"Because the professor is beautiful."

Zuang looked at the senior with a smile, telling them not to lie.
The senior didn't respond.
Zuang looked at them suspiciously, probing.
The senior still didn't respond.
Finally, the smile disappeared from Zuang's face.
A strange silence fell.

"W-well, that's possible! How important is appearance for a person? You can't help animal instincts!"

I defended the senior out of a strange sense of guilt, and Zuang nodded with an expression that clearly showed disgust but was willing to let it go for the sake of the atmosphere.
Meanwhile, a certain conviction grew within me.
Surely, beneath Professor Sadako's long hair must be a face of surprising beauty.
Otherwise, how could the professor have admirers?

"Which part of the professor's face is the prettiest?"
"The mouth."
"...Really?"
"Her smile is beautiful."

Recalling that professor's laugh, I couldn't imagine what kind of smile she would have...?
The more I tried to imagine it, the more chilling the scene became, but we didn't realize it yet.

"...Apart from taking the same professor's class, I'll give you one last piece of advice, seeing your diligence."
"Yes!"
"Raiding red-toothed mushroom farms is like declaring war on the second-years. It would be good to prepare your heart in advance..."

We had no idea that something truly chilling was about to begin for the carefree, innocent first-year freshmen.


* *


Several second-year red-toothed mushroom farm managers from the 980th class gathered in one place.

"Six of our red-toothed mushrooms have disappeared from our farm. They were frozen alive and kidnapped."
"Over here, mushroom stems that were grilled on an iron plate and left over were found at the scene."
"Is it the seniors' doing?"
"No. From what I've learned, Dragon Principal has given first-years the task of practicing cooking with red-toothed mushrooms, saying he would teach them cooking."
"...First-years touched our farm?"
"Is that true?"
"These brats, do they think we're so easy that they can touch our property, their seniors'?"

As the farm managers raised their voices in anger, someone who wasn't invited to the secret meeting entered.

"My, my. What good does it do for us to be angry among ourselves? The ones who need to be taught a lesson are the first-years with their bad habits."
"Who are you!"
"Outsiders can't attend our meeting, can they?"
"Who brought this guy!"

When no one stepped forward, the tense 980th class second-years raised their staffs, drew their swords, or held vials and thick law books in their hands.
In the tense atmosphere, one student who had been uncertain suddenly shouted, "Ah!"

"Wait. I recognize that voice. This person is the <Consultant> who received points in exchange for teaching us how to create farms, organizing related lecture lists, and giving advice!"
"What? So this person is the one who founded our Red-Toothed Mushroom Managers Association?"

The very person who introduced the good culture of mana farming to the second-years and helped them unite as an illegal profit group by providing meeting places and establishing an underground organization.
The unofficial hidden power behind the Red-Toothed Mushroom Managers Association, the external Advisor, had appeared.

"You, what's your purpose! If it's points, you've already received plenty for your criminal advice!"
"I'm not pleased with the first-years' actions. Especially one of them who interfered with my business."

Bellocasio, the vicious contract fraudster who had trapped classmate errand boys in a trap, turning them into half-slaves.
In a normal playthrough, he would have been satisfied with trapping one influential first-year through contract fraud and using them like a minion, but this time, due to Oknodie's intervention, he hadn't achieved satisfactory results.
Harboring ill feelings toward Oknodie for interfering with his business, Bellocasio saw this red-toothed mushroom commotion as an unexpected opportunity.

"I'm thinking of teaching those arrogant juniors a lesson. Care to join?"

Bellocasio, the 980th class second-year mastermind.
The members of the Red-Toothed Mushroom Managers Association unanimously agreed to his proposal.
A storm was beginning to approach the peaceful daily lives of the first-years.
